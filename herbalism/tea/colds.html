10 best herbal teas for the common cold

When it comes to herbal teas for the common cold, there are several options that can provide relief.

Peppermint tea is a popular choice due to its ability to soothe a sore throat and alleviate congestion. Echinacea tea is another good option, as it is believed to boost the immune system and reduce the duration of a cold.

Ginger tea is also beneficial, as it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the throat and nasal passages. Chamomile tea is a great choice for its calming and sleep-promoting properties, which can be helpful in getting a good night's rest despite a stuffy nose.

Additionally, a tea blend containing a mix of these herbs can provide a well-rounded and soothing relief.

1. Elderberry

Elderberry tea provides relief from common cold symptoms due to its antioxidant properties, which strengthen the immune system.

This tea contains high levels of vitamins A, B, and C, as well as bioflavonoids, which actively fight off cold-causing viruses. Furthermore, elderberry tea's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties alleviate symptoms by reducing inflammation and inhibiting the growth of bacteria. Additionally, its soothing effect on the throat and nasal passages provides instant comfort and relief.

Drinking elderberry tea regularly ensures a faster recovery from the common cold, thanks to the synergistic benefits of its powerful nutrients.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in elderberry tea that help with the common cold.

  • Flowers: Rich in flavonoids, which are known to have antioxidant properties, providing relief from cold symptoms.
  • Berries: Packed with vitamin C, supporting the immune system during cold.
  • Rhyzomes: Contain tannins, known for their astringent properties, helping alleviate sore throats.
  • Bark: Source of anti-inflammatory compounds, useful in soothing irritations caused by cold.
  • Leaves: Abundant in vital nutrients, supporting the body's natural defenses against common cold viruses.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic elderberry tea for the common cold.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of elderberries, 3 cups of water, and 1-2 cinnamon sticks; simmer for 30 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the elderberries and cinnamon; discard solids.
  3. Stir in 1-2 tablespoons of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice to taste.
  4. Reheat the tea if necessary, but do not let it boil.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea brings relief to those suffering from a common cold due to its menthol content, a natural decongestant.

This organic compound reduces inflammation in the nasal passages, providing immediate relief from nasal congestion. Moreover, peppermint tea soothes sore throats by coating the throat with its warm, aromatic liquid, thereby minimizing irritation. Additionally, this herbal tea contains essential oils and antioxidants that strengthen the immune system, helping the body fight off the cold virus.

Lastly, the act of sipping on a comforting cup of peppermint tea hydrates the body, which is crucial during a cold.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with the common cold.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and rosmarinic acid, peppermint leaves help unclog sinuses and soothe a sore throat.
  • Flowers: High in flavonoids, peppermint flowers promote relaxation and alleviate coughing fits.
  • Stems: Containing essential oils, peppermint stems aid in breaking up mucus and reducing fever.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for the common cold.

  1. In a tea infuser or teapot, place 1-2 teaspoons of dried peppermint leaves.
  2. Pour boiling water (212°F) over the peppermint leaves, allowing enough water for 1 cup of tea.
  3. Steep the peppermint leaves for 5-7 minutes to extract the essential oils and desired flavor.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the peppermint leaves from the teapot.
  5. Serve the peppermint tea immediately, optionally adding honey or lemon to taste.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea brings immediate relief from a common cold due to its potent anti-inflammatory properties, which soothe a sore throat and alleviate congestion.

This warm beverage stimulates perspiration, facilitating the removal of toxins from the body and reducing fever. Additionally, ginger tea's high antioxidant content strengthens the immune system, enabling the body to fight off the cold virus more effectively. Furthermore, ginger's natural oils, specifically gingerols and shogaols, possess antiviral and antibacterial qualities that help eliminate the cold-causing pathogens.

Lastly, ginger tea's active components ease stomach discomfort and vomiting, common symptoms associated with the common cold.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with the common cold.

  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of ginger are used to make medicinal tea for the common cold because they contain natural oils and phenol compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Leaves: Ginger leaves are used in medicinal tea for the common cold due to their high levels of vitamins and minerals that help boost the immune system.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers are used in medicinal tea for the common cold because they have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties which can help soothe a sore throat and relieve congestion.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds are used in medicinal tea for the common cold as they are a rich source of essential oils, such as citral and geraniol, that have antimicrobial and antiviral properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for the common cold.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger root.
  2. In a saucepan, combine ginger slices with 4 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, then stir in honey and lemon juice to taste.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy the soothing benefits.

4. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains properties that directly address symptoms associated with the common cold.

First, its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce inflammation in the nose and throat, relieving congestion and cough. Second, chamomile's antioxidants boost the immune system, helping the body fight off the cold virus.

Third, chamomile's soothing nature calms the stomach, alleviating symptoms of nausea or digestive upset often accompanying a cold. Fourth, chamomile's drying properties can help reduce excess mucus production.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with the common cold.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help alleviate symptoms of the common cold.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile have calming effects that can help reduce fever and soothe a sore throat.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in antioxidants that help boost the immune system and speed up recovery from a common cold.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for the common cold.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for 1 minute.
  2. Pour the hot water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers.
  3. Steep the chamomile flowers in the hot water for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain out the chamomile flowers, leaving only the tea.
  5. Sip the warm chamomile tea throughout the day for relief of cold symptoms.

  2. Let the echinacea steep for 10-15 minutes to allow the herb to release its medicinal properties.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the echinacea particles for a smooth drink.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for added flavor and soothing properties.
  5. Drink the echinacea tea while warm, up to 3 times a day, to help alleviate symptoms of the common cold.

6. Licorice

Licorice tea contains glycyrrhizin, a compound with proven antiviral properties that directly combats cold-causing viruses.

This tea also soothes throat inflammation, reducing discomfort and making it easier to speak and swallow. Moreover, licorice tea's expectorant qualities loosen and expel mucus, helping to clear airways. Additionally, this herbal tea boosts the immune system, empowering the body to fight off infections.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with the common cold.

  • Roots: Rich in glycyrrhizin, they help soothe a sore throat and reduce inflammation.
  • Rhizomes: High in antioxidants, they boost the immune system and help fight off cold viruses.
  • Leaves: Packed with flavonoids, they provide respiratory support and relieve congestion.
  • Flowers: Contain chalcones, which have antiviral properties, helping to stop the progression of a cold.
  • Seeds: Loaded with coumarins, they act as expectorants, making it easier to expel mucus.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for the common cold.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat, add 1 teaspoon of honey (or to taste) and stir until it's fully dissolved.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the licorice root.
  5. Sip the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day, to help soothe a sore throat and relieve congestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tea for the common cold.

  1. Harvest fresh yarrow flowers and leaves, rinse, and let dry (if not already dried).
  2. Chop or crush 2 tablespoons of dried yarrow material (or 4 tablespoons of fresh) into small pieces.
  3. Steep the yarrow in 1 cup of boiling water, covered, for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove yarrow particles.
  5. Sip the tea while still warm, up to three times a day, for a maximum of five days.

  2. Reduce heat to low, cover and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to absorb the sage's properties.
  3. Remove from heat and strain the liquid to discard the leaves.
  4. Add honey or lemon juice to taste for additional soothing and immune-boosting effects (optional).
  5. Pour the sage tea into a cup and enjoy while warm, up to three times a day.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for the common cold.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in thyme tea that help with the common cold.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and volatile oils, thyme leaves are used to make medicinal tea that helps alleviate cough and respiratory symptoms associated with the common cold.
  • Flowers: High in flavonoids, thyme flowers create a soothing medicinal tea that helps reduce inflammation and congestion in the upper respiratory tract during a common cold.
  • Stems: Packed with essential oils, thyme stems contribute to a medicinal tea known to ease throat irritation and help clear nasal passages during a common cold.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic thyme tea for the common cold.

  1. In a small pot, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, which takes about 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried thyme leaves or 1 tablespoon of fresh thyme to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let the tea simmer for 5-7 minutes to allow the thyme to release its oils.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the thyme leaves and pour it into a cup.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, then let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature before sipping.
