10 Best Herbal Teas For Colds

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients like ginger, echinacea, and eucalyptus, can help alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to their anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and expectorant properties.

The hot liquid of the tea can soothe a sore throat and alleviate congestion by promoting the loosening and expulsion of mucus. Some herbs, like chamomile, have calming effects that can help improve sleep quality, which is crucial for aiding the immune system in fighting off the cold virus. Additionally, many herbal teas are rich in antioxidants, which can support the immune system and reduce inflammation.

Lastly, herbal teas can provide hydration, which is essential for overall health and can help replace fluids lost due to cold symptoms like nasal congestion and sweating.

1. Elderberry

Elderberry tea helps with colds because it contains flavonoids, vitamin C, and antioxidants that strengthen the immune system, making it easier for the body to fight off cold viruses.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties also help reduce symptoms such as sore throat, runny nose, and fever. Elderberries have been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to their high antiviral and immune-boosting properties.

Drinking elderberry tea regularly during cold season can help prevent colds or shorten their duration if you do get sick.

The following procedure explaing how to make elderberry tea for colds.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried elderberries and place them in a heat-resistant cup or teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the elderberries, then let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the elderberries, and discard them.
  4. Add sweetener (such as honey or maple syrup) and lemon juice to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the warm tea throughout the day, up to 3 times daily, for relief of cold symptoms.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with colds because it contains several compounds that offer therapeutic benefits for respiratory issues and immune system support.

These include gingerols, shgaols, and zingibain, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce the pain and swelling associated with a cold. Additionally, ginger tea is rich in antioxidants, such as vitamin C and polyphenols, which can boost the immune system and help fight off the virus causing the cold. Drinking ginger tea also helps soothe a sore throat by coating it with a warming layer of gel, making it easier to swallow and reducing discomfort.

Furthermore, the steam from the hot tea can help clear nasal congestion and provide relief for sinus pressure, making it an effective remedy for both the symptoms and the root cause of a cold.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for colds.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root, then combine with 4 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Bring the ginger and water to a boil over high heat, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove saucepan from heat, and stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey and the juice of half a lemon.
  4. Strain the ginger tea into a mug, discarding the ginger pieces.
  5. Serve immediately, sipping slowly to soothe a sore throat and warm the body during a cold.

3. Echinacea

Echinacea tea helps with colds because it is rich in bioactive compounds that boost the immune system, reducing the severity and duration of cold symptoms.

These compounds include alkamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and flavonoids which enhance white blood cell production, promote phagocytosis, and inhibit the growth of bacteria and viruses. Additionally, echinacea tea contains antioxidants that protect the body from oxidative stress caused by the immune response during a cold, helping to reduce inflammation and fever.

Furthermore, regular consumption of echinacea tea has been shown to improve respiratory function and relieve sore throats, making it an effective natural remedy for alleviating cold symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make echinacea tea for colds.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried echinacea flowers, leaves, and/or roots to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the echinacea and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the echinacea pieces.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy your soothing echinacea tea.

4. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with colds because it contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the throat and nasal passages, providing relief from symptoms such as sore throat, coughing, and congestion.

Additionally, chamomile tea has been shown to boost the immune system, helping the body fight off the cold virus more effectively. The calming effects of chamomile tea can also help soothe restlessness or anxiety caused by discomfort associated with a cold. Furthermore, the warm temperature of the tea can provide temporary relief from a runny nose and chest congestion by promoting mucus drainage and providing hydration to the body.

Overall, drinking chamomile tea regularly when experiencing a cold can help alleviate symptoms, support immune function, and promote overall wellness.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for colds.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for a couple of minutes until it reaches a temperature of around 205 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers or tea bag and let it steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag and optionally add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Sip the chamomile tea while it is still warm, 2-3 times a day, to help alleviate cold symptoms and promote relaxation.

5. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with colds because it contains menthol, an essential oil that soothes the throat and nasal passages.

This cooling effect can help alleviate symptoms such as coughing and congestion. Additionally, peppermint tea has antiviral properties, which may help strengthen the immune system and prevent the common cold. It is also caffeine-free and hydrating, making it a comforting beverage choice for those feeling under the weather.

Sipping on peppermint tea throughout the day can provide relief and promote overall wellness during a cold.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for colds.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1 peppermint tea bag or 1-2 teaspoons of loose peppermint leaves to a teapot or directly into a mug.
  3. Once the water has reached boiling point, pour it over the tea bag or loose peppermint leaves in the teapot or mug.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-7 minutes for optimal extraction of peppermint's beneficial compounds.
  5. Remove the tea bag or strain out the loose peppermint leaves, then enjoy your soothing peppermint tea. Add honey or lemon for added cold-fighting benefits and flavor, if desired.

6. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with colds because it contains several compounds that soothe irritated throat tissues and act as expectorants, making it easier to expel mucus from the respiratory system.

The antioxidants present in licorice root help protect the body against free radicals, which can contribute to the development of cold symptoms. Additionally, licorice root tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation and fever associated with colds. Its soothing effects on the digestive system can also help alleviate stomach discomfort commonly experienced during illness.

Overall, licorice root tea is a natural remedy that provides relief for various symptoms of colds and helps support overall body health.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for colds.

  1. Boil 1 quart of water in a saucepan.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used licorice root.
  5. Sip the tea while warm, up to three times a day, to help soothe a sore throat and alleviate cold symptoms.

7. Ginseng

Ginseng tea helps with colds because it contains compounds that boost the immune system, reduce inflammation, and provide antioxidant protection.

The active ingredients in ginseng, specifically ginsenosides, have been shown to enhance white blood cell production and activity, which are essential for fighting off viruses. Furthermore, ginseng tea's warming properties help soothe sore throats and provide comfort when experiencing cold symptoms.

Lastly, the calming effects of ginseng can alleviate stress, contributing to better overall health during cold season.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginseng tea for colds.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a saucepan and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried ginseng root or 3-4 ginseng tea bags.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to absorb the ginseng's nutrients.
  3. Remove from heat and let it cool for a few minutes, then strain the tea to remove the ginseng pieces or tea bags.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential cold-fighting benefits.
  5. Sip the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day, to help alleviate cold symptoms and boost your immune system.

8. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea helps with colds because it contains various compounds that have antiviral, anti-inflammatory, and immune-boosting properties.

These properties help to soothe sore throats, reduce fever, and alleviate congestion caused by common cold viruses. Additionally, the calming effects of lemon balm tea can help reduce stress and anxiety, which can further support the body's natural response to fighting off illnesses.

Finally, drinking lemon balm tea regularly during cold season or when feeling under the weather can help prevent colds and boost overall immune system function.

The following procedure explaing how to make lemon balm tea for colds.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh lemon balm leaves and 4 cups of water.
  2. Rinse lemon balm leaves under cold water to remove any dirt or impurities.
  3. Coarsely chop the leaves and place them in a heatproof pitcher or teapot.
  4. Bring water to a boil, then pour it over the lemon balm leaves.
  5. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes, then strain and discard the leaves.

9. Thyme

Thyme tea helps with colds because it contains powerful antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, including thymol and carvacrol.

These compounds work to soothe sore throats, reduce coughing, and help fight off bacterial infections that can worsen cold symptoms. Additionally, the warmth of the tea helps to alleviate congestion by opening up nasal passages and promoting hydration, which is essential for maintaining good health during a cold.

Finally, the calming effects of thyme tea can help reduce stress and anxiety, making it an effective natural remedy for managing symptoms and improving overall well-being.

The following procedure explaing how to make thyme tea for colds.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried thyme leaves (or 2-3 sprigs of fresh thyme) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover the saucepan, and let the thyme steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the thyme leaves or sprigs.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy while warm.

10. Sage

Sage tea helps with colds because it contains compounds called rosmarinic acid and beta-thujone, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in the respiratory system.

It also acts as an expectorant, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the lungs and sinuses. Sage tea's warming properties can help soothe a sore throat and relieve congestion, making it an effective natural remedy for managing cold symptoms. Additionally, sage tea has antibacterial properties, which may help fight off any bacterial infections that could be contributing to the cold.

Overall, sage tea offers multiple benefits for those suffering from colds, making it a popular and effective herbal remedy.

The following procedure explaing how to make sage tea for colds.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a saucepan on the stove until it reaches a rolling boil.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of fresh sage leaves (or 1 teaspoon of dried sage) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let the sage steep in the hot water for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid to remove the sage leaves, leaving only the sage tea.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and drink the sage tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.
