10 best herbal teas for sore throat

For a sore throat, consider trying herbal teas that have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

Slippery elm tea is known for its ability to coat and soothe inflamed mucous membranes. Similarly, marshmallow root tea can provide a similar effect.

Licorice root tea is another option, as it can help reduce inflammation and ease pain. For an added immune boost, consider echinacea or goldenseal tea.

As always, before starting any new herbal remedy, it's important to consult with a healthcare provider, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.

1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea brings instant relief to a sore throat due to the tree's inner bark, which contains a substance called mucilage.

This mucilage becomes a slick gel when mixed with water, coating the throat's lining and reducing irritation. Moreover, this soothing gel's anti-inflammatory properties further alleviate the pain by tempering swelling. Slippery elm's demulcent qualities also encourage mucous membrane production, forming a protective barrier against external agents causing discomfort.

Ultimately, slippery elm tea brings swift and effective comfort to a sore throat through its natural, gel-like substance's inherent calming and shielding properties.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with sore throat.

  • Bark: The inner bark of the slippery elm tree is used to make medicinal tea for sore throat. It contains a gel-like substance that coats and soothes the throat.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the slippery elm plant are used to make tea that helps relieve irritation in the throat due to their mucilage content.
  • Buds: Slippery elm buds are a rich source of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, making them useful for soothing a sore throat when brewed as a tea.
  • Seeds: Slippery elm seeds contain mucilage that helps to reduce inflammation and soothe a sore throat when used to make tea.
  • Roots: The roots of the slippery elm plant contain mucilage, which helps to soothe and protect irritated throat tissues, making it useful for medicinal tea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for sore throat.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark.
  2. Place the bark in a tea infuser or empty tea bag.
  3. Steep the infuser in boiling water for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the infuser and let the tea cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat and soothe your throat.

2. Chamomile

Chamomile tea brings immediate relief to a sore throat due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which directly target the swelling in the throat.

This herbal tea contains a powerful antioxidant called apigenin, which is known for reducing inflammation and providing quick pain relief. Moreover, chamomile tea's soothing warmth creates a protective layer over the mucous membranes in the throat, helping to prevent further irritation. Additionally, its mild antibacterial and antifungal properties can help fight off infection, promoting a faster recovery process.

Lastly, chamomile tea's natural muscle relaxant qualities alleviate any discomfort or tension caused by coughing or throat spasms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with sore throat.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers contain inflammatory fightings agents, making them perfect for soothing a sore throat.
  • Leaves: The bitter leaves of chamomile plants provide essential oils and antioxidants, known to alleviate throat irritation.
  • Seeds: Rich in flavonoids, chamomile seeds create a soothing tea that helps reduce sore throat discomfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for sore throat.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it rest for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Put 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers in a tea infuser.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser and stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey for added soothing properties.
  5. Sip the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day for best results.

3. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea provides immediate relief for sore throats due to its demulcent properties, which create a protective film over the mucous membranes.

This film reduces irritation and inflammation, promoting healing. The active compounds in marshmallow root, specifically mucilage, convert into a gel-like substance when combined with water, forming a soothing coating. Furthermore, this tea stimulates the production of saliva, which helps keep the throat lubricated and wash away harmful bacteria.

Marshmallow tea, therefore, offers a natural and efficient remedy for sore throats by forming a protective barrier, reducing inflammation, and boosting the throat's natural defense mechanisms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with sore throat.

  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, the marshmallow flowers help soothe inflammation in the throat.
  • Leaves: Packed with mucilage, marshmallow leaves create a protective coating in the mouth and throat, easing irritation.
  • Bark: Containing anti-inflammatory compounds, the bark of marshmallow plant helps reduce redness and swelling in the throat.
  • Roots: Abundant in healing properties, marshmallow roots soothe sore throats and ease coughing due to their mucilage content.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for sore throat.

  1. In a pot, combine 2 cups of water, 3 chopped marshmallows, and 1 tablespoon of honey; simmer for 10 minutes to let the marshmallows dissolve.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of grated ginger and 1 cinnamon stick to the pot; continue simmering for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a mug, discarding the solids.
  4. Stir in 1 tablespoon of lemon juice and a pinch of salt; adjust the sweetness with more honey if desired.
  5. Sip the soothing tea while it's still warm, allowing the honey, ginger, and cinnamon to ease your sore throat.

  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.

5. Ginger

Ginger tea brings instant relief to a sore throat due to the presence of natural oils, such as gingerols and shogaols, which possess anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds work by inhibiting the production of inflammatory hormones, like prostaglandins, thus reducing swelling and pain in the throat. Moreover, ginger's antibacterial and antiviral qualities help combat throat infections, further contributing to a faster recovery. Additionally, ginger tea soothes the throat by promoting saliva production, keeping the mucous membranes moist and washing away irritants.

Lastly, the warm temperature of ginger tea stimulates blood circulation, enhancing the delivery of immune cells to the affected areas, thereby bolstering the body's defense mechanisms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with sore throat.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are dense and knotty, underground stems that are widely used in teas to alleviate sore throat. They contain natural oils and bioactive compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to soothe irritation and provide relief.
  • Leaves: Ginger leaves are succulent and green, used in teas to treat sore throats due to their rich source of vitamins and minerals. These nutrients boost the immune system and help the body fight off infections causing throat irritation.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers are aromatic and bright, adding a touch of elegance to medicinal teas. They contain potent antiseptic and analgesic properties, making them ideal for treating sore throats and easing pain.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds are small and black, containing concentrated active ingredients that make them useful in soothing sore throats. When steeped in tea, they release oils and compounds that have a calming effect on irritated throats.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for sore throat.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger root.
  2. In a saucepan, combine ginger slices with 4 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Optional: Strain the tea to remove ginger pieces.
  5. Stir in honey and lemon juice to taste, then let it cool slightly before drinking.

8. Peppermint

Peppermint tea brings almost immediate relief to a sore throat due to its active components, menthol and methyl salicylate.

Menthol's cooling property eases the pain by numbing the throat, while methyl salicylate boasts anti-inflammatory qualities that decrease swelling. Furthermore, peppermint tea's warm temperature soothes irritation and encourages coughing, helping to clear mucus. Its natural antibacterial agents combat infection-causing bacteria, promoting healing.

Ultimately, peppermint tea's multifaceted benefits alleviate the discomfort and symptoms associated with a sore throat.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with sore throat.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol, the leaves of the peppermint plant bring a cooling sensation to soothe sore throats.
  • Flowers: Packed with flavonoids, peppermint flowers help reduce inflammation and provide relief from throat irritation.
  • Stems: Containing essential oils, the stems of peppermint plants offer antispasmodic properties, alleviating discomfort from coughs and sore throats.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for sore throat.

  1. In a small pot, boil 8 ounces of water and add 1 heaped teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves.
  2. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 10 minutes to allow the water to absorb the peppermint's properties.
  3. Strain the tea into a mug, discarding the peppermint leaves.
  4. Add honey to taste, stirring until fully dissolved. Use 1-2 teaspoons for added soothing effects.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your throat and providing relief for your sore throat.
