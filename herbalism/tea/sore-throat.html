10 Best Herbal Teas For Sore Throat

Herbal teas can help soothe a sore throat by providing warmth and hydration to the mouth and throat, which can alleviate irritation and reduce inflammation.

Some herbs, such as chamomile and licorice root, have anti-inflammatory properties that can further reduce redness and swelling in the throat. Additionally, some herbal teas contain ingredients with natural analgesic properties, such as honey or ginger, which can help to numb the pain and provide further relief. Furthermore, sipping on warm tea can help to stimulate saliva production, which can help to flush out bacteria and other irritants that may be contributing to the sore throat.

Finally, the soothing and calming effects of drinking herbal tea can help to reduce stress and promote relaxation, which can also be beneficial for overall throat health.

1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with sore throat because it contains mucilage, which coats and soothes the throat tissue.

Mucilage also acts as a demulcent, meaning it reduces inflammation and irritation. The tea's antioxidant properties help fight bacteria and viruses that can cause sore throats. Additionally, slippery elm tea is caffeine-free and naturally sweet, making it a soothing beverage for those suffering from sore throats.

Its warming temperature provides added comfort and relief.

The following procedure explains how to make slippery elm tea for sore throat.

  1. Gather your ingredients: 1-2 tablespoons of dried slippery elm bark, 8 ounces of water, and a sweetener of your choice (optional).
  2. Heat 8 ounces of water until it reaches a boiling point, then remove from heat.
  3. Add 1-2 tablespoons of dried slippery elm bark to the hot water and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Stir the mixture well and strain out the bark pieces using a fine-mesh strainer.
  5. Add sweetener (if desired) and drink the tea while it is still warm, up to 3 times a day as needed.

2. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with sore throat because it has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that can help reduce swelling and pain in the throat.

The tea's soothing warmth can also help to alleviate irritation and discomfort. Chamomile contains antioxidants that can boost the immune system and help the body fight off infections that may be causing the sore throat. Additionally, chamomile has been shown to have antibacterial and antiviral properties, which may help to eliminate the underlying cause of the sore throat.

Finally, drinking chamomile tea can help to keep the throat moist and hydrated, which can provide additional relief and promote healing.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for sore throat.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on high heat.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) to a teapot or mug.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers/tea bag and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added soothing and immune-boosting properties.
  5. Strain the tea (if using loose chamomile flowers) and drink while warm, up to 3 times a day for sore throat relief.

3. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with sore throat because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slippery gel when mixed with water, which coats and soothes the throat, reducing irritation.

This soothing effect can help to reduce the pain and discomfort associated with a sore throat. Additionally, marshmallow root has anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to reduce swelling in the throat and alleviate symptoms.

Furthermore, the tea can increase moisture in the mouth and throat, which can help to reduce dryness and irritation. Marshmallow root tea is also considered generally safe, with few side effects, making it a popular natural remedy for sore throats.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using marshmallow root or any other natural remedy to treat a sore throat, especially if it is severe or persistent.

The following procedure explains how to make marshmallow root tea for sore throat.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the marshmallow root in the teapot or teacup.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the marshmallow root.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove any solid particles.
  5. Drink the marshmallow root tea while warm, up to three times a day, to soothe a sore throat and promote healing.

4. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with sore throat because it contains glycyrrhizic acid, a compound with powerful anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help reduce inflammation, soothe irritation, and loosen mucus in the respiratory tract.

Furthermore, it has natural demulcent effects, forming a protective film on the mucous membranes, which helps to alleviate pain and discomfort. Additionally, licorice root tea can help stimulate the production of healthy mucus, restoring moisture to the throat and easing the symptoms of a sore throat. Its antiviral and antibacterial properties also aid in fighting off infections that can cause or worsen a sore throat.

Lastly, licorice root tea's soothing and healing effects on the throat make it an ideal choice for those seeking a natural remedy to alleviate sore throat symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice root tea for sore throat.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover the saucepan, and let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used licorice root.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your throat, and repeat as needed up to 3 times a day.

5. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with sore throat because it contains anti-inflammatory compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which can help reduce swelling in the throat and provide pain relief.

Additionally, ginger has natural antibacterial properties that can help fight off infections causing the sore throat. The warm temperature of the tea can also help soothe irritation and provide a comforting sensation. Furthermore, ginger tea can stimulate saliva and mucus production, which can help keep the throat lubricated and moist, reducing irritation.

Lastly, ginger tea is a natural alternative to over-the-counter medications, which can have side effects like drowsiness or stomach upset.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for sore throat.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root (about 2-4 tablespoons), then bruise the slices with the back of a spoon to release their oils.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine the ginger slices with 4 cups of water and bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover, and let the ginger simmer for 15-20 minutes to extract its beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in honey (1-2 tablespoons) and fresh lemon juice (1-2 tablespoons) to taste, stirring until the honey dissolves.
  5. Strain the ginger tea through a fine-mesh sieve into mugs, and enjoy while warm. Sip as needed throughout the day for soothing relief.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for sore throat.

  3. Pour the boiling water over the tea bag or loose peppermint leaves in the teapot or mug.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-7 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve optimal flavor.
  5. Remove the tea bag or strain out the loose peppermint leaves, then enjoy the soothing hot peppermint tea, preferably without adding sugar or sweeteners, to help alleviate your sore throat.
