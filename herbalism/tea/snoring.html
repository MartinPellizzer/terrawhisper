10 best herbal teas for snoring

If you're looking for a natural way to help reduce snoring, certain herbal teas might be worth considering.

Chamomile tea is known for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help relax the throat muscles and open up the airways. Peppermint tea is another good option, as it can soothe irritated throats and act as a natural decongestant.

Lastly, ginger tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the throat and nostrils, making it easier to breathe. However, it's important to note that while these herbal teas may help alleviate mild snoring, they are not a cure for obstructive sleep apnea or other serious medical conditions.

If your snoring is severe or accompanied by other symptoms, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains antioxidants that reduce inflammation, promoting muscle relaxation.

Its anti-inflammatory properties soothe the membranes in the nose and throat, easing breathing. Chamomile tea's calming effects on the mind and body promote better sleep, reducing snoring caused by sleep disorders. Furthermore, chamomile tea's muscle relaxant properties alleviate tension in the throat, allowing smoother airflow during sleep and less snoring.

Ultimately, regular consumption of chamomile tea can strengthen the immune system, contributing to overall health and reduced snoring.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with snoring.

  • Flowers: Chamomile tea typically uses flowers due to their calming properties, which can potentially promote better sleep and reduce snoring.
  • Leaves: The leaves are sometimes included in the tea as they contain antioxidants and may have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help alleviate respiratory issues that cause snoring.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds can be used in the tea for their essential oils, which can promote relaxation and soothe the digestive system, addressing one potential cause of snoring.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for snoring.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes until it reaches a rolling boil.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the chamomile flowers from the teapot.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea 30 minutes before bedtime for best results.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea brings immediate relief to the throat and nose, opening up obstructed airways and allowing for easier breathing.

This herbal tea's anti-inflammatory properties reduce swelling in the lining of the throat, while its natural muscle relaxants calm any tension in the nasal muscles. Furthermore, peppermint tea's menthol content soothes the throat, acting as a natural decongestant that shrinks swollen membranes, all of which greatly alleviate snoring.

Ultimately, by drinking peppermint tea regularly, one can significantly decrease their snoring, as well as improve their sleep quality.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with snoring.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol, the leaves help relax the throat muscles and alleviate snoring.
  • Flowers: Containing rosmarinic acid, flowers reduce inflammation and swelling in the nasal passages, easing congestion.
  • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants, seeds support respiratory health and decrease snoring caused by congestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for snoring.

  1. In a cup, add boiling water (1 cup) to dried peppermint leaves (1 teaspoon).
  2. Steep the mixture for 5-10 minutes to allow the peppermint flavor to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink the peppermint tea 30 minutes before sleeping.

3. Valerian

Valerian tea aids in reducing snoring due to its natural sedative properties, which originate from its active compounds that influence the central nervous system.

This tea's ability to promote relaxation and decrease nervous tension facilitates smoother muscle function in the throat, thereby reducing the likelihood of vibrations that cause snoring. Furthermore, valerian tea's potential to alleviate sleep disturbances allows for more restful sleep, lowering the overall frequency and intensity of snoring. It is important to note that, while valerian tea can help with snoring, individual experiences may vary, and consulting with a healthcare professional is always advisable.

Lastly, consistent ingestion of valerian tea, as part of a calming nighttime routine, can foster improved sleep quality and, consequently, diminished snoring.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in valerian tea that help with snoring.

  • Roots: Valerian roots are rich in anti-snoring compounds that help relax throat muscles, reducing snoring.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhizomes of valerian contain medicinal properties that promote deep sleep and reduce snoring caused by sleep disturbances.
  • Leaves: Valerian leaves have calming effects that help alleviate snoring caused by anxiety or stress-related tension.
  • Flowers: Valerian flowers contain essential oils and active compounds that soothe the respiratory system, reducing congestion-related snoring.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tea for snoring.

  1. In a stove-top teapot, add 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root to 1 cup of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root pieces.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea 30 minutes before bedtime to help reduce snoring.

  3. Strain the tea into a teacup or mug, discarding the spearmint leaves.

  4. Stir in honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the eucalyptus tea before bedtime to help alleviate snoring.

  4. Steep the tea for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  5. Strain out the lavender flowers before drinking, if using loose leaves. Enjoy your soothing lavender tea up to 3 times a day.

Sage tea provides relief from snoring due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

This herbal tea, derived from the Salvia officinalis plant, tones the membranes of the mouth, throat, and tongue, reducing the likelihood of vibrations that cause snoring. Sage tea's rosmarinic acid content relaxes muscles, preventing the throat's over-relaxation that often results in snoring. Moreover, sage tea's astringent properties tighten the tissues of the throat, thus minimizing the narrowing of the airways that contributes to snoring.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in sage tea that help with snoring.

  • Roots: The roots of the sage plant contain rosmarinic acid, which is known to reduce inflammation and swelling of airways, alleviating snoring.

  • Seeds: Sage seeds are rich in essential oils and antioxidants, providing anti-inflammatory benefits that can help alleviate snoring.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic sage tea for snoring.

  1. In a stove-top pot, boil 2 cups of water for 5-7 minutes.
  2. Stir in 1 tablespoon of fresh sage leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried sage, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Remove pot from heat, cover and let the tea steep for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain out the sage leaves and optionally add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink this soothing sage tea 30-60 minutes before bedtime for best results.
