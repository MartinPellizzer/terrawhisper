10 Best Herbal Teas For Energy Boost

Herbal teas, often made from plants and spices, can provide an energy boost due to the natural compounds they contain.

For instance, teas made from plants like ginseng and rhodiola have been shown to improve mental and physical performance by reducing fatigue and increasing alertness. Mint and ginger teas can also provide an energy boost by enhancing digestion and blood circulation, which in turn increases the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to the body's cells.

Additionally, many herbal teas contain caffeine, a natural stimulant that can increase alertness and focus. However, it's important to note that the effects of herbal teas can vary depending on the type and quality of the ingredients used, as well as the individual's tolerance and sensitivity to certain compounds.

Finally, while herbal teas can provide a natural and gentle energy boost, they should not be relied on as a substitute for a healthy lifestyle that includes regular exercise, a balanced diet, and adequate sleep.

1. Ginseng

Ginseng tea helps with energy boost because it contains high levels of antioxidants and beneficial compounds, such as ginsenosides, which can help to improve physical performance and endurance.

These compounds work by stimulating the body's cells to produce more energy, while also improving the body's ability to use oxygen. Additionally, ginseng tea has been shown to increase mental clarity and focus, which can help to reduce feelings of fatigue and improve overall energy levels. Furthermore, ginseng tea can help to regulate blood sugar levels, preventing the energy crashes that can occur after eating sugary or processed foods.

Finally, ginseng tea has adaptogenic properties, which means it can help the body to better cope with stress and fatigue, providing a sustained energy boost over time.

The following procedure explains how to make ginseng tea for energy boost.

  1. In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil, then add 2 tablespoons of dried ginseng root and reduce heat to low; let it simmer for about 20 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  2. Strain the tea into a teapot or a jug, discarding the spent ginseng root, and add honey or lemon to taste for added flavor and additional health benefits.
  3. Warm up your favorite teacup by rinsing it with hot water and discarding the rinse water.
  4. Pour the ginseng tea into the pre-warmed teacup, taking care not to burn yourself with the hot liquid.
  5. Sip the ginseng tea slowly, savoring the taste and feeling the invigorating effects of the energy-boosting tea. Enjoy!

2. Yerba Mate

Yerba mate tea helps with energy boost because it contains a natural caffeine source called mateine, which provides a milder and longer-lasting stimulant effect compared to coffee.

Additionally, it has a high antioxidant content, which aids in improving physical performance and endurance, and it contains vitamins and minerals that support the body's metabolic functions, contributing to increased energy levels. Furthermore, yerba mate tea promotes mental clarity and focus, reducing mental fatigue and enhancing alertness, making it an excellent alternative to traditional energy drinks.

Lastly, its unique composition of nutrients and compounds works synergistically to provide a balanced and sustained energy boost without the typical crash associated with other caffeinated beverages.

The following procedure explains how to make yerba mate tea for energy boost.

  1. Boil 1 quart of water and let cool for 5 minutes until it reaches a temperature of 175-185°F (80-85°C), then pour into a gourd or large mug, leaving enough room for the yerba mate and any additional ingredients.
  2. Add 1-2 tablespoons (about 4-8 grams) of loose leaf yerba mate to the gourd or mug, ensuring it is fully submerged in the water.
  3. Insert a bombilla (metal straw with a filter on one end) into the gourd or mug, pushing it gently through the yerba mate until it reaches the bottom.
  4. Pour the hot water over the yerba mate, allowing it to flow through the bombilla and into the gourd or mug, then let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes to extract the desired amount of caffeine and antioxidants.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, refilling the gourd or mug with hot water and allowing it to steep again as needed, for a total of 2-3 steeps or until the flavor of the yerba mate becomes too weak.

3. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea helps with energy boost because it contains compounds called withanolides, which have adaptogenic properties that may help to reduce stress and anxiety, thereby increasing energy levels.

Additionally, ashwagandha has been shown to improve physical performance and endurance, making it a potential natural supplement for fighting fatigue. Furthermore, it may also help to balance hormones and improve thyroid function, both of which can contribute to increased energy. However, it's important to note that the effects of ashwagandha can vary from person to person, and it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

Finally, while ashwagandha tea can be a helpful tool for boosting energy levels, it should be consumed as part of a balanced diet and healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and adequate sleep.

The following procedure explains how to make ashwagandha tea for energy boost.

  1. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1 teaspoon of dried ashwagandha root, reduce heat to a simmer, and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh strainer or cheesecloth, discarding the spent herbs.
  3. Warm 1/2 cup of milk (dairy or non-dairy) and add it to the ashwagandha tea.
  4. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey or maple syrup, as desired, for added sweetness and health benefits.
  5. Drink the ashwagandha tea in the morning or afternoon for an energy boost, up to two cups per day.

4. Rhodiola Rosea

Rhodiola rosea tea helps with energy boost because it contains a unique combination of active ingredients, particularly rosavin and salidroside, which have been shown to enhance physical and mental stamina.

This adaptogenic herb works by increasing the body's resistance to stress, allowing it to adapt and respond more effectively to physical, emotional, and environmental stressors. Rhodiola rosea tea also improves cellular energy metabolism by stimulating the production of ATP, the primary energy currency of the cells, and by activating enzymes involved in energy production pathways. Furthermore, it has been found to optimize oxygen utilization, enhancing endurance and reducing fatigue during prolonged periods of mental and physical exertion.

Lastly, Rhodiola rosea tea supports the function of the adrenal glands, which play a crucial role in the body's stress response, thereby promoting overall energy, vitality, and well-being.

The following procedure explains how to make rhodiola rosea tea for energy boost.

  1. In a teapot, add 1 teaspoon (2g) of dried Rhodiola rosea root to 8 ounces of boiling water, then let it steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve a balanced flavor.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the Rhodiola root, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable drinking experience.
  3. Optional: add a natural sweetener, such as honey or stevia, to taste, if desired for a slightly sweet flavor.
  4. For an added energy boost, squeeze half a lemon (about 1 tablespoon of lemon juice) into the tea, as vitamin C enhances Rhodiola's absorption.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, taking slow sips to fully appreciate its earthy and slightly bitter taste, and feel the invigorating effects of Rhodiola rosea take hold.

5. Guarana

Guarana tea helps with energy boost because it contains guarana, a plant native to the Amazon basin that is well-known for its stimulant properties.

The guarana seed has a higher concentration of caffeine than coffee beans, which can help increase alertness, mental clarity, and physical energy. Additionally, guarana has been found to release its caffeine content more slowly and steadily than other caffeine sources, providing a longer-lasting energy boost without the crash associated with other caffeinated beverages. Furthermore, guarana may also improve physical performance by increasing stamina and endurance, making it a popular supplement among athletes and fitness enthusiasts.

However, it's important to note that excessive consumption of guarana can lead to side effects such as insomnia, anxiety, and rapid heart rate, so it's recommended to consume it in moderation.

The following procedure explains how to make guarana tea for energy boost.

  1. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1 teaspoon of guarana tea leaves or 1 guarana tea bag to the water; reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  2. Remove the tea from heat and let it cool for a few minutes, then strain the tea to remove the leaves (if using loose leaf tea).
  3. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and sweetness.
  4. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of sugar or a sugar substitute, if desired, for added energy.
  5. Serve and enjoy immediately, or refrigerate and serve cold for a refreshing energy boost.

6. Goji Berry

Goji berry tea helps with energy boost because it contains high levels of antioxidants, particularly polysaccharides, which can help fight fatigue and improve stamina.

Additionally, goji berries are a natural source of caffeine, providing a gentle and sustained energy boost without the crash associated with other caffeinated beverages. Furthermore, they contain significant amounts of vitamins and minerals, such as vitamin C, iron, and calcium, which can help support overall health and well-being, contributing to increased energy levels. Goji berries also have adaptogenic properties, which can help the body adapt to stress and promote balance, further enhancing energy and vitality.

Finally, the act of drinking tea itself can be a calming and meditative experience, helping to reduce stress and anxiety, which can sap energy and lead to fatigue.

The following procedure explains how to make goji berry tea for energy boost.

  1. In a tea pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of goji berries, then bring the water to a boil on high heat (3-5 minutes).
  2. Reduce the heat to low, let the goji berries simmer for 10-15 minutes, then add 1 teaspoon of green tea leaves to the pot.
  3. After 2-3 minutes, strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids, and stir in 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey to taste.
  4. For an extra energy boost, add 1/4 teaspoon of spirulina powder and mix well.
  5. Serve immediately, sipping slowly, and enjoy the natural energy and health benefits of goji berry tea.

7. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with energy boost because it contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which can be converted in the body to a substance called dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA).

DHEA is a natural hormone that can help increase energy levels, improve mood, and reduce fatigue. Additionally, licorice root tea has been shown to support adrenal function, which is crucial for maintaining energy levels, particularly during times of physical or mental stress. Furthermore, licorice root tea may help to regulate blood sugar levels, preventing the energy crashes that can occur after eating sugary or processed foods.

Finally, licorice root tea has mild stimulant properties, which can help to increase alertness and mental clarity without the jittery effects of caffeine.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice root tea for energy boost.

  1. To make licorice root tea for an energy boost, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root pieces, then add a small amount of raw honey (if desired) for added sweetness and additional health benefits.
  3. For an extra energy boost, consider adding 1-2 slices of fresh ginger or a pinch of cinnamon to the boiling water before adding the licorice root.
  4. Drink the tea while it's still warm, preferably in the morning or early afternoon, as consuming licorice root in the evening may interfere with your sleep.
  5. Enjoy the soothing and invigorating effects of licorice root tea, which can help increase energy levels, reduce stress, and support overall well-being.

8. Eleuthero

Eleuthero tea helps with energy boost because it contains a natural substance called eleutheroside, which is known to improve physical endurance and reduce fatigue.

This adaptogenic herb has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to enhance stamina and combat stress, thereby increasing energy levels. Eleuthero tea also stimulates the production of ATP, a molecule that provides energy to the cells, and improves blood circulation, ensuring that oxygen and nutrients are efficiently delivered to the body's tissues. Furthermore, eleuthero tea contains antioxidants that help protect cells from damage, reducing oxidative stress and promoting overall health and well-being.

Therefore, drinking eleuthero tea can provide a natural and sustainable energy boost, making it an excellent alternative to caffeine-laden drinks.

The following procedure explains how to make eleuthero tea for energy boost.

  1. To make eleuthero tea for an energy boost, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried eleuthero root and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the eleuthero root pieces.
  3. For added sweetness and flavor, consider adding a natural sweetener like raw honey to taste.
  4. For best results, drink the tea in the morning or early afternoon to avoid interfering with your sleep cycle.
  5. Sip slowly and enjoy the warming, invigorating effects of eleuthero, a powerful adaptogenic herb known to boost energy and stamina.

9. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with energy boost because it contains menthol and mentone, compounds that can promote mental clarity and increase alertness.

Additionally, peppermint tea has a invigorating and refreshing scent that can help stimulate the senses and improve mood, leading to increased energy levels. Furthermore, peppermint tea is caffeine-free, making it a healthier alternative to coffee and energy drinks for sustained energy throughout the day. The hydrating properties of peppermint tea can also help prevent fatigue and improve overall physical performance.

Lastly, peppermint tea has been shown to aid in digestion, which can help the body better absorb nutrients and convert them into energy more efficiently.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for energy boost.

  1. In a small pot, bring 8 ounces of water to a boil, then remove from heat and add 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves or 1 peppermint tea bag, letting it steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and flavor.
  2. While the tea is steeping, grate 1/4 of a small, fresh peppermint leaf into a mug for added freshness and additional energy-boosting properties.
  3. Squeeze half a lemon into the mug for an extra vitamin C boost, which can help increase iron absorption and support the immune system.
  4. Remove the peppermint tea bag or strain out the peppermint leaves from the water, then combine the tea with the grated peppermint and lemon juice in the mug.
  5. Add a small dollop of raw honey (about 1 teaspoon) to the mug, stirring until fully dissolved, and enjoy your invigorating peppermint tea for a natural energy lift.

10. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea helps with energy boost because it contains high levels of antioxidants that aid in increasing energy yield by improving mitochondria efficiency, which is responsible for producing energy in our cells.

Additionally, it dilates blood vessels, improving blood flow and oxygen supply to the brain and other organs, leading to increased alertness and mental clarity. Furthermore, ginkgo biloba has been shown to reduce inflammation, which can help alleviate fatigue and boost energy levels. It also helps regulate neurotransmitters, such as dopamine and serotonin, which can enhance mood and increase motivation, further contributing to its energy-boosting effects.

Lastly, ginkgo biloba tea is a natural and mild stimulant, making it a healthier alternative to caffeine-based energy drinks, which can cause jitters and crashes.

The following procedure explains how to make ginkgo biloba tea for energy boost.

  1. To make ginkgo biloba tea for an energy boost, start by boiling 2 cups of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried ginkgo biloba leaves; allow it to simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the leaves, then add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  3. While the tea is still hot, mix in 1 teaspoon of organic matcha green tea powder for an additional energy boost (optional).
  4. Stir well until the matcha is fully dissolved and enjoy your homemade ginkgo biloba tea as a natural energy booster.
  5. For best results, drink 1-2 cups daily, preferably in the morning or early afternoon, as ginkgo biloba may interfere with sleep if consumed too close to bedtime.
