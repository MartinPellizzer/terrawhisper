10 Best Herbal Teas For Alertness

Herbal teas, particularly those containing ingredients like ginseng, rosemary, and peppermint, can help increase alertness.

These herbs have natural compounds that can stimulate the central nervous system, improving mental clarity and focus. Additionally, many herbal teas contain a moderate amount of caffeine, which can help to increase alertness and decrease feelings of fatigue.

Furthermore, the act of drinking a hot beverage, such as herbal tea, can help to increase blood flow and oxygenation to the brain, further promoting alertness. The soothing and calming effects of herbal teas can also help to reduce stress and anxiety, which can improve cognitive function and increase overall alertness.

Lastly, some herbal teas like ginkgo biloba have been shown to improve circulation and increase blood flow to the brain, which can enhance cognitive abilities and increase alertness.

1. Guayusa

Guayusa tea helps with alertness because it naturally contains more caffeine per cup than coffee, around 95mg.

Additionally, theobromine and theophylline, two other stimulating alkaloids, are also present in the tea. These compounds work synergistically with caffeine to enhance mental clarity and focus without causing jitters or anxiety.

Guayusa is traditionally consumed by indigenous people of the Amazon rainforest during long hunting sessions due to its energizing effects, further highlighting its ability to promote alertness.

The following procedure explaing how to make guayusa tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water for every 1 teaspoon of dried guayusa leaves you plan to use.
  2. Add the desired amount of guayusa leaves to a tea infuser or empty tea bag.
  3. Steep the leaves in the hot water for 5-7 minutes to extract the caffeine and other beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or bag to prevent over-steeping and bitterness.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy your alertness-promoting guayusa tea.

2. Yerba Mate

Yerba mate tea helps with alertness because it contains high levels of caffeine, which is a natural stimulant.

Theobromine and theophylline, two other xanthines like caffeine, are also present in yerba mate tea and contribute to increased energy levels. Additionally, yerba mate tea contains antioxidants that help protect the brain from oxidative stress, which can improve cognitive function and focus. The unique way that yerba mate is consumed – by pouring hot water over a hollow gourd filled with the leaves and sipping it through a metal straw – allows for a steady release of caffeine and other stimulants throughout the day, preventing the jitters or crash often associated with coffee or energy drinks.

Overall, yerba mate tea offers a natural and sustainable energy boost that can help improve alertness and mental clarity.

The following procedure explaing how to make yerba mate tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 1 liter of water and let it cool down until it reaches a temperature of 80-85°C (176-185°F).
  2. In a gourd or large mug, place 3-4 tablespoons (about 30 grams) of yerba mate tea leaves.
  3. Pour a small amount of the hot water (about 1/4 cup) over the tea leaves, allowing them to soften and release their natural bitterness for 1-2 minutes.
  4. After the initial rinse, fill the gourd with the remaining hot water, leaving about 1 cm (0.5 inch) of space at the top.
  5. Insert a filtered bombilla (metal straw) into the gourd, making sure the bottom end is immersed in the tea. Sip the tea while keeping the bombilla in place, allowing it to filter out the tea leaves as you drink. Refill the gourd with hot water and repeat the process up to 5-6 times, or until the flavor weakens. Enjoy your alertness-boosting yerba mate tea!

3. Green Tea

Green tea helps with alertness because it contains caffeine, an essential stimulant that enhances brain function and boosts energy levels.

The amino acid L-theanine, also present in green tea, promotes relaxation without drowsiness, allowing for a clearer mind and improved focus. The synergistic effects of these compounds, combined with the antioxidants found in green tea, help to reduce mental fatigue and increase reaction time.

Consuming green tea regularly can lead to long-term benefits, such as improved memory retention and overall cognitive function.

The following procedure explaing how to make green tea tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool down to 160-180°F (71-82°C) after it reaches a boil.
  2. In a teapot or cup, add 1-2 teaspoons (about 2-4 grams) of high-quality green tea leaves.
  3. Pour the hot water over the tea leaves, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 2-3 minutes for a balanced flavor and optimal alertness benefits.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the leaves, and enjoy your alertness-promoting green tea. Add a small amount of honey or lemon if desired.

4. Gingko Biloba

Gingko biloba tea helps with alertness because it contains compounds called flavonoids and terpenoids, which are antioxidants that can help improve brain function by increasing blood flow to the brain and protecting nerve cells from damage.

Additionally, gingko biloba has been shown to enhance cognitive performance, particularly in tasks requiring attention, memory, and concentration. Studies have demonstrated that consumption of gingko biloba tea can lead to improvements in mental clarity, focus, and alertness, making it a popular choice for individuals seeking natural alternatives to stimulant-based energy drinks or coffee.

Furthermore, the tea's ability to support healthy brain function may also contribute to its potential benefits for overall cognitive health and longevity.

The following procedure explaing how to make gingko biloba tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried Gingko Biloba leaves (or 1-2 Gingko Biloba tea bags) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to a simmer and let the tea steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the leaves or tea bag.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy your Gingko Biloba tea for alertness.

5. Gotu Kola

Gotu kola tea helps with alertness because of its rich content of bioactive compounds, including asiatic acid and madecassic acid, which have been shown to enhance cognitive function and improve focus in studies on animals and humans.

The tea's ability to increase blood flow to the brain also contributes to improved mental clarity, and some research suggests that gotu kola may help reduce stress and anxiety, leading to greater alertness and concentration. Additionally, the calming effects of gotu kola can help counteract the jitters caused by caffeine or other stimulants, making it a popular alternative for those seeking a more balanced energy boost.

Overall, the combination of cognitive-enhancing compounds, increased blood flow, and stress reduction make gotu kola tea an effective beverage for promoting alertness and focus in everyday life.

The following procedure explaing how to make gotu kola tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot and add 2 teaspoons of dried gotu kola leaves.
  2. Reduce heat to a simmer and let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent gotu kola leaves.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Enjoy your alertness-promoting why gotu kola tea!

6. Maca

Maca root tea helps with alertness because it contains various nutrients that stimulate the central nervous system, including high levels of calcium and iron which are essential for brain function.

Additionally, maca root is rich in B vitamins and phenylethylamine, a natural compound known to boost mood and mental energy. The tea also has adaptogenic properties, allowing the body to cope with stress more effectively, leading to improved focus and concentration.

Lastly, maca root tea contains various minerals like iodine, zinc, and magnesium which are important for proper nerve function, contributing to overall brain health and alertness.

The following procedure explaing how to make maca tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on medium heat.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of dried maca root powder once the water reaches a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy your alertness-promoting maca root tea.

7. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea helps with alertness because it contains the adaptogenic compound, withanolides, which assists the body in managing stress and anxiety.

This reduction in mental stress can lead to improved cognitive functions like focus and memory recall, leading to heightened alertness. Additionally, ashwagandha is a natural source of antioxidants that protect the brain from oxidative damage, further contributing to enhanced mental clarity and alertness. Moreover, it has been shown to increase cortisol production in the body, which can help combat feelings of fatigue and improve energy levels, ultimately leading to better concentration and alertness throughout the day.

Regular consumption of ashwagandha tea may even improve sleep quality, allowing for more restorative rest and greater alertness during wakefulness hours.

The following procedure explaing how to make ashwagandha tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of dried ashwagandha root; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the liquid to remove the root and pour it into a cup.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of raw honey and a squeeze of lemon juice for taste (optional).
  4. Mix in 1/4 teaspoon of organic extra-virgin coconut oil to enhance absorption.
  5. Sip slowly and enjoy the benefits of ashwagandha for alertness and mental clarity.

8. Rhodiola Rosea

Rhodiola rosea tea helps with alertness because it contains adaptogens, which are natural substances that help the body manage stress and improve mental performance.

One of the key adaptogens in rhodiola rosea is rosavin, which has been shown to enhance brain function and boost energy levels. The tea also contains salidroside, another adaptogen that improves cognitive abilities and reduces mental fatigue. Additionally, rhodiola rosea tea has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the brain and improve overall brain health.

Finally, drinking rhodiola rosea tea regularly may help protect against age-related cognitive decline, making it an excellent choice for those looking to maintain alertness and mental clarity throughout their day.

The following procedure explaing how to make rhodiola rosea tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool down for a couple of minutes until it reaches a temperature of around 200 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried Rhodiola rosea root (or 1 Rhodiola rosea tea bag) to a teapot or directly into a mug.
  3. Pour the hot water over the Rhodiola rosea and let it steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the Rhodiola rosea root or tea bag and discard.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy your alertness-promoting Rhodiola rosea tea.

9. Holy Basil

Holy basil tea helps with alertness because of its unique properties, including adaptogenic and anxiolytic effects.

Adaptogens help the body respond to stress by regulating cortisol levels, which can contribute to fatigue and brain fog. Holy basil also contains essential oils and phenolic compounds that can boost mental clarity and cognitive function. The tea's calming properties, due to its anxiolytic effects, can help reduce stress and anxiety, allowing for better focus and concentration.

Additionally, holy basil tea has antioxidant properties, which can protect the brain from damage caused by free radicals and inflammation, further enhancing alertness and cognitive performance.

The following procedure explaing how to make holy basil tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down to 200-212°F (93-100°C), then add 1 teaspoon of dried holy basil leaves (Ocimum tenuiflorum) and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  2. In a separate cup, squeeze half of a fresh lemon to extract 1-2 tablespoons of lemon juice.
  3. After steeping, strain the holy basil tea into the cup with lemon juice.
  4. Add 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey, depending on your preferred sweetness, and stir until fully dissolved.
  5. Enjoy your homemade holy basil tea with lemon and honey for alertness.

10. Schisandra Berries

Schisandra berries tea helps with alertness because it contains compounds called lignans, which have been shown to protect the brain from oxidative stress and promote neuronal communication.

These lignans, specifically schisandrins, help to improve cognitive function by increasing blood flow to the brain, allowing for better oxygenation and nutrient delivery to the brain cells. Furthermore, studies have indicated that Schisandra berries tea can also enhance mental focus and attention, making it a popular choice among students and professionals seeking to boost their alertness and productivity.

Finally, because schisandra berries are an adaptogen, they help the body to better cope with stress, reducing fatigue and promoting overall energy levels, allowing for greater sustained mental clarity and alertness throughout the day.

The following procedure explaing how to make schisandra berries tea for alertness.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot and add 1 tablespoon of dried Schisandra berries or 2 tablespoons of Schisandra berry tea bags.
  2. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover the pot, and let it steep for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to 2-3 times a day for alertness.
