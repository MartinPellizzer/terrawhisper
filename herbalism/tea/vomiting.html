10 best herbal teas for vomiting

When dealing with vomiting, certain herbal teas can provide relief and help soothe an upset stomach.

Ginger tea is one of the best options, as it contains natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce nausea and settle the stomach. Peppermint tea is another excellent choice, as it has calming effects on the gastrointestinal tract and can help alleviate symptoms of vomiting and digestive discomfort. Chamomile tea is also a popular choice for easing vomiting, thanks to its anti-spasmodic properties that can help relax the stomach muscles and reduce the likelihood of vomiting.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider or herbalist before consuming any herbal teas, especially during pregnancy or if taking medication, as they can interact with certain conditions or drugs.

1. Ginger

Ginger tea provides direct relief to the stomach lining, quelling irritation and reducing the frequency of vomiting.

Furthermore, ginger tea's natural compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, sway the nervous system away from vomiting signals. Additionally, this soothing beverage replenishes lost fluids while its mild flavor eases nausea, making ginger tea a reliable remedy for vomiting.

Lastly, ginger tea's renowned anti-inflammatory properties alleviate digestive tract inflammation, further discouraging the sensation of vomiting.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with vomiting.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are widely used to make medicinal tea for vomiting due to their proven antiemetic properties.
  • Roots: Ginger roots contain bioactive compounds that alleviate vomiting, making it an effective natural remedy.
  • Seeds: Rich in shogaols, ginger seeds help reduce gastric motility, providing relief from vomiting.
  • Leaves: Ginger leaves have volatile oils which soothe the stomach, thereby helping to control nausea and vomiting.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for vomiting.

  1. Peel and grate 1 inch of ginger, then steep in boiling water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the ginger pieces from the water.
  3. Add 1-2 teaspoons of honey and 1/2 lemon's juice to the ginger water, stir well.
  4. Warm up the mixture if it has cooled down.
  5. Sip the tea slowly until you feel relieved.

Peppermint tea relieves vomiting due to its antispasmodic properties, which relax the gastrointestinal tract's smooth muscles, reducing contractions that trigger nausea and vomiting.

Moreover, peppermint tea's warmth helps speed up digestion, enabling the body to process food and eliminate waste more efficiently, thereby reducing the sensation of nausea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with vomiting.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and other volatile oils, peppermint leaves are known to have antiemetic properties that aid in reducing nausea and promoting digestion.
  • Flowers: The blooms of the peppermint plant contain calming compounds that alleviate sensations of queasiness and vomiting, making them an ideal addition to medicinal teas.
  • Seeds: Peppermint seeds are a powerhouse of therapeutic agents which include anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help settle the stomach and reduce incidents of vomiting.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for vomiting.

  1. In a tea infuser or reusable tea bag, place one peppermint tea bag or 1-2 teaspoons of loose peppermint leaves.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for about 1 minute to ensure it's not boiling hot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the peppermint tea in a cup.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the medicinal properties of the peppermint.
  5. Remove the tea bag or loose leaves and drink the tea in small sips to soothe vomiting.
