10 Best Herbal Teas For Heartburn

Herbal teas, especially those made from chamomile, licorice root, marshmallow root, and slippery elm, can help alleviate heartburn symptoms.

These herbs have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the irritated esophageal lining caused by acid reflux. They can also help reduce gastric acid production, making the stomach environment less acidic.

Additionally, some herbs, like licorice root, create a protective coating on the esophageal and stomach lining, preventing acid from causing further irritation. Furthermore, these teas can stimulate the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, providing a barrier against stomach acid.

Lastly, herbal teas can promote digestion and relaxation, reducing stress-induced heartburn.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with heartburn because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, which can cause heartburn.

It contains antispasmodic properties that help relax the muscles of the digestive tract, preventing acid reflux and easing heartburn symptoms. The tea also has a calming effect on the body, which can help reduce stress and anxiety that can trigger heartburn. Furthermore, chamomile tea has been shown to increase the production of mucus in the stomach, forming a protective barrier against stomach acid.

Lastly, its soothing and gentle nature makes it a safer alternative to over-the-counter heartburn medications, which can have side effects such as stomach ulcers or kidney damage.

2. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with heartburn because it contains a substance called mucilage, which becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water, forming a protective coating on the esophageal lining, preventing stomach acid from causing irritation.

This soothing effect reduces inflammation and pain, making it an effective natural remedy for heartburn. Moreover, marshmallow tea has anti-inflammatory properties that further help in healing the damaged tissue in the esophagus. Additionally, it can increase the mucus secretion in the gastrointestinal tract, providing further protection against stomach acid.

Marshmallow tea is a caffeine-free and gentle herbal tea, making it a safer alternative to antacids, which can have side effects with long-term use.

3. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with heartburn because it contains a substance called mucilage, which becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water.

This slippery substance coats the lining of the esophagus, providing a protective barrier against stomach acid. As a result, slippery elm tea can help reduce the irritation and inflammation caused by acid reflux, providing relief from heartburn symptoms.

Additionally, slippery elm tea can help increase the production of saliva, which can neutralize stomach acid and help wash it down the esophagus. Slippery elm tea is also thought to have anti-inflammatory properties, which may further help soothe and heal the esophageal lining.

Overall, slippery elm tea can provide a natural and effective way to manage heartburn and promote digestive health.

4. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with heartburn because it contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which can increase the mucus lining of the esophagus and stomach, protecting them from acidic contents.

This action helps prevent the backflow of stomach acid into the esophagus, reducing the likelihood of heartburn. Additionally, licorice tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe irritated tissues in the digestive tract, further alleviating discomfort.

It's important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects like high blood pressure and potassium depletion, so it should be consumed in moderation. Deglycyrrhizinated licorice (DGL) tea, which has had the glycyrrhizin removed, is a safer alternative for long-term use.

Lastly, while licorice tea can be beneficial for occasional heartburn, it is not a substitute for medical treatment, especially for chronic or severe heartburn symptoms.

5. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea helps with heartburn because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus caused by acid reflux.

The natural compounds found in cinnamon, such as cinnamaldehyde, can inhibit the growth of bacteria responsible for causing acid production in the stomach, thereby preventing heartburn. Moreover, cinnamon tea has been shown to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, which can help speed up digestion and prevent stomach acid from backing up into the esophagus. Additionally, cinnamon tea can help soothe and calm the stomach muscles, reducing the likelihood of spasms and acid reflux.

Furthermore, the antioxidant properties of cinnamon tea can help reduce the damage caused by free radicals in the digestive system, which can contribute to the development of heartburn and other digestive issues.

6. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with heartburn because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, thereby alleviating the symptoms of heartburn.

Additionally, ginger is known to help speed up gastric emptying, which can prevent the buildup of stomach acid that can cause heartburn. Furthermore, ginger contains compounds called gingerols and shogaols, which can help relax the smooth muscle in the digestive tract, reducing the likelihood of acid reflux. Ginger tea is also a natural remedy for nausea, which can be a common symptom of heartburn, making it a soothing and effective treatment option.

Finally, ginger tea is a caffeine-free alternative to other beverages, such as coffee and soda, which can trigger heartburn in some individuals.

Cloves tea helps with heartburn because it has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can help reduce inflammation and provide pain relief in the esophagus.

The tea's natural flavorings and compounds, including eugenol, can aid in the healing process of the digestive tract lining, thus preventing acid reflux. Moreover, cloves tea can stimulate the production of digestive enzymes and gastric juices, improving digestion and preventing the occurrence of heartburn. 10. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with heartburn because it contains a natural ingredient called silymarin, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach lining, thereby alleviating heartburn symptoms.

Moreover, milk thistle tea can increase mucus production in the stomach, creating a protective barrier against stomach acids and preventing the backflow of acid into the esophagus. Additionally, milk thistle tea has been shown to improve digestion and absorption of nutrients, reducing the production of stomach acids and lessening the likelihood of heartburn. Lastly, milk thistle tea has been used as a natural remedy for various digestive issues, including heartburn, due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular and effective alternative to over-the-counter medications.
