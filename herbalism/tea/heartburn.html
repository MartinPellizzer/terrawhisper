10 Best Herbal Teas For Heartburn

Herbal teas, especially those made from chamomile, licorice root, marshmallow root, and slippery elm, can help alleviate heartburn symptoms.

These herbs have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the irritated esophageal lining caused by acid reflux. They can also help reduce gastric acid production, making the stomach environment less acidic.

Additionally, some herbs, like licorice root, create a protective coating on the esophageal and stomach lining, preventing acid from causing further irritation. Furthermore, these teas can stimulate the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, providing a barrier against stomach acid.

Lastly, herbal teas can promote digestion and relaxation, reducing stress-induced heartburn.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with heartburn because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, which can cause heartburn.

It contains certain compounds that can help balance the levels of stomach acids and promote digestion, reducing the likelihood of acid reflux. The tea also has a calming effect on the stomach muscles, helping to prevent the backflow of stomach acid into the esophagus. Furthermore, chamomile tea is a natural relaxant, which can help alleviate stress and anxiety, common triggers of heartburn.

Lastly, it is caffeine-free, making it a gentle and soothing option for those experiencing heartburn.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for heartburn.

  1. "Pour one cup of boiling water over one chamomile tea bag or one teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers, and let it steep for 5-10 minutes."
  2. "While the tea is steeping, squeeze half a lemon into the cup for added heartburn relief."
  3. "After steeping, remove the tea bag or strain out the flowers."
  4. "Add honey to taste, if desired, for a natural sweetener."
  5. "Drink the tea slowly, up to three times a day, between meals, for best results."

2. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with heartburn because it contains a substance called mucilage, which creates a protective barrier over the lining of the esophagus and stomach, reducing irritation and inflammation.

This soothing effect can help to alleviate the pain and discomfort associated with heartburn. Additionally, marshmallow tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce swelling in the esophagus and stomach, further preventing heartburn. Furthermore, marshmallow tea can help to increase the mucus production in the stomach, which can help to protect the stomach lining from acid and bile, reducing the likelihood of heartburn.

Finally, marshmallow tea has been shown to have a relaxing effect on the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, which can help to prevent the spasms and constrictions that can cause acid to back up into the esophagus, leading to heartburn.

The following procedure explains how to make marshmallow tea for heartburn.

  1. In a small pot, combine 2 cups of water, 2 crushed calcium-rich antacid tablets, and 1 cinnamon stick; bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for 5 minutes to create the marshmallow tea base.
  2. Reduce heat to low, add 4 large marshmallows (preferably homemade or natural) to the pot, and stir until completely melted and integrated into the tea base.
  3. Remove the pot from heat, let it cool down for about 3 minutes, then mix in 1 teaspoon of pure vanilla extract for additional soothing properties.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or serving container, making sure to press on the solids to extract all liquid.
  5. Serve the marshmallow tea warm, optionally garnishing with a cinnamon stick and additional marshmallows for decoration. Enjoy within 24 hours for maximum benefits.

3. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with heartburn because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water, providing a protective barrier to the esophagus and stomach lining.

This barrier can prevent acid from irritating and damaging the digestive tract, reducing the severity of heartburn symptoms. Additionally, slippery elm can increase the production of healthy mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, which further shields the digestive system from acid and other harmful substances.

Slippery elam tea also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, providing relief from heartburn. Furthermore, it is thought to help balance stomach acid levels, either by reducing excessive acid production or by neutralizing acid in the stomach.

Lastly, slippery elm is a natural demulcent, which means it soothes and protects mucous membranes, making it an effective remedy for various gastrointestinal issues, including heartburn.

The following procedure explains how to make slippery elm tea for heartburn.

  1. Fill a cup with 8 ounces of fresh water and bring it to a boil (3-5 minutes).
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark to the boiling water, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Remove the tea from heat, cover, and let it steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove any bark particles, then allow it to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, one spoonful at a time, to soothe heartburn symptoms.

4. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with heartburn because it contains a compound called glycyrrhizic acid, which can increase the mucus lining of the esophagus, stomach, and intestines, thus providing a protective barrier against stomach acid.

This tea can also help to heal the stomach lining and reduce inflammation, which is often a cause of heartburn. Moreover, licorice tea has been shown to increase the mucus secretion in the gastrointestinal tract, further protecting the stomach lining from acid. However, it's important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure, so it's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before consuming it for heartburn relief.

Finally, while licorice tea can be helpful for some people, it's not a substitute for medical treatment, and those experiencing persistent heartburn should seek medical attention.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice tea for heartburn.

  1. In a pot, add 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil (3-4 minutes).
  2. Crush 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root and add it to the boiling water (1 minute).
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the licorice pieces.
  5. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature before consuming.

5. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea helps with heartburn because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus caused by acid reflux.

Additionally, cinnamon is known to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, which can improve digestion and prevent the production of excess stomach acid that can lead to heartburn. Furthermore, cinnamon has antibacterial properties that can help eliminate harmful bacteria in the stomach that can cause acid reflux. The soothing effect of cinnamon tea can also help to coat the esophagus and provide relief from the pain and discomfort of heartburn.

Lastly, cinnamon tea is a natural remedy that is caffeine-free and has no side effects, making it a safe and effective alternative to over-the-counter heartburn medications.

The following procedure explains how to make cinnamon tea for heartburn.

  1. In a small pot, combine 1 cup of water, 1 cinnamon stick (or 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon), and 1 teaspoon of honey, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. After 10 minutes, remove the pot from heat and remove the cinnamon stick (if using).
  3. Optional: Add 1 teaspoon of lemon juice for added flavor and potential heartburn relief.
  4. Let the tea cool for a few minutes, then strain it into a cup.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat and soothe your throat and chest. Enjoy!

6. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with heartburn because it contains properties that can reduce inflammation and soothe the digestive tract.

The ginger root in the tea can help to neutralize stomach acids, preventing them from backing up into the esophagus and causing discomfort. Additionally, ginger has been shown to speed up gastric emptying, which can help to alleviate symptoms of heartburn caused by a slow-moving stomach. Furthermore, ginger tea is a natural remedy that is caffeine-free and has fewer side effects compared to over-the-counter heartburn medications.

However, it is important to note that while ginger tea can help with occasional heartburn, it may not be effective for everyone, and those with chronic heartburn or a related condition such as gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) should consult a healthcare professional for treatment.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for heartburn.

  1. Peel and finely chop 1 inch of fresh ginger root, then steep in 8 cups of boiling water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the ginger-infused water to remove ginger pieces.
  3. Add the juice of half a lemon and 1-2 teaspoons of honey to the ginger water, stirring until fully dissolved.
  4. Allow the mixture to cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Sip the ginger tea slowly, enjoying its soothing and heartburn-relieving benefits.

7. Cloves

Cloves tea helps with heartburn because it has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that can help soothe the inflammation and irritation in the esophagal lining caused by acid reflux.

The main active compound in cloves, eugenol, has been shown to inhibit gastric acid production, which can help prevent heartburn. Additionally, cloves tea can stimulate the production of mucus in the digestive tract, providing a protective barrier against stomach acid. Furthermore, cloves tea has been used traditionally to aid in digestion, which can help prevent the occurrence of heartburn.

However, it is important to note that while cloves tea may help alleviate occasional heartburn, it should not replace any medication prescribed by a healthcare professional for chronic heartburn or GERD.

The following procedure explains how to make cloves tea for heartburn.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of water, 1 teaspoon of dried cloves (or 3-4 whole cloves), and honey (optional, for taste).
  2. Pour water into a small pot and bring to a boil.
  3. Add cloves to boiling water, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove cloves, add honey for taste if desired.
  5. Let it cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature, then slowly sip the tea.

8. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with heartburn because it contains curcumin, a compound with anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus caused by acid reflux.

Additionally, curcumin has been shown to stimulate the production of bile, which aids in digestion and may help prevent the formation of stomach acid. Furthermore, turmeric tea can help strengthen the lower esophageal sphincter, a muscle that acts as a barrier between the stomach and the esophagus, preventing stomach acid from flowing back up into the esophagus. Moreover, turmeric tea has been used in traditional medicine to treat digestive disorders, including heartburn, due to its soothing effects on the digestive system.

However, it is important to note that while turmeric tea may help alleviate symptoms of heartburn, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially for severe or chronic heartburn.

The following procedure explains how to make turmeric tea for heartburn.

  1. In a small pot, combine 2 cups of water, 1 teaspoon of freshly grated turmeric root (or 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric), and a pinch of black pepper (to enhance absorption), and bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, add honey or lemon to taste (optional), and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Discard the turmeric residue.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, it should help with heartburn.

9. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with heartburn because it aids in digestion and may increase gastric juice production, which can help stomach contents empty more quickly, potentially reducing acid reflux.

Additionally, dandelion tea has anti-inflammatory properties that may help soothe inflammation in the esophagus caused by acid reflux. Furthermore, dandelion tea is a natural diuretic, which can help the body eliminate excess acid and alleviate heartburn symptoms. It is also believed that dandelion tea may help balance the stomach's pH levels, creating an environment that is less conducive to heartburn.

However, it is important to note that individual responses to dandelion tea may vary, and it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating new remedies into a health regimen.

The following procedure explains how to make dandelion tea for heartburn.

  1. Gather 2-3 dandelion heads, carefully ensuring they have not been treated with chemicals, and rinse them gently under cold water to remove dirt.
  2. Separate the yellow petals from the green calyxes, as the latter may cause bitterness, and steep the petals in hot water (around 212°F or 100°C) for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the dandelion petals from the water, creating a mildly bitter and earthy dandelion tea.
  4. Optional: Add a touch of honey or lemon to enhance the tea's flavor and soothe the heartburn.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat the throat and esophagus, providing relief for heartburn symptoms.

10. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with heartburn because it contains a powerful antioxidant called silymarin, which supports healthy digestion and can reduce inflammation in the stomach and esophagus.

This antioxidant also helps to repair and strengthen the mucous lining of the stomach, preventing acid from backing up into the esophagus and causing heartburn. Furthermore, milk thistle tea can increase the production of bile in the liver, which aids in the breakdown and digestion of fats, reducing the risk of acid reflux. Additionally, it has been shown to reduce symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), a common cause of chronic heartburn.

However, it is important to note that while milk thistle tea may help alleviate heartburn, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially for severe or persistent heartburn.

The following procedure explains how to make milk thistle tea for heartburn.

  1. Ingredients: Milk thistle seeds (2 teaspoons), honey (1 teaspoon, optional), and water (2 cups).
  2. Lightly crush the milk thistle seeds to release their oils, but do not grind them into a powder.
  3. Combine the crushed seeds and water in a pot, then bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat to a simmer and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea, add honey to taste if desired, and drink it while warm.
