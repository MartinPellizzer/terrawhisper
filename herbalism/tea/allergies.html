10 Best Herbal Teas For Allergies

Herbal teas can help alleviate allergy symptoms by reducing inflammation and supporting the immune system.

Many herbal teas contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can help reduce the production of histamines and alleviate symptoms like congestion and sneezing. For example, chamomile tea is known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, while nettle tea is often recommended for its natural antihistamine effects. Additionally, some herbal teas, like ginger and licorice root, can help soothe a sore throat and alleviate coughing associated with allergies.

However, it's important to note that not all herbal teas are created equal, and some may interact with medications or have potential side effects, so it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating herbal teas into an allergy relief routine.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with allergies because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and irritation in the nasal passages and eyes.

The flavonoids apigenin and quercetin found in chamomile have been shown to inhibit the release of histamines, a chemical responsible for allergic reactions. Chamomile tea also contains antioxidants that help to boost the immune system, making it easier for the body to fight off allergens. Additionally, sipping on warm chamomile tea can provide a soothing effect, helping to calm down irritated airways and alleviate symptoms such as coughing and sneezing.

Overall, drinking chamomile tea during allergy season may help alleviate symptoms and improve overall comfort and well-being.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for allergies.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a saucepan on the stove, allowing it to reach a rolling boil.
  2. In a separate teapot or heat-resistant container, add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) and pour the boiling water over them.
  3. Steep the chamomile in the hot water for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength and flavor.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent chamomile flowers or composting them.
  5. Optional: Add a small amount of raw honey (1/2-1 teaspoon) to sweeten the tea, if desired, and enjoy up to 3 times a day for allergy relief.

2. Elderflower

Elderflower tea helps with allergies because of its natural antihistamine and anti-inflammatory properties.

The flavonoids and terpenes present in the tea work together to reduce inflammation and ease symptoms such as itchy eyes, runny nose, and sneezing. Consumption of elderflower tea has been shown to inhibit the release of histamines in the body, providing relief for allergy sufferers. Furthermore, elderflower tea contains quercetin, a powerful antioxidant that helps to strengthen the immune system and reduce inflammation.

Regular consumption of elderflower tea during allergy season can help alleviate symptoms and improve overall health.

The following procedure explains how to make elderflower tea for allergies.

  1. Gather 1-2 handfuls of fresh elderflowers (around 1 cup) from a reliable source, making sure to avoid any green parts or stems due to potential toxicity.
  2. Rinse the elderflowers gently under cold running water to remove any dirt or insects, then let them air dry on a clean towel.
  3. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot, then remove it from heat once boiling.
  4. Add the dried elderflowers to the hot water, covering the pot and allowing it to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent flowers, and enjoy it warm or at room temperature. You can also add honey or lemon to taste, if desired. Consume 2-3 cups a day for allergy relief.

3. Echinacea

Echinacea tea helps with allergies because it contains several active compounds, such as cichoric acid and echinacoside, which have anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties.

These compounds can help reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and airways, providing relief from allergy symptoms like congestion, sneezing, and coughing. Additionally, echinacea may help strengthen the immune system, making it more effective at fighting off allergens and reducing the severity of allergic reactions.

Furthermore, echinacea has been shown to have antihistamine effects, which can help alleviate symptoms caused by the release of histamine during an allergic response. However, it's important to note that echinacea is not a cure for allergies and its effectiveness may vary depending on the individual and the severity of their symptoms.

As with any supplement, it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting to take echinacea tea for allergies.

The following procedure explains how to make echinacea tea for allergies.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried echinacea flowers, leaves, and/or roots to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Once the water reaches a boil, pour it over the echinacea in the teapot or infuser.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea, then enjoy it while it's still warm, preferably between meals for best results.

4. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with allergies because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and irritation in the nasal passages and airways.

The warming sensation of ginger tea also helps to clear congestion, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, ginger contains antioxidants that help to boost the immune system and fight off allergens. Some studies suggest that ginger may also have anti-histamine effects, further helping to alleviate allergy symptoms.

Drinking ginger tea regularly during allergy season can provide significant relief, making it a popular natural remedy for those suffering from seasonal allergies.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for allergies.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root, then place slices in a pot.
  2. Add 4 cups of water to the pot and bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover, and let simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the ginger to release its medicinal properties.
  4. Remove from heat, then stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey and the juice of half a lemon for added soothing and immune-boosting benefits.
  5. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, and enjoy it while warm, up to 3 times a day as needed for allergy relief.

5. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea helps with allergies because it contains unique compounds, primarily terpene lactones and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce the production of histamine, a chemical that triggers allergic reactions. Additionally, ginkgo biloba tea is known for its ability to increase blood flow, particularly in the brain and lungs, which can aid in the relief of nasal congestion commonly experienced during allergy season. Furthermore, ginkgo biloba tea has antioxidant properties that can help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to inflammation and allergic responses.

Lastly, regular consumption of ginkgo biloba tea may help desensitize the body over time, reducing the severity and frequency of allergy symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make ginkgo biloba tea for allergies.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried ginkgo biloba leaves to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the ginkgo biloba leaves and let them steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the leaves.
  5. Drink the ginkgo biloba tea up to three times a day as needed for allergy relief.

6. Hawthorn Berry

Hawthorn berry tea helps with allergies because it contains flavonoids, antioxidants that act as natural anti-histamines, reducing inflammation and easing symptoms such as itchy eyes, runny nose, and sneezing.

The astringent properties of hawthorn berries help to constrict blood vessels, which can reduce nasal congestion and swelling. Hawthorn tea also has anti-inflammatory effects that can help soothe irritated mucous membranes in the nose and throat. Furthermore, the tea's calming properties can help alleviate anxiety and stress, common triggers for allergic reactions.

Lastly, hawthorn berry tea is caffeine-free and can be consumed regularly without causing negative side effects.

The following procedure explains how to make hawthorn berry tea for allergies.

  1. Measure out 1 teaspoon of dried hawthorn berries and place them in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and pour it over the hawthorn berries in the teapot or teacup.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the hawthorn berries.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the hawthorn berries.
  5. Drink the hawthorn berry tea up to three times a day as needed to help alleviate allergy symptoms.

7. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with allergies because it contains compounds called flavonoids and glycyrrhizin, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce swelling and irritation in the nasal passages and respiratory system.

Additionally, licorice root tea acts as a demulcent, forming a protective coating over the mucous membranes in the throat and nose, providing relief from itchiness and coughing. Licorice root tea also contains antioxidants that help to boost the immune system, making it easier for the body to fight off allergens. Furthermore, licorice root tea has been shown to have anti-histamine properties, helping to alleviate symptoms such as sneezing and watery eyes.

Overall, licorice root tea is a natural and effective remedy for allergy sufferers, providing relief from inflammation, itchiness, and other common allergy symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice root tea for allergies.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root, then let it cool down for a few minutes.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to 2-3 times a day, before or after meals.

8. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with allergies because it possesses potent anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce swelling and irritation in the nasal passages and respiratory system.

Its mucilage content coats and soothes the throat, providing relief from itching and soreness commonly experienced during an allergic reaction. Additionally, marshmallow root tea has antihistamine effects, which can help alleviate symptoms such as runny nose, watery eyes, and sneezing. Furthermore, its calming properties may also contribute to reducing anxiety and stress, common triggers for allergic responses in some individuals.

Lastly, marshmallow root tea is a natural alternative to over-the-counter allergy medications, making it a preferred option for those seeking a more holistic approach to managing their allergies.

The following procedure explains how to make marshmallow root tea for allergies.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the marshmallow root in the teapot or teacup.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the medicinal properties of the marshmallow root.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove any solid particles.
  5. Drink the marshmallow root tea up to 3 times a day, as needed, to help alleviate allergy symptoms.

9. Nettle Leaf

Nettle leaf tea helps with allergies because it contains compounds called histamines and histamine blockers.

These natural components work together to alleviate allergy symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, and watery eyes. By acting both as a histamine and blocking its effects, nettle leaf tea provides quick relief and long-term benefits for those suffering from seasonal or environmental allergies.

Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of nettle leaf tea help reduce swelling and irritation in the nasal passages and sinuses, providing further comfort and easing the discomfort caused by allergic reactions.

The following procedure explains how to make nettle leaf tea for allergies.

  1. Gather 1-2 handfuls of fresh stinging nettle leaves (Urtica dioica), making sure to wear gloves to avoid skin irritation.
  2. Rinse the nettle leaves thoroughly under cold running water to remove any dirt or impurities.
  3. Coarsely chop the rinsed nettle leaves and place them in a heatproof pitcher or teapot.
  4. Pour 4 cups of boiling water over the chopped nettle leaves, then cover and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, then enjoy the warm nettle leaf tea throughout the day to help alleviate allergy symptoms.

10. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with allergies because it contains natural menthol, which is a muscle relaxant and an anti-inflammatory agent.

This means that it can help soothe the respiratory system and reduce inflammation in the nasal passages, making breathing easier for those suffering from seasonal allergies. Additionally, peppermint tea has antioxidant properties, which help protect the body against damage caused by free radicals, reducing inflammation throughout the body. The calming effect of peppermint tea also helps alleviate anxiety and stress, common symptoms of allergy sufferers.

Finally, drinking peppermint tea can help break up mucus and phlegm, making it easier to expel from the body and reducing congestion caused by allergies.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for allergies.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a saucepan on the stove, allowing it to reach a rolling boil.
  2. Crush 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves or use 1 peppermint tea bag and place it in a teapot or mug.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the peppermint leaves or tea bag in the teapot or mug.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer the taste.
  5. Remove the peppermint leaves or tea bag, and let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature before consuming. Enjoy your homemade peppermint tea for allergies!
