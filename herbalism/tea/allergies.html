10 best herbal teas for allergy

For those seeking relief from allergies, certain herbal teas can offer natural relief.

NETTLE LEAF tea is well-known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate allergic reactions. Meanwhile, GREEN TEA contains epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), a compound with antihistamine effects.

Chamomile tea is another popular choice, as it has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that may help soothe irritated membranes and alleviate allergy symptoms. Lastly, LEMON BALM tea has been shown to have antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential ally in the fight against allergies.

However, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedies.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains flavonoids, particularly quercetin, which possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Quercetin stabilizes mast cells, preventing them from releasing histamine and other allergy-inducing chemicals. By reducing histamine levels, chamomile tea alleviates common allergy symptoms such as congestion, runny nose, and itchy eyes. Moreover, chamomile's soothing effects on the digestive system may help counteract allergic reactions that cause stomach discomfort.

Regular consumption of chamomile tea, therefore, reinforces the body's defenses against allergens, promoting overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with allergy.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers are rich in flavonoids and volatile oils, offering anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping alleviate allergy symptoms.
  • Leaves: The delicate, feathery chamomile leaves contain soothing agents and essential oils that help reduce inflammation and histamine response, making them ideal for calming allergic reactions.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are a powerhouse of therapeutic compounds, including anti-allergenic and antispasmodic properties, providing allergy relief by easing symptoms such as itching, sneezing, and congestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for allergy.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle or pot, reaching a temperature of 212°F (100°C) for optimal extraction of chamomile's bioactive compounds.
  2. In a teapot or infuser, place 2 teaspoons (about 2 grams) of dried chamomile flowers, ensuring sufficient surface area for even water distribution and effective flavor release.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers, allowing the tea to steep for 5-7 minutes to obtain the desired strength and medicinal benefits.
  4. Strain the chamomile tea into a cup, discarding the spent flowers, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature to prevent harming delicate oral tissues.
  5. Sip the chamomile tea slowly, savoring its soothing taste and enjoying a potential allergy symptom reduction through its natural anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

2. Elderflower

Elderflower tea combats allergies due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and alleviate stuffiness.

This tea's flavonoids, such as quercetin, possess natural antihistamine properties, helping to minimize the release of histamines responsible for allergy symptoms. Furthermore, elderflower tea strengthens the immune system, enabling the body to better fight off allergens.

Additionally, it soothes itchiness in the throat and eyes, providing quick relief for allergy sufferers.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in elderflower tea that help with allergy.

  • Flowers: The elderflower contains bioflavonoids and antioxidants that help alleviate allergy symptoms by reducing inflammation and strengthening the immune system.
  • Berries: The berries of the elderflower plant contain vitamins that support the immune system and help reduce symptoms of allergies.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the elderflower plant have antihistamine properties that help reduce allergic reactions.
  • Stems: The stems of the elderflower plant contain tannins that have an astringent effect, helping to soothe mucous membranes and alleviate allergy symptoms.
  • Bark: The bark of the elderflower plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory issues, including allergies, due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic elderflower tea for allergy.

  1. Rinse 4-5 fresh elderflower heads to remove any dirt or insects (5 minutes).
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil, then add the rinsed elderflowers (10 minutes).
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the flowers, then let it cool to drinking temperature (10 minutes).
  5. Stir in honey to taste and drink up to 3 cups per day for allergy relief.

3. Echinacea

Echinacea tea contains ingredients known for reducing inflammation, specifically, phenols and caffeic acid.

These compounds inhibit the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause allergic reactions. Moreover, echinacea stimulates the immune system, aiding the body in fighting off foreign substances like allergens. Additionally, it has antioxidant properties that protect the body from cellular damage due to allergic responses.

Furthermore, echinacea tea alleviates allergy symptoms, such as sneezing, itching, and congestion, by blocking histamine production, a chemical involved in allergic reactions.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in echinacea tea that help with allergy.

  • Roots: The roots of the echinacea plant are rich in substances that boost the body's immune system, making them an ideal choice for combating allergies.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the echinacea plant contain flavonoids and essential oils that have anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate allergy symptoms.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the echinacea plant are a natural source of antioxidants that support the immune system and provide relief for allergy sufferers.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the echinacea plant contain high levels of phenols, which have antihistamine properties that can help to reduce allergic reactions.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic echinacea tea for allergy.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried echinacea flowers and/or leaves.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the echinacea pieces.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.

4. Ginger

Ginger tea provides allergy relief due to its natural anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties.

Gingerol, the active component in ginger, inhibits the production of inflammatory compounds, reducing allergy symptoms. Moreover, ginger enhances the absorption of essential nutrients, supporting overall immune function and bolstering the body's defenses against allergens. Furthermore, ginger acts as a natural antihistamine, alleviating symptoms such as congestion, sneezing, and itchy eyes.

Lastly, ginger's soothing warmth relaxes constricted airways, promoting easier breathing and providing additional relief for allergy sufferers.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with allergy.

  • Rhizomes: The underground part of the ginger plant, used for medicinal tea for allergy due to its anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Roots: The subterranean organs of the ginger plant, utilized for making medicinal tea for allergy because of their richness in antioxidants.
  • Leaves: The aerial parts of the ginger plant, used in medicinal tea for allergy due to their ability to inhibit the release of histamine.
  • Flowers: The reproductive part of the ginger plant, used in medicinal tea for allergy because of their antimicrobial and antiviral properties.
  • Seeds: The reproductive organs of the ginger plant, used in medicinal tea for allergy due to their potential to alleviate respiratory symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for allergy.

  1. Peel and slice 1 inch of fresh ginger, then bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan.
  2. Add the ginger to the boiling water, reduce heat to low, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and the juice of half a lemon.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the ginger pieces.
  5. Sip and enjoy while the tea is still warm, up to three times a day for allergy relief.

Ginkgo Biloba tea contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which possess potent antioxidant properties.

Moreover, this herbal tea enhances the production of prostacyclin, a compound that promotes blood flow and reduces inflammation.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginkgo biloba tea that help with allergy.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the Ginkgo biloba plant contain powerful antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and alleviate allergy symptoms.
  • Seeds: Ginkgo biloba seeds are known to have antihistamine properties, which help in relieving symptoms of allergies.
  • Bark: The bark of the Ginkgo biloba tree contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it effective in treating allergies.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes of the Ginkgo biloba plant have been used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory ailments and allergies due to their anti-inflammatory properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginkgo biloba tea for allergy.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling point (100°C) for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo Biloba leaves to a tea infuser.
  3. Pour the hot water over the Ginkgo Biloba leaves and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser to avoid over-steeping and bitter taste.
  5. Enjoy your homemade Ginkgo Biloba tea, preferably 30 minutes before an allergy episode.

  3. Strain the tea to remove solids, and discard used berries.

Furthermore, it promotes expectoration, assisting in the expulsion of phlegm and alleviating congestion. The soothing effect of marshmallow tea on the throat provides relief from itching and irritation caused by allergies.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with allergy.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the marshmallow plant also contain mucilage, which can help reduce inflammation and irritation in the throat and nose, alleviating allergy symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for allergy.

  1. In a pot, combine 2 cups of water, 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root, and 1 teaspoon of honey; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root; discard the solids.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of lemon juice and stir well.
  4. Warm the tea if necessary, then pour into a cup.
  5. Sip slowly, enjoying the soothing effects of this allergy-friendly beverage.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic nettle tea for allergy.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh nettle leaves, ensuring they are free from pesticides and pollen (5-10 minutes).
  2. Rinse the nettle leaves thoroughly under cold running water, then coarsely chop them (2-3 minutes).
  3. Place the chopped nettle leaves in a heatproof pot or teapot and add 4 cups of boiling water (1 minute).
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain out the leaves using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth (5-10 minutes).
  5. Serve the nettle tea hot, optionally adding honey or lemon to taste (instant).

10. Peppermint

Peppermint tea contains rosmarinic acid, a compound with proven anti-inflammatory properties.

This compound works to suppress the release of histamines, chemicals that the immune system produces during an allergic reaction. By drinking peppermint tea, you provide your body with antioxidants that help in soothing inflammation in the respiratory tract and minimizing allergy symptoms. Furthermore, peppermint tea's natural decongestant and expectorant properties help clear nasal passages and reduce phlegm, making breathing easier.

Lastly, peppermint tea's calming effects on the gastrointestinal tract can alleviate allergy-related digestive issues like bloating and nausea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with allergy.

  • Leaves: Peppermint leaves are rich in antihistamine and anti-inflammatory properties, making them effective in providing relief from allergies.
  • Flowers: Peppermint flowers are known for their mild numbing effect, which can help alleviate symptoms of allergies such as itchy throat and runny nose.
  • Stems: The stems of peppermint plants contain rosmarinic acid, a compound with antioxidant and anti-allergic properties, beneficial for managing allergic reactions.
  • Seeds: Peppermint seeds contain essential oils that have been used in traditional medicine to treat allergies.
  • Buds: Unopened flower buds are potent sources of the soothing menthol that helps clear nasal passages and soothe irritated skin caused by allergies.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for allergy.

  1. In a teapot, add boiling water (1 cup) to dried peppermint leaves (1 teaspoon).
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove the solid particles, leaving behind peppermint tea.
  4. Optional: Add a natural sweetener, such as honey (to taste), if desired.
  5. Serve the tea warm, ideally during or right after experiencing allergy symptoms.
