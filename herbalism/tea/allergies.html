10 best herbal teas for allergy

For those seeking relief from allergies, certain herbal teas can offer natural relief.

NETTLE LEAF tea is well-known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate allergic reactions. Meanwhile, GREEN TEA contains epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), a compound with antihistamine effects.

Chamomile tea is another popular choice, as it has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that may help soothe irritated membranes and alleviate allergy symptoms. Lastly, LEMON BALM tea has been shown to have antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential ally in the fight against allergies.

However, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedies.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains flavonoids, particularly quercetin, which possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Quercetin stabilizes mast cells, preventing them from releasing histamine and other allergy-inducing chemicals. By reducing histamine levels, chamomile tea alleviates common allergy symptoms such as congestion, runny nose, and itchy eyes. Moreover, chamomile's soothing effects on the digestive system may help counteract allergic reactions that cause stomach discomfort.

Regular consumption of chamomile tea, therefore, reinforces the body's defenses against allergens, promoting overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with allergy.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers are rich in flavonoids and volatile oils, offering anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping alleviate allergy symptoms.
  • Leaves: The delicate, feathery chamomile leaves contain soothing agents and essential oils that help reduce inflammation and histamine response, making them ideal for calming allergic reactions.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are a powerhouse of therapeutic compounds, including anti-allergenic and antispasmodic properties, providing allergy relief by easing symptoms such as itching, sneezing, and congestion.
