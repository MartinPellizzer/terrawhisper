10 Best Herbal Teas For Hair Growth

Herbal teas can promote hair growth due to the various beneficial compounds found in the herbs used to make them.

For instance, nettle tea is rich in nutrients like silica and sulfur, which can strengthen hair follicles and enhance growth. Green tea, another popular herbal tea, contains high levels of antioxidants that can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the scalp, both of which can hinder hair growth.

Moreover, some herbal teas like rosemary and ginger tea can help improve circulation in the scalp, ensuring that nutrients reach the hair follicles more efficiently. Additionally, some herbal teas have anti-androgenic properties, which can help block the production of DHT, a hormone that can cause hair loss.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can support hair growth, they are not a magic solution and should be used in conjunction with a healthy diet and other hair care practices for optimal results.

1. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with hair growth because it is rich in nutrients and minerals, such as silica and sulfur, which are essential for hair strength and growth.

Additionally, nettle tea can stimulate blood circulation in the scalp, promoting hair growth and reducing hair loss. It also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce scalp inflammation and dandruff, which can hinder hair growth. Furthermore, nettle tea is a natural source of antioxidants, which can help to protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthy hair growth.

Lastly, nettle tea has been shown to inhibit the production of DHT, a hormone that can cause hair loss, making it an effective natural remedy for hair growth.

The following procedure explains how to make nettle tea for hair growth.

  1. Steep 1 cup of dried nettle leaves in 4 cups of boiling water for 30 minutes; allow the mixture to cool.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, reserving the liquid.
  3. Shampoo and rinse your hair; then pour or spray the cooled nettle tea onto your scalp and hair, ensuring even coverage.
  4. Gently massage the tea into your scalp for 5-10 minutes to stimulate blood flow.
  5. Do not rinse out the nettle tea; instead, let it air dry or dry your hair as usual, allowing the nutrients to absorb into your hair and scalp.

2. Horsetail

Horsetail tea helps with hair growth because it is rich in silica and other minerals that can strengthen hair follicles, promote hair growth, and reduce hair loss.

The silica content in horsetail tea can also improve the health of the scalp by enhancing blood circulation, which ensures that more nutrients reach the hair follicles. Additionally, horsetail tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe an irritated scalp and reduce inflammation, which can lead to healthier hair growth. Furthermore, horsetail tea contains antioxidants that can protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to hair thinning and breakage.

Finally, drinking horsetail tea can also improve the overall health of the body, leading to stronger and healthier hair growth, as it supports the immune system, reduces inflammation, and promotes the absorption of essential nutrients.

The following procedure explains how to make horsetail tea for hair growth.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried horsetail herb, simmer for 10 minutes (Horsetail Tea Ingredient);
  2. After 10 minutes, remove from heat and let it steep for another 5 minutes (Steeping Time);
  3. Strain the tea to remove horsetail herb;
  4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature;
  5. Apply the tea to your hair, massaging it into the scalp, and leave it on for 15-20 minutes before rinsing (Application Time).

3. Rosemary

Rosemary tea helps with hair growth because it stimulates blood circulation in the scalp, ensuring that more nutrients reach hair follicles.

It also contains anti-inflammatory compounds that can help reduce inflammation and irritation, promoting a healthy scalp environment for hair to thrive. Additionally, rosemary tea is rich in antioxidants which can help protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals, while its ability to inhibit DHT production may help prevent hair loss.

Furthermore, it has been shown to increase the number of hair follicles and the length of hair in a study, making it a promising natural remedy for those seeking to enhance their hair growth.

The following procedure explains how to make rosemary tea for hair growth.

  1. In a pot, add 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil (3-5 minutes).
  2. Strip 10-15 fresh rosemary leaves from the stem, rinse, and add to the boiling water (immediate).
  3. Reduce heat to low, simmer the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then remove from heat.
  4. Let the tea steep for 30 minutes, then strain out the rosemary leaves.
  5. Allow the rosemary tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature before using it as a hair rinse, after shampooing.

4. Saw Palmetto

Saw palmetto tea helps with hair growth because it contains bioactive compounds, particularly fatty acids and sterols, that can inhibit the activity of 5-alpha-reductase, an enzyme that converts testosterone into dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone that contributes to hair loss.

By reducing DHT production, saw palmetto may help prevent hair follicle miniaturization and subsequent hair thinning. Additionally, it might prolong the anagen (growth) phase of the hair cycle, promoting healthier and stronger hair. Furthermore, saw palmetto has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can protect hair follicles from oxidative stress and inflammation, both of which can negatively impact hair growth.

However, it is essential to note that individual results may vary, and further research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms and extent of saw palmetto's hair-growth-promoting effects.

The following procedure explains how to make saw palmetto tea for hair growth.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling point (approximately 2-3 minutes in a microwave or 8-10 minutes on a stovetop).
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried saw palmetto berries to a tea infuser or teapot and pour the hot water over the berries.
  3. Allow the saw palmetto tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the saw palmetto berries from the tea.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of saw palmetto tea daily, preferably before or after meals, for hair growth benefits.

5. Birch

Birch tea helps with hair growth because it is rich in nutrients like vitamins B and C, potassium, and calcium, which can strengthen hair follicles and promote circulation in the scalp.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce scalp inflammation, which can in turn promote healthy hair growth. Birch tea also contains antioxidants that can help protect hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to hair loss. Additionally, birch tea has been shown to stimulate the production of collagen, a protein that is essential for healthy hair growth, and can help prevent hair thinning and breakage.

Finally, regular use of birch tea as a hair rinse or in a tea-based hair mask can help maintain the hair's natural pH balance, which is important for maintaining healthy, strong hair and preventing hair loss.

The following procedure explains how to make birch tea for hair growth.

  1. Gather 2-3 dried birch leaves, rinse and place in a pot with 2 cups of water, bring to a boil and simmer for 10 minutes (creates a birch tea base).
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of rosemary leaves and 1 tablespoon of nettle leaves to the pot, simmer for an additional 5 minutes (adds herbal compounds that promote hair growth).
  3. Let the mixture cool down, then strain the liquid to remove the leaves (ensures a smooth tea for application).
  4. Massage the birch tea into your scalp and throughout your hair, let it sit for 15-20 minutes (allows the nutrients to absorb into the hair follicles).
  5. Rinse the tea out of your hair with warm water, no need for shampoo (finishes the treatment and leaves your hair feeling rejuvenated).

6. Hibiscus

Hibiscus tea helps with hair growth because it is rich in vitamins and minerals, including vitamin C, which are essential for healthy hair growth.

It can help increase collagen production, improving the strength of the hair shaft and reducing breakage. Additionally, hibiscus tea has been found to potentially improve blood flow to the scalp, promoting hair growth. Its anti-inflammatory properties may also help to soothe a dry, itchy scalp and reduce hair fall.

Furthermore, hibiscus tea can help to naturally condition the hair, leaving it looking shiny and feeling soft.

The following procedure explains how to make hibiscus tea for hair growth.

  1. In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil, then add 1/4 cup of dried hibiscus flowers, reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Allow the mixture to cool, then strain it to remove the flowers, and discard them.
  3. Shampoo and rinse your hair, then pour the cooled hibiscus tea mixture through your hair, massaging it into your scalp.
  4. Leave the tea in your hair for 10 minutes, then rinse it out thoroughly.
  5. Repeat this process once a week for best results.

7. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea helps with hair growth because it contains high levels of antioxidants and flavonoids, which can help to improve blood circulation in the scalp, thereby stimulating hair follicles for growth.

Additionally, ginkgo biloba is known to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, both of which can contribute to hair loss. The tea can also help to strengthen hair by promoting the production of keratin, a key protein that makes up hair strands. Furthermore, ginkgo biloba has been shown to inhibit the effects of 5-alpha-reductase, an enzyme that converts testosterone into DHT, a hormone that can cause hair loss.

Finally, drinking ginkgo biloba tea can help to reduce stress and anxiety, which can both contribute to hair loss, making it a holistic approach to promoting healthy hair growth.

The following procedure explains how to make ginkgo biloba tea for hair growth.

  1. In a pot, combine 4 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried Ginkgo Biloba leaves, and bring to a boil (10 minutes).
  2. Reduce heat, cover and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Allow the tea to cool down, then strain the liquid to remove the leaves.
  4. Before shampooing, massage the Ginkgo Biloba tea into your scalp and let it sit for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Rinse thoroughly with warm water, then proceed with your regular shampoo and conditioner routine.

8. Ginseng

Ginseng tea helps with hair growth because it contains beneficial properties that can enhance the health of the scalp and hair follicles.

First, ginseng is known to improve blood circulation, which helps increase the delivery of nutrients and oxygen to the scalp, promoting hair growth. Additionally, ginseng contains antioxidants that can protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, reducing hair fall and thinning.

Furthermore, ginseng has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe an irritated scalp, creating a healthy environment for hair to grow. Lastly, ginseng tea can help reduce stress and anxiety, which are common factors that can lead to hair loss.

Therefore, drinking ginseng tea can provide a natural and holistic approach to promoting hair growth and maintaining healthy hair.

The following procedure explains how to make ginseng tea for hair growth.

  1. In a pot, combine 4 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried ginseng root; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  2. Allow the mixture to cool, then strain out the ginseng pieces using a cheesecloth or fine mesh strainer.
  3. To the strained ginseng tea, add 1-2 teaspoons of honey (optional) and stir until fully dissolved.
  4. Wet your hair thoroughly and pour the ginseng tea over your scalp and hair, massaging gently for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Rinse your hair with water, then let it air dry or style as usual. Enjoy your ginseng tea hair treatment!

9. Gotu Kola

Gotu kola tea helps with hair growth because it is rich in antioxidants and nutrients that promote healthy hair, such as vitamin C and collagen.

These antioxidants help to protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to hair loss. Additionally, gotu kola tea has been shown to improve circulation and increase blood flow to the scalp, which can stimulate hair growth. The tea also contains amino acids that can help to strengthen hair strands, reducing breakage and promoting longer, healthier hair.

Furthermore, gotu kola tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to soothe a irritated scalp, creating a healthy environment for hair to thrive.

The following procedure explains how to make gotu kola tea for hair growth.

  1. In a pot, add 4 cups of water and bring it to a boil, then add 2 tablespoons of dried gotu kola leaves, reduce heat to low, and simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. After simmering, remove the pot from heat, and let the tea cool down for about 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the gotu kola leaves, then pour the tea into a clean glass bottle or jar.
  4. To use, massage the gotu kola tea onto your scalp and throughout your hair, then let it sit for 30 minutes before rinsing it out with water.
  5. For best results, use the gotu kola tea on your hair 2-3 times a week and continue using it regularly for several months to see an improvement in hair growth.

10. Burdock

Burdock tea helps with hair growth because it contains beneficial compounds such as phytosterols, essential fatty acids, and vitamin A, which can stimulate hair follicles and promote hair growth.

The tea's antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties can also improve blood circulation in the scalp, ensuring that nutrients reach the hair follicles more efficiently. Additionally, burdock tea has been shown to help balance the hormones that can affect hair growth, such as androgens and estrogens. Furthermore, burdock tea's ability to eliminate toxins and promote the elimination of waste products from the body can also contribute to healthier hair growth.

Finally, burdock tea has been used traditionally as a natural remedy for various hair and scalp conditions, including dandruff and eczema, which can also contribute to hair loss and inhibit hair growth.

The following procedure explains how to make burdock tea for hair growth.

  1. Rinse and finely chop 1 cup of fresh burdock root, then simmer in 4 cups of water for 30 minutes.
  2. Allow the mixture to cool, then strain out the solid burdock pieces.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for flavor.
  4. Massage the cooled burdock tea into your scalp and throughout your hair.
  5. Leave the tea in your hair for 15-20 minutes, then rinse out thoroughly.
