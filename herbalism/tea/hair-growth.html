10 best herbal teas for hair growth

Herbal teas, such as nettle, rosemary, and horsetail, are often recommended for hair growth due to their rich nutrient content and therapeutic properties.

Nettle tea is packed with vitamins A, C, and minerals like iron and magnesium, which promote circulation in the scalp and support healthy hair growth. Rosemary tea is known for its ability to stimulate hair follicles and improve cellular generation, resulting in thicker and longer hair. Horsetail tea is a great source of silica, a mineral that strengthens hair, preventing breakage and promoting growth.

Regular consumption of these herbal teas can help improve the health and appearance of your hair, while also providing numerous other health benefits.

1. Nettle

Nettle tea promotes hair growth due to its rich nutrient content, including vitamins A, C, and B vitamins, as well as minerals like magnesium, zinc, and iron.

These elements enhance the health of hair follicles, encouraging growth and reducing hair fall. Nettle tea's high antioxidant levels protect hair from damage caused by free radicals, which can hinder growth and lead to brittleness. Furthermore, nettle tea's anti-inflammatory properties alleviate scalp inflammation, facilitating a nourishing environment for robust hair development.

Lastly, nettle tea stimulates blood circulation in the scalp, ensuring that essential nutrients reach hair follicles, fueling growth and overall hair health.

  • Roots: Known for their healing properties, roots are used in medicinal tea for hair growth to stimulate the scalp and improve blood circulation.

4. Saw Palmetto

Saw palmetto tea promotes hair growth due to its rich supply of fatty acids and sterols, which nourish the scalp and hair follicles.

This tea's potent extracts block the enzyme 5-alpha-reductase, preventing testosterone from converting into DHT, a hormone associated with hair loss. Furthermore, saw palmetto tea's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties encourage cell growth and improve circulation in the scalp, enhancing the development of healthy hair. Regular consumption of this herbal tea or direct application to the scalp through rinses or creams provides essential nutrients for hair nourishment and supports reduced hair fall and thicker hair growth.

Lastly, scientific studies validate saw palmetto tea's effectiveness in the treatment of androgenetic alopecia and other hair-related conditions, ensuring a reliable and natural solution for those striving for healthier hair growth.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in saw palmetto tea that help with hair growth.

  • Berries: These are the seeds of the saw palmetto plant. They contain compounds that block 5-alpha-reductase, an enzyme that converts testosterone to DHT, a hormone that contributes to hair loss.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the saw palmetto plant contain essential fatty acids and plant sterols that help to nourish the scalp and promote hair growth.
  • Rhizomes: These are the underground stems of the saw palmetto plant. They contain high levels of fatty acids and phytonutrients that help to stimulate hair growth and prevent hair loss.
