10 best herbal teas for nasal congestion

When it comes to relieving nasal congestion, certain herbal teas can be very effective.

Peppermint tea, for instance, contains menthol which helps to open up the nasal passages and alleviate congestion. Similarly, eucalyptus tea, with its natural decongestant properties, can also provide relief.

Ginger tea, known for its anti-inflammatory properties, can help reduce swelling in the nasal passages. Lastly, chamomile tea, with its soothing and calming effects, can help reduce inflammation and promote sleep, which is often much needed when suffering from a cold or flu.

Drinking these herbal teas can help manage nasal congestion and provide much-needed relief.

1. Eucalyptus

Eucalyptus tea provides relief from nasal congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, which reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and help expel mucus.

The primary compound responsible, cineole, targets the inflammation that leads to blocked nasal passages. European studies confirm eucalyptus tea's ability to alleviate congestion, demonstrating its efficacy in treating cold and flu symptoms. Furthermore, drinking the tea allows the medically beneficial vapors to enter the respiratory system, offering additional relief.

Ultimately, eucalyptus tea's therapeutic benefits make it a reliable choice for managing nasal congestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in eucalyptus tea that help with nasal congestion.

  • Leaves: The eucalyptus leaves contain eucalyptol, a compound with expectorant properties that help alleviate nasal congestion.
  • Bark: Eucalyptus bark is rich in tannins that have anti-inflammatory effects, providing relief from inflamed nasal passages.
  • Buds: Eucalyptus buds are a potent source of cineole, a compound with decongestant properties that help clear a blocked nose.
  • Flowers: Eucalyptus flowers contain flavonoids that have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, reducing inflammation in the respiratory tract.
  • Stems: Eucalyptus stems are a natural source of menthol, a compound that helps soothe and clear nasal congestion.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids nasal congestion due to its active ingredient, menthol.

This compound possesses strong cooling and soothing properties that directly affect the mucous membranes of the nostrils, providing almost instant relief. Moreover, menthol also boasts decongestant and expectorant abilities, helping to break down thick mucus and easing its expulsion from the respiratory system. Furthermore, peppermint tea's warm temperature increases nasal airflow by causing the tissues lining the nasal cavity to swell slightly less, facilitating unobstructed breathing.

Finally, as an added bonus, peppermint tea's soothing nature calms the body, reducing overall discomfort often associated with nasal congestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with nasal congestion.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol, leaves help to thin mucus and shrink swollen membranes in the nasal passages.
  • Flowers: Contain rosmarincic acid, flowers have anti-inflammatory properties that aid in relieving nasal congestion.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential oils, seeds provide decongestant and expectorant benefits, helping to clear nasal passages.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea brings immediate relief from nasal congestion due to its powerful anti-inflammatory properties.

The main active ingredient in ginger, gingerol, reduces inflammation in the nasal passages by inhibiting the production of nitric oxide. This results in expanded airways and improved breathing. Furthermore, ginger's natural spiciness behaves as a natural decongestant, helping to break up and expel mucus.

Finally, ginger tea soothes inflamed throat tissue, providing further relief from the discomfort of nasal congestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with nasal congestion.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are widely used in medicinal tea due to their potent aromatic compounds, which provide natural relief for nasal congestion.
  • Roots: The knotted and fibrous ginger roots contain bioactive compounds that alleviate inflammation and mucus buildup, making them an excellent choice for easing nasal congestion.
  • Buds: Ginger buds are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents, making them ideal for soothing irritated nasal passages and promoting sinus drainage.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers are a less common but powerful ingredient in medicinal tea for nasal congestion, as they contain essential oils that help reduce inflammation and alleviate stuffiness.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds are packed with active compounds that have expectorant and decongestant properties, providing quick and effective relief for nasal congestion.

4. Turmeric

Turmeric tea supports nasal congestion relief due to its active component, curcumin, which contains anti-inflammatory properties.

This substance inhibits the release of prostaglandins and leukotrienes, chemicals that cause nasal passages to swell. Moreover, curcumin's antioxidant attributes bolster the immune system, battling infections leading to congestion. The warmth of the tea also soothes inflamed tissues, promoting drainage, and relieving the discomfort associated with nasal congestion.

Therefore, turmeric tea's multifaceted actions target inflammation, infections, and offer soothing warmth, making it an effective tool against nasal congestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in turmeric tea that help with nasal congestion.

  • Rhizomes: These horizontal stem sections of the turmeric plant contain curcumin, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate nasal congestion.
  • Roots: The turmeric roots, sharing similarities with rhizomes, have curcumin and provide natural decongestant effects to ease breathing.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, turmeric leaves contribute to reducing inflammation, helping to clear up nasal passages.
  • Flowers: Turmeric flowers contain essential oils with therapeutic properties, providing relief from nasal congestion when used in medicinal tea.

5. Echinacea

Echinacea tea contains powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, derived from its phenolic compounds, which directly address the inflammation and oxidative stress associated with nasal congestion.

This tea's active constituents, including alkamides and caffeic acid, alleviate nasal congestion by reducing inflammation in the respiratory mucosa, thereby enabling the smooth passage of air. Furthermore, echinacea tea's ability to stimulate the immune system enhances the body's response to upper respiratory infections, an underlying cause of nasal congestion, while its antimicrobial properties fight infection and inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria.

Finally, echinacea tea's demulcent properties soothe irritated nasal passages and throat, providing additional relief from the discomfort of nasal congestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in echinacea tea that help with nasal congestion.

  • Roots: The roots of echinacea contain active compounds that help boost the immune system, providing relief to nasal congestion.
  • Aerial parts: above-ground parts like stems, leaves, and flowers are used to make tea for their anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate nasal congestion).
  • Flowers: Echinacea flowers have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, making them useful in reducing symptoms of nasal congestion.

Additionally, chamomile tea's calming effects can promote better sleep, allowing the body to rest and recover from cold or allergy symptoms.

7. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which possess potent anti-inflammatory properties.

Additionally, this herbal tea increases blood circulation, which aids in the opening of constricted airways, thereby alleviating stuffiness.

Lastly, ginkgo biloba tea's ability to decrease the viscosity of mucus allows for improved drainage, further easing nasal congestion.

8. Elderberry

Elderberry tea provides relief from nasal congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

The tea's anthocyanidins, a type of flavonoid, reduce inflammation in the nasal passages, alleviating congestion. Furthermore, its expectorant properties help loosen and expel mucus, making breathing easier. Additionally, elderberry tea's wealth of vitamins, particularly vitamin C, bolsters the immune system, aiding the body in fighting off infections that cause nasal congestion.

Lastly, the soothing warmth of the tea helps to clear nasal passages by triggering a reflexive narrowing of blood vessels, facilitating decongestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in elderberry tea that help with nasal congestion.

  • Flowers: Rich in anthocyanins, the flowers help alleviate inflammation in the nasal passages.
  • Berries: Packed with vitamin C and antioxidants, the berries aid in soothing irritations in the respiratory tract.
  • Leaves: Containing tannins and essential oils, the leaves help reduce swelling in nasal passages.
  • Bark: A source of flavonoids and phenolic acids, the bark aids in relieving congestion symptoms.
  • Rhyzomes: With anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, rhyzomes support respiratory health and ease congestion.

9. Licorice

Licorice tea provides relief from nasal congestion due to its natural expectorant properties.

This tea contains substances like glycyrrhizin, which thins and loosens mucus, promoting its expulsion from the respiratory tract. Moreover, licorice tea's anti-inflammatory properties reduce inflammation in the nasal passages, facilitating easier breathing. Additionally, it soothes irritation in the throat and nasal lining, alleviating discomfort associated with congestion.

Furthermore, licorice tea's antiviral and antibacterial effects help protect the respiratory system from infections that can cause nasal congestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with nasal congestion.

  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of the licorice plant are rich in flavonoids, which have expectorant effects and can help loosen phlegm and mucus, providing relief from a stuffy nose.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the licorice plant contain volatile oils that act as a natural decongestant, helping to open up the nasal passages and promoting easier breathing.

  • Seeds: The seeds of the licorice plant are a rich source of coumarins, which have natural antihistamine properties, making them useful for reducing symptoms of nasal congestion caused by allergies.

10. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the marshmallow plant's roots and leaves, contains mucilage, a substance known for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

This mucilage, when combined with hot water, forms a viscous solution that coats the throat and nasal passages. As a result, this tea helps reduce inflammation, providing immediate relief to swollen tissues and facilitating the draining of congested nasal passages. Moreover, marshmallow tea's hydrating effect thins down the mucus accumulated in the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel.

Ultimately, the tea's demulcent and anti-inflammatory properties ensure a smoother breathing process and alleviate discomfort brought by nasal congestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with nasal congestion.

  • Flowers: The flowers of the marshmallow plant contain mucilage, which helps soothe irritated membranes in the nose and reduce nasal congestion.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the marshmallow plant also contain mucilage, making them an effective ingredient in teas that alleviate nasal congestion.
  • Roots: The roots of the marshmallow plant have demulcent properties, which can help reduce inflammation and relieve nasal congestion.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the marshmallow plant contain a substance called polysaccharides, which can help soothe irritated membranes and reduce nasal congestion.
