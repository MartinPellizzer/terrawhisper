10 Best Herbal Teas For Nasal Congestion

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients like ginger, peppermint, and eucalyptus, can help alleviate nasal congestion due to their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

These herbs can help reduce inflammation in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, the heat from the tea can help to thin and loosen up mucus, making it easier to expel.

The menthol present in peppermint and eucalyptus teas can also help to open up the airways and alleviate congestion. Furthermore, drinking herbal tea can help you stay hydrated, which is essential for maintaining healthy nasal and throat tissues.

However, it is important to note that while herbal teas can provide relief for mild nasal congestion, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment for severe or persistent congestion.

1. Eucalyptus

Eucalyptus tea helps with nasal congestion because of its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds work to reduce inflammation in the nasal passages, allowing for improved airflow and drainage. Additionally, eucalyptus contains menthol, which acts as a decongestant and helps to open up the sinuses, providing immediate relief. The steam from drinking eucalyptus tea also functions as a natural humidifier, moistening dry nasal passages and soothing irritation.

Overall, eucalyptus tea is an effective, all-natural remedy for relieving nasal congestion due to its anti-inflammatory, decongestant, and hydrating properties.

The following procedure explaing how to make eucalyptus tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Boil 8 cups of water in a large pot.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried eucalyptus leaves (or 3-4 fresh eucalyptus leaves) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove eucalyptus leaves, then let it cool down until warm.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day, to help alleviate nasal congestion.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with nasal congestion because it contains menthol, which is a natural decongestant.

When consumed, the steam from the hot tea helps to open up the sinuses and relieve pressure in the nose. Additionally, peppermint tea has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling in the nasal passages. The calming effects of the tea can also help alleviate stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for sinus congestion.

Lastly, peppermint tea's ability to stimulate the production of saliva and increase mucus flow helps to thin out and drain the nasal passages, providing further relief from congestion.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for 2-3 minutes until it reaches a temperature of 160-170°F (71-77°C).
  2. Steep 1 peppermint tea bag or 1-2 teaspoons of dried peppermint leaves in the hot water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Remove the tea bag or strain out the peppermint leaves.
  4. Add 1-2 teaspoons of honey (optional) to the tea for added soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
  5. Sip the peppermint tea slowly, allowing it to warm your throat and chest, and breathe in the steam to help clear your nasal passages. Enjoy 2-3 cups of peppermint tea per day as needed.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with nasal congestion because it contains compounds that fight inflammation and open up airways.

Its active components, such as gingerols and shgaols, act as natural decongestants by thinning mucus and reducing swelling in the sinuses and nasal passages. Additionally, the warmth of the tea can help soothe irritated nasal membranes and provide relief from symptoms like sneezing and coughing.

Furthermore, ginger tea's antioxidant properties can strengthen the immune system, helping to prevent future bouts of congestion caused by viruses or allergens.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root (about 2-4 tablespoons), then bruise the slices with a heavy object or the back of a knife to release their essential oils.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the bruised ginger slices and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Allow the ginger to simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the water has reduced by about half, creating a strong ginger-infused tea.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat, then stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey and the juice of half a lemon (about 1-2 tablespoons) for added soothing and immune-boosting properties.
  5. Strain the ginger tea into a mug, discarding the spent ginger slices, and enjoy while warm for relief of nasal congestion.

4. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with nasal congestion because of its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which reduce inflammation and fight free radicals in the body.

The active ingredient in turmeric, curcumin, is known to open up nasal passages and improve breathing by breaking down mucus and reducing swelling. Drinking turmeric tea regularly can also boost immunity, helping to prevent the root cause of nasal congestion – colds and allergies.

Additionally, the soothing warm liquid offers instant relief for nasal discomfort and congestion, making it a popular natural remedy for those suffering from sinus issues.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper (to enhance turmeric's bioavailability) and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, then stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey for added flavor and anti-inflammatory benefits (optional).
  5. Sip the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day, as needed for nasal congestion relief.

5. Echinacea

Echinacea tea helps with nasal congestion because it contains alkylamides, which are unique compounds found in the plant that have been shown to reduce inflammation and inhibit bacterial growth.

These properties make echinacea an effective natural remedy for relieving nasal congestion caused by both viral infections and allergies. Additionally, the tea's antioxidant content helps to boost the immune system, reducing the duration and severity of colds and other respiratory infections. Furthermore, echinacea tea can help soothe sore throats and ease coughing, providing relief for the entire respiratory system when dealing with nasal congestion.

Lastly, echinacea is generally considered safe and free from side effects, making it a popular choice among those seeking natural remedies for nasal congestion and other common cold symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make echinacea tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried echinacea flowers, leaves, and/or roots to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the echinacea and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and relieve nasal congestion. Enjoy 2-3 cups of echinacea tea per day for best results.

6. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with nasal congestion because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the nasal passages, allowing for easier breathing.

Additionally, its warm temperature can help to soothe inflamed membranes and clear nasal passages by increasing the movement of mucus. Chamomile tea is also known for its antispasmodic properties, which can help to relax the muscles that line the nasal passages, reducing constriction and improving airflow. Furthermore, chamomile contains antioxidants that can help to boost the immune system, which can help the body to fight off infections that may be causing the congestion.

Finally, drinking chamomile tea can promote relaxation and better sleep, which can be beneficial for overall health and well-being, including reducing the severity and duration of nasal congestion.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down until it reaches a comfortable drinking temperature.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag to a cup.
  3. Pour the warm water over the chamomile flowers or tea bag and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the chamomile flowers or tea bag and add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the chamomile tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your throat and relieve nasal congestion. Enjoy 2-3 cups of chamomile tea per day for best results.

7. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea helps with nasal congestion because it contains antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can dilate blood vessels and improve blood flow, which may help reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and alleviate congestion.

Additionally, ginkgo biloba has been shown to have a relaxing effect on smooth muscles, which can help to open up the airways and relieve stuffiness. Furthermore, the tea may also have an expectorant effect, helping to thin and loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. However, it's important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms behind ginkgo biloba's potential benefits for nasal congestion.

As with any natural remedy, it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginkgo biloba tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot and add 2 teaspoons of dried ginkgo biloba leaves.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the leaves.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste (optional) and stir well.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to relieve your nasal congestion. Enjoy 2-3 cups of this tea per day for best results.

8. Elderberry

Elderberry tea helps with nasal congestion because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling in the nasal passages, allowing for easier breathing.

Additionally, elderberry is rich in antioxidants and vitamins, such as vitamin C, which can help boost the immune system and fight off infections that may be causing congestion. The tea's natural decongestant properties can also help to shrink swollen nasal tissues, making it easier to breathe through the nose. Furthermore, elderberry tea can help to soothe a sore throat, which is often associated with nasal congestion.

Finally, drinking warm liquids like elderberry tea can help to thin out mucus, making it easier to expel and alleviating congestion.

The following procedure explaing how to make elderberry tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Gather 2/3 cup of dried elderberries, 4 cups of water, 1-2 tablespoons of freshly grated ginger (or 1 teaspoon of dried ginger), 1 cinnamon stick, and 1-2 tablespoons of honey (optional, for added sweetness).
  2. Combine the water, dried elderberries, ginger, and cinnamon stick in a medium saucepan.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 30-45 minutes. This will allow the berries to release their medicinal properties and create a strong tea.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh strainer into a mug or teapot, pressing on the solids to extract as much liquid as possible. Discard the solids. If using, mix in the honey while the tea is still warm, stirring until fully dissolved.
  5. Sip the warm tea throughout the day, up to 3 times daily, to help alleviate nasal congestion.

9. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with nasal congestion because it contains substances that can help reduce inflammation and mucus production in the respiratory tract.

One of these substances, glycyrrhizin, has been shown to have expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help loosen phlegm and soothe irritation in the throat and nasal passages. Additionally, licorice root tea may help to relax the muscles in the respiratory tract, making it easier to breathe. The tea may also have a mild decongestant effect, helping to narrow swollen blood vessels in the nasal passages and reduce congestion.

However, it's important to note that excessive consumption of licorice root tea can have side effects, so it's best to consult with a healthcare provider before using it as a treatment for nasal congestion.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) to it.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the water to extract the medicinal properties of the licorice root.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used licorice root, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added soothing and antimicrobial effects.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your throat and help alleviate nasal congestion. Enjoy 2-3 cups of licorice root tea per day as needed.

10. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with nasal congestion because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery when wet and can help soothe irritated membranes in the nose and throat, reducing inflammation and congestion.

This mucilage can also help to trap and remove excess mucus, providing relief from a stuffy or runny nose. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of marshmallow root may help to reduce swelling in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe. Marshmallow root tea may also have a mild decongestant effect, helping to further clear blocked nasal passages.

Overall, marshmallow root tea can provide a natural and soothing relief for nasal congestion.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow root tea for nasal congestion.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and pour it over the marshmallow root in the infuser or teapot.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the medicinal properties of the marshmallow root.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove any solid particles.
  5. Drink the marshmallow root tea while warm, up to 3 times a day, to help soothe and relieve symptoms of nasal congestion.
