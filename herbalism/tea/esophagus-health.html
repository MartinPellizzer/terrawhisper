10 best herbal teas for esophagus health

For optimal esophagus health, consider incorporating herbal teas such as chamomile, marshmallow root, and slippery elm into your daily routine.

Chamomile is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which may help soothe and heal esophageal irritations. Marshmallow root and slippery elm contain mucilage, a substance that forms a protective film along the mucous membranes, potentially providing relief from acid reflux symptoms and reducing esophageal inflammation.

Enjoying a few cups of these herbal teas throughout the day can contribute to a healthy esophagus and support overall digestive well-being.

1. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the marshmallow plant's roots, contains a substance called mucilage.

This mucilage turns into a slippery gel when combined with water, providing a soothing coating for the esophagus. This coating helps protect the esophageal lining from irritants, reducing discomfort and promoting healing. Marshmallow tea's demulcent properties alleviate acid reflux and related symptoms by shielding the esophagus from stomach acid.

Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce inflammation in the esophagus, making it an ideal natural remedy for maintaining esophageal health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with esophagus health.

  • Leaves: Rich in mucilage, the leaves protect and soothe irritated esophageal tissues.
  • Flowers: Contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that help reduce inflammation in the esophagus.
  • Stems: High in fiber, stems aid in maintaining a healthy mucosal lining in the esophagus.
  • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants and soothing properties, seeds help alleviate esophageal discomfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for esophagus health.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water, 1 tablespoon of crushed marshmallow root, and 1 tablespoon of sliced licorice root; simmer for 20 minutes to extract herbs' healing properties.
  2. Strain the tea to remove solid particles, then add 1 teaspoon of honey and 1/4 teaspoon of cinnamon powder to the liquid for soothing and anti-inflammatory effects; stir well.
  3. Warm up the tea to a comfortable drinking temperature, but avoid boiling it to preserve its beneficial components.
  4. Pour the marshmallow tea into a cup, then slowly sip and swallow it, allowing it to coat your esophagus for maximum relief.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of marshmallow tea daily for at least a week to alleviate esophagus inflammation and promote overall health.

Slippery elm tea produces a demulcent effect, creating a slick film that coats the esophagus.

This soothing film eases irritation and reduces inflammation in the esophagus. Slippery elm tea's mucilage content is responsible for this film, as it consists of complex polysaccharides that absorb water and swell. By forming this protective barrier, slippery elm tea alleviates the pain and discomfort associated with esophageal issues.

Furthermore, it encourages the healing of damaged esophageal tissues, promoting overall esophagus health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with esophagus health.

  • Bark: The bark of the slippery elm plant contains mucilage, a substance that forms a soothing film when mixed with water, which helps ease irritation and inflammation in the esophagus.
  • Inner Bark: The inner bark is rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that help protect and heal the esophageal lining.
  • Leaves: Slippery elm leaves also contain mucilage, which coats and soothes the esophagus, providing relief from discomfort and pain.
  • Seeds: Slippery elm seeds are a good source of fiber and mucilage, which helps to bulk up stools, relieve constipation, and promote healthy digestion, including esophageal health.
  • Powdered Bark: Dried and powdered slippery elm bark is often used to make medicinal teas for its demulcent and emollient properties, forming a protective film on the esophageal lining, reducing irritation and promoting healing.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for esophagus health.

  1. Measure - Precisely measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark.
  2. Heat - Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool down for a couple of minutes.
  3. Steep - Place the slippery elm bark in a tea infuser and steep it in the hot water for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Stir - Mix the tea well to ensure all the slippery elm particles are well-blended.
  5. Serve - Pour the tea into a cup, and drink it while it's still warm, up to three times a day for esophagus health.

Moreover, plantain tea's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties alleviate irritation and relax esophageal muscles, further benefiting esophagus health. Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in plantain tea that help with esophagus health.

  • Leaves: Rich in iridoid glycosides, which have anti-inflammatory properties, making them suitable for soothing irritations in the esophagus.
  • Seeds: Contain mucilage, which can help protect the mucous membrane of the esophagus, providing a soothing effect.
  • Bark: Has tannins that possess astringent qualities, helping to reduce inflammation and providing relief for esophageal discomfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic plantain tea for esophagus health.

  1. Peel and slice 2 ripe plantains into thin rounds, then rinse in cold water.
  2. Combine plantain slices, 4 cups of water, and 1 cinnamon stick in a pot, bringing to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a heatproof container, discarding solids.
  5. Serve tea warm, optionally adding honey or lemon to taste, and drink up to 3 times daily for esophagus health.
