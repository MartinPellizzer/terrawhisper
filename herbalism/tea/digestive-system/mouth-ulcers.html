10 Best herbal teas for mouth ulcers

The best herbal teas for mouth ulcers are chamomile, calendula, echinacea, licorice, marshmallow, sage, goldenseal, myrrh, clove, and peppermint.

These herbal teas contain powerful anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial properties that help soothe painful mouth ulcers, reduce inflammation, and promote healing. Chamomile, for instance, has been shown to have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects, making it an effective remedy for mouth ulcers.

Calendula is another excellent choice due to its wound-healing and anti-inflammatory properties. Licorice tea can help relieve pain and reduce inflammation, while marshmallow tea contains mucilage that forms a protective layer over the ulcers, providing relief from pain and discomfort. Sage, goldenseal, myrrh, clove, and peppermint teas are also known for their antimicrobial, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help accelerate healing and improve the quality of life of people with mouth ulcers.

With regular use, these herbal teas can help reduce the frequency and severity of mouth ulcers, allowing individuals to enjoy their daily activities without discomfort.

Chamomile tea helps with mouth ulcers because it contains anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that can reduce inflammation and alleviate pain in the affected area.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with potent antimicrobial properties that aids in preventing infection in mouth ulcers.
  • Chamazulene: A compound with strong anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that helps promote wound healing in mouth ulcers.
  • Flavonoids: A group of plant compounds with antioxidant properties that help protect the mucous membranes of the mouth from damage and inflammation.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with mouth ulcers.

  • Flowers: Contain azulene, a compound that has anti-inflammatory properties, helpful in reducing pain and inflammation in mouth ulcers.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that aid in wound healing and alleviating discomfort in mouth ulcers.
  • Rhyzomes: Contain chamazulene, which has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties beneficial for soothing the symptoms of mouth ulcers.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for mouth ulcers.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water, then steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers.
  3. Let the tea cool down to a warm temperature that is still comfortably drinkable.
  4. Rinse your mouth with the chamomile tea, then swish it around your mouth for 30 seconds.
  5. Spit out the tea and repeat as needed up to 3 times a day.

The best way to treat mouth ulcers with herbal tea is by using chamomile or sage tea.

What are the most common causes of mouth ulcers that are treatable with herbal tea?

The primary causes of mouth ulcers that are treatable with herbal tea are nutritional deficiencies, stress, and tissue injury.

Nutritional deficiencies, particularly in vitamin B12, iron, and folic acid, can lead to mouth ulcers. Chronic stress can also trigger mouth ulcers, as it weakens the immune system.

Lastly, tissue injury from dental work, accidental bites, or braces can cause mouth ulcers, and these can be alleviated with herbal tea.
