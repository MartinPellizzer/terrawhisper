1. Chamomile tea

Chamomile tea helps with mouth ulcers because it contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can alleviate pain and hasten the healing process.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid with antimicrobial properties that aids in preventing infection in mouth ulcers.
  • Chamazulene: A potent anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce swelling and irritation in mouth ulcers.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin also has anti-inflammatory effects that help alleviate symptoms of mouth ulcers.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with mouth ulcers.

  • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce pain and inflammation in mouth ulcers.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids that have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help speed up the healing of mouth ulcers.
  • Seeds: Contain oil with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe and heal mouth ulcers.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for mouth ulcers.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a kettle, reaching a temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a teapot or infuser.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers, allowing the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the chamomile flowers from the tea, discarding the solids.
  5. Allow the chamomile tea to cool down to a warm temperature before sipping slowly, avoiding direct contact with mouth ulcers.
