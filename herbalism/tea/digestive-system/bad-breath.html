10 Best herbal teas for bad breath

Rosemary and sage teas have antimicrobial and astringent properties that help reduce inflammation and prevent plaque buildup. Thyme tea contains thymol, a compound with antiseptic and antibacterial properties that help eliminate bad breath-causing bacteria. Cinnamon tea contains cinnamaldehyde, a compound with antimicrobial properties that help eliminate bad breath. Ginger tea contains gingerol, a compound with antibacterial properties that help kill bad breath-causing bacteria. Eucalyptus tea contains eucalyptol, a compound with anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help eliminate bad breath and soothe sore throats.

By incorporating these herbal teas into their daily routine, people with bad breath can enjoy the benefits of improved oral hygiene and fresh breath.

1. Parsley tea

Parsley tea helps with bad breath because it contains chlorophyll, a natural deodorizer that neutralizes odors caused by Volatile Sulfur Compounds (VSCs) produced by bacteria in the mouth.

Moreover, parsley's antibacterial properties inhibit the growth of these odor-causing bacteria, providing long-lasting fresh breath.

Drinking parsley tea also encourages saliva production, which helps wash away food particles and bacteria, further reducing the risk of bad breath.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in parsley tea that aid with bad breath.

  • Chlorophyll: Known for its powerful deodorizing properties, chlorophyll effectively neutralizes bad breath caused by oral bacteria.
  • Essential oil: Containing antibacterial compounds, these oils help reduce the population of odor-causing bacteria in the mouth.
  • Vitamin c: Boosts overall oral health by supporting collagen production and reducing inflammation, which can help prevent gum disease and bad breath.
  • Flavonoids: Possessing antimicrobial abilities, flavonoids inhibit the growth of bacteria responsible for bad breath.
  • Furanocoumarins: These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, reducing potential sources of bad breath related to inflammation and infection.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in parsley tea that help with bad breath.

  • Leaves: Contain chlorophyll and essential oils that have antibacterial properties, helping to eliminate the bacteria that cause bad breath.
  • Seeds: Rich in antioxidants and contain oils that have antimicrobial properties, aiding in reducing bad breath-causing bacteria.
  • Stems: Contain essential oils and tannins that possess astringent properties, helping to control bad breath by inhibiting the growth of bacteria.
  • Flowers: Packed with volatile oils, they have potent antimicrobial properties to fight the bacteria responsible for bad breath.
  • Roots: Have a high concentration of essential oils, specifically apiol and myristicin, which have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects that help neutralize bad breath and maintain oral health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic parsley tea for bad breath.

  1. Chop 1 cup of fresh parsley, discarding stems, and place in a pot.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to the pot and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea, then let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the parsley tea slowly, allowing it to coat your mouth and neutralize bad breath.
