10 Best herbal teas for dry mouth

The best herbal teas for dry mouth are marshmallow, slippery elm, chamomile, licorice, aloe vera, basil, fennel, sage, ginger, and rosemary.

These teas contain primary properties that help alleviate dry mouth by coating and lubricating the mouth and throat, stimulating saliva production, and reducing inflammation. Marshmallow and slippery elm, for instance, contain mucilage that forms a slick gel when mixed with water, providing a soothing effect. Chamomile and licorice have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce irritation in the mouth.

Aloe vera, basil, fennel, and sage contain antimicrobial properties that help maintain oral hygiene, while ginger and rosemary have been shown to stimulate saliva production. By incorporating these herbal teas into their daily routine, people with dry mouth experience improved oral comfort, reduced irritation, and better overall oral health. For example, sipping on chamomile or licorice tea before bedtime helps lubricate the mouth and throat, allowing for a more comfortable night's sleep.

Similarly, drinking fennel or sage tea after meals helps freshen breath and reduce dryness, making it easier to speak and eat.

1. Marshmallow tea

Marshmallow tea helps with dry mouth because it contains mucilage, a sticky substance that comes from the root of the marshmallow plant.

This mucilage coats the mouth and throat, providing a soothing and lubricating effect that helps to alleviate dry mouth symptoms. Additionally, marshmallow tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce irritation and inflammation in the mouth and throat, further helping to ease discomfort caused by dry mouth.

The tea's natural sweetness also stimulates the production of saliva, which can help keep the mouth moisturized and hydrated.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in marshmallow tea that aid with dry mouth.

  • Mucilage: It forms a protective film on mouth membranes, providing relief from dry mouth.
  • Polysaccharides: These compounds help to retain moisture, keeping the mouth hydrated.
  • Flavonoids: They possess anti-inflammatory properties, reducing irritation in dry mouth.
  • Ascorbic acid: It promotes saliva production, alleviating dry mouth symptoms.
  • Phenolic acids: They have a soothing effect, providing comfort to dry mouth sufferers.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in marshmallow tea that help with dry mouth.

  • Leaves: Rich in triterpene saponins, which stimulate saliva production.
  • Flowers: Contain mucilage, which coats and soothes dry oral tissues.
  • Roots: Packed with starch and polysaccharides, providing long-lasting hydration.
  • Stems: Store reserves of water and nutrients, aiding in moisture retention.
  • Bark: High in tannins, possessing astringent properties that alleviate dry mouth symptoms.
  • Buds: Richest in antioxidants, protecting oral tissues from damage caused by dryness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for dry mouth.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let 2 chamomile tea bags steep for 10 minutes.
  2. Stir in 1 tablespoon of organic honey until fully dissolved.
  3. Add 10 large marshmallows to the hot tea and stir until completely melted.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any marshmallow residue.
  5. Serve the marshmallow tea immediately for best results.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for dry mouth.
