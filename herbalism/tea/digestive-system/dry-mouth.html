10 Best herbal teas for dry mouth

The best herbal teas for dry mouth are marshmallow, slippery elm, chamomile, licorice, aloe vera, basil, fennel, sage, ginger, and rosemary.

These teas contain primary properties that help alleviate dry mouth by coating and lubricating the mouth and throat, stimulating saliva production, and reducing inflammation. Marshmallow and slippery elm, for instance, contain mucilage that forms a slick gel when mixed with water, providing a soothing effect. Chamomile and licorice have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce irritation in the mouth.

Aloe vera, basil, fennel, and sage contain antimicrobial properties that help maintain oral hygiene, while ginger and rosemary have been shown to stimulate saliva production. By incorporating these herbal teas into their daily routine, people with dry mouth can experience improved oral comfort, reduced irritation, and better overall oral health. For example, sipping on chamomile or licorice tea before bedtime can help lubricate the mouth and throat, allowing for a more comfortable night's sleep.

Similarly, drinking fennel or sage tea after meals can help freshen breath and reduce dryness, making it easier to speak and eat.

1. Marshmallow tea

Marshmallow tea helps with dry mouth because it contains mucilage, a sticky substance that comes from the plant's roots and leaves.

This mucilage coats the mouth and throat, providing a soothing and moisturizing effect. It also stimulates saliva production, which helps to keep the mouth hydrated and reduce the feeling of dryness. Additionally, marshmallow tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to alleviate any discomfort or irritation in the mouth.

Drinking marshmallow tea regularly can provide relief for those suffering from dry mouth and improve overall oral health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in marshmallow tea that aid with dry mouth.

  • Mucilage: Provides a demulcent effect, forming a protective film over the mucous membranes in the mouth to relieve dryness.
  • Polysaccharides: These complex sugars, especially arabinoxylan, hold significant water-binding capabilities, keeping the mouth hydrated.
  • Flavonoids: Possess anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate irritation and soreness in the mouth caused by dryness.
  • Pectin: Acts as a natural emollient, soothing and moisturizing irritated and dry oral tissues.
  • Ascorbic acid: Promotes saliva production, aids in maintaining a balanced oral environment, and supports overall oral health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in marshmallow tea that help with dry mouth.

  • Leaves: Contain mucilage, a substance that coats and lubricates the mouth, providing relief from dry mouth.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, help soothe inflammation and stimulate saliva production, reducing dry mouth symptoms.
  • Roots: High in medicinal compounds, promote saliva secretion and provide long-lasting hydration for dry mouth.
  • Bark: Contains tannins that have astringent properties, helping to tighten and protect irritated, dry mouth tissues.
  • Stems: Packed with nutrients and mildly astringent, stems can help to alleviate dry mouth discomfort by stimulating saliva flow.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for dry mouth.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water and add 2 dry marshmallows, simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Remove marshmallows with a slotted spoon, let cool and finely chop.
  3. In the same water, steep 1 tea bag of chamomile tea for 5 minutes.
  4. Discard tea bag, add chopped marshmallows back to tea.
  5. Stir well, let cool to drinking temperature, and sip slowly.

What is bad breath and why is important to treat it?

Dry mouth is a condition that occurs when the salivary glands in the mouth do not produce enough saliva to keep the mouth wet.

This lack of saliva leads to a dry, sticky feeling in the mouth and throat, making it difficult to speak, swallow, taste, and chew food. Dry mouth can also cause bad breath and increase the risk of tooth decay, gum disease, and mouth infections. It can impact people's lives significantly by affecting their ability to communicate effectively, enjoy food, and maintain good oral hygiene.

In severe cases, it can even lead to malnutrition and mental health issues such as depression and anxiety.

What other health problems are likely to experience people when they have dry mouth?

When people have dry mouth, they are also likely to experience the health problems listed below.

  • Bad breath: Dry mouth allows bacteria to thrive on the tongue and mouth surfaces, producing unpleasant odors.
  • Dry throat: Insufficient saliva cannot moisten and lubricate the throat, resulting in dryness and discomfort.
  • Thick saliva: Decreased saliva production leads to thicker and stickier saliva, making it harder to swallow.
  • Grooved tongue: Lack of moisture can cause the tongue to become dry and take on a grooved or textured appearance.
  • Cracked lips: Chronic dry mouth can lead to chapped and cracked lips due to a lack of moisture.
  • Difficulty swallowing: With reduced saliva, moving food through the throat and esophagus becomes more challenging.
  • Hoarseness: Dryness in the mouth and throat can affect vocal cords, causing hoarseness or changes in vocal quality.
  • Dry nasal passages: Saliva helps keep the nasal passages moist, and reduced saliva can cause dryness in the nose.
  • Trouble speaking: Insufficient saliva can interfere with the movement of the tongue and mouth muscles, affecting speech.
