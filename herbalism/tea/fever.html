10 Best Herbal Teas For Fever

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients like ginger, peppermint, and chamomile, can help alleviate symptoms of fever due to their anti-inflammatory, diaphoretic, and calming properties.

These herbs can help to reduce inflammation in the body, promoting a decrease in fever. Additionally, diaphoretic herbs, like ginger and peppermint, can increase perspiration, which helps the body to cool down and release toxins.

Chamomile has calming properties that can help a person relax and get much-needed rest while fighting a fever. However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can provide relief and support the immune system, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in severe or persistent fever cases.

Always consult a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and guidance.

1. Yarrow

Yarrow tea helps with fever because it promotes sweating, which is the body's natural way of cooling down and releasing toxins.

The herb contains anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties that help reduce inflammation and strengthen the immune system, thereby helping to fight off infections that can cause fever. Yarrow tea also has a diaphoretic effect, which increases blood circulation to the skin, further aiding in the reduction of fever. Additionally, it is known to stimulate the production of white blood cells, which play a crucial role in defending the body against foreign invaders.

However, it is important to note that while yarrow tea can help alleviate symptoms of fever, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in severe or persistent cases.

The following procedure explaing how to make yarrow tea for fever.

  1. To make yarrow tea for fever, start by boiling 1 cup of water (240 mL) in a pot, then add 2 teaspoons (10 mL) of dried yarrow flowers and leaves.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep, covered, for 10 minutes to help extract the medicinal compounds and essential oils.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the plant material, and discard the used yarrow.
  4. For added flavor and additional health benefits, consider adding a slice of lemon and a teaspoon of honey to your yarrow tea.
  5. Drink the tea while still warm, up to three times a day, as needed to help reduce fever and promote sweating.

2. Elderflower

Elderflower tea helps with fever because it contains several compounds with antipyretic and anti-inflammatory properties, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds work together to help reduce fever by promoting perspiration, which helps the body eliminate toxins and cool down. Additionally, elderflower tea is known to stimulate the immune system, aiding in the body's overall response to infection and fever. The antioxidants present in elderflower tea also help protect cells from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to a faster recovery.

Lastly, elderflower tea has a mild diaphoretic effect, increasing blood circulation to the skin and promoting the elimination of waste products, further supporting the body's efforts to reduce fever.

The following procedure explaing how to make elderflower tea for fever.

  1. To make elderflower tea for fever, begin by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot, then add 2 tablespoons of dried elderflowers or 4-5 fresh elderflower heads; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. After simmering, remove the pot from heat and strain the tea to remove any flower parts or debris.
  3. Optional: For added flavor and additional health benefits, add 1 tablespoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice to the tea.
  4. Stir the honey and lemon juice into the tea until fully combined and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Serve the warm elderflower tea, sipping it slowly throughout the day to help soothe fever symptoms and promote relaxation.

3. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with fever because it contains menthol, a natural organic compound that can help cool down the body by promoting perspiration, which in turn helps reduce fever and cleanses toxins from the body.

Additionally, peppermint tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate the discomfort and pain associated with fever, while its soothing and calming effects can help relax the body and mind, supporting the body's natural healing processes. Furthermore, peppermint tea has been shown to boost the immune system, which can help the body fight off infections and reduce fever symptoms.

However, it's important to note that while peppermint tea can help alleviate fever symptoms, it's not a substitute for medical treatment, and anyone experiencing a high fever or persistent symptoms should seek medical attention.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for fever.

  1. In a tea infuser or empty tea bag, combine 1-2 teaspoons of dried peppermint leaves (adjust to personal taste) and place it in a cup or teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool down for about 2-3 minutes to ensure it's not too hot, as very hot water can destroy the beneficial compounds in peppermint.
  3. Pour the heated water over the peppermint leaves in the teapot or cup and let it steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the release of peppermint's medicinal properties.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag to prevent over-steeping and bitterness.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy this soothing, potentially fever-reducing peppermint tea.

4. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with fever because it contains natural ingredients that promote sweat production, which is the body's way of cooling down and releasing toxins.

The antiviral and antibacterial properties of ginger also help fight off infections that may be causing the fever. Additionally, ginger has anti-inflammatory effects that can help alleviate the symptoms associated with fever, such as headaches and body aches. Furthermore, ginger tea can help rehydrate the body, which is important when fighting off a fever.

Lastly, ginger's warming properties can help improve blood circulation, thereby promoting a faster recovery.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for fever.

  1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, then combine it with 4 cups of water in a saucepan; bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Remove the saucepan from heat, add 2 cinnamon sticks and 2 whole cloves, then cover and let the mixture steep for 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a teapot or a pitcher, discarding the solids; stir in 2 tablespoons of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice.
  4. Warm up 1 cup of the ginger tea, then slowly sip it while still hot, adding more honey or lemon to taste if needed.
  5. Repeat step 4 every 2-3 hours, or as directed by your healthcare provider, to help alleviate fever and soothe symptoms.

5. Holy Basil

Holy basil tea helps with fever because it contains therapeutic properties that work to reduce fever by promoting sweating, which is the body's natural way of cooling down.

The tea's anti-microbial and anti-inflammatory properties also help in fighting off infections that may be causing the fever. Additionally, holy basil tea is known to boost the immune system, which aids in preventing further illnesses. Furthermore, it helps to alleviate symptoms associated with fever, such as headaches and sore throat, by providing a calming and soothing effect.

Lastly, holy basil tea has been used in traditional medicine for centuries as a natural remedy for various ailments, including fever, making it a popular and trusted choice for many people.

The following procedure explaing how to make holy basil tea for fever.

  1. In a pot, add 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil, then add 1 teaspoon of dried holy basil leaves or 2.5 teaspoons of fresh holy basil leaves and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let the tea steep for another 5 minutes, then strain the tea to remove the leaves.
  3. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste for added flavor and potential additional health benefits.
  4. Drink the tea while still warm, up to three times a day, as needed to help reduce fever and soothe symptoms.
  5. Discard any remaining tea that has not been consumed within 24 hours.

6. Clove

Clove tea helps with fever because it has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can help reduce inflammation and pain associated with fever.

The main active compound in cloves, eugenol, is thought to be responsible for these effects. Additionally, clove tea can help stimulate sweating, which can help cool the body and reduce fever. Furthermore, cloves have been used traditionally for their antimicrobial properties, which may help fight off infections that could be causing the fever.

However, it's important to note that while clove tea can be a helpful addition to fever treatment, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in severe cases.

The following procedure explaing how to make clove tea for fever.

  1. To make clove tea for fever, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 3-5 whole cloves (or 1/2 teaspoon of ground cloves) to the water; allow the mixture to simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the clove-infused water to remove the solid clove particles and retain the liquid tea.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for improved flavor and additional health benefits.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature, ensuring it's not too hot to consume.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of this clove tea daily, while rest and other self-care measures are also essential for fever management.

7. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea helps with fever because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation and swelling in the body, contributing to fever reduction.

Additionally, cinnamon has antimicrobial properties that can help fight off infections, which are a common cause of fevers. The warm temperature of the tea can also help promote sweating, which can help cool down the body and reduce fever. Furthermore, cinnamon tea is rich in antioxidants that can help boost the immune system and aid in the body's natural defense against illness.

Lastly, cinnamon tea can provide hydration, which is essential for feverish individuals as fever can lead to dehydration, worsening the condition.

The following procedure explaing how to make cinnamon tea for fever.

  1. In a pot, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1 cinnamon stick or 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Remove the pot from heat and optionally add 1 teaspoon of honey or lemon juice for added flavor and vitamin C.
  3. Stir in 1 teaspoon of freshly grated ginger or 1/2 teaspoon of ground ginger for its additional fever-reducing properties.
  4. Cover the pot and let the tea steep for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids, and enjoy your homemade cinnamon tea for fever.

8. Lemongrass

Lemongrass tea helps with fever because it contains anti-pyretic properties that work to reduce fever by bringing down the body's elevated temperature.

Additionally, it has anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects that help alleviate discomfort and pain associated with fever. Furthermore, lemongrass tea is rich in vitamins and minerals, such as vitamin C and magnesium, which support the immune system and promote faster recovery. Its diaphoretic properties also help stimulate perspiration, which aids in eliminating toxins and excess heat from the body.

Lastly, lemongrass tea's calming and soothing effects can help promote rest and relaxation, which are crucial for a speedy recovery.

The following procedure explaing how to make lemongrass tea for fever.

  1. To make lemongrass tea for fever, start by bringing 4 cups of water to a boil, then add 3 stalks of fresh lemongrass (bruised and cut into 2-inch pieces) and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. After simmering, remove the pot from heat and optionally add 1-2 tablespoons of honey and a squeeze of lime juice for added flavor and vitamin C.
  3. Stir the honey and lime juice into the tea until fully dissolved.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or directly into teacups to remove the lemongrass pieces.
  5. Serve the lemongrass tea while warm, and enjoy its soothing and potentially fever-reducing properties. Drink 2-3 times a day for best results.

10. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with fever because it contains several chemical components, such as bisabolol and matricin, that possess anti-inflammatory and antiphlogistic properties.

These compounds help in reducing inflammation and swelling, which in turn, assist the body in fighting off infections that may be causing the fever. Moreover, chamomile tea is known for its mild sedative effects, which can help alleviate the discomfort and restlessness often associated with fevers. Additionally, chamomile has been found to promote perspiration, which can help cool the body and reduce feverish symptoms.

However, it is essential to note that chamomile tea should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in severe fever cases, and it is always best to consult a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and guidance.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for fever.

  1. Begin by boiling 8 ounces of water and, while waiting, measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag.
  2. Once the water has reached a boil, pour it over the chamomile flowers or tea bag in a heat-resistant container or mug.
  3. Allow the chamomile to steep in the hot water for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer your tea.
  4. Remove the chamomile flowers or tea bag, and sweeten the tea with honey to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day, to help alleviate fever and promote relaxation.
