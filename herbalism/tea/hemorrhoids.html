10 best herbal teas for hemorrhoids

Hemorrhoids can be uncomfortable and painful, but certain herbal teas may provide relief.

Chamomile tea is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and discomfort in the affected area. Peppermint tea is another option, as it can help soothe the digestive system and alleviate pain. Additionally, research suggests that butcher's broom, an herb commonly found in tea form, may help strengthen blood vessel walls and reduce symptoms of hemorrhoids.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment regimen, especially if you are taking medication or have a medical condition.

1. Witch Hazel

Witch hazel tea treats hemorrhoids due to its potent anti-inflammatory properties, derived from tannins and gallic acid.

These compounds act as astringents, constricting blood vessels and reducing swelling. The tea's flavonoids bolster venous walls, enhancing their resilience. Furthermore, witch hazel's antioxidants promote healing by combating free radicals around the affected area.

Ultimately, witch hazel tea alleviates hemorrhoid-related discomfort through these combined mechanisms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in witch hazel tea that help with hemorrhoids.

  • Bark: The bark of the witch hazel plant contains tannins and oils that have astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe hemorrhoids.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the witch hazel plant are rich in antioxidants and possess anti-inflammatory qualities, providing relief from pain and itching associated with hemorrhoids.
  • Twigs and Buds: Twigs and buds of the witch hazel plant are a potent source of volatile oils and polyphenols, which exhibit astringent and healing effects beneficial for hemorrhoids.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the witch hazel plant contain flavonoids and other beneficial compounds, contributing to the plant's overall soothing and anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate hemorrhoid symptoms.

The tea's antispasmodic qualities, resulting from its bisabolol content, alleviate pain by relaxing muscles in and around hemorrhoids. Chamomile tea's tannins act as astringents that constrict blood vessels and lessen swelling while its mucilage forms a protective layer, soothing any irritation.

Consequently, the anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antispasmodic, astringent, and soothing properties of chamomile tea make it an effective remedy for hemorrhoids.
