10 best herbal teas for hemorrhoids

Hemorrhoids can be uncomfortable and painful, but certain herbal teas may provide relief.

Chamomile tea is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and discomfort in the affected area. Peppermint tea is another option, as it can help soothe the digestive system and alleviate pain. Additionally, research suggests that butcher's broom, an herb commonly found in tea form, may help strengthen blood vessel walls and reduce symptoms of hemorrhoids.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment regimen, especially if you are taking medication or have a medical condition.

1. Witch Hazel

Witch hazel tea treats hemorrhoids due to its potent anti-inflammatory properties, derived from tannins and gallic acid.

These compounds act as astringents, constricting blood vessels and reducing swelling. The tea's flavonoids bolster venous walls, enhancing their resilience. Furthermore, witch hazel's antioxidants promote healing by combating free radicals around the affected area.

Ultimately, witch hazel tea alleviates hemorrhoid-related discomfort through these combined mechanisms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in witch hazel tea that help with hemorrhoids.

  • Bark: The bark of the witch hazel plant contains tannins and oils that have astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe hemorrhoids.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the witch hazel plant are rich in antioxidants and possess anti-inflammatory qualities, providing relief from pain and itching associated with hemorrhoids.
  • Twigs and Buds: Twigs and buds of the witch hazel plant are a potent source of volatile oils and polyphenols, which exhibit astringent and healing effects beneficial for hemorrhoids.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the witch hazel plant contain flavonoids and other beneficial compounds, contributing to the plant's overall soothing and anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate hemorrhoid symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic witch hazel tea for hemorrhoids.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried witch hazel leaves or bark.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove solid particles and discard them.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea, 2-3 times a day, for hemorrhoid relief.

2. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory properties due to the presence of 3-4'-dideoxyflavone and its glycosides, which reduce inflammation by restricting blood flow to the affected area.

Its antioxidants, including phenolic acids and flavonoids, promote recovery by boosting the immune system and combating free radicals. The tea's antispasmodic qualities, resulting from its bisabolol content, alleviate pain by relaxing muscles in and around hemorrhoids. Chamomile tea's tannins act as astringents that constrict blood vessels and lessen swelling while its mucilage forms a protective layer, soothing any irritation.

Consequently, the anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antispasmodic, astringent, and soothing properties of chamomile tea make it an effective remedy for hemorrhoids.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with hemorrhoids.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain anti-inflammatory properties, making them effective in soothing the irritation and swelling associated with hemorrhoids.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids, chamomile leaves help reduce pain and promote healing of the tissue around hemorrhoids.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes of chamomile have antiseptic qualities, aiding in preventing infections in the inflamed area caused by hemorrhoids.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for hemorrhoids.

  1. Measure - Boil 8 ounces of water in a tea kettle.
  2. Prepare - Place one teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers in a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Steep - Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain - Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Serve - Drink the chamomile tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.

3. Horse Chestnut

Horse chestnut tea addresses hemorrhoids by tightening blood vessels, reducing inflammation, and improving circulation in the affected area.

This tree's seeds contain aescin, a saponin, which boasts anti-inflammatory properties. Moreover, this compound strengthens veins, thereby alleviating symptoms associated with hemorrhoids such as pain, swelling, and itching.

Overall, horse chestnut tea offers a natural solution that brings relief and healing to individuals suffering from this common ailment.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in horse chestnut tea that help with hemorrhoids.

  • Roots: Contain anti-inflammatory compounds that help reduce swelling and discomfort in hemorrhoids.
  • Bark: Rich in tannins that have astringent properties, helping to constrict blood vessels and alleviate symptoms of hemorrhoids.
  • Seeds: They contain saponins, which have been shown to reduce pain and itching associated with hemorrhoids.
  • Leaves: High in flavonoids, which possess anti-oxidant and vein-strengthening properties, beneficial for managing hemorrhoid-related symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic horse chestnut tea for hemorrhoids.

  1. Gather 20-30 fresh horse chestnut seeds, ensuring they are ripe (usually in autumn) and outer shells are uncracked.
  2. Remove outer shells and protective brown coating, then chop seeds into small pieces.
  3. Combine 2 cups of water and 1 tablespoon of chopped horse chestnut seeds in a pot, bringing to a boil.
  4. Simmer the mixture for 15-20 minutes, then strain and discard solids.
  5. Drink the resulting tea, up to three times a day, for a maximum of one week, to alleviate hemorrhoid symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic butcher's broom tea for hemorrhoids.

  2. Place the crushed butcher's broom into a tea infuser or tea ball.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the butcher's broom in a cup.
  4. Steep the butcher's broom in hot water for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Remove the tea infuser or tea ball and enjoy the butcher's broom tea, up to three times a day.

Goldenseal tea aids hemorrhoids due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, which act to reduce swelling and tighten tissue.

  • Roots: Goldenseal roots contain berberine, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it effective in treating hemorrhoids.
  • Rhizomes: Goldenseal rhizomes are rich in isoquinoline alkaloids, known for their astringent and styptic properties, providing relief from hemorrhoidal symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic goldenseal tea for hemorrhoids.

  1. In a stovepot, boil 2 cups of water for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried goldenseal root, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove goldenseal particles, then let it cool for a few minutes.
  4. Mix in honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, 2-3 times a day after meals for hemorrhoid relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for hemorrhoids.

  1. Gather 2-3 dandelion heads and rinse them thoroughly under running water to remove dirt and insects.
  2. Separate the yellow petals from the green base and discard the bases.
  3. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot, then add the dandelion petals, reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 2-3 times a day, before or after meals, for best results.
