1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with food poisoning because it contains antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties that can aid in soothing the gastrointestinal lining and reducing symptoms like abdominal cramps, nausea, and diarrhea.

The herbal tea is also known for its antimicrobial and antioxidant effects, which may help fight off the bacterial infections causing food poisoning while promoting recovery. Furthermore, chamomile tea has been shown to have a calming influence on the nervous system, which can be beneficial in alleviating stress, anxiety, and insomnia that often accompany foodborne illnesses. Additionally, its gentle nature makes it a suitable choice for individuals with sensitive stomachs or those seeking a more natural alternative to over-the-counter medications.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional if symptoms persist or worsen, as food poisoning can sometimes lead to severe complications.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with food poisoning.

  1. Azulene: Has anti-inflammatory and anti-bacterial properties that can help reduce inflammation and fight off bacteria causing food poisoning.
  2. Bisabolol: Possesses anti-spasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate symptoms such as cramps and spasms caused by food poisoning.
  3. Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin has anti-inflammatory and anti-bacterial effects, aiding in the reduction of inflammation and fighting bacterial infections.
  4. Apigenin: With anti-oxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, apigenin can help in reducing oxidative stress and inflammation caused by food poisoning.
  5. Chamazulene: This compound exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, assisting in reducing inflammation and combating microorganisms responsible for food poisoning.
  6. Alpha-Bisabolol Oxide A, B, and C: Having anti-inflammatory and anti-microbial properties, these compounds can aid in reducing inflammation and combating bacteria causing food poisoning.

The following list reveals what parts of chamomile are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for food poisoning.

  1. Flowers: Chamomile flowers are used due to their anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and carminative properties that can help soothe the digestive system and alleviate symptoms of food poisoning.
  2. Leaves: Chamomile leaves are included in teas for food poisoning because they contain flavonoids and essential oils that have calming effects on the gastrointestinal tract, reducing inflammation and cramping.
  3. Seeds: Chamomile seeds are used for their mild sedative qualities and ability to promote relaxation, which can help the body recover from food poisoning by reducing stress and encouraging rest.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for food poisoning.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle, reaching a temperature of 212°F (100°C) for full extraction of chamomile compounds.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon (about 2 grams) of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and let them steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors and medicinal properties to infuse.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the chamomile flowers from the teapot.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day, for its soothing and potential anti-inflammatory effects to help alleviate food poisoning symptoms.
