10 best herbal teas for oily skin

Certain herbal teas can be beneficial for individuals with oily skin, as they can help regulate sebum production and reduce the likelihood of acne and other skin impurities.

Some of the best herbal teas for oily skin include green tea, chamomile, and dandelion tea. Green tea is rich in antioxidants, which help to reduce inflammation and improve skin complexion.

Chamomile tea has anti-inflammatory and calming properties, which can soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. Dandelion tea is a natural diuretic, which can help to flush out toxins from the body and reduce the production of oils in the skin.

By drinking these herbal teas regularly, individuals with oily skin may notice an improvement in their skin's texture, tone, and overall health.

1. Green Tea

Green tea contains bioactive compounds, such as catechins and flavonoids, that exhibit potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help regulate sebum production, reducing the excess oil that clogs pores and leads to acne. Furthermore, green tea's antimicrobial properties inhibit the growth of Cutibacterium acnes, a bacterium responsible for causing acne. Additionally, green tea's anti-inflammatory effects soothe skin irritation, alleviating redness and swelling.

Lastly, green tea's antioxidants protect the skin from environmental stressors and damage, promoting overall skin health and combating the factors that exacerbate oily skin.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in green tea tea that help with oily skin.

  • Leaves: The leaves of green tea are rich in antioxidants that help to reduce the production of sebum, making it ideal for oily skin.
  • Buds: The buds of green tea contain high concentrations of catechins, known for their astringent properties that help to control excess oil on the skin.
  • Seeds: Green tea seeds are a natural source of vitamin E and fatty acids, which aid in maintaining the skin's natural barrier and reducing the appearance of oily skin.
  • Stems: Green tea stems are rich in flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe irritated skin and regulate sebum production.
  • Flowers: The flowers of green tea contain essential oils with antimicrobial properties, which can help to balance the skin's natural flora and reduce the appearance of oily skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic green tea tea for oily skin.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Steep 1 green tea bag in hot water for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Squeeze juice from 1/2 lemon into tea and stir.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey and mix well.
  5. Let the tea cool down, then drink, ideally 1-2 times a day for best results.

2. Black Tea

Black tea contains tannins and caffeine, which possess astringent properties that help to reduce excess oil on the skin.

The antioxidants in black tea, such as polyphenols, protect the skin from damage while controlling sebum production. Furthermore, black tea's antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties combat acne-causing bacteria and soothe inflamed skin. By using black tea as a toner or face mask, individuals with oily skin can reap these benefits and maintain a balanced, healthy complexion.

Regular application of black tea to oily skin tightens pores and creates a barrier against environmental pollutants, ensuring long-term skin health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in black tea tea that help with oily skin.

  • Leaves: Rich in tannins and caffeine, black tea leaves help reduce oil production when used in medicinal tea.
  • Buds: Abundant in antioxidants, black tea buds aid in maintaining skin's balance and reducing excess oil.
  • Rhyzomes: Black tea rhyzomes contain anti-inflammatory properties, helpful in soothing irritated skin and controlling oiliness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic black tea tea for oily skin.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of high-quality black tea leaves; let it steep for 5 minutes to extract the anti-oxidants.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice to create a soothing and exfoliating face mask.
  3. After the tea has steeped, remove the tea leaves and let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  4. Cleanse your face with the cooled black tea, massaging it gently onto your skin for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Apply the honey and lemon face mask, avoiding the eye area, and let it sit for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it off with warm water.

  4. Let the mixture cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink the tea and repeat 2-3 times a day for best results.

4. Peppermint

Peppermint tea's primary components, menthol and menthone, possess natural astringent properties.

Astringents work by shrinking pores and reducing the production of sebum, the skin's natural oil. By doing so, peppermint tea helps control the overproduction of sebum, a common cause of oily skin. Additionally, it boasts anti-inflammatory and antibacterial qualities that aid in soothing irritation and preventing acne breakouts.

Regularly consuming peppermint tea or using it as a compress or toner offers a chemical-free, cost-effective approach to managing oily skin.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with oily skin.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, peppermint leaves brew a potent tea that aids in controlling sebum production.
  • Flowers: High in antioxidants, peppermint flowers produce a soothing tea that combats skin inflammation and balances oily skin.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential oils, peppermint seeds create a nourishing tea that helps regulate oil glands and maintains skin's natural moisture.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for oily skin.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Steep the mixture for 5-10 minutes, allowing the peppermint leaves to release their oils.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the spent peppermint leaves.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Sip the peppermint tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and balance your oily skin.

5. Spearmint

Spearmint tea contains anti-androgenic properties that reduce the production of sebum, a natural oil that leads to oily skin when over-secreted.

This tea's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory qualities fight acne-causing bacteria and soothe skin inflammation. Moreover, spearmint tea's antioxidants protect skin cells from damage while promoting cell regeneration. By hydrating the skin and maintaining its balance, spearmint tea prevents excessive oil production.

Ultimately, drinking spearmint tea consistently leads to improved skin health, combating oily skin effectively.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in spearmint tea that help with oily skin.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, spearmint leaves help control sebum production and soothe irritated skin.
  • Flowers: Packed with essential oils, spearmint flowers offer potent astringent properties that can help regulate oiliness and tighten pores.
  • Seeds: Spearmint seeds contain high levels of skin-nourishing fatty acids, promoting balanced sebum production and supporting a healthy skin barrier.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic spearmint tea for oily skin.

  1. In a small pot, boil 2 cups of water for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried spearmint leaves to the boiling water, reduce heat, and let it simmer for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Turn off the heat, cover the pot, and let the tea steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the spearmint leaves, then let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink the spearmint tea twice daily for best results.

6. Yarrow

Yarrow tea provides a natural solution for oily skin, thanks to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

This herbal tea, made from the yarrow plant, tightens pores and reduces the production of sebum, leading to a balanced skin appearance. The presence of azulene, a compound in yarrow, soothes irritated skin and helps eliminate excess oil. Furthermore, yarrow tea's antimicrobial qualities combat acne-causing bacteria, promoting clearer skin.

Regular consumption of yarrow tea or topical application of its infusions contributes to a healthier, more matte complexion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in yarrow tea that help with oily skin.

  • Leaves: Contain azulene, which helps regulate sebum production in the skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tea for oily skin.

  1. Harvest 2-3 tablespoons of fresh yarrow flowers and leaves, making sure they are free of pesticides and chemicals.
  2. Rinse the harvested yarrow in cool water, then crush the flowers and leaves to release their natural oils.
  3. In a tea infuser or teapot, steep the crushed yarrow in 16 ounces of boiling water for 10-15 minutes.
  4. After steeping, remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the yarrow solids.
  5. Drink the warm yarrow tea, or let it cool and apply it topically to oily skin using a clean cotton ball.

7. Nettle

Nettle tea provides a natural solution for oily skin due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties, derived from plant acids, help to tighten pores and reduce the production of sebum. Furthermore, nettle tea contains essential minerals and vitamins, such as vitamin C and iron, which support skin health and collagen production. As a result, drinking nettle tea promotes balanced skin, reducing shine and providing a more even skin tone.

Lastly, its antioxidant qualities protect the skin from oxidative stress, preserving its youthful appearance.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in nettle tea that help with oily skin.

  • Leaves: High in nutrients, leaves help regulate oil production when used in medicinal tea for oily skin.
  • Stems: Astringent properties of stems tighten pores and reduce excess oil in medicinal tea for oily skin.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, flowers help soothe irritated skin and control oiliness in medicinal tea for oily skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic nettle tea for oily skin.

  1. Gather 2 handfuls of fresh stinging nettle leaves, ensuring they are free from pesticides and pollutants.
  2. Rinse the nettle leaves under cold running water to remove any remaining impurities and insects.
  3. Crush or chop the nettle leaves to release their natural oils and active compounds.
  4. Place the crushed nettle leaves in a teapot and pour 1 liter of boiling water over them. Let the tea steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the nettle tea into a cup, discarding the spent leaves, and allow it to cool before drinking. Enjoy 2-3 cups daily for best results.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in calendula tea that help with oily skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic calendula tea for oily skin.

  1. Infuse 1-2 teaspoons of dried calendula flowers in 8 ounces of boiling water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the flowers, leaving only the tea.
  3. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  4. Apply the cooled tea to your face with a cotton ball, avoiding the eye area.
  5. Let the tea dry on your skin for 10-15 minutes before rinsing off with warm water.

9. Burdock

Burdock tea provides a natural solution for oily skin due to its potent detoxifying properties.

This herbal tea comes from the burdock root, which contains essential compounds that purge the skin of excess oil and impurities. As a natural astringent, burdock tea tightens the skin, reducing the appearance of pores and controlling excessive oil production. Its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial qualities soothe irritations and help prevent acne, resulting in a more balanced, matte complexion.

Furthermore, burdock tea offers abundant antioxidants that stimulate skin cell regeneration, promoting a youthful, refreshed appearance.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in burdock tea that help with oily skin.

  • Roots: Burdock roots are rich in fatty oils and plant compounds that help regulate sebum production in the skin, making them ideal for medicinal tea to address oily skin.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes of burdock contain essential nutrients and antioxidants that support skin health, reducing excess oil and promoting a balanced complexion.
  • Leaves: Burdock leaves are a potent source of tannins and flavonoids, which have astringent properties that help control oil production and soothe irritated skin.
  • Flowers: The flowers of burdock are known for their anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which can help reduce redness and irritation associated with oily skin.
  • Seeds: Burdock seeds are rich in fatty acids, which provide moisturizing and nourishing benefits to the skin, while also balancing oil production.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic burdock tea for oily skin.

  1. Chop 2 tablespoons of dried burdock root into small pieces and rinse under cold water.
  2. Place the chopped burdock root in a pot and add 2 cups of water, bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the burdock root solids.
  5. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature before consuming.

Red clover tea contains isoflavones, which possess estrogenic properties that regulate sebum production in the skin.

This tea's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds, such as tocopherol and sitosterol, reduce skin inflammation and fight free radicals that cause skin damage. The tea's cleansing properties help remove impurities from clogged pores, preventing further skin irritations. Regular consumption of red clover tea promotes overall skin health by improving circulation and delivering essential nutrients to the skin cells.

Consequently, red clover tea effectively combats oily skin by controlling sebum production, reducing inflammation, and cleansing impurities.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in red clover tea that help with oily skin.

  • Flowers: Rich in isoflavones, the flowers help balance hormones promoting healthy skin.
  • Seeds: Packed with fatty acids, they support skin hydration and natural oil regulation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic red clover tea for oily skin.

  1. In a stove-top pot, combine 2 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried red clover flowers, then bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes to allow the nutrients to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the flowers, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Optionally, add a squeeze of lemon or a small amount of honey for flavor.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of this tea daily for best results in maintaining balanced, clear skin.
