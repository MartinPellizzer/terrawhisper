10 Best Herbal Teas For Oily Skin

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients like chamomile, mint, and nettle, can help with oily skin due to their anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

These herbs can help regulate sebum production, reducing the amount of oil on the skin. Additionally, some herbal teas contain antioxidants that can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals. Drinking herbal teas can also help support the body's overall health, which can in turn lead to improved skin health.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be a helpful addition to a skincare routine, they are not a cure-all solution and should be used in conjunction with other skincare practices and a healthy lifestyle.

1. Green Tea

Green tea helps with oily skin because Green tea helps with oxydenation and inflammation, making it an excellent option for managing oily skin.

The tea is rich in antioxidants called catechins, which reduce sebum production and prevent the clogging of pores. Furthermore, the anti-inflammatory properties of green tea soothe irritated skin and alleviate redness caused by excessive oiliness. Additionally, green tea's antibacterial properties help control the growth of acne-causing bacteria, leading to clearer and healthier-looking skin.

Lastly, green tea can be applied topically as a toner or a mask, providing a gentle and natural alternative to harsher chemical treatments for oily skin.

The following procedure explains how to make green tea tea for oily skin.

  1. In a non-metal teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of high-quality green tea leaves with 8 ounces of filtered water that has been brought to a boil, then let it cool down to around 160-170°F (71-77°C); steep for 2-3 minutes to extract the antioxidants and create a soothing green tea brew.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk 1 egg white until frothy, then gently fold in 1 tablespoon of the cooled green tea without over-mixing, creating a nourishing and gentle face mask.
  3. Cleanse your face with a mild cleanser, then apply the green tea and egg white mixture evenly onto your skin, avoiding the eye area, to provide deep pore cleansing, oil control, and mild exfoliation.
  4. Relax for 10-15 minutes, allowing the green tea and egg white mask to dry on your skin, drawing out impurities, tightening pores, and reducing oiliness.
  5. Rinse off the mask with warm water, then splash your face with cold water to close the pores, and gently pat your skin dry with a clean towel, leaving you with refreshed, matte, and revitalized oily skin.

2. Black Tea

Black tea helps with oily skin because it contains tannins and antioxidants that can help reduce oil production and soothe inflammation.

The astringent properties of black tea help to shrink pores and tighten the skin, which can reduce the amount of oil that is released. Additionally, black tea has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help to reduce redness, swelling, and the risk of acne breakouts. Drinking black tea or using it as a toner can also help to balance the skin's pH levels, which can help to regulate oil production and promote healthy skin.

Furthermore, black tea is a natural source of caffeine, which can help to improve blood flow and promote the growth of new skin cells, leading to a clearer and more even complexion.

The following procedure explains how to make black tea tea for oily skin.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling, then steep 1 teaspoon of high-quality loose leaf black tea, such as Assam or Darjeeling, for 5 minutes; allow the tea to cool, then transfer to a spray bottle and store in the refrigerator.
  2. In a small bowl, mix 1 tablespoon of brewed black tea with 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar until a smooth paste forms.
  3. Cleanse oily skin thoroughly, then apply the paste to your face, avoiding the eye area; let it sit for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Rinse off the mask with warm water, then splash your face with cold water to close the pores.
  5. Gently pat your skin dry with a clean towel and apply a toner and moisturizer suitable for oily skin. Repeat this process 1-2 times a week for best results.

3. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with oily skin because it contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that can help regulate sebum production, reducing the appearance of oily skin.

The tea's antiseptic and healing properties can also help soothe irritation and inflammation caused by excess oil, while its mild astringent properties can help tighten pores and give the skin a matte finish. Furthermore, chamomile tea has anti-bacterial properties that can help prevent acne breakouts caused by oily skin. Drinking chamomile tea can also help reduce stress and hormonal imbalances that can contribute to oily skin.

Overall, incorporating chamomile tea into your skincare routine can help balance your skin's oil production, reduce inflammation, and promote a clearer, healthier complexion.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for oily skin.

  1. To make chamomile tea for oily skin, start by boiling 2 cups of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers; let it simmer for 10 minutes to allow the nutrients to infuse.
  2. After 10 minutes, strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers and let it cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  3. Optionally, add a squeeze of half a lemon for its astringent properties, which can help balance the skin's oil production.
  4. To use the chamomile tea as a toner, soak a cotton pad in the tea and gently sweep it over your face, avoiding the eye area.
  5. Let the chamomile tea absorb into your skin for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it off with warm water and patting your face dry with a clean towel. Use this toner once a day, ideally in the evening, for best results.

4. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with oily skin because Peppermint tea aids in controlling oily skin due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can help regulate the production of sebum, the oily substance produced by the skin's sebaceous glands.

The menthol present in peppermint tea has a cooling effect on the skin, reducing inflammation and providing relief to irritated skin. Additionally, peppermint tea contains antioxidants that can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthier-looking skin. Furthermore, drinking peppermint tea can help reduce stress, which is a common trigger for increased sebum production, leading to oilier skin.

Lastly, peppermint tea can be used topically as a toner or compress, providing a gentle, natural alternative to chemical-laden products that can strip the skin of its natural oils and exacerbate the problem of oily skin.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for oily skin.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of fresh peppermint leaves, then let it steep for 15-20 minutes to allow the oils from the peppermint leaves to release into the water.
  2. After steeping, strain the tea to remove the leaves, then let it cool down to a comfortable temperature to avoid damaging skin.
  3. Wash your face with a gentle cleanser, then soak a clean washcloth in the peppermint tea and place it on your face for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Rinse your face with cool water, then pat it dry with a soft towel.
  5. Apply a light, non-comedogenic moisturizer to lock in hydration and prevent dryness. Enjoy your refreshed, oil-balanced skin!

5. Spearmint

Spearmint tea helps with oily skin because it contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help reduce excess oil production and soothe irritation.

The menthol present in spearmint tea has a cooling effect on the skin, which can help regulate sebum production and unclog pores. Additionally, spearmint tea is rich in antioxidants that can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthier-looking skin. Drinking spearmint tea regularly may also help balance hormones that can contribute to oily skin, such as androgens.

However, it is important to note that while spearmint tea can be beneficial for oily skin, it should not be used as a substitute for a consistent skincare routine and medical treatment if necessary.

The following procedure explains how to make spearmint tea for oily skin.

  1. To make spearmint tea for oily skin, start by boiling 8 cups of water in a large pot, then add 1 cup of fresh spearmint leaves (or 1/2 cup of dried spearmint) and let it simmer for 15 minutes to allow the nutrients to infuse.
  2. While the tea is steeping, rinse out a clean 1-quart glass jar and its lid with hot water to ensure they are sterilized; set aside.
  3. After 15 minutes, strain the spearmint tea through a fine-mesh sieve into the sterilized glass jar, discarding the used leaves; allow the tea to cool to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  4. To use, wet a clean washcloth or cotton pad with the spearmint tea and apply it as a gentle toner to freshly cleansed oily skin, avoiding the eye area; let the tea cool down in the refrigerator for a refreshing, anti-inflammatory treatment.
  5. Store the spearmint tea in the refrigerator for up to 1 week; shake or stir before each use, and enjoy the calming, oil-balancing benefits of this natural remedy.

6. Yarrow

Yarrow tea helps with oily skin because it contains anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, which can help regulate sebum production and reduce skin redness and irritation.

The tea also has antimicrobial qualities that can help prevent acne breakouts caused by bacteria that thrive in oily environments. Additionally, yarrow tea contains flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones, which have been shown to improve skin health and reduce excess oiliness. Drinking yarrow tea or using it as a toner can help maintain the skin's natural pH balance, preventing the overproduction of sebum that leads to oily skin.

Overall, yarrow tea offers a natural and holistic approach to managing oily skin, providing a range of benefits that can support overall skin health.

The following procedure explains how to make yarrow tea for oily skin.

  1. To make yarrow tea for oily skin, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried yarrow flowers and leaves; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. After simmering, strain the tea to remove the yarrow herbs, then let it cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  3. Optionally, squeeze half a lemon into the yarrow tea for its astringent properties, which can help reduce oiliness.
  4. To use the yarrow tea as a toner, soak a clean cotton pad in the tea and gently dab it onto your face, avoiding the eye area.
  5. Store any remaining yarrow tea in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, and use it as part of your daily skincare routine, preferably in the morning and/or evening.

7. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with oily skin because it has astringent properties that can help regulate the production of sebum, the oily substance produced by the skin's sebaceous glands.

This makes nettle tea an effective natural remedy for reducing excess oil and preventing clogged pores, which can lead to breakouts and acne. Additionally, nettle tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe irritated skin and reduce redness and swelling associated with acne.

Furthermore, nettle tea is rich in vitamins and minerals, including vitamin C, vitamin E, and iron, which are essential for maintaining healthy skin and promoting collagen production. Drinking nettle tea regularly can also help support the body's natural detoxification processes, which can improve overall skin health and reduce the appearance of oily skin.

Overall, incorporating nettle tea into your skincare routine can provide a natural and holistic approach to managing oily skin.

The following procedure explains how to make nettle tea for oily skin.

  1. Steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried nettle leaves in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-10 minutes; allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, and discard them; you should be left with only the liquid.
  3. Clean your face with warm water and pat it dry using a clean towel.
  4. Soak a cotton ball in the cooled nettle tea and apply it to your face, focusing on oily areas such as the T-zone.
  5. Let the nettle tea absorb into your skin for 10-15 minutes, then rinse off with cool water and pat your face dry.

8. Calendula

Calendula tea helps with oily skin because it contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that regulate sebum production, reducing excess oil on the skin.

The tea's gentle astringent qualities help to tighten pores, preventing the buildup of dirt and oil that can lead to acne and other skin issues. Furthermore, calendula tea has antimicrobial properties that fight bacteria responsible for causing skin infections, promoting clearer and healthier skin. Drinking calendula tea can also support the body's internal balance, which may contribute to overall skin health and reduced oil production.

Additionally, using calendula tea as a toner or compress can soothe irritated skin and reduce redness, providing a more even skin tone.

The following procedure explains how to make calendula tea for oily skin.

  1. To make calendula tea for oily skin, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried calendula flowers; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. While the tea is steeping, prepare a clean infuser or tea strainer and a heat-resistant container.
  3. After 10-15 minutes, remove the tea from heat and carefully pour it through the infuser into the container, ensuring all the beneficial compounds from the calendula flowers are extracted.
  4. Allow the calendula tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature before using it as a toner or rinse for oily skin, either by soaking a cotton pad and gently patting it on your face or pouring it directly onto your skin.
  5. For best results, use the calendula tea consistently, storing any leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

9. Burdock

Burdock tea helps with oily skin because it has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help regulate sebum production, reducing the likelihood of acne breakouts.

The tea is rich in antioxidants that help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting skin health and reducing excess oil. Burdock tea also contains essential nutrients such as vitamin A, B, and E, which are known to improve skin tone and reduce the appearance of oily skin. Furthermore, burdock tea has diuretic properties that can help eliminate toxins from the body, reducing inflammation and promoting healthy skin.

Lastly, burdock tea has been used traditionally as a blood purifier, which can help improve overall skin health and reduce the symptoms of oily skin.

The following procedure explains how to make burdock tea for oily skin.

  1. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil, then add 2 tablespoons of dried burdock root, reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. While the tea is steeping, prepare a clean tea infuser or teapot with a built-in strainer.
  3. Pour the burdock tea through the infuser or strainer into a teapot or heat-resistant container.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature, then gently rinse your face with the tea using a clean washcloth or your hands.
  5. Let the tea soak into your skin for about 10 minutes, then rinse off with warm water and pat your face dry.

10. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with oily skin because Red clover tea helps with oxydenezenze production, which can reduce excess oil on the skin.

It has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe irritation and redness caused by oily skin. Additionally, red clover tea contains isoflavones, which can help regulate sebum production, preventing the skin from becoming too oily. The tea also has a gentle exfoliating effect, removing dead skin cells and unclogging pores, which can help reduce the appearance of oily skin.

Furthermore, red clover tea has been shown to have antibacterial properties, which can help prevent acne breakouts that are often associated with oily skin.

The following procedure explains how to make red clover tea for oily skin.

  1. To make red clover tea for oily skin, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. While the tea is steeping, prepare a infusion by combining 1/4 cup of boiling water with 1 tablespoon of crushed red clover leaves; let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. After both the tea and infusion have finished steeping, strain the liquid from both mixtures and discard the solids.
  4. Combine the strained red clover tea and infusion, then let the mixture cool to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. To use, drink 1-2 cups of the red clover tea daily, or use it as a toner by allowing it to cool completely and applying it to your skin with a cotton ball.
