10 Best Herbal Teas For Gastroparesis

Herbal teas can help with gastroparesis by stimulating the production of digestive enzymes and promoting muscle contractions in the gastrointestinal tract, which can enhance motility and alleviate symptoms of delayed gastric emptying.

Certain herbs, such as chamomile, peppermint, and ginger, have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that can help reduce inflammation and pain in the gastrointestinal tract, providing relief from cramps and spasms. Additionally, some herbal teas, such as those made from fennel or anise, can help soothe the stomach and alleviate symptoms of nausea and vomiting. Furthermore, herbal teas can help hydrate the body, which is essential for maintaining optimal digestive function, and they are caffeine-free, making them a gentle and soothing option for individuals with gastroparesis.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating herbal teas into a treatment plan for gastroparesis, as some herbs may interact with medications or exacerbate certain medical conditions.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with gastroparesis because it has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help to relax the muscles in the gastrointestinal tract, promoting motility and alleviating symptoms of delayed gastric emptying.

Its calming effects on the stomach can reduce nausea, vomiting, and abdominal discomfort associated with gastroparesis. Chamomile tea is also known for its carminative properties, which can help to expel gas from the stomach and intestines, reducing bloating and pain. Furthermore, it contains flavonoids that stimulate the production of bile, which aids in digestion and the absorption of nutrients.

Lastly, chamomile tea has been shown to have a relaxing effect on the mind and body, which can help to alleviate stress-related gastroparesis symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Heat 4 cups of water in a kettle or pot until boiling (10-15 minutes), then remove from heat.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of dried chamomile flowers to a teapot or infuser, then pour the hot water over the chamomile.
  3. Allow the chamomile to steep for 5-10 minutes, then remove the infuser or strain out the chamomile flowers.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for added flavor and potential gastrointestinal benefits.
  5. Serve the tea warm, sipping slowly and allowing it to gently soothe and potentially aid in digestion for those with gastroparesis.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with gastroparesis because it contains menthol and methyl salicylate, compounds that have antispasmodic properties, which can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and improve the passage of food through the digestive system.

Additionally, peppermint tea has been shown to increase gastric emptying, reducing symptoms of gastroparesis such as bloating, nausea, and vomiting. Its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects may also help alleviate discomfort and pain in the gastrointestinal tract. However, it is important to note that peppermint tea may not be suitable for everyone, especially those with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) or a history of gallstones.

As with any treatment, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating peppermint tea or any other herbal remedies into a treatment plan for gastroparesis.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for gastroparesis.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 cup of fresh, washed peppermint leaves (or 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint) and 8 cups of water, let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Optional: For enhanced benefits, add 1 teaspoon of crushed caraway seeds and 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds to the teapot before steeping.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves and seeds, if used.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to stay in your mouth for a moment before swallowing, to ensure maximum exposure to the minty goodness. Enjoy!

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with gastroparesis because it contains active components like gingerols and shogaols, which have been shown to promote gastric motility and accelerate gastric emptying.

These properties make ginger tea beneficial for people with gastroparesis, a condition characterized by delayed gastric emptying. Moreover, ginger tea has anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which can alleviate abdominal pain and discomfort associated with gastroparesis.

Additionally, ginger tea can help alleviate nausea and vomiting, common symptoms of gastroparesis, by regulating the activities of the gastrointestinal tract's nervous system. However, it is important to note that ginger tea should be consumed in moderation, as excessive consumption may exacerbate heartburn or acid reflux.

Lastly, individuals should consult with their healthcare provider before incorporating ginger tea or any other natural remedies into their treatment plan for gastroparesis.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Peel and finely chop 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root; place in a small saucepan.
  2. Add 1.5 cups of water to the saucepan and bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover and let simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, add honey or lemon to taste (optional), and let cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to gently stimulate digestion.

4. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with gastroparesis because it contains substances called antrachinones and glycyrrhizin, which can stimulate the production of bile and gastric juices, promoting digestion and motility in the gastrointestinal tract.

Additionally, licorice tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, which is often a contributing factor in gastroparesis. Furthermore, licorice tea can help soothe irritated mucous membranes and provide relief from symptoms such as nausea, bloating, and heartburn. However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and potassium depletion, so it should be consumed in moderation.

Lastly, it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedies, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking prescription medications.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice tea for gastroparesis.

  1. In a tea infuser, combine 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds, 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root, and 1 teaspoon of dried dill seed (adjust quantities to taste).
  2. Place the tea infuser in a cup, and pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the herbs.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
  4. Remove the tea infuser and discard the herbs.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day between meals to aid digestion and soothe gastroparesis symptoms.

5. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with gastroparesis because it contains mucilage, a substance that can create a protective barrier in the stomach and aid in the soothing of mucous membranes.

This barrier can help to reduce inflammation and irritation in the stomach, which can be particularly beneficial for individuals with gastroparesis as delayed stomach emptying can cause food to sit in the stomach for extended periods, leading to increased acid production and irritation. Additionally, the mucilage in marshmallow tea can help to lubricate the digestive tract, making it easier for food to pass through, and potentially reducing symptoms such as bloating, cramping, and pain. Marshmallow tea may also help to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, which can improve overall digestion and absorption of nutrients, further benefiting those with gastroparesis.

However, it is important to note that while marshmallow tea may provide some relief for gastroparesis symptoms, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment or prescription medications, and individuals with gastroparesis should consult with their healthcare provider before incorporating marshmallow tea or any other herbal remedies into their treatment plan.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Ingredients: - 1 cup of water, - 4 marshmallows (preferably without gelatin for gastroparesis-friendly option), - A small dash of cinnamon powder (optional, for added flavor and potential aiding in digestion).
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a small pot on the stove.
  3. Once boiling, add 4 marshmallows to the pot and reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Gently stir the marshmallows until they are fully melted and integrated into the water. Add a small dash of cinnamon powder if desired.
  5. Pour the marshmallow tea through a strainer into a cup to remove any remaining marshmallow solids. Enjoy while warm.

6. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with gastroparesis because it aids in the relaxation of the gastrointestinal tract muscles, which can help improve the motility and evacuation of the stomach's contents.

This is particularly beneficial for individuals with gastroparesis, a condition characterized by delayed gastric emptying. Fennel tea contains volatile oils, such as anethole, which have been shown to have antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, potentially alleviating symptoms of gastroparesis like bloating, pain, and discomfort.

Moreover, fennel tea has been traditionally used as a carminative, an agent that helps in expelling gas from the stomach and intestines, further easing gastroparesis symptoms. Additionally, fennel tea may stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, promoting better digestion and absorption of nutrients, which can be compromised in individuals with gastroparesis.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating fennel tea or any other herbal remedy into a gastroparesis treatment plan.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Thinly slice 1 tablespoon of fresh fennel bulb, steep in 8 ounces of boiling water for 10 minutes, then strain to make the tea.
  2. Optionally, add 1 teaspoon of honey or lemon juice to taste for improved flavor.
  3. Drink the tea slowly, allowing it to cool down to a comfortable temperature first.
  4. Consume 2-3 cups of fennel tea per day, between meals, for best results.
  5. For severe gastroparesis, consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new treatment.

7. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with gastroparesis because it promotes digestion by stimulating the production of gastric juices, thereby facilitating the movement of food from the stomach to the small intestine.

This herbal tea is also known for its ability to tone and strengthen the stomach muscles, which can be beneficial for individuals with gastroparesis as weakened stomach muscles are a common cause of this condition. Furthermore, dandelion tea has natural diuretic properties that can help reduce bloating and discomfort associated with gastroparesis. Additionally, it contains anti-inflammatory compounds that can help soothe the gastrointestinal tract, reducing inflammation and promoting overall digestive health.

Finally, dandelion tea is caffeine-free, making it a gentler alternative to other stimulant drinks that can exacerbate gastroparesis symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Gather 4-5 fresh dandelion heads, carefully removing their yellow flowers from the green stems to avoid bitterness, and rinse them gently.
  2. In a teapot or a heat-resistant container, steep the dandelion flowers in 2 cups of boiling water for 10-15 minutes to extract their nutrients and flavors.
  3. Strain the dandelion tea through a fine-mesh sieve or a tea strainer into a cup, discarding the spent flowers.
  4. Add honey or another natural sweetener to taste, stirring until fully dissolved, to help mask any bitterness and soothe the stomach.
  5. Sip the warm dandelion tea slowly, allowing it to gently promote digestion and alleviate gastroparesis symptoms. Enjoy it 2-3 times a day, especially after meals.

8. Skullcap

Skullcap tea helps with gastroparesis because it contains antispasmodic properties that help relax the muscles in the gastrointestinal (GI) tract, reducing symptoms such as cramping, bloating, and pain.

Additionally, skullcap tea has anti-inflammatory effects that can help reduce inflammation in the GI tract, which is often a contributing factor in gastroparesis. Furthermore, skullcap tea has been shown to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, which can help improve digestion and absorption of nutrients, reducing symptoms of gastroparesis. Skullcap tea may also help regulate stomach acid production, which can help prevent acid reflux and other digestive issues associated with gastroparesis.

Finally, skullcap tea has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce stress and anxiety, both of which can exacerbate symptoms of gastroparesis.

The following procedure explaing how to make skullcap tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Take 2 teaspoons of dried skullcap herb (Scutellaria lateriflora), place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer, and pour 1 cup of boiling water over it, letting it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. While the skullcap tea is brewing, grate a 1-inch piece of fresh ginger root and squeeze the juice out, obtaining about 1 teaspoon of ginger juice.
  3. After the skullcap tea has steeped, add the 1 teaspoon of ginger juice to the tea and stir well.
  4. Optional: Add honey or stevia to taste, if desired, for added sweetness.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent herbs, and drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day between meals.

9. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with gastroparesis because it promotes healthy digestion by stimulating the production of bile and digestive enzymes, which in turn facilitates the breakdown and absorption of nutrients in the small intestine.

Additionally, milk thistle contains silymarin, an antioxidant that protects the liver and supports its function in processing and eliminating toxins, which can help alleviate symptoms of gastroparesis. Furthermore, milk thistle tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, easing discomfort and promoting regularity. Moreover, milk thistle tea has been shown to improve motility and regulate muscle contractions in the gastrointestinal tract, which can help alleviate the delayed gastric emptying associated with gastroparesis.

Lastly, milk thistle tea has been used traditionally as a natural remedy for various digestive ailments, including gastroparesis, due to its potential to improve overall digestive health and function.

The following procedure explaing how to make milk thistle tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Crush 1-2 teaspoons of milk thistle seeds and steep in boiling water for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the crushed seeds.
  3. Add honey or lemon for taste, if desired.
  4. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.
  5. Consult with a healthcare provider before using milk thistle if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

10. Artichoke

Artichoke tea helps with gastroparesis because it contains cynarin, a compound known to stimulate bile production and facilitate digestion.

This aids in the breakdown of food in the stomach and reduces the symptoms of gastroparesis, such as bloating, nausea, and vomiting. Additionally, artichoke tea is rich in antioxidants that help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, further alleviating gastroparesis symptoms. Furthermore, it contains fiber, which promotes gut motility and regularity, helping to move food through the digestive system more efficiently.

Lastly, artichoke tea has been shown to regulate gastric juice secretion, ensuring proper stomach acid levels and enhancing overall digestive function.

The following procedure explaing how to make artichoke tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Rinse and drain 1 fresh artichoke, then separate and roughly chop the leaves, simmering them in 4 cups of water for 15 minutes to extract their flavors and medicinal properties.
  2. Strain the tea, discarding the solids, and allow it to cool for several minutes before stirring in 1 teaspoon of raw honey to enhance the taste and soothe the stomach.
  3. For added anti-inflammatory benefits and improved digestion, squeeze the juice of half a lemon into the tea and stir well.
  4. To further alleviate gastroparesis symptoms, grate a 1-inch piece of fresh ginger and steep it in the hot artichoke tea for 3-5 minutes before removing and discarding the ginger.
  5. Serve the artichoke tea warm, sipping it slowly to allow it to gently stimulate digestion and soothe the symptoms of gastroparesis.
