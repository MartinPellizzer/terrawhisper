10 best herbal teas for gastroparesis

Gastroparesis is a stomach condition that can cause discomfort, pain, and digestive issues.

While there is no cure for gastroparesis, certain herbal teas may help alleviate some of its symptoms. Peppermint tea is known for its ability to soothe the stomach and improve digestion, making it a popular choice for those with gastroparesis.

Ginger tea is another good option, as it can help reduce inflammation and nausea. Chamomile tea has anti-spasmodic properties that can help relax the stomach muscles and relieve pain. Additionally, licorice root tea may help increase mucus secretion in the gastrointestinal tract, which can protect the stomach lining and reduce inflammation.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before adding any new herbs or supplements to your diet, especially if you have a medical condition like gastroparesis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Measure - boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle or pot.
  2. Add - place one chamomile tea bag or 1-2 teaspoons of loose chamomile flowers in a cup.
  3. Pour - once water reaches a boil, pour it over the tea bag or flowers.
  4. Steep - let the tea steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  5. Remove - take out the tea bag or strain loose flowers before drinking.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fennel tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Chop one half of a fresh fennel bulb into thin slices, then rinse them in cold water.
  2. Place the fennel slices in a pot and pour 4 cups of water, then bring it to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a teapot or a large bowl, discarding the fennel pieces.
  5. Serve the fennel tea hot, optionally adding honey or lemon to taste. Enjoy up to three cups a day.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Pick 2-3 cups of fresh dandelion flowers, making sure they haven't been treated with pesticides, and rinse them gently.
  2. Leave the flowers to wilt in a cool place for 12-24 hours to reduce their bitterness.
  3. Coarsely chop the wilted dandelion flowers and place them in a pot with 4 cups of water.
  4. Slowly bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea, discarding the solids, and let it cool before drinking up to three cups a day to help alleviate gastroparesis symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic artichoke tea for gastroparesis.

  1. Rinse and drain two globe artichokes, then separate the leaves and chop the hearts into small pieces.

  3. Strain the artichoke tea into a cup, discarding the solid pieces.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for flavor.
  5. Serve the artichoke tea warm and drink up to three times a day for gastroparesis relief.
