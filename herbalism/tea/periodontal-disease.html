10 best herbal teas for periodontal disease

While more research is needed, some herbal teas may have properties that could potentially benefit individuals with periodontal disease.

For instance, green tea is rich in antioxidants called catechins, which may help reduce inflammation and combat harmful bacteria in the mouth. Chamomile tea, known for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, could also potentially soothe gum irritation and help maintain oral health.

Additionally, echinacea tea has been suggested to boost the immune system, which might aid in fighting off periodontal infections. However, it's crucial to remember that herbal teas should not replace regular dental care and professional treatment.

Always consult with a dental healthcare provider for personalized advice.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains powerful anti-inflammatory properties due to the presence of flavonoids, which reduce inflammation in the gums caused by periodontal disease.

Its antimicrobial properties, derived from the compound Chamazulene, inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria in the mouth that leads to periodontal disease. Chamomile tea also promotes mouth healing through its ability to stimulate the growth of new cells, which helps in the repair of damaged gum tissues. Furthermore, the soothing effect of chamomile helps alleviate mouth pain and soreness, providing relief to those suffering from periodontal disease.

Lastly, regular consumption of chamomile tea strengthens the immune system, enhancing the body's ability to fight off oral infections that cause periodontal disease.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with periodontal disease.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain anti-inflammatory properties that help in reducing pain and swelling in the gums.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, chamomile leaves help in preventing bacterial infection, a common cause of periodontal disease.
  • Rhyzomes: Chamomile rhyzomes have antimicrobial properties that aid in controlling plaque, a primary cause of periodontal disease.
  • Stems: The antiseptic properties found in chamomile stems assist in fighting oral infections.
  • Roots: Chamomile roots contain azulene, which provides anti-inflammatory and antiphlogistic effects, helpful for treating gum inflammation.

2. Echinacea

Echinacea tea contains active compounds, such as alkamides and polysaccharides, which demonstrate potent immunostimulatory properties.

These compounds activate the immune system, increasing the production of white blood cells and enhancing their ability to fight infections. As a result, echinacea tea helps reduce the severity of periodontal disease by diminishing bacterial load and inflammation in the gum tissue. Moreover, echinacea's anti-inflammatory properties help control the production of prostaglandins and cytokines, which are pro-inflammatory molecules contributing to periodontal tissue destruction.

Regular consumption of echinacea tea bolsters the body's defense mechanisms, promoting overall oral health and accelerating the healing of inflamed gums and periodontal tissues.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in echinacea tea that help with periodontal disease.

  • Roots: Containing active compounds that fight off oral bacteria, roots are effective in decreasing inflammation and preventing infections in the gums.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, flowers help to reduce oxidative stress and promote healing in the gums and surrounding tissues.
  • Leaves: Packed with tannins that have astringent properties, leaves help to tighten gum tissue, reduce gum bleeding, and soothe irritation.
  • Buds: High in essential oils that have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, buds aid in preventing plaque buildup, freshening breath, and promoting oral health.

3. Goldenseal

Goldenseal tea contains berberine, a powerful alkaloid, which demonstrably exhibits antimicrobial properties.

This potent compound targets periodontal disease-causing bacteria, effectively reducing their colonization. Goldenseal tea's anti-inflammatory qualities, derived from its berberine content, alleviate periodontal tissue swelling and inflammation. Moreover, goldenseal tea's astringent properties, owing to the tannins present, help control gum bleeding.

Consequently, goldenseal tea bolsters overall oral health by promoting periodontal tissue healing and deterring the progression of periodontal disease.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in goldenseal tea that help with periodontal disease.

  • Roots: Goldenseal roots contain berberine, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making them effective in managing periodontal disease.
  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of goldenseal, a horizontal stem that grows underground, have rich alkaloid content, including berberine, which helps reduce bacteria responsible for gum inflammation.
  • Leaves: Goldenseal leaves possess antimicrobial and astringent qualities that can help promote gum tissue health and alleviate periodontal disease symptoms.
  • Flowers: Although rarely used, goldenseal flowers contain beneficial compounds that can support oral health, contributing to the reduction of periodontal disease-causing bacteria.

4. Myrrh

Myrrh tea's antiseptic properties, proven by numerous scientific studies, destroy harmful bacteria in the mouth.

This plant's resin contains active compounds like terpenoids and sesquiterpenes that directly combat periodontal disease-causing microorganisms. Furthermore, myrrh tea's anti-inflammatory abilities reduce gum inflammation, a common symptom of periodontal disease.

Additionally, myrrh tea promotes mouth tissue regeneration, healing damaged gums affected by periodontal disease.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in myrrh tea that help with periodontal disease.

  • Roots: The roots of the myrrh plant contain compounds with potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making them effective for treating periodontal disease.
  • Rhyzomes: These horizontal underground stems have similar medicinal properties as roots and are used to make tea that helps reduce gum inflammation and fight infections causing periodontal disease.
  • Bark: The bark of the myrrh tree is rich in antioxidants that help in healing gum tissues and preventing further damage in periodontal disease.
  • Buds: Myrrh buds are potent sources of antiseptic and analgesic properties, which are beneficial in managing pain and preventing infections in periodontal disease.
  • Seeds: Myrrh seeds contain essential oils and resins that are used to make teas with potent antimicrobial effects, aiding in treating periodontal disease by inhibiting the growth of harmful bacteria in the mouth.

5. Turmeric

Turmeric tea contains curcumin, a compound with proven anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help reduce inflammation in the gums, a key symptom of periodontal disease. Curcumin also inhibits the growth of harmful bacteria that cause gum infections, promoting healthier oral flora. Furthermore, it prevents the breakdown of collagen, a protein that supports gum tissue, thereby strengthening periodontal tissues.

Lastly, curcumin's antioxidant effects protect gum tissues from further damage, contributing to overall oral health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in turmeric tea that help with periodontal disease.

  • Rhizomes: The underground stem of turmeric contains curcumin, a compound with strong anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help reduce inflammation and improve gum health in periodontal disease.
  • Roots: The turmeric roots, closely related to rhizomes, also contain curcumin, making it effective in reducing periodontal inflammation and improving overall oral health.
  • Leaves: Turmeric leaves, rich in antioxidants, are used to make medicinal tea that helps soothe gum irritation, minimize inflammation, and promote healing in periodontal disease.
  • Flowers: Infrequently used but valuable, turmeric flowers contain curcumin and other bioactive compounds, making them useful for making medicinal tea to alleviate symptoms of periodontal disease and support gum health.

By sipping ginger tea, individuals experience a decrease in the severity of periodontal disease symptoms due to ginger's natural healing abilities.
