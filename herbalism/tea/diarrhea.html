10 Best Herbal Teas For Diarrhea

Herbal teas, particularly those made from chamomile, peppermint, and ginger, can help alleviate diarrhea due to their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These herbs can help soothe the gastrointestinal tract, reducing inflammation and irritation that can contribute to diarrhea. Additionally, they may help slow down the rate of digestion, allowing the body to absorb more water and nutrients from food, which can help solidify stools. The astringent properties of some herbal teas, such as those made from blackberry leaves or raspberry leaves, can help tighten and tone the intestinal tissue, reducing the frequency and urgency of bowel movements.

Furthermore, herbal teas can provide hydration, which is essential for preventing dehydration caused by diarrhea.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with diarrhea because of its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and mild astringent properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the intestines, relax the muscles of the intestinal wall, and help slow down intestinal movements.

The soothing effects of chamomile may also help alleviate symptoms associated with diarrhea, such as cramping and abdominal pain. Additionally, chamomile contains tannins, which can help reduce fluid secretion and promote the absorption of water back into the body, thereby helping to solidify stools. However, it's important to note that while chamomile tea can help manage symptoms of diarrhea, it's not a substitute for medical treatment, especially in cases of severe or persistent diarrhea.

Lastly, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before using chamomile tea or any other herbal remedy to treat medical conditions.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for diarrhea.

  1. To make chamomile tea for diarrhea, start by boiling 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle (let it reach a rolling boil).
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) to a teapot or directly into a cup.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers (or tea bag) and let it steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers (if used) and allow it to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of chamomile tea throughout the day, as needed, for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that may help alleviate diarrhea symptoms.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with diarrhea because it contains antispasmodic properties, which can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and reduce the frequency of contractions, thereby reducing the urgency and frequency of bowel movements.

The menthol present in peppermint oil has an antimicrobial effect, which can help eliminate harmful bacteria in the intestines that may be causing diarrhea. Peppermint oil also has an anti-inflammatory effect, which can help reduce inflammation in the gut and alleviate symptoms of diarrhea. Additionally, peppermint tea can help soothe and calm an upset stomach, providing relief from discomfort and pain associated with diarrhea.

However, it is important to note that while peppermint tea can be helpful for managing diarrhea, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in cases of severe or persistent diarrhea.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for diarrhea.

  1. To make peppermint tea for diarrhea, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves or 1 peppermint tea bag to a teapot or cup.
  2. Allow the peppermint tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve the desired flavor.
  3. After steeping, remove the peppermint leaves or tea bag from the teapot or cup to prevent over-extraction and bitterness.
  4. For added benefits, squeeze half a fresh lemon (about 1-2 tablespoons) into the peppermint tea for its soothing and antibacterial properties.
  5. Finally, enjoy the peppermint tea warm, sipping slowly to help alleviate symptoms of diarrhea, and drink 2-3 cups daily as needed.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with diarrhea because it contains volatile oils, such as gingerols and shogaols, which have been shown to help reduce inflammation and irritation in the intestines, while also inhibiting the contraction of the intestinal muscles, thereby allowing for more normal digestion and alleviating diarrhea symptoms.

Additionally, ginger has antimicrobial properties that can help eliminate harmful bacteria in the digestive tract that may be causing diarrhea. Furthermore, ginger tea can help rehydrate the body, as diarrhea can lead to dehydration, and the warm temperature of the tea can provide soothing relief to an upset stomach.

However, it is important to note that while ginger tea can be helpful for mild cases of diarrhea, it is not a substitute for medical treatment for severe or persistent diarrhea.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for diarrhea.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger root, then place in a pot and add 2 cups of water; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 20 minutes.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and 1/2 teaspoon of lemon juice.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the ginger pieces.
  4. Let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink the tea in small sips throughout the day, up to three times a day, until diarrhea symptoms subside.

4. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with diarrhea because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water, creating a protective coating for the digestive tract.

This coating can help to alleviate irritation in the gut, reducing the frequency and urgency of bowel movements. Additionally, the gel-like substance can help to bind stools together, slowing down the speed at which they pass through the intestines.

Slippery elm tea may also help to soothe inflammation in the gut, which can contribute to the development of diarrhea. Furthermore, it is believed that slippery elm may have antibacterial properties, which could help to eliminate any harmful bacteria that may be contributing to diarrhea.

However, it is important to note that while slippery elm tea may be helpful for managing diarrhea, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in cases of severe or chronic diarrhea.

The following procedure explains how to make slippery elm tea for diarrhea.

  1. To make the slippery elm tea, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark to the boiling water.
  2. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes to allow the active compounds to be extracted from the bark.
  3. After simmering, remove the pot from the heat and let it cool down for a few minutes before straining the tea through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the bark particles.
  4. To enhance the flavor and benefits of the tea, consider adding a slice of lemon and a teaspoon of honey before drinking it.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, 2-3 times a day, between meals, to help alleviate diarrhea and soothe your digestive system.

5. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with diarrhea because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a gel-like consistency when mixed with water.

This gel-like substance can help soothe the inflammation in the gut, slowing down the digestion process and thereby reducing the frequency of diarrhea. The following procedure explains how to make marshmallow root tea for diarrhea.

  1. To make marshmallow root tea for diarrhea, bring 1 quart of water to a boil, then add 2-4 tablespoons of dried marshmallow root and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. After simmering, turn off the heat and cover the pot, allowing the tea to steep for 30 minutes to an hour for optimal extraction of its medicinal properties.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a mug or teapot, discarding the spent marshmallow root.
  4. For diarrhea relief, drink 3 cups of marshmallow root tea daily, sipping it slowly throughout the day, and store any leftover tea in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
  5. If symptoms persist for more than a few days or worsen, consult a healthcare professional for further evaluation and treatment.

10. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea helps with diarrhea because it contains cinnamaldehyde, a compound with antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation.

Additionally, cinnamon is known for its astringent qualities, which can help slow down the movement of the intestines and reduce the frequency of bowel movements. Furthermore, cinnamon tea can also help restore lost electrolytes and fluids due to diarrhea, thanks to its hydrating properties. Moreover, it has been suggested that cinnamon may help inhibit the growth of certain types of bacteria that can cause diarrhea, providing an additional layer of protection.

Lastly, cinnamon tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to other beverages, making it a gentle and soothing option for those experiencing diarrhea.

The following procedure explains how to make cinnamon tea for diarrhea.

  1. In a pot, boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of cinnamon powder or a small piece of cinnamon stick, then let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let the cinnamon-infused water steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove any cinnamon pieces and add the juice of half a lemon and 1-2 teaspoons of honey for taste and added benefits.
  4. Mix well and warm up the cinnamon tea if it has cooled down, ensuring it's still warm but not too hot to drink.
  5. Sip the cinnamon tea slowly, ideally between meals, to help soothe your stomach and alleviate diarrhea symptoms.
