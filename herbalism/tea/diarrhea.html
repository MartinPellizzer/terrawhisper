10 Best Herbal Teas For Diarrhea

Herbal teas, particularly those made from chamomile, peppermint, and ginger, can help alleviate diarrhea due to their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These herbs can help soothe the gastrointestinal tract, reducing inflammation and irritation that can contribute to diarrhea. Additionally, they may help slow down the rate of digestion, allowing the body to absorb more water and nutrients from food, which can help solidify stools. The astringent properties of some herbal teas, such as those made from blackberry leaves or raspberry leaves, can help tighten and tone the intestinal tissue, reducing the frequency and urgency of bowel movements.

Furthermore, herbal teas can provide hydration, which is essential for preventing dehydration caused by diarrhea.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with diarrhea because it has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help reduce inflammation in the intestines and alleviate cramping and spasms in the intestinal wall, which can help slow down bowel movements.

Additionally, chamomile contains tannins, which have astringent properties that can help tighten intestinal tissue and reduce the flow of fluids into the intestines, thereby helping to solidify stools. Chamomile may also help to relax the smooth muscles of the intestines, which can help reduce the frequency and urgency of diarrhea. Furthermore, chamomile has antibacterial properties that may help eliminate harmful bacteria that can cause diarrhea, while promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria in the gut.

However, it is important to note that while chamomile tea may help alleviate mild cases of diarrhea, it is not a substitute for medical treatment, especially in severe or chronic cases.

  2. Chamazulene: This aromatic compound has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in the management of diarrhea caused by gastrointestinal inflammation.
  3. Apigenin: This flavonoid has antispasmodic and antidiarrheal properties, helping to relieve smooth muscle spasms in the intestines and reduce the frequency of diarrheal episodes.
  4. Flavonoids: These plant metabolites have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in the reduction of intestinal inflammation that can lead to diarrhea.
  5. Tannins: These polyphenolic compounds have astringent properties, helping to alleviate diarrhea by reducing intestinal inflammation and promoting the formation of a protective barrier in the gut.

The following list reveals what parts of chamomile are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for diarrhea.

  1. Flowers: The chamomile flowers are used in medicinal tea for diarrhea because they contain flavonoids and terpenoids that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which can help reduce intestinal inflammation and cramping.
  2. Leaves: Chamomile leaves are included in the tea due to their high content of tannins, which can help alleviate diarrhea by reducing intestinal contractions and irritation.
  3. Seeds: The seeds of the chamomile plant are used in the tea to take advantage of their essential oils, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, that have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help eliminate the cause of diarrhea and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for diarrhea.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle or pot, bringing it to a rolling boil.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the chamomile flowers from the tea.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day between meals, as needed.
