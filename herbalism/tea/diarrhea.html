10 best herbal teas for diarrhea

"For those seeking a more natural approach to alleviating diarrhea, certain herbal teas can be a soothing remedy.

Chamomile tea is known for its anti-inflammatory properties which can help reduce intestinal inflammation. Peppermint tea is another popular choice as it relaxes the gastrointestinal muscles, thereby reducing spasms that exacerbate diarrhea. Ginger tea has been used traditionally to treat various stomach ailments, including diarrhea, due to its carminative effects.

However, it's always advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new treatment regimen.".

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea relieves diarrhea due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which stem from its chief compounds, including bisabolol oxides and flavonoids.

These components reduce inflammation in the intestines, thereby alleviating irritation and diminishing the frequency of bowel movements. Furthermore, chamomile tea's antispasmodic effect relaxes the smooth muscle lining of the intestines, decreasing instances of involuntary muscle contractions that cause diarrhea. As a result, chamomile tea's soothing qualities and active components provide a natural, reliable method to manage diarrhea.

Lastly, chamomile tea's astringent properties, derived from tannins, help solidify stools by constricting body tissues and reducing fluid leakage into the intestines, thereby controlling diarrhea and promoting normal bowel movements.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with diarrhea.

  • Flowers: The chamomile flowers contain volatile oils and flavonoids that possess anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, helping to relieve diarrhea by reducing intestinal inflammation and spasms.
  • Leaves: Rich in phenolic compounds, chamomile leaves help alleviate diarrhea symptoms by providing astringent and antibacterial effects, which can help soothe the gastrointestinal tract and support healthy digestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for diarrhea.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for 2 minutes.
  2. Pour the hot water into a cup with 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers.
  3. Steep the chamomile flowers for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  4. Strain the chamomile flowers and discard.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea warm, up to three times a day, for diarrhea relief.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea relieves diarrhea due to its antispasmodic properties, which reduce the contractions of the gastrointestinal muscles.

This herbal tea contains menthol, a compound that boasts therapeutic qualities and helps manage irritable bowel syndrome symptoms, such as diarrhea. Moreover, peppermint oil, found in peppermint tea, inhibits the growth of harmful bacteria in the intestines, promoting a healthier gut and aiding in resolving diarrhea. Furthermore, peppermint tea alleviates abdominal pain and discomfort associated with diarrhea, ensuring quicker recovery.

Lastly, the soothing warmth of peppermint tea relaxes the body, encourages hydration, and gently flushes out the gastrointestinal tract, further assisting in managing diarrhea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with diarrhea.

  • Leaves: Rich in volatile oils, the leaves of the peppermint plant are used to make medicinal tea for diarrhea due to their antispasmodic properties.
  • Flowers: Packed with flavonoids, peppermint flowers make an effective addition to medicinal tea for diarrhea, thanks to their ability to soothe the digestive system.
  • Seeds: A natural source of menthol, peppermint seeds are used to make medicinal tea for diarrhea, as they help alleviate gastrointestinal symptoms and reduce inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for diarrhea.

  1. In a teapot, add 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves or 1 peppermint tea bag for every 8 ounces of water.
  2. Pour boiling water into the teapot and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Cover the teapot to retain heat and essential oils from the peppermint.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used leaves or tea bag.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day, as needed for diarrhea relief.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea offers relief from diarrhea due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties, which stem from its active compounds, gingerols and shogaols.

These substances work by relaxing the intestinal muscles, thereby reducing contractions and allowing for more normal bowel movements. Moreover, ginger tea's ability to inhibit gastric motility aids in controlling diarrhea by decreasing the frequency of bowel movements. Additionally, ginger's antimicrobial properties help combat potential infections causing diarrhea.

Lastly, ginger tea's soothing nature helps alleviate associated discomfort and pain.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with diarrhea.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are a popular choice for making medicinal tea to treat diarrhea due to their antiemetic and carminative properties, which help to soothe the digestive system and alleviate symptoms.
  • Roots: Ginger roots contain active compounds such as gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, making them useful for treating diarrhea caused by gastrointestinal inflammation.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds are rich in essential oils and antioxidants, which support digestion and reduce inflammation, making them effective for managing diarrhea symptoms.
  • Leaves: Ginger leaves have antispasmodic and sedative properties, which help to relax the intestinal muscles and reduce cramping, providing relief from diarrhea.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, which help to eliminate harmful bacteria and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, making them useful for treating diarrhea caused by bacterial infections.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for diarrhea.

  1. Peel and chop 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root into small slices.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil, then add ginger slices and reduce heat to low.
  3. Simmer ginger in water, covered, for 15-20 minutes to release flavors and health benefits.
  4. Strain the ginger tea into a mug, discarding the ginger pieces.
  5. Squeeze half a lemon and add honey to taste, then sip the ginger tea warm to soothe diarrhea symptoms.

4. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides relief for diarrhea due to its demulcent and emollient properties, which mean it has a soothing and softening effect.

This tree's inner bark contains mucilage, a substance that turns into a slick gel when mixed with water, creating a protective coating in the digestive tract. This coating helps to alleviate irritation in the gut, slow down bowel movements, and decrease the frequency of diarrhea. By adding bulk to stools, slippery elm tea helps restore normal bowel function and reduces the discomfort associated with diarrhea.

Overall, the gel formed by slippery elm tea provides a shielding barrier in the digestive system, promoting healing and providing gentle, effective relief for diarrhea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with diarrhea.

  • Bark: The bark of the slippery elm plant contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water. This gel can help alleviate irritation in the gastrointestinal tract, thereby controlling diarrhea.
  • Inner Bark: The inner bark is rich in tannins that have astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and providing relief from diarrhea.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the slippery elm plant contain high amounts of mucilage, which can help soothe and protect the digestive tract, providing relief from diarrhea symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for diarrhea.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark.
  2. Place the bark in a cup or teapot.
  3. Pour boiling water over the bark, covering it by 1 inch.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain out the bark and drink the tea while it's still warm.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic raspberry tea for diarrhea.

  1. In a small pot, combine 1 cup of fresh raspberries and 2 cups of water, then bring the mixture to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the raspberries to release their antioxidants and soothing properties.
  3. Strain the mixture to remove raspberry solids, then add 1 teaspoon of honey and 1 teaspoon of lemon juice for flavor and additional benefits.
  4. Mix well and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Consume the raspberry tea, ideally sipping it slowly throughout the day for best results in relieving diarrhea.

  2. Strain the blackberry mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot, pressing on solids to extract all liquid.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers and 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary leaves to the blackberry juice in the teapot.
  4. Pour boiling water over the herbs in the teapot, cover, and let steep for 15 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding solids, and drink warm up to three times a day between meals for diarrhea relief.

Oak bark tea aids diarrhea due to tannins, naturally occurring compounds in the bark.

Tannins possess astringent properties, which help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. This leads to minimized intestinal spasms, a common cause of diarrhea. Furthermore, tannins' ability to convert proteins into molecules that can form a protective film in the intestines helps control bowel movement.

Lastly, this film formed by tannins also restricts the growth of bacteria responsible for some cases of diarrhea, contributing to oak bark tea's effectiveness in treating this condition.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in oak bark tea that help with diarrhea.

  • Bark: The inner cork layer of oak bark contains tannins that have astringent properties, helping to alleviate diarrhea by reducing inflammation in the gut.
  • Roots: Oak roots are rich in various compounds including ellagitannins, which have been found to have anti-diarrheal effects.
  • Leaves: Containing gallotannins, oak leaves can help treat diarrhea through their astringent properties, reducing intestinal irritation and mucous membrane inflammation.
  • Rhyzomes: Similar to roots, rhyzomes contain therapeutic substances like tannins, effective in managing diarrhea by constricting the intestines and decreasing intestinal secretions.
  • Seeds: Although less common, oak seeds may also help treat diarrhea due to their astringent qualities derived from tannins present in them.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic oak bark tea for diarrhea.

  1. Collect oak bark: Gather 2-3 pieces of dry oak bark, each about 3-4 inches long, from a mature oak tree.
  2. Clean bark: Rinse the bark under cold running water to remove any dirt or debris, then let it air dry.
  3. Shave bark: Using a knife, shave the outer layer of the bark into thin strips or small pieces.
  4. Steep tea: Place the shaved oak bark in a pot, add 2 cups of water, and bring to a low simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Strain and serve: Strain the tea into a cup, let it cool down, and drink 1/2 cup of the tea, 3 times a day, until diarrhea subsides.

Cinnamon tea combats diarrhea due to its astringent properties, which stem from tannins, a type of polyphenol compound.

These tannins work to shrink and tighten bodily tissues, including intestinal walls, reducing the frequency of stools and their looseness. Moreover, cinnamon's essential oils, particularly cinnamaldehyde, exhibit antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects, helping to alleviate diarrhea caused by infectious agents or inflammation. Furthermore, cinnamon's essential oils stimulate the gastrointestinal tract, enhancing digestion and absorption processes, preventing fluids from accumulating in the intestines.

Additionally, cinnamon tea's warmth increases blood circulation, promoting nutrient and oxygen delivery to the intestines, facilitating the healing of damaged tissues and the normalization of bowel movements.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in cinnamon tea that help with diarrhea.

  • Bark: The bark of the cinnamon tree is used for medicinal tea for diarrhea due to its tannins content, which has astringent properties.
  • Seeds: Cinnamon seeds are used in medicinal tea for diarrhea because of their essential oils and antimicrobial properties.
  • Leaves: Cinnamon leaves are used in medicinal tea for diarrhea as they contain eugenol and cinnamaldehyde, which have antispasmodic and antibacterial effects.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic cinnamon tea for diarrhea.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of bruised cinnamon sticks, letting it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. In a separate cup, steep 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers in hot water for 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the cinnamon sticks from the first mixture and combine it with the chamomile tea.
  4. Add 1 teaspoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice for taste, stirring well.
  5. Consume the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day, as needed.
