10 best herbal teas for good health

Herbal teas are a great option for those seeking a natural and healthy beverage.

Some of the best herbal teas for good health include chamomile, which is known for its calming properties and ability to aid digestion; peppermint, which can help with digestive issues and freshen breath; ginger, which has anti-inflammatory properties and can help with nausea; and rooibos, which is high in antioxidants and may help boost the immune system. Echinacea tea is also a popular choice for fighting off colds and boosting the immune system.

Drinking herbal teas not only helps to keep you hydrated, but also provides a variety of health benefits, making them a great addition to any daily routine.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea contains essential oils and antioxidants that actively contribute to good health.

Its antioxidants, including rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, possess anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the body. Furthermore, peppermint tea's rosmarinic acid exhibits allergy-relieving properties, making it beneficial for those who suffer from seasonal allergies. Additionally, its menthol content provides natural relief for digestive issues by relaxing gastrointestinal muscles, thereby alleviating symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome.

Lastly, the soothing warmth of peppermint tea aids in stress reduction, promoting overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with good health.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, peppermint leaves produce a calming tea that aids in digestion and relieves headaches.
  • Flowers: High in antioxidants, peppermint flowers create a soothing tea that helps boost the immune system and alleviates symptoms of cold and cough.
  • Stems: Containing rosmarinic acid, peppermint stems produce a healing tea that reduces inflammation and offers relaxation.
  • Seeds: Packed with vitamins and minerals, peppermint seeds contribute to a nutritious tea that promotes overall well-being and energy.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for good health.

  1. Measure - Boil 1 cup of water and let 1 peppermint tea bag steep for 5-7 minutes.
  2. Select - Choose organically grown peppermint leaves, if using loose leaf tea, measure out 1 teaspoon.
  3. Steep - Submerge tea bag or loose leaves in just-boiled water, then cover and allow to steep.
  4. Time - Steep for the full 5-7 minutes to ensure optimal release of peppermint oils.
  5. Remove - Carefully take out tea bag or strain loose leaves before drinking.

2. Chamomile

Chamomile tea, known for its daisy-like flowers, brings a range of benefits to those who consume it regularly.

Its high concentration of antioxidants combats harmful free radicals, promoting cellular health and reducing the risk of chronic diseases. Chamomile tea's soothing properties stem from the compound bisabolol, which boasts anti-inflammatory and sedative effects, aiding in digestion and promoting better sleep.

Furthermore, this aromatic tea demonstrates antibacterial qualities, making it an ideal beverage to bolster the immune system and defend against infections. Chamomile tea even addresses skin concerns, as topical application of its cooled brew or chamomile extract soothes irritations, acne, and even accelerates wound healing.

Thus, chamomile tea indisputably contributes to overall well-being by providing antioxidants, aiding digestion, supporting the immune system, and improving skin health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with good health.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers are rich in flavonoids and volatile oils, making them ideal for promoting sleep and digestion.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain antioxidants that support the immune system and help reduce inflammation.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are a great source of essential fatty acids, which can improve heart health and alleviate skin conditions.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for good health.

  1. Measure - Pour 1 cup of water into a teapot or saucepan.
  2. Heat - Bring water to a boil for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add - Place 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers into a tea infuser or teapot.
  4. Steep - Pour boiling water over the chamomile and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain - Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent chamomile flowers.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea's beneficial effects on health stem from its antioxidant properties and ability to boost the immune system.

The ginger root contains gingerols and shogaols, compounds with powerful anti-inflammatory qualities that help alleviate pain and reduce inflammation. Furthermore, ginger tea aids in digestion by stimulating the production of digestive enzymes, making it an effective remedy for nausea, indigestion, and other gastrointestinal issues. Additionally, ginger tea's warming properties promote blood circulation, which can help decrease congestion and relieve respiratory symptoms.

Lastly, ginger tea has been shown to help manage blood sugar levels, contributing to overall metabolic health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with good health.

  • Rhyzomes: These underground stems contain beneficial compounds that aid in digestion and fight inflammation when consumed as a tea.
  • Roots: Rich in gingerols and shaogals, these underground portions help alleviate nausea and vomiting, making them a popular choice for medicinal tea.
  • Leaves: Packed with antioxidants, these aerial parts support immune function and offer a refreshing taste in teas.
  • Flowers: High in antioxidants, these vibrant additions to tea blends protect cells from damage and promote overall well-being.
  • Seeds: Although rarely used in teas, these tiny packages store energy for future growth; when consumed, they provide vital nutrients that support heart health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for good health.

  1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, then combine with 4 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat, and add 2 teaspoons of honey and juice from half a lemon.
  4. Strain the tea into cups, discarding the ginger pieces.
  5. Serve and enjoy your warm ginger tea for good health.

4. Echinacea

Echinacea tea contains antioxidants and phenols that strengthen the immune system, offering protection against damaging free radicals.

This tea's anti-inflammatory properties alleviate symptoms of various conditions, such as reducing pain and swelling. Furthermore, echinacea tea's ability to soothe throats and ease congestion makes it a popular remedy for cold and flu symptoms. Additionally, this tea's antimicrobial effects combat infections, promoting good health.

Lastly, echinacea tea supports gut health by encouraging the growth of beneficial bacteria, contributing to a robust immune response.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in echinacea tea that help with good health.

  • Roots: The roots of the echinacea plant contain medicinal properties that help in boosting the immune system.
  • Rhyzomes: Rhyzomes of echinacea have anti-inflammatory properties that aid in soothing respiratory issues.
  • Flowers: Echinacea flowers are rich in antioxidants, making them effective for fighting against harming free radicals in the body.
  • Buds: Buds contain essential oils that can stimulate the immune system and have mild pain-relieving effects.
  • Seeds: Seeds of echinacea contain lipids that can moisturize and sooth dry and irritated skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic echinacea tea for good health.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried echinacea flowers or 4 tablespoons of fresh echinacea flowers.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove plant material, reserving the liquid.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for best results.

5. Lavender

Lavender tea's soothing qualities stem from its relaxing scent and therapeutic properties, which directly impact the nervous system, promoting a sense of calm and alleviating anxiety.

This calming effect, in turn, helps improve sleep quality, allowing the body to rejuvenate and maintain overall health. Furthermore, lavender tea contains antioxidants that aid in cellular damage prevention, strengthening the immune system. Additionally, lavender tea boasts anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the body and potentially alleviate symptoms of various inflammatory conditions.

Lastly, lavender tea's antimicrobial effects contribute to gut health by restricting the growth of harmful bacteria, thereby facilitating digestion and bolstering the immune system.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in lavender tea that help with good health.

  • Flowers: Lavender tea's calming properties come from its rich blend of fragrant flowers, known for their soothing effects on both body and mind.
  • Leaves: The antioxidant-rich lavender leaves help to protect cells from damage, making them an essential part of the medicinal tea.
  • Buds: Lavender buds contain the highest concentration of active compounds, offering immense health benefits and giving tea its unique, potent taste.
  • Stems: The stems of the lavender plant add structural support to the tea blend, while also releasing beneficial compounds during infusion, aiding in the overall health impact.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lavender tea for good health.

  1. In a small pot, boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of dried culinary lavender flowers, then reduce heat to low.
  3. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes, ensuring a strong lavender flavor.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the lavender flowers, preserving only the liquid.
  5. Serve the lavender tea hot and enjoy its calming and potentially health-boosting properties.

6. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea contains antioxidants that contribute to overall health by neutralizing harmful free radicals in the body.

This soothing tea also boasts calming properties, supporting relaxation and occasional sleep disturbances. Furthermore, lemon balm tea encourages a healthy response to stress through its stress-relieving compounds. Additionally, this tea has antimicrobial qualities, helping maintain a balanced microbiome.

Finally, lemon balm tea promotes digestion due to its carminative effects, aiding in the reduction of gas and bloating.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in lemon balm tea that help with good health.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, leaves are used for their soothing effects on digestion and nerves.
  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids that help reduce inflammation, making them ideal for a calming tea.
  • Stems: Provide necessary oils and beneficial compounds, contributing to the tea's balancing properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemon balm tea for good health.

  1. Harvest 2 teaspoons of fresh lemon balm leaves or use 1 teaspoon of dried leaves.
  2. Rinse the lemon balm leaves under cold water to remove any dirt or impurities.
  3. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for a minute or two.
  4. Place the lemon balm leaves in a teapot or infuser and pour the hot water over them.
  5. Steep the tea for 5-7 minutes, then strain and enjoy.

7. Licorice

Licorice tea, derived from the root of the Glycyrrhiza glabra plant, offers several health benefits.

First, this tea provides antioxidants that combat free radicals, contributing to overall well-being. Second, licorice tea soothes digestive issues by reducing inflammation in the stomach and intestines.

Additionally, it helps maintain a healthy immune system by boosting cortisol levels in the body. Furthermore, licorice tea eases throat irritations and coughs due to its demulcent and expectorant properties.

Finally, the tea promotes cardiovascular health by regulating cholesterol and blood pressure, contributing to long-term wellness.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with good health.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice plant contain glycyrrhizin, which helps soothe throat irritation and boosts the immune system.
  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of the licorice plant have anti-inflammatory properties that aid in reducing inflammation and relieving pain.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the licorice plant are rich in flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the body against damage from free radicals.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the licorice plant contain essential oils that have calming and sedative effects, helping to reduce stress and promote relaxation.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the licorice plant are a natural laxative, helping to relieve constipation and promote digestive health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for good health.

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root, discarding the solid parts.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Serve the tea while warm, up to twice a day for good health.

8. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea contains silymarin, a potent antioxidant that guards cells against damaging free radicals, promoting overall health.

This tea's silymarin fortifies liver cells, enhancing their capacity to regenerate and shielding the liver from toxins. Moreover, milk thistle tea boasts anti-inflammatory properties, soothing internal inflammation and supporting good health. Additionally, it demonstrates notable benefits for cardiovascular health by inhibiting the accumulation of plaque in arteries and promoting healthy blood pressure levels.

Milk thistle tea also showcases anticancer properties, discouraging cancer cell growth and fostering a stronger immune system.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in milk thistle tea that help with good health.

  • Seeds: The seeds of milk thistle are rich in a flavonoid compound called silymarin, which is well-known for its antioxidant properties and liver-protecting benefits, making it an excellent choice for medicinal tea.
  • Leaves: Milk thistle leaves are a powerhouse of antioxidants, helping to support healthy cell function and offering protective qualities that contribute to overall good health.
  • Flowers: The bright purple flowers of milk thistle contain bioactive compounds that aid in maintaining a healthy immune system, providing a floral touch to a soothing medicinal tea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic milk thistle tea for good health.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a teapot or saucepan for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of crushed milk thistle seeds to an infuser or teapot strainer.
  3. Pour the hot water over the milk thistle seeds and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the infuser or strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea immediately, up to three times a day for best results.

9. Nettle

Nettle tea provides numerous benefits for good health due to its rich nutritional content.

It contains essential vitamins, such as A, C, and K, as well as several B vitamins, which support overall health. Nettle tea also provides minerals like calcium, iron, magnesium, and potassium, promoting bone, muscle, and nerve function.

Its anti-inflammatory properties, derived from antioxidants like quercetin and kaempferol, help reduce inflammation and improve immune response. Nettle tea's diuretic effect aids in detoxification, supporting kidney function and maintaining healthy blood pressure levels.

Furthermore, it relieves seasonal allergy symptoms by inhibiting histamine release, alleviating congestion and irritation.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in nettle tea that help with good health.

  • Stems: The stem of the nettle plant contains valuable constituents that have documented therapeutic effects, making it an ideal addition to medicinal tea for good health.
  • Leaves: Rich in essential nutrients, the leaves of the nettle plant are a popular choice for making medicinal tea, promoting overall well-being.
  • Flowers: Packed with antioxidants, the flowers of the nettle plant contribute to the health benefits of medicinal tea, supporting the body's defenses against damage.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the nettle plant offer a unique set of medicinal properties, contributing to the effectiveness of medicinal tea for maintaining and improving health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic nettle tea for good health.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh nettle leaves, rinse them thoroughly, and let them air dry.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot, then add the dried nettle leaves and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, allowing the nutrients from the nettle leaves to infuse into the water.
  4. Strain the tea into a teapot or cup, discarding the used nettle leaves.
  5. Serve the nettle tea hot, optionally adding honey or lemon for flavor, and enjoy daily for good health.

10. Red Clover

Red clover tea contains isoflavones, plant compounds that possess estrogen-like properties, which benefit women by reducing menopause symptoms like hot flashes and night sweats.

This tea also boasts antioxidants, including tocopherol and cinnamic acid, that protect the body from oxidative stress and disease. Furthermore, red clover tea demonstrates potential in supporting cardiovascular health, as it helps lower bad cholesterol levels and thin the blood, reducing the risk of dangerous clots. Additionally, isoflavones in the tea exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, which can alleviate symptoms of osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis.

Lastly, red clover tea's isoflavone content may help prevent certain cancers, like prostate, endometrial, and breast cancers, by inhibiting tumor growth.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in red clover tea that help with good health.

  • Flowers: Rich in isoflavones, they possess estrogenic properties beneficial for treating symptoms of menopause.
  • Leaves: High in minerals and vitamins, they aid in supporting the immune system and promoting overall health.
  • Stems: Contain anti-inflammatory compounds, helpful in soothing symptoms of respiratory ailments and skin irritations.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic red clover tea for good health.

  1. In a stove-top pan, bring 4 cups of water to a boil, then add 1 tablespoon of dried red clover flowers.
  2. Reduce heat to low, cover the pan, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a teapot or a bowl, separating the liquid from the plant material.
  4. Optionally, add honey or lemon to taste while the tea is still hot.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for best results.
