10 best herbal teas for gallstones

Gallstones are small, hard deposits that form in the gallbladder, a small organ located beneath the liver.

While there is no proven cure for gallstones, certain herbal teas may help support gallbladder health and alleviate symptoms. Among the best herbal teas for gallstones are dandelion, milk thistle, and chicory root tea.

Dandelion tea is known to promote bile production and flow, which can help break down gallstones. Milk thistle tea contains silymarin, a compound that supports liver and gallbladder function. Chicory root tea, on the other hand, is a caffeine-free alternative to coffee that can aid in digestion and reduce inflammation in the gallbladder.

However, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedy, especially if you have a history of gallbladder or liver problems.

1. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea contains silymarin, a flavonoid known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

This compound helps promote the generation of new liver cells while shielding existing ones from damage, thus aiding in overall liver function. Improved liver performance leads to better bile production, which reduces the risk of gallstone formation. Moreover, milk thistle tea's bile-thinning effect helps prevent gallstone growth, as thicker bile is more prone to creating gallstones.

Lastly, milk thistle's choleretic properties stimulate the production and flow of bile, further diminishing the likelihood of gallstone development.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in milk thistle tea that help with gallstones.

  • Seeds: The seeds of the milk thistle plant are used to make medicinal tea for gallstones due to their high concentration of a flavonoid compound called silymarin, which is known to protect the liver and gallbladder.
  • Leaves: Milk thistle leaves are a good source of antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory properties, making them useful for treating gallstones.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the milk thistle plant contain bioflavonoids, which can help to increase bile flow and improve gallbladder function.
  • Rhyzomes: Milk thistle rhyzomes have been used in traditional medicine for their hepatoprotective and cholagogue properties, making them useful for treating gallstones.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic milk thistle tea for gallstones.

  1. In a stove-top pan, add 1 cup of water and bring to a boil.
  2. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of crushed milk thistle seeds, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove seeds, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of milk thistle tea daily, ideally between meals, for best results in supporting gallbladder health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in dandelion tea that help with gallstones.

  • Leaves: Packed with nutrients, dandelion leaves encourage healthy digestion, assisting in gallstone prevention.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for gallstones.

  1. Pick 2-3 cups of fresh dandelion flowers, making sure they haven't been treated with pesticides, and rinse them gently.
  2. Submerge the flowers in 3 cups of boiling water, then let them steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture to remove solid particles, preserving the liquid as the tea.
  4. Optional step: Add honey or lemon for flavor, but avoid adding milk or sugar.
  5. Consume 3 cups of this tea daily, preferably before meals, for best results in supporting gallstone treatment.

Peppermint tea eases gallstone pain by relaxing the muscles of the bile ducts, allowing stones to pass more smoothly.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with gallstones.

  • Flowers: Containing anti-inflammatory agents, peppermint flowers help alleviate inflammation in the biliary tract, reducing pain from gallstones.
  • Seeds: Possessing high lipid content, peppermint seeds provide necessary fats that assist in the digestion of bile, supporting overall gallbladder health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for gallstones.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves with 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the peppermint leaves in hot water for 5-10 minutes to allow the beneficial compounds to be released.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the spent peppermint leaves, preventing unwanted particles from entering the final drink.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature, as very hot beverages may irritate the digestive system.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of peppermint tea daily, between meals, to potentially help manage gallstones and associated symptoms.

4. Turmeric

Turmeric tea contains curcumin, a substance with documented anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These properties make it effective in alleviating gallstone pain by reducing inflammation in the gallbladder and relaxing the bile ducts. Moreover, curcumin helps stimulate bile production, aiding in breaking down dietary fats and preventing gallstone formation. Additionally, turmeric's antioxidant attributes protect the liver and gallbladder from damage, further lowering the risk of gallstone development.

Lastly, this herbal tea possesses cholesterol-lowering abilities, which is crucial since high cholesterol levels contribute to gallstone formation.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in turmeric tea that help with gallstones.

  • Rhizomes: These underground stems of turmeric contain curcumin, which has been shown to aid in the dissolution of gallstones.
  • Leaves: The leaves of turmeric contain essential oils and antioxidants that support liver function and help in the prevention of gallstone formation.
  • Seeds: Turmeric seeds are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that can help reduce inflammation and pain associated with gallstones.
  • Roots: The roots of turmeric, also known as turmeric fingers, have been used in traditional medicine to treat gallstones due to their ability to increase bile production and help in the disintegration of gallstones.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for gallstones.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 4 cups of water, 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric, and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper. (Preparation time: 5 minutes)
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes. (Steep time: 10 minutes)
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or mug.
  4. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey or maple syrup, and a squeeze of lemon juice to taste. (Customization time: 1 minute)
  5. Serve hot and enjoy up to three cups per day. (Serving time: Immediate)

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic borotutu bark tea for gallstones.

  1. Peel and chop the bark of a Borotutu tree root (20 grams) into small pieces, rinse and place in a pot.
  2. Pour cold water (1 liter) into the pot, bring to a boil and then reduce heat to a simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea, discard the solid remains and let the tea cool down.
  4. Drink one cup of the Borotutu bark tea, three times a day, before meals.
  5. Store the remaining tea in the refrigerator for up to three days.
