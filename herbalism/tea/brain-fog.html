10 Best Herbal Teas For Brain Fog

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients like ginkgo biloba, ginseng, and peppermint, are known to improve brain function and help reduce brain fog.

This is because these herbs contain compounds that increase blood flow to the brain, improve memory and concentration, and have a calming and soothing effect on the mind. Additionally, many herbal teas have antioxidant properties that help protect the brain from damage and support overall brain health. Furthermore, staying hydrated is crucial for maintaining cognitive function, and drinking herbal tea can help meet your daily fluid intake needs.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be a helpful tool in reducing brain fog, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment or a healthy lifestyle.

1. Gotu Kola

Gotu kola tea helps with brain fog because it is rich in antioxidants and medicinal compounds, including asiaticoside and madecassoside, which have been shown to improve cognitive function and protect the brain from oxidative stress.

Additionally, gotu kola has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the brain, a common cause of brain fog. The tea also contains centella asiatica, which has been used traditionally for thousands of years to improve memory and mental clarity.

Finally, studies have shown that gotu kola can increase the production of nerve growth factors, which are essential for the growth and maintenance of neurons in the brain.

The following procedure explains how to make gotu kola tea for brain fog.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot and add 1 tablespoon of dried gotu kola leaves (also known as Centella asiatica) when the water starts to boil.
  2. Reduce heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to absorb the medicinal properties of the gotu kola.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the leaves, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential additional health benefits.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to gently hydrate and nourish your brain, potentially helping to reduce brain fog.

2. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea helps with brain fog because it is an adaptogen that can reduce stress and anxiety, two major contributors to cognitive impairment and mental confusion.

It contains antioxidants that protect the brain from oxidative damage and inflammation, which are known to contribute to brain fog. Ashwagandha also increases blood flow to the brain, delivering essential nutrients and oxygen more effectively. Furthermore, it enhances the production of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter important for memory, concentration, and cognitive function.

Finally, ashwagandha has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the brain, which is linked to various neurological conditions and cognitive decline.

The following procedure explains how to make ashwagandha tea for brain fog.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of dried ashwagandha root; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let the mixture steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the ashwagandha root pieces.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to 2-3 times a day for best results.

3. Bacopa Monnieri

Bacopa monnieri tea helps with brain fog because it is a powerful adaptogen that has been used for centuries in traditional Ayurvedic medicine to enhance cognitive function and reduce stress.

The active compounds in the tea, including bacosides A and B, work by protecting the brain from oxidative stress and increasing nerve growth factors that improve neural connectivity. Consuming this tea regularly can help improve memory, focus, and concentration, making it an effective natural remedy for individuals experiencing brain fog or cognitive decline.

Additionally, bacopa monnieri tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the brain, which is a common contributor to brain fog and other neurological disorders.

The following procedure explains how to make bacopa monnieri tea for brain fog.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a tea kettle or pot until it reaches a rolling boil.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon (about 2 grams) of dried Bacopa Monnieri leaves or 1 Bacopa teabag to a teapot or mug.
  3. Pour the hot water over the Bacopa Monnieri leaves or teabag in the teapot or mug.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  5. Remove the teabag or strain out the leaves, then enjoy the tea while warm.

4. Holy Basil

Holy basil tea helps with brain fog because it is rich in antioxidants that protect the brain from oxidative stress, which can contribute to cognitive decline and brain fog.

The adaptogenic properties of holy basil tea also help reduce stress levels, as chronic stress is a known contributor to brain fog. Holy basil tea contains essential oils such as eugenol and linalool, which have anti-inflammatory effects that can help reduce inflammation in the brain, contributing to improved cognitive function and reduced brain fog. Additionally, holy basil tea contains compounds like rosmarinic acid and catechins, which have been shown to improve memory and attention, further helping to alleviate brain fog.

Overall, the antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, stress-reducing, and cognitive-enhancing properties of holy basil tea make it an effective natural remedy for combating brain fog.

The following procedure explains how to make holy basil tea for brain fog.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried holy basil leaves (Ocimum tenuiflorum) or 2-3 fresh holy basil leaves.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes to allow the water to absorb the beneficial compounds from the holy basil.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add a small slice of lemon or a dash of lemon juice for added vitamin C and improved absorption of holy basil's active ingredients.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to gently coat your mouth and throat, and feel the soothing and clarifying effects on your mind.

5. Lion'S Mane Mushroom

Lion's mane mushroom tea helps with brain fog because it contains compounds called hericenones and erinacines, which are believed to stimulate the growth and differentiation of neurons in the hippocampus and cerebellum - areas of the brain associated with memory, learning, and cognitive function.

This can lead to improved mental clarity and focus, reducing feelings of brain fog. Moreover, lion's mane mushroom tea has antioxidant properties that help protect the brain from oxidative stress and inflammation, which are known contributors to cognitive decline and brain fog. Additionally, it contains nerve growth factor (NGF), which is essential for the maintenance, functioning, and survival of neurons, further contributing to its brain-boosting benefits.

Finally, some studies suggest that lion's mane mushroom tea may also improve mood by increasing levels of serotonin and dopamine, two neurotransmitters that play a role in regulating emotions and motivation.

The following procedure explains how to make lion's mane mushroom tea for brain fog.

  1. In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil, then add 2 tablespoons of dried Lion's Mane mushroom; reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  2. After simmering, turn off the heat and let the mushroom tea steep for an additional 10 minutes to continue drawing out the medicinal properties.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or directly into a mug, discarding the spent mushrooms.
  4. Optional: add a natural sweetener, such as 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey, to improve the palatability of the tea if desired.
  5. Sip the Lion's Mane mushroom tea slowly, allowing it to gently warm and soothe your mind, potentially helping to alleviate brain fog. Enjoy daily for best results.

6. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea helps with brain fog because it is rich in antioxidants and terpene lactones, which protect the brain cells from damage caused by free radicals.

Its flavonoids improve blood flow to the brain, enhancing the delivery of oxygen and nutrients essential for cognitive function. Additionally, ginkgo biloba tea has been shown to stimulate the production of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter crucial for memory and focus.

Lastly, its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce inflammation in the brain, contributing to improved mental clarity and reduced brain fog.

The following procedure explains how to make ginkgo biloba tea for brain fog.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried ginkgo biloba leaves (or 1-2 tea bags) to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Once the water reaches a boil, pour it over the ginkgo biloba leaves and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag, and let the tea cool down for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Pour the ginkgo biloba tea into a cup, and enjoy it up to three times a day to help alleviate brain fog.

7. Rhodiola Rosea

Rhodiola rosea tea helps with brain fog because it is an adaptogen that works by regulating cortisol levels, reducing oxidative stress, and increasing the production of certain neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine.

This herbal tea has been shown to improve cognitive function in individuals experiencing mental fatigue, as well as reduce symptoms of depression and anxiety that can contribute to brain fog.

Additionally, rhodiola rosea contains salidroside, a compound that boosts energy production within the brain and enhances communication between brain cells, further contributing to its ability to help alleviate brain fog.

The following procedure explains how to make rhodiola rosea tea for brain fog.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool down to 200-212°F (93-100°C).
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of dried Rhodiola rosea root to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the Rhodiola rosea and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the spent herb.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to 2-3 times a day as needed for brain fog relief.

8. Bael

Bael tea helps with brain fog because of its antioxidant properties, which protect the brain from oxidative stress and inflammation.

The tea contains vitamin C and other essential nutrients that support cognitive function, particularly memory and concentration. Additionally, bael tea has been shown to improve blood flow to the brain, enhancing oxygen delivery and nutrient uptake. Its calming effects on the nervous system also contribute to better mental clarity and focus.

Overall, drinking bael tea regularly can help alleviate symptoms of brain fog, improving cognitive performance and promoting overall brain health.

The following procedure explains how to make bael tea for brain fog.

  1. Bring 2 cups of water to a boil, then add 1 tablespoon of dried bael fruit leaves (also known as bilva or wood apple leaves); reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat, cover the pot, and let the tea steep for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, and discard them.
  4. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste for flavor and additional health benefits.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to 3 times a day as needed for reducing brain fog.

9. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with brain fog because it contains glycyrrhizic acid, which acts as an adaptogen by reducing stress and inflammation in the body.

This, in turn, can improve cognitive function, including attention, memory, and focus. Additionally, licorice root tea is rich in antioxidants, which help protect the brain from oxidative damage caused by free radicals. Furthermore, it has anti-inflammatory properties, making it an effective natural remedy for those experiencing brain fog due to inflammation caused by conditions such as multiple sclerosis or Lyme disease.

Lastly, licorice root tea can help regulate blood sugar levels, which is important for maintaining optimal brain function and preventing the energy crashes that can contribute to brain fog.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice root tea for brain fog.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) when the water reaches a rolling boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to extract the medicinal properties of the licorice root.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used licorice root, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add a natural sweetener like honey (to taste) if desired, as licorice root tea can be quite bitter.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your mouth and throat, and take note of any relief in brain fog symptoms. Aim to drink 1-2 cups of licorice root tea per day for best results.

10. Schisandra Berries

Schisandra berries tea helps with brain fog because it contains a unique combination of active compounds, including lignans and polyphenols, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that protect the brain from damage caused by free radicals and inflammation.

These compounds also promote improved cognitive function, memory, and focus by increasing blood flow to the brain and supporting the production of neurotransmitters such as acetylcholine and dopamine. Additionally, schisandra berries tea contains adaptogenic properties that help the body cope with stress, which is a common contributor to brain fog.

Lastly, the calming effects of this tea, due to its small amount of caffeine content and the presence of essential oils like linalool and limonene, make it an excellent choice for those looking to improve their mental clarity and overall cognitive health.

The following procedure explains how to make schisandra berries tea for brain fog.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a small pot on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried schisandra berries to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover the pot, and let it steep for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to 2-3 times a day as needed for brain fog relief.
