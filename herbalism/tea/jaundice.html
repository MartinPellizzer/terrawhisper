10 best herbal teas for jaundice

Jaundice, a condition characterized by the yellowing of the skin and eyes, is often a symptom of an underlying liver problem.

While it's important to seek medical attention for jaundice, certain herbal teas may help support liver function and aid in recovery. Milk thistle tea is renowned for its liver-protecting properties, containing silymarin, a potent antioxidant that promotes cell regeneration.

Dandelion root tea is another excellent choice, as it stimulates bile production and digestion, easing the burden on the liver. Lastly, Turmeric tea, with its active ingredient curcumin, has been shown to reduce inflammation and protect liver cells.

Before incorporating these teas into your routine, consult with a healthcare professional to ensure they're suitable for your specific condition.

1. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea aids in jaundice treatment due to its active ingredient, silymarin.

This compound possesses robust anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. By shielding the liver from damage, milk thistle tea encourages liver cell regeneration. Consequently, bile production normalizes, reducing bilirubin levels in the bloodstream.

Ultimately, milk thistle tea's hepatoprotective effects bring relief to jaundice symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in milk thistle tea that help with jaundice.

  • Seeds: The seeds of the milk thistle plant are used to make medicinal tea for jaundice due to their high concentration of silymarin, a flavonoid that is believed to support liver health.
  • Leaves: Milk thistle leaves are used in medicinal tea for jaundice because they contain beneficial compounds that can help protect and regenerate liver cells.
  • Flowers: Milk thistle flowers are used in medicinal tea for jaundice because they contain antioxidants that can help reduce oxidative stress and support liver function.
  • Rhyzomes: Milk thistle rhyzomes are used in medicinal tea for jaundice because they contain anti-inflammatory compounds that can help reduce inflammation in the liver and promote healing.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic milk thistle tea for jaundice.

  1. In a stove-top pot, pour 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried milk thistle seeds to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove milk thistle seeds and discard them.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.

2. Dandelion

Dandelion tea aids jaundice due to its proven liver-boosting properties, as confirmed by scientific research.

This tea, derived from the dandelion plant, contains taraxacum officinale, which stimulates bile production in the liver, enhancing its function. The increased bile flow promotes the elimination of waste bilirubin, reducing its levels in the bloodstream. Moreover, dandelion tea exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, further supporting liver health and combating jaundice.

Finally, consistent consumption of dandelion tea demonstrably improves liver function, effectively alleviating jaundice symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in dandelion tea that help with jaundice.

  • Roots: Rich in beneficial compounds, the roots of dandelions possess potent liver-cleansing properties that help treat jaundice.
  • Leaves: High in antioxidants, dandelion leaves support liver function, making them an ideal ingredient for jaundice-relieving tea.
  • Flowers: Packed with anti-inflammatory properties, dandelion flowers help reduce inflammation and promote recovery in jaundice patients.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for jaundice.

  1. Gather 2-3 dandelion heads and rinse them thoroughly under running water to remove dirt and insects.
  2. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot, then add the rinsed dandelion heads and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove dandelion particles, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential health benefits.
  5. Consume 2-3 times a day, as needed, to help treat jaundice symptoms, but consult a healthcare provider first for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Turmeric

Turmeric tea provides relief from jaundice due to its high curcumin content, a compound known to stimulate bile production, thereby assisting in the elimination of bilirubin from the body.

This tea's anti-inflammatory properties also help soothe the liver, reducing inflammation and aiding in the restoration of normal liver function. Moreover, turmeric's antioxidant effects protect liver cells from further damage, promoting overall liver health.

The regular consumption of turmeric tea, therefore, helps alleviate jaundice symptoms by supporting bile flow, reducing liver inflammation, and protecting liver cells.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in turmeric tea that help with jaundice.

  • Rhizomes: The underground stem of the turmeric plant contains curcumin, a potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compound, which helps in the treatment of jaundice by aiding liver function and reducing inflammation.
  • Leaves: Rich in vitamins and minerals, turmeric leaves have been used in traditional medicine to treat jaundice, as they promote bile secretion and help restore normal liver function.
  • Roots: The turmeric root is a powerful detoxifier and liver stimulant, making it an ideal ingredient for medicinal tea to treat jaundice, as it helps eliminate toxins from the body and improve overall liver health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for jaundice.

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water with 1 teaspoon of grated fresh turmeric or 1/2 teaspoon of powdered turmeric for 10 minutes.
  2. Add 1/2 inch of grated ginger (or 1/4 teaspoon of powdered ginger) and continue to boil for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, then mix in 1 teaspoon of honey and 1/2 lemon juice (optional for taste).
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 2-3 times a day, gradually increasing the amount of turmeric up to 1 tablespoon per day.

  5. Serve: Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature and consume 2-3 times a day.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic greater celandine tea for jaundice.

  3. Steep - Place the dried celandine in a tea infuser and steep in a cup of boiling water for 10-15 minutes.

  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and possess anti-inflammatory properties, chamomile flowers are used to make medicinal tea for jaundice due to their ability to promote liver health and facilitate detoxification.
  • Leaves: Containing therapeutic compounds, chamomile leaves are used in medicinal tea for jaundice because they support liver function and help reduce bilirubin levels in the blood.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for jaundice.

  1. Measure - Pour 2 cups of water into a saucepan and add 2 tablespoons of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Heat - Place the saucepan on the stove and bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Steep - Remove the saucepan from heat, cover it, and let the chamomile flowers steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Strain - Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the chamomile flowers, and let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Serve - Slowly sip the chamomile tea, aiming to drink 2-3 cups per day to help manage jaundice symptoms.
