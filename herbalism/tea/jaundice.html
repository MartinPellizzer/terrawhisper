1. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with jaundice because it contains silymarin, a flavonoid that exhibits protective and restorative properties essential for liver health.

The antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects of silymarin assist in protecting liver cells from damage, while promoting the regeneration of new ones. This aids in improving the liver's functionality and its ability to process bilirubin, a yellow pigment responsible for the symptoms of jaundice. Additionally, milk thistle tea's potential in reducing oxidative stress and fibrosis further benefits individuals suffering from jaundice, particularly those with liver conditions such as hepatitis, cirrhosis, and alcohol-related liver diseases.

However, it's crucial to consult with healthcare professionals before incorporating milk thistle tea into a treatment plan for jaundice.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in milk thistle tea that help with jaundice.

  1. Silibinin: This flavonoid ligand is the most active constituent in milk thistle, and it helps with jaundice by protecting the liver from toxins and promoting the regeneration of liver tissue.
  2. Silybin: A flavonolignan, silybin aids in the treatment of jaundice by supporting liver function and reducing oxidative stress.
  3. Silychristin: As a antioxidant, silychristin found in milk thistle helps with jaundice by protecting liver cells from damage and inflammation.
  4. Silydianin: A flavonolignan, silydianin has been shown to have hepatoprotective properties, making it beneficial in the management of jaundice.
  5. Isosilybin: This bioactive compound found in milk thistle helps with jaundice by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and liver-protecting effects.

The following list reveals what parts of milk thistle are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for jaundice.

  1. Seeds: The seeds of the milk thistle plant are used to make medicinal tea for jaundice due to their high concentration of silymarin, a flavonoid with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can help protect and repair liver cells.
  2. Leaves: The leaves of the milk thistle plant are used in medicinal tea for jaundice because they contain flavonoids and other compounds that have been shown to support liver function and promote bile production.
  3. Flowers: The flowers of the milk thistle plant are used in medicinal tea for jaundice as they contain antioxidants that can help protect the liver from damage and promote the regeneration of liver cells.

The following procedure explains how to make milk thistle tea for jaundice.

  1. In a cup, combine 1 teaspoon of crushed milk thistle seeds with 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the mixture for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the seeds, resulting in a milk thistle extract.
  4. Consume the tea, up to three times a day, preferably between meals.
  5. Continue this regimen for several weeks, unless directed otherwise by a healthcare professional.

The following procedure explains how to make turmeric tea for jaundice.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of water, 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper (which enhances turmeric's absorption); bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat, let it simmer for 10 minutes to allow the beneficial compounds to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the turmeric residue.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste (optional), but avoid adding milk as it can interfere with turmeric's absorption.
  5. Wait for the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature before consumption, ideally 2-3 times a day for relief from jaundice symptoms.
