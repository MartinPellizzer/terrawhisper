10 best herbal teas for lost voice

When you have a lost voice, sipping on certain herbal teas can help soothe a sore throat and alleviate inflammation.

One of the best herbal teas for a lost voice is slippery elm tea, as it coats and protects the throat due to its demulcent properties. Another good option is licorice root tea, known for its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.

Additionally, marshmallow root tea and chamomile tea are gentle on the throat and may help reduce irritation while promoting healing. Peppermint tea can also offer relief, as it has mild analgesic properties that can help numb throat pain.

Remember to always consult with a healthcare professional if your lost voice persists for an extended period.

1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides relief for a lost voice due to its powerful demulcent properties, which involve the extraction of mucilage from the bark.

This mucilage forms a soothing, protective film when in contact with membranes in the mouth and throat, reducing irritation. By alleviating inflammation, slippery elm tea enables the vocal cords to heal and regain their normal function more quickly. Furthermore, this herbal tea stimulates saliva production, keeping the mucous membranes moist and flexible, which is essential for a healthy and robust voice.

As a result, slippery elm tea is a time-honored natural remedy for restoring a lost voice, providing quicker recovery and comfort.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with lost voice.

  • Bark: The bark of the slippery elm plant is used to make medicinal tea for a lost voice due to its demulcent properties, which mean it has a soothing effect on irritated mucous membranes.
  • Leaves: Slippery elm leaves contain mucilage, a gel-like substance that coats and soothes inflamed throat tissues, making them suitable for medicinal tea.
  • Buds: The buds of the slippery elm plant have a high concentration of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, which help reduce irritation and promote healing in the throat.
  • Roots: The roots of the slippery elm plant are rich in nutrients and have a high mucilage content, making them useful for creating a soothing tea that coats and protects the throat.
  • Seeds: Slippery elm seeds contain mucilage, which can help soothe a sore throat and reduce inflammation, making them a valuable addition to medicinal teas.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for lost voice.

  1. Measure - Precisely measure out 1-2 teaspoons of slippery elm bark and place it in a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Boil - Bring 8 ounces of water to a boil, then pour it over the slippery elm bark.
  3. Steep - Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain - Strain the tea to remove the bark before drinking, discarding the used bark.
  5. Serve - Sip the slippery elm tea warm to soothe a sore or lost voice, ideally consuming 2-3 cups daily.

2. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea assists with a lost voice due to its demulcent properties, which involve a soothing effect on irritated mucous membranes.

This herbal tea contains mucilage, a substance that turns into a slippery gel when mixed with water, forming a protective layer over the throat's lining. By reducing friction from talking or coughing, marshmallow tea alleviates throat inflammation and irritation. Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with lost voice.

  • Leaves: Rich in mucilage, the leaves soothe inflammation and help relieve irritation in the throat.
  • Flowers: Containing anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, the flowers help reduce swelling and promote healing of sore throats.
  • Seeds: Packed with nutrients and germination stimulants, the seeds support throat health and aid in voice restoration.
  • Stems: High in mucilage and fiber, the stems provide a soothing coating to the throat, reducing irritation and hoarseness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for lost voice.

  1. Measure out 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil in a small pot.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of dried marshmallow root to the boiling water, then reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the marshmallow root solids.
  4. Optionally, add honey to taste for sweetness.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day for a sore throat.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic plantain tea for lost voice.

  2. Strain the plantain-infused water into a separate container, discarding the solids.
  3. Add the juice of 1 lemon and 1 tablespoon of honey to the plantain water, stirring until fully combined.
  4. Warm the plantain tea on low heat until it reaches a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Pour the plantain tea into a cup and enjoy up to three times a day for a soothed throat.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for lost voice.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of ginger root.

  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, then stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy the soothing benefits for your throat.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for lost voice.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 4 cups of water and 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric; bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat, cover, and let simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or mug.
  4. Stir in honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.
