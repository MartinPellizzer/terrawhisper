10 Best Herbal Teas For Adrenal Fatigue

Herbal teas can help with adrenal fatigue due to the soothing properties of certain herbs.

Adrenal fatigue is thought to occur when the adrenal glands, which produce hormones like cortisol, become overworked or fatigued. Some herbs, such as ashwagandha and rhodiola, are adaptogens, meaning they can help the body adapt to stress and potentially support adrenal function.

Licorice root is another herb that can be beneficial for adrenal fatigue, as it can help to regulate cortisol levels. Chamomile and lemon balm are also commonly used in herbal teas for adrenal fatigue due to their calming effects, which can help to reduce stress and promote relaxation.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas may be helpful for managing symptoms of adrenal fatigue, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, and individuals with adrenal fatigue should consult with a healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment.

1. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it is an adaptogen, which means it can help the body manage stress more effectively.

Adrenal fatigue is a condition characterized by chronic stress that can lead to exhaustion and various symptoms such as anxiety, depression, insomnia, and weight gain. Ashwagandha tea contains compounds called withanolides, which have been shown in studies to help reduce cortisol levels, the hormone responsible for the "stress response." Additionally, ashwagandha tea can help improve sleep quality, another common symptom of adrenal fatigue.

Lastly, regular consumption of ashwagandha tea may also help improve energy levels and cognitive function, both of which can be affected by adrenal fatigue.

The following procedure explains how to make ashwagandha tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add 1 teaspoon of dried ashwagandha root.
  2. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used herbs.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for adrenal support.

2. Holy Basil

Holy basil tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it is an adaptogenic herb known to reduce stress and anxiety, which are common causes of adrenal fatigue.

It contains essential oils such as eugenol and ursolic acid that have antioxidant properties, helping to protect the body from oxidative stress that can contribute to adrenal exhaustion. Holy basil tea also contains compounds like rosmarinic acid and catechins that help regulate cortisol levels in the body, which is crucial for adrenal health. Additionally, holy basil tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate inflammation in the body, which is a common symptom of adrenal fatigue.

Lastly, holy basil tea has been shown to improve energy levels and mental clarity, making it an effective natural remedy for those suffering from adrenal fatigue.

The following procedure explains how to make holy basil tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a saucepan, then add 1 tablespoon of dried holy basil leaves (also known as tulsi).
  2. Reduce heat to a simmer, allowing the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes for optimal extraction of beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent basil leaves.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added soothing properties and flavor enhancement.
  5. Sip slowly, enjoying the warm beverage 1-2 times a day for supporting adrenal health and combating adrenal fatigue.

3. Rhodiola

Rhodiola tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it contains adaptogens, specifically rosavins and salidroside, which have been shown to effectively normalize the production of cortisol, the primary stress hormone.

This herbal tea's ability to regulate cortisol levels contributes to reducing symptoms such as energy crashes, anxiety, and difficulty sleeping. Furthermore, rhodiola has anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate inflammation in the body, a common contributor to adrenal fatigue.

Lastly, it also enhances mental clarity and focus by improving cognitive function and decreasing brain fog, allowing those experiencing adrenal fatigue to perform daily tasks more efficiently.

The following procedure explains how to make rhodiola tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of filtered water in a saucepan, then reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried Rhodiola rosea root (depending on desired strength) to the simmering water.
  3. Allow the Rhodiola to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent Rhodiola root.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy 1-2 cups daily for adrenal support.

4. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it contains glycyrrhizic acid, which is a natural compound that mimics cortisol and supports the body's stress response system.

This adaptogenic property allows licorice root tea to help regulate cortisol levels in the body, reducing symptoms of adrenal fatigue such as energy crashes and mood swings. Additionally, licorice root tea contains various vitamins and minerals, including potassium, which is essential for maintaining healthy adrenal function. Furthermore, licorice root tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the body, which is often a contributing factor to adrenal fatigue.

Lastly, licorice root tea's calming effects on the nervous system can help promote better sleep quality, which is crucial for adrenal recovery and overall health.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice root tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a saucepan, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) and reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Allow the tea to simmer for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the licorice root.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used licorice root, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add a natural sweetener, such as honey or stevia, to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, aiming to drink 2-3 cups of licorice root tea daily, before meals or as directed by a healthcare professional.

5. Maca

Maca root tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it is rich in essential nutrients, including vitamins B, C, and E, as well as calcium, zinc, and iron.

These nutrients support the production of hormones, particularly those related to energy and stress response, such as cortisol and adrenaline. Maca root also contains adaptogens, which are natural substances that help the body cope with stress by regulating the production of stress hormones and promoting overall balance. Additionally, maca root tea may improve mood and reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression, which are common in individuals with adrenal fatigue.

Regular consumption of this tea can contribute to improved energy levels, reduced fatigue, and increased resilience to stress.

The following procedure explains how to make maca tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried maca root powder and stir well.
  2. Reduce heat to a simmer and let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid into a cup, discarding the solids.
  4. Add a natural sweetener, such as honey or stevia, to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, ideally during the morning or early afternoon, to support adrenal function.

6. Schisandra Berry

Schisandra berry tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it contains the active compounds schisandrins and lignans, which have been shown to reduce stress and improve energy levels in individuals with adrenal fatigue.

Additionally, schisandra berries are an adaptogen, meaning they help the body adapt to stressors by regulating the hormonal response. Furthermore, the antioxidant properties of schisandra berry tea help protect against oxidative damage caused by chronic stress, which can contribute to adrenal fatigue.

Lastly, the calming effects of the tea's natural flavonoids and essential oils also help promote relaxation and reduce anxiety, further supporting adrenal health.

The following procedure explains how to make schisandra berry tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of fresh water in a small pot on the stove.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried schisandra berries to the pot once the water reaches a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover the pot, and let the berries steep for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the berries, and let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Enjoy 2-3 cups of this tea daily for adrenal support, adding honey or lemon if desired.

7. Chaga Mushroom

Chaga mushroom tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it contains a rich variety of adaptogenic compounds, including polysaccharides, triterpenes, and betulinic acid.

These compounds work together to help regulate the production of cortisol, a hormone that is closely related to stress management and the functioning of the adrenal glands. By supporting a balanced cortisol level, chaga mushroom tea can alleviate the symptoms of adrenal fatigue, such as chronic fatigue, brain fog, and mood swings. Additionally, chaga mushrooms have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can further reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, thereby promoting overall adrenal health.

Lastly, chaga mushroom tea is caffeine-free, making it an ideal beverage for those seeking to reduce their caffeine intake and support adrenal recovery.

The following procedure explains how to make chaga mushroom tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Fill a pot with 4 cups of filtered water and bring to a boil.
  2. Add 1-2 tablespoons of dried chaga mushroom pieces or 1 chaga mushroom tea bag to the pot.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-30 minutes (the longer, the stronger the tea).
  4. Remove from heat, add honey or lemon to taste, and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea, discard the mushroom pieces, and enjoy your adrenal-boosting chaga tea.

8. Gynostemma

Gynostemma tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it contains adaptogens, compounds that help the body cope with stress.

These adaptogens, specifically gypenosides, work to improve cortisol regulation and reduce the impact of stress on the adrenal glands. Additionally, gynostemma tea contains magnesium and vitamin C, both essential nutrients for adrenal health. The combination of these nutrients and adaptogens helps to support the body's natural ability to manage stress, making it an effective remedy for those suffering from adrenal fatigue.

Furthermore, gynostemma tea has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the body that may contribute to adrenal fatigue symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make gynostemma tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot and add 2 tablespoons of dried Gynostemma leaves.
  2. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the nutrients to infuse into the water.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent leaves, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add raw honey or stevia to taste, if desired, for a touch of natural sweetness.
  5. Sip slowly and enjoy this soothing, adaptogenic tea 1-2 times a day, especially during periods of adrenal fatigue.

9. Passionflower

Passionflower tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it contains several nutrients that support the adrenal glands, including magnesium and antioxidants.

The flavonoids in passionflower tea have been shown to reduce stress and anxiety levels, which are common symptoms of adrenal fatigue. Passionflower tea also contains tryptophan, an amino acid that helps the body produce serotonin and melatonin, hormones essential for proper sleep and stress management. Additionally, passionflower tea has calming properties that help reduce cortisol levels in the body, allowing the adrenal glands to rest and recover from chronic stress.

Regular consumption of passionflower tea can lead to improved energy levels, better mood regulation, and overall reduced symptoms of adrenal fatigue.

The following procedure explains how to make passionflower tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried passionflower herb (or 1 passionflower tea bag) to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the passionflower and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag and discard the used passionflower.
  5. Drink the passionflower tea 2-3 times a day, as needed, to help support adrenal function and alleviate adrenal fatigue.

10. Rehmannia

Rehmannia root tea helps with adrenal fatigue because it contains compounds that can support adrenal gland function and help regulate the production of stress hormones like cortisol.

This adaptogenic herb may also help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which can contribute to adrenal fatigue. Additionally, Rehmannia root tea has been shown to have immune-boosting and anti-anemic effects, which can help to improve overall energy and vitality. Furthermore, this herbal tea may help to improve sleep quality and reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression, which are often associated with adrenal fatigue.

Finally, Rehmannia root tea has a long history of use in traditional Chinese medicine for treating kidney and adrenal disorders, and its benefits have been supported by some modern research studies.

The following procedure explains how to make rehmannia tea for adrenal fatigue.

  1. In a pot, boil 4 cups of water and add 2 tablespoons of rehmannia root.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the rehmannia root, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential adrenal support.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of this tea daily, preferably between meals, to help alleviate adrenal fatigue symptoms.
