10 Best Herbal Teas For Appetite Suppression

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients known for their appetite-suppressing qualities, can help reduce hunger and promote feelings of fullness.

Some herbs, such as hoodia and green tea, contain natural compounds that may help regulate appetite hormones, making it easier to consume fewer calories. Additionally, the warm temperature and soothing effect of herbal teas can contribute to a sense of satisfaction and fullness, reducing the desire to snack or overeat. Furthermore, herbal teas often have a diuretic effect, helping the body eliminate excess fluids and reducing bloating, which can also contribute to a slimmer appearance.

Lastly, incorporating herbal teas into a mindful routine, such as sipping tea slowly during meal times or as a replacement for high-calorie beverages, can foster a healthier relationship with food and support overall weight management goals.

1. Green Tea

Green tea helps with appetite suppression because it contains bioactive compounds that can have various beneficial effects on health, including weight loss.

One of these compounds is EGCG (Epigallocatechin Gallate), a catechin that can boost metabolism and help burn fat, particularly in the abdominal area. Additionally, green tea has been shown to reduce levels of the hormone ghrelin, which is known as the "hunger hormone" as it signals the brain when the body needs food.

Furthermore, green tea has caffeine, which can increase energy expenditure and enhance physical performance, leading to a greater calorie burn. Drinking green tea regularly may also help to regulate blood sugar levels, preventing spikes and crashes that can trigger hunger and cravings.

Therefore, incorporating green tea into a balanced diet and exercise routine can be a useful strategy for those looking to manage their weight and appetite.

The following procedure explains how to make green tea tea for appetite suppression.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool down to 175-185°F (80-85°C), then pour it over a tea bag or 1 teaspoon of loose green tea in a cup.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 2-3 minutes for a mild flavor, or up to 5 minutes for a stronger taste, ensuring to prevent bitterness by not over-steeping.
  3. Remove the tea bag or strain out the loose tea leaves, discarding them properly.
  4. Optional: add a small amount of raw honey or stevia for a touch of natural sweetness, if desired.
  5. Drink the green tea slowly, savoring the taste, and enjoy the appetite-suppressing benefits throughout the day, ideally 2-3 cups daily, between meals.

2. Oolong

Oolong tea helps with appetite suppression because it is rich in polyphenols, particularly flavonoids, which are known to reduce hunger and increase feelings of fullness.

These compounds work by promoting the production of hormones such as cholecystokinin (CCK), which signals to the brain that the stomach is satisfied. Additionally, oolong tea has a moderate amount of caffeine, which can further support appetite control by boosting metabolism and energy levels. The antioxidants in oolong tea may also play a role in suppressing appetite, as they help regulate blood sugar levels and insulin resistance.

Furthermore, the soothing and calming effects of drinking oolong tea can contribute to a more mindful and balanced eating experience, making it easier to tune into natural hunger and fullness cues.

The following procedure explains how to make oolong tea for appetite suppression.

  1. Boil 16 ounces of fresh, filtered water and allow it to cool down to 190-205 degrees Fahrenheit, then pour it over 1 teaspoon of high-quality oolong tea leaves in a teapot or infuser.
  2. Steep the tea for 3-5 minutes, depending on desired strength, as oolong tea leaves are delicate and can become bitter if steeped for too long.
  3. Remove the tea leaves from the teapot or infuser to prevent over-steeping and ruining the taste or benefits.
  4. For appetite suppression, drink 2-3 cups of oolong tea per day, sipping slowly and mindfully before or between meals.
  5. For optimal results, avoid adding sweeteners or milk, as they can interfere with the tea's natural ability to suppress appetite and aid in weight loss.

3. Yerba Mate

Yerba mate tea helps with appetite suppression because it contains a variety of nutrients and compounds, including caffeine, that can help reduce hunger.

The caffeine in yerba mate tea stimulates the nervous system, which sends direct signals to the fat cells to break down fat, releasing energy and promoting feelings of fullness. Additionally, yerba mate has been shown to increase the release of certain hormones, such as cholecystokinin (CCK), that help regulate appetite and satiety. Furthermore, yerba mate's bitter taste can also help slow down the consumption of food, allowing the brain to receive signals of fullness before overeating.

Finally, yerba mate tea's ability to boost metabolism can also contribute to its appetite-suppressing effects, as a faster metabolism can help the body burn calories more efficiently and reduce overall hunger.

The following procedure explains how to make yerba mate tea for appetite suppression.

  1. To make yerba mate tea for appetite suppression, start by boiling 1 liter of water and then letting it cool down to 80-85°C (176-185°F).
  2. Place 10 grams of loose leaf yerba mate (about 2 tablespoons) into a gourd or French press, and gently shake to create a small hill shape with the leaves.
  3. Pour 1/3 of the hot water into the gourd, allowing the tea to bloom for 2 minutes before filling the rest of the gourd.
  4. Use a bombilla or a slotted spoon to stir the tea, making sure to saturate all the leaves and remove any clumps.
  5. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then enjoy your appetite-suppressing yerba mate tea. For subsequent brews, increase the steeping time by 2 minutes.

4. Rooibos

Rooibos tea helps with appetite suppression because it is rich in unique bioactive compounds, including aspalathin, not found in other teas.

These compounds are thought to influence several physiological processes related to hunger and satiety, such as increasing leptin sensitivity and reducing ghrelin levels. Furthermore, rooibos tea is naturally caffeine-free and contains antioxidants, which may contribute to its potential appetite-suppressing effects by promoting overall health and well-being. The tea's soothing and comforting properties may also help reduce stress-related eating, while its mild flavor and lack of bitterness make it a satisfying alternative to high-calorie beverages.

Ultimately, incorporating rooibos tea into a balanced diet and exercise routine may help support weight management and healthy eating habits.

The following procedure explains how to make rooibos tea for appetite suppression.

  1. To make rooibos tea for appetite suppression, bring 8 ounces of water to a boil, then pour over 1 teaspoon of loose rooibos tea or a tea bag in a cup.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 5-7 minutes to release the flavonoids and polyphenols, which have been found to help with appetite suppression.
  3. Remove the tea bag or strain out the loose tea, then add a squeeze of lemon juice (about 1 tablespoon) for added vitamin C and to further aid in nutrient absorption.
  4. Optionally, add a natural sweetener like raw honey (to taste) if desired, but avoid artificial sweeteners as they can increase appetite.
  5. Drink this tea warm, 2-3 times a day, 30 minutes before meals to help control hunger and promote weight loss.

5. Hibiscus

Hibiscus tea helps with appetite suppression because it is high in both hydroxycitric acid and a type of plant acid known as flavonoids, which have been shown to aid in weight loss by decreasing hunger and fat accumulation.

Additionally, hibiscus tea is a natural diuretic, helping the body to eliminate excess water and reduce bloating, which can contribute to a feeling of fullness. The tea also has a mild laxative effect, helping to promote regularity and aid in digestion. Furthermore, hibiscus tea has been found to slow down the speed at which the stomach empties, leading to an extended feeling of fullness.

Overall, hibiscus tea's unique combination of properties makes it an effective tool in appetite suppression and weight management.

The following procedure explains how to make hibiscus tea for appetite suppression.

  1. To make hibiscus tea for appetite suppression, start by boiling 8 cups of water in a large pot, then add 2/3 cup of dried hibiscus flowers and 1 tablespoon of crushed fennel seeds, reducing heat to a simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. After 15 minutes, turn off the heat and mix in 1 teaspoon of grated ginger, 1 cinnamon stick, and 1 tablespoon of raw honey, letting the mixture steep for 10 more minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a large pitcher or teapot, discarding the solids.
  4. Serve the hibiscus tea hot or cold, garnishing with a few fresh mint leaves, a slice of lemon, or a cinnamon stick if desired.
  5. Drink this tea up to three times a day, 30 minutes before meals, to help curb your appetite and promote weight loss. Enjoy!

6. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with appetite suppression because it contains menthol and methyl salicylate, compounds that are known to reduce hunger cravings.

The soothing and calming properties of peppermint tea can also help to manage stress-related eating by promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. Furthermore, the cooling sensation and strong flavor of peppermint tea can help to distract the mind from thinking about food, thus reducing the urge to eat. Additionally, peppermint tea has been shown to increase the production of digestive enzymes, which can help to improve digestion and reduce feelings of bloating and fullness, further aiding in appetite suppression.

Lastly, drinking peppermint tea regularly can help to establish a healthy routine and replace unhealthy snacking habits, leading to a decrease in overall calorie intake.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for appetite suppression.

  1. In a teapot, combine 8 cups of water, 1 tablespoon of fresh peppermint leaves (or 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves), and 1 teaspoon of honey (optional, for added sweetness) and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. While the tea is steeping, crush the peppermint leaves gently using a spoon to release the oils, which enhances the appetite-suppressing properties.
  3. Strain the tea into a large mug or thermos to remove the leaves and debris.
  4. Add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice (about 1/2 a lemon) for added vitamin C and a boosted metabolism.
  5. Drink the tea throughout the day, up to 3-4 cups, 30 minutes before meals to help curb your appetite and promote weight loss.

7. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with appetite suppression because it contains unique compounds like gingerols and shogaols that stimulate digestion and suppress hunger, making you feel full for a longer period.

Additionally, ginger tea has been shown to increase satiety after meals, reducing the desire to snack between meals. Furthermore, ginger's natural spiciness can help slow down the speed of eating, giving your body more time to signal fullness to your brain. Moreover, ginger tea can help regulate blood sugar levels, preventing spikes and crashes that can lead to increased hunger.

Lastly, ginger tea's soothing and calming properties can help reduce stress and anxiety, which are often linked to overeating and poor appetite control.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for appetite suppression.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 2 inches of fresh ginger root, then combine with 4 cups of water in a saucepan; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 20 minutes.
  2. Remove saucepan from heat and stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey (to taste) and the juice of half a lemon.
  3. Allow the tea to cool for a few minutes, then strain out the ginger pieces.
  4. Pour the tea into a mug, and if desired, add an additional boost of appetite suppression by adding a pinch of cayenne pepper.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, especially before meals, to help curb your appetite and promote weight loss.

8. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with appetite suppression because it contains certain compounds, such as flavonoids, that may help reduce appetite and food cravings.

These compounds work by influencing the production of certain hormones in the body, including ghrelin and leptin, which regulate hunger and fullness. Additionally, chamomile tea has a mildly bitter taste that can help stimulate digestion and reduce appetite. Drinking chamomile tea before meals may also help slow down the eating process, allowing the body more time to recognize when it is full.

Furthermore, chamomile tea has a calming effect on the body and mind, which may help reduce stress-related eating and promote mindful eating habits.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for appetite suppression.

  1. To start, boil 8 ounces of water and allow a chamomile tea bag (or 1-2 teaspoons of loose chamomile flowers) to steep for 5-10 minutes in a cup.
  2. Remove the tea bag or strain out the loose flowers, then add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice (about 1/4 of a lemon) to the chamomile tea for enhanced appetite suppression.
  3. Stir in a half teaspoon of raw honey (optional) for added flavor, if desired.
  4. For increased effectiveness, drink the chamomile tea 30 minutes before meals, up to three times a day.
  5. Finally, pair this natural appetite suppressant with a balanced diet and regular exercise routine for optimal weight management results.

9. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with appetite suppression because it contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which can help regulate hormones related to hunger and fullness.

This active ingredient can help to reduce the levels of the hormone ghrelin, which is responsible for stimulating appetite, and increase the levels of the hormone adiponectin, which helps to suppress appetite. Furthermore, licorice root tea has been shown to slow down gastric emptying, prolonging the feeling of fullness and satisfaction after meals. Additionally, the soothing and calming properties of licorice root tea may help to alleviate stress-related eating habits, providing an additional benefit for appetite control.

However, it is important to consume licorice root tea in moderation, as excessive consumption of glycyrrhizin can lead to serious side effects.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice root tea for appetite suppression.

  1. To make licorice root tea for appetite suppression, start by boiling 8 ounces of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) once the water has reached a rolling boil.
  2. Allow the licorice root to steep in the boiling water for 10-15 minutes, covered, to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve a strong tea with a noticeable flavor.
  3. After steeping, strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the licorice root pieces, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable drinking experience.
  4. For optimal appetite suppression, consider adding a small amount of raw honey (to taste) and a squeeze of lemon juice to the tea, which can help mask any bitterness and enhance the tea's overall flavor profile.
  5. Drink the licorice root tea 30 minutes before meals, up to three times a day, to help curb your appetite and promote healthy weight management. Remember to consult with a healthcare professional before consuming licorice root tea for appetite suppression, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking medications.

10. Fenugreek

Fenugreek tea helps with appetite suppression because it contains high levels of soluble fiber, particularly a type called galactomannan, which can help promote feelings of fullness and reduce hunger.

Additionally, fenugreek has been found to slow down gastric emptying, prolonging the sensation of fullness and reducing the desire to eat. The tea may also help regulate blood sugar levels, as it can slow down the absorption of carbohydrates and reduce the impact of high-sugar meals, further contributing to appetite control. Furthermore, fenugreek contains certain compounds, such as diosgenin, that may have an effect on neurotransmitters and hormones related to appetite, potentially reducing cravings and promoting satiety.

However, it's important to note that individual results may vary, and further research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms behind fenugreek's appetite-suppressing effects.

The following procedure explains how to make fenugreek tea for appetite suppression.

  1. In a tea pot, add 1 teaspoon of dried fenugreek seeds to 1 cup of boiling water, then let it steep for 10 minutes to allow the active compounds to be released.
  2. After 10 minutes, strain the tea to remove the fenugreek seeds, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable drinking experience.
  3. Optional: add a small amount of honey or lemon to enhance the flavor of the fenugreek tea, as the taste can be bitter for some people.
  4. Pour the tea into a cup and drink it slowly, savoring the warm and soothing sensation as it travels down your throat.
  5. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day, 30 minutes before meals, to help curb your appetite and promote weight loss.
