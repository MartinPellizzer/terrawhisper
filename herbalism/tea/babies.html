10 Best Herbal Teas For Babies

Herbal teas can be beneficial for babies, but it's important to consult with a healthcare provider before administering them.

Certain herbal teas, such as chamomile or lemon balm, are believed to have calming properties that can help soothe a baby and promote sleep. Peppermint tea may provide relief for digestive issues like colic or gas. However, it's crucial to use only a tiny amount of tea and dilute it with water, as a baby's digestive system is sensitive and can't handle large quantities or concentrations.

Also, avoid giving tea to babies under 6 months old, except on the advice of a healthcare professional.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with babies because it contains natural compounds that promote sleep and calmness, such as apigenin and bisabolol.

These compounds interact with certain receptors in the brain to reduce anxiety and induce relaxation. Chamomile tea is also known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe colic, teething pain, and other digestive issues common in babies. Additionally, chamomile tea does not contain caffeine or artificial additives, making it a safe alternative to over-the-counter sleep aids or medications for babies.

Lastly, chamomile tea can be administered to babies in various ways, including as a warm bath, a compress, or a cup of diluted tea, providing versatility in its use for soothing baby discomforts.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for babies.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down to a comfortable temperature, around 150-170°F (65-75°C), to ensure it's not too hot for the baby.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag to a cup or tea infuser.
  3. Pour the warm water over the chamomile flowers or tea bag, ensuring it's fully submerged.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve the desired flavor.
  5. Strain or remove the tea bag and let it cool down to a safe temperature, around 100-110°F (38-43°C), before serving it to the baby.

2. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with babies because it contains anethole, a natural compound that acts as a gastrointestinal aid.

Anethole relaxes the muscles in the digestive tract, helping to alleviate symptoms of colic and gastric discomfort. Additionally, fennel tea is known for its ability to stimulate the production of breast milk in new mothers, making it an essential addition to their postpartum diet. The calming effects of fennel tea also help soothe irritable babies and improve sleep quality.

Overall, fennel tea provides numerous benefits for both mother and baby, making it a valuable natural remedy during the early stages of parenthood.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel tea for babies.

  1. Wash and rinse 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds, then lightly crush them using a mortar and pestle to release their aroma.
  2. In a small pot, combine the crushed fennel seeds with 1 cup of fresh water and bring it to a simmer over medium heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the fennel steep for 10-15 minutes to extract its beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain the fennel tea to remove any solid particles, and allow it to cool down to a comfortable temperature for the baby.
  5. Serve 1-2 tablespoons of the lukewarm fennel tea to your baby, up to 3 times a day, to help soothe their digestive system and alleviate symptoms like colic or gas.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with babies because it contains natural anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe their sensitive digestive systems, reducing inflammation and easing discomfort caused by conditions like colic or teething.

The warming properties of ginger tea also help to alleviate symptoms of colds or respiratory infections by promoting sweating, which can help babies feel more comfortable and improve their overall health. Additionally, the calming effects of ginger can aid in reducing stress and anxiety for both the baby and caregiver, promoting a more peaceful and relaxed environment.

Furthermore, ginger tea is a natural remedy that has been used for generations and does not contain any added artificial flavors or preservatives, making it a safe and healthy option for babies when prepared correctly with appropriate dilution ratios.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for babies.

  1. Peel and grate 1 small piece of fresh ginger (about 1 tablespoon), then combine it with 2 cups of water in a small saucepan.
  2. Bring the ginger-water mixture to a simmer over medium heat, then reduce heat to low and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the ginger-infused water to remove the solids, then return the liquid to the saucepan.
  4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey (or to taste) and a squeeze of lemon juice, then warm the mixture over low heat until it's comfortably hot.
  5. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before serving it to your baby in a feeding bottle or sippy cup.

4. Raspberry Leaf

Raspberry leaf tea helps with babies because it contains high levels of nutrients essential for labor and childbirth.

This herbal tea is rich in fragile tannins, which tone the uterus and pelvic muscles, making labor more efficient and reducing the need for medical interventions. Additionally, raspberry leaf tea has been shown to shorten labor duration and decrease the risk of postpartum hemorrhage by strengthening the uterine walls. Furthermore, some studies suggest that regular consumption of raspberry leaf tea during pregnancy may help prevent preterm labor and reduce the symptoms of morning sickness.

Overall, raspberry leaf tea is a natural and effective way to support a healthy pregnancy, labor, and postpartum recovery.

The following procedure explaing how to make raspberry leaf tea for babies.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan and let it cool down to a comfortable temperature for the baby.
  2. Crush 1-2 raspberry leaves (dried or fresh) to release their nutrients and place them in a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the lukewarm water over the crushed raspberry leaves and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the raspberry leaves.
  5. Serve the raspberry leaf tea to your baby, ensuring it's not too hot, and adjust the quantity according to their age and preference.

5. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea helps with babies because it contains natural compounds called terpenes, flavonoids, and rosmarinic acid, which have been shown to calm the nervous system, promote relaxation, reduce inflammation, and aid in digestion.

These benefits can help soothe colic, ease teething discomfort, support proper neural development, and improve sleep quality for babies.

Additionally, lemon balm tea is caffeine-free and gentle on the digestive system, making it a safe and effective option for use during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

The following procedure explaing how to make lemon balm tea for babies.

  1. Wash and dry your hands thoroughly before beginning the tea preparation to ensure the cleanliness of the final product.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water in a tea kettle or pot, then remove it from heat once boiling.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of dried lemon balm leaves or 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon balm leaves to a tea infuser or strainer.
  4. Place the tea infuser or strainer with lemon balm leaves in a teapot or mug, then pour the hot water over the leaves.
  5. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes, then remove the infuser or strainer with leaves. Let the tea cool down to a safe temperature (around 100°F/38°C) before serving it to the baby.

6. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with babies because it contains mucilage, a substance that forms a soothing film in the mouth and digestive tract, providing relief for irritation and inflammation.

This natural anti-inflammatory effect can alleviate symptoms of digestive discomfort, such as colic, acid reflux, and stomach pain. Moreover, marshmallow tea can help soothe a sore throat and reduce coughing, making it an effective remedy for respiratory ailments in babies.

As a demulcent, it forms a protective layer in the mouth and throat, reducing irritation and pain. Marshmallow tea is also caffeine-free and gentle on the stomach, making it a safe and mild option for infants.

However, it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before administering any herbal remedies to babies.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow tea for babies.

  1. Gather 2 cups of water, 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root, and a heat-safe container or pot.
  2. Combine the marshmallow root and water in the container, then bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the container, and let the mixture simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. After simmering, remove the mixture from heat and let it cool before straining out the marshmallow root.
  5. Serve the marshmallow root tea to your baby at body temperature, ensuring it is not too hot.

7. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with babies because it is rich in nutrients, including iron and calcium, which are essential for their growth and development.

The tea also contains vitamin K, which supports healthy blood clotting and bone formation. Additionally, nettle tea can help soothe colic symptoms by reducing inflammation in the digestive tract. Furthermore, it has been traditionally used to prevent and treat eczema in infants, as its anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce redness and itching.

Nettle tea is safe for babies when prepared correctly, with cooled, strained water and no added sweeteners or herbs.

The following procedure explaing how to make nettle tea for babies.

  1. Collect fresh stinging nettle leaves (Urtica dioica) from a clean, organic source, ensuring they have not been sprayed with pesticides; rinse leaves thoroughly.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried nettle leaves or 1 tablespoon of fresh nettle leaves and place them in a heat-resistant container or teapot.
  3. Boil 1 cup of filtered water and let it cool down for 2-3 minutes before pouring it over the nettle leaves.
  4. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength; cover the container to preserve nutrients.
  5. Strain the tea to remove leaves before serving; allow it to cool down to a safe temperature (around 98.6°F or 37°C) before offering it to your baby.

8. Passionflower

Passionflower tea helps with babies because it contains natural compounds that promote relaxation and calmness, which can be beneficial for babies experiencing colic or teething discomfort.

These compounds include flavonoids and alkaloids, such as chrysin and apigenin, that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Additionally, passionflower tea may aid in improving sleep quality for babies suffering from insomnia or irregular sleep patterns.

Lastly, the tea is caffeine-free, making it a safe and natural alternative to traditional sleep aids and stimulants that are not suitable for infants.

The following procedure explaing how to make passionflower tea for babies.

  1. Gather 1 cup of passionflower leaves and 4 cups of water.
  2. Rinse passionflower leaves thoroughly to remove any impurities.
  3. Boil water in a pot, then add passionflower leaves and reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain out the passionflower leaves.
  5. Let the tea cool down to a safe temperature before serving it to the baby in a bottle or cup.

9. Chicory

Chicory tea helps with babies because it contains inulin, a type of prebiotic that nurtures the growth of good bacteria in the baby's digestive system.

This can help improve symptoms of colic and gas, as well as contribute to better overall gut health. Additionally, chicory tea has been shown to have mild diuretic properties, which can be beneficial for babies who are prone to constipation or experiencing teething discomfort that may cause water retention. The roasted flavor of chicory tea also makes it a gentle and soothing alternative to black or green tea for parents who want to enjoy a warm beverage without caffeine.

Lastly, chicory tea is naturally free of caffeine and sugars, making it an ideal choice for parents who want to promote healthy habits from an early age while providing their baby with potential digestive benefits.

The following procedure explaing how to make chicory tea for babies.

  1. Sterilize all utensils and equipment by boiling them in water for 5 minutes to ensure a clean and safe preparation environment for the baby.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried chicory root and rinse under cold water to remove any impurities, then place it in a cup or infant teapot.
  3. Fill a kettle with fresh, cold water and bring it to a boil. Once boiled, pour 8 ounces (1 cup) of hot water (175°F - 205°F) over the chicory root.
  4. Allow the chicory root to steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength; then, remove and discard the chicory root from the liquid.
  5. Cool the chicory tea to a safe temperature (around 115°F) before serving it to the baby in a feeding bottle or cup.

10. Anise

Anise tea helps with babies because it contains natural soothing compounds, such as anethole and anisaldehyde.

These elements work together to alleviate common infant digestive issues like colic, gas, and bloating. Additionally, its sweet licorice-like flavor appeals to infants and encourages them to drink more, helping keep them hydrated. Parents often find that administering anise tea as a warm brew or adding it to a bottle helps ease fussy or gassy babies into a more comfortable state.

Furthermore, it has been used historically in traditional medicine for its calming effects on the digestive system and overall well-being.

The following procedure explaing how to make anise tea for babies.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a tea kettle.
  2. Crush a pinch (about 1/16 teaspoon) of anise seeds in a mortar and pestle.
  3. Once the water is boiling, add the crushed anise seeds to the water.
  4. Allow the anise to steep in the boiling water for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the anise tea into a cup, let it cool to a safe temperature, then serve to your baby.
