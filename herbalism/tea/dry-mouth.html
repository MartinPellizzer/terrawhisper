1. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with dry mouth because it contains compounds called glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which stimulate the production of saliva, thereby alleviating the symptoms of dry mouth.

Moreover, these compounds also have anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties that can help protect the mouth from infections and irritations. Additionally, licorice tea has been used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues, including acid reflux and heartburn, which can exacerbate dry mouth symptoms. However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and potassium depletion.

Therefore, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using licorice tea as a remedy for dry mouth or any other health condition.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with dry mouth.

  1. Glycyrrhizin: This compound is the main active component in licorice and is responsible for its sweet taste. It stimulates the production of saliva, which can help relieve dry mouth.
  2. Flavonoids: These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. They can help protect the mucous membranes in the mouth and relieve symptoms of dry mouth.
  3. Saponins: These compounds give licorice its foamy texture. They can help stimulate saliva flow and provide relief from dry mouth.
  4. Coumarins: These compounds have anti-coagulant properties. They can help improve blood flow to the mucous membranes in the mouth and relieve dry mouth.
  5. Volatile oils: These compounds give licorice its aroma. They can help stimulate saliva flow and provide relief from dry mouth.

The following list reveals what parts of licorice are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for dry mouth.

  1. Roots: The roots of the licorice plant contain glycyrrhizin, a compound that stimulates the production of saliva, making it effective for alleviating dry mouth.
  2. Rhizomes: The rhizomes of the licorice plant are used in medicinal tea due to their anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe irritation in the mouth and throat.
  3. Leaves: The leaves of the licorice plant have expectorant properties, which can help keep the mouth moist by promoting the production of mucus.
  4. Flowers: The flowers of the licorice plant contain flavonoids that have antioxidant properties, which can help protect the mouth from damage caused by dryness.
  5. Seeds: The seeds of the licorice plant contain a high concentration of plant sterols, which can help soothe inflammation and moisturize the mouth.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice tea for dry mouth.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 cup of cold water and 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root.
  2. Cover and bring the mixture to a boil, allowing it to simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root, then let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey (optional) for added sweetness and health benefits.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to moisten your mouth and relieve dryness.
