10 Best Herbal Teas For Upset Stomach

Herbal teas, especially those made from chamomile, ginger, peppermint, and licorice root, are known to soothe upset stomachs due to their anti-inflammatory and calming properties.

These herbs can help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, relax the smooth muscles of the intestines, and alleviate symptoms such as nausea, cramps, and bloating. Moreover, herbal teas promote fluid intake, which is essential for maintaining proper hydration and supporting overall digestive health. The warmth of the tea can also stimulate digestion, enhance blood flow to the digestive tract, and provide comfort during times of discomfort.

Lastly, herbal teas are caffeine-free, making them a gentle and soothing option for those with sensitive stomachs.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with upset stomach because it contains chemical compounds like bisabolol, bisabolol oxides and matricin that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation, relax the smooth muscles of the digestive tract, and relieve spasms, making it easier to pass stools and reduce stomach discomfort. Moreover, chamomile tea is also known to stimulate the production of bile in the liver, which aids in the digestion of fats and may help to prevent the formation of gas in the gut. Additionally, its calming effects can help reduce stress and anxiety, which are often linked to digestive problems.

However, it's important to note that while chamomile tea can be helpful for occasional bouts of upset stomach, it's not a substitute for medical treatment, especially for chronic or severe digestive conditions.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with upset stomach because it contains gingerol, a compound that boasts impressive anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

This substance can help relax the intestines, allowing food to move through the stomach more easily and reducing the symptoms of an upset stomach. Moreover, ginger tea has been shown to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, thereby facilitating the digestion process and preventing stomach discomfort. The soothing warmth of ginger tea can also help alleviate feelings of nausea and promote a sense of calm, further contributing to its gut-soothing abilities.

Lastly, ginger tea is a caffeine-free beverage, making it a gentler alternative to other hot beverages that may exacerbate stomach issues, allowing for effective and comfortable stomach relief.

3. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with upset stomach because it contains menthol, a compound that relaxes the gastrointestinal muscles, which can help to reduce spasms and cramping.

Additionally, peppermint oil has been shown to increase bile flow, helping to speed up digestion and alleviate symptoms of indigestion. Furthermore, the soothing warmth of the tea can help to provide immediate relief from discomfort, while the absence of caffeine makes it a gentle option for those with sensitive stomachs. The calming properties of peppermint tea may also help to reduce stress and anxiety, which can exacerbate digestive issues.

Overall, peppermint tea's combination of muscle relaxation, digestion stimulation, warmth, and calming effects make it an effective natural remedy for upset stomachs.

4. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with upset stomach because it contains volatile oils, such as anethole, which have antispasmodic properties that help relax the gastrointestinal tract, reducing stomach cramps and gas.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties, due to its rosmarinic acid content, can help soothe and reduce inflammation in the stomach lining. Furthermore, fennel's carminative effects promote the expulsion of excess gas from the digestive tract, alleviating bloating and discomfort. Additionally, fennel tea stimulates the production of gastric juices and bile, enhancing digestion and absorption, which can help alleviate symptoms of indigestion and nausea.

Lastly, fennel's mild diuretic effect can help eliminate excess fluids and toxins from the body, supporting overall gastrointestinal health and function.

5. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with upset stomach because it contains active compounds, particularly glycyrrhizin, which can help decrease inflammation in the stomach lining, increasing mucus production and protecting the gut.

This soothing effect on the gastrointestinal tract makes it effective in treating symptoms of gastric disorders, such as heartburn, acid reflux, and indigestion. Furthermore, licorice tea has been shown to stimulate the production of prostaglandins and mucin, substances that can help to regenerate and heal the stomach lining. It can also inhibit the growth of Helicobacter pylori, a bacterium that can cause stomach ulcers.

However, it's important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and potassium depletion, so it's recommended to consume it in moderation or use deglycyrrhizinated licorice (DGL) tea instead.

6. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with upset stomach because it contains curcumin, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that aid in soothing the digestive tract and alleviating symptoms of upset stomach.

The heating process involved in making turmeric tea enhances curcumin's bioavailability, allowing it to be better absorbed by the body and provide faster relief. Additionally, turmeric tea has been shown to stimulate the production of bile, which is essential for digesting fats and preventing stomach discomfort. Furthermore, its natural calming and warming effects can help relax the muscles in the gastrointestinal tract, reducing cramping and promoting normal bowel movements.

Lastly, turmeric tea's soothing and mild flavor makes it a comforting and accessible natural remedy for those seeking relief from an upset stomach.

7. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with upset stomach because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water.

This gel can coat and soothe the lining of the stomach and intestines, reducing irritation and inflammation. Additionally, slippery elm tea can help to protect the stomach from excess acid, further alleviating symptoms of upset stomach. Slippery elm may also help to speed up the healing process of any stomach ulcers or irritations, and it can help to regulate digestion, reducing symptoms of both diarrhea and constipation.

Overall, slippery elm tea provides a natural and soothing relief for upset stomach, making it a popular choice for those looking for a more holistic approach to managing digestive issues.

8. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with upset stomach because it contains a substance called mucilage, which becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water, creating a protective coating on the lining of the stomach, soothing inflammation and reducing irritation.

This soothing effect helps to alleviate symptoms of indigestion, heartburn, and acid reflux, making it an effective natural remedy for digestive discomfort. Additionally, marshmallow tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, promoting healthy digestion and easing digestive symptoms. Marshmallow tea is also a demulcent, which means it can help to relieve irritation and inflammation of the mucous membranes, further aiding in the relief of an upset stomach.

Moreover, marshmallow tea has been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety that can exacerbate digestive problems, providing additional relief for an upset stomach.

9. Catnip

Catnip tea helps with upset stomach because it contains nepetalactone, a compound that can help reduce intestinal spasms and inflammation, providing relief for various digestive issues.

Additionally, catnip has antispasmodic properties that can soothe and calm the stomach muscles, reducing discomfort and pain. Furthermore, catnip tea can promote relaxation and stress reduction, which can indirectly contribute to easing an upset stomach as stress is often a trigger for digestive problems. Moreover, catnip has been shown to have carminative effects, helping to eliminate excess gas from the digestive tract and alleviating bloating and cramping.

Lastly, catnip tea is a gentle and caffeine-free alternative to traditional teas, making it a suitable option for individuals with sensitive stomachs or digestive issues.

10. Lemongrass

Lemongrass tea helps with upset stomach because it has anti-inflammatory and calming properties that can assist in reducing inflammation in the digestive tract, thereby alleviating discomfort.

The tea is also known to stimulate the production of digestive juices, which can improve nutrient absorption and promote regularity, leading to a more settled stomach. Furthermore, lemongrass tea contains citral, a compound with antimicrobial qualities that may help combat harmful bacteria in the gut, thus reducing the likelihood of stomach irritation. Additionally, lemongrass is recognized for its soothing aroma, which can help minimize stress and anxiety, both of which can contribute to digestive issues.

Lastly, lemongrass tea is caffeine-free and gentle on the stomach, making it an ideal beverage for individuals seeking a natural remedy to alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach.
