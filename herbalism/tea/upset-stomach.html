10 best herbal teas for upset stomach

For an upset stomach, herbal teas can provide great relief. Peppermint tea is one of the best options due to its calming and antispasmodic properties that help soothe the stomach and improve digestion. Ginger tea is another excellent choice, as it is well-known for its anti-inflammatory and nausea-reducing qualities. Chamomile tea, with its soothing and gentle sedative effect, can help relax the stomach muscles and reduce stomach inflammation. Lastly, licorice root tea may help increase mucus production in the stomach lining, providing a protective barrier against stomach acid. Before starting any herbal tea remedy, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional to ensure it's safe and suitable for your specific needs.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which soothe the stomach lining and reduce inflammation. The calming effects of chamomile tea's volatile oils target the smooth muscle of the gastrointestinal tract, helping to relax stomach muscles and alleviate discomfort. Furthermore, chamomile's daisy-like flowers possess flavonoids that stimulate the production of gastric juices, promoting healthy digestion and aiding in the prevention of acid reflux. Chamomile tea's mild sedative properties can also help reduce stress and anxiety, indirectly contributing to overall digestive well-being by diminishing the negative impacts of these factors on the gastrointestinal system. Lastly, chamomile tea's tannins act as astringents, helping to protect the mucous membrane of the stomach and intestines, and reducing the risk of irritation and inflammation.

2. Ginger

Ginger, a key ingredient in ginger tea, contains gingerols and shogaols, compounds with proven anti-inflammatory and digestive properties. These compounds work by blocking nerve signals that cause nausea, reducing inflammation in the digestive tract, and relaxing intestinal muscles to alleviate stomach discomfort. Additionally, ginger increases saliva and bile production, which aids in the digestion of food and movement of food through the gastrointestinal tract. Ginger tea, when consumed during or after meals, can effectively soothe upset stomachs by enhancing digestion and providing rapid relief from symptoms such as cramps, bloating, and indigestion. Thus, ginger tea's ability to help with upset stomachs is a result of its active components' ability to influence various aspects of the digestive process.

3. Peppermint

Peppermint tea provides relief for upset stomachs due to its antispasmodic properties, which relax the gastrointestinal muscles, enabling smoother digestion. This herbal tea contains menthol, a compound that soothes the stomach lining, reducing inflammation and providing instant comfort. Moreover, peppermint tea stimulates the production of bile, thus accelerating the digestion process and preventing the buildup of gas and bloating. Furthermore, its calming aroma and mild flavor make it an ideal beverage for those experiencing nausea, helping to alleviate symptoms quickly. Therefore, peppermint tea is an effective and natural remedy for settling an upset stomach.

4. Fennel

Fennel tea aids digestion by stimulating the production of gastric juices, shortening digestion time, and facilitating the movement of food through the digestive tract. Its carminative properties reduce bloating and gas, providing relief from uncomfortable stomach distension. Fennel's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic agents soothe the gastrointestinal lining, alleviating irritation and discomfort. Furthermore, fennel tea's antibacterial qualities combat harmful bacteria in the stomach, promoting a healthy gut environment and preventing further stomach issues. Consequently, fennel tea is an effective natural remedy for upset stomachs.

5. Licorice

Licorice tea combats upset stomach by soothing inflammation in the digestive tract, as it contains substances like glycyrrhizic acid. This acid generates anti-inflammatory and mucosal protective effects, providing relief to the stomach lining. Moreover, licorice tea encourages the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, forming a protective barrier against acidic environments. Additionally, it promotes the secretion of digestive enzymes, enhancing the breakdown of food and reducing stomach discomfort. Lastly, licorice tea addresses upset stomach by stimulating the regeneration of stomach cells, contributing to overall gut health and quicker recovery.

6. Turmeric

Turmeric tea combats upset stomach due to curcumin, its active ingredient, which possesses anti-inflammatory properties. This substance inhibits the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like compounds responsible for causing inflammation and resulting stomach pain. Furthermore, curcumin stimulates the release of bile, aiding in digestion and alleviating symptoms of indigestion and bloating. The soothing warmth of the tea increases blood flow to the abdominal area and promotes muscle relaxation, thereby further reducing discomfort. Finally, turmeric's antioxidant characteristics protect the stomach lining from damage, offering a comprehensive approach to managing digestive issues.

7. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides relief for upset stomachs due to its antioxidant and demulcent properties. This tea contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water. This gel coats the lining of the stomach and digestive tract, protecting it from acid and irritation. Slippery elm also stimulates the production of natural mucus in the stomach, providing further protection and soothing effects. Moreover, it encourages the healing of any inflammation or damage in the stomach lining, while its antioxidant compounds help fight off free radicals that can cause harm to cells.

8. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the marshmallow plant's root, contains mucilage, a substance known for its soothing properties. This mucilage forms a protective layer on the stomach lining, reducing irritation. As a result, marshmallow tea alleviates discomfort and decreases painful stomach inflammation. Moreover, it helps control excessive stomach acid, which often leads to acid reflux and heartburn. Additionally, this tea's calming effect on the digestive tract promotes intestinal movement, thus relieving bloating and constipation.

9. Catnip

Catnip tea contains nepetalactone, an essential oil known for its anti-spasmodic and calming properties. This oil helps soothe the smooth muscle linings of the digestive tract, reducing intestinal spasms and gas that cause upset stomachs. Moreover, catnip acts as a mild sedative and nervine, alleviating stress and anxiety that often accompany digestive issues. Its carminative properties facilitate the expulsion of excess gas, while its astringent qualities help tone the gastrointestinal system. As a result, catnip tea brings quick relief to an upset stomach by directly addressing multiple causes of discomfort.

10. Lemongrass

Lemongrass tea brings immediate relief to an upset stomach due to its carminative properties, which move gas from the intestinal tract and alleviate symptoms of bloating and discomfort. This tea's antimicrobial qualities, derived from its citral and geraniol components, combat harmful bacteria in the stomach contributing to indigestion. Furthermore, lemongrass tea's soothing nature reduces inflammation in the digestive tract, calming any irritation and promoting healing. Additionally, its rich antioxidant profile combats free radicals, supporting overall gastrointestinal health and aiding in the prevention of future stomach upsets. Ultimately, the relaxing effects of lemongrass tea, which can be attributed to its essential oil content, settle the stomach and help restore normal digestive function.
