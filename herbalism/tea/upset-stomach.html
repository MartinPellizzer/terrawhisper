10 Best Herbal Teas For Upset Stomach

Herbal teas, especially those made from chamomile, ginger, peppermint, and licorice root, are known to soothe upset stomachs due to their anti-inflammatory and calming properties.

These herbs can help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, relax the smooth muscles of the intestines, and alleviate symptoms such as nausea, cramps, and bloating. Moreover, herbal teas promote fluid intake, which is essential for maintaining proper hydration and supporting overall digestive health. The warmth of the tea can also stimulate digestion, enhance blood flow to the digestive tract, and provide comfort during times of discomfort.

Lastly, herbal teas are caffeine-free, making them a gentle and soothing option for those with sensitive stomachs.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with upset stomach because It contains bioactive compounds, including apigenin and bisabolol, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

These properties help soothe the gastrointestinal tract and alleviate symptoms such as bloating, cramping, and diarrhea. Additionally, chamomile tea is caffeine-free and has a calming effect on the body, which can help reduce stress and anxiety that may contribute to an upset stomach.

Lastly, studies have shown that drinking chamomile tea before bed can improve sleep quality, which can also aid in digestion and reduce the frequency of upset stomachs.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for upset stomach.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down to 200°F (93°C) before proceeding.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let them steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent flowers.
  5. Sip the chamomile tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your upset stomach. Enjoy up to 3 cups a day as needed.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with upset stomach because ginger contains properties that aid in digestion and reduce inflammation.

The phenolic compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, have been shown to alleviate gastrointestinal irritation and reduce nausea. Ginger tea can also help to relax the smooth muscle in the gut lining, which can help to reduce spasms and relieve stomach discomfort. Additionally, ginger has been found to increase gastric emptying, which can help to move food through the digestive system more quickly and reduce symptoms of indigestion.

Lastly, ginger tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to over-the-counter medications, making it a safe and effective option for managing an upset stomach.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for upset stomach.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root (about 2-4 tablespoons), then place the slices in a pot.
  2. Pour 4 cups of water into the pot and bring it to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes to allow the water to absorb the ginger's medicinal properties.
  4. Strain the ginger tea into a cup, discarding the ginger slices, then add honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Sip the warm ginger tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and settle your upset stomach. Enjoy frequently throughout the day as needed.

3. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with upset stomach because it contains menthol, a compound with natural antispasmodic properties that relax the gastrointestinal muscles, thereby alleviating discomfort and pain.

Furthermore, it has carminative effects, which mean it can help expel excess gas from the stomach and intestines, reducing bloating and cramping. Additionally, peppermint oil found in the tea has been shown to increase bile secretion, improving digestion and nutrient absorption while reducing inflammation in the gut lining. The soothing warmth of the tea can also create a calming effect on the stomach, providing instant relief from symptoms of indigestion, nausea, and acid reflux.

Lastly, peppermint tea is caffeine-free and hydrating, making it a gentle and comforting beverage for those experiencing stomach issues.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for upset stomach.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Crush or tear 1 peppermint tea bag to release oils and place it in a mug.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the tea bag in the mug.
  4. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  5. Remove the tea bag, squeeze out excess liquid, and enjoy your soothing peppermint tea.

4. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with upset stomach because it contains volatile oils, such as anethole, fenchone, and estragole, which have antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties that can help relax the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, reducing cramping, bloating, and gas.

Additionally, fennel tea can stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, speeding up digestion and alleviating symptoms of indigestion, such as heartburn and nausea. Furthermore, fennel's carminative effect can help dispel built-up gas in the intestines, providing relief from discomfort and pain. The soothing and calming effect of fennel tea on the digestive system may also help reduce stress and anxiety, which can exacerbate gastrointestinal issues.

Lastly, fennel tea is a caffeine-free and sugar-free beverage, making it a healthier alternative to other stomach-irritating drinks, like coffee or soda.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel tea for upset stomach.

  1. Coarsely chop 1 tablespoon of fresh fennel seeds and place them in a teapot or heat-resistant glass container.
  2. Boil 2 cups of water and pour it over the fennel seeds, allowing them to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the fennel seeds, pouring the liquid into a mug or teacup.
  4. Optionally, add a small amount of honey or lemon to taste, but avoid milk or sugar.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and calm your upset stomach. Enjoy the warmth and comfort of this natural remedy.

5. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with upset stomach because it contains glycyrrhizic acid, a compound that can increase the mucus lining of the stomach, reducing irritation and inflammation.

This soothing effect can help alleviate symptoms of acid reflux and heartburn. Additionally, licorice root has been shown to have antibacterial properties, potentially killing off harmful bacteria in the stomach that can cause discomfort. Furthermore, it may help with digestion by increasing the mucus secretions in the intestines, promoting a healthy gut.

However, it's important to note that excessive consumption of licorice root can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and hormonal imbalances, so it's recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating it into your routine.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for upset stomach.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the licorice root pieces.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat and soothe your stomach. Enjoy up to three cups of licorice root tea per day.

6. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with upset stomach because it contains curcumin, a compound with anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help soothe the digestive tract and alleviate symptoms of indigestion, gas, and bloating.

Furthermore, turmeric can stimulate the production of bile in the gallbladder, which can improve digestion and reduce the likelihood of stomach discomfort. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of turmeric can help protect the stomach lining from damage caused by free radicals, reducing the risk of inflammation and irritation. The soothing and warming effects of turmeric tea can also help to relax the muscles of the stomach and intestines, providing relief from cramps and spasms.

Overall, turmeric tea offers a natural and effective way to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of upset stomach.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric tea for upset stomach.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, then stir in 1 tablespoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice for flavor (optional).
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, it is recommended to drink 2-3 times a day for best results.

7. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with upset stomach because it contains a substance called mucilage, which becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water, creating a protective coating for the digestive tract.

This soothing barrier can help to alleviate irritation, inflammation, and discomfort in the stomach and intestines. Additionally, slippery elm can help to reduce acidity in the stomach, which can contribute to feelings of indigestion and discomfort. The tea may also help to increase the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, which can further protect and soothe irritated tissues.

Overall, slippery elm tea can provide a natural and effective way to manage symptoms of an upset stomach, such as pain, inflammation, and acid reflux.

The following procedure explaing how to make slippery elm tea for upset stomach.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark, 1 cup of water, and a heat-resistant container or mug.
  2. Pour the water into a pot and bring it to a boil on the stove.
  3. Once boiling, add the dried slippery elm bark to the pot and reduce heat to a simmer.
  4. Allow the mixture to steep for 3-5 minutes, then carefully pour the liquid (slippery elm tea) into the heat-resistant container or mug.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed for an upset stomach.

8. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with upset stomach because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slippery gel when mixed with water, which coats and soothes the lining of the stomach, reducing irritation and inflammation.

This soothing effect can help to alleviate symptoms such as pain, cramping, and indigestion. Additionally, marshmallow root has been found to have antibacterial properties, which may help to combat stomach bugs that can cause upset stomach. Furthermore, it may also help to reduce acid production in the stomach, providing relief for those who experience acid reflux or heartburn.

Lastly, marshmallow root tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to over-the-counter medications, making it a gentle and safe option for people of all ages.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow root tea for upset stomach.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root (Althaea officinalis) and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let the mixture steep for an additional 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root pieces.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed for an upset stomach.

9. Catnip

Catnip tea helps with upset stomach because it contains calming properties that can help soothe the digestive system and reduce inflammation.

The herb's volatile oils, particularly nepetalactone, can help relax the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, which can alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and cramping. Additionally, catnip has been shown to have antispasmodic effects, which can further help to reduce the painful spasms and cramping associated with an upset stomach. Furthermore, catnip tea can help stimulate the production of digestive juices, which can improve nutrient absorption and overall digestion.

However, it is important to note that catnip tea should not be consumed in large quantities, as it can have a laxative effect and potentially worsen diarrhea.

The following procedure explaing how to make catnip tea for upset stomach.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried catnip leaves or 2 teaspoons of fresh catnip leaves and place them in a tea infuser or a reusable tea bag.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool down for 2-3 minutes until it reaches a temperature of around 200°F - 212°F (93°C - 100°C).
  3. Place the tea infuser or tea bag with catnip leaves in a teapot or a large mug and pour the hot water over it.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-7 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer the taste.
  5. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag, let it cool down for a few minutes, and enjoy your catnip tea.

10. Lemongrass

Lemongrass tea helps with upset stomach because it contains anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help soothe the gastrointestinal tract, reduce inflammation, and alleviate muscle spasms in the stomach.

Additionally, lemongrass has carminative effects, which can help to expel gas from the digestive tract, reducing discomfort and bloating. Furthermore, it can stimulate the production of digestive juices, promoting healthy digestion and nutrient absorption, while its antimicrobial properties can help to eliminate harmful bacteria in the gut that may be causing stomach upset.

Lastly, lemongrass tea is a caffeine-free alternative to traditional teas and beverages, making it a gentle and soothing option for those with sensitive stomachs or digestive issues.

The following procedure explaing how to make lemongrass tea for upset stomach.

  1. Gather 1-2 fresh lemongrass stalks, 1 inch of ginger root, 1 teaspoon of honey, and 1 cup of water.
  2. Rinse and thinly slice the lemongrass stalks and ginger root.
  3. Bring water to a boil in a small pot, then add lemongrass and ginger slices.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea, discard solids, add honey, and enjoy while warm.
