10 Best Herbal Teas For Upset Stomach

Herbal teas, especially those made from chamomile, ginger, peppermint, and licorice root, are known to soothe upset stomachs due to their anti-inflammatory and calming properties.

These herbs can help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, relax the smooth muscles of the intestines, and alleviate symptoms such as nausea, cramps, and bloating. Moreover, herbal teas promote fluid intake, which is essential for maintaining proper hydration and supporting overall digestive health. The warmth of the tea can also stimulate digestion, enhance blood flow to the digestive tract, and provide comfort during times of discomfort.

Lastly, herbal teas are caffeine-free, making them a gentle and soothing option for those with sensitive stomachs.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with upset stomach because it contains chemical compounds like bisabolol, bisabolol oxides and matricin that have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation, relax the smooth muscles of the digestive tract, and relieve spasms, making it easier to pass stools and reduce stomach discomfort. Moreover, chamomile tea is also known to stimulate the production of bile in the liver, which aids in the digestion of fats and may help to prevent the formation of gas in the gut. Additionally, its calming effects can help reduce stress and anxiety, which are often linked to digestive problems.

However, it's important to note that while chamomile tea can be helpful for occasional bouts of upset stomach, it's not a substitute for medical treatment, especially for chronic or severe digestive conditions.

  1. Azulene: Has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining caused by upset stomach.
  2. Bisabolol: Possesses anti-spasmodic properties that can help relax stomach muscles and alleviate symptoms of upset stomach such as cramping and pain.
  3. Chamazulene: Has been shown to have gastroprotective effects, which can help protect the stomach lining from damage caused by acid and other irritants.
  4. Apigenin: Has anti-inflammatory and relaxant properties that can help reduce inflammation and spasms in the digestive tract, providing relief from upset stomach.
  5. Flavonoids: These plant compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help soothe and protect the stomach lining, reducing symptoms of upset stomach.
  6. Terpenoids: These compounds have anti-inflammatory, anti-spasmodic, and stomachic properties that can help alleviate symptoms of upset stomach such as cramping, pain, and inflammation.
  7. Matricin: This compound is a precursor to chamazulene and has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach and digestive tract.

The following list reveals what parts of chamomile are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for upset stomach.

  1. Flowers: Chamomile flowers are commonly used in medicinal tea for upset stomach due to their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
  2. Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain therapeutic compounds that can help soothe an upset stomach and promote digestion.
  3. Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in essential oils, which can help alleviate digestive discomfort and reduce stomach inflammation.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for upset stomach.

  1. In a small pot, pour 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil, which takes about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag to the pot.
  3. Reduce heat to low, let it simmer for 5 minutes to allow the water to absorb the chamomile's properties.
  4. Remove from heat, then strain the tea to separate the chamomile flowers or tea bag from the liquid.
  5. Sip the tea while still warm, up to three times a day, as needed for soothing your upset stomach.
