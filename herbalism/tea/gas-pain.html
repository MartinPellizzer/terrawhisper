10 best herbal teas for gas pain

Certain herbal teas can be very effective in providing relief from gas pain.

Peppermint tea is one of the best options, as it contains menthol which helps to relax the gastrointestinal muscles and reduce spasms. Ginger tea is another excellent choice, as ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate gas pain.

Chamomile tea is also known for its calming effects on the digestive system and can help reduce gas and bloating. Fennel tea is another good option, as it can help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes and reduce inflammation.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be helpful in managing gas pain, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment if you are experiencing severe or persistent symptoms.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea treats gas pain due to its antispasmodic and carminative properties.

Antispasmodics relax the smooth muscles of the intestines, alleviating painful spasms. Carminatives expel excess gas from the stomach and intestines, easing discomfort. Chamomile contains volatile oils, including bisabolol and matricaria, which possess these medicinal properties.

Therefore, drinking chamomile tea brings prompt relief from gas pain by reducing muscle spasms and expelling trapped gas.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with gas pain.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain volatile oils and flavonoids that have established antispasmodic properties, useful for alleviating smooth muscle spasms in the gastrointestinal tract.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile plants have been used traditionally to soothe gastrointestinal disturbances, including gas pains, due to their bitter constituents that stimulate digestion.
