10 Best Herbal Teas For Flu

Herbal teas can help with flu by providing hydration and soothing a sore throat.

Some herbs, like chamomile and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the throat and nasal passages. Additionally, some herbal teas contain vitamins and antioxidants that can help boost the immune system and fight off the virus. Furthermore, the warm temperature of the tea can help alleviate congestion and break up mucus.

Lastly, drinking herbal tea can promote relaxation and help individuals rest, which is crucial for recovery from the flu.

1. Elderberry

Elderberry tea helps with flu because it contains properties that can help to reduce symptoms and duration of the virus.

The tea is rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, particularly vitamin C, that can help to boost the immune system and fight off the virus. Additionally, elderberries have been found to contain antiviral properties that can prevent the virus from replicating, thereby reducing the severity and duration of the flu. Furthermore, elderberry tea can help to alleviate symptoms such as fever, headache, and sore throat, providing relief and comfort to those suffering from the flu.

However, it is important to note that while elderberry tea can help to manage flu symptoms, it is not a substitute for medical treatment, and individuals should consult with a healthcare professional for severe or persistent symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make elderberry tea for flu.

  1. To make elderberry tea for the flu, start by boiling 1 cup of water in a saucepan, then add 1-2 tablespoons of dried elderberries (or 2-3 tablespoons of fresh elderberries) and let it simmer on low heat for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea into a cup, using a fine-mesh strainer or cheesecloth, to remove the elderberries.
  3. Optional: To enhance the tea's medicinal properties and soothe a sore throat, add 1-2 slices of fresh ginger, 1 cinnamon stick, or a few whole cloves during the simmering process (step 1).
  4. For added sweetness and vitamin C, squeeze half a lemon and stir in 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey after straining the tea.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, 2-3 times a day, as needed, for relief of flu symptoms.

2. Echinacea

Echinacea tea helps with flu because it contains properties that can boost the immune system, potentially reducing the duration and severity of flu symptoms.

The tea is made from the leaves and flowers of the echinacea plant, which are rich in antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory effects. Some studies suggest that echinacea may help to reduce inflammation in the respiratory system, alleviating congestion and coughing. Additionally, echinacea may have antiviral properties, which could help to prevent the flu virus from replicating in the body.

However, it's important to note that echinacea should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment for the flu, and individuals with certain medical conditions or who are taking certain medications should consult with a healthcare provider before using echinacea.

The following procedure explaing how to make echinacea tea for flu.

  1. To make echinacea tea for the flu, start by boiling 2 cups of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried echinacea flowers and/or leaves, then let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Optional: add other flu-fighting ingredients such as 1 tablespoon of freshly grated ginger, 1 cinnamon stick, and/or a few cloves for added flavor and immune-boosting properties.
  3. After simmering, remove the tea from heat and let it cool for a few minutes before straining out the solid plant material.
  4. Stir in a natural sweetener like raw honey (to taste, usually 1-2 teaspoons) for added soothing effects on a sore throat.
  5. Sip the tea while still warm, 2-3 times a day, to help alleviate flu symptoms and support the immune system in fighting off the virus.

3. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with flu because it contains menthol, a compound with natural decongestant and expectorant properties that help clear nasal congestion and soothe a sore throat.

Its antiviral and antimicrobial effects can potentially reduce flu symptoms and shorten the duration of the illness. The warm temperature of the tea can help alleviate body aches and pains, while its hydrating properties can prevent dehydration caused by fever and excessive sweating.

Additionally, peppermint tea can help stimulate digestion and improve nutrient absorption, which is crucial for boosting the immune system and promoting faster recovery.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for flu.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves or 2 peppermint tea bags with 8 ounces of boiling water, then let it steep for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors and therapeutic oils to infuse.
  2. While the tea is steeping, crush 1 peppermint candy or peppermint lozenge using a mortar and pestle or the back of a spoon, creating small, fine pieces.
  3. After the steeping time has elapsed, remove the peppermint leaves or tea bags and stir in the crushed peppermint candy until fully dissolved.
  4. Optional: For added vitamin C and soothing qualities, squeeze half a lemon's juice into the tea and add a small dollop of honey (about 1/2 to 1 teaspoon) to taste.
  5. Serve the peppermint tea warm, sipping it slowly to soothe a sore throat and relieve congestion, and enjoy the calming effects to help you rest and recover from the flu.

4. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with flu because it contains antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties that can help the body fight off the flu virus and alleviate symptoms.

The gingerol and shaogals in ginger tea can inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract. Additionally, ginger tea can help soothe a sore throat, relieve congestion, and boost the immune system due to its high antioxidant content. Furthermore, ginger tea can also help alleviate nausea and vomiting, common symptoms associated with the flu.

Overall, ginger tea's natural anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and immune-boosting properties make it an effective natural remedy for treating flu symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for flu.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger, then combine in a pot with 4 cups of water, 1 cinnamon stick, and 1 tablespoon of honey; bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat to low, cover and let it simmer for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  3. Remove from heat, add the juice of half a lemon and optional 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric; stir well.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup, pressing on the solids to extract all liquid.
  5. Serve warm and enjoy this soothing ginger tea, perfect for helping to alleviate flu symptoms.

5. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with flu because it has antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce symptoms and duration of the flu.

The active compound in licorice root, glycyrrhizin, inhibits the replication of the flu virus and boosts the immune system's response to the virus. Additionally, licorice root tea can soothe a sore throat, relieve coughing, and alleviate congestion, making it easier to breathe. Furthermore, it can help break up phlegm and mucus, making it easier to expel them from the body.

However, it is important to note that licorice root tea should be consumed in moderation, as excessive consumption can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and hormonal imbalances.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for flu.

  1. To make licorice root tea for flu, bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add 1-2 tablespoons of dried licorice root and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea into a teapot or a heat-resistant container, discarding the used licorice root.
  3. Optionally, add honey or lemon to taste for added flavor and potential additional flu-fighting benefits.
  4. Pour the tea into a cup, ensuring it's warm and comfortable to drink.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your throat and potentially alleviate some flu symptoms.

6. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with flu because it has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that can alleviate symptoms such as fever, headache, and muscle aches.

Its soothing effect on the digestive system can help reduce nausea and vomiting, common in flu cases. Chamomile tea is also a natural antioxidant and contains antimicrobial properties that can help boost the immune system and fight off the flu virus. Furthermore, its calming effects can promote better sleep, which is essential for the body to recover from the flu.

However, it should be noted that while chamomile tea can help manage flu symptoms, it is not a cure and should not replace medical treatment.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for flu.

  1. In a cup, pour boiling water (8 ounces) over 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers, cover and let it steep for 15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers and discard them.
  3. Add honey (1-2 teaspoons) or lemon juice (1-2 teaspoons) to taste, if desired.
  4. Warm the tea in the microwave or on the stove if it has cooled down, to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, relaxing and allowing the chamomile's soothing properties to help with flu symptoms.

7. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea helps with flu because it has anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antioxidant properties that can help alleviate symptoms and strengthen the immune system.

The warm temperature of the tea can soothe a sore throat, while the natural oils in cinnamon, such as cinnamaldehyde, can help fight off viruses and bacteria. Additionally, cinnamon tea can help reduce congestion and promote respiratory health by acting as an expectorant, helping to clear mucus and phlegm from the respiratory tract. Furthermore, cinnamon tea can help boost energy levels and reduce fatigue, which are common symptoms of the flu.

Lastly, cinnamon tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to other beverages, making it a healthy and comforting choice for those looking to soothe flu symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make cinnamon tea for flu.

  1. In a pot, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1 cinnamon stick and 1 teaspoon of honey, reducing heat to a simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Crush 1/2 teaspoon of cloves and add to the pot, simmering for an additional 3 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, then add 1 teaspoon of lemon juice and optional 1/2 teaspoon of grated ginger; cover and steep for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  5. Serve hot, sip slowly, and repeat as needed for relief of flu symptoms.

8. Yarrow

Yarrow tea helps with flu because it contains anti-viral and anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce symptoms and shorten the duration of the illness.

The tea's natural chemicals, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, can help to stimulate the immune system and fight off the flu virus. Additionally, yarrow tea can help to alleviate fever, reduce coughing, and soothe a sore throat. Its diaphoretic properties can also promote sweating, which can help to eliminate toxins from the body and speed up recovery.

Furthermore, yarrow tea has been used traditionally as a natural remedy for respiratory and digestive issues, making it a valuable tool in managing flu-related symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make yarrow tea for flu.

  1. To make yarrow tea for the flu, start by boiling 2 cups of water and adding 2-3 teaspoons of dried yarrow flowers or 4-6 fresh yarrow flowering tops to a teapot.
  2. Allow the water to steep with the yarrow for 10 minutes, covered, to help extract the medicinal compounds and essential oils.
  3. After 10 minutes, strain the tea to remove the yarrow plant material, leaving only the liquid.
  4. For added flavor and additional flu-fighting properties, consider adding 1 teaspoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice to your yarrow tea before drinking.
  5. Sip the yarrow tea slowly, allowing it to warm you from the inside and soothe your sore throat, helping to alleviate symptoms of the flu. Enjoy up to three cups of yarrow tea per day as needed.

10. Sage

Sage tea helps with flu because it has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can aid in alleviating symptoms and reducing the duration of the illness.

The rosmarinic acid and safficinolide present in sage have been shown to inhibit the growth of viruses, including those that cause the flu. Additionally, sage tea can help soothe a sore throat, reduce fever, and alleviate coughing fits, providing relief from some of the most unpleasant symptoms of the flu. Furthermore, sage is rich in antioxidants, which can help boost the immune system and speed up recovery.

Lastly, drinking warm sage tea can promote relaxation and improve sleep, which is essential for the body to fight off the flu virus and recover more quickly.

The following procedure explaing how to make sage tea for flu.

  1. To make sage tea for flu, first bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1-2 teaspoons of fresh sage leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried sage leaves, ensuring the water is covering the herbs.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 10-15 minutes, ensuring it's covered to preserve the essential oils from evaporating.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the sage leaves, discarding them once strained.
  4. If desired, add honey or lemon to taste for added soothing properties and flavor.
  5. Sip the warm sage tea, ideally 2-3 times a day, while symptoms persist to potentially alleviate flu symptoms.
