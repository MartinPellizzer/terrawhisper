10 best herbal teas for eye health

Certain herbal teas are known to benefit eye health due to their antioxidant properties and ability to reduce inflammation.

Among the best herbal teas for eye health are green tea, chamomile tea, and rooibos tea. Green tea is rich in catechins, which have been shown to protect the eyes from oxidative stress and reduce the risk of cataracts and age-related macular degeneration.

Chamomile tea contains antioxidants that can help reduce inflammation in the eyes and soothe irritation. Rooibos tea is also high in antioxidants and has been shown to improve eye health by protecting the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

Drinking these herbal teas regularly can help maintain healthy eyes and reduce the risk of eye diseases.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains antioxidants, like flavonoids, which directly benefit eye health.

These flavonoids protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, preventing age-related macular degeneration and promoting overall eye health. Furthermore, chamomile tea's anti-inflammatory properties reduce inflammation in the eye, alleviating irritation and soothing strained eyes. The tea's antimicrobial properties also inhibit the growth of bacteria that can lead to eye infections.

Additionally, chamomile tea's ability to relax muscles, combined with its cool temperature when used as a compress, can reduce eye strain and fatigue.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with eye health.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain antioxidants that support eye health by fighting damaging free radicals.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe irritated eyes.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in flavonoids, which are known to protect the eyes from UV radiation.

Ginkgo Biloba tea contains antioxidants that protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, according to scientific research.

4. Bilberry

Bilberry tea contains high levels of antioxidants, particularly anthocyanins, which protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

These antioxidants enhance the health of the eyes by improving blood flow in the capillaries of the eye and strengthening the collagen in eye tissue. Moreover, bilberry tea reinforces rhodopsin, a purple pigment in the eye responsible for night vision, facilitating vision in low-light conditions. Additionally, it reduces eye fatigue and inflammation, providing relief for individuals who use digital devices for extended periods.

Regular consumption of bilberry tea, therefore, promotes overall eye health by providing essential antioxidants and supporting crucial eye functions.

  • Flowers: Containing flavonoids and other beneficial compounds, bilberry flowers are used in medicinal teas that enhance eye health by reducing inflammation.

5. Rooibos

Rooibos tea, native to South Africa, contains several antioxidants such as aspalathin and quercetin, which protect the eyes from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals.

One specific antioxidant, aspalathin, found exclusively in rooibos, positively influences the body's antioxidant defenses and helps maintain eye health. Moreover, rooibos tea's anti-inflammatory properties, owing to the presence of the antioxidant quercetin, alleviate inflammation in the eye tissue, further contributing to overall eye health. Additionally, rooibos tea's ability to improve circulation ensures a consistent supply of oxygen and essential nutrients to the eyes, promoting their overall well-being.

Lastly, rooibos tea's natural supply of vital minerals, including zinc and copper, contributes to enzyme production and supports the long-term maintenance of eye health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in rooibos tea that help with eye health.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, the leaves of the rooibos plant are used to make medicinal tea that supports eye health by neutralizing free radicals.

6. Saffron

Saffron tea, derived from the Crocus sativus flower, contains saffron, a potent antioxidant.

This antioxidant, known as crocin, protects the eyes by neutralizing free radicals that damage cells. Research demonstrates that saffron tea stimulates eye health by reducing oxidative stress, improving retinal function, and slowing down age-related macular degeneration. Furthermore, saffron's anti-inflammatory properties alleviate eye inflammation, ensuring optimal eye health.

Consequently, regular consumption of saffron tea bolsters eye health, contributing to overall visual well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in saffron tea that help with eye health.

  • Flowers: Saffron flowers contain high levels of antioxidants, which help protect the eyes from damage and improve eye health.
  • Stigmas: The stigmas of saffron flowers, also known as saffron threads, contain crocin and safranal, which are beneficial for eye health by improving vision and reducing eye inflammation.
  • Leaves: Saffron leaves have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that support eye health, reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration.

8. Nettle

Regular consumption of nettle tea provides these nutrients, supporting long-term eye health and reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in nettle tea that help with eye health.
