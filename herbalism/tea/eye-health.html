10 Best Herbal Teas For Eye Health

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients known to benefit eye health such as chamomile, bilberry, and ginkgo biloba, can help support eye health due to their antioxidant properties.

These antioxidants help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to age-related macular degeneration and cataracts. Additionally, some herbal teas contain anti-inflammatory compounds that can help reduce inflammation in the eyes and alleviate symptoms of dry eye.

Furthermore, herbal teas like green tea contain epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), which has been shown to improve blood flow to the eyes and potentially prevent glaucoma. However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can support eye health, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment or regular eye exams.

Lastly, it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before consuming herbal teas for eye health or any other health condition.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with eye health because it is rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce eye strain and puffiness.

The tea's antioxidants, such as flavones and terpenoids, can help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to age-related macular degeneration. Drinking chamomile tea may also help reduce the risk of developing cataracts due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Additionally, chamomile tea contains riboflavin, or vitamin B2, which is essential for maintaining healthy eyes and preventing eye fatigue.

However, it is important to note that while chamomile tea can support eye health, it should not replace medical treatment for eye conditions.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for eye health.

  1. To make chamomile tea for eye health, start by boiling 8 ounces of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers; let it steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain and allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  2. While the tea is cooling, soak two clean washcloths or cotton pads in cold water and wring them out.
  3. Place the soaked washcloths or cotton pads in the refrigerator to chill for about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Once the chamomile tea has cooled and the washcloths are chilled, soak the washcloths in the chamomile tea and wring out the excess liquid.
  5. Finally, lie down and place the chamomile-soaked washcloths over your closed eyes for 10-15 minutes, repeating this process 2-3 times a week for best results.

2. Eyebright

Eyebright tea helps with eye health because it contains nutrients and antioxidants that are beneficial for the eyes.

The plant eyebright, from which the tea is derived, has traditionally been used in folk medicine to treat eye-related conditions. Eyebright tea is believed to contain anti-inflammatory and astringent properties that may help reduce eye redness, irritation, and puffiness. Additionally, some studies suggest that eyebright may have antimicrobial properties that could potentially protect the eyes from infections.

However, it is important to note that while eyebright tea may support overall eye health, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment of serious eye conditions.

The following procedure explains how to make eyebright tea for eye health.

  1. To make eyebright tea, start by boiling 8 ounces of water, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried eyebright herb (depending on your taste preference) to a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Allow the eyebright herb to steep in the hot water for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  3. After steeping, remove the tea infuser or strain out the herbs from the teapot.
  4. For best results, let the eyebright tea cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Finally, enjoy a cup of eyebright tea once or twice a day, preferably after meals, to support eye health.

3. Ginkgo Biloba

Ginkgo biloba tea helps with eye health because it contains high levels of antioxidants that can protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

These antioxidants help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the eyes, which can lead to improved vision and reduced risk of age-related macular degeneration. Additionally, ginkgo biloba tea can improve blood flow to the eyes, ensuring that they receive the necessary nutrients and oxygen for optimal function. Some studies have also suggested that ginkgo biloba may help to reduce eye strain and improve visual processing speed.

Furthermore, ginkgo biloba tea has been shown to protect the health of the optic nerve, which is crucial for maintaining overall eye health and preventing conditions such as glaucoma.

The following procedure explains how to make ginkgo biloba tea for eye health.

  1. To make Ginkgo Biloba tea for eye health, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot, then add 2 teaspoons of dried Ginkgo Biloba leaves, reduce heat to low, cover and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. After 10 minutes, turn off the heat and let the tea steep for an additional 15 minutes to allow all the beneficial compounds to be released into the water.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine mesh strainer to remove any leaves or sediment.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for flavor enhancement.
  5. Drink one cup of this tea twice daily, preferably after meals, to maintain good eye health.

4. Bilberry

Bilberry tea helps with eye health because it contains high levels of antioxidants, particularly anthocyanosides, which are known to strengthen blood vessels, including the delicate capillaries in the eyes.

These antioxidants also protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts. Additionally, bilberry tea can help reduce eye strain and inflammation, and improve night vision, thanks to its ability to help regulate the production of rhodopsin, a pigment in the eyes that is sensitive to light.

Furthermore, bilberry tea has been shown to improve circulation in the eye area, ensuring that the eyes receive a consistent supply of oxygen and nutrients.

The following procedure explains how to make bilberry tea for eye health.

  1. To make bilberry tea for eye health, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1 teaspoon of dried bilberry leaves or 2 teaspoons of fresh bilberry leaves; let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, and allow the liquid to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  3. For an added boost to eye health, squeeze half a lemon (about 2 tablespoons) into the tea for its vitamin C content, which aids in bilberry absorption.
  4. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey to sweeten the tea, if desired, and to provide additional antioxidants.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of this bilberry tea daily for at least a month to notice improvements in eye health.

5. Rooibos

Rooibos tea helps with eye health because it contains high levels of antioxidants, such as aspalathin and quercetin, which protect the eyes from oxidative stress and damage.

One antioxidant in particular, called alpha-glucosidase inhibitor, has been shown to prevent the formation of cataracts by inhibiting the formation of certain sugars that lead to cataracts. Rooibos tea also contains zinc, which is essential for maintaining eye health as it helps bring vitamin A from the liver to the retina to produce melanin, a pigment that protects the eyes. Furthermore, rooibos tea's anti-inflammatory properties can help alleviate eye inflammation and irritation, promoting overall eye health.

Lastly, rooibos tea's high levels of vitamin C, which is known to boost the immune system, can also help protect the eyes from infections and promote overall eye health.

The following procedure explains how to make rooibos tea for eye health.

  1. To make rooibos tea for eye health, bring 8 cups of water to a boil, then add 1-2 tablespoons of high-quality loose-leaf rooibos tea or 4-6 rooibos tea bags.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to maximize the release of antioxidants, which are beneficial for eye health.
  3. While the tea is still warm, add 1-2 teaspoons of honey to sweeten it naturally and enhance the tea's anti-inflammatory properties.
  4. Optionally, squeeze half a lemon into the tea for an extra dose of vitamin C, which can further support eye health.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature, ideally 1-2 times a day, to maintain healthy eyes and overall eye function.

6. Saffron

Saffron tea helps with eye health because it contains saffron, a spice derived from the crocus sativus flower, which has been shown to contain compounds that may help protect the eyes from damage and improve vision.

These compounds, including crocin and crocetin, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce the risk of age-related macular degeneration and other eye diseases. Saffron has also been shown to improve blood flow to the eye, which can help improve overall eye health and function. Additionally, saffron may help protect the eyes from the damaging effects of blue light, which is emitted by screens and can contribute to digital eye strain.

Regularly consuming saffron tea can provide these eye health benefits, making it a simple and delicious way to support healthy vision.

The following procedure explains how to make saffron tea for eye health.

  1. In a small saucepan, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then reduce heat to low and stir in 15-20 saffron threads, letting it simmer for 10-15 minutes to extract the color and flavor.
  2. Warm 1 cup of milk in a separate pot over medium heat, but avoid bringing it to a boil.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of honey or maple syrup and a pinch of ground cardamom to the warmed milk, stirring until the sweetener is fully dissolved.
  4. Combine the saffron-infused water with the sweetened milk in a teapot or teacup, mixing well.
  5. Let the saffron tea cool down to a drinkable temperature, then enjoy this soothing and potentially vision-boosting beverage once or twice a day.

7. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with eye health because it contains antioxidants like vitamin C and bioflavonoids that can help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

Additionally, fennel tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the eyes and alleviate symptoms of dry eye syndrome. Furthermore, fennel tea may help improve blood flow to the eyes, which can help nourish the eyes and improve overall eye health. The tea's mild diuretic effect can also help reduce eye puffiness and dark circles.

Finally, fennel tea has been shown to have potential benefits for preventing age-related macular degeneration, a leading cause of vision loss in older adults.

The following procedure explains how to make fennel tea for eye health.

  1. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds, reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat, cover the pot and let it steep for an additional 10 minutes to allow the nutrients to be released into the water.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the fennel seeds, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add a drizzle of honey or a squeeze of lemon for taste, if desired, but avoid adding milk or other dairy products.
  5. Drink up to three cups of this tea daily for best results, sipping it slowly to fully enjoy its taste and benefits for your eye health.

8. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with eye health because it is rich in nutrients that are essential for optimal eye function.

It contains high levels of vitamin A, a key nutrient for maintaining healthy vision, as well as antioxidants like vitamin C and vitamin E that protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. Nettle tea also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the eyes and alleviate symptoms of conditions like dry eye and conjunctivitis. Additionally, nettle tea may help improve circulation, including blood flow to the eyes, which can support overall eye health.

Finally, some studies suggest that nettle tea may have protective effects against age-related macular degeneration, a leading cause of vision loss in older adults.

The following procedure explains how to make nettle tea for eye health.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh stinging nettle leaves (wear gloves to handle them and avoid skin contact), rinse thoroughly, and place in a pot with 4 cups of water; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 10 minutes.
  2. After simmering, remove pot from heat and let the tea steep for an additional 10 minutes to extract maximum nutrients; then strain the tea to remove leaves and discard them.
  3. While waiting for the tea to steep, squeeze fresh lemon juice from half a lemon (about 2 tablespoons) into a large mug.
  4. Pour the strained nettle tea into the mug with lemon juice, stir well, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1 cup of this nettle tea with lemon juice daily, preferably in the morning or early afternoon, for its potential benefits to eye health.

9. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with eye health because it contains high levels of antioxidants, such as zeaxanthin and lutein, which are known to protect the eyes from damage caused by harmful blue light and oxidative stress.

These antioxidants also help to reduce the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts, two common eye conditions that can lead to vision loss. Additionally, dandelion tea is rich in vitamin A, a nutrient that is essential for maintaining healthy vision and preventing night blindness. The tea may also help to reduce inflammation in the eyes, which can alleviate symptoms of dry eye and other eye irritations.

Furthermore, dandelion tea has been shown to improve blood circulation, including the circulation to the eyes, which can help to nourish the eyes and keep them healthy.

The following procedure explains how to make dandelion tea for eye health.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh dandelion flowers, making sure they are free of pesticides and other contaminants, and rinse them gently in cool water; let the flowers dry naturally.
  2. Once dry, place the dandelion flowers in a heat-resistant container and pour 2 cups of boiling water over them; cover the container and let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the dandelion flowers, and discard the used flowers; you should have approximately 16 ounces of dandelion tea.
  4. Optional: add a small amount of raw honey or lemon juice to enhance the flavor of the tea, if desired.
  5. Consume the tea while it is still warm, 1-2 times a day, to help support eye health; store any remaining tea in the refrigerator for up to 48 hours.

10. Linden

Linden tea helps with eye health because it contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce eye strain and inflammation.

The tea is rich in bioflavonoids and vitamin C, which are known to support eye health by strengthening capillary walls and improving circulation in the eye area. Additionally, linden tea has been shown to have a soothing effect on the eyes, potentially alleviating symptoms of dry eye and computer vision syndrome. Furthermore, its antioxidant properties may help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts.

Finally, regular consumption of linden tea may also help regulate blood sugar levels, which is important for overall eye health, as high blood sugar levels can increase the risk of diabetic retinopathy.

The following procedure explains how to make linden tea for eye health.

  1. To make linden tea for eye health, bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried linden flowers, reducing heat to a simmer for 10 minutes to allow the nutrients to infuse.
  2. After simmering, remove the tea from heat and let it cool down for a few minutes, ensuring it's still warm but not too hot to the touch.
  3. Strain the tea into a clean glass or ceramic container, discarding the used linden flowers.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature, about 10-15 more minutes, before using it as a warm compress on closed eyes for 10-15 minutes.
  5. For best results, use this linden tea compress once or twice daily, massaging any remaining tea into the skin around the eyes gently before rinsing with cool water.
