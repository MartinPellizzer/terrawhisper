10 best herbal teas for nausea

Certain herbal teas are known to help soothe nausea and provide relief for an upset stomach.

Ginger tea is one of the most popular choices due to its carminative properties, which can help break up and expel intestinal gas. Peppermint tea is another excellent option, as it contains menthol, which can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and alleviate nausea.

Chamomile tea is also known for its calming effects on the stomach, and its anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Lastly, licorice root tea can help coat the stomach lining and protect it from acid, making it a good choice for those experiencing nausea due to acid reflux. However, it is essential to note that licorice root tea should be used with caution, as excessive consumption can lead to high blood pressure and other health issues.

As with any natural remedy, it is always a good idea to consult with a healthcare provider before consuming herbal teas for nausea, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.

1. Ginger

Ginger contains active compounds proven to alleviate digestive issues, including nausea.

Specifically, its primary component, gingerol, neutralizes acid in the stomach and stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, promoting smooth digestion. Consequently, ginger tea effectively soothes queasiness, providing rapid relief.

Additionally, this natural remedy has no known side effects, making it a popular and reliable choice for managing nausea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with nausea.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are a powerful ingredient in medicinal tea for nausea. They contain phenolic compounds which are known to help reduce feelings of sickness and relax the gastrointestinal muscles.
  • Roots: Ginger roots are widely used in teas to alleviate nausea. They have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the stomach and provide relief from queasiness.
  • Leaves: Ginger leaves are an excellent addition to medicinal tea for nausea. They are rich in antioxidants that can aid in digestion and reduce the symptoms of nausea.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers are used in teas to help combat nausea. They contain active compounds that can help regulate the digestive system and provide quick relief from queasiness.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds are sometimes included in medicinal tea for nausea. They have been found to have calming effects on the stomach and can help to alleviate the symptoms of nausea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for nausea.

  1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger root.
  2. In a teapot, combine grated ginger with 1 cup of water and boil for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the ginger-water mixture into a cup, discarding the solid ginger.
  4. Add honey and lemon juice to taste, if desired.
  5. Sip the ginger tea slowly while it is still warm to soothe nausea.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea alleviates nausea due to its active components, particularly menthol.

This compound relaxes the gastrointestinal tract, reducing symptoms of nausea. Furthermore, peppermint tea's soothing warmth stimulates the vagus nerve, which controls stomach emptying. Consequently, this process eases feelings of nausea.

Additionally, peppermint tea's aroma activates the brain's satisfaction center, leading to a decrease in nausea sensation.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with nausea.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, peppermint leaves are well-known for soothing the stomach and alleviating nausea symptoms.
  • Flowers: High in volatile oils, peppermint flowers offer calming effects on the digestive system, helping to reduce nausea.
  • Stems: With similar properties to leaves, peppermint stems contribute to the tea's soothing effects on the stomach and aid in easing nausea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for nausea.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 cup of boiling water with 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves.
  2. Steep the tea for 5-10 minutes to allow the peppermint leaves to release their oils.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the used peppermint leaves.
  4. Optionally, add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your stomach and alleviate nausea.

3. Chamomile

Chamomile tea combats nausea due to its antiemetic properties, which come from its volatile oils like chamazulene and bisabolol.

These compounds help relax the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, reducing tension and spasms that can cause nausea. Moreover, chamomile acts as a natural nervine, calming the nervous system and alleviating stress-induced nausea. Additionally, it contains flavonoids that act on the same receptors as anti-nausea drugs, further helping to combat feelings of queasiness.

Lastly, chamomile's soothing warmth enhances gastric juices' flow, promoting healthy digestion and preventing nausea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with nausea.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers are used in medicinal tea for nausea due to their calming properties that help soothe the stomach.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain essential oils and antispasmodic properties that aid in reducing nausea.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in antioxidants and have carminative effects that help alleviate nausea and promote digestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for nausea.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes to release chamomile's calming properties.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) to the hot water.
  3. Steep chamomile in the water for 5-10 minutes to extract its soothing compounds.
  4. Strain or remove tea bag, discarding solids.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your mouth and soothe your stomach.

4. Licorice

Licorice tea relieves nausea due to its active component, glycyrrhizin, which possesses anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties.

This compound stimulates the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, protecting the stomach lining and reducing irritation. Moreover, glycyrrhizin inhibits the enzyme responsible for breaking down prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that regulate the contraction and relaxation of muscles in the stomach, thus decreasing the intensity and frequency of nausea. Additionally, licorice tea alleviates stress and anxiety, which often trigger nausea, by promoting relaxation and balancing cortisol levels.

Lastly, this soothing beverage hydrates the body, replenishing essential fluids lost due to vomiting, further contributing to the reduction of nausea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with nausea.

  • Roots: The underground root system of the licorice plant contains glycyrrhizin, a compound that helps soothe digestive system and alleviate nausea.
  • Rhyzomes: Similar to roots, the underground stem-like structures called rhyzomes of the licorice plant are rich in glycyrrhizin, providing relief from nausea.
  • Leaves: Although not as potent as roots and rhyzomes, the leaves of the licorice plant also contain beneficial compounds that can help reduce sensations of nausea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for nausea.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Steep the tea for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root pieces.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your mouth and throat, and soothe your stomach.

5. Fennel

Fennel tea provides relief from nausea due to its carminative and anti-emetic properties.

This brew, derived from Foeniculum vulgare, contains volatile oils like anethole, fenchone, and estragole that relax gastrointestinal spasms. Fennel's active components accelerate gastric emptying, alleviating symptoms of nausea. Moreover, fennel tea's antispasmodic qualities soothe irritated mucous membranes in the digestive tract, further reducing sensations of queasiness.

Lastly, clinical studies support fennel tea's ability to reduce the frequency and intensity of nausea, particularly in postoperative settings.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in fennel tea that help with nausea.

  • Seeds: Fennel seeds are excellent for making medicinal tea for nausea due to their carminative properties that help relieve digestive issues.
  • Leaves: The leaves of fennel contain volatile oils, which are beneficial in reducing nausea and promoting healthy digestion.
  • Stems: Fennel stems have calming properties that soothe the stomach, making them an ideal addition to a medicinal tea for nausea.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the fennel plant contain antispasmodic compounds, which are known to alleviate nausea and aid in digestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fennel tea for nausea.

  1. Chop: Slice 1 tablespoon of fresh fennel seeds or 1 teaspoon of crushed fennel seeds.
  2. Steep: Place chopped fennel in a tea infuser, and steep in a cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain: Remove the infuser, and discard the used fennel.
  4. Sweeten (optional): Stir in honey or sugar to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve: Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed to alleviate nausea.

7. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea combats nausea through its powerful demulcent properties, coating the lining of the stomach and easing irritation.

This soothing action reduces inflammation, creates a protective barrier, and encourages healing of any stomach lining damage causing nausea. Furthermore, it promotes mucus production, which lubricates the gastrointestinal tract, further alleviating symptoms.

Ultimately, these mechanisms of action work together to provide relief from nausea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with nausea.

  • Bark: The inner bark of the slippery elm tree is used to make medicinal tea for nausea due to its demulcent properties, which mean it has a soothing effect on the mucous membranes lining the digestive tract.
  • Leaves: Slippery elm leaves contain high amounts of mucilage, a gel-like substance that coats the stomach and helps alleviate nausea.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the slippery elm plant contain a high concentration of mucilage, making them useful for creating a tea that can help soothe an upset stomach and reduce feelings of nausea.
  • Roots: While less commonly used than other parts of the plant, slippery elm roots can also be used to make medicinal tea to help treat nausea, thanks to their high mucilage content.
  • Stems: Slippery elm stems can be used to create a tea that can provide relief from nausea due to the demulcent and emollient properties present in the stem's mucilage.
  • Buds: Slippery elm buds are rich in mucilage, which can be used to make a tea that can help soothe the stomach and alleviate feelings of nausea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for nausea.

  1. Measure: Scoop 1 teaspoon of slippery elm bark powder.
  2. Combine: Stir the powder into 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Steep: Let the mixture simmer for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Strain: Pour the tea through a fine-mesh strainer into a cup.
  5. Sip: Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed.

8. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the Marshmallow plant's roots, has long-established medicinal uses due to its mucilage content.

This mucilage forms a soothing, protective layer in the digestive tract, easing irritation. By reducing inflammation, the tea alleviates one primary cause of nausea. Moreover, its antispasmodic properties relax the stomach muscles, further minimizing discomfort.

Consequently, marshmallow tea's natural components effectively combat nausea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with nausea.

  • Leaves: Rich in tannins and flavonoids, leaves of the marshmallow plant bring anti-inflammatory properties that soothe digestive lining, alleviating nausea.
  • Flowers: Marshmallow flowers contain mucilage that coats the stomach, protecting it from irritants and offering quick relief from nausea.
  • Roots: The roots of marshmallow plant are abundant in mucilage and starch, forming a soothing barrier against digestive issues, including nausea.
  • Seeds: Marshmallow seeds contain high levels of mucilage, making it an effective demulcent, providing comfort to the gastrointestinal tract, and reducing nausea symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for nausea.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 2 slices of fresh ginger, then bring to a boil and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried marshmallow root and 1 cinnamon stick, then simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, add 1 teaspoon of honey (or to taste), and let the mixture cool for a few minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, relaxing and breathing deeply to aid digestion.

10. Valerian

Valerian tea's soothing properties stem from its active compounds, including valerenic acid and valepotriates, which impact the central nervous system.

These components produce a calming effect on the gastrointestinal tract, reducing muscle spasms and decreasing acid production, ultimately alleviating the sensation of nausea. In addition, valerian tea's sedative effects can help regulate stress hormones that often exacerbate nausea. Thus, the combined actions of these constituents found in valerian tea make it an effective natural remedy for tackling nausea, providing users with a drug-free alternative to combat queasiness.

These mechanisms are well-established and contribute to the therapeutic potential of valerian tea, offering a time-tested approach to managing nausea and promoting overall digestive well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in valerian tea that help with nausea.

  • Roots: Valerian roots are used to make medicinal tea for nausea due to their richness in antispasmodic compounds, which help relax the stomach muscles.
  • Rhizomes: Valerian rhizomes are used in tea to alleviate nausea, as they contain active constituents that have a calming effect on the gastrointestinal tract.
  • Leaves: Valerian leaves are included in medicinal tea for nausea because they possess volatile oils and antioxidants that support digestive health and help reduce symptoms of queasiness.
  • Flowers: Valerian flowers are used in tea for nausea treatment, as they have soothing properties that help relieve digestive discomfort and promote relaxation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tea for nausea.

  1. In a teapot, add 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root to 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Cover and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day.
