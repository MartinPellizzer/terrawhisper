1. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with nausea because it contains gingerol, a compound that helps reduce nausea and vomiting by acting on the digestive system's nervous system.

This compound is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, further helping to alleviate feelings of nausea. Ginger tea can also help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, promoting the breakdown of foods and easing the passage of food through the digestive system, thereby preventing nausea. Additionally, the warm temperature of ginger tea can help relax the stomach muscles, reducing the likelihood of nausea and providing a soothing effect on the stomach.

Lastly, ginger's long history as a natural remedy for nausea and digestive issues lends credibility to its effectiveness, as many people have found relief from nausea through the consumption of ginger in various forms, including tea.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with nausea.

  1. Gingerols: Gingerols, the main bioactive compound in ginger, are known to reduce nausea by interacting with the gut's nervous system, providing soothing relief.
  2. Shogaols: Shogaols, a pungent compound formed when ginger is dried or cooked, can help alleviate nausea by activating neurotransmitters that regulate stomach contractions and calm feelings of queasiness.
  3. Paradols: Paradols, a milder derivative of gingerols and shogaols, can help manage nausea by modulating serotonin receptors in the brain, thus reducing symptoms of queasiness and vomiting.
  4. Zingerone: Zingerone, an aromatic compound in ginger, has anti-emetic properties and can help with nausea by inhibiting the production of gastric acid and promoting gut motility.

The following list reveals what parts of ginger are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for nausea.

  1. Rhizomes: The underground stem of ginger, known as rhizomes, is used in medicinal tea to alleviate nausea due to its anti-emetic properties.
  2. Roots: Ginger roots are rich in gingerols and shogaols, which make them effective in reducing inflammation and nausea.
  3. Buds: The tender, immature flower buds of ginger contain high levels of antioxidants that help soothe the stomach and reduce feelings of nausea.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for nausea.

  1. Peel and chop one inch of fresh ginger into small slices, then bring 2 cups of water to a boil.
  2. Add the chopped ginger to the boiling water, reduce heat, and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, and stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and the juice of half a lemon.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the ginger pieces, then let it cool for a few minutes.
  5. Slowly sip the ginger tea to soothe your stomach and alleviate nausea.
