10 Best Herbal Teas For Bowel Movement

Herbal teas, particularly those made from senna, licorice root, ginger, and peppermint, can promote bowel movements due to their natural laxative, anti-inflammatory, and carminative properties.

Senna contains anthraquinones, which stimulate the intestines and increase muscle contractions, helping to move stool through the digestive tract. Licorice root can soothe the gastrointestinal lining and help maintain a healthy mucosal barrier, reducing inflammation and facilitating bowel movements.

Ginger has been shown to accelerate gastric emptying and stimulate antral contractions, alleviating digestive issues and encouraging regular bowel movements. Peppermint oil, derived from peppermint leaves, can relax gastrointestinal smooth muscles, thereby alleviating symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome and supporting normal bowel function.

Overall, these herbal teas can help maintain a healthy gastrointestinal system and support regular bowel movements.

1. Senna Leaves

Senna leaves tea helps with bowel movement because it contains natural stimulant laxatives called anthraquinones.

These compounds work by increasing the water absorption in the intestines, which softens the stool and makes it easier to pass. Additionally, senna leaves tea can stimulate the muscles in the intestinal walls to contract, helping to move the stool through the digestive tract more efficiently. The tea is commonly used as a natural remedy for constipation and has been shown in several studies to be effective in promoting regular bowel movements.

It's important to note that senna leaves tea should only be used short-term and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as long-term use can lead to dependency and other potential side effects.

The following procedure explaing how to make senna leaves tea for bowel movement.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a saucepan or kettle.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried senna leaves (or 1 senna tea bag) to a teapot or cup.
  3. Pour the hot water over the senna leaves in the teapot or cup.
  4. Steep the senna leaves for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea (if using loose leaves) and drink it. Enjoy the tea as part of your daily routine for promoting bowel movement.

2. Slippery Elm Bark

Slippery elm bark tea helps with bowel movement because of its high mucilage content.

Mucilage is a type of fiber that absorbs water and forms a gel-like substance in the digestive tract. This gel coats and soothes the intestinal walls, allowing for easier passage of stool.

Additionally, slippery elm bark tea contains demulcent properties which help reduce inflammation in the digestive system, further promoting regular bowel movements. The tea's ability to absorb water also helps increase the bulk of stools, making them softer and easier to pass.

Finally, slippery elm bark tea has been used traditionally for centuries as a natural remedy for constipation and irregularity, making it a trusted choice for those seeking relief from bowel issues.

The following procedure explaing how to make slippery elm bark tea for bowel movement.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot and add 2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark to it.
  2. Reduce heat to a simmer and let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove any solid particles and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea 1-2 times a day, as needed, to help promote bowel movement.

3. Psyllium Husk

Psyllium husk tea helps with bowel movement because it is a natural source of soluble fiber, which absorbs water and forms a gel-like substance in the intestines.

This gel-like substance can help to add bulk to stools, making them softer and easier to pass. Additionally, psyllium husk can stimulate the muscles in the intestines to contract, which can help to move stools through the digestive tract more efficiently. As a result, drinking psyllium husk tea can help to promote regular bowel movements and alleviate symptoms of constipation.

However, it is important to drink plenty of water when consuming psyllium husk tea, as the fiber can expand and cause blockages in the throat or intestines if not properly hydrated.

The following procedure explaing how to make psyllium husk tea for bowel movement.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle or pot.
  2. In a separate cup, mix 1 teaspoon of psyllium husk powder with a small amount of water to form a paste.
  3. Once the water has boiled, pour it over the psyllium husk paste and stir well.
  4. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Drink the tea immediately, followed by a large glass of water.

4. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with bowel movement because it contains mucilage, a soothing substance that protects and coats the digestive tract.

This protective layer allows for the muscles in the intestinal walls to relax, facilitating the passage of stool through the colon. The mild laxative properties of marshmallow root also contribute to its ability to encourage regular bowel movements. Moreover, marshmallow root tea has anti-inflammatory effects that can help alleviate inflammation and irritation in the digestive system, further promoting healthy bowel function.

Additionally, the soothing qualities of marshmallow root tea make it an effective remedy for individuals experiencing symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), such as bloating, cramping, and constipation.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow root tea for bowel movement.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and pour it over the marshmallow root in the infuser or teapot.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the marshmallow root.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove any solid particles.
  5. Drink the marshmallow root tea, preferably between meals, to soothe and stimulate bowel movements. Enjoy it 1-2 times a day as needed.

5. Chamomile Flowers

Chamomile flowers tea helps with bowel movement because it contains an abundant amount of antioxidants, including apigenin and quercetin, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract.

The relaxation effect of chamomile tea on the muscles of the gastrointestinal system can also aid in alleviating constipation by promoting peristalsis, the contractions that move food through the intestines. Additionally, chamomile tea may help soothe irritation and inflammation in the mucosal lining of the digestive tract, which can make bowel movements more comfortable. Chamomile tea has been used for centuries to treat various gastrointestinal issues, including constipation, due to its calming effect on the digestive system.

Its gentle and natural properties make it an excellent choice for those looking for a safe and effective way to support regular bowel movements.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile flowers tea for bowel movement.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot on the stove.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot with an infuser.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the flowers.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea as needed for a gentle laxative effect. It is best to drink it twice a day, in the morning and evening.

6. Ginger Root

Ginger root tea helps with bowel movement because it has been used for centuries as a natural remedy for digestive issues, including constipation.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, stimulate the production of digestive juices and increase intestinal motility, which encourages a bowel movement. Drinking ginger root tea after meals can also help prevent bloating, gas, and cramping, further promoting healthy bowel function. Additionally, ginger's anti-inflammatory properties can reduce inflammation in the gut, allowing for smoother muscle contractions and easier elimination.

Overall, ginger root tea is a simple yet effective natural solution to support regular bowel movements and improve digestive health.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger root tea for bowel movement.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root (about 2-4 tablespoons), then bruise the slices with a heavy object or the back of a knife to release their essential oils.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the bruised ginger slices and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Allow the ginger to simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the water has taken on a rich, golden hue and the ginger's flavor has infused the liquid.
  4. Strain the ginger pieces from the water and pour the ginger-infused liquid into a teapot or mug.
  5. Add a squeeze of lemon (about 1 tbsp) and a drizzle of honey (about 1-2 tsp) to taste, then enjoy your homemade ginger root tea for bowel movement.

7. Turmeric Root

Turmeric root tea helps with bowel movement because of its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the digestive system.

The tea contains curcumin, a compound that stimulates the gallbladder to release bile, aiding in the breakdown and absorption of fats in the small intestine. Additionally, turmeric has been shown to increase the activity of gut enzymes, improving nutrient absorption and promoting regular bowel movements.

Furthermore, the tea's warming properties can help soothe and relax the digestive tract, providing relief from bloating, cramping, and other symptoms associated with irregular bowel movements.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric root tea for bowel movement.

  1. Peel and finely chop 1 tablespoon of fresh turmeric root, then grate it into a small pot.
  2. Add 2 cups of water to the pot and bring it to a boil over medium-high heat.
  3. Once boiling, reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, then add honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to twice a day as needed for promoting bowel movement.

8. Fennel Seeds

Fennel seeds tea helps with bowel movement because of its carminative properties, which aid in the expulsion of intestinal gas.

The anethole compound found in fennel seeds stimulates the production of digestive enzymes and bile, improving overall digestion and easing constipation. Fennel tea also has antispasmodic effects, reducing cramping and easing muscle contractions within the digestive tract to promote smoother bowel movements. Furthermore, fennel seeds have a high fiber content, adding bulk to stools and increasing frequency and ease of bowel movements.

Lastly, fennel tea's calming effect on the nervous system can help reduce stress and anxiety, which can contribute to digestive issues and difficulty with bowel movements.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel seeds tea for bowel movement.

  1. Crush or roughly grind 1-2 teaspoons of fennel seeds using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder.
  2. In a teapot or saucepan, combine the crushed fennel seeds with 16 ounces (2 cups) of water.
  3. Slowly bring the water to a boil over medium heat, allowing the fennel seeds to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used fennel seeds.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, preferably between meals, to help stimulate bowel movement and alleviate digestive discomfort. Enjoy up to three cups of fennel seed tea per day as needed.

9. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with bowel movement because it contains glycyrrhizic acid, which stimulates the production of mucus and coats the digestive tract.

This lining soothes inflammation and irritation, allowing for smoother muscle contractions in the intestines that facilitate bowel movements. Additionally, licorice root tea is a natural demulcent and antispasmodic agent, meaning it can help reduce cramping and spasms in the digestive system. Moreover, its mild laxative properties may stimulate bowel movement directly by increasing water retention and softening stool consistency.

Overall, licorice root tea's anti-inflammatory, soothing, and mildly laxative effects make it an effective natural remedy for constipation and promoting healthy bowel movements.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for bowel movement.

  1. Boil 1 quart (32 ounces) of water in a pot, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the water to extract the beneficial compounds from the licorice root.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup or teapot, separating the solids from the liquid.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for flavor enhancement.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, 1-2 times a day, before or between meals, for promoting bowel movement.

10. Dandelion Root

Dandelion root tea helps with bowel movement because it contains powerful natural laxatives, including bitter compounds like taraxasterol and chicoric acid.

These compounds stimulate the production of bile in the liver, which in turn promotes the contraction of smooth muscles in the intestinal walls, facilitating the passage of stool through the digestive tract. Dandelion root tea also increases fluid intake and water retention in the body, softening stools and making them easier to pass. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory properties help soothe the intestines and reduce inflammation that can contribute to constipation and other digestive issues.

Overall, dandelion root tea is a gentle yet effective natural remedy for promoting healthy bowel movements and supporting overall digestive health.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion root tea for bowel movement.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried dandelion root and 1 cup of water.
  2. Crush or grind the dried dandelion root to increase its surface area.
  3. Bring the water to a boil, then add the crushed dandelion root.
  4. Simmer the mixture on low heat for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea, let it cool, and drink 1-2 times a day for bowel movement.
