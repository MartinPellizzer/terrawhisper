10 best herbal teas for laryngitis

For laryngitis, herbal teas can provide soothing relief for irritated throats.

Slippery elm tea is a popular choice due to its demulcent properties, which can help reduce inflammation and coat the throat. Licorice root tea is another option, as it has a natural sweetness and anti-inflammatory effects.

Marshmallow root tea, rich in mucilage, can also help soothe inflammation and moisturize the throat. Additionally, ginger tea with its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties can offer comfort.

However, it is crucial to consult a healthcare provider if symptoms persist for more than one week, as laryngitis can sometimes be a sign of a more severe condition.

1. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides relief for laryngitis due to its demulcent property, meaning it has a softening and soothing effect.

This tree's inner bark contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water. By coating the throat's lining, this gel provides a barrier against irritants and reduces inflammation. Furthermore, it alleviates pain and irritation while promoting healing in the affected tissues.

Slippery elm tea stands out as a time-honored natural remedy for laryngitis, approved by the German Commission E for treating inflammation of the mouth and throat.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with laryngitis.

  • Bark: The slippery elm's inner bark is loaded with mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water. This gel coats and soothes inflamed mucous membranes, making it perfect for laryngitis relief.
  • Leaves: Slippery elm leaves are also rich in mucilage. When steeped in hot water, they release this soothing substance, providing a natural remedy for laryngitis-related irritation.
  • Seeds: While not as mucilaginous as the bark or leaves, slippery elm seeds still contain some of this beneficial slimy substance. They can be used to make tea and assist in alleviating laryngitis symptoms.
  • Roots: Although less commonly used than the bark or leaves, slippery elm roots can also be employed to make medicinal tea. They contain mucilage, which helps to soothe and protect inflamed tissues in the throat.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for laryngitis.

  1. Measure: Scoop 1 tablespoon of slippery elm bark powder into a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil: Pour 8 ounces of water that has reached a rolling boil over the slippery elm bark.
  3. Steep: Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to release the medicinal properties.
  4. Stir: Mix in honey or lemon juice to enhance the taste and soothing effect, if desired.
  5. Serve: Pour the tea into a cup and drink it while warm, up to three times a day for laryngitis relief.

2. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea brings immediate relief to laryngitis due to the demulcent property of its main ingredient, Althaea officinalis.

This plant-based substance creates a protective film over the mucous membranes, reducing irritation and inflammation. As a result, soreness in the throat significantly decreases, and the natural hydrating effect of marshmallow tea soothes dryness. Furthermore, it promotes expectoration of phlegm, enabling clearer breathing and improved vocal cord function.

Ultimately, marshmallow tea's anti-inflammatory and healing qualities address the discomfort and symptoms of laryngitis effectively.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with laryngitis.

  • Leaves: Rich in anti-inflammatory properties, marshmallow leaves soothe irritated throat tissues due to laryngitis.
  • Flowers: Abundant in mucilage, marshmallow flowers coat and protect inflamed mucous membranes, providing relief from laryngitis.
  • Roots: High in antioxidants, marshmallow roots boost the immune system, helping the body fight laryngitis.
  • Bark: Containing healing polysaccharides, marshmallow bark forms a protective layer over throat tissues, alleviating symptoms of laryngitis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for laryngitis.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water, 2 tablespoons of dried marshmallow root, and 1 teaspoon of grated ginger; bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove solids, then stir in 1 tablespoon of honey and the juice of half a lemon.
  3. Warm up the tea again, but don't let it boil.
  4. Pour the tea into a mug and optionally garnish with a lemon slice.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your throat, and drink up to 3 times a day as needed.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for laryngitis.

  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the thyme leaves.

10. Turmeric

Turmeric tea provides relief from laryngitis due to its active ingredient, curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory properties.

This compound works by inhibiting the production of inflammation-causing molecules, thus reducing swelling in the throat. Furthermore, curcumin's antibacterial qualities combat infection, a common cause of laryngitis. Turmeric tea also soothes irritation, creating a protective barrier in the throat.

Additionally, this warming beverage helps loosen mucus, promoting its elimination and alleviating congestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in turmeric tea that help with laryngitis.

  • Rhizomes: These are the horizontal underground stems of the turmeric plant that are rich in curcumin, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective in soothing irritation in the throat caused by laryngitis.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the turmeric plant contain therapeutic compounds that have been used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory ailments, including laryngitis, due to its expectorant and soothing properties.
  • Roots: The turmeric roots, or tubers, are used in medicinal teas for laryngitis because of their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help reduce inflammation and promote healing in the throat.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the turmeric plant contain bioactive compounds that have been used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory infections and reduce inflammation, making it a suitable addition to medicinal teas for laryngitis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric tea for laryngitis.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 4 cups of water and 1 teaspoon of grated fresh turmeric (or 1/2 teaspoon of dried turmeric powder).
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a mug, pressing on the turmeric solids to extract all liquid.
  4. Stir in honey and lemon juice to taste (optional, but recommended for soothing a sore throat).
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed.
