10 Best Herbal Teas For Fatigue

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients like ginseng, licorice root, and peppermint, can help alleviate fatigue due to their natural properties.

Ginseng is known to boost energy levels and improve mental performance, while licorice root can help regulate stress hormones that can cause fatigue. Peppermint tea, on the other hand, can enhance mental clarity and alertness. Additionally, many herbal teas contain caffeine-free antioxidants that can improve circulation and increase oxygen supply to cells, providing an overall rejuvenating effect.

Furthermore, the soothing and comforting nature of drinking a warm beverage can also contribute to reducing feelings of fatigue.

1. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea helps with fatigue because it contains adaptogenic properties that help the body manage stress and reduce its effects on energy levels.

These stress-fighting compounds include withanolides, which have been shown to boost energy production within the mitochondria of cells. Additionally, ashwagandha tea has anti-inflammatory effects, which can help alleviate chronic fatigue symptoms by reducing inflammation in the body. Furthermore, it contains antioxidants that protect the body from oxidative stress, which can contribute to fatigue and exhaustion.

Lastly, ashwagandha tea is also known for its ability to improve sleep quality, allowing the body to rest and recover more effectively, ultimately reducing feelings of fatigue during the day.

The following procedure explains how to make ashwagandha tea for fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of ashwagandha root powder and reduce heat to low.
  3. Simmer the mixture for 10-15 minutes, then remove from heat and let cool for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids, and add honey or lemon to taste if desired.
  5. Sip slowly and enjoy the calming effects of this traditional Ayurvedic remedy for fatigue.

2. Ginseng

Ginseng tea helps with fatigue because it contains compounds called ginsenosides, which have been shown to improve energy production in the body, enhance mental clarity and focus, reduce inflammation, and promote overall physical and mental well-being.

These benefits can help individuals who experience fatigue due to stress, lack of sleep, or chronic illnesses feel more energized and alert throughout the day. Additionally, ginseng tea is a gentle stimulant that provides a sustained energy boost without causing jitters or anxiety like some other caffeinated beverages do.

Overall, incorporating ginseng tea into your daily routine can help combat fatigue and promote a more balanced, energized state of being.

The following procedure explains how to make ginseng tea for fatigue.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a saucepan on medium heat.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried ginseng root or 3-4 ginseng tea bags to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids, and let it cool for a few minutes.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste (optional) and drink the tea while it's still warm, 1-2 times a day for fatigue.

3. Holy Basil

Holy basil tea helps with fatigue because it contains adaptogenic properties that can help to reduce stress and anxiety, which are common causes of fatigue.

Additionally, holy basil is known to contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that can help to improve overall health and well-being, thereby reducing feelings of tiredness and weakness. Furthermore, holy basil tea has been shown to support healthy blood sugar levels, which can help to maintain energy levels throughout the day. Additionally, holy basil tea may help to improve cognitive function and reduce symptoms of depression, both of which can contribute to feelings of fatigue.

Finally, holy basil tea is a caffeine-free alternative to other beverages, making it a gentle and soothing option for those experiencing fatigue.

The following procedure explains how to make holy basil tea for fatigue.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried holy basil leaves (Ocimum tenuiflorum) with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve a strong, aromatic infusion.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent leaves, to remove any potential impurities and obtain a smooth, clear liquid.
  4. For added flavor and potential synergy, mix in a squeeze of lemon juice (approximately 1 tablespoon) and a small dollop of raw honey (about 1 teaspoon) to taste.
  5. Sip the soothing holy basil tea slowly, allowing it to gently rejuvenate your body and alleviate feelings of fatigue. Enjoy up to 3 cups daily for optimal benefits.

4. Rhodiola

Rhodiola tea helps with fatigue because it contains adaptogens, natural substances that help the body respond to stress.

One of its active compounds, rosavin, has been shown to increase stamina and improve physical performance by enhancing the production of ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the primary energy currency in the human body. Additionally, rhodiola tea can reduce cortisol levels, a stress hormone that contributes to fatigue when chronically elevated. It also contains salidroside, another active compound that has antioxidant properties and protects cells from damage caused by oxidative stress, which can lead to fatigue.

Regular consumption of rhodiola tea may help improve energy levels, reduce feelings of mental and physical exhaustion, and promote overall well-being.

The following procedure explains how to make rhodiola tea for fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried Rhodiola rosea root or 2-3 Rhodiola rosea tea bags.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to extract the medicinal compounds from the Rhodiola root.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used Rhodiola root or tea bags.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and stir well.
  5. Sip slowly and enjoy the warm, soothing beverage, ideally in the morning or early afternoon to help combat fatigue.

5. Maca

Maca tea helps with fatigue because it is rich in essential nutrients such as iron, zinc, and copper, which are vital for energy production in the body.

Additionally, maca contains adaptogens that help regulate stress hormones, reducing feelings of mental and physical exhaustion. Furthermore, studies have shown that maca can increase levels of ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the primary energy currency in the human body. Moreover, maca tea's ability to improve thyroid function can also contribute to reducing fatigue, as an underactive thyroid gland can cause chronic fatigue symptoms.

Lastly, drinking maca tea regularly may help improve overall mood and mental clarity, which can also help combat feelings of tiredness and lethargy.

The following procedure explains how to make maca tea for fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a small saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of dried maca root powder, mixing thoroughly to avoid clumping.
  3. Allow the mixture to simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine mesh strainer into a cup, pressing on the solids to extract as much liquid as possible.
  5. Optional: Add honey or another sweetener to taste, then enjoy your homemade maca tea as an energy-boosting beverage.

6. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with fatigue because it contains an abundant amount of antioxidants, including apigenin, which binds to certain receptors in the brain that promote relaxation and sleep.

Additionally, the calming effects of chamomile tea can help reduce stress levels, allowing for better quality sleep and increased energy levels during the day. Furthermore, the tea's anti-inflammatory properties may help alleviate muscle pain and joint inflammation, which can contribute to feelings of fatigue.

Regular consumption of chamomile tea has been shown to improve overall energy levels and cognitive function, making it a popular choice for those seeking natural remedies for fighting fatigue.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for a couple of minutes until it reaches a temperature of around 205 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers or tea bag and let it steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag and add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while it's still warm, 1-2 times a day, to help alleviate fatigue and promote relaxation.

7. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea helps with fatigue because it contains compounds that have calming effects on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation.

This can lead to improved sleep quality, which is essential for combating fatigue. Additionally, lemon balm tea has antioxidant properties that help protect cells from damage caused by free radicals, contributing to overall energy levels. Furthermore, it contains vitamins and minerals such as vitamin C, calcium, and magnesium, which support metabolic processes and provide essential nutrients for the body.

Lastly, lemon balm tea has been shown in studies to increase mental alertness and focus, providing a boost of energy that can help combat feelings of fatigue during the day.

The following procedure explains how to make lemon balm tea for fatigue.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of fresh lemon balm leaves (Melissa officinalis) or 1 teaspoon of dried lemon balm leaves and rinse them thoroughly.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add the lemon balm leaves once it reaches a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the leaves, and let it cool down for a few minutes.
  5. Add honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired, and enjoy your soothing lemon balm tea for fatigue.

8. Passionflower

Passionflower tea helps with fatigue because of its natural sedative properties, which promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

The herb's flavonoids, including apigenin, vitexin, and quercetin, have been shown to reduce inflammation in the body, contributing to increased energy levels. Additionally, passionflower tea contains essential minerals like magnesium and calcium, which are important for muscle function and energy production. Furthermore, it has been shown to help regulate blood sugar levels, preventing energy crashes throughout the day.

Overall, passionflower tea's calming effects, anti-inflammatory properties, and mineral content make it an effective natural remedy for addressing feelings of fatigue.

The following procedure explains how to make passionflower tea for fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down to 212°F (100°C).
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of dried passionflower leaves or 1 passionflower tea bag to a teapot or infuser.
  3. Pour the hot water over the passionflower and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain or remove the tea bag, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of passionflower tea daily, as needed, to help alleviate symptoms of fatigue.

9. Sage

Sage tea helps with fatigue because it contains compounds called rosmarinic acid and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the body from oxidative stress and inflammation, common contributors to fatigue.

Additionally, sage tea is rich in B vitamins, including thiamine, riboflavin, and niacin, which are essential for energy production within the body. The tea's caffeine content, although relatively low compared to other teas or coffee, can also provide a mild stimulating effect that helps combat feelings of fatigue. Furthermore, sage tea has been shown in studies to improve cognitive function and focus, allowing individuals to feel more alert and energized throughout the day.

Overall, the combination of antioxidants, B vitamins, and mild caffeine make sage tea an effective natural remedy for combating fatigue and promoting overall energy levels.

The following procedure explains how to make sage tea for fatigue.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove until it reaches a simmer (around 212°F or 100°C).
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of fresh sage leaves (or 1 teaspoon of dried sage) to the simmering water.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer the flavor.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used sage leaves.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy your soothing sage tea for fatigue.

10. Valerian Root

Valerian root tea helps with fatigue because it contains compounds that act on the brain and nervous system to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety, which can contribute to feelings of fatigue.

These compounds, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, may increase the amount of a neurotransmitter called gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, which can have a calming effect and help improve sleep quality. Improved sleep can help reduce fatigue and improve energy levels during the day. Additionally, valerian root tea may also help to reduce inflammation and improve circulation, which can provide an additional boost to energy levels.

However, it's important to note that while valerian root tea may help with occasional fatigue, it is not a substitute for medical treatment of chronic or severe fatigue.

The following procedure explains how to make valerian root tea for fatigue.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove, then reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried valerian root (Valeriana officinalis) to the simmering water.
  3. Allow the tea to steep, covered, for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root, then let it cool for a few minutes.
  5. Drink the tea, preferably unsweetened, up to three times a day as needed for relief from fatigue.
