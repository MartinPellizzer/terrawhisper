10 Best Herbal Teas For Gastritis

Herbal teas, particularly those made from chamomile, licorice root, and ginger, can help soothe gastritis due to their anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

These herbs can help reduce inflammation in the lining of the stomach, providing relief from pain and discomfort. Additionally, they can stimulate the production of mucus, which protects the stomach from acid and digestive enzymes.

Ginger, in particular, can help alleviate nausea and vomiting, common symptoms of gastritis. Furthermore, these herbal teas can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles, reducing spasms and cramping.

Lastly, herbal teas are caffeine-free and generally gentle on the stomach, making them a suitable alternative to traditional teas and coffee.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with gastritis because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the lining of the stomach, which is often a key factor in gastritis.

Additionally, chamomile tea has been shown to help relax the muscles of the digestive tract, potentially reducing discomfort and cramping associated with gastritis. The tea may also help to balance acid levels in the stomach, which can contribute to gastritis symptoms. Furthermore, chamomile tea contains antispasmodic agents that can help prevent vomiting, a common symptom of gastritis.

However, it is important to note that while chamomile tea may help alleviate some symptoms of gastritis, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in severe cases.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for gastritis.

  1. Heat 2 cups of water in a pot until boiling, then add 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers, reducing heat to low and simmering for 15 minutes.
  2. Turn off heat and let the mixture steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers.
  4. Optionally, add a small amount of honey to taste, but avoid milk or lemon as they may worsen gastritis symptoms.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed for gastritis relief.

2. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with gastritis because it contains substances, particularly glycyrrhizic acid, that can increase the mucus production in the stomach, creating a protective barrier against stomach acids.

This herbal tea can also help to heal and soothe the inflamed mucous membranes of the stomach lining, providing relief from pain and discomfort. Moreover, licorice tea has been shown to have antibacterial properties, potentially killing the Helicobacter pylori bacteria, one of the most common causes of gastritis. However, it is essential to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and potassium depletion.

Therefore, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before consuming licorice tea for gastritis treatment.

The following procedure explains how to make licorice tea for gastritis.

  1. In a pot, add 1 cup of cold water and 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root, then bring to a boil and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat, let it steep for an additional 5 minutes, then strain the tea to remove the licorice pieces.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for improved flavor, but do not add milk or other ingredients that could interfere with the licorice.
  4. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day, but avoid consuming it close to bedtime.
  5. Leftover tea can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 48 hours, but make sure to reheat it before drinking.

3. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with gastritis because it contains mucilage, a substance that coats the stomach lining and protects it from acid and digestive juices.

This soothing effect can reduce inflammation and irritation in the stomach, providing relief from gastritis symptoms. Additionally, marshmallow tea has antibacterial properties that may help eliminate H.

pylori, a bacterium linked to gastritis. The tea's demulcent quality can also help alleviate pain and discomfort by forming a protective layer over the mucous membrane of the digestive tract.

Furthermore, marshmallow tea's anti-inflammatory properties can help decrease inflammation in the stomach, promoting healing and overall gastric health.

The following procedure explains how to make marshmallow tea for gastritis.

  1. In a small pot, combine 1 cup of water, 1 teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers, and 1 teaspoon of dried marshmallow root; let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the solids and discard them.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of honey or maple syrup to a mug (optional, for added sweetness).
  4. Pour the strained tea into the mug and stir to dissolve the sweetener.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your gastritis; drink 2-3 times a day, as needed.

4. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with gastritis because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water, providing a protective barrier to the digestive tract.

This coating helps to alleviate irritation, inflammation, and pain in the stomach lining, common symptoms of gastritis. Additionally, slippery elm tea can help reduce acidity in the stomach, as it has natural antacid properties. Furthermore, it is believed to stimulate the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, promoting healing and providing further protection to the stomach lining.

Lastly, its demulcent and soothing qualities make it an effective natural remedy for gastritis, providing relief from discomfort and supporting overall gastrointestinal health.

The following procedure explains how to make slippery elm tea for gastritis.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of boiling water with 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove any bark particles.
  4. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for gastritis relief.

5. Meadowsweet

Meadowsweet tea helps with gastritis because it contains anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can reduce inflammation in the stomach lining and alleviate pain associated with gastritis.

It also has antacid effects that neutralize stomach acid, preventing the irritation of the stomach lining that can cause gastritis symptoms. Additionally, meadowsweet tea contains tannins, which have astringent properties that can help protect and heal the damaged stomach lining. Furthermore, meadowsweet tea has been shown to stimulate the production of mucin, a glycoprotein that forms a protective layer in the stomach, providing further protection against gastritis.

Lastly, meadowsweet tea is a natural alternative to conventional medications, which can have side effects and risks, making it a safer and more gentle option for those with gastritis.

The following procedure explains how to make meadowsweet tea for gastritis.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling (2-3 minutes in a microwave or on the stovetop), then pour over 1-2 teaspoons of dried meadowsweet flowers.
  2. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the flowers and discard them.
  4. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of meadowsweet tea daily, between meals, for best results in relieving gastritis symptoms.

6. Catnip

Catnip tea helps with gastritis because it contains anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help soothe and calm the stomach lining, reducing inflammation and discomfort.

Additionally, catnip has been shown to stimulate the production of mucus in the stomach, which can provide an extra layer of protection for the stomach lining and help speed up the healing process. Furthermore, catnip tea can help relax the smooth muscles in the gastrointestinal tract, reducing the likelihood of spasms and cramping that can exacerbate gastritis symptoms. The soothing and calming effects of catnip tea can also help reduce stress and anxiety, which can contribute to the development and worsening of gastritis.

Finally, catnip tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to traditional teas and medications, making it a gentle and accessible option for those looking to manage their gastritis symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make catnip tea for gastritis.

  1. Ingredients: - 1 tablespoon of dried catnip leaves, - 1 cup of water.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of dried catnip leaves to the hot water.
  4. Steep the catnip in hot water for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and let it cool down before drinking. Enjoy your catnip tea for gastritis!

7. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with gastritis because it contains volatile oils, such as anethole, fenchone, and estragole, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining and relax gastrointestinal muscles, thereby alleviating symptoms of gastritis.

Additionally, fennel tea has been shown to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, promoting healthy digestion and reducing the risk of acid reflux, which can exacerbate gastritis. Furthermore, fennel tea has antibacterial properties that may help eliminate Helicobacter pylori, a bacterium commonly associated with gastritis. The soothing effect of fennel tea on the gastrointestinal tract can also help alleviate symptoms of nausea, bloating, and abdominal pain commonly associated with gastritis.

Lastly, fennel tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to traditional medications for gastritis, making it a safe and accessible option for those looking to manage their symptoms.

The following procedure explains how to make fennel tea for gastritis.

  1. Roughly chop 1 tablespoon of fresh fennel seeds (or 1 teaspoon of crushed seeds) and steep in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the seeds.
  3. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  4. Slowly sip the tea while warm.
  5. Drink 2-3 times a day, between meals, for gastritis relief.

8. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with gastritis because it contains menthol and methyl salicylate, compounds with antispasmodic properties that can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and reduce inflammation.

This can alleviate some of the symptoms associated with gastritis, such as abdominal pain and cramping. Additionally, peppermint tea has been shown to improve the flow of bile, which can help the stomach digest food more efficiently and potentially reduce irritation. Furthermore, peppermint tea is naturally caffeine-free and non-acidic, making it a gentle and soothing option for those with gastritis.

However, it is important to note that peppermint tea may not be suitable for everyone, especially those with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) or a history of gallstones, as it can exacerbate these conditions.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for gastritis.

  1. In a small pot, add 1 cup of fresh water and bring it to a boil (3-4 minutes).
  2. Crush 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves or use 1 peppermint tea bag, then add to the pot (instant).
  3. Steep the peppermint in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  4. Strain out the peppermint leaves or remove the tea bag, then let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip and enjoy the soothing peppermint tea, which can help alleviate gastritis symptoms (recommended up to 3 cups a day).

9. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with gastritis because it contains properties that can reduce inflammation and pain in the stomach lining, making it an effective natural remedy for gastritis.

The ginger root used in ginger tea is known for its gastroprotective effects, which can help prevent and treat stomach ulcers caused by gastritis. Additionally, ginger tea can help regulate acid production in the stomach, reducing the risk of acid reflux and other symptoms associated with gastritis. Furthermore, ginger's anti-emetic properties can help alleviate nausea and vomiting, common symptoms of gastritis.

Finally, ginger tea's soothing and warming qualities can provide relief from the discomfort and pain caused by gastritis, making it a popular and effective natural remedy for this condition.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for gastritis.

  1. Ingredients: Fresh ginger (1 inch, sliced), 1 cup of water, and honey (1 teaspoon, optional).
  2. Preparation time: 15 minutes.
  3. Step 1: Place the sliced ginger in a pot and add 1 cup of water.
  4. Step 2: Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Step 3: Strain the tea, add honey (if desired), and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature before consuming.

10. Skullcap

Skullcap tea helps with gastritis because it contains anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help reduce inflammation and discomfort in the digestive tract.

Additionally, skullcap tea has been shown to promote the production of digestive juices, aiding in the breakdown of food and preventing excess acid production that can contribute to gastritis. Furthermore, skullcap tea contains antioxidants that can help protect the lining of the stomach and reduce the damage caused by gastritis. It's important to note that while skullcap tea can be helpful in managing gastritis, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in severe or chronic cases.

Lastly, it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedies.

The following procedure explains how to make skullcap tea for gastritis.

  1. Take 2 teaspoons of dried skullcap herb (Scutellaria lateriflora), place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a built-in strainer.
  2. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the skullcap herb and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the herbs.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of skullcap tea daily, between meals, to soothe gastritis symptoms.
