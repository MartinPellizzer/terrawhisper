10 Best Herbal Teas For Gastritis

Herbal teas, particularly those made from chamomile, licorice root, and ginger, can help soothe gastritis due to their anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

These herbs can help reduce inflammation in the lining of the stomach, providing relief from pain and discomfort. Additionally, they can stimulate the production of mucus, which protects the stomach from acid and digestive enzymes.

Ginger, in particular, can help alleviate nausea and vomiting, common symptoms of gastritis. Furthermore, these herbal teas can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles, reducing spasms and cramping.

Lastly, herbal teas are caffeine-free and generally gentle on the stomach, making them a suitable alternative to traditional teas and coffee.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with gastritis because it contains anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help reduce inflammation and irritation in the lining of the stomach.

The tea's calming effects may also help relax the muscles in the digestive tract, alleviating symptoms such as cramps, spasms, and pain. Chamomile tea is also known for its carminative properties, which can help relieve gas, bloating, and other gastrointestinal disturbances. Furthermore, chamomile contains certain compounds, such as apigenin and bisabolol, that have been shown to possess gastroprotective effects, further aiding in the prevention and treatment of gastritis.

Finally, chamomile tea is a caffeine-free beverage, making it a gentler and less irritating option for those with sensitive stomachs or digestive issues.

2. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with gastritis because it contains compounds called glycyrrhizic acid and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and mucosal protective properties.

These compounds can help to reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, providing relief from gastritis symptoms. Additionally, licorice tea may increase the mucus production in the gastrointestinal tract, creating a protective barrier against stomach acid. Furthermore, it can help to stimulate the production of prostaglandins, which protect the stomach lining and aid in the healing process.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to serious side effects, such as high blood pressure and potassium depletion, so it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before consuming licorice tea for gastritis treatment.

3. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with gastritis because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a gel when mixed with fluid.

This gel coats the lining of the digestive tract, providing a protective barrier against stomach acids. Moreover, it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, providing relief from gastritis symptoms.

Additionally, it may also help to soothe and heal irritated tissues in the digestive tract. Marshmallow tea is considered safe for most people, with few side effects, making it a popular natural remedy for gastritis.

However, it is still important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment regimen.

4. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with gastritis because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and gel-like when mixed with water.

This mucilage coats the lining of the stomach, providing a protective barrier against acid and digestive juices, which can reduce inflammation and irritation caused by gastritis. Slippery elm tea also has anti-inflammatory and immune-stimulating properties, which can help the body heal and repair the damaged stomach lining.

Additionally, it can help alleviate symptoms such as abdominal pain, bloating, and indigestion associated with gastritis. Slippery elm tea has been used traditionally as a natural remedy for digestive issues, and its soothing and healing effects on the digestive tract make it a valuable tool in managing gastritis.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatments for gastritis, as slippery elm tea may interact with certain medications or underlying health conditions.
