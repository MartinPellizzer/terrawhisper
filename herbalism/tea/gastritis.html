10 best herbal teas for gastritis

Gastritis, or inflammation of the stomach lining, can cause discomfort and pain.

Certain herbal teas may help soothe symptoms and promote healing. Chamomile tea is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which may help reduce inflammation in the stomach.

Licorice root tea, which contains deglycyrrhizinated licorice (DGL), can help increase the mucus coating of the stomach, protecting it from acid and irritants. Peppermint tea can also aid in digestion and alleviate symptoms of gastritis.

However, it's important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedies, especially for those with underlying health conditions or taking medications.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea aids gastritis relief due to its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce inflammation in the stomach lining.

This tea's antispasmodic qualities alleviate stomach cramps and spasms associated with gastritis. Chamomile contains protective compounds, including flavonoids, that safeguard the stomach against damage from acid and bacteria. Furthermore, chamomile tea's soothing effects on the digestive system promote relaxation, helping to decrease stress-induced gastritis symptoms.

Lastly, chamomile tea's mild bitter taste stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, facilitating better nutrient absorption and faster stomach emptying, ultimately benefiting gastritis symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with gastritis.

  • Flowers: The chamomile flowers contain anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the stomach lining, making it effective for gastritis.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids, chamomile leaves help decrease stomach inflammation, providing relief from gastritis symptoms.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are known for their carminative properties, which aid in relieving gastritis-related bloating and dispelling gas.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for gastritis.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser and place in a cup.
  3. Pour the boiled water over the chamomile flowers and let steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser and discard the chamomile flowers.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while warm, up to 3 times a day, after meals.

2. Licorice

Licorice tea aids gastritis by soothing inflamed mucous membranes in the stomach, thanks to its active compound, glycyrrhizin.

This compound generates a protective coating along the stomach lining, preventing hydrochloric acid from causing further damage. Moreover, licorice tea encourages the production of mucin, a vital component of the stomach's defensive mucosal barrier. As a result, the tea alleviates pain, decreases inflammation, and fosters the healing process for gastritis symptoms.

Furthermore, licorice tea boasts antimicrobial properties, hindering the growth of H. pylori, a bacterium frequently responsible for gastritis.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with gastritis.

  • Roots: Rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound with anti-inflammatory properties that helps soothe irritated gastric mucosa.
  • Rhizomes: Contain substances like triterpenoid saponins which protect the stomach lining, providing relief from gastritis.
  • Leaves: Packed with flavonoids, offering antioxidant and anti-ulcer properties, helping in the treatment of gastritis.
  • Flowers: Contain essential oils and possess carminative properties, aiding in the expulsion of gas from the stomach, alleviating symptoms of gastritis.
  • Seeds: Loaded with glycosides and polysaccharides, they help in the management of gastritis by reducing inflammation and promoting mucosal healing.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for gastritis.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root and 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Steep the mixture for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root material.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste, but avoid milk or sugar.
  5. Consume the tea while it's still warm, ideally after meals, for gastritis relief.

3. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the marshmallow plant's root, contains mucilage, a substance that turns into a gel when mixed with water.

This gel coats the stomach lining, providing a protective barrier against acid and digestive juices. By forming this barrier, marshmallow tea alleviates gastritis symptoms, such as pain, inflammation, and irritation. Additionally, this soothing action encourages the healing of stomach ulcers and lesions caused by gastritis.

Marshmallow tea's demulcent properties, combined with its anti-inflammatory effects, make it an ideal natural remedy for treating gastritis, offering quick relief and promoting overall gastric health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with gastritis.

  • Roots: The roots of the marshmallow plant contain mucilage, which coats the stomach lining and helps protect it from acid and inflammation.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the marshmallow plant have a high mucilage content, which helps soothe and protect the stomach lining from acid and inflammation.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the marshmallow plant contain flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce gastritis symptoms.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the marshmallow plant contain mucilage, which can help soothe and protect the stomach lining from acid and inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for gastritis.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water, 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root, and 1 teaspoon of grated ginger; simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or mug, pressing on the solids to extract all liquid.
  3. Discard the marshmallow root and ginger solids.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve the marshmallow tea warm and enjoy up to 3 times daily for gastritis relief.
