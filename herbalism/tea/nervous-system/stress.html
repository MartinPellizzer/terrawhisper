10 best herbal tinctures for stress

The best herbal tinctures for stress are ashwagandha, golden root, lemon balm, valerian, passionflower, skullcap, kava kava, lavender, chamomile, and holy basil.

These tinctures possess adaptogenic and nervine properties that help the body cope with stress by reducing cortisol levels, promoting relaxation, and improving mood. By incorporating these herbal tinctures into their daily routine, individuals with stress can experience improved sleep quality, increased focus and productivity, and a greater sense of calm and well-being. For example, someone who has trouble falling asleep due to stress could take a tincture containing valerian and passionflower before bed, promoting a more restful night's sleep.

Additionally, someone who experiences anxiety throughout the day could benefit from taking a tincture containing ashwagandha and holy basil, which can help reduce stress and improve mood.

1. Ashwagandha tincture

Ashwagandha tincture helps with stress because it contains adaptogenic properties that enable the body to better cope with stress.

This herbal extract works by regulating the levels of cortisol, a stress-induced hormone, in the body. By reducing cortisol levels, ashwagandha tincture promotes relaxation, improves mood, and enhances mental clarity, all of which contribute to stress reduction.

Moreover, it supports the adrenal glands, which are responsible for releasing stress hormones, allowing them to function optimally and minimizing the negative effects of stress on the body.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ashwagandha tincture that aid with stress.

  • Withanolides: These compounds have been shown to reduce stress and anxiety by inhibiting cortisol production in the body.
  • Alkaloids: Certain alkaloids present in ashwagandha, like isopelletierine and anaferine, have a calming effect on the nervous system.
  • Sitoindosides: These steroidal lactones promote the activity of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that has a relaxing effect on the brain.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in ashwagandha tincture that help with stress.

  • Roots: The roots of ashwagandha are used to make medicinal tea for stress due to their high concentration of withanolides, which have been shown to reduce stress and anxiety.
  • Leaves: The leaves of ashwagandha contain adaptogenic properties that help the body cope with stress and promote relaxation.
  • Flowers: The flowers of ashwagandha contain antioxidants that help protect the body from the damaging effects of stress.
  • Seeds: The seeds of ashwagandha contain triethylene glycol, which has been shown to have a sedative effect, making it useful for reducing stress and promoting sleep.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ashwagandha tincture for stress.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of dried ashwagandha root into small pieces.
  2. Combine root pieces and 1 pint of vodka or rum in a glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks.
  4. Shake the jar gently every few days to help the extraction process.
  5. Strain the mixture and store the resulting tincture in a dark glass bottle.

2. Golden root tincture

Golden root tincture helps with stress because it contains adaptogenic properties that regulate the body's stress response.

This herbal remedy, also known as Rhodiola rosea, has been scientifically proven to reduce fatigue, exhaustion, and anxiety levels. By promoting homeostasis in the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, golden root tincture helps the body adapt to stressors, minimizing the impact of stress on mental and physical health.

Regular consumption of golden root tincture can improve overall well-being and enhance resilience against stress.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in golden root tincture that aid with stress.

  • Rosavin: Acts as a natural adaptogen, helping the body to cope with stress and promoting mental balance.
  • Salidroside: Enhances mood and alleviates symptoms of stress, such as anxiety and depression.
  • Hypericin: Has a calming effect on the nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing irritability caused by stress.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in golden root tincture that help with stress.

  • Roots: Contain high levels of adaptogenic compounds, helping the body adapt to stress.
  • Rhizomes: Rich in antioxidants that protect cells from damage caused by stress.
  • Leaves: High in calming compounds, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.
  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids known to decrease stress hormone levels.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential oils that soothe the nervous system and alleviate stress symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic golden root tincture for stress.

  1. Chop 1 oz of dried golden root (Rhodiola rosea) root into small pieces and combine with 2 cups of 40% alcohol or higher in a clean glass jar.
  2. Seal the jar and let it steep in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking vigorously every few days.
  3. After steeping, strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a clean, dark glass bottle, squeezing out all liquid from the plant material.
  4. Discard the plant material and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.
  5. Take 30 drops of the tincture 1-3 times daily, as needed, for stress relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tincture for stress.

  3. Secure a lid on the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  4. Strain the tincture using cheesecloth or a fine-mesh strainer, discarding the solid plant material.
  5. Store the finished valerian tincture in a tightly sealed glass bottle in a cool, dark place for up to 2 years.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic skullcap tincture for stress.

  1. Harvest 1 ounce of dried skullcap herb, cutting stems and flowers at peek bloom, and allow to air dry for 3 days.
  2. Fill a clean mason jar with 8 ounces of 80 proof vodka, completely submerging the skullcap herb.
  3. Secure a lid on the jar, store in a cool, dry place, and shake vigorously for 1 minute every day for 2 weeks.
  4. Strain the liquid using cheesecloth into a dark glass dropper bottle, squeezing out all excess liquid and discarding the plant material.
  5. Label the bottle with the name, ingredients, date, and dosage suggestions, storing in a cool, dark place for up to 2 years.

  4. Shake the jar daily and strain the liquid through a cheesecloth after the allotted time.
  5. Store the final tincture in a dropper bottle, refrigerate, and take 1-2 dropperfuls up to three times a day as needed for stress relief.
