10 Best herbal teas for stress

The best herbal teas for stress are chamomile, lemon balm, and passionflower.

These teas contain properties that promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. Chamomile is known for its calming effects and can help improve sleep quality.

Lemon balm has been shown to reduce feelings of stress and anxiety, while passionflower helps calm the nervous system and promote a sense of peace. By incorporating these herbal teas into their daily routine, individuals with stress can experience improved mood, reduced anxiety, and better sleep, allowing them to better manage their stress levels and enhance their overall well-being.

For example, someone who feels overwhelmed with work and has trouble sleeping can drink a cup of chamomile tea before bed to help them relax and get a good night's sleep.

1. Chamomile tea

Chamomile tea helps with stress because it contains the antioxidant apigenin, which binds to certain receptors in the brain that help decrease anxiety and initiate sleep.

Moreover, chamomile tea has calming effects due to the presence of glycine, a compound that acts as a nerve relaxant.

Regular consumption of chamomile tea helps reduce stress and its associated symptoms, promoting relaxation and improved mental health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with stress.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, helping to reduce anxiety and promote sleep.
  • Luteolin: A flavonoid that inhibits the release of inflammatory cytokines, helping to reduce stress-induced inflammation.
  • Chamazulene: A terpene with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to protect cells from damage caused by stress.
  • Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene with anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, helping to alleviate muscle tension and pain caused by stress.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, with anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, helping to relax muscles and reduce stress-related spasms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with stress.

  • Flowers: Contain volatile oils and antioxidants that help promote relaxation and reduce stress.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids that have a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to alleviate stress.
  • Seeds: Contain high levels of essential oils such as Chamazulene, known for their anti-inflammatory and stress-reducing properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for stress.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit (100 degrees Celsius) for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Place one heaping teaspoon (about 2 grams) of dried chamomile flowers in a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the chamomile flowers.
  5. Serve the chamomile tea hot, allowing it to cool slightly to avoid burning your tongue, and enjoy its calming effects.

2. Lavender tea

Lavender tea helps with stress because it contains an active ingredient called linalool, which is known for its sedative and calming properties.

This compound inhibits voltage-gated calcium channels, leading to reduced inflammation and relaxation of the mind. Furthermore, lavender's soothing scent activates the limbic system, particularly the amygdala, promoting a feeling of tranquility and emotional balance.

By consuming lavender tea regularly, individuals can potentially alleviate symptoms of stress, anxiety, and depression, fostering a greater sense of well-being and serenity.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lavender tea that aid with stress.

  • Linalool: A terpene alcohol known for its relaxing properties, helping to reduce anxiety and promote sleep.
  • Lavandulol: A monoterpene with calming effects, shown to decrease stress and provide a sense of well-being.
  • Eucalyptol: A monoterpene with stress-relieving properties, helping to alleviate mental fatigue and improve mood.
  • Camphor: A terpene ketone that increases blood flow and reduces inflammation, leading to a decrease in physical tension and stress.
  • Geraniol: A monoterpene alcohol that acts as a mild sedative, aiding in relaxation and reducing stress.
  • Cineole: A monoterpene with anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, contributing to the reduction of physical and emotional stress.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in lavender tea that help with stress.

  • Flowers: Contain high levels of antioxidants that help reduce stress and promote relaxation.
  • Leaves: Rich in soothing compounds, including flavonoids, that help alleviate anxiety and promote calmness.
  • Buds: Packed with essential oils known for their stress-relieving properties, promoting a sense of tranquility.
  • Stems: Contain mild sedative qualities that help reduce stress and tension, supporting overall mental well-being.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lavender tea for stress.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a teapot or kettle for 3-5 minutes.
  2. In a teapot or teacup, add 1-2 teaspoons of dried culinary lavender flowers.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the lavender flowers in the teapot or teacup.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the flavor and health benefits.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent lavender flowers, and enjoy while hot.

3. Lemon balm tea

Lemon balm tea helps with stress because it contains rosmarinic acid and eugenol, which are known to have anxiety-reducing properties.

These compounds work together to promote relaxation and reduce stress hormone levels in the body. Furthermore, lemon balm has been shown to increase GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) activity in the brain.

GABA is a neurotransmitter that inhibits the activity of nerve cells, producing a calming effect. By increasing GABA activity, lemon balm tea can help alleviate symptoms of stress and promote a sense of calm.

Additionally, the warm and soothing nature of tea can help reduce muscle tension and induce relaxation, making lemon balm tea an effective natural remedy for stress relief.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lemon balm tea that aid with stress.

  • Rosmarinic acid: Possesses anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation caused by stress.
  • Citral: A terpene with sedative and mood-enhancing effects that can alleviate anxiety and promote relaxation.
  • Nerol: A volatile oil that has calming and soothing properties, helping to reduce stress-induced restlessness.
  • Geraniol: Possesses mild sedative effects that can help reduce stress-related symptoms such as insomnia and restlessness.
  • Eugenol: Exhibits anxiolytic properties, relieving anxiety and promoting mental calmness during stressful situations.
  • Tannins: Contribute to lemon balm's astringent effect, helping to soothe nerves and reduce stress-related irritability.
  • Flavonoids: Have antioxidant effects, protecting the body from oxidative stress and promoting overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in lemon balm tea that help with stress.

  • Leaves: Contain rosmarinic acid and eugenol, compounds known to reduce anxiety and provide calming effects.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, helping to alleviate stress-induced oxidative damage.
  • Stems: Provide structure and essential oils, contributing to the tea's soothing aroma and relaxing properties.
  • Seeds: Contain high levels of essential oils, enhancing the tea's stress-relieving effects.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemon balm tea for stress.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes, reaching a rolling boil.
  2. Crush 2 teaspoons of dried lemon balm leaves or 4 teaspoons of fresh lemon balm leaves.
  3. Place the crushed lemon balm leaves in a tea infuser or teapot.
  4. Pour the boiling water over the lemon balm leaves and steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea, discarding the lemon balm leaves.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic kava kava tea for stress.

  1. Obtain 2 tablespoons of dried kava kava root and 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the kava kava root.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or container.
  5. Consume the tea while warm, up to three times a day, for stress relief.

How to best treat stress with herbal tea?

The best way to treat stress with herbal teas is by regularly drinking soothing blends that promote relaxation.

Consider chamomile, known for its calming properties, or lemon balm, which helps reduce anxiety. Peppermint tea can also alleviate stress-related symptoms like headaches and nausea. For a powerful adaptogenic effect, choose ashwagandha or holy basil teas to combat stress and fatigue.

Incorporate these stress-busting herbal teas into your daily routine for a more relaxed and balanced state of mind.

What are the most common causes of stress that are treatable with herbal tea?

The primary causes of stress treatable with herbal teas are daily tensions, restlessness, and mild anxiety.

These teas can also assist with stress-related issues such as insomnia, lack of focus, and irritability. Additionally, herbal teas can be beneficial for managing stress-induced digestive problems. They help promote relaxation, soothe frayed nerves, and maintain a sense of calmness, thus improving overall mental and physical well-being.

However, it is crucial to remember that, while herbal teas can be helpful, they are not a substitute for professional medical advice or treatment for chronic or severe stress.
