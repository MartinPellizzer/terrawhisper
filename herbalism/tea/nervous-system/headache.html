10 best herbal tinctures for headaches

The best herbal tinctures for headaches are feverfew, willow, ginkgo biloba, butterbur, peppermint, lavender, skullcap, valerian, wood betony, and chamomile.

These tinctures contain active compounds that address the primary causes of headaches, such as inflammation, tension, and blood vessel constriction. Feverfew, for instance, contains parthenolide, which inhibits the release of substances that cause inflammation and pain. Willow bark contains salicin, similar to aspirin, which alleviates pain and reduces inflammation.

Ginkgo biloba improves blood flow to the brain, reducing the risk of tension headaches. Butterbur contains petasins, which reduce inflammation and spasms in blood vessels. Peppermint and lavender contain menthol and linalool, which relax muscles and alleviate pain. Skullcap, valerian, wood betony, and chamomile contain calming compounds that reduce anxiety and promote sleep, addressing a common trigger for headaches.

By incorporating these tinctures into a daily routine, individuals can experience fewer and less intense headaches, improving their overall quality of life.

1. Feverfew tincture

Feverfew tincture helps with headaches because it contains parthenolide, a compound that has been shown to inhibit the release of prostaglandins, serotonin, and other substances that cause inflammation and pain in blood vessels surrounding the brain.

This action helps to reduce the swelling of blood vessels, thereby alleviating the pressure that causes headaches. Additionally, feverfew has been found to prevent the contraction of smooth muscles surrounding blood vessels, further reducing the risk of headaches.

Its anti-inflammatory and smooth muscle relaxation properties make feverfew tincture an effective and reliable remedy for tension and migraine headaches.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in feverfew tincture that aid with headaches.

  • Parthenolide: Inhibits the release of pro-inflammatory chemicals that can cause headaches.
  • Tanacetin: Acts as a natural anti-inflammatory agent, reducing swelling in blood vessels that may contribute to headaches.
  • Camphor: Improves blood flow to the brain, alleviating tension and reducing headache symptoms.
  • Chamazulene: Possesses potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, helping to manage pain associated with headaches.
  • Sesquiterpene lactones: Demonstrate strong antispasmodic effects, relaxing constricted blood vessels and easing headache tension.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in feverfew tincture that help with headaches.

  • Leaves: Contain parthenolide, a compound shown to help reduce the frequency and severity of headaches.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, helping to alleviate headache symptoms.
  • Buds: High in essential oils, providing soothing properties for headache relief.
  • Seeds: Packed with beneficial compounds, including parthenolide, that help in managing headache disorders.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic feverfew tincture for headaches.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of feverfew plant, fresh or dried, into small pieces.
  2. Submerge the chopped feverfew in a clean jar, filling it with 8 ounces of 80-proof vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for two to four weeks, shaking it daily.
  4. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the excess liquid.
  5. Store the resulting tincture in a tightly sealed glass container, in a cool, dark place, for up to one year. Take 30 drops every few hours as needed for headaches.

2. Willow tincture

Willow tincture helps with headaches because it contains salicin, a compound that is a natural anti-inflammatory and analgesic.

When ingested, salicin is metabolized in the body to produce salicylic acid, which works to reduce inflammation, relieve pain, and bring down fever. This makes willow tincture an effective treatment for headaches caused by inflammation and tension.

Furthermore, it has been shown to have fewer side effects compared to over-the-counter pain medications, making it a safer option for those who experience frequent headaches.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in willow tincture that aid with headaches.

  • Salicin: A natural anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce swelling and relieve headache pain.
  • Flavonoids: Powerful antioxidants that help improve circulation and reduce inflammation, providing relief for headaches.
  • Tannins: Astringent compounds that can help reduce inflammation and constrict blood vessels, alleviating headache symptoms.
  • Phenolic acids: Active compounds with analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe headaches.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in willow tincture that help with headaches.

  • Bark: Contains salicin, a natural pain reliever, making it effective for treating headaches.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids and tannins, possess anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate headache symptoms.
  • Buds: High in essential oils and antioxidants, offering soothing relief for headaches and promoting relaxation.
  • Rhyzomes: Packed with phenolic acids, act as natural analgesics and reduce inflammation, providing headache relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tincture for headaches.

  1. Harvest vigorous willow tree branches, selecting those with a diameter of up to 1 cm; gather enough to produce 2 cups of bark.
  2. In a pot of water, boil the bark for 30 minutes, then let it steep for 8 hours.
  3. Strain the liquid, squeezing out the excess liquid from the bark, and discard the solid material.
  4. Measure the remaining liquid; combine it with an equal amount of vodka or rum, and mix well.
  5. Store the mixture in a tightly sealed glass container for 2 weeks before using, shaking it daily; then, strain and store the tincture in a dark glass bottle.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginkgo biloba tincture for headaches.

  1. Obtain 1 ounce of dried Ginkgo Biloba leaves and a clean glass jar with a lid.
  2. Fill the jar with 1 cup of vodka or rum at 40-50% alcohol, ensuring the leaves are fully submerged.

What are the most common causes of headaches that are treatable with herbal tincture?

The primary causes of headaches treatable with herbal tincture include tension, sinus congestion, and inflammation.

These headaches often result from stress, allergies, or muscle strain. Tension headaches cause a constant ache or pressure on both sides of the head, sometimes accompanied by a tender scalp, neck, and shoulder muscles.

Sinus headaches are characterized by a deep and constant pain in the cheekbones, forehead, or bridge of the nose. Inflammatory headaches are often due to inflammation in the body, such as from an infection or autoimmune disorder.

Herbal tinctures can help alleviate these symptoms by reducing tension, clearing nasal passages, and fighting inflammation.
