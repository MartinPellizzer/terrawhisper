10 Best herbal teas for headaches

The best herbal teas for headaches are feverfew, willow, ginger, peppermint, chamomile, skullcap, licorice, valerian, lavender, and basil.

These teas contain properties that help alleviate headaches. Feverfew has parthenolide, a compound that reduces inflammation and constricts blood vessels, easing headaches. Willow has salicin, which acts like aspirin in relieving pain. Ginger contains properties that inhibit prostaglandin synthesis and has anti-inflammatory effects that reduce pain and inflammation. Peppermint has menthol, which provides a cooling sensation and helps relax muscles. Chamomile has anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and calming effects that help reduce headaches.

Skullcap contains sedative and calming properties that ease headaches. Licorice contains anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and pain. Valerian has sedative qualities that help relax muscles. Lavender has soothing and relaxing properties that alleviate tension headaches. Basil has anti-inflammatory and muscle-relaxant properties that reduce headache pain. By consuming these teas, people with headaches can find relief and improve their overall well-being. For instance, a person who regularly suffers from tension headaches could drink chamomile or lavender tea before bed to reduce muscle tension and improve sleep.

Another person could drink ginger or feverfew tea during a migraine attack to reduce pain and inflammation.

1. Feverfew tea

Feverfew tea helps with headaches because it contains parthenolide, a compound that is known to reduce inflammation and constrict blood vessels in the brain.

These actions can alleviate the symptoms of headaches, including pain and pressure. Furthermore, feverfew has been shown to inhibit the release of serotonin and other chemicals that can cause constriction of blood vessels in the brain and lead to headaches.

Therefore, drinking feverfew tea can provide relief for those suffering from headaches caused by inflammation and constricted blood vessels.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in feverfew tea that aid with headaches.

  • Parthenolide: A compound that constricts blood vessels, reducing headache-inducing inflammation.
  • Tanacetin: A sesquiterpene lactone that exhibits anti-inflammatory effects and helps alleviate headaches.
  • Camphor: A natural terpene that activates cold receptors in the skin, providing relief for headache symptoms.
  • Eucalyptol: A compound found in feverfew that functions as a smooth muscle relaxant, helpings to ease headache tension.
  • Camphene: A compound that increases cerebral blood flow, thereby reducing headache pain and frequency.
  • Artemisinin: A sesquiterpene lactone with antioxidant and anti-nociceptive properties that help in treating headaches.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in feverfew tea that help with headaches.

  • Leaves: Contain parthenolide, a compound that helps reduce inflammation and relieve headaches.
  • Flowers: Rich in flavonoids and tanins, contributing to the plant's overall analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential oils, offering pain-relieving and soothing effects.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic feverfew tea for headaches.

  1. Gather 2-3 fresh feverfew leaves or 1-2 dried feverfew teaspoons.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for 30 seconds.
  3. Place the feverfew leaves or dried tea in a teapot or infuser.
  4. Pour the hot water over the feverfew and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea, discard the leaves, and enjoy the warm beverage.

2. Willow tea

Willow tea helps with headaches because it contains salicin, a compound similar to aspirin.

This substance works by blocking the production of prostaglandins, chemicals that cause inflammation and blood vessel constriction in the brain, leading to headaches. By reducing inflammation and improving blood flow, willow tea effectively alleviates headache symptoms, providing a natural and herbal alternative to traditional pain relievers.

Its therapeutic properties have been recognized for centuries, making it a popular remedy for those seeking relief from headache discomfort.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in willow tea that aid with headaches.

  • Salicin: A natural anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce swelling and relieves pain, including headaches.
  • Flavonoids: Powerful antioxidants that help improve cerebral blood flow, reducing the risk of headaches and migraines.
  • Tannins: Astringent compounds that help constrict blood vessels, reducing pressure and pain in the head.
  • Phenolic acids: Natural chemicals that have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate headaches.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in willow tea that help with headaches.

  • Bark: Contains salicin, a natural pain reliever that helps alleviate headaches.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids that possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties to reduce headache symptoms.
  • Buds: High in tannins and essential oils, which have a soothing effect on headache-prone vessels.
  • Rhizomes: A source of phenolic acids, which can help reduce the frequency and intensity of headaches.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tea for headaches.

  1. Gather 2-3 willow bark strips (providing 240-360 mg of salicin) from a willow tree and rinse them thoroughly.
  2. Boil the willow bark in 4 cups of water for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal components.
  3. Strain the tea to remove bark particles and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Drink the entire willow tea preparation to alleviate headaches, but do not exceed more than one dose in 6 hours.

3. Ginger tea

Ginger tea helps with headaches because it contains anti-inflammatory compounds such as gingerols and shogaols, which work to inhibit prostaglandin synthesis, thereby reducing inflammation and associated pain.

Furthermore, ginger has been shown to possess analgesic properties, elevating its effectiveness in providing relief from headaches.

By sipping on ginger tea, one can benefit from its soothing warmth and symptomatic alleviation.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tea that aid with headaches.

  • Gingerols: Gingerols have potent anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce prostaglandin synthesis, which is associated with causing headaches.
  • Shogaols: Shogaols act as serotonin receptor agonists, helping to alleviate headache symptoms by regulating serotonin levels in the brain.
  • Paradols: Paradols exhibit strong antioxidant properties that help reduce oxidative stress, a contributing factor in tension headaches.
  • Zingerone: Zingerone has analgesic properties, providing relief from headache pain by blocking the transmission of pain signals in the nervous system.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in ginger tea that help with headaches.

  • Rhizomes: Contain anti-inflammatory compounds that help alleviate headaches.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that can reduce inflammation and ease headache symptoms.
  • Buds: High in essential oils known to have pain-relieving properties for headaches.
  • Flowers: Contain bioactive compounds that can help relax blood vessels and alleviate headache pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for headaches.

  1. Peel and grate 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, then let it steep in boiling water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the ginger pieces from the water, leaving behind ginger-infused tea.
  3. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and the juice of half a lemon for added soothing benefits.
  4. Warm the tea gently over low heat, ensuring it doesn't reach a boil.
  5. Pour the ginger tea into a cup and sip slowly to alleviate headache symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with headaches.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for headaches.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle, reaching a temperature of 212 degrees Fahrenheit, for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Place one teaspoon (about 2 grams) of dried chamomile flowers in a teapot or infuser.
  3. Pour the boiled water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the flowers, pouring it into a cup.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed.

How to best treat headaches with herbal tea?

The best way to treat headaches with herbal tea is by choosing teas with natural relaxing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Two effective options are chamomile and feverfew teas. Chamomile tea, made from the flowers of the Matricaria chamomilla plant, has calming effects that can help alleviate tension headaches. Feverfew tea, derived from the Tanacetum parthenium herb, is known for its ability to reduce inflammation and constrict blood vessels, which can help in treating migraines.

Regular consumption of these teas, 2-3 times a day, can help manage headaches and promote overall well-being.

What are the most common causes of headaches that are treatable with herbal tea?

The primary causes of headaches treatable with herbal tea are tension and stress.

These types of headaches often result from tightened muscles in the neck, shoulders, and head due to anxiety or tension. Another cause is sinus headaches, which occur when the sinuses become inflamed or congested, leading to discomfort and pressure in the head.

Lastly, menstrual migraines are a common cause of headaches treatable with herbal tea. These migraines typically affect women and are caused by hormonal fluctuations during menstruation.

Herbal teas, such as chamomile or ginger, have anti-inflammatory and relaxing properties that can help alleviate these types of headaches.
