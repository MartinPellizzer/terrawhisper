10 Best herbal teas for sleep deprivation

The best herbal teas for sleep deprivation are valerian, chamomile, lavender, passionflower, lemon balm, hops, magnolia, St. John's wort, California poppy, and kava kava.

These teas have primary properties that encourage sleep and relaxation, making them ideal for those suffering from sleep deprivation. Valerian root is a natural sedative, chamomile contains the antioxidant apigenin, which binds to certain receptors in the brain that decrease anxiety and initiate sleep. Lavender has calming and soothing effects, while passionflower increases gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) levels in the brain, which helps promote sleep.

Lemon balm and hops are both known for their sedative effects. Magnolia bark has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for anxiety and stress relief. St. John's wort, California poppy, and kava kava are all herbs that have been shown to improve sleep quality and reduce symptoms of insomnia. By incorporating these herbal teas into their daily routine, people with sleep deprivation can experience improved sleep, increased energy levels, and a better overall quality of life.

For example, a person who has trouble falling asleep might drink a cup of chamomile tea before bed, while someone who wakes up frequently during the night might benefit from a cup of valerian root tea.

1. Valerian tea

Valerian tea helps with sleep deprivation because it contains active compounds, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, that influence the brain's level of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter responsible for regulating nerve impulse transmissions.

By increasing GABA activity, valerian tea reduces the overall excitability of the nervous system, calming down the brain and preparing it for sleep. Moreover, valerian tea has been shown to hasten the onset of sleep, improve overall sleep quality, and decrease the number of nighttime awakenings in individuals experiencing sleep deprivation.

This therapeutic effect of valerian tea on sleep is both safe and well-established, with minimal side effects.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tea that aid with sleep deprivation.

  • Valerenic acid: Reduces anxiety and promotes sleep by interacting with GABA receptors in the brain.
  • Valepotriates: Possess sedative and muscle relaxant properties, thereby improving sleep quality.
  • Isovaleric acid: Has a calming effect on the nervous system, aiding in reducing sleep disturbances.
  • Linalool: Exhibits anxiolytic and sedative effects, contributing to improved sleep.
  • Hesperidin: Enhances sleep quality by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in valerian tea that help with sleep deprivation.

  • Roots: Contain active compounds that promote sleep and reduce anxiety.
  • Rhizomes: Rich in valerenic acid, shown to improve sleep quality.
  • Flowers: Contain volatile oils that have a relaxing and calming effect.
  • Leaves: High in antioxidants that help reduce stress and induce sleep.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tea for sleep deprivation.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Steep the mixture, covered, for 10-15 minutes to allow the valerian to release its compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor.
  5. Consume the tea 30-60 minutes before bedtime for optimal sleep aid.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for sleep deprivation.

  2. Add one heaping tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser or teapot, releasing its soothing scent and promoting relaxation.

  4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey, if desired, for a touch of natural sweetness to enhance chamomile's comforting effects.

What are the most common causes of sleep deprivation that are treatable with herbal tea?

The primary causes of sleep deprivation that are treatable with herbal tea are stress, anxiety, and restlessness.

However, it's important to remember that while herbal teas can be beneficial, they are not a substitute for addressing any underlying conditions that may be causing sleep disturbances.
