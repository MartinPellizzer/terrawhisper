1. Valerian tincture

Valerian tincture helps with sleep deprivation because it contains valerenic acid and other compounds that promote relaxation by increasing the amount of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain.

GABA is a neurotransmitter that inhibits nerve cells from firing, thereby reducing neural activity and producing a calming effect. Valerian tincture's ability to enhance GABA levels helps alleviate anxiety, restlessness, and insomnia, making it an effective natural remedy for sleep deprivation.

Furthermore, valerian tincture has minimal side effects and is non-addictive, making it a safer alternative to prescription sleep aids.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tincture that aid with sleep deprivation.

  • Valerenic acid: Reduces anxiety and promotes sleep by interacting with GABA receptors in the brain.
  • Valepotriates: Possess sedative and muscle relaxant properties, helping to alleviate symptoms of sleep deprivation.
  • Isovaleric acid: Acts as a sedative and sleep-promoting agent, aiding in the treatment of sleep disorders.
  • Bornyl acetate: Exhibits calming and sedative effects, helping to improve sleep quality and reduce sleep disturbances.
  • Hydroxyvalerenic acid: Contributes to the relaxant and sedative properties of valerian, supporting healthy sleep patterns.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in valerian tincture that help with sleep deprivation.

  • Roots: Contain active compounds that promote relaxation and sleep.
  • Rhizomes: Rich in valerenic acid, known for its sedative properties.
  • Flowers: Offer calming effects, aiding in reducing sleep disturbances.
  • Leaves: Contain soothing compounds, which help in alleviating sleep deprivation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tincture for sleep deprivation.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of dried valerian root into small pieces and place in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 10 ounces of vodka or rum over the chopped valerian root, ensuring all pieces are fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store in a dark, cool place for a minimum of 2 weeks, shaking every other day.
  4. After 2 weeks, strain the liquid from the jar using cheesecloth into a dark glass bottle, squeezing out any excess liquid.
  5. Label the bottle with the contents and date, storing in a cool, dark place for up to 2 years, taking 1-2 mL before bed as needed for sleep deprivation.
