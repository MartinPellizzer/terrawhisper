10 best herbal tinctures for anxiety

The best herbal tinctures for anxiety are valerian, passionflower, ashwagandha, lemon balm, hops, chamomile, kava kava, skullcap, St. John's wort, and oat straw.

These natural remedies possess anxiety-reducing properties due to their calming and soothing effects on the nervous system. Valerian contains active compounds that promote relaxation, passionflower has a mild sedative effect that alleviates anxiety symptoms, ashwagandha regulates stress hormones, and lemon balm has a calming effect on the brain. Hops and chamomile have been used for centuries to soothe nerves and promote sleep.

Kava kava, skullcap, and St. John's wort have also been shown to reduce anxiety and promote mental clarity. Oat straw contains nutrients that nourish the nervous system and support overall mental well-being. By incorporating these herbal tinctures into their daily routine, individuals with anxiety can experience a decrease in symptoms such as restlessness, irritability, and insomnia.

For example, a person who struggles with anxiety and has trouble falling asleep can take a calming tincture made from a combination of these herbs before bedtime, promoting relaxation and a restful night's sleep.

1. Valerian tincture

Valerian tincture helps with anxiety because it contains valuable compounds, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, that have a calming effect on the brain.

These compounds bind to receptors in the brain responsible for regulating anxiety and relaxation, promoting a sense of tranquility and reducing symptoms of anxiety. Moreover, valerian tincture increases the production of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that inhibits nerve cells from overfiring, leading to a reduction in anxiety levels.

Regular intake of valerian tincture has been shown to improve anxiety symptoms, promoting a sense of well-being and reducing feelings of stress and tension.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tincture that aid with anxiety.

  • Valepotriates: Reduce anxiety by inhibiting the breakdown of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), promoting relaxation.
  • Iridoids: Possess anxiolytic properties, acting on neurotransmitter systems involved in stress response.
  • Flavanones: Exhibit anti-anxiety effects by modulating neurotransmitter activity and reducing oxidative stress.
  • Essential oil: Contains sedative compounds like bornyl acetate and camphene, helping to alleviate anxiety symptoms.
  • Alkaloids: Interact with GABA receptors, inducing relaxation and reducing anxiety levels.
  • Tannins: Exert an astringent effect, providing a soothing sensation that may help in managing anxiety.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in valerian tincture that help with anxiety.

  • Roots: Contain active compounds that act as a natural sedative, helping to reduce anxiety.
  • Rhizomes: Rich in valepotriates, which have been shown to have anti-anxiety effects.
  • Flowers: Contain volatile oils and flavonoids that have a calming effect on the nervous system.
  • Leaves: Packed with soothing compounds, including rosmarinic acid, which has been shown to reduce anxiety.
  • Seeds: Contain beneficial compounds, including essential oil, which may help to alleviate anxiety symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tincture for anxiety.

  1. Measure 1 ounce of dried valerian root, chop it finely, and place it in a clean jar.
  2. Pour 5 ounces of vodka or rum (80 proof) over the valerian root, ensuring all pieces are covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  4. After the extraction period, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine mesh strainer, squeezing to release all liquid.
  5. Transfer the liquid (valerian tincture) to a dark glass bottle, label it, and store it in a cool, dark place. Use within 2 years.
