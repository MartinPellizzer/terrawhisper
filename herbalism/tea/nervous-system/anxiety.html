10 Best herbal teas for anxiety

The best herbal teas for anxiety are chamomile, lemon balm, and passionflower.

These teas contain properties that promote relaxation and reduce anxiety. Chamomile is known for its calming effects due to the presence of flavonoids that bind to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain. Lemon balm contains rosmarinic acid and eugenol, which have anti-anxiety properties.

Passionflower has flavonoids that increase gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) levels, reducing anxiety and improving sleep quality. By incorporating these teas into their daily routine, individuals with anxiety can experience a decrease in symptoms and an overall improvement in their quality of life. For example, someone who experiences anxiety before bed can drink a cup of chamomile tea to help them relax and fall asleep more easily.

Similarly, someone who experiences anxiety during the day can drink lemon balm or passionflower tea to help them stay calm and focused.

1. Chamomile tea

Chamomile tea helps with anxiety because it contains the antioxidant apigenin, which binds to specific receptors in the brain that decrease anxiety and initiate sleep.

This herbal tea is also known for its soothing properties due to its warm temperature and calming aroma. Furthermore, chamomile tea promotes relaxation by helping to lower the levels of the stress hormone cortisol in the body.

Regular consumption of chamomile tea contributes to a sense of tranquility, making it an excellent natural remedy for individuals experiencing anxiety symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that aid with anxiety.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, acting as a natural sedative.
  • Chamazulene: A volatile oil that has anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce anxiety-induced inflammation in the body.
  • Luteolin: A flavonoid that contributes to the anxiolytic effect of chamomile, reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene that possesses anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and sedative properties, helping to alleviate anxiety symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in chamomile tea that help with anxiety.

  • Flowers: Rich in calming compounds, including apigenin and luteolin.
  • Leaves: Contain flavonoids that promote relaxation and reduce anxiety.
  • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants and essential oils that soothe the nervous system.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for anxiety.

  1. Gather 1 teabag of chamomile tea and 8 ounces of water.
  2. Boil water and let it rest for 2-3 minutes to cool slightly.
  3. Place teabag in a cup and pour hot water over it.
  4. Steep teabag for 5-7 minutes to allow full infusion.
  5. Remove teabag and enjoy the soothing warmth of your freshly brewed chamomile tea.

2. Lavender tea

Lavender tea helps with anxiety because it contains linalool and linalyl acetate, compounds known for their calming and soothing properties.

These compounds work by reducing the activity of voltage-gated channels in the brain, which leads to decreased excitation of neurons. This, in turn, promotes relaxation and can alleviate symptoms of anxiety. Furthermore, lavender is also known to have anxiolytic effects by modulating the GABA receptor, which is a neurotransmitter that regulates anxiety levels in the brain.

Therefore, drinking lavender tea can provide a natural and effective way to manage anxiety.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lavender tea that aid with anxiety.

  • Linalool: A terpene alcohol with sedative properties that helps reduce anxiety by promoting relaxation.
  • Lavandulol: A monoterpene with anxiolytic effects that decreases anxiety by promoting calmness.
  • Eucalyptol: A terpene known to reduce anxiety by improving mental clarity and focus.
  • Camphor: A terpene ketone that alleviates anxiety by acting as a mild stimulant, increasing mental alertness.
  • Caryophyllene: A sesquiterpene that exhibits anxiolytic effects by interacting with CB2 receptors in the endocannabinoid system.
  • Geraniol: A terpene alcohol that helps reduce anxiety by having a calming effect on the nervous system.
  • Limonene: A terpene that decreases anxiety by improving mood and reducing stress levels.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in lavender tea that help with anxiety.

  • Flowers: Contain volatile oils with calming properties that help reduce anxiety.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants that promote relaxation and alleviate anxiety symptoms.
  • Buds: High in essential oils, providing strong soothing effects, making them ideal for anxiety relief.
  • Stems: Contribute to the tea's aroma and taste, enhancing the relaxing experience when used for anxiety.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lavender tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water to 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried organic culinary lavender flowers to a tea infuser.
  3. Place the tea infuser in a cup and pour the hot water over it.
  4. Steep the lavender in hot water for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Remove the tea infuser and enjoy your calming lavender tea.

3. Lemon balm tea

Lemon balm tea helps with anxiety because it contains rosmarinic acid and eugenol, which have been found to possess anxiolytic properties.

These compounds work by inhibiting the GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) receptors in the brain, increasing the levels of this inhibitory neurotransmitter, which promotes relaxation and reduces feelings of anxiety. Additionally, lemon balm has been shown to decrease the activity of the enzyme COMT (catechol-O-methyltransferase), resulting in higher levels of dopamine and noradrenaline, neurotransmitters that contribute to feelings of well-being and happiness.

Drinking lemon balm tea, therefore, can effectively help manage anxiety and promote a sense of calm and tranquility.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lemon balm tea that aid with anxiety.

  • Rosmarinic acid: Possesses anti-anxiety properties, reduces anxiety symptoms.
  • Citral: Exhibits calming effects, helps alleviate anxiety.
  • Nerol: Acts as a nervine, aids in reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation.
  • Geraniol: Has anxiolytic effects, helps in managing anxiety.
  • Caffeic acid: Contributes to stress reduction, beneficial for anxiety relief.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in lemon balm tea that help with anxiety.

  • Leaves: Contain rosmarinic acid and eugenol, known for their calming and anti-anxiety effects.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, support relaxation, and help reduce anxiety symptoms.
  • Stems: Contribute to the plant's soothing properties, mildly relaxing and easing nervous tension.
  • Seeds: Packed with beneficial essential oils, providing mild sedative effects, and reducing anxiety.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemon balm tea for anxiety.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of fresh or 1 teaspoon of dried lemon balm leaves and rinse them under cold water.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the lemon balm leaves.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-10 minutes, then remove the leaves.
  4. Optional: Add honey or stevia for sweetness and chamomile flowers for added relaxation.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day.

4. Passionflower tea

Passionflower tea helps with anxiety because it contains harman alkaloids, such as harmine and harmaline, which act on the brain in a similar way to benzodiazepines - a type of medication commonly used to treat anxiety disorders.

These alkaloids boost the levels of a neurotransmitter called gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain. GABA is a chemical that helps regulate nerve impulses in the brain and can have a calming effect on the nervous system.

By increasing GABA levels, passionflower tea can help alleviate anxiety symptoms, promoting relaxation and reducing feelings of stress and tension.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in passionflower tea that aid with anxiety.

  • Flavonoids: These antioxidants help reduce anxiety by inhibiting the breakdown of neurotransmitters that regulate mood.
  • Alkaloids: Certain alkaloids in passionflower bind to GABA receptors in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.
  • Gaba: Passionflower contains Gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that blocks impulses between nerves, reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation.
  • Chrysin: This flavonoid in passionflower has anxiolytic and antidepressant-like effects, helping to lower anxiety levels.
  • Vitexin: Acting as a sedative, vitexin in passionflower aids in reducing anxiety and inducing calmness.
  • Isovitexin: Similar to vitexin, isovitexin in passionflower has sedative properties that help alleviate anxiety and promote relaxation.
  • Harman: Acting as a monoamine oxidase inhibitor, harman in passionflower increases neurotransmitter levels, reducing anxiety and promoting mood balance.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in passionflower tea that help with anxiety.

  • Leaves: Contain calming compounds, making them effective for reducing anxiety.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants that promote relaxation and alleviate anxiety symptoms.
  • Stems: Have anxiety-relieving properties, making them a crucial part for medicinal tea.
  • Buds: High in soothing agents, providing effective relief from anxiety.
  • Seeds: Contain beneficial compounds that aid in reducing anxiety levels.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic passionflower tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water, then pour it over 1 tablespoon of dried passionflower leaves.
  2. Steep the mixture, covered, for 10 minutes to extract the flavonoids and other calming compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the spent plant material.
  4. Optionally, add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day, to manage symptoms of anxiety.

Valerian tea helps with anxiety because it contains valerenic acid and other compounds that influence GABA levels in the brain.

By interacting with GABA receptors, valerian tea helps decrease neuronal activity, thereby soothing the nervous system, alleviating anxiety symptoms, and encouraging relaxation.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tea that aid with anxiety.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in valerian tea that help with anxiety.

  • Roots: Contain high levels of antioxidants and valerenic acid, which have calming effects on the nervous system.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tea for anxiety.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried valerian root with 8 ounces of boiling water.

  3. Strain the tea to remove the valerian root pieces.
  4. Optionally, add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while still warm, up to three times a day for anxiety relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ashwagandha tea for anxiety.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried ashwagandha root, then simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey for added sweetness, if desired.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your nerves.

7. Kava kava tea

Kava kava tea helps with anxiety because it contains active compounds called kavalactones, which have been scientifically shown to reduce anxiety by interacting with brain receptors responsible for regulating mood and relaxation.

These kavalactones bind to the limbic system, promoting a calming effect and alleviating feelings of stress and anxiety.

Moreover, kava kava tea does not cause dependence or tolerance, unlike many prescription anxiety medications, making it a safer and more natural alternative for managing symptoms of anxiety.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in kava kava tea that aid with anxiety.

  • Kavalactones: These are the main active compounds in kava kava tea that produce anxiety-reducing effects by interacting with the limbic system, which is involved in emotional processing.
  • Chalcones: These flavonoids found in kava kava tea possess anxiolytic properties due to their ability to increase GABA levels in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.
  • Flavokawains: These neuroprotective compounds in kava kava tea help alleviate anxiety by inhibiting the breakdown of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), an inhibitory neurotransmitter that promotes calmness.
  • Dihydromethysticin: A kavalactone responsible for the anxiolytic effects of kava kava tea, it works by activating GABA receptors in the brain to produce a calming effect.
  • Desmethoxyyangonin: A kavalactone that contributes to the anxiolytic properties of kava kava tea through its interaction with dopamine receptors, enhancing feelings of well-being and reducing anxiety.
  • Yangonin: A kavalactone found in kava kava tea that exhibits anxiolytic effects by inhibiting the reuptake of dopamine and norepinephrine, leading to increased levels of these neurotransmitters and reduced anxiety.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in kava kava tea that help with anxiety.

  • Roots: Contain high levels of kavalactones, the main active ingredients in kava kava tea, known to help reduce anxiety.
  • Rhizomes: Rich in starchy materials and kavalactones, providing calming effects and supporting anxiety relief.
  • Leaves: Contain low levels of kavalactones, contributing to the plant's relaxing and anxiety-reducing properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic kava kava tea for anxiety.

  1. Measure out 2-3 teaspoons of dried kava kava root, depending on desired potency.
  2. In a blender, blend the kava root with 1 cup of room temperature water for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Strain the blended mixture through a cheesecloth or nut milk bag into a pot.
  4. Warm the strained liquid over low heat for 5-10 minutes, but do not let it boil.
  5. Pour the kava kava tea into a cup and enjoy for anxiety relief.

Hops tea helps with anxiety because it contains volatile oils, including humulene and lupulone, that have been shown to produce a calming effect on the nervous system.

These compounds act as natural sedatives, helping to reduce feelings of anxiety and promote relaxation. Additionally, hops tea contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which also have been linked to reducing anxiety and promoting overall mental health.

Drinking hops tea regularly can help individuals manage their anxiety symptoms and improve their overall quality of life.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in hops tea that aid with anxiety.

  • Luteolin: A flavonoid that has anxiolytic effects, potentially by reducing inflammation.
  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that has been shown to have a calming effect by promoting GABA receptor activity.
  • Humulone: A bitter acid that may help reduce anxiety by activating certain serotonin receptors.
  • Xanthohumol: A prenylated chalcone with potential anxiolytic effects, possibly by inhibiting the enzyme that breaks down serotonin.
  • Myrcene: A terpene with sedative properties, which may help reduce anxiety by promoting relaxation.
  • Caryophyllene: A sesquiterpene that activates CB2 receptors in the brain, potentially leading to reduced anxiety.
  • Farnesene: A sesquiterpene found in hops that may have anxiolytic effects, possibly due to its ability to act as a positive allosteric modulator of GABA receptors.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in hops tea that help with anxiety.

  • Flowers: Hops tea made from the flowers of the hop plant contains nutrients and chemicals that have a calming effect on the nervous system, making it effective for reducing anxiety.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the hop plant have mild sedative properties and can be used to make a soothing tea that alleviates symptoms of anxiety.
  • Stems: The stems of the hop plant contain essential oils and bitter acids that contribute to its anxiety-reducing properties and are often included in tea blends.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hops tea for anxiety.

  1. Measure out 1 teaspoon of dried hop flowers and place them in a tea infuser.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool for 1 minute to ensure it does not damage the delicate hop compounds.
  3. Pour the hot water over the hop flowers in the tea infuser and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser and discard the spent hop flowers.
  5. Enjoy your calming cup of hops tea, knowing it was prepared correctly to help with anxiety.

Furthermore, St. john's wort has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help protect nerve cells from damage, promoting overall brain health and well-being.

  • Flavonoids: Possess anxiolytic properties, helping to alleviate anxiety by interacting with GABA receptors in the brain.
  • Essential oils: Contain calming compounds like linalool and geraniol, which aid in reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation.
  • Phenolic acids: Exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, potentially contributing to reduced anxiety and improved overall well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in st. john's wort tea that help with anxiety.

  • Leaves: Also contain hypericin and hyperforin, and are often used in teas for their calming effects.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic st. john's wort tea for anxiety.

  1. Measure out 2 teaspoons of dried St. John's wort flowers and leaves.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the St. John's wort.
  3. Steep the tea for 10 minutes to release the active compounds.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the plant material.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.

How to best treat anxiety with herbal tea?

The best way to treat anxiety with herbal tea is by selecting teas with natural calming properties, such as chamomile, lemon balm, or passionflower.

These teas contain compounds that promote relaxation and help reduce anxiety symptoms. To maximize benefits, drink these teas regularly, ideally before bedtime or during stressful moments.

Keep in mind that individual responses may vary, and it's essential to find the tea that works best for you.

What are the most common causes of anxiety that are treatable with herbal tea?

The primary causes of anxiety treatable with herbal tea include daily stress, restlessness, and mild sleep disturbances.

These teas can help promote relaxation, improve mood, and ease muscle tension, acting as a calming agent for the body and mind.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be beneficial, they should never replace any prescribed medications or professional medical advice.
