1. Calendula

Calendula tea helps with oral thrush because it has anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties, which are effective in treating oral thrush, a condition caused by the overgrowth of the Candida fungus in the mouth.

The calendula plant, from which the tea is derived, contains flavonoids that have a protective effect on tissues, helping to reduce inflammation and promote healing in the mouth. Moreover, calendula tea's antimicrobial properties can help prevent the spread of the Candida fungus, while its soothing effects can alleviate pain and discomfort associated with oral thrush. Regular consumption of calendula tea may help maintain a healthy oral environment, preventing the recurrence of oral thrush and promoting overall oral health.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare provider before using calendula tea or any other alternative treatment for oral thrush.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in calendula tea that help with oral thrush.

  1. Anti-inflammatory compounds: Calendula contains anti-inflammatory agents, which help reduce inflammation and irritation in the mouth, providing relief from oral thrush.
  2. Antifungal agents: Calendula has natural antifungal properties that can help inhibit the growth of Candida fungus, the primary cause of oral thrush.
  3. Wound-healing compounds: The healing properties of calendula promote tissue regeneration, helping to repair damaged oral tissues affected by oral thrush.
  4. Flavonoids: Flavonoids in calendula have antimicrobial and antiviral properties, which can help control the overgrowth of Candida fungus and support overall oral health.

The following list reveals what parts of calendula are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for oral thrush.

  1. Flowers: Calendula's flower petals are rich in antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making them an ideal part for treating oral thrush.
  2. Leaves: The leaves of calendula contain similar medicinal properties as the flowers and can be used as a milder alternative for treating oral thrush.
  3. Stems: Although less potent, the stems of calendula still contain medicinal properties, contributing to the plant's overall effectiveness in treating oral thrush when used in teas.

The following procedure explains how to make calendula tea for oral thrush.

  1. Pick 1-2 teaspoons of dried calendula petals and place them in a tea infuser or loose in a tea pot.
  2. Steep the calendula in hot water for 10-15 minutes to allow the beneficial compounds to extract.
  3. After steeping, remove the tea infuser or strain out the petals.
  4. Sip the calendula tea, allowing it to swish in your mouth for a minute before swallowing, for oral thrush relief. Repeat as needed up to 3 times daily.
