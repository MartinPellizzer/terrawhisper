10 best herbal teas for breastfeeding

When it comes to herbal teas that are beneficial for breastfeeding, there are a few standout options.

Fennel tea is a popular choice due to its potential to increase milk supply and its ability to soothe digestive issues in both mother and baby. Fenugreek tea is another great option for increasing milk production. Raspberry leaf tea is also commonly recommended for breastfeeding mothers, as it can help tone the uterus and shorten the recovery time after childbirth.

However, it's important to note that while these herbs are generally considered safe, it's always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedy, especially when breastfeeding.

1. Fenugreek

Fenugreek tea contains phytoestrogens that resemble female sex hormones, boosting the body's estrogen levels and promoting milk production.

This herbal tea's galactagogue properties stimulate mammary glands, increasing prolactin release and thereby milk flow. Moreover, fenugreek tea's high concentration of beneficial nutrients, like vitamins and antioxidants, supports postnatal health and general well-being. As a natural, caffeine-free beverage, fenugreek tea offers breastfeeding mothers a safe and effective option for enhancing their milk supply and maintaining optimal hydration.

Finally, centuries of anecdotal evidence and traditional wisdom endorse fenugreek tea's effectiveness in promoting lactation, making it a time-honored remedy for breastfeeding mothers.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in fenugreek tea that help with breastfeeding.

  • Seeds: The seeds of the fenugreek plant contain phytoestrogens and diosgenin, which stimulate breast milk production and are therefore used to make medicinal tea for breastfeeding.
  • Leaves: Fenugreek leaves possess galactagogue properties, making them an excellent addition to medicinal teas aimed at boosting breast milk supply.
  • Flowers: Fenugreek flowers are rich in bioactive compounds that enhance mammary gland function, making them a popular choice for medicinal teas to support breastfeeding.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fenugreek tea for breastfeeding.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove seeds, then stir in honey and lemon juice to taste.
  3. Warm the tea if necessary, but do not boil.
  4. Drink 2-3 cups of this tea daily, ideally between meals.
  5. Continue drinking fenugreek tea for several days to notice increased breast milk production.

2. Blessed Thistle

Blessed thistle tea aids breastfeeding by stimulating milk production.

This herbal tea contains active compounds that promote mammary gland tissue growth, which enhances milk supply. By consuming blessed thistle tea, nursing mothers introduce phytoestrogens that mimic estrogen, leading to increased prolactin levels.

Subsequently, prolactin stimulates milk production. Historically, blessed thistle has been a trusted galactagogue, or milk-producing agent, employed by many cultures to support breastfeeding.

Thus, blessed thistle tea unequivocally boosts breast milk supply in lactating women.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in blessed thistle tea that help with breastfeeding.

  • Leaves: Rich in vitamins and minerals, leaves are often used in breastfeeding tea to support overall health.
  • Flowers: Known for their galactagogue properties, flowers help increase milk production in breastfeeding mothers.
  • Seeds: High in essential fatty acids, seeds can promote mammary gland health and aid in milk let-down.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic blessed thistle tea for breastfeeding.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1-2 teaspoons of crushed blessed thistle herb.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the herb particles, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Stir in honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of the blessed thistle tea throughout the day, preferably between feedings.

3. Fennel

Fennel tea aids breastfeeding by enhancing milk production.

This herbal tea contains anethole, a compound that stimulates prolactin release, a hormone essential for milk production. Regular consumption of fennel tea by nursing mothers leads to increased breast milk volume. Moreover, fennel's galactagogue properties, rooted in its historical use, support its effectiveness in promoting lactation.

Additionally, fennel's antispasmodic and carminative effects can alleviate gastrointestinal issues in both mother and baby, further facilitating breastfeeding.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in fennel tea that help with breastfeeding.

  • Roots: The roots of fennel contain phytoestrogens that can stimulate milk production in breastfeeding mothers.
  • Seeds: Fennel seeds are rich in volatile oils, which can help increase milk flow and reduce inflammation in the breast.
  • Leaves: The leaves of fennel have been used traditionally to promote breast milk production and soothe digestive issues in both mothers and infants.
  • Flowering tops: The flowering tops of fennel contain essential oils and antioxidants that can support healthy lactation and alleviate discomfort during breastfeeding.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fennel tea for breastfeeding.

  1. Chop one tablespoon of fresh fennel seeds or one teaspoon of fennel seeds powder.
  2. Place the chopped fennel seeds in a cup and pour 8 ounces of boiling water over them.
  3. Let the fennel seeds steep in the boiling water for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the fennel seeds from the water and discard them.
  5. Drink the fennel tea, up to three cups a day, to help with breastfeeding.

4. Nettle

Nettle tea provides vital nutrients, like calcium and iron, that promote breast milk production.

This herbal tea's natural chemical composition includes high levels of minerals and vitamins, which nursing mothers need to maintain their health and support milk supply. Nettle tea's nutrients also help ease common discomforts experienced during breastfeeding, such as postpartum cramping. Furthermore, nettle tea's diuretic properties assist the body in eliminating toxins, thereby enhancing overall well-being and lactation.

Thus, consuming nettle tea bolsters the breastfeeding process by nourishing both mother and baby.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in nettle tea that help with breastfeeding.

  • Leaves: Rich in vitamins and minerals, the leaves promote milk production and alleviate engorgement.
  • Seeds: Containing essential fatty acids, seeds support mammary gland health and boost milk quality.
  • Flowers: With mild diuretic properties, flowers help maintain breast comfort and assist in postpartum recovery.
  • Stems: High in chlorophyll, stems aid in blood purification and encourage milk letdown.
  • Rhyzomes: Packed with nutrients, rhyzomes strengthen the immune system of both mother and baby.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic nettle tea for breastfeeding.

  1. Pick 2 handfuls of fresh stinging nettle leaves, avoiding the stalks and flowers.
  2. Rinse the nettle leaves thoroughly under cold running water to remove any dirt or bugs.
  3. Crush or chop the nettle leaves to release their natural chemicals and aid infusion.
  4. Place the nettle leaves in a teapot and add 1 liter of boiling water.
  5. Let the nettle tea steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain and serve. Enjoy up to 3 cups a day.

Alfalfa tea enhances breastfeeding by providing an abundance of nutrients, including vitamins, minerals, and phytoestrogens, that transfer to breast milk.

This tea's high levels of phytoestrogens boost the mother's estrogen levels, which encourages mammary gland growth and milk production. Moreover, alfalfa tea contains essential amino acids, assisting in the development and repair of body tissues while providing the nutrients necessary for plentiful milk supply. Ultimately, alfalfa tea's unique combination of nutrients, phytoestrogens, and saponins promotes an ample milk supply and contributes to an effective breastfeeding experience.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in alfalfa tea that help with breastfeeding.

  • Leaves: Rich in vitamins and minerals, alfalfa leaves promote lactation and provide essential nutrients for both mother and baby.
  • Flowers: Packed with phytoestrogens, alfalfa flowers support hormonal balance and increase breast milk production.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic alfalfa tea for breastfeeding.

  1. Measure - Boil 2 cups of water and let 1 teaspoon of dried alfalfa leaves steep for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Steep - Cover and allow the alfalfa leaves to steep in hot water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain - Strain the alfalfa leaves from the water, discarding the leaves.
  4. Sweeten - Add honey or sugar to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve - Let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature before consuming. Enjoy!

6. Goat'S Rue

Goat's rue tea contains natural galactagogues, specifically deguelin and 4-hydroxyisoleucine, which prompt the body to produce an increased supply of breast milk.

These active compounds stimulate mammary gland growth and milk-secreting cells, enhancing milk production. By consuming goat's rue tea, nursing mothers experience a boost in their milk supply, providing them with the confidence that they can adequately nourish their infants. This tea offers a time-tested, bioactive solution for breastfeeding mothers seeking to support their milk production.

Furthermore, goat's rue tea's long history of traditional use and its scientifically validated components provide a reliable option for those looking for natural ways to promote lactation.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in goat's rue tea that help with breastfeeding.

  • Leaves: Rich in galactagogues, the leaves of goat's rue are known to promote milk flow in breastfeeding mothers.
  • Seeds: Containing high concentrations of fermentable carbohydrates, goat's rue seeds aid in increasing prolactin levels, stimulating breast milk production.
  • Flowers: Goat's rue flowers are a potent source of isoflavones, which have estrogenic effects that support and enhance lactation during breastfeeding.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic goat's rue tea for breastfeeding.

  1. Measure 1-2 teaspoons of dried goat's rue herb, placing it in an infuser or teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water, then pour it over the herb, allowing it to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Cover and let the mixture rest for a few minutes to maximize beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the infuser or strain the tea, discarding the spent herb.
  5. Serve and enjoy the warm goat's rue tea, up to three times a day.

7. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, made from the Marshmallow plant's roots, contains mucilage, a substance known to soothe and coat membranes in the body.

This tea aids breastfeeding by easing irritation in the mother's breasts, reducing discomfort during nursing. The mucilage also promotes milk flow by moistening breast tissue, making it more flexible and receptive to let-down reflexes. Moreover, the tea's anti-inflammatory properties can alleviate engorgement and mastitis symptoms, ensuring a steady milk supply.

Finally, marshmallow tea's demulcent qualities can relieve gas and colic in breastfed infants, improving overall feeding experiences for both mother and baby.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with breastfeeding.

  • Roots: The marshmallow root contains mucilage, which soothes inflammation in the breast tissue and promotes milk production.
  • Leaves: The leaves are a demulcent, providing a soothing effect to the breastfeeding mother's sensitive breast tissues.
  • Flowers: The flowers have been used to treat breast engorgement and promote let-down due to their anti-inflammatory properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for breastfeeding.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water, 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root, and 1/4 teaspoon of dried fennel seeds; bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the solids.
  4. Add honey to taste, stir until dissolved.
  5. Drink the tea warm, up to three times a day.

8. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea aids breastfeeding by increasing milk supply, thanks to its active compound, silymarin.

This compound stimulates the mammary glands, promoting milk production. Moreover, milk thistle is an effective galactagogue, a substance that enhances breast milk production. Additionally, it contains antioxidants that protect breast tissue from damage and support overall breast health.

Ultimately, drinking milk thistle tea consistently helps nursing mothers maintain a sufficient milk supply and supports their breast health during lactation.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in milk thistle tea that help with breastfeeding.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic milk thistle tea for breastfeeding.

  2. Steep the mixture, covered, for 10-15 minutes to allow the nutrients to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the seeds and discard them.
  4. Optionally, add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day.

9. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains bioactive substances, including apigenin, which produces a mild sedative effect.

This calming property helps new mothers relax and reduces stress, fostering an optimal environment for breastmilk production. Chamomile tea's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic components alleviate digestive discomfort, promoting better nutrient absorption and increased milk supply. Furthermore, the tea's antioxidants support overall health, contributing to a robust immune system that aids in milk secretion.

Lastly, as a caffeine-free beverage, chamomile tea helps maintain a healthy sleep cycle, ensuring mothers receive adequate rest needed for successful and consistent breastfeeding.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with breastfeeding.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers contain volatile oils, flavonoids, and other compounds that possess anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, making them helpful for promoting relaxation and potentially increasing milk supply in breastfeeding mothers.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile plants have been used in teas for their mild sedative effects, which can help reduce stress and promote rest, both beneficial for breastfeeding mothers.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in antioxidants and have been used to treat gastrointestinal issues, which can help alleviate discomfort and promote overall well-being in breastfeeding mothers.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for breastfeeding.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle or pot, which typically takes around 5-10 minutes on a stovetop.
  2. Place one chamomile tea bag or 1-2 teaspoons of loose chamomile flowers in a teapot or directly into a cup.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the tea bag or loose chamomile flowers.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for about 5-7 minutes to extract the flavors and health benefits.
  5. Remove the tea bag or strain out the loose chamomile flowers before drinking. Enjoy your soothing chamomile tea.

10. Lavender

Lavender tea aids breastfeeding by promoting milk production, thanks to its natural phytoestrogens that stimulate prolactin release.

This herbal tea, moreover, contains antioxidants and soothing properties, which help new mothers relax and alleviate stress, thereby facilitating let-down reflex and smooth milk flow. Furthermore, lavender tea's calming effects encourage better sleep quality, enabling lactating mothers to maintain their energy levels and overall well-being. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties also help soothe breast discomfort and ease engorgement, making lavender tea an ideal support for breastfeeding.

Lastly, lavender tea's delightful taste and aroma create a comforting and enjoyable breastfeeding experience, fostering a strong bond between mother and baby.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in lavender tea that help with breastfeeding.

  • Flowers: Rich in nutrients and antioxidants, lavender flowers promote milk supply and soothe engorged breasts.
  • Leaves: Containing phytoestrogens, lavender leaves support hormonal balance and increase prolactin levels.
  • Buds: High in essential oils, lavender buds alleviate stress and anxiety, promoting relaxation during breastfeeding.
  • Stems: Infused with gentle bitter compounds, lavender stems stimulate digestion and relieve colic in breastfed infants.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lavender tea for breastfeeding.

  1. In a small pot, bring 8 ounces of water to a boil, which takes about 5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried culinary lavender flowers, crushing them slightly to release oils, and let steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup, discarding the spent lavender.
  4. Optionally, add 1 teaspoon of honey for sweetness, mixing until fully dissolved.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three cups per day, for natural breastfeeding support.
