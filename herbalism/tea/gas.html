10 Best Herbal Teas For Gas

Herbal teas, particularly those made from ingredients like ginger, peppermint, chamomile, and fennel, can help alleviate gas due to their carminative and antispasmodic properties.

These herbs can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles, allowing trapped gas to be released more easily. Additionally, they can reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, further aiding in the expulsion of gas. Furthermore, some of these herbs, like ginger and peppermint, can stimulate the production of digestive enzymes and juices, enhancing the breakdown of food and preventing the formation of excess gas.

Lastly, these teas are caffeine-free and generally considered gentle on the stomach, making them a suitable option for those with sensitive digestive systems.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with gas because it has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help to relax the smooth muscles in the gastrointestinal tract, reducing the contractions that can lead to gas buildup.

Additionally, chamomile contains volatile oils, such as alpha-bisabolol and matricin, which have been shown to have carminative effects, which help to expel gas from the digestive tract. The soothing effect of chamomile tea on the stomach and intestines can also help to alleviate symptoms of bloating, indigestion, and stomach cramps associated with gas. Furthermore, chamomile tea has been used traditionally as a digestive aid and its calming properties may help to reduce stress and anxiety, which can exacerbate digestive issues and lead to the production of gas.

Ultimately, chamomile tea can be a gentle and effective natural remedy for reducing gas and promoting overall digestive health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with gas.

  1. Bisabolol: This terpene has anti-inflammatory and spasmolytic properties that help relax the smooth muscles of the digestive tract, alleviating gas and bloating.
  2. Chamazulene: This chemical compound possesses anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that aid in relieving inflammation and reducing painful spasms in the gastrointestinal tract, thereby alleviating gas.
  3. Apigenin: A flavonoid with antispasmodic effects, apigenin helps reduce smooth muscle spasms in the digestive tract, which can help relieve gas and associated discomfort.
  4. Matricin: This precursor to chamazulene has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe inflamed tissues in the digestive tract, reducing gas and associated symptoms.
  5. Spiroethereol: This sesquiterpene lactone has been found to have antispasmodic effects, which can help alleviate gastrointestinal spasms and reduce gas accumulation.

The following list reveals what parts of chamomile are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for gas.

  1. Flowers: Chamomile flowers are used due to their calming properties, which can help alleviate gas and bloating in the stomach.
  2. Leaves: The leaves of the chamomile plant are often included in medicinal teas as they contain volatile oils, which can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and reduce the formation of gas.
  3. Seeds: Chamomile seeds are sometimes used in medicinal teas due to their high essential oil content, which can aid in breaking down food and preventing the build-up of gas in the digestive tract.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for gas.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water, reaching a temperature of 212°F, for full chamomile flower extraction.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers or 1 chamomile tea bag to a cup or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers or tea bag, allowing it to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Cover the cup or teapot during the steeping process to preserve the beneficial compounds and temperature.
  5. Strain or remove the tea bag, then let the chamomile tea cool down until it's comfortably warm before drinking, ideally 3-5 minutes after preparation.
