10 best herbal teas for overeating

For those looking to manage overeating, certain herbal teas can be a great tool.

Peppermint tea is known to help control appetite and reduce cravings, making it an excellent choice. Ginger tea can also aid in digestion and reduce the bloating that often accompanies overeating. Chamomile tea is another good option, as it has calming properties that can help reduce stress-related eating.

Lastly, dandelion tea can help stimulate digestion and prevent fluid retention, making it a beneficial tea to drink after overeating.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea inhibits excessive eating through its powerful aroma and soothing effect on the stomach.

This tea triggers a sense of fullness in the brain, reducing the desire for excessive food intake. Moreover, the menthol present in peppermint tea slows down the digestion process, allowing the brain to receive satiety signals promptly. Consequently, peppermint tea helps control appetite and aids in portion control.

Ultimately, peppermint tea's ability to promote relaxation also reduces stress-related overeating.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with overeating.

  • Leaves: Peppermint leaves are rich in menthol and menthone, compounds known to promote healthy digestion and reduce appetite, making it an ideal choice for medicinal tea to support overeating management.
  • Buds: Peppermint buds contain essential oils that aid in digestion, providing relief from symptoms of overeating such as bloating and discomfort.
  • Flowers: Peppermint flowers are a natural source of antioxidants and calming agents, effective in soothing digestive issues related to overeating.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for overeating.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves with 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the tea for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, creating a clear liquid.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the peppermint tea slowly after overeating to soothe the stomach.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea's effectiveness in restricting overeating stems from its active components, such as gingerols and shogaols, which possess appetite-suppressing properties.

These compounds work by increasing fullness and satisfaction, as they slow down the rate at which the stomach empties. As a result, ginger tea consumption reduces the urge to eat excessively. Furthermore, ginger's impact on delaying gastric emptying extends to enhancing digestion and nutrient absorption, discouraging hasty or overindulgent eating habits.

Additionally, ginger tea alleviates gastrointestinal discomforts and inflammation, fostering a healthier and more balanced digestive system that is less prone to overeating caused by discomfort or irritation.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with overeating.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are a popular choice for medicinal tea to help with overeating due to their natural digestive properties.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the ginger plant possess anti-inflammatory compounds, making them useful for soothing the digestive system after overeating.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers contain antioxidants that support overall digestive health, making them a valuable addition to medicinal tea for overeating.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds are rich in essential oils that aid in digestion and reduce feelings of bloating and discomfort after overeating.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for overeating.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger, then place in a pot with 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, and stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey and the juice of half a lemon.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the ginger pieces, if desired.
  5. Serve and enjoy the warm ginger tea to soothe your digestive system after overeating.

3. Chamomile

Chamomile tea promotes overeating reduction due to its nutritional components, specifically applicable phytochemicals.

One such compound, Chrysanthenol, present in chamomile, triggers a sensation of fullness, consequently decreasing food intake. Additionally, chamomile's mildly bitter properties contribute to this satiating effect, further diminishing overeating tendencies. Furthermore, chamomile's antioxidant-rich nature, provided by flavonoids like apigenin, contributes to stress alleviation, indirectly limiting emotional eating.

Lastly, chamomile tea's catechin content facilitates metabolism, aiding in digestion and weight management.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with overeating.

  • Flowers: The chamomile flowers are used in medicinal tea for overeating due to their calming properties that can help reduce anxiety and promote feelings of fullness.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain antispasmodic properties that help soothe the digestive system, making it beneficial for overeating.
  • Seeds: The seeds of chamomile are rich in flavonoids that have antioxidant properties, helping to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and prevent overeating.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for overeating.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes to prepare the base for the tea.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers to a teapot or infuser and pour the hot water over them.
  3. Steep the chamomile flowers for 5-7 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve a soothing flavor.
  4. Strain the chamomile flowers from the tea using a fine-mesh strainer or infuser.
  5. Serve the chamomile tea hot, optionally adding a small amount of honey or lemon for taste, to aid in digestion and reduce the symptoms of overeating.

4. Licorice

Licorice tea suppresses overeating by stabilizing blood sugar levels, reducing cravings for sweet or salty foods.

This tea contains glycyrrhizin, a compound that inhibits the breakdown of cortisol, a stress hormone responsible for weight gain. Moreover, licorice tea promotes satiety by expanding in the stomach, signaling fullness to the brain. Consequently, it decreases overall food intake while boosting metabolism, contributing to weight loss.

Finally, licorice tea ensures a healthier digestive system by reducing inflammation, easing indigestion, and relieving bloating.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with overeating.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice plant are used to make medicinal tea for overeating due to their ability to slow down the digestion process, helping to control hunger.
  • Rhizomes: These horizontal underground stems contain high levels of glycyrrhizin, providing a sweet flavor and soothing effect on the stomach.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the licorice plant are used in teas for their mild laxative properties, aiding in the elimination of excess food from the stomach.
  • Flowers: Though less common, the flowers can be used in teas for their anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe an upset stomach caused by overeating.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the licorice plant contain similar properties to the roots, making them a suitable alternative for tea-making, aiding in digestion and reducing hunger.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for overeating.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove pot from heat, add 1 teaspoon of honey (if desired), and let the tea cool for a few minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent licorice root.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your stomach and aid digestion after overeating.

5. Dandelion

Dandelion tea promotes digestion by containing nutrients that stimulate the production of digestive enzymes in the liver.

This increase in enzymes allows the body to break down food more efficiently, reducing the sensation of overeating. Moreover, dandelion tea boasts high levels of fiber, which encourages satiety and decreases the likelihood of overindulging. Additionally, this herbal tea's gentle diuretic properties assist in flushing excess fluids and toxins from the body, further supporting optimal digestive function.

Finally, the soothing effect of dandelion tea on the stomach lining contributes to a reduced appetite and healthier eating habits.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in dandelion tea that help with overeating.

  • Roots: Rich in beneficial compounds, the roots aid in digestion and help control overeating.
  • Leaves: Packed with nutrients, the leaves help in curbing appetite and maintaining a healthy digestive system.
  • Flowers: High in antioxidants, the flowers support metabolism and reduce the urge to overeat.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for overeating.

  1. Gather 2-3 dandelion heads with intact yellow flowers, ensuring they're from a pesticide-free area; rinse under cold water to remove dirt.
  2. Separate flower petals from the green base and place them in a teapot or infuser.
  3. Pour 1 cup (250ml) of boiling water over the petals, cover, and let it steep for 5-7 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the petals, pour it into a cup, and let it cool for 2-3 minutes before drinking.
  5. Sip the dandelion tea slowly, allowing it to potentially soothe your stomach and aid digestion after overeating.

6. Fennel

Fennel tea aids in digestion due to its natural compounds, including volatile oils and fenchone, that stimulate the production of digestive enzymes.

These enzymes break down food more efficiently, allowing the body to absorb nutrients better and reducing the sensation of overeating. Additionally, fennel tea's carminative properties relieve gas and bloating, making it an effective tool against overindulgence. Furthermore, fennel's rosmarinic acid offers anti-inflammatory benefits, soothing the gastrointestinal tract and promoting a feeling of satiety.

Finally, the anethole in fennel tea has been shown to suppress appetite, contributing to an overall reduction in food intake.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in fennel tea that help with overeating.

  • Seeds: Fennel seeds are an effective traditional remedy for overeating due to their carminative properties that help reduce bloating and aid digestion.
  • Leaves: The feathery leaves of fennel are beneficial for making medicinal tea to control overeating as they contain volatile oils that stimulate the production of digestive enzymes.
  • Stems: Fennel stems have a mild anise flavor, making them suitable for medicinal tea for overeating; they contain dietary fiber that promotes satiety and regulates appetite.
  • Flowers: The bright yellow flowers of fennel contain potent antioxidants, making them ideal for medicinal tea to alleviate overeating-induced digestive discomfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fennel tea for overeating.

  1. Chop: Slice 1 tablespoon of fennel seeds or fresh fennel bulb.
  2. Steep: Place fennel in a tea infuser and steep in boiling water for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain: Remove the infuser and discard the fennel.
  4. Sweeten (optional): Stir in honey or another sweetener to taste.
  5. Serve: Drink the tea hot, up to three times a day after meals.

7. Fenugreek

Fenugreek tea combats overeating due to its natural ability to slow down gastric emptying, thereby extending the feeling of fullness and satiety.

This herbal tea achieves this effect thanks to the presence of galactomannan, a type of soluble fiber, which turns into a gel-like substance in the digestive tract and delays nutrient absorption. Studies demonstrate that fenugreek tea consumption leads to reduced calorie intake during subsequent meals, making it an effective tool for portion control. Furthermore, fenugreek tea has no known addictive properties, unlike some pharmaceutical appetite suppressants, making it a safer long-term solution.

Lastly, the soothing and relaxing qualities of fenugreek tea help mitigate stress-related overeating by promoting mental well-being and regulating cortisol levels.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in fenugreek tea that help with overeating.

  • Seeds: Fenugreek seeds are rich in fiber, helping to reduce appetite and slow down the digestion process, making it useful for managing overeating.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the fenugreek plant contain galactomannan, a type of soluble fiber that aids in curbing excessive hunger, making it ideal for managing overeating.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fenugreek tea for overeating.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Turn off heat, add 1 teaspoon of honey and 1/4 teaspoon of lemon juice (if desired); stir well.
  3. Cover and let the mixture steep for 5 more minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the seeds before pouring it into a cup.
  5. Enjoy your homemade fenugreek tea, preferably between meals to help manage overeating.

8. Valerian

Valerian tea aids in reducing overeating due to its active compounds, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, which have an impact on the neurotransmitter GABA in the brain.

These compounds found in valerian tea bind to GABA receptors, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, thus minimizing the likelihood of stress-induced overeating. Furthermore, valerian tea has the ability to regulate the hypothalamus, a part of the brain that controls appetite, by influencing the release of hormones responsible for hunger and satiety signals. Additionally, it contains antioxidants, such as flavonoids and essential oils, that help combat oxidative stress and inflammation, both of which can contribute to compulsive eating behaviors.

Lastly, valerian tea's soothing properties can enhance sleep quality, as poor sleep contributes to increased appetite and weight gain, making it an effective tool in curbing overeating and maintaining a healthy weight.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in valerian tea that help with overeating.

  • Roots: The roots of the valerian plant are rich in active compounds that promote digestion and have a soothing effect on the body, making them ideal for medicinal tea to aid overeating.
  • Rhizomes: Valerian rhizomes contain natural sedatives that regulate the digestive system, making it an excellent choice for medicinal tea to control overeating.
  • Leaves: Valerian leaves have calming properties that help reduce anxiety and promote healthy digestion, making them suitable for medicinal tea to manage overeating.
  • Flowers: Valerian flowers contain essential oils that aid in digestion and reduce bloating, making them a perfect addition to medicinal tea for overeating.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tea for overeating.

  1. In a tea infuser, place one teaspoon of dried valerian root.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the infuser.
  3. Let the tea steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the infuser and discard the used valerian root.
  5. Drink the tea as needed, up to three times a day, to help alleviate overeating.

9. Skullcap

Skullcap tea combats overeating due to its governing effect on the appetite center in the brain.

This tea's bitterness signals the brain to reduce hunger, promoting satiety. Its active components, including flavonoids, bind to dopamine receptors, influencing feelings of fullness.

Furthermore, skullcap tea's soothing properties can alleviate stress-related overeating, thus helping to control food intake.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in skullcap tea that help with overeating.

  • Leaves: Rich in rosmarinic acid, they help suppress appetite and aid in healthy digestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic skullcap tea for overeating.

  1. Measure - Use 2 teaspoons of dried skullcap herb for every 8 ounces of water.
  2. Boil - Bring the water to a boil in a pot or kettle.
  3. Steep - Place the skullcap herb in a tea infuser or empty tea bag, then pour the boiling water over it and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain - Remove the tea infuser or bag and discard the used herb.
  5. Serve - Drink the skullcap tea warm, up to three times a day, to help manage overeating.

10. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, made from the marshmallow plant's roots, contains a substance called mucilage that creates a barrier in the stomach, contributing to a feeling of fullness.

This sense of satisfaction reduces overeating. Moreover, this herbal tea's soothing properties ease digestion, discouraging overindulgence. In addition, its mild flavor and comforting warmth make it an appealing alternative to high-calorie snacks, aiding weight management.

Finally, marshmallow tea's historic use for addressing digestive concerns lends credibility to its ability to help control overeating.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with overeating.

  • Roots: Rich in fiber, the roots of the marshmallow plant help manage overeating by promoting feelings of fullness.
  • Leaves: High in organic compounds, marshmallow leaves soothe the stomach and decrease excessive hunger.
  • Flowers: Packed with antioxidants, marshmallow flowers help reduce inflammation in the digestive system, aiding in appetite control.
  • Seeds: Abundant in mucilage, marshmallow seeds create a protective coating in the stomach, suppressing excessive appetite.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for overeating.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root, then bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root pieces.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Serve the tea warm and enjoy.
