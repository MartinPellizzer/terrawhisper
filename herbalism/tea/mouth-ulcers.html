1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with mouth ulcers because it has anti-inflammatory and healing properties that can reduce pain and inflammation, while promoting tissue regeneration.

The chamomile plant contains essential oils like bisabolol and matricin, which have been found to accelerate the healing process of mouth ulcers. Moreover, its antimicrobial properties can help in preventing infections in the ulcerated area, thereby speeding up recovery. Additionally, chamomile's soothing effects can provide relief from the discomfort caused by mouth ulcers, making it easier for individuals to eat, drink, and maintain their oral hygiene.

Drinking chamomile tea as a warm compress can also increase blood flow to the affected area, facilitating the healing process.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with mouth ulcers.

  1. Apigenin: A flavonoid that has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, helping to reduce pain and fight infection in mouth ulcers.
  2. Luteolin: A flavonoid with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in tissue repair and alleviating discomfort in mouth ulcers.
  3. Chamazulene: A aromatic compound formed during steam distillation, having anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, helping to soothe irritation and inflammation in mouth ulcers.
  4. Bisabolol: A sesquiterpene alcohol known for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and healing properties, beneficial for promoting tissue regeneration and relieving pain in mouth ulcers.

The following list reveals what parts of chamomile are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for mouth ulcers.

  1. Flowers: The chamomile flowers contain anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce pain and inflammation in mouth ulcers.
  2. Leaves: The leaves of chamomile have antimicrobial properties that can help in preventing infections in mouth ulcers.
  3. Seeds: The chamomile seeds are rich in flavonoids that can help in reducing the size and discomfort caused by mouth ulcers.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for mouth ulcers.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and add 1 heaped teaspoon (2.5 grams) of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes to extract the chamomile's properties.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the chamomile flowers from the liquid.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a warm temperature, but not hot enough to cause discomfort to mouth ulcers.
  5. Sip the chamomile tea slowly, swishing it gently around the mouth before swallowing. Repeat as needed throughout the day.
