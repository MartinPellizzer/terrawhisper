10 best herbal teas for mouth ulcers

When it comes to herbal teas that may help with mouth ulcers, chamomile and calendula teas are often recommended due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. Chamomile tea can help reduce inflammation and promote healing, while calendula tea can help soothe irritated mouth tissue. Licorice root tea is another option, as it can form a protective layer over the ulcer, helping to reduce pain and further irritation.

The tea's antiseptic qualities, derived from its primary constituents like bisabolol, chase away microbial agents that can exacerbate ulcer-related infections. Furthermore, chamomile's soothing effects, attributed to its therapeutic compounds, provide relief on the mucous membranes of the oral cavity. Additionally, the tea's ability to promote wound healing, facilitated by its rich supply of azulenes, assists in the faster recovery of mouth ulcers.

Ultimately, drinking chamomile tea offers a holistic approach to treating mouth ulcers, addressing inflammation, infection, pain, and healing simultaneously.
