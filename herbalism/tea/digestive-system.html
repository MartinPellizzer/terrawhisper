10 best herbal teas for digestive system

Herbal teas can be a soothing and natural way to support a healthy digestive system.

Some of the best herbal teas for digestion include chamomile, ginger, peppermint, and fennel. Chamomile is known for its calming properties and can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract.

Ginger tea is well-known for its ability to alleviate symptoms of nausea and vomiting, while peppermint tea has been shown to help relax the gut and reduce bloating. Fennel tea is often used as a natural remedy for gas, bloating, and indigestion.

Drinking these herbal teas either before or after meals can provide relief and promote overall digestive health.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids digestion by stimulating the release of digestive enzymes, which speeds up the digestion process.

This herbal tea contains menthol, a compound known to relax the gastrointestinal muscles, thereby alleviating symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome such as bloating, gas, and abdominal pain. Furthermore, peppermint tea inhibits the growth of harmful bacteria in the gut, promoting a balanced gut microbiome. Its anti-inflammatory properties also soothe the digestive tract lining, providing relief from inflammation-induced discomfort.

Lastly, peppermint tea's carminative effect reduces the formation of gas in the gastrointestinal tract, preventing feelings of fullness and discomfort after meals.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with digestive system.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the peppermint plant are used in medicinal tea to soothe digestive discomfort due to their calming and antispasmodic properties.
  • Seeds: Peppermint seeds are a source of essential oils and are used in medicinal tea to alleviate digestive issues such as bloating, gas and indigestion.
  • Stems: Peppermint stems contain menthol and other volatile oils, making them a popular addition to medicinal tea for their soothing and calming effects on the digestive system.
  • Flowers: Peppermint flowers add a delicate flavor to medicinal tea, and contain therapeutic compounds known to relieve symptoms of digestive distress, such as nausea and cramping.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for digestive system.

  1. Measure - Take 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves or 2 teaspoons of fresh peppermint leaves.
  2. Boil - Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for a minute.
  3. Steep - Pour the hot water over the peppermint leaves and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain - Strain the tea to remove the leaves.
  5. Serve - Drink the tea while it's still warm, preferably between meals for optimal digestive system benefits.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea aids digestion due to its rich gingerol and shogaol content, which stimulate the production of digestive enzymes in the stomach.

These active compounds help speed up stomach emptying, alleviating feelings of discomfort and bloating. Furthermore, ginger tea's carminative properties reduce intestinal gas, preventing unpleasant sensations and promoting smoother digestion. Additionally, ginger's antioxidative effects shield the digestive tract from inflammation and damage, enhancing overall gut health.

Lastly, ginger tea's soothing warmth encourages blood flow to the digestive organs, bolstering their functionality and efficiency in breaking down food.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with digestive system.

  • Rhizomes: The underground stem of ginger, known for its pungent properties, is used to make medicinal tea that aids in digestion by relieving bloating and spasms.
  • Roots: The fibrous, spindle-shaped part of ginger that grows underground contains active compounds that alleviate digestive discomfort and promote proper digestion.
  • Buds: The immature flowering shoots of ginger are rich in antioxidants that support healthy digestion and help reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract.
  • Seeds: The small, oval-shaped seeds of ginger contain volatile oils that have carminative properties, making them useful for reducing gas and cramps in the digestive system.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for digestive system.

  1. Peel and slice 1 inch of fresh ginger, then let it steep in 2 cups of boiling water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the ginger pieces from the water, leaving only the liquid.
  3. Add the juice of half a lemon and 1-2 teaspoons of honey to the ginger water, stirring until well combined.
  4. Warm the mixture on low heat for a few minutes, if desired.
  5. Pour the ginger tea into a mug and enjoy up to three times a day for best digestive benefits.

3. Chamomile

Chamomile tea aids the digestive system due to its antispasmodic properties, which relax the smooth muscles lining the digestive tract, alleviating discomfort and preventing bloating.

This herbal tea also contains tannins that have astringent qualities, helping to reduce inflammation in the stomach and intestines. Furthermore, chamomile tea's mild bitter taste stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, enhancing the breakdown of food in the stomach and improving nutrient absorption. Its carminative effects help to expel excess gas from the digestive tract, relieving symptoms of indigestion.

Additionally, chamomile tea's calming properties soothe the nervous system, reducing stress-induced digestive issues, and promoting overall gastrointestinal health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with digestive system.

  • Flowers: Chamomile flowers are rich in calming compounds that promote steady digestion and soothe the digestive tract.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain therapeutic oils known to stimulate the digestive system, aiding in the breakdown of food.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are a potent source of antispasmodic properties, providing relief for cramps and discomfort in the digestive system.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for digestive system.

  1. Measure - Boil 8 ounces of water and let cool for a minute or two, so it's not boiling hot when it hits the chamomile flowers.
  2. Prepare - Pour the hot water over 1-2 tablespoons of dried chamomile flowers in a tea infuser or reusable tea bag.
  3. Steep - Allow the chamomile to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the maximum amount of beneficial compounds.
  4. Strain - Remove the tea infuser or squeeze out the tea bag to strain out the chamomile flowers.
  5. Serve - Enjoy your homemade chamomile tea, preferably without added sweeteners or milk, for optimal digestive benefits.

4. Fennel

Fennel tea aids digestion due to its carminative properties, which move gas from the intestines to alleviate bloating and cramping.

This tea's hydrocholoric acid stimulates the production of digestive enzymes in the stomach, promoting efficient food breakdown. Fennel contains antispasmodic agents, which soothe the gastrointestinal tract, reducing discomfort and encouraging regularity. Furthermore, the rosmarinic acid in fennel tea possesses anti-inflammatory qualities, helping to decrease inflammation in the digestive system.

Ultimately, fennel tea's active components facilitate smooth muscle contractions, pushing food along the digestive tract and supporting overall digestive health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in fennel tea that help with digestive system.

  • Seeds: Fennel seeds are rich in oil that aids in digestion and alleviates symptoms like bloating, flatulence, and indigestion.
  • Stems: The hollow stems of fennel contain numerous compounds that promote the production of digestive enzymes, easing digestion.
  • Leaves: Fennel leaves possess volatile oils and antioxidants that soothe the stomach, reduce inflammation, and improve overall digestion.
  • Flowers: Fennel flowers contain anti-spasmodic agents that relax the gastrointestinal tract, making them ideal for easing digestive discomfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fennel tea for digestive system.

  1. Chop one tablespoon of fresh fennel seeds or two teaspoons of crushed fennel seeds.
  2. Bring four cups of water to a boil, then add the chopped fennel seeds.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the fennel seeds.
  5. Serve the tea immediately, optionally adding honey or lemon to taste.

5. Licorice

Licorice tea aids digestion due to its active compound, glycyrrhizin, which soothes the stomach lining.

This compound stimulates the production of mucus, protecting the stomach from hydrochloric acid and reducing inflammation. Additionally, licorice tea encourages the production of digestive enzymes, enhancing nutrient absorption and overall digestive efficiency. Furthermore, this tea alleviates symptoms of indigestion, such as bloating, cramps, and heartburn, by speeding up the movement of food through the digestive tract.

Lastly, licorice tea's antispasmodic properties help relax the gut, promoting regular bowel movements and contributing to a healthy, balanced digestive system.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with digestive system.

  • Roots: Rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound with anti-inflammatory properties that aids in digestion.
  • Rhizomes: Contain various compounds like flavonoids and coumarins, known to stimulate the digestive system.
  • Leaves: High in antioxidants that help soothe the gastrointestinal tract and relieve digestive issues.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential oils that promote the production of digestive enzymes.
  • Bark: Contains various beneficial compounds like asperuloside and ursolic acid, known to alleviate digestive problems.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for digestive system.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of chopped licorice root with 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove licorice root pieces.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Drink 1 cup of licorice tea, 2-3 times a day, after meals for digestive system benefits.

6. Dandelion

Dandelion tea aids digestion due to its bitter properties that stimulate the production of digestive enzymes in the liver and bile in the gallbladder.

This tea also boasts mild laxative effects, promoting regular bowel movements and alleviating constipation. Furthermore, dandelion tea's rich supply of antioxidants helps maintain a healthy gut by protecting the gastrointestinal tract from oxidative damage. The tea's diuretic properties facilitate the removal of toxins from the body, reducing the stress on the digestive system.

Finally, dandelion tea's soothing effect on inflammation helps decrease inflammation in the digestive tract, allowing the system to function more efficiently.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in dandelion tea that help with digestive system.

  • Roots: Rich in beneficial compounds, the roots of dandelions promote digestion by supporting the production of digestive enzymes and bile.
  • Leaves: High in antioxidants, dandelion leaves aid the digestive system by stimulating appetite and facilitating proper nutrient absorption.
  • Flowers: Packed with anti-inflammatory properties, dandelion flowers soothe the digestive tract, alleviating symptoms of indigestion and acid reflux.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for digestive system.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh dandelion flowers, making sure they are free of pesticides and other contaminants.
  2. Rinse the flowers thoroughly under cold running water, then let them dry on a paper towel for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Coarsely chop the dried dandelion flowers and place them in a heatproof container or teapot.
  4. Pour 4 cups of boiling water over the chopped flowers and let them steep, covered, for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the flowers, and enjoy it while it's still warm, up to three times a day after meals.

7. Senna

Senna tea directly stimulates the intestines, promoting muscle contraction that moves waste through the digestive system.

This natural laxative effect, derived from the senna plant's active compounds, shortens the intestinal transit time. As a result, senna tea alleviates constipation and encourages regular bowel movements. Furthermore, this herbal tea's anthraquinones increase water and electrolyte secretion in the intestines, softening stools and reducing straining.

Ultimately, senna tea supports overall digestive health by maintaining a balanced and efficient digestive system.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in senna tea that help with digestive system.

  • Leaves: Rich in anthraquinones, which stimulate the digestive tract and alleviate constipation.
  • Pods: Contain chrysophanic acid, a potent anti-inflammatory agent that soothes the digestive lining and eases irritation.
  • Seeds: High in mucilage, which forms a protective gel-like coating on the digestive tract, relieving inflammation and promoting regularity.
  • Bark: Has tannins that have astringent properties, reducing inflammation in the digestive system and helping to heal irritations.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic senna tea for digestive system.

  1. Measure - Precisely measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried senna leaves or equivalent senna tea bag.
  2. Boil - Boil 8 ounces of water in a kettle or pot until it reaches a rolling boil.
  3. Steep - Steep the senna leaves or tea bag in the boiling water for 5-10 minutes, depending on desired strength.
  4. Strain - Strain the liquid to remove senna leaves, if necessary, and discard them.
  5. Serve - Serve the senna tea hot, or let it cool down and store in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.

8. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the marshmallow plant's roots, contains a substance called mucilage.

This mucilage turns into a gel-like consistency when mixed with water, providing a protective coating to the digestive tract. This coating soothes inflammation in the stomach and intestines, alleviating discomfort. Moreover, the mucilage aids in the elimination of toxins from the body, promoting overall digestive health.

Additionally, this herbal tea facilitates the production of mucus in the digestive tract, further lubricating and easing the digestive process.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with digestive system.

  • Leaves: Contain high amounts of fiber, helping maintain a healthy digestive system.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, they support gut health and aid in digestion.
  • Seeds: Packed with nutrients, they promote gut motility and soothe the digestive tract.
  • Roots: Full of medicinal compounds that soothe the stomach lining and reduce digestive discomforts.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for digestive system.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root, then bring to a simmer and let it steep for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root pieces.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  4. Warm up the tea again if needed.
  5. Pour the marshmallow tea into a cup and enjoy.

9. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides direct relief to the digestive system due to the demulcent properties of slippery elm bark.

This tea releases a substance that forms a slick film along the mucous membrane of the digestive tract, providing a protective barrier against excess acidity and irritants. As a result, slippery elm tea eases inflammation, soothes pain, and facilitates the healing of any lesions in the gastrointestinal system. Additionally, this tea stimulates the production of mucus, which encourages the normalization of bowel movements and alleviates symptoms of both diarrhea and constipation.

Slippery elm tea, therefore, brings immediate comfort and supports the overall health of the digestive system.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with digestive system.

  • Bark: The bark of the slippery elm tree is used in medicinal tea due to its mucilage content, which helps soothe the digestive tract.
  • Inner Bark: The inner bark contains large amounts of antioxidants and provides a demulcent effect, making it ideal for digestive system relief.
  • Leaves: Slippery elm leaves have been used in teas for their tannin content, which aids in reducing inflammation in the digestive system.
  • Seeds: Slippery elm seeds are rich in nutrients and fiber, supporting digestive health when used in tea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for digestive system.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark and place it in a tea infuser or loose leaf teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for 2-3 minutes to ensure it doesn't damage the slippery elm bark's therapeutic compounds.
  3. Pour the hot water over the slippery elm bark and let it steep for 5-10 minutes to allow the herb to release its beneficial properties.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to get rid of any loose bark.
  5. Drink the tea warm, up to three times a day, to soothe and support the digestive system.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic skullcap tea for digestive system.

  3. Warm - Gently re-warm the liquid if it has cooled down.
  4. Flavor - Add honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Serve - Drink the tea while warm, preferably after meals, for best digestive benefits.
