10 Best Herbal Teas For Digestive System

Herbal teas, also known as tisanes, can be beneficial for the digestive system due to the variety of plants and herbs used in their composition.

Many of these plants contain volatile oils and other compounds that have been found to help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, potentially leading to improved digestion. For instance, chamomile tea is known for its calming effects on the stomach, possibly reducing inflammation and relaxing the smooth muscles of the intestines.

Peppermint tea is another popular choice, as it may help relieve symptoms of indigestion, such as gas and bloating, by relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles. Ginger tea is well-known for its ability to alleviate nausea and vomiting, making it a potential remedy for digestive issues caused by motion sickness or pregnancy. Lastly, licorice root tea has been suggested to soothe the mucous membranes of the stomach and duodenum, potentially providing relief for minor discomforts of the digestive tract.

However, it is essential to remember that individual responses to herbal teas may vary, and it is always best to consult with a healthcare professional if you have any concerns or preexisting conditions that might interact negatively with these herbs.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with digestive system because Peppermint tea aids in digestion due to its carminative properties, which help to move gas through the digestive tract, relieving symptoms of bloating and discomfort.

The menthol present in peppermint tea is known to improve the flow of bile from the liver to the gallbladder, thereby speeding up the digestion process. Furthermore, peppermint tea has antispasmodic effects that relax the gastrointestinal muscles, reducing spasms and cramping in the stomach and intestines. Additionally, peppermint tea can help soothe the gastrointestinal lining, providing relief from inflammation and irritation.

Lastly, peppermint tea has been shown to have antibacterial and antiviral properties, which can help to eliminate harmful bacteria in the digestive tract, promoting overall digestive health.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for digestive system.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of crushed dried peppermint leaves with 1 cup of boiling water, then let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, pouring the liquid into a cup.
  3. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added soothing properties and flavor.
  4. Wait for the tea to cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to gently soothe and improve your digestive system.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with digestive system because Ginger tea aids in the digestive process because it contains phenolic compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, which stimulate the production of digestive enzymes and increase gastric motility, helping to move food through the gastrointestinal tract more smoothly.

Additionally, ginger's natural anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, providing relief from symptoms such as bloating, cramping, and pain. Ginger tea can also help alleviate nausea and vomiting, making it a popular remedy for managing digestive discomforts associated with conditions like motion sickness or morning sickness during pregnancy. Furthermore, ginger's carminative properties can help expel excess gas from the digestive tract, reducing feelings of bloating and discomfort.

Lastly, ginger tea has been shown to help maintain a healthy gut microbiome by promoting the growth of beneficial gut bacteria, which can improve overall digestive health.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for digestive system.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger root, then combine it with 4 cups of water in a saucepan; bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  2. Remove the saucepan from heat, add 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice to the hot ginger-water mixture, stirring until the honey is fully dissolved.
  3. Optional: Add a cinnamon stick or a few whole cloves for additional flavor and digestive benefits, letting it steep for a few minutes before removing.
  4. Strain the ginger tea into a teapot or a large mug, discarding the solids and retaining the aromatic, amber-colored liquid.
  5. Serve the ginger tea warm, sipping it slowly to fully appreciate its soothing warmth and spicy, tangy taste, and to allow it to gently support your digestive system.

3. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with digestive system because Chamomile tea helps with the digestive system because it contains volatile oils, flavonoids, and antioxidants that work together to support gastrointestinal health.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation, relax muscles, and alleviate symptoms of indigestion such as bloating, gas, and cramping. Chamomile tea has been shown to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, which help break down food and speed up the digestion process. Additionally, its soothing properties can help to reduce stress and anxiety, which can often exacerbate digestive issues.

Overall, chamomile tea offers a natural and gentle way to support a healthy digestive system.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for digestive system.

  1. To make a soothing cup of chamomile tea for your digestive system, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) to a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Allow the chamomile flowers to steep in the hot water for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve the desired strength of flavor and potency.
  3. While the chamomile is steeping, grate a small knob of fresh ginger (about the size of a thumbnail) for added digestive support and anti-inflammatory properties.
  4. After the chamomile has steeped, remove the tea infuser or tea bag and stir in the grated ginger, along with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice (about 1 tablespoon) for added vitamin C and immune support.
  5. Finally, sweeten the tea to taste with a natural sweetener like raw honey (about 1-2 teaspoons) and enjoy warm for a soothing and calming effect on the digestive system.

4. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with digestive system because Fennel tea aids in the digestive system because it contains volatile oils, such as anethole, fenchone, and estragole, which have antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe the digestive tract, relieve gas, and reduce bloating.

The tea's carminative effect assists in breaking down food in the stomach, making it easier to digest, particularly after consuming heavy meals. Furthermore, fennel tea can stimulate the production of gastric enzymes, promoting a healthier and more efficient digestion process. Additionally, it may help alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders like irritable bowel syndrome and dyspepsia by relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles and reducing inflammation.

Lastly, fennel tea's mild laxative effect can help regulate bowel movements and maintain a healthy digestive system.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel tea for digestive system.

  1. Thoroughly rinse and roughly chop 2 tablespoons of fresh fennel seeds, then place them in a teapot or saucepan and add 16 ounces of boiling water; let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors and beneficial compounds to infuse.
  2. Strain the tea using a fine-mesh sieve or a tea strainer to remove the solid fennel seeds, pouring the resulting liquid into a cup or mug.
  3. Optional: For added sweetness and additional digestive benefits, add 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey, preferably manuka honey, to the fennel tea while it's still warm, stirring until fully dissolved.
  4. If desired, garnish the tea with a thin slice of lemon or a few sprigs of fresh mint leaves for an enhanced aroma and an extra layer of flavor.
  5. Sip the fennel tea slowly, allowing it to gently coat your throat and mouth, and appreciate the soothing warmth as it travels down to your stomach, aiding in digestion and providing relief from any discomfort.

5. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with digestive system because Licorice root tea aids in digestion due to its active compound, glycyrrhizin, which stimulates the production of mucin, a slippery substance that lubricates and protects the digestive tract.

This soothing effect can help reduce inflammation, prevent stomach ulcers, and alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) such as heartburn and acid reflux. Furthermore, licorice root tea has been shown to increase the mucus secretion in the gastrointestinal tract, which can help maintain the integrity of the gut lining and promote overall digestive health.

Additionally, it can help relieve constipation by increasing muscle contractions in the intestines, thereby facilitating bowel movements. Lastly, licorice root tea may also have antibacterial properties, potentially inhibiting the growth of harmful bacteria like H.

pylori, which is known to cause stomach ulcers and other digestive issues.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for digestive system.

  1. In a pot, add 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root, then bring the mixture to a boil.
  2. Once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. After simmering, strain the tea to remove the licorice root pieces.
  4. Pour the tea into a cup, then add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day, for best results in aiding digestion.

6. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with digestive system because Dandelion tea aids in the digestive system because it promotes the production of stomach acid and bile, which are essential for breaking down food in the stomach and intestines.

Its natural diuretic properties help in eliminating waste and toxins from the body, thereby improving overall digestion and reducing bloating. The tea is also rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that soothe the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and irritation. Furthermore, dandelion tea has been shown to alleviate constipation and maintain a healthy gut microbiome, which is crucial for proper nutrient absorption and immune function.

Lastly, its mild laxative effect can help regulate bowel movements, providing relief from various digestive discomforts.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion tea for digestive system.

  1. Gather 2-3 teaspoons of freshly picked dandelion flowers, ensuring they are free of pesticides and other contaminants; rinse the flowers gently.
  2. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot, then add the dandelion flowers and let them steep for about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the flowers, then optionally add some honey or lemon juice for taste.
  4. Warm up the tea if it has cooled down, and consume it at a comfortable temperature.
  5. For optimal digestive system benefits, drink this dandelion tea twice a day, preferably after meals.

7. Senna

Senna tea helps with digestive system because Senna tea helps with the digestive system because it contains natural compounds called anthraquinones, which stimulate the colon and promote bowel movements.

This herbal tea has been used for centuries as a natural laxative to treat constipation, cleanse the colon, and improve overall digestive health. Senna tea works by irritating the lining of the intestines, which triggers contractions and helps move waste through the digestive tract more quickly. Additionally, it can help alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and may even aid in weight loss by promoting regularity.

However, it is important to use senna tea in moderation, as overuse can lead to dependence and potential electrolyte imbalances.

The following procedure explaing how to make senna tea for digestive system.

  1. In a tea infuser or heat-resistant strainer, place one senna tea bag or 1-2 teaspoons of dried senna leaves (approximately 2 grams).
  2. Boil 1 cup (8 ounces) of water and let it cool down for about 2-3 minutes before pouring it over the senna tea bag or leaves in a teapot or cup.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10 minutes to ensure extraction of beneficial compounds, such as anthraquinones, from the senna leaves.
  4. Remove the tea bag or strain the leaves from the liquid, discarding the used plant material.
  5. Optional: For added flavor and digestive benefits, consider adding a small slice of fresh ginger, a cinnamon stick, or a squeeze of lemon to your senna tea before consuming.

8. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with digestive system because Marshmallow root tea helps with the digestive system because it contains mucilage, a gel-like substance that coats the stomach and intestines, protecting them from irritation and inflammation.

This soothing effect can help to reduce symptoms of digestive disorders such as heartburn, indigestion, and inflammatory bowel disease. Additionally, marshmallow root tea has been shown to have antibacterial properties, which can help to eliminate harmful bacteria in the gut that can cause digestive issues. Furthermore, it can help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, promoting regular bowel movements and alleviating constipation.

Lastly, marshmallow root tea can also help to increase the production of mucus in the digestive tract, which can help to lubricate the digestive system and promote the healthy movement of food through the digestive tract.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow root tea for digestive system.

  1. To make marshmallow root tea for the digestive system, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot, then add 2-3 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and let it simmer on low heat for 15-20 minutes.
  2. After simmering, turn off the heat and let the tea cool down for about 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root pieces, then pour the tea into a cup.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and potential additional digestive benefits.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day, for best results in soothing and supporting the digestive system.

9. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with digestive system because Slippery elm tea helps with the digestive system because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water, providing a protective coating to the digestive tract.

This soothing coating helps to alleviate irritation, inflammation, and discomfort in the stomach and intestines, making it beneficial for conditions like acid reflux, gastritis, and irritable bowel syndrome. Slippery elm tea also promotes the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, which further shields the digestive system from acid and other harmful substances. Additionally, it may help to regulate stomach acid levels, making it helpful for both acid reflux and excessive acid production.

Lastly, slippery elm tea can act as a gentle laxative, promoting regular bowel movements and alleviating constipation, while its anti-inflammatory properties can help soothe inflammation in the digestive tract, providing overall relief to the digestive system.

The following procedure explaing how to make slippery elm tea for digestive system.

  1. In a pot, boil 1 cup of water and add 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark; reduce heat to a simmer and let it steep, covered, for 10-15 minutes.
  2. After steeping, strain the tea to remove any solid particles and discard them.
  3. Pour the slippery elm tea into a mug, adding honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added soothing properties.
  4. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat the mouth and throat, and promote healing in the digestive system.
  5. Dispose of any leftover tea and residue properly, as slippery elm can be sticky and difficult to clean.

10. Skullcap

Skullcap tea helps with digestive system because Skullcap tea helps with the digestive system because it contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the gastrointestinal tract, reducing inflammation and promoting overall digestive health.

Additionally, it has been shown to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, aiding in the breakdown of food and nutrient absorption. Furthermore, skullcap tea can alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders such as gastritis, ulcers, and irritable bowel syndrome by relaxing the smooth muscles of the intestines, which can help to reduce cramping, bloating, and pain. Its calming effects on the nervous system can also help to manage stress-related digestive issues, as stress can often exacerbate symptoms of poor digestion.

Lastly, skullcap tea has been used traditionally as a remedy for nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, making it a valuable tool in managing various digestive complaints.

The following procedure explaing how to make skullcap tea for digestive system.

  1. To make skullcap tea for the digestive system, start by boiling 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle; once boiling, add 1-2 teaspoons of dried skullcap herb (depending on desired strength) and reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Allow the skullcap herb to simmer for 10-15 minutes, releasing its medicinal properties and creating a strong tea.
  3. After simmering, strain the tea into a cup using a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth to remove any plant material.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste, but avoid adding milk as it may interfere with skullcap's digestive benefits.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your digestive tract and soothe any discomfort or irritation. Enjoy up to three cups per day as needed for digestive support.
