10 Best Herbal Teas For Acid Reflux

Herbal teas, especially those made from chamomile, ginger, licorice root, and marshmallow root, can help alleviate acid reflux symptoms.

These herbs have natural anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach lining, soothing the discomfort caused by acid reflux. Additionally, some herbs, like ginger and licorice root, can help speed up the digestion process, preventing acid from building up in the stomach.

Furthermore, herbal teas can help increase the production of mucus in the stomach, which can protect the lining of the esophagus and stomach from acid. The warm temperature of the tea can also help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, improving overall digestion.

Lastly, herbal teas are caffeine-free and can be a healthier alternative to traditional teas or coffee, which can exacerbate acid reflux symptoms.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with acid reflux because of its natural anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The flavonoids present in chamomile, such as apigenin, act on the gastrointestinal tract to reduce inflammation and relax the muscles, thereby preventing or alleviating symptoms of acid reflux. Furthermore, chamomile tea can increase the production of mucus in the stomach, which acts as a protective barrier against stomach acids flowing back into the esophagus. Additionally, the calming effect of chamomile tea on the nervous system may help reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for acid reflux.

Overall, chamomile tea is a safe, natural remedy for acid rereflux that offers multiple benefits for those suffering from this condition.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for acid reflux.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on medium heat.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) to a tea infuser or a tea ball.
  3. Once the water reaches boiling point, remove the saucepan from heat and place the tea infuser or tea bag in the hot water.
  4. Allow the chamomile to steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you prefer your tea. For acid reflux, it is recommended to steep for at least 5 minutes to release the maximum amount of soothing compounds.
  5. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag, and let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature before consuming. Enjoy 2-3 cups of chamomile tea throughout the day for best results in relieving acid reflux symptoms.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with acid reflux because it contains active compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and relax the esophageal sphincter.

These properties help prevent the backward flow of stomach acid into the esophagus, relieving symptoms such as heartburn and regurgitation. Additionally, ginger tea has antioxidant effects that protect the lining of the digestive tract from damage caused by acid reflux. Furthermore, it stimulates the production of saliva and digestive juices in the stomach, enhancing the digestion process and reducing pressure on the esophageal sphincter.

Lastly, ginger tea is a natural remedy that has been used for centuries to treat various digestive disorders including acid reflux, making it a safe and effective option for those seeking relief from their symptoms without relying on medications.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for acid reflux.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root (about 20-30 grams), then place the slices in a pot.
  2. Add 4 cups of water to the pot and bring it to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the ginger to release its healing properties.
  4. Remove the pot from heat, strain the tea into a cup, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Optional: add a squeeze of lemon or a drizzle of honey for added flavor and potential acid reflux relief. Enjoy up to 3 cups a day as needed.

3. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with acid reflux because it contains glycyrrhizic acid, which naturally coats the esophagus and forms a barrier against stomach acid.

This protective effect reduces the frequency of heartburn and symptoms associated with acid reflux. Additionally, licorice root tea has anti-inflammatory properties, making it beneficial for those experiencing inflammation in the digestive tract due to chronic acid reflux. Furthermore, licorice root tea increases mucus production in the stomach lining, which can help protect against irritation caused by stomach acid.

Lastly, licorice root tea has been shown to stimulate the production of mucus in the small intestine, helping to neutralize stomach acid and prevent it from flowing back into the esophagus.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for acid reflux.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used licorice root.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your throat and digestive system. Enjoy up to three cups of licorice root tea per day, but avoid consuming it for extended periods or in large quantities, as excessive intake may lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and potassium depletion. Consult with a healthcare professional before consuming licorice root tea if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.

4. Fennel Seed

Fennel seed tea helps with acid reflux because it contains compounds such as anethole, which has antispasmodic properties that can relax the muscles of the esophagus and prevent the backflow of stomach acid.

Additionally, fennel seeds are rich in fiber, which can help promote healthy digestion and reduce bloating, further easing the symptoms of acid reflux. Furthermore, the tea acts as a natural carminative, helping to expel intestinal gas and alleviate pressure on the abdomen that can contribute to acid reflux. Moreover, fennel seed tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe inflammation in the esophagus and reduce irritation caused by stomach acid.

Lastly, the calming effects of fennel seed tea on the digestive system make it a great natural remedy for those suffering from the discomfort and pain of acid reflux.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel seed tea for acid reflux.

  1. Boil 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds in 1 cup of water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the seeds, leaving only the fennel tea.
  3. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  4. Drink the fennel tea slowly, sipping it gently to ensure proper digestion.
  5. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day, especially after meals, for best results.

5. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with acid reflux because of its anti-inflammatory and mucilaginous properties.

It forms a protective coating over the esophagus, reducing irritation and inflammation caused by stomach acid backing up into the throat. The tea's soothing effects help neutralize acid and ease discomfort, making it an effective natural remedy for those suffering from frequent heartburn or acid reflux symptoms.

Furthermore, marshmallow root contains antioxidants that can help repair damage to the esophagus caused by prolonged exposure to stomach acid.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow root tea for acid reflux.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and 1 cup of water.
  2. Place marshmallow root in a pot and pour the water over it.
  3. Slowly bring the mixture to a simmer over medium-low heat, then reduce heat to low and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root, then let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of marshmallow root tea daily, before or between meals, to help soothe acid reflux symptoms.

6. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with acid reflux because it forms a protective coating over the esophagus, reducing irritation from stomach acids.

The bark of the slippery elm tree contains mucilage, which is a thick, slippery substance that acts as a barrier between the acid and the esophagus. This natural remedy also has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe any inflammation in the esophagus caused by acid reflux. Furthermore, slippery elm tea is caffeine-free and does not contain theophylline or other stimulants that can worsen acid reflux symptoms.

Lastly, it has been used as a traditional remedy for gastrointestinal issues, including acid reflux, for centuries.

The following procedure explaing how to make slippery elm tea for acid reflux.

  1. Gather your ingredients: 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark and 8-12 ounces of water.
  2. Heat the water in a kettle or pot until it reaches a boiling point.
  3. Add the dried slippery elm bark to a tea infuser or tea bag and place it in a heat-resistant container.
  4. Pour the boiling water over the slippery elm bark and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag, let it cool down to a drinkable temperature, and enjoy your soothing slippery elm tea for acid reflux.

7. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with acid reflux because it contains curcumin, a potent anti-inflammatory compound that soothes the esophagus and reduces inflammation in the digestive tract.

Its antioxidant properties help neutralize stomach acid, which can irritate the lining of the esophagus and cause heartburn. Additionally, turmeric tea may improve the lower esophageal sphincter's function, preventing stomach acid from flowing back into the esophagus.

Furthermore, ginger, a common ingredient in turmeric tea, has been shown to alleviate symptoms of acid reflux by relaxing the digestive muscles and increasing bile production, which can aid in digestion and reduce pressure on the lower esophageal sphincter.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric tea for acid reflux.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of ground turmeric and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or heatproof container.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Pour the tea into a cup and enjoy 1-2 times a day, between meals, for acid reflux relief.

8. Gingko Biloba

Gingko biloba tea helps with acid reflux because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the esophagus, which is a common cause of acid reflux symptoms.

Additionally, it contains flavonoids and antioxidants that help protect the lining of the stomach from damage caused by stomach acid. Gingko biloba also improves blood flow to the digestive system, which can enhance digestion and reduce pressure on the lower esophageal sphincter, helping prevent acid from backing up into the esophagus. Furthermore, some studies suggest that gingko biloba may have a relaxing effect on the muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, making it easier for food to move through the digestive system and reducing the likelihood of acid reflux occurring.

Overall, drinking gingko biloba tea can provide relief from acid reflux symptoms by reducing inflammation, protecting the stomach lining, improving digestion, and relaxing gastrointestinal muscles.

The following procedure explaing how to make gingko biloba tea for acid reflux.

  1. Boil 1 quart (32 ounces) of water in a pot and add 1-2 tablespoons (about 2-4 grams) of dried Ginkgo Biloba leaves.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to extract the beneficial compounds from the leaves.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent leaves, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Drink the Ginkgo Biloba tea slowly, sipping it gently, to prevent any potential irritation to the esophagus.
  5. Repeat this process up to three times a day, between meals, for potential relief of acid reflux symptoms.

9. Angelica Root

Angelica root tea helps with acid reflux because it possesses several therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

These properties help to reduce inflammation in the esophagus and relax the muscles of the lower esophageal sphincter, preventing the backup of stomach acid into the esophagus. Additionally, angelica root tea contains compounds that stimulate the production of mucus in the digestive tract, which can help to neutralize stomach acid and protect the esophagus from damage.

The following procedure explaing how to make angelica root tea for acid reflux.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan and add 1 teaspoon of dried angelica root.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to absorb the medicinal properties of the root.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the solid residue and pour it into a cup.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature, making sure it's not too hot to avoid irritating the esophagus.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of this tea per day, between meals, to help alleviate acid reflux symptoms.

10. Dandelion Root

Dandelion root tea helps with acid reflux because it naturally stimulates the production of digestive enzymes and bile in the stomach, improving the overall digestive process.

Its mildly bitter taste activates the production of hydrochloric acid, which aids in breaking down food and preventing acid backflow into the esophagus. Additionally, dandelion root contains antioxidants that help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, further alleviating symptoms of acid reflux.

Lastly, drinking this tea regularly can also increase the tone and strength of the lower esophageal sphincter muscle, preventing stomach acid from escaping into the esophagus and causing discomfort.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion root tea for acid reflux.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried dandelion root and 1 cup of water.
  2. Coarsely grind or chop the dried dandelion root, if it is in whole root form.
  3. Place the ground or chopped dandelion root in a pot and pour the water over it.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, let it cool down to a drinkable temperature, and consume 2-3 times a day for acid reflux relief.
