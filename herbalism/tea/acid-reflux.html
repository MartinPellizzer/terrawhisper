10 Best Herbal Teas For Acid Reflux

Herbal teas, especially those made from chamomile, ginger, licorice root, and marshmallow root, can help alleviate acid reflux symptoms.

These herbs have natural anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the esophagus and stomach lining, soothing the discomfort caused by acid reflux. Additionally, some herbs, like ginger and licorice root, can help speed up the digestion process, preventing acid from building up in the stomach.

Furthermore, herbal teas can help increase the production of mucus in the stomach, which can protect the lining of the esophagus and stomach from acid. The warm temperature of the tea can also help stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, improving overall digestion.

Lastly, herbal teas are caffeine-free and can be a healthier alternative to traditional teas or coffee, which can exacerbate acid reflux symptoms.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with acid reflux because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach and esophagus, which can be caused by acid reflux.

The tea also has antispasmodic properties, which means it can help relax the muscles in the stomach and esophagus, thereby reducing the symptoms of acid reflux. Additionally, chamomile tea is known to increase the production of mucus in the stomach, which can protect the lining of the stomach and esophagus from acid. Furthermore, chamomile tea has been shown to have a calming effect on the body, which can help reduce stress and anxiety, common triggers of acid reflux.

Finally, chamomile tea is naturally caffeine-free, making it a gentle and soothing option for those experiencing acid reflux symptoms.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with acid reflux because it contains gingerol, a compound that helps speed up stomach emptying and reduces the risk of acid reflux.

Additionally, ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate related discomfort. Furthermore, ginger tea can help enhance digestion, increase digestive juices and enzymes, and neutralize stomach acids, thereby preventing acid reflux. Additionally, ginger tea can help improve overall gut health by promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria, which can help prevent digestive issues related to acid reflux.

Finally, ginger tea is a caffeine-free alternative to traditional teas and beverages, making it a suitable option for those who experience acid reflux symptoms after consuming caffeinated drinks.

3. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with acid reflux because it contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which can increase the mucus lining in the stomach, thereby protecting the stomach lining from acidic damage.

This action helps to prevent acid reflux and reduces inflammation. Moreover, licorice tea has been shown to help increase the gastric mucosal defense and sustain the mucous barrier, which further reduces the risk of acid reflux.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and potassium depletion, so it is generally recommended to consume deglycyrrhizinated licorice (DGL) tea instead. DGL tea provides the benefits of licorice tea without the potential side effects of glycyrrhizin.

Consulting with a healthcare provider is always recommended before starting any new treatment.

4. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with acid reflux because it contains a volatile oil called anethole, which has antispasmodic properties that help relax the gastrointestinal tract, reducing symptoms of acid reflux.

This tea also stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, helping the body to break down food more efficiently and reducing the likelihood of acid reflux. Additionally, fennel tea has been found to reduce gastric inflammation, which can exacerbate acid reflux symptoms. The tea's ability to increase gastric motility also aids in the faster passage of food from the stomach to the intestines, further preventing the backflow of acid into the esophagus.

Furthermore, fennel's soothing effect on the stomach lining can provide relief from the discomfort and pain associated with acid reflux.

5. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea helps with acid reflux because it contains a substance called mucilage, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe and protect the irritated esophageal lining.

This mucilage can create a barrier over the mucous membranes, providing a protective coating that can help reduce discomfort and irritation caused by acid reflux. Additionally, marshmallow tea has been shown to have a demulcent effect, which can help reduce inflammation and irritation in the digestive tract. The tea can also help increase the mucus production in the gastrointestinal tract, which can help lubricate and protect the esophagus from acid.

Marshmallow tea is a natural and caffeine-free option that can provide relief from acid reflux symptoms, as well as offering potential long-term benefits for overall digestive health.

6. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with acid reflux because it contains a substance called mucilage, which becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water.

This gel-like substance can coat and soothe the lining of the esophagus, providing relief from the irritation caused by acid reflux. Moreover, the mucilage can also protect the esophagus from future acid exposure by creating a physical barrier.

Slippery elm tea may also stimulate the production of more mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, further shielding the esophagus and stomach from acid. Additionally, slippery elm tea is a natural remedy that does not appear to cause harm or interact negatively with other medications or conditions, making it a potentially safer alternative to traditional acid reflux treatments.

However, it is always advisable to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment regimen.

7. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with acid reflux because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the esophagus, resulting from acid reflux.

The main active ingredient in turmeric, curcumin, stimulates the production of bile, which aids in digestion and may help prevent acid reflux. Moreover, turmeric tea can help strengthen the lower esophageal sphincter, a muscle that, when weakened, allows stomach acid to flow back into the esophagus. Furthermore, turmeric's antioxidant properties can help repair the damage caused by acid reflux, providing relief from symptoms.

Lastly, turmeric tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to traditional teas, making it a healthier option for those looking to alleviate acid reflux symptoms.

8. Gingko Biloba

Gingko biloba tea helps with acid reflux because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the stomach lining, thereby alleviating the symptoms of acid reflux.

Additionally, this herbal tea has been shown to improve digestion and gut motility, which can help prevent the backflow of stomach acid into the esophagus. Gingko biloba is also known for its ability to enhance circulation, including blood flow to the digestive system, which can further support healthy digestion and reduce the occurrence of acid reflux. Furthermore, this tea contains antioxidants that can protect the stomach and esophagus from damage caused by acid reflux, and its calming effects may help reduce stress and anxiety, which can trigger acid reflux symptoms.

Therefore, incorporating gingko biloba tea into your daily routine can provide a natural and effective way to manage and prevent acid reflux.

9. Angelica

Angelica tea helps with acid reflux because it contains compounds that can stimulate the production of digestive juices, helping the stomach to digest food more efficiently and reducing the likelihood of acid backing up into the esophagus.

Additionally, angelica tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe inflammation in the esophagus and stomach lining, reducing irritation and discomfort caused by acid reflux. Furthermore, the tea has been shown to help relax the muscles in the gastrointestinal tract, which can prevent the tightening of the esophageal sphincter, a common cause of acid reflux. Angelica tea is also a natural diuretic, helping the body to eliminate excess fluid and reducing the pressure on the stomach, further preventing acid reflux.

Finally, angelica tea has been used in traditional medicine for centuries as a remedy for digestive issues, including acid reflux, and its effectiveness has been supported by some modern research studies.

10. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with acid reflux because it aids in digestion, reducing the risk of acid reflux.

The tea contains natural enzymes that support the breakdown of food in the stomach, thereby preventing the occurrence of acid reflux. Additionally, dandelion tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the digestive lining, reducing inflammation and discomfort caused by acid reflux. Furthermore, it acts as a natural diuretic, helping the body eliminate excess acid and fluids, which can alleviate symptoms of acid reflux.

Lastly, dandelion tea is known to stimulate the production of bile in the liver, promoting proper digestion and absorption of nutrients, thus minimizing the likelihood of acid reflux.
