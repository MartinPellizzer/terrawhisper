10 best herbal teas for peptic ulcers

Certain herbal teas can provide relief for individuals suffering from peptic ulcers due to their anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

Slippery elm tea is known for its demulcent qualities, which can help protect the stomach lining and promote healing. Marshmallow root tea has a similar effect, soothing and coating the digestive tract.

Chamomile tea is another excellent choice, as it contains anti-inflammatory compounds that can help reduce inflammation in the stomach and ease pain. Licorice root tea can also be beneficial, as it may increase the mucus lining of the stomach, offering protection against stomach acid.

However, it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating these herbal teas into a treatment plan, as they can interact with certain medications and medical conditions.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that directly address the underlying causes of peptic ulcers, such as irritation, oxidative stress, and erosion of the stomach lining.

Specifically, its bioactive compounds, like apigenin and luteolin, inhibit the enzyme responsible for damaging the mucosal lining. Additionally, its soothing effects on the gastrointestinal tract promote the production of protective mucus, further contributing to the healing process.

Consequently, regularly consuming chamomile tea provides consistent relief and supports stomach health, helping prevent the recurrence of peptic ulcers.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with peptic ulcers.

  • Flowers: The chamomile flowers contain flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to reduce inflammation and damage in the stomach lining caused by peptic ulcers.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain tannins that have astringent properties, helping to protect and soothe the stomach lining affected by peptic ulcers.
  • Seeds: The chamomile seeds are rich in essential oils, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, that possess anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, aiding in the healing process of peptic ulcers.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for peptic ulcers.

  1. Measure - Pour 1 cup of boiling water over 1 heaped teaspoon (2.5 grams) of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Steep - Let the chamomile flowers steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain - Strain the chamomile flowers from the tea.
  4. Sweeten (optional) - Add honey or sugar to taste.
  5. Serve - Drink the chamomile tea while warm, up to 3 times a day for peptic ulcer relief.

2. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the Althaea officinalis plant, contains mucilage that creates a protective layer in the digestive tract.

This protective layer soothes and relieves irritation in the stomach lining, reducing exposure to digestive acids. The mucilage in marshmallow tea also exhibits antibacterial properties, eliminating the H. pylori bacteria responsible for many peptic ulcers. Moreover, it promotes mucosal regeneration, accelerating the healing process of the damaged tissue.

Ultimately, marshmallow tea offers a natural, effective method for treating peptic ulcers, alleviating symptoms, and fostering recovery.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with peptic ulcers.

  • Roots: Rich in medicinal compounds, the roots of marshmallow plant produce a soothing tea that helps heal peptic ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Leaves: High in mucilage, marshmallow leaves create a demulcent tea that coats and protects the stomach lining, easing pain and promoting ulcer healing.
  • Flowers: Packed with antioxidants, marshmallow flowers contribute to a healing tea that fights free radicals, supporting the stomach's defense against peptic ulcers.
  • Seeds: Abundant in healing properties, marshmallow seeds produce a comforting tea that alleviates symptoms of peptic ulcers, thanks to their mucilage content.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for peptic ulcers.

  1. Combine 1 teaspoon of dried marshmallow root and 1 cup of water in a saucepan, allowing it to steep for 10-15 minutes on medium heat.
  2. Strain the liquid to remove the marshmallow root, keeping the golden-brown liquid for consumption.
  3. Pour the marshmallow root tea into a cup, optionally adding honey or lemon to taste.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature, ensuring it's not too hot for the stomach lining.
  5. Sip the marshmallow root tea slowly, consuming 2-3 times a day for soothing relief of peptic ulcers.

3. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides relief for peptic ulcers due to the demulcent properties of its main component, slippery elm bark.

This bark contains mucilage, a substance that turns into a slick gel when mixed with water. This gel creates a protective barrier over the mucous membrane of the digestive tract, reducing irritation and inflammation. By coating the stomach lining, slippery elm tea lessens the pain associated with peptic ulcers and encourages the healing of damaged tissues.

Moreover, it has been historically used to alleviate gastrointestinal issues, including acid reflux, making it a popular natural remedy for ulcer treatment.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with peptic ulcers.

  • Bark: The inner bark of the slippery elm tree is used to make medicinal tea for peptic ulcers due to its demulcent properties, which means it has a soothing and healing effect on the mucous membranes of the digestive tract.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the slippery elm plant are used in teas for peptic ulcers because they contain mucilage, a substance that coats and protects the stomach lining.
  • Seeds: Slippery elm seeds are used in teas for peptic ulcers because they contain large amounts of mucilage, which can help reduce inflammation and promote healing.
  • Roots: While not as commonly used as the bark, leaves, and seeds, the roots of the slippery elm plant can also be used in teas for peptic ulcers due to their demulcent and emollient properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for peptic ulcers.

  1. Measure - Using a measuring spoon, measure out 1 teaspoon of dried slippery elm bark.
  2. Steep - Place the measured bark into a tea infuser or loose leaf tea bag, then steep in boiling water for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Stir - After steeping, stir the tea to ensure all the slippery elm particles are well mixed.
  4. Strain - Strain the tea through a fine mesh strainer into a cup to remove any remaining bark particles.
  5. Drink - Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day between meals to soothe peptic ulcers.

Licorice tea aids peptic ulcers due to its ability to increase the mucus lining of the stomach, which protects it from acid and digestive enzymes.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with peptic ulcers.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the licorice plant contain various compounds, such as triterpenoid saponins, that have been shown to have anti-ulcer effects, making them useful for treating peptic ulcers.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the licorice plant contain high levels of flavonoids and coumarins, which have been shown to reduce gastric acid secretion and have anti-ulcer effects, making them a valuable addition to medicinal tea for treating peptic ulcers.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for peptic ulcers.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of chopped licorice root with 4 cups of water, then bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the licorice pieces.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 3 cups a day, between meals, for up to 2 weeks.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic meadowsweet tea for peptic ulcers.

  1. In a mortar, crush 2 tablespoons of dried meadowsweet flowers using a pestle to release their beneficial compounds.

  3. Pour 1 liter of boiling water over the infuser and let it steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal properties.
  4. Remove the infuser, discard the used flowers, and allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Consume 2-3 cups of meadowsweet tea per day, between meals, to help soothe and heal peptic ulcers.

10. Plantain

Plantain tea aids in treating peptic ulcers due to its demulcent properties, which involve the formation of a soothing film over the mucous membrane lining the stomach.

This film protects the lining from acid and digestive juices, reducing the risk of further damage. Moreover, plantain tea contains mucilage, a complex carbohydrate that swells when in contact with water, forming a barrier against harmful substances. Its anti-inflammatory and healing effects, attributed to the compound allantoin, further contribute to the alleviation of pain and inflammation associated with peptic ulcers.

Lastly, plantain tea contains tannins, which exhibit astringent properties, helping to decrease secretions and promote tissue repair, fostering the healing process of existing ulcers.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in plantain tea that help with peptic ulcers.

  • Roots: The roots of the plantain plant contain high amounts of iridoids, which have been found to protect the gastric mucosa from damage caused by acid and pepsin, thus helping to heal peptic ulcers.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the plantain plant are rich in mucilage, a gel-like substance that coats and protects the stomach lining, providing relief from ulcer symptoms.
  • Seeds: Plantain seeds are a demulcent, meaning they soothe irritated tissues and can help reduce inflammation caused by peptic ulcers.
  • Bark: The bark of the plantain plant contains tannins, which have astringent properties that can help shrink and tighten mucous membranes, offering protection to the stomach lining and promoting the healing of ulcers.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic plantain tea for peptic ulcers.

  1. Peel and chop 2 ripe plantains into small pieces, then rinse them under cold water.
  2. In a pot, combine the plantain chunks with 4 cups of water and bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let the plantains simmer for 20 minutes, or until the liquid is reduced by half.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the plantain pieces, then let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the plantain tea up to three times a day, ideally before meals, for best results in managing peptic ulcers.
