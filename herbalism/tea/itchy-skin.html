10 Best Herbal Teas For Itchy Skin

Herbal teas, especially chamomile and nettle tea, are known to have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce skin inflammation and irritation, thereby alleviating itchy skin.

These teas also contain antioxidants that help protect the skin from damage and support the skin's natural healing process. Moreover, they have been found to have antihistamine effects, which can help relieve allergic reactions that can cause itchy skin. Additionally, drinking herbal teas can help hydrate the body, including the skin, which can help reduce dryness and itchiness.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can provide relief for itchy skin, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially for severe or persistent itching.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with itchy skin because it has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe and heal irritated skin.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can reduce inflammation and swelling, providing relief from itchiness. Chamomile also contains flavonoids, which have been shown to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, further aiding in the reduction of skin irritation. Additionally, chamomile tea can help to moisturize the skin, preventing dryness which can exacerbate itchiness.

The soothing effects of chamomile tea can also help to alleviate stress and anxiety, which can sometimes contribute to skin irritation and itchiness.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for itchy skin.

  1. In a cup, pour boiling water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) and let it steep for 5-10 minutes to allow the active compounds to infuse and create a strong tea.
  2. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste, but avoid any additives that may irritate the skin further.
  3. After the tea has cooled down to a comfortable temperature, soak a clean washcloth in the tea and wring out excess liquid.
  4. Gently apply the chamomile-infused washcloth to the itchy skin for 5-10 minutes, allowing the anti-inflammatory and soothing properties of chamomile to alleviate the itch.
  5. Repeat the process as needed, up to 3 times a day, to help manage itchy skin and promote skin healing.

2. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with itchy skin because it has anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties that can reduce inflammation and histamine production, which often cause itchiness.

The tea is also rich in nutrients like vitamin C, iron, and chlorophyll, which can support skin health and promote skin cell regeneration. Furthermore, nettle tea can help detoxify the body by flushing out toxins and promoting kidney function, which can indirectly improve skin health and reduce itchiness. Additionally, nettle tea has been shown to have a positive effect on the immune system, which can help prevent or reduce allergic reactions that may cause itchy skin.

Lastly, drinking nettle tea can help hydrate the body, keeping the skin moisturized and reducing dryness, which can alleviate itchiness.

The following procedure explaing how to make nettle tea for itchy skin.

  1. To make nettle tea for itchy skin, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot and adding 2 teaspoons of dried nettle leaves; let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  2. After simmering, remove the pot from heat and let the tea cool down for about 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the used nettle leaves.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, 2-3 times a day, to help soothe itchy skin.

3. Calendula

Calendula tea helps with itchy skin because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation and irritation, thereby alleviating the itch.

The tea is made from calendula flowers, which contain flavonoids, antioxidants that protect cells from damage and can help soothe irritated skin. Furthermore, calendula tea has been found to have antibacterial and antifungal properties, which can help keep skin infections at bay, a common cause of itchy skin. The tea can be applied topically to the affected area, allowing the soothing properties to be absorbed directly into the skin.

Additionally, drinking calendula tea can help boost the immune system, which can help the body fight off any underlying infections that may be causing the itch.

The following procedure explaing how to make calendula tea for itchy skin.

  1. To make calendula tea for itchy skin, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a saucepan, then add 2 teaspoons of dried calendula flowers or 1 calendula tea bag.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 10-15 minutes, covered, to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve a golden-yellow color.
  3. In the meantime, clean the area of itchy skin and ensure it's dry before applying the tea.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the flowers or discard the tea bag, then let it cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Apply the cooled calendula tea to the affected area using a clean cloth or spray bottle, allowing it to air dry for best results. Repeat as needed for relief.

4. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with itchy skin because it contains menthol, a natural anti-inflammatory and analgesic compound that can soothe skin irritation and reduce itching.

The cool temperature of the tea can also provide instant relief to inflamed skin. Furthermore, peppermint tea has antimicrobial properties that can help prevent skin infections caused by constant scratching. Additionally, drinking peppermint tea can help hydrate the body, which is essential for maintaining healthy skin.

However, it is important to note that while peppermint tea can provide temporary relief, it should not replace medical treatment for severe or chronic skin conditions.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for itchy skin.

  1. To make peppermint tea for itchy skin, start by boiling 2 cups of water and adding 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves; let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. While waiting, soak a clean washcloth in cold water and wring it out, then place it in the refrigerator to chill.
  3. After steeping, strain the peppermint tea leaves and let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  4. When the tea is cool, soak the chilled washcloth in the tea and apply it to the itchy skin for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Repeat the application as needed, up to three times a day, to soothe and relieve itchy skin.

5. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with itchy skin because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation and soothe irritated skin.

The antioxidants present in ginger can help repair damaged skin cells and promote the growth of new ones, which can alleviate itching. Ginger tea also boosts blood circulation, allowing the skin to receive more nutrients and oxygen, which can help speed up the healing process of itchy skin. Furthermore, ginger has natural antihistamine properties that can help block the body's production of histamine, a compound that can cause allergic reactions and lead to itchy skin.

Lastly, ginger tea can help hydrate the skin, keeping it moisturized and reducing the likelihood of itchiness caused by dryness.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for itchy skin.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger root, then combine with 4 cups of water in a saucepan; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Remove saucepan from heat, add 1 teaspoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, stirring until honey is fully dissolved.
  3. Strain the ginger tea into a teapot or mug, pressing on the ginger slices to extract all liquid.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the ginger tea daily, as needed, to help alleviate itchy skin.

6. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with itchy skin because it contains anti-inflammatory and antipruritic properties that can alleviate the symptoms of itchy skin.

The isoflavones present in red clover tea have been shown to reduce inflammation and irritation, providing relief for dry, itchy skin. Additionally, red clover tea can improve circulation, promoting the delivery of nutrients and oxygen to the skin, which can help to soothe and heal irritated skin. Furthermore, red clover tea has antioxidant properties that can protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, reducing the likelihood of itchy skin.

Lastly, red clover tea has been used traditionally as a blood purifier, which can help to eliminate toxins from the body, reducing the occurrence of itchy skin.

The following procedure explaing how to make red clover tea for itchy skin.

  1. To make red clover tea for itchy skin, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers; let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. After simmering, strain the tea to remove the flowers and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  3. If you prefer, add a natural sweetener like honey to taste.
  4. Drink 2-3 cups of this tea daily to help soothe itchy skin.
  5. For external use, let the tea cool down completely and use it as a compress on the affected areas for 15-20 minutes, 2-3 times a day.

7. Burdock

Burdock tea helps with itchy skin because it has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can reduce inflammation and soothe irritated skin.

It contains essential vitamins and minerals, such as Vitamin A, Vitamin C, and silicon, which can improve skin health and alleviate itchiness. Burdock tea also has diuretic properties that can help eliminate toxins from the body, reducing the likelihood of skin irritations. Furthermore, it has antibacterial and antifungal properties that can prevent skin infections and promote the healing of damaged skin.

Lastly, burdock tea can improve blood circulation, allowing for better nutrient delivery to the skin, promoting skin cell regeneration and reducing itchiness.

The following procedure explaing how to make burdock tea for itchy skin.

  1. To make burdock tea for itchy skin, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot, then add 2 tablespoons of dried burdock root, reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the burdock root, then allow it to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  3. If you prefer a sweet taste, add a small amount of honey (to taste) and stir until fully dissolved.
  4. To apply the tea, soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply it as a compress to the itchy areas of your skin.
  5. Discard any remaining tea after use, and store the cooled tea in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours if you want to reuse it as a compress.

8. Yellow Dock

Yellow dock tea helps with itchy skin because it contains anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can reduce inflammation and irritation, soothing the skin.

The roots of the yellow dock plant contain high levels of iron, which can help improve blood flow and oxygenation to the skin, promoting overall skin health. Yellow dock tea also has natural cleansing and detoxifying effects, which can help eliminate toxins and impurities from the body that may be contributing to skin issues. Additionally, yellow dock tea may help balance the skin's pH levels, creating an unfavorable environment for harmful bacteria and fungi that can cause itchy skin.

Finally, yellow dock tea has been used traditionally as a blood purifier, which can help clear heat and toxins from the blood, further supporting skin health and reducing itchiness.

The following procedure explaing how to make yellow dock tea for itchy skin.

  1. To make yellow dock tea for itchy skin, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried yellow dock root; let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the solid particles and allow it to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  3. If desired, add a natural sweetener like honey to taste, but avoid artificial sweeteners.
  4. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, or as needed, to alleviate itchy skin.
  5. For external use, let the tea cool down completely and apply it to the affected areas using a clean cloth or spray bottle.

9. Sarsaparilla

Sarsaparilla tea helps with itchy skin because it contains saponins, which are natural compounds known to have anti-inflammatory and anti-pruritic properties, meaning they can help reduce inflammation and itchiness on the skin.

Additionally, sarsaparilla tea has been found to possess significant antioxidant effects, which can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and promote skin health. Furthermore, the tea is also believed to have mild diuretic properties, which can help eliminate toxins from the body that may contribute to skin issues. The anti-microbial properties of sarsaparilla tea may also help prevent skin infections that can cause itching.

Lastly, sarsaparilla tea has been used traditionally as a blood purifier, and by promoting blood circulation and cleansing, it can help improve overall skin health and alleviate itchy skin.

The following procedure explaing how to make sarsaparilla tea for itchy skin.

  1. In a pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil, then add 1 tablespoon of dried sarsaparilla root and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let the mixture steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the sarsaparilla root and let it cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of sarsaparilla tea daily, as needed, to help soothe itchy skin.

10. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with itchy skin because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation and swelling, which can alleviate the itchiness.

The tea is also rich in antioxidants that help to combat free radicals, promoting skin cell repair and reducing oxidative stress that can cause itchiness. Furthermore, dandelion tea has been shown to improve digestion and liver function, which can help eliminate toxins from the body, thus reducing the likelihood of skin irritations. Additionally, the tea contains vitamin C and A, which are essential for maintaining healthy skin, promoting collagen production, and reducing inflammation that can cause itchy skin.

Lastly, dandelion tea has been used traditionally as a diuretic, helping the body eliminate excess fluids and toxins through urination, which can help reduce skin itchiness and irritation.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion tea for itchy skin.

  1. To make dandelion tea for itchy skin, start by boiling 2 cups of water and adding 2 tablespoons of dried dandelion flowers or 4 tablespoons of fresh dandelion flowers; let it steep for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove any flower particles, then let it cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  3. Optionally, add a teaspoon of raw honey for added skin-soothing benefits and to improve the taste.
  4. Drink 1-2 cups of this tea daily, depending on the severity of your itchy skin.
  5. For best results, use fresh, organic dandelions from a pesticide-free area and drink the tea consistently for at least one week.
