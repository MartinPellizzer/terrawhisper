10 Best Herbal Teas For Acne

Herbal teas can help with acne due to their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can reduce redness and swelling in the skin.

Some herbal teas, like chamomile and green tea, contain compounds that may help regulate hormonal imbalances and decrease sebum production, both of which can contribute to acne. Additionally, certain herbal teas have antibacterial properties that can help eliminate acne-causing bacteria on the skin. Drinking herbal teas can also promote overall hydration, which is important for maintaining healthy skin and preventing acne.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can be a helpful addition to an acne treatment plan, they should not be relied on as a sole treatment and should be used in conjunction with other medical treatments and a healthy skincare routine.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with acne because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and swelling.

The tea contains bisabolol, an antioxidant that fights free radicals and soothes irritated skin. Drinking chamomile tea regularly can also improve gut health by reducing inflammation and promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria.

Acne is often linked to digestive issues, so improving gut health may help prevent breakouts. Additionally, the tea's calming effects on the body can help reduce stress levels, which can contribute to acne flare-ups.

By drinking chamomile tea and applying it topically as a cool compress, people with acne can enjoy the benefits of this natural remedy.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for acne.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove until it reaches a boil.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) to a tea infuser or directly into the pot.
  3. Allow the chamomile to steep in the hot water for 5-7 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag, and let the chamomile tea cool to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of chamomile tea daily, ideally without adding any sugar or sweeteners, to help reduce inflammation and improve acne.

2. Lavender

Lavender tea helps with acne because it has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling, making it an effective natural remedy for treating inflammatory acne.

The tea also contains antibacterial compounds, such as linalool and linalyl acetate, which help to kill the bacteria responsible for causing acne. Additionally, lavender tea's calming effects on the skin can help to soothe irritated acne-prone areas and prevent future breakouts by reducing stress and anxiety. Drinking lavender tea regularly can also improve overall skin health by promoting the production of collagen and reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

Regular consumption of this calming herbal beverage can lead to clearer, more radiant skin.

The following procedure explaing how to make lavender tea for acne.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried organic culinary lavender flowers to a tea infuser or teapot with a built-in strainer.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the lavender in the teapot or infuser and let steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent lavender flowers.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and drink while warm, up to 3 cups per day.

3. Calendula

Calendula tea helps with acne because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling associated with acne lesions.

The tea's antioxidants help fight free radicals, which can damage skin cells and contribute to acne formation. Additionally, calendula has antibacterial properties, making it effective in killing the bacteria responsible for causing acne. Regular consumption of calendula tea can also improve overall skin health by promoting collagen production and reducing inflammation throughout the body.

Applying cooled calendula tea to the skin as a compress or toner can provide additional benefits, helping to soothe and heal existing acne lesions while preventing new ones from forming.

The following procedure explaing how to make calendula tea for acne.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down until it's warm, but not hot (around 100°F to 120°F).
  2. Steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried calendula flowers in the warm water for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture to remove the calendula flowers, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of calendula tea daily, preferably after meals, to help reduce acne.

4. Green Tea

Green tea helps with acne because it contains bioactive compounds, including polyphenols and catechins, which can reduce inflammation and inhibit the growth of bacteria associated with acne.

Drinking green tea or applying it topically as a compress can help unclog pores and reduce oil production in the skin, preventing future breakouts. The antioxidants in green tea also help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to acne development. Additionally, some studies suggest that green tea may regulate insulin levels and improve insulin sensitivity, reducing the risk of hormonal acne caused by high blood sugar levels.

Overall, incorporating green tea into your daily routine through drinking or topical application can be a simple, natural way to help prevent and reduce the appearance of acne.

The following procedure explaing how to make green tea tea for acne.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water and let it cool down to 170-180°F (75-80°C), then steep 2 teaspoons of high-quality green tea leaves in the hot water for 2-3 minutes.
  2. While the tea is steeping, mix 1 teaspoon of raw honey with 1/2 teaspoon of lemon juice in a separate cup.
  3. After 2-3 minutes, remove the tea leaves and combine the green tea with the honey-lemon mixture in the same cup.
  4. Stir the mixture well until the honey is fully dissolved, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink the green tea with honey-lemon concoction twice a day, preferably after meals, for at least a month or until you see improvements in your acne.

5. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with acne because it contains isoflavones, which have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce the size and redness of pimples.

It also contains salicylic acid, a beta hydroxy acid that exfoliates dead skin cells and unclogs pores. The antioxidants in red clover tea neutralize free radicals that can damage skin cells and contribute to acne. Drinking red clover tea regularly can help balance hormones, which can also prevent acne breakouts.

Lastly, the phytoestrogens in red clover tea regulate oil production in the skin, preventing excess sebum buildup that can lead to acne.

The following procedure explaing how to make red clover tea for acne.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot and add 2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the nutrients from the red clover to be extracted into the water.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the red clover flowers, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of this red clover tea daily, before or between meals, for at least a month to see improvements in acne.

6. Burdock Root

Burdock root tea helps with acne because it has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can reduce the redness and swelling of pimples while eliminating acne-causing bacteria.

Its high concentration of antioxidants, such as phenolic acids and quercetin, helps protect the skin from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals, which can worsen acne. Burdock root tea also promotes blood circulation and detoxification, which can help remove toxins and impurities from the blood, reducing the occurrence of acne. Furthermore, it contains essential nutrients, including vitamin C, vitamin E, and calcium, which support skin health and collagen production, contributing to the overall improvement of acne-prone skin.

Lastly, burdock root tea has been used traditionally as a diuretic, helping the body eliminate excess fluids and waste, which can help reduce the severity of acne and improve the skin's appearance.

The following procedure explaing how to make burdock root tea for acne.

  1. Coarsely chop 1-2 tablespoons of dried burdock root into small pieces.
  2. In a saucepan, bring 2 cups of water to a boil, then add the chopped burdock root.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the water has taken on a brownish-red color.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the burdock root pieces.
  5. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature, then consume 1-2 times a day, as needed.

7. Dandelion Root

Dandelion root tea helps with acne because it possesses powerful antioxidants, including beta-carotene and polyphenols, which can reduce inflammation and neutralize free radicals that damage skin cells, contributing to the development of acne.

Additionally, dandelion root has diuretic properties, helping the body eliminate toxins through urination, potentially reducing the amount of toxins that cause hormonal imbalances linked to acne. Furthermore, it stimulates the production of bile and improves liver function, aiding in the elimination of toxins from the body and reducing the overall burden on the organ systems that can lead to acne outbreaks.

Lastly, the tea's anti-inflammatory properties help reduce redness and swelling associated with acne, providing both internal and external benefits for those struggling with this common skin condition.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion root tea for acne.

  1. Gather 2-3 teaspoons of dried dandelion root and rinse it under cold water to remove any impurities.
  2. Place the cleaned dandelion root in a pot and add 1 cup of fresh, cold water.
  3. Slowly bring the water to a boil over medium heat, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent dandelion root, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of this dandelion root tea daily, preferably between meals, to help reduce acne and improve skin health.

8. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with acne because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that reduce redness and swelling, as well as antioxidants that combat free radicals which can contribute to acne formation.

Additionally, ginger tea helps to improve digestion, as poor digestion has been linked to acne breakouts. Consuming the tea regularly can also help to balance hormones, which is a common cause of acne in many individuals.

Lastly, the warmth of the tea can provide soothing relief for irritated skin and help to unclog pores, making it an effective natural remedy for those suffering from acne.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for acne.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger, then place it in a pot with 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat, add 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, stirring until fully combined.
  4. Strain the ginger pieces from the liquid, then pour the tea into a mug.
  5. Sip and enjoy the warm ginger tea up to three times a day for best results.

9. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with acne because it contains curcumin, an active ingredient with potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can reduce redness, swelling, and pain associated with acne.

Additionally, curcumin's anti-inflammatory effects can help prevent future breakouts by regulating sebum production and inhibiting the growth of acne-causing bacteria. Furthermore, turmeric tea has antioxidants that can help repair skin damage caused by acne and improve overall skin health. Drinking turmeric tea regularly may also boost the immune system, which can help the body fight off infections and inflammation, including those that contribute to acne.

However, it's important to note that while turmeric tea can be a helpful addition to an acne treatment plan, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment or a skincare routine prescribed by a dermatologist.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric tea for acne.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper (to enhance turmeric's absorption) into the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, and add honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to 2 times a day for best results.

10. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with acne because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce redness and swelling.

It contains mucilage, a soothing substance that protects and heals the skin. Marshmallow root also has antibacterial properties, making it effective in fighting acne-causing bacteria. Additionally, marshmallow root tea can help maintain skin hydration and improve overall complexion by providing essential nutrients to the skin.

Regular consumption of this herbal tea may help reduce the severity and frequency of acne breakouts.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow root tea for acne.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water in a pot, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 10-15 minutes, covered, to extract the beneficial compounds from the marshmallow root.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root pieces, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Optionally, add a squeeze of lemon or a small amount of raw honey for taste, but avoid adding sugar which can aggravate acne.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of marshmallow root tea daily, preferably between meals, to help soothe inflammation and support skin health.
