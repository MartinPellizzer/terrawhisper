10 best herbal teas for hangover

If you're looking for a natural way to ease the symptoms of a hangover, certain herbal teas can be very helpful.

Peppermint tea is one option, as it can settle an upset stomach and help with nausea. Ginger tea is another good choice, as it can also soothe the stomach and reduce inflammation.

Chamomile tea is known for its calming properties and can help promote relaxation and better sleep, which can be helpful after a night of drinking. Finally, licorice root tea can help replenish the body's potassium levels, which can be depleted after drinking alcohol.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can help alleviate some hangover symptoms, they are not a substitute for staying hydrated and getting rest.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea's calming properties, recognized by historical medical practices such as Greco-Roman and Egyptian medicine, help alleviate hangover symptoms.

This herbal remedy contains the antioxidant apigenin, which reduces oxidative stress from alcohol metabolism. Additionally, its mild sedative effects help improve sleep quality, a crucial factor in recovering from alcohol consumption. Chamomile's soothing properties ease digestive discomfort, a common hangover symptom.

Thus, chamomile tea unequivocally aids in alleviating hangover symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with hangover.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers contain calming properties that help soothe an upset stomach and promote relaxation, which can alleviate hangover symptoms.
  • Leaves: The bitter leaves of chamomile help stimulate the digestive system and relieve bloating, making them useful for hangover relief.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in antioxidants that help protect the liver from damage caused by alcohol, which can help reduce hangover severity.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids in hangover relief due to its established abilities to soothe the stomach and digestive tract, as confirmed by scientific research.

Its organic compounds, specifically menthol and methyl salicylate, possess potent anti-inflammatory properties that alleviate nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Furthermore, peppermint tea hydrates the body, counteracting the dehydrating effects of alcohol consumption. Additionally, it facilitates the breakdown and elimination of toxins, such as acetaldehyde, which accumulate during alcohol metabolism.

Consequently, these combined actions make peppermint tea an effective natural remedy for hangover relief.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with hangover.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, peppermint leaves are known for their ability to soothe digestion and alleviate headaches.
  • Flowers: High in antioxidants, peppermint flowers aid in detoxification and support the liver's function in hangover recovery.
  • Stems: Containing essential oils, peppermint stems help settle the stomach and reduce nausea associated with hangovers.
  • Seeds: Packed with nutrients, peppermint seeds provide hydration and electrolyte balance, crucial for hangover relief.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea provides relief from hangover through its active components, such as gingerols and shogaols, which ease nausea and vomiting.

This soothing effect on the stomach results from ginger's ability to neutralize acid and reduce inflammation. Additionally, ginger tea's mild diuretic effect helps the body eliminate toxins and rehydrate, alleviating hangover symptoms.

Lastly, ginger tea restores lost electrolytes and vitamins, promoting the body's overall recovery from alcohol's diuretic actions.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with hangover.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are a popular choice for making medicinal tea to treat hangovers due to their potent anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe upset stomachs.
  • Roots: Ginger roots are rich in gingerols and shaogals, compounds known to alleviate nausea and vomiting, making them an ideal ingredient for hangover tea.
  • Leaves: Ginger leaves contain high levels of antioxidants that help detoxify the liver and reduce the severity of hangover symptoms.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers are known for their calming effects on the digestive system and are often used in hangover tea to provide relief from abdominal discomfort.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds are a powerful stimulant that promote blood circulation, helping to speed up the metabolism of alcohol and reduce the severity of hangover symptoms.

7. Licorice

Additionally, licorice tea supports the liver's function by promoting the release of bile, an essential substance for breaking down and eliminating toxins.

As a result, licorice tea effectively combats fatigue, reduces inflammation, and helps the body recover from alcohol consumption.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with hangover.
