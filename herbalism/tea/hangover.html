10 Best Herbal Teas For Hangover

Herbal teas can help with hangover symptoms due to their hydrating properties, which can help restore fluids and electrolytes lost through alcohol consumption.

Some herbal teas, like ginger and chamomile, can also help soothe an upset stomach and promote relaxation, reducing nausea and anxiety. Additionally, certain herbal teas, such as milk thistle and dandelion, have been suggested to support liver function, which is essential for alcohol metabolism and detoxification. However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can help alleviate some hangover symptoms, they are not a cure-all and it's still crucial to drink responsibly and stay hydrated.

Lastly, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before consuming herbal teas for medicinal purposes, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with hangover because it has calming properties that can alleviate symptoms such as anxiety and irritability, which are common after drinking alcohol.

The tea's anti-inflammatory components can reduce the inflammation caused by alcohol, helping to ease headaches and muscle aches. Chamomile tea is also a natural diuretic, promoting the body to flush out toxins and rehydrate, thereby reducing the intensity of a hangover. Furthermore, its soothing effects on the digestive system can help settle an upset stomach and relieve nausea.

Lastly, chamomile tea has been shown to improve sleep quality, allowing the body to rest and recover from the effects of alcohol.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with hangover because it is known to soothe the digestive system, which can be irritated after excessive alcohol consumption.

The menthol present in peppermint tea can help in relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles, thereby alleviating symptoms such as nausea and abdominal pain. Peppermint tea is also a natural diuretic, promoting fluid balance and helping to restore essential electrolytes lost due to alcohol-induced dehydration. Furthermore, its calming properties can aid in reducing stress and anxiety often associated with hangovers, while the warm temperature of the tea can help increase blood circulation, expediting the body's metabolism and detoxification process.

Lastly, peppermint tea is caffeine-free, offering a gentle alternative to coffee that won't exacerbate dehydration or cause further stomach irritation.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with hangover because it contains natural ingredients that are effective in alleviating the symptoms associated with alcohol consumption.

First, ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce the inflammation caused by alcohol, helping to soothe headaches and body aches. Second, ginger is known for its ability to aid digestion, making it easier for the body to process and eliminate toxins, which can help to reduce nausea and vomiting.

Third, ginger tea can help to rehydrate the body, which is essential for recovering from a hangover as alcohol can cause dehydration. Fourth, ginger tea contains antioxidants that can help to protect the liver from damage caused by alcohol, promoting overall liver health.

Lastly, ginger tea is a caffeine-free alternative to coffee, which can help to avoid the dehydrating effects of caffeine, making it a better choice for those looking to recover from a hangover.

4. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea helps with hangover because it contains antioxidants that support liver function and speed up the breakdown of alcohol and its toxic byproducts.

Additionally, it has calming properties that can help alleviate anxiety and stress often associated with hangovers. Furthermore, lemon balm tea can restore the balance of essential neurotransmitters in the brain, such as GABA, which can be depleted due to excessive alcohol consumption. Its anti-inflammatory effects may also help reduce headaches and muscle cramps that are common hangover symptoms.

Finally, lemon balm tea can rehydrate the body and replenish essential electrolytes lost due to alcohol-induced dehydration.

5. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with hangover because it promotes liver health and detoxification.

The tea's active compound, silymarin, is an antioxidant that helps protect liver cells from damage caused by alcohol. It also aids in the removal of toxins from the body, including the byproducts of alcohol metabolism. Furthermore, milk thistle tea can help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, both of which are exacerbated by alcohol consumption.

Finally, it may also help restore glutathione levels, a key antioxidant that is often depleted after heavy drinking, thus alleviating hangover symptoms.

6. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with a hangover because it aids in liver function, which is essential for the body to break down and eliminate alcohol.

The tea is a natural diuretic, helping the body to flush out toxins and rehydrate, which can alleviate symptoms such as headache and nausea. Dandelion tea is also rich in antioxidants, which can help to reduce inflammation and damage to cells caused by excessive alcohol consumption. Furthermore, it contains vitamins and minerals, such as vitamin C, potassium, and magnesium, which can help to replenish essential nutrients lost during a night of drinking.

Finally, dandelion tea has been used in traditional medicine for its calming and soothing properties, which can provide relief from anxiety and restlessness associated with hangovers.

7. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with a hangover because it contains compounds called glycyrrhizin and glabridin, which can aid in detoxifying the body and protecting the liver.

These substances work by stimulating the production of enzymes that help break down alcohol and other toxins, thus speeding up the body's natural recovery process. Additionally, licorice tea can help soothe an upset stomach and alleviate symptoms such as nausea and vomiting, which are common during a hangover. Furthermore, licorice tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation caused by excessive alcohol consumption, which can contribute to headaches and other hangover symptoms.

Finally, licorice tea may also help replenish essential minerals and electrolytes lost during a night of heavy drinking, helping to restore balance to the body and alleviate dehydration, another common cause of hangover symptoms.

8. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with hangover because it has anti-inflammatory properties that can alleviate symptoms such as headaches and nausea caused by the dehydration resulting from alcohol consumption.

It also contains essential nutrients like vitamins A, C, and certain B vitamins, as well as minerals like iron, potassium, magnesium, and calcium, which can help replenish the body and speed up the detoxification process. Additionally, nettle tea is a natural diuretic, promoting fluid balance and helping the body flush out toxins more efficiently. Its soothing effects on the digestive system can also help with stomach upset and aid in regulating bowel movements.

Furthermore, nettle tea's antioxidant properties can help combat oxidative stress and support liver function, which is essential in the metabolism and elimination of alcohol from the body.

9. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with hangover because it contains high levels of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, which can help to reduce the symptoms of a hangover by combating the effects of alcohol on the liver and other organs.

The tea's diuretic properties can also help to flush toxins from the body, while its calming effects may soothe a nervous or upset stomach. Additionally, red clover tea is known to contain compounds that can help to support the liver's detoxification processes, which can aid in the breakdown and elimination of alcohol from the body.

Furthermore, red clover tea contains isoflavones, a type of phytoestrogen, which can help to regulate hormones and alleviate symptoms such as headaches and mood swings, common during hangovers.

10. Skullcap

Skullcap tea helps with a hangover because it functions as a natural sedative, easing anxiety and promoting relaxation, which can be beneficial in managing symptoms of a hangover.

It contains antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation caused by alcohol consumption, thereby alleviating hangover symptoms. Skullcap tea can also help to regulate glucose levels, which can become unbalanced due to alcohol intake, thus providing some relief from hangover-related fatigue and weakness. Furthermore, it has been suggested that skullcap tea may have hepatoprotective effects, potentially aiding in the prevention and treatment of alcohol-related liver damage, a common consequence of excessive alcohol consumption.

Lastly, skullcap tea can help to rehydrate the body, as it contains essential vitamins and minerals, which can become depleted due to alcohol-induced diuresis, a contributing factor in the onset of hangover symptoms.
