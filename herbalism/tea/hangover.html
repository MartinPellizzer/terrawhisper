10 Best Herbal Teas For Hangover

Herbal teas can help with hangover symptoms due to their hydrating properties, which can help restore fluids and electrolytes lost through alcohol consumption.

Some herbal teas, like ginger and chamomile, can also help soothe an upset stomach and promote relaxation, reducing nausea and anxiety. Additionally, certain herbal teas, such as milk thistle and dandelion, have been suggested to support liver function, which is essential for alcohol metabolism and detoxification. However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can help alleviate some hangover symptoms, they are not a cure-all and it's still crucial to drink responsibly and stay hydrated.

Lastly, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before consuming herbal teas for medicinal purposes, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with hangover because it contains compounds like matricin and apigenin that can help reduce inflammation and promote relaxation, which can help alleviate some of the symptoms of a hangover such as headache and anxiety.

Additionally, chamomile tea is known for its antioxidant properties, which can help the body eliminate toxins more efficiently, including those that contribute to hangovers. The tea can also help rehydrate the body, which is crucial after a night of drinking, as dehydration can exacerbate hangover symptoms. Furthermore, chamomile tea can help soothe the stomach and improve digestion, reducing feelings of nausea and discomfort.

Finally, chamomile tea has been shown to improve sleep quality, which can be disrupted by alcohol consumption and further contribute to hangover symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for hangover.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling (2-3 minutes in a microwave or 4-5 minutes in a kettle).
  2. Crush 2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers and place them in a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the crushed chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey or maple syrup (optional) and mix well.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discard the chamomile flowers, and enjoy your soothing hangover remedy.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with hangover because it contains menthol, a natural compound known to soothe digestion and ease symptoms of nausea, a common hangover symptom.

The tea also helps to rehydrate the body, as alcohol is a diuretic and can lead to dehydration, which exacerbates hangover symptoms. Additionally, peppermint tea has been found to reduce inflammation, which may help alleviate headaches and muscle cramps associated with hangovers. Its calming properties can also help to reduce stress and anxiety, common feelings during a hangover.

However, it's important to note that while peppermint tea can help alleviate some hangover symptoms, the best way to prevent a hangover is to drink alcohol in moderation.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for hangover.

  1. In a small pot, add 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil (3-4 minutes).
  2. Crush 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves or use 1 peppermint tea bag and add it to the pot.
  3. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes then turn off the heat.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, add honey or lemon as per taste, and let it cool down to warm temperature.
  5. Slowly sip the tea, allowing it to soothe your stomach and help with hangover symptoms.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with hangover because it contains powerful antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that assist in eliminating toxins and reducing inflammation in the body, which are both common effects of heavy alcohol consumption.

Additionally, ginger is well-known for its ability to soothe digestion and alleviate nausea, common symptoms of a hangover. The warm temperature of the tea can also help to relax muscles and promote circulation, further aiding in the detoxification process. Furthermore, ginger tea has been shown to help regulate blood sugar levels, which can be disrupted by alcohol consumption, and can provide hydration, helping to combat the dehydrating effects of alcohol.

Lastly, ginger tea is a natural and caffeine-free alternative to coffee, providing a gentle stimulant to help recover from the fatigue and grogginess that often accompany a hangover.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for hangover.

  1. In a pot, combine 2 cups of water, 1 inch of fresh ginger (thinly sliced), and 1 tablespoon of honey; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Add the juice of 1/2 a lemon and a pinch of cayenne pepper to the pot; continue simmering for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  4. Let the tea cool to a drinkable temperature, or add ice to speed up the process.
  5. Serve and sip slowly for best results, helping to alleviate your hangover symptoms.

4. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea helps with hangover because it contains rosmarinic acid, a potent antioxidant that helps reduce oxidative stress and inflammation caused by alcohol consumption.

Furthermore, it has calming properties that can help alleviate symptoms such as anxiety and irritability. Additionally, lemon balm may help promote better sleep, which is often disrupted by alcohol consumption. Its antispasmodic effects can also soothe an upset stomach and help with nausea.

Lastly, lemon balm tea can help rehydrate the body, which is essential in replacing fluids lost due to alcohol consumption, thus aiding in the recovery process.

The following procedure explaing how to make lemon balm tea for hangover.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of fresh lemon balm leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried lemon balm leaves, and rinse them gently; this preparation step should take less than 5 minutes.
  2. Place the lemon balm leaves in a tea infuser or teapot, then pour boiling water (about 8 ounces) over the leaves, allowing the tea to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. While the tea is steeping, squeeze half a lemon (about 1 tablespoon of lemon juice) into a cup, which will help to further alkalize and detoxify your body.
  4. Combine the freshly brewed lemon balm tea and lemon juice, then optionally add honey or another sweetener to taste, if desired.
  5. Stir the tea and enjoy it slowly, allowing the soothing and rehydrating properties of the lemon balm and lemon to alleviate your hangover symptoms; this drink can be consumed as needed throughout the day.

5. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with hangover because it promotes liver health and detoxification, which aids in the rapid metabolism and elimination of alcohol and its byproducts.

Its active compound, silymarin, protects liver cells from damage and stimulates the production of new liver cells, speeding up the detoxification process. Additionally, milk thistle tea has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation and alleviate hangover symptoms such as headaches and nausea. Furthermore, it replenishes essential vitamins and antioxidants that are depleted during heavy alcohol consumption, supporting overall recovery.

Lastly, milk thistle tea's soothing and calming effects on the digestive system can help restore balance and ease hangover-related gastrointestinal issues.

The following procedure explaing how to make milk thistle tea for hangover.

  1. Crush 1-2 teaspoons of milk thistle seeds and steep in hot water for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the crushed seeds.
  3. Add honey or lemon for taste, if desired.
  4. Drink the tea slowly, allowing it to cool if necessary.
  5. Repeat as needed every 4-6 hours for hangover relief.

6. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with hangover because Dandelion tea helps with hangovers because it aids in liver function, which is essential for processing alcohol and its toxic byproducts.

The tea is a natural diuretic, helping to restore fluid balance and replace electrolytes lost through alcohol-induced dehydration. Additionally, dandelion tea contains antioxidants that help combat oxidative stress and inflammation caused by alcohol consumption. Its mild bitter taste can also stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, supporting the digestion of fats and alcohol.

Lastly, dandelion tea can help replenish essential vitamins and minerals, such as vitamin C, potassium, and magnesium, which are often depleted due to alcohol consumption.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion tea for hangover.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh dandelion flowers, ensuring they are free from pesticides and pollutants; let the flowers dry for 1-2 days.
  2. Rinse the dried dandelion flowers gently, then steep them in 4 cups of boiling water for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove flower particles, creating a smooth liquid for consumption.
  4. Optional: Add a small amount of honey or lemon to enhance the tea's flavor, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and rehydrate your body, best consumed throughout the day to alleviate hangover symptoms.

7. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with hangover because Licorice tea helps with a hangover because it contains compounds called glycyrrhizic acid and glabridin, which are known to aid in detoxifying the body and protecting the liver.

These compounds also help in reducing inflammation and irritation in the stomach lining, providing relief from symptoms such as nausea and upset stomach. Moreover, licorice tea can help replenish essential minerals and fluids lost due to alcohol consumption, thereby rehydrating the body and alleviating hangover symptoms. Additionally, licorice tea has been shown to help regulate blood sugar levels, which can become imbalanced due to alcohol consumption, further contributing to hangover symptoms.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice tea can lead to side effects, so it is recommended to drink it in moderation.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice tea for hangover.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 liter of water and 2 tablespoons of dried licorice root, then bring to a boil, simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the licorice pieces, pour the liquid into a teapot or a large container.
  3. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for added flavor and electrolytes.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature, or serve it over ice for a refreshing beverage.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, aiming to drink the entire liter over the course of a few hours to help alleviate hangover symptoms.

8. Nettle

Nettle tea helps with hangover because it contains nutrients and antioxidants that help detoxify the liver, which is essential after a night of heavy drinking.

The tea's anti-inflammatory properties can also help reduce headaches and muscle aches that are often associated with hangovers. Nettle tea is a natural diuretic, helping the body to flush out toxins more efficiently, thereby speeding up the hangover recovery process. Additionally, nettle tea can help restore the body's balance of essential minerals and electrolytes that are lost due to alcohol consumption, providing further relief from hangover symptoms.

Lastly, nettle tea has been used traditionally as a remedy for various ailments, including hangovers, thanks to its soothing and healing effects on the digestive system.

The following procedure explaing how to make nettle tea for hangover.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh stinging nettle leaves (use gloves to protect your skin), rinse them thoroughly, and let them steep in boiling water for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove leaves and discard them, leaving behind the dark green liquid.
  3. Optional: Add honey, lemon, or peppermint leaves to enhance the flavor.
  4. Drink the nettle tea while it's still warm, which can help soothe your stomach and provide relief from hangover symptoms.
  5. Repeat as needed, waiting at least an hour between each cup, to rehydrate your body and support your recovery process.

9. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with hangover because it promotes the body's detoxification process, aiding in the elimination of alcohol toxins.

It contains high levels of antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress caused by alcohol consumption. Red clover tea is also a natural diuretic, which helps the body expel excess fluids and salts, thereby rehydrating the body and alleviating hangover symptoms. Moreover, it has isoflavones, which are known to ease menopause symptoms, and they can also help with hangover-induced headaches and mood swings.

Lastly, red clover tea can help restore the body's vitamin and mineral balance, particularly vitamin C, B-complex vitamins, and potassium, which are depleted due to alcohol consumption.

The following procedure explaing how to make red clover tea for hangover.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers and let them steep in hot water for 10-15 minutes (steep time may vary depending on desired strength).
  2. Strain the mixture to remove plant material, resulting in a red clover tea.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for improved flavor.
  4. Drink the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe and potentially alleviate hangover symptoms.
  5. Dispose of used plant material properly and rinse out any remaining tea from the cup.

10. Skullcap

Skullcap tea helps with hangover because it contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that work to reduce the effects of alcohol on the liver and brain.

It helps to relax the nervous system, alleviating symptoms such as anxiety, restlessness, and muscle tension that are often associated with hangovers. Skullcap tea also aids in rehydration, replenishing the fluids and electrolytes lost due to alcohol consumption, which can help to reduce the severity of hangover symptoms. Furthermore, it has been suggested that skullcap may help to protect the liver from damage caused by alcohol, reducing the likelihood of severe hangovers in the future.

Lastly, skullcap tea can promote better sleep, allowing the body to rest and recover more effectively from the effects of alcohol, leading to a quicker hangover recovery.

The following procedure explaing how to make skullcap tea for hangover.

  1. In a stovetop teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried skullcap herb with 1 cup of water, let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the herb, leaving behind only the liquid.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for improved flavor.
  4. Drink the skullcap tea while warm, preferably before bedtime or upon first signs of a hangover.
  5. Repeat as needed, waiting at least 4 hours between each serving, and avoid exceeding 2 cups in a day.
