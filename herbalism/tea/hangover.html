10 best herbal teas for hangover

If you're looking for a natural way to ease the symptoms of a hangover, certain herbal teas can be very helpful.

Peppermint tea is one option, as it can settle an upset stomach and help with nausea. Ginger tea is another good choice, as it can also soothe the stomach and reduce inflammation.

Chamomile tea is known for its calming properties and can help promote relaxation and better sleep, which can be helpful after a night of drinking. Finally, licorice root tea can help replenish the body's potassium levels, which can be depleted after drinking alcohol.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can help alleviate some hangover symptoms, they are not a substitute for staying hydrated and getting rest.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea's calming properties, recognized by historical medical practices such as Greco-Roman and Egyptian medicine, help alleviate hangover symptoms.

This herbal remedy contains the antioxidant apigenin, which reduces oxidative stress from alcohol metabolism. Additionally, its mild sedative effects help improve sleep quality, a crucial factor in recovering from alcohol consumption. Chamomile's soothing properties ease digestive discomfort, a common hangover symptom.

Thus, chamomile tea unequivocally aids in alleviating hangover symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with hangover.

  • Flowers: Chamomile's daisy-like flowers contain calming properties that help soothe an upset stomach and promote relaxation, which can alleviate hangover symptoms.
  • Leaves: The bitter leaves of chamomile help stimulate the digestive system and relieve bloating, making them useful for hangover relief.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in antioxidants that help protect the liver from damage caused by alcohol, which can help reduce hangover severity.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for hangover.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers and place them in a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool slightly for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove the flowers.
  5. Drink the chamomile tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids in hangover relief due to its established abilities to soothe the stomach and digestive tract, as confirmed by scientific research.

Its organic compounds, specifically menthol and methyl salicylate, possess potent anti-inflammatory properties that alleviate nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Furthermore, peppermint tea hydrates the body, counteracting the dehydrating effects of alcohol consumption. Additionally, it facilitates the breakdown and elimination of toxins, such as acetaldehyde, which accumulate during alcohol metabolism.

Consequently, these combined actions make peppermint tea an effective natural remedy for hangover relief.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with hangover.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, peppermint leaves are known for their ability to soothe digestion and alleviate headaches.
  • Flowers: High in antioxidants, peppermint flowers aid in detoxification and support the liver's function in hangover recovery.
  • Stems: Containing essential oils, peppermint stems help settle the stomach and reduce nausea associated with hangovers.
  • Seeds: Packed with nutrients, peppermint seeds provide hydration and electrolyte balance, crucial for hangover relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for hangover.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Let the tea steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, pouring the liquid into a cup.
  4. Sweeten the tea with honey to taste, if desired, and stir well.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your stomach and rehydrate your body.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea provides relief from hangover through its active components, such as gingerols and shogaols, which ease nausea and vomiting.

This soothing effect on the stomach results from ginger's ability to neutralize acid and reduce inflammation. Furthermore, ginger's antioxidant properties counteract oxidative stress caused by alcohol consumption. Additionally, ginger tea's mild diuretic effect helps the body eliminate toxins and rehydrate, alleviating hangover symptoms.

Lastly, ginger tea restores lost electrolytes and vitamins, promoting the body's overall recovery from alcohol's diuretic actions.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with hangover.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are a popular choice for making medicinal tea to treat hangovers due to their potent anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe upset stomachs.
  • Roots: Ginger roots are rich in gingerols and shaogals, compounds known to alleviate nausea and vomiting, making them an ideal ingredient for hangover tea.
  • Leaves: Ginger leaves contain high levels of antioxidants that help detoxify the liver and reduce the severity of hangover symptoms.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers are known for their calming effects on the digestive system and are often used in hangover tea to provide relief from abdominal discomfort.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds are a powerful stimulant that promote blood circulation, helping to speed up the metabolism of alcohol and reduce the severity of hangover symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for hangover.

  1. Peel and chop 1 inch of fresh ginger, then bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan and add the chopped ginger.
  2. Reduce heat to low, cover and let it simmer for 15 minutes to allow the ginger to release its healing properties.
  3. Remove from heat, then stir in 1 teaspoon of honey and 1 teaspoon of lemon juice.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the ginger pieces.
  5. Pour the tea into a cup and enjoy while hot to soothe your hangover.

4. Lemon Balm

Lemon balm tea relieves hangover symptoms through its well-documented medicinal properties.

This herbal tea, containing rosmarinic acid, a potent antioxidant, combats oxidative stress caused by alcohol consumption. Furthermore, lemon balm's calming effects on the nervous system, attributed to its flavonoids, help alleviate anxiety and insomnia associated with hangovers. Additionally, lemon balm's soothing properties settle upset stomachs and improve appetite, common symptoms during a hangover.

Thus, lemon balm tea unequivocally aids in hangover recovery.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in lemon balm tea that help with hangover.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and rosmarinic acid, leaves help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress caused by alcohol consumption.
  • Flowers: Packed with flavonoids and essential oils, flowers aid in calming the digestive system and soothing headaches from hangovers.
  • Stems: Containing trace amounts of tannins and bitters, stems help stimulate digestion and alleviate nausea associated with hangovers.
  • Seeds: Abundant in essential oils and antioxidants, seeds support detoxification and liver function, combating hangover symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemon balm tea for hangover.

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Crush 2 tablespoons of dried lemon balm leaves and add them to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea and discard the leaves.
  5. Serve lemon balm tea hot, or let it cool and drink it as a refreshing iced tea.

5. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea contains silymarin, a potent antioxidant known to protect the liver from damage, thereby reducing the severity of hangover symptoms.

This flavonoid speeds up regeneration of liver cells, helping the organ to metabolize and eliminate toxins from alcohol more efficiently. Furthermore, milk thistle's anti-inflammatory properties alleviate painful hangover symptoms such as headaches and nausea.

Lastly, milk thistle tea supports healthy digestion, which often becomes disrupted after excessive alcohol consumption.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in milk thistle tea that help with hangover.

  • Seeds: The seeds of the milk thistle plant are used to make medicinal tea for hangovers due to their high concentration of silymarin, a flavonoid that helps protect the liver.
  • Leaves: Milk thistle leaves are a good source of antioxidants, making them useful for reducing oxidative stress and inflammation caused by alcohol consumption.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the milk thistle plant contain bioactive compounds that support liver function and help detoxify the body, making them a key ingredient in medicinal tea for hangovers.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic milk thistle tea for hangover.

  1. In a tea infuser, place 1 teaspoon of crushed milk thistle seeds.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the infuser and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the infuser and discard the spent seeds.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day.

6. Dandelion

Dandelion tea aids hangovers by promoting liver function.

This plant contains compounds that speed up alcohol metabolism. Dandelion tea also restores essential minerals and hydrates the body, counteracting alcohol's diuretic effects.

Finally, its antioxidants help combat oxidative stress caused by heavy drinking.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in dandelion tea that help with hangover.

  • Roots: The dandelion root contains a bitter compound that supports liver function, helping the body to eliminate toxins from the blood and alleviating hangover symptoms.
  • Leaves: Rich in vitamins and minerals, dandelion leaves can help restore electrolyte balance and support the liver's detoxification process, easing hangover discomfort.
  • Flowers: High in antioxidants, dandelion flowers can help neutralize free radicals in the body, reducing oxidative stress and inflammation caused by alcohol consumption.
  • Buds: Dandelion buds contain a variety of beneficial compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones, that can help stimulate bile production and promote digestion, alleviating hangover-related nausea and discomfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for hangover.

  1. Gather 2-3 dandelion heads and rinse them thoroughly under running water to remove dirt and insects.
  2. Separate the yellow petals from the green base and calyxes, as the green parts can make the tea bitter (takes about 5 minutes).
  3. Put the petals in a tea infuser or tea ball and place it in a cup, then pour boiling water over the petals and let them steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the infuser or tea ball from the cup and discard the petals.
  5. Sweeten the tea with honey or sugar to taste and drink it while it's still warm, up to three times a day.

7. Licorice

Licorice tea aids in alleviating hangover symptoms due to its active component, glycyrrhizin.

This compound triggers the adrenal glands to produce higher levels of cortisol, a natural hormone that counteracts stress and boosts energy. Moreover, glycyrrhizin stimulates the production of prostaglandins, which facilitate blood flow and help to decrease inflammation in the body. Additionally, licorice tea supports the liver's function by promoting the release of bile, an essential substance for breaking down and eliminating toxins.

As a result, licorice tea effectively combats fatigue, reduces inflammation, and helps the body recover from alcohol consumption.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with hangover.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice plant contain glycyrrhizin, which helps to protect the stomach lining and reduce inflammation.
  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of the licorice plant have similar medicinal properties as the roots, but also contain more volatile oils that aid in digestion.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the licorice plant contain flavonoids, which have been shown to possess anti-oxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate hangover symptoms.
  • Stems: The stems of the licorice plant contain glycosides, which help to soothe gastrointestinal problems, often associated with hangovers.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the licorice plant have high concentrations of essential oils, offering antispasmodic and calming effects, providing relief from nausea and headaches.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for hangover.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 liter of water and 2 tablespoons of dried licorice root.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root pieces.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, aiming to finish the liter over the course of several hours.

8. Nettle

Nettle tea fights hangovers effectively due to its high levels of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

These components aid in flushing out toxins, such as those found in alcohol, from the body. Moreover, nettle tea restores vital nutrients, including vitamins and minerals, which alcohol depletes. Additionally, it contains tannins that aid in soothing and tightening tissues in the digestive tract, reducing irritation and discomfort.

Lastly, nettle tea's diuretic properties promote kidney function, further assisting in the removal of harmful substances and rehydrating the body.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in nettle tea that help with hangover.

  • Stems: The young, supple stems are used in making medicinal tea for hangovers due to their natural anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate headaches and ease digestion.
  • Leaves: The leaves are used as they contain high levels of antioxidants and nutrients that help to detoxify the liver, supporting its function in processing and eliminating toxins from the body.
  • Seeds: The seeds are a powerhouse of nutrients that aid in rehydration and re-establishing vital electrolyte balance, making them an ideal addition to hangover tea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic nettle tea for hangover.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh stinging nettle leaves, avoiding touching the tips with bare hands to prevent irritation (preparation time: 10-15 minutes).
  2. Rinse the nettle leaves thoroughly under cold running water, then quickly blanch in boiling water for 1-2 minutes to neutralize any remaining sting.
  3. Coarsely chop the blanched nettle leaves and place them in a teapot or infuser, adding 1 liter of freshly boiled water.
  4. Steep the nettle mixture for 5-10 minutes, then strain the tea to remove the leaves.
  5. Drink the nettle tea warm, up to three cups throughout the day, to help alleviate hangover symptoms (drinking time: 30-60 minutes).

9. Red Clover

Red clover tea relieves hangovers due to its rich isoflavone content, particularly genistein and daidzein, which accelerate alcohol metabolism in the liver.

This tea's antioxidant properties, derived from phenolic acids and flavonoids, combat oxidative stress caused by alcohol consumption, protecting liver cells from damage. Furthermore, red clover tea's diuretic effect, promoted by its high potassium content, helps eliminate toxic byproducts of alcohol metabolism from the body. Additionally, it replenishes essential nutrients, such as vitamins and minerals, depleted by alcohol consumption, contributing to overall rehydration and well-being.

Red clover tea's calming effect on the nervous system, owed to its significant content of aglycones and isoflavones, also alleviates anxiety and irritability often associated with hangovers.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in red clover tea that help with hangover.

  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, they help neutralize toxins and reduce inflammation caused by alcohol consumption.
  • Leaves: Contain high levels of calcium and magnesium, which help replenish essential minerals lost during heavy drinking.
  • Stems: Known for their diuretic properties, they aid in eliminating metabolic waste and promoting kidney function.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential amino acids, they support liver health and help in alcohol metabolism.
  • Rhyzomes: Have isoflavones that help alleviate headaches, one of the common hangover symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic red clover tea for hangover.

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers and add them to a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the red clover flowers, allowing them to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. While the tea is steeping, squeeze half a lemon into a mug for added vitamin C and to help balance the tea's flavor.
  4. After steeping, pour the red clover tea into the mug with lemon juice.
  5. Enjoy the warm tea to help alleviate hangover symptoms such as headache and nausea.
