10 Best Herbal Teas For Hangover

Herbal teas can help with hangover symptoms due to their hydrating properties, which can help restore fluids and electrolytes lost through alcohol consumption.

Some herbal teas, like ginger and chamomile, can also help soothe an upset stomach and promote relaxation, reducing nausea and anxiety. Additionally, certain herbal teas, such as milk thistle and dandelion, have been suggested to support liver function, which is essential for alcohol metabolism and detoxification. However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can help alleviate some hangover symptoms, they are not a cure-all and it's still crucial to drink responsibly and stay hydrated.

Lastly, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional before consuming herbal teas for medicinal purposes, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking any medications.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with hangover because it has calming properties that can alleviate symptoms such as anxiety and irritability, which are common after drinking alcohol.

The tea's anti-inflammatory components can reduce the inflammation caused by alcohol, helping to ease headaches and muscle aches. Chamomile tea is also a natural diuretic, promoting the body to flush out toxins and rehydrate, thereby reducing the intensity of a hangover. Furthermore, its soothing effects on the digestive system can help settle an upset stomach and relieve nausea.

Lastly, chamomile tea has been shown to improve sleep quality, allowing the body to rest and recover from the effects of alcohol.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with hangover.

  1. Apigenin: A flavonoid that binds to benzodiazepine receptors in the brain, helping to reduce anxiety and promote sleep, which can be beneficial during a hangover.
  2. Chamazulene: A chemical compound with anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce headaches and inflammation associated with hangovers.
  3. Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol with anti-inflammatory and calming effects, which may help alleviate symptoms of hangover such as nausea and restlessness.
  4. Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin has anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties that can help with hangover-related headaches and muscle pain.
  5. Spiroether: A compound with antispasmodic properties that can help relieve abdominal cramps and improve digestion, which can be beneficial during a hangover.

The following list reveals what parts of chamomile are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for hangover.

  1. Flowers: Chamomile flowers are used for medicinal tea for hangover due to their calming and anti-inflammatory properties which can help soothe an upset stomach and headaches.
  2. Leaves: The leaves of chamomile contain flavonoids and essential oils that can help aid digestion and relieve symptoms of overindulgence.
  3. Seeds: Chamomile seeds are rich in antioxidants and can help support liver function, which can be beneficial in reducing the symptoms of a hangover.

The following procedure explains how to make chamomile tea for hangover.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to 212°F (100°C) for 1-2 minutes to optimize chamomile compounds extraction.
  2. Pour hot water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers, submerging them fully in a teapot or teacup.
  3. Steep the chamomile in hot water for 5-10 minutes for the flavors and beneficial compounds to infuse.
  4. Strain the steeped chamomile flowers out of the tea, discarding the spent flowers.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste (optional), then sip and relax as chamomile works its soothing magic up to 30 minutes.
