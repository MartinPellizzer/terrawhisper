10 Best Herbal Teas For Digestion

Herbal teas, particularly those made from plants with known carminative properties, can help alleviate digestive discomforts by reducing inflammation and relaxing gastrointestinal muscles, thereby enabling smoother passage of food and easing the expulsion of gas.

Many herbal teas also contain volatile oils and antioxidants that can stimulate the production of digestive enzymes and bile, enhancing nutrient absorption and promoting overall digestive health. Furthermore, these teas can help manage stress and anxiety, which are often linked to digestive issues, by encouraging relaxation and stress reduction. Additionally, herbal teas are caffeine-free and naturally decaffeinated, making them a gentler alternative to traditional teas and coffee that can sometimes irritate the stomach lining and exacerbate digestive problems.

Lastly, the soothing warmth of herbal teas can provide comfort and alleviate symptoms of indigestion, such as bloating and cramping, making them an ideal beverage to consume after meals.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with digestion because it contains volatile oils, flavonoids, and antioxidants that have been shown to support gastrointestinal health.

These compounds work together to reduce inflammation, relax the smooth muscles of the intestines, and alleviate symptoms such as gas, bloating, and indigestion. Furthermore, chamomile tea has been found to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, which help break down food particles and facilitate nutrient absorption. Additionally, its soothing properties can help reduce stress and anxiety, both of which can negatively impact digestion.

Therefore, drinking chamomile tea regularly may promote overall digestive well-being and support a healthy gastrointestinal tract.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for digestion.

  1. To make chamomile tea for digestion, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) to a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Allow the chamomile flowers to steep in the hot water for 5-10 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve a strong, aromatic tea.
  3. Optional: for added digestive support, include 1 slice of fresh ginger or a pinch of cinnamon during the steeping process.
  4. After steeping, remove the chamomile flowers or tea bag and let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Finally, enjoy your soothing chamomile tea after meals to aid in digestion and promote relaxation.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with digestion because it contains menthol and methyl salicylate, compounds that have antispasmodic properties, which helps relax the gastrointestinal muscles and allows for the smooth passage of food and gas through the digestive system.

This can reduce symptoms of digestive discomfort such as bloating, cramping, and gas. Additionally, peppermint oil has been shown to increase bile flow, which aids in the digestion of fats. The soothing effect of drinking peppermint tea may also help to reduce stress and anxiety, which can contribute to digestive issues.

However, it's important to note that excessive consumption of peppermint tea can exacerbate acid reflux or GERD symptoms, so it's best consumed in moderation.

The following procedure explaing how to make peppermint tea for digestion.

  1. To make peppermint tea for digestion, boil 2 cups of water and then steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried peppermint leaves (or 1 peppermint tea bag) in the boiling water for 5-10 minutes.
  2. After steeping, strain the tea to remove the peppermint leaves (if using loose leaves), then let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  3. Add a small slice of fresh ginger root (about the size of your thumb) to the tea for added digestive benefits, if desired.
  4. Sweeten the tea with a small amount of raw honey (about 1 teaspoon) for a touch of natural sweetness, if preferred.
  5. Drink the peppermint tea slowly, sipping it after meals or whenever you need relief from digestive discomfort.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with digestion because it contains phenolic compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, which stimulate the production of digestive enzymes and increase gastric motility, helping to move food through the gastrointestinal tract more efficiently.

Additionally, ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, alleviating symptoms of indigestion and bloating. Ginger tea can also help alleviate nausea and vomiting, making it a popular remedy for digestive issues such as motion sickness and morning sickness during pregnancy. Furthermore, ginger tea can help soothe the stomach and provide relief from discomfort caused by indigestion, gas, and acid reflux.

Lastly, ginger tea has been shown to help regulate blood sugar levels, which can help prevent the spikes and crashes that can contribute to poor digestion and related symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for digestion.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root, then combine with 4 cups of water in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let the ginger simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the water to absorb the ginger's beneficial compounds.
  3. Remove from heat, then stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey (or to taste) and the juice of half a lemon for added flavor and vitamin C.
  4. Strain the ginger tea into a teapot or mug, making sure to remove the ginger pieces.
  5. Serve immediately while warm, sipping slowly to promote digestion and soothe the stomach. Enjoy your homemade ginger tea!

4. Licorice Root

Licorice root tea helps with digestion because it contains glycyrrhizic acid, a compound that stimulates the production of mucus in the stomach, which can protect the stomach lining and reduce inflammation.

This can help soothe symptoms of indigestion, such as heartburn, bloating, and stomach pain. Additionally, licorice root tea may increase the secretion of digestive enzymes, which can help break down food more efficiently and prevent digestive discomfort. Furthermore, it has been suggested that licorice root may have antibacterial properties, which could help eliminate harmful bacteria in the gut that can interfere with digestion.

However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice root tea can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and hormonal imbalances, so it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating it into your diet.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for digestion.

  1. In a tea pot, add 1 teaspoon of dried licorice root to 8 ounces of boiling water, then let it steep for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to be released and to ensure a potent and effective tea.
  2. For enhanced benefits, consider adding 1 small piece of fresh ginger or a pinch of fennel seeds to the tea pot before adding water, as these ingredients can further aid in digestion and soothe the stomach.
  3. After steeping, strain the tea into a cup using a fine mesh strainer or tea strainer to remove the licorice root and any other solid particles, which can be composted or discarded.
  4. To enhance the flavor and customize the tea to your taste, consider adding a natural sweetener like honey or stevia, a squeeze of lemon juice, or a splash of almond or coconut milk.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, preferably shortly after meals or as needed to alleviate digestive discomfort, and enjoy the soothing and calming effects of licorice root on your digestive system.

5. Fennel

Fennel tea helps with digestion because it contains carminative properties that help to expel gas from the stomach and intestines, providing relief from bloating and discomfort.

The tea's antispasmodic qualities also aid in relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles, which can alleviate symptoms of indigestion, such as cramps and spasms. Furthermore, fennel tea has been shown to increase the production of digestive enzymes, facilitating the breakdown of food in the stomach and improving overall digestion. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory properties can help soothe inflammation in the digestive tract, reducing the risk of conditions like irritable bowel syndrome.

Lastly, fennel tea's calming effect on the digestive system can promote regularity and prevent issues like constipation, making it a natural and beneficial aid for overall digestive health.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel tea for digestion.

  1. To make fennel tea for digestion, start by boiling 1 cup of water in a saucepan or kettle; once boiling, add 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds, reduce heat, and let it simmer for 10 minutes to allow the water to absorb the fennel's beneficial compounds.
  2. After simmering, remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool for a few minutes, then pour the fennel-infused water through a strainer into a teacup or mug, ensuring that no seeds make it into the final drink.
  3. For added flavor and additional digestive benefits, consider adding 1 thinly sliced slice of fresh ginger root or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice to the fennel tea.
  4. Stir the tea gently to mix in any added ingredients, then let it steep for an additional 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Finally, gently blow on the tea to cool it down to a drinkable temperature, then enjoy this soothing and potentially digestion-aiding beverage after meals.

6. Dandelion

Dandelion tea helps with digestion because it promotes the production of digestive juices, including stomach bile, which aids in the breakdown of food in the stomach and intestines.

This, in turn, can help to alleviate symptoms of digestive distress such as bloating, constipation, and indigestion. Additionally, dandelion is a natural diuretic, which can help to flush toxins out of the body and improve overall gut health. The tea is also rich in fiber, which is essential for maintaining a healthy digestive system by supporting regular bowel movements and promoting the growth of beneficial gut bacteria.

Furthermore, dandelion tea contains several beneficial compounds, including phenolic acids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, potentially reducing inflammation in the digestive tract and promoting overall digestive health.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion tea for digestion.

  1. To make dandelion tea for digestion, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot, then add 1-2 teaspoons of dried dandelion leaves or 1 dandelion tea bag.
  2. Allow the water to simmer for 5-10 minutes to let the active compounds in the dandelion leaves infuse into the water.
  3. Turn off the heat and strain out the dandelion leaves or remove the tea bag, then let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. For added flavor and potential synergistic effects on digestion, consider adding 1 teaspoon of raw honey and a squeeze of lemon juice to the tea.
  5. Drink the dandelion tea while it's still warm, preferably after meals, to aid digestion and soothe your stomach.

7. Senna

Senna tea helps with digestion because it contains natural compounds called sennosides, which stimulate the intestines and promote muscle contractions that help move waste through the bowels.

This can help alleviate constipation and encourage regular bowel movements. Additionally, senna tea has been shown to increase the production of digestive enzymes, which can improve the breakdown and absorption of nutrients in the gut. Furthermore, it may have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe irritation and inflammation in the digestive tract, providing relief from symptoms such as bloating and discomfort.

However, it's important to note that senna tea should not be used as a long-term solution for digestive issues, as overuse can lead to dependence and potential damage to the digestive system.

The following procedure explaing how to make senna tea for digestion.

  1. To make senna tea for digestion, start by boiling 8 ounces of water in a tea kettle or pot; once boiling, add 1-2 teaspoons of dried senna leaves and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. While waiting, warm up your favorite teacup with hot water to help maintain the temperature of the senna tea.
  3. After steeping, remove the senna leaves from the water using a strainer or infuser, and discard the leaves.
  4. If desired, add honey or lemon to taste for flavor, but avoid adding milk or other dairy products as they may interfere with senna's effectiveness.
  5. Drink the tea while still warm, preferably between meals or in the early evening, as senna can stimulate bowel movements and may interfere with sleep if consumed too close to bedtime.

8. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with digestion because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and gel-like when wet, which can help to soothe and protect the lining of the stomach and intestines.

This mucilage can also help to add bulk to stools, making them easier to pass and helping to prevent constipation. Additionally, marshmallow root tea has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate symptoms such as pain, bloating, and cramping. The tea may also help to increase the production of digestive enzymes, which can improve the breakdown and absorption of nutrients from food.

Finally, marshmallow root tea has been shown to have a prebiotic effect, meaning it can help to promote the growth of beneficial bacteria in the gut, which is important for overall digestive health.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow root tea for digestion.

  1. In a cup, steep 1 teaspoon of dried marshmallow root in 8 ounces of boiling water for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and create a marshmallow root tea.
  2. After the steeping time, strain the tea to remove the marshmallow root pieces, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable tea-drinking experience.
  3. For added digestive support, consider adding 1 teaspoon of raw honey or a squeeze of lemon juice, if desired, to enhance the tea's soothing properties.
  4. Gently stir the honey or lemon juice into the marshmallow root tea, making sure it is well-mixed and warm before consuming.
  5. Sip the marshmallow root tea slowly, allowing it to gently coat and soothe the digestive tract, promoting healthy digestion and offering relief from discomfort.

9. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea helps with digestion because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and thick when mixed with water, which coats and soothes the digestive tract, helping to alleviate symptoms of indigestion such as heartburn, acid reflux, and gastric ulcers.

This coating effect also helps to protect the stomach lining from damaging digestive juices, reducing inflammation and promoting healing. Additionally, slippery elm tea can increase the production of mucus in the gastrointestinal tract, which helps to lubricate the digestive system and move food through the intestines more efficiently, reducing the risk of constipation and promoting regularity. Slippery elm tea may also stimulate the release of bile from the liver, which is necessary for the digestion of fats, further aiding in the overall digestive process.

Lastly, slippery elm tea has been shown to have prebiotic properties, meaning it can help to promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria, which is essential for maintaining a healthy gut microbiome and optimal digestive health.

The following procedure explaing how to make slippery elm tea for digestion.

  1. To make slippery elm tea for digestion, start by boiling 2 cups of water in a pot, then reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of powdered slippery elm bark, mixing thoroughly to ensure there are no clumps.
  3. Allow the mixture to simmer for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any remaining bark particles.
  5. Pour the strained tea into a cup and enjoy it while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed for digestive relief.

10. Skullcap

Skullcap tea helps with digestion because it contains antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds, such as flavonoids and polyphenols, which can help soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation.

These compounds also help to stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, which can improve the breakdown and absorption of nutrients in the gut. Additionally, skullcap tea has been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety, common triggers of digestive discomfort. Furthermore, skullcap tea has been used traditionally to treat various gastrointestinal issues, such as gastritis, bloating, and constipation, due to its anti-spasmodic properties that can help relax the smooth muscles of the digestive tract.

Lastly, skullcap tea is caffeine-free, making it a gentle and soothing option for those with sensitive digestive systems or those looking to avoid stimulants.

The following procedure explaing how to make skullcap tea for digestion.

  1. To make skullcap tea for digestion, start by boiling 1 cup of water and adding 2 teaspoons of dried skullcap herb (Scutellaria lateriflora); let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the herbs, then squeeze half a lemon (about 1-2 tablespoons of juice) into the tea for added vitamin C and to help with iron absorption.
  3. For improved digestion, grate a small piece of ginger (about 1/2 teaspoon) and add it to the tea.
  4. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of raw honey for a touch of natural sweetness, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while still warm, up to three times a day, 30 minutes before or after meals.
