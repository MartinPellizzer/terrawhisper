10 best herbal teas for digestion

There are several herbal teas that are well-known for their ability to support digestion and soothe the digestive tract.

Peppermint tea is a popular choice due to its menthol content, which can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and reduce inflammation. Ginger tea is another excellent option, as it contains anti-inflammatory compounds that can help alleviate nausea, bloating, and other digestive discomforts.

Chamomile tea is also commonly recommended for its calming properties and ability to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Lastly, licorice root tea can help increase mucus secretion in the gastrointestinal tract, providing a protective barrier that can help heal and soothe the digestive lining.

However, it is important to note that licorice root tea should be consumed in moderation, as excessive consumption may lead to high blood pressure and other health issues.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea aids digestion due to its antispasmodic and carminative properties.

These properties relax the smooth muscle lining of the digestive tract, reducing the risk of indigestion and gas build-up. Moreover, chamomile contains certain volatile oils, such as bisabolol, that have a calming effect on the stomach and intestines, thereby alleviating discomfort and promoting healthy digestion. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory and healing properties, attributed to the presence of flavonoids like apigenin, can soothe inflammation in the digestive tract and facilitate the healing of any existing digestive issues.

Consequently, chamomile tea enhances the overall efficiency of the digestive process and fosters a sense of well-being.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with digestion.

  • Flowers: The chamomile flowers contain oils and antioxidants that promote digestion by reducing inflammation and relaxing the muscles in the digestive system.
  • Leaves: The leaves of chamomile plants have calming properties that help soothe the stomach and alleviate digestive discomfort.
  • Seeds: The seeds of chamomile plants are rich in antioxidants that aid in digestion by stimulating the production of digestive enzymes.

2. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids digestion due to its active compounds, primarily menthol and methyl salicylate.

These components boast antispasmodic properties, relaxing the gastrointestinal muscles and thus alleviating digestive discomforts like cramps and spasms. Moreover, peppermint tea enhances the flow of bile from the gallbladder to the small intestine, expediting the digestion process. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects help soothe the digestive tract, providing relief from irritation and inflammation.

Finally, peppermint tea's carminative properties allow it to expel excessive gas from the digestive system, preventing bloating, discomfort, and other symptoms associated with poor digestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with digestion.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, peppermint leaves promote digestion by relaxing gastrointestinal muscles and reducing inflammation.
  • Flowers: High in essential oils, peppermint flowers stimulate bile production, aiding in fat digestion and reducing symptoms of digestive discomfort.
  • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants and volatile oils, peppermint seeds help soothe the stomach, alleviate gas, and improve overall digestive function.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea aids digestion due to its carminative properties, which move gas from the intestines to the stomach, relieving bloating and discomfort.

This beverage contains gingerols and shogaols, compounds with anti-inflammatory effects that help soothe the digestive tract, reducing inflammation. Furthermore, ginger stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, accelerating food breakdown and nutrient absorption. Additionally, ginger tea's warmth enhances blood flow and relaxes the intestinal muscles, alleviating constipation and promoting regular bowel movements.

Therefore, ginger tea's natural properties make it an excellent aid for healthy digestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with digestion.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger's thick, underground stem called the rhizome is widely used in medicinal teas for digestion due to its carminative properties that help alleviate bloating and flatulence.
  • Roots: The knobby, fibrous roots of the ginger plant contain bioactive compounds that aid in digestion, making them an excellent choice for medicinal tea.
  • Leaves: Fresh or dried ginger leaves are used in some medicinal teas for digestion, as they contain volatile oils that stimulate digestive juices and promote healthy digestion.
  • Flowers: While not as commonly used as the rhizomes, ginger flowers can be included in medicinal teas for digestion, as they are rich in antioxidants that support overall gut health.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds are occasionally added to medicinal teas for digestion, as they contain essential oils that help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and reduce digestive discomfort.

4. Licorice

Licorice tea aids digestion due to its active compound, glycyrrhizin, which stimulates the production of mucin, a slippery substance that lubricates the digestive tract, allowing food to pass through smoothly.

This tea also encourages the release of bile from the gallbladder, which helps break down fats during digestion. Moreover, licorice tea soothes the stomach lining, reducing inflammation and irritation, and alleviating symptoms such as heartburn and acid reflux.

Furthermore, it combats digestive discomfort by minimizing the secretion of gastric acid, thereby preventing stomach ulcers and promoting overall gut health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in licorice tea that help with digestion.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice plant contain glycyrrhizin, a compound with anti-inflammatory and soothing properties for the stomach lining, making it effective for digestive problems.
  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of the licorice plant are used in medicinal teas for digestion due to their ability to stimulate the production of mucin, which protects the stomach lining.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the licorice plant contain flavonoids that help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, making it effective for soothing digestive issues.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the licorice plant contain various compounds, including coumarins and flavonoids, that have been shown to aid in digestion and soothe digestive problems.

5. Fennel

Fennel tea aids digestion due to its carminative properties, which stem from the volatile oils found in fennel seeds.

These oils, including anethole, facilitate the expulsion of intestinal gas and reduce bloating. Moreover, fennel tea's antispasmodic qualities help relax the gastrointestinal muscles, easing the digestive process. Additionally, fennel contains rosmarinic acid, which boasts anti-inflammatory effects that can alleviate inflammation in the digestive tract.

The tea's high antioxidant content further promotes digestive health by safeguarding the gastrointestinal system from oxidative stress.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in fennel tea that help with digestion.

  • Seeds: Fennel seeds are an effective digestive aid due to their carminative and antispasmodic properties that help relieve bloating, gas, and indigestion.
  • Leaves: The feathery leaves of fennel plants have a mild anise flavor and aid digestion by promoting the production of digestive enzymes.
  • Stems: Fennel stems, though less commonly used, contain valuable essential oils that can stimulate digestion and soothe gastrointestinal discomfort.
  • Flowers: Fennel flowers contribute a subtle, aromatic flavor to tea and offer antioxidant properties that support overall digestive health.

6. Dandelion

Dandelion tea promotes digestion due to its bitter properties that stimulate the production of digestive enzymes in the liver.

This tea also contains inulin, a type of soluble fiber that serves as food for beneficial gut bacteria, encouraging their growth and maintaining a healthy gut flora. Moreover, dandelion tea's mild laxative effect facilitates bowel movement, easing strain during defecation. Additionally, this herbal tea's ability to stimulate bile production in the gallbladder ensures better fat emulsification, further aiding digestion.

Lastly, the anti-inflammatory properties of dandelion tea alleviate digestive discomforts, such as bloating and cramps, ensuring a smooth and efficient digestive process.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in dandelion tea that help with digestion.

  • Roots: Dandelion roots are rich in beneficial compounds that support the digestive system by stimulating appetite and aiding in digestion.
  • Leaves: The bitter properties of dandelion leaves help promote saliva and stomach acid production, encouraging healthy digestion.
  • Flowers: Dandelion flowers contain flavonoids that support the overall digestive process, making them an excellent addition to medicinal teas.

7. Senna

Senna tea contains compounds known as sennosides, which have a stimulant effect on the intestines.

These sennosides trigger contractions in the colon, accelerating the movement of stool through the digestive tract. Consequently, senna tea reduces the time that waste materials remain in the intestines, alleviating symptoms of constipation and promoting regularity. Additionally, this herbal tea softens stool, making it easier to pass.

Overall, senna tea's active components enhance digestion by encouraging bowel movements and maintaining a healthy gastrointestinal tract.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in senna tea that help with digestion.

  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids and hydroxycinnamic acids, senna leaves produce a potent tea that stimulates digestion by triggering contractions in the intestines.
  • Pods: The dried, ripe fruit of senna, also known as pods, contain anthraquinones that promote digestion by increasing intestinal muscle contractions.
  • Flowers: Although less potent than leaves and pods, senna flowers contribute to medicinal tea for digestion with anthraquinone glycosides, enhancing gut motility.
  • Rhyzomes: Packed with senna's active compounds, rhyzomes (underground stems) help alleviate digestive issues when brewed into a soothing, effective tea for digestion.

8. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the marshmallow plant's roots, contains a gelatinous substance known as mucilage.

This mucilage creates a protective coating on the gastrointestinal tract, easing irritation and reducing inflammation. As a result, it promotes digestion by soothing the stomach lining, allowing it to function optimally. Moreover, marshmallow tea's mild laxative effect further aids digestion by gently stimulating bowel movements, while its anti-inflammatory properties help in alleviating symptoms of digestive disorders such as leaky gut and Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS).

Ultimately, the numerous beneficial compounds in marshmallow tea work synergistically to enhance the digestive process and maintain overall gastrointestinal health.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in marshmallow tea that help with digestion.

  • Leaves: Rich in fiber, leaf tea aids digestion by supporting bowel regularity.
  • Stems: A natural source of flavonoids, stem tea facilitates nutrient absorption.

9. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a slick gel when mixed with water.

This gel moves through the digestive system, providing a protective coating for the digestive tract's lining. This coating soothes irritation, relieves inflammation, and alleviates digestive issues like indigestion, constipation, and stomach pain. Moreover, the gel's slippery texture facilitates the passage of food through the stomach and intestines, promoting regularity and overall digestive health.

Consequently, slippery elm tea's key component effectively addresses various digestive problems, ensuring a more comfortable and efficient digestive process.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in slippery elm tea that help with digestion.

  • Bark: The bark of the slippery elm tree is used to make medicinal tea for digestion due to its high mucilage content, which aids in soothing digestive irritation.
  • Inner Bark: The inner bark, in particular, contains beneficial properties that help protect and heal the gastrointestinal tract.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the slippery elm plant are used in teas for their anti-inflammatory properties, helping alleviate digestive discomfort.
  • Seeds: Slippery elm seeds are rich in antioxidants and contribute to soothing the digestive system when consumed as a tea.
