10 Best Herbal Teas For Cramps

Herbal teas, particularly those made from chamomile, peppermint, and ginger, are known to provide relief from cramps due to their antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These natural herbs help relax the smooth muscles in the uterus and digestive tract, thereby alleviating painful contractions and spasms. Additionally, they can stimulate blood flow and reduce inflammation, which further contributes to easing cramp-related discomfort. Furthermore, herbal teas are caffeine-free and rich in antioxidants, making them a healthier alternative to traditional teas and coffee.

The soothing warmth of the tea can also have a comforting effect on the body, promoting relaxation and stress reduction during menstruation or digestive issues.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea helps with cramps because it contains anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can reduce muscle tension and pain associated with menstrual cramps.

The active compounds in chamomile, including bisabolol and apigenin, work to relax the uterus and ease cramping. Drinking a cup of warm chamomile tea during menstruation can also help alleviate symptoms of bloating and gas, which can worsen cramps. Additionally, the calming effects of chamomile may help reduce stress and anxiety that can exacerbate cramping and discomfort.

Overall, chamomile tea is a natural and effective remedy for menstrual cramps due to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and relaxing properties.

The following procedure explaing how to make chamomile tea for cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool for a minute or two, so it's not boiling when you add the chamomile (this can damage the delicate compounds in the chamomile).
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers (or 1 chamomile tea bag) to a tea infuser or empty tea bag.
  3. Pour the hot water over the chamomile flowers and let them steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you like your tea.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag and discard the chamomile flowers.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and enjoy your soothing cup of chamomile tea for cramps.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with cramps because it contains gingerols and shgaols, two active compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the muscles, which can alleviate menstrual cramps. Additionally, ginger tea has been shown to relax smooth muscles in the digestive tract, making it an effective remedy for stomach cramps and spasms. Furthermore, ginger tea's warming properties can help to increase blood flow to the affected area, providing relief from cramping pain.

Lastly, consuming ginger tea regularly during menstruation can help to prevent cramps before they even start by keeping inflammation levels in check.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for cramps.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root (adjust based on personal preference), then bruise the slices with a heavy object to release their essential oils.
  2. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan, then add the bruised ginger slices and reduce heat to a simmer.
  3. Allow the ginger to simmer for 15-20 minutes to extract its beneficial compounds, creating a potent ginger tea.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the ginger slices, then add honey or lemon to taste (optional) and stir well.
  5. Sip the warm ginger tea slowly, allowing it to soothe menstrual cramps and provide relief. Enjoy up to three cups per day as needed.

3. Cramp Bark

Cramp bark tea helps with cramps because it contains an active ingredient called viburnum opulus, which is a natural muscle relaxant.

This herbal remedy has been used for centuries to alleviate menstrual cramps and muscle spasms, especially in the abdominal and uterine areas. The tea works by relaxing the muscles around blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing inflammation.

Additionally, cramp bark tea may help relieve pain by acting as a mild analgesic, providing additional comfort to those suffering from menstrual or muscle cramps.

The following procedure explaing how to make cramp bark tea for cramps.

  1. In a pot, add 1-2 teaspoons (2-4 grams) of dried cramp bark to 1 cup (250 mL) of boiling water.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the active compounds to be extracted.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the solid plant material, leaving only the liquid.
  4. Drink 1-2 cups of cramp bark tea per day, as needed, to help alleviate menstrual cramps.
  5. For best results, drink the tea regularly for several days leading up to and during menstruation.

4. Raspberry Leaf

Raspberry leaf tea helps with cramps because it contains high levels of magnesium, an essential mineral that plays a crucial role in muscle relaxation.

This property makes raspberry leaf tea particularly effective for menstrual cramps, as the muscles in the uterus are responsible for the painful contractions women experience during their periods. The tea's natural antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds also contribute to its ability to reduce inflammation and soothe cramping sensations in the body. Additionally, raspberry leaf tea has been used historically as a traditional remedy for labor pains, further highlighting its muscle-relaxing properties.

Regular consumption of this herbal tea can help alleviate menstrual cramps and provide relief for those who experience discomfort during their cycles.

The following procedure explaing how to make raspberry leaf tea for cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried raspberry leaf to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Once the water is boiling, pour it over the raspberry leaf and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea before pouring it into a cup.
  5. Drink up to 3 cups of raspberry leaf tea per day as needed to help alleviate menstrual cramps.

5. Fennel Seed

Fennel seed tea helps with cramps because it contains anethole, a compound known for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Anethole works by relaxing the muscles in the digestive tract and uterus, reducing the intensity and frequency of cramps. Additionally, fennel tea is a natural source of essential minerals like calcium, magnesium, and potassium, which are crucial for maintaining proper muscle function and preventing cramping. Furthermore, the warming properties of fennel tea can help increase blood flow to the affected area, providing additional relief.

Finally, studies have shown that fennel seed extract may be effective in reducing menstrual pain, including both cramps and bloating, making it a popular natural remedy for women's health concerns.

The following procedure explaing how to make fennel seed tea for cramps.

  1. Boil 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds in 1 cup of water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the seeds, leaving only the fennel-infused water.
  3. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, for flavor.
  4. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your mouth and throat. Drink up to three cups a day, as needed.

6. Turmeric

Turmeric tea helps with cramps because it contains curcumin, the primary active ingredient, which is a potent anti-inflammatory compound that reduces inflammation and pain associated with menstrual cramps.

Additionally, turmeric tea has antispasmodic properties, meaning it helps to relax smooth muscles in the uterus and digestive system, easing cramping and spasms. The tea's warm temperature also provides comfort and relaxation, making it a popular home remedy for menstrual discomfort.

Furthermore, turmeric tea may help alleviate symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS) such as bloating, mood swings, and breast tenderness, providing overall relief during menstruation.

The following procedure explaing how to make turmeric tea for cramps.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir in 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper (to enhance turmeric's absorption).
  3. Allow the mixture to simmer for about 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, then stir in 1 tablespoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice for taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed for cramps.

8. Willow Bark

Willow bark tea helps with cramps because it contains salicylic acid, a natural pain reliever and anti-inflammatory compound.

This herbal remedy has been used for centuries to alleviate menstrual cramps and muscle spasms. Salicylic acid works by blocking the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that cause inflammation and pain in the body. Drinking willow bark tea during menstruation can help reduce cramping and discomfort, allowing for a more enjoyable and productive experience.

Additionally, willow bark tea has antioxidant properties that help protect the body from cellular damage caused by free radicals, contributing to overall health and wellness.

The following procedure explaing how to make willow bark tea for cramps.

  1. Boil 1-2 teaspoons of dried willow bark in 8 ounces of water for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove any bark pieces.
  3. Let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon for taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of willow bark tea per day to help alleviate cramps.

9. Dandelion Root

Dandelion root tea helps with cramps because it contains potassium, magnesium, and vitamin B6, which are essential minerals for muscle function and relaxation.

Its mild diuretic effect encourages the body to eliminate excess fluid from tissues and reducing bloating and pressure that may worsen cramps. Additionally, dandelion root tea's anti-inflammatory properties help soothe irritated muscles and reduce inflammation that can contribute to cramping.

Moreover, drinking this tea regularly can help regulate menstrual cycles by balancing hormones, further reducing the likelihood of experiencing cramps.

The following procedure explaing how to make dandelion root tea for cramps.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried dandelion root and place in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water and pour over the dandelion root in the infuser or teapot.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes for optimal extraction of beneficial compounds.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea before pouring it into a cup.
  5. Enjoy the warm dandelion root tea up to three times a day as needed for cramp relief.

10. Lemongrass

Lemongrass tea helps with cramps because of its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can reduce inflammation and alleviate pain in the muscles and abdomen.

The essential oils present in lemongrass tea, particularly citral and geranial, have been shown to possess antispasmodic effects that help relax cramped muscles. Lemongrass tea also contains calcium, magnesium, and potassium, which are essential minerals for muscle function and may contribute to the relief of cramps by maintaining optimal muscle contraction and relaxation.

Furthermore, lemongrass tea is naturally caffeine-free and can be consumed in large quantities without causing dehydration or other side effects commonly associated with caffeinated beverages, making it an ideal choice for those experiencing menstrual cramps.

The following procedure explaing how to make lemongrass tea for cramps.

  1. Gather 1-2 fresh lemongrass stalks, 1 teaspoon of honey, and 1 cup of water.
  2. Rinse the lemongrass stalks thoroughly, then bruise and cut them into 2-inch pieces.
  3. Bring the water to a boil in a small saucepan, then add the lemongrass pieces.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the water to turn into a light brown color.
  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the lemongrass pieces, and stir in the honey until fully dissolved. Enjoy your homemade lemongrass tea for cramps!
