10 Best Herbal Teas For Cough

Herbal teas, such as chamomile, peppermint, and ginger, have long been used as home remedies for coughs due to their anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and soothing properties.

The warm liquid of herbal teas can help loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract, providing relief from a persistent cough. Some herbs, like thyme and licorice root, contain compounds that have been shown to relax the throat muscles and reduce coughing fits. Additionally, the calming effect of herbal teas can promote better sleep, which is crucial for the body's natural healing processes and overall immune function.

Lastly, herbal teas are caffeine-free and hydrating, making them a healthier alternative to over-the-counter cough suppressants and a more gentle choice for individuals with sensitive stomachs.

1. Marshmallow Root

Marshmallow root tea helps with cough because it contains mucilage, which coats and soothes the throat, reducing irritation and inflammation.

The anti-inflammatory properties of marshmallow root also help to alleviate chest congestion and coughing caused by respiratory issues. Marshmallow root tea is a natural demulcent, meaning it forms a protective layer over the mucous membranes in the throat and bronchial tubes, providing relief from persistent coughing. Furthermore, marshmallow root tea has antibacterial properties that can help fight off infections contributing to coughs, making it an effective remedy for both dry and productive coughs.

Lastly, marshmallow root tea is caffeine-free and naturally sweet, making it a soothing and comforting option for those suffering from a persistent cough.

The following procedure explaing how to make marshmallow root tea for cough.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried marshmallow root and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the marshmallow root in the teapot or teacup.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds from the marshmallow root.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to remove any solid particles.
  5. Drink the marshmallow root tea while warm, up to three times a day as needed to soothe a cough and promote respiratory health.

2. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with cough because It contains anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the throat and reduce inflammation caused by coughing.

Ginger also has antioxidants that help break down mucus in the respiratory system, making it easier to expel from the body. The warmth of ginger tea can help alleviate congestion in the chest, making it easier for individuals to breathe and cough effectively. Additionally, ginger tea acts as a natural expectorant, helping to loosen and bring up phlegm and other mucus from the lungs and airways.

Overall, ginger tea is an effective natural remedy for soothing a cough, reducing inflammation, breaking down mucus, alleviating congestion, and acting as an expectorant to help expel phlegm from the body.

The following procedure explaing how to make ginger tea for cough.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1-2 inches of fresh ginger root; add to a pot with 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the ginger and water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove pot from heat, and stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey and the juice of half a lemon.
  4. Let the mixture cool for a few minutes, then strain the tea into a cup, discarding the ginger pieces.
  5. Sip the ginger tea as needed to soothe a cough, or drink up to three times a day for relief.

3. Slippery Elm Bark

Slippery elm bark tea helps with cough because it contains mucilage, which coats and soothes the throat, reducing irritation and inflammation.

The demulcent properties of this herbal tea help to relieve dry coughs by providing a protective layer to the throat mucous membranes. Additionally, slippery elm bark tea has antitussive effects, meaning it helps to suppress coughing fits, giving relief from persistent and bothersome coughs. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties help to alleviate the underlying causes of a cough, such as infections or irritants, allowing for faster recovery.

Overall, slippery elm bark tea is an effective natural remedy for soothing coughs, providing both immediate relief and long-term healing benefits.

The following procedure explaing how to make slippery elm bark tea for cough.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried slippery elm bark and place it in a teapot or heat-resistant container.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the bark and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove any solid particles and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Add honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired, for added sweetness or flavor.
  5. Sip the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your throat and soothe your cough. Aim to drink 3-4 cups of slippery elm tea daily for best results.

4. Mullein Leaf

Mullein leaf tea helps with cough because it contains expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the irritated respiratory system, making it easier for mucus to be expelled from the lungs and airways.

Its antitussive effects help to reduce the frequency and intensity of coughing fits by coating the throat and calming irritated mucous membranes. The demulcent qualities of mullein leaf tea provide a soothing effect on the throat, reducing inflammation and providing relief from soreness and discomfort associated with persistent coughing. Additionally, mullein leaf tea may help to relieve bronchitis symptoms by acting as an antibacterial agent against certain strains of bacteria commonly found in respiratory infections.

Overall, the natural healing properties of mullein leaf tea make it an effective remedy for soothing a persistent cough and providing relief from associated symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make mullein leaf tea for cough.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water per 1 teaspoon of dried mullein leaf (approximately 2 grams) in a saucepan.
  2. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove any solid particles and discard them.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of mullein leaf tea per day, as needed, to help soothe a cough.

Overall, licorice root tea offers multiple benefits for those suffering from a persistent cough, making it a popular natural remedy for relief.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice root tea for cough.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the licorice root in the teapot or teacup with infuser.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the beneficial compounds and achieve the desired flavor.
  4. Remove the licorice root from the tea by lifting the infuser or pouring the tea through a strainer into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day, to help soothe a cough and relieve irritation in the throat.

6. Elderberry

Elderberry tea helps with cough because it contains flavonoids, which are powerful antioxidants that help reduce inflammation in the throat and respiratory system.

The tea's demulcent properties coat the throat, providing relief from irritation and soothing a persistent cough. Furthermore, elderberries have been shown to have expectorant effects, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the lungs, making it easier to cough up and eliminating the need to constantly suppress a cough.

Lastly, the tea's warm temperature can help soothe a sore throat and provide comfort, making it an effective natural remedy for relieving cough symptoms.

The following procedure explaing how to make elderberry tea for cough.

  1. Gather 2/3 cup of dried elderberries, 4 cups of water, 1-2 inches of fresh ginger (sliced), 1 cinnamon stick, and 1-2 tablespoons of honey.
  2. Combine the elderberries, water, ginger, and cinnamon stick in a medium saucepan, and bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan, and simmer the mixture for 30 minutes. After simmering, remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh strainer into a mug, discarding the solids. Stir in 1-2 tablespoons of honey, depending on your preferred level of sweetness.
  5. Sip the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day, to help soothe a cough and support immune function.

7. Thyme

Thyme tea helps with cough because of its natural expectorant properties, allowing the body to expel mucus from the respiratory system more effectively.

The volatile compounds in thyme, such as thymol and carvacrol, have antitussive effects that help soothe a persistent cough. Thyme tea also has antibacterial properties, which can help combat respiratory infections that may contribute to a cough. Additionally, the warm temperature of the tea provides comfort and relief for sore throats and irritated airways, making it an effective remedy for both the symptoms and underlying causes of a cough.

Lastly, thyme tea's calming effects on the nervous system can help reduce anxiety and stress, which can exacerbate coughing.

The following procedure explaing how to make thyme tea for cough.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on the stove.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried thyme leaves or 2-3 fresh thyme sprigs to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover the saucepan, and let the thyme steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the thyme leaves or sprigs.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and drink up to three times a day as needed for cough relief.

8. Eucalyptus

Eucalyptus tea helps with cough because it contains eucalyptol, a compound that acts as an expectorant and decongestant.

This means it thins mucus in the lungs making it easier to expel, while also reducing inflammation in the respiratory system that can contribute to a persistent cough. The soothing properties of the tea can help calm irritated throat tissues and reduce inflammation, providing relief for those suffering from a dry or productive cough. Furthermore, eucalyptus tea's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects may help fight off infections that can cause coughing, such as the common cold or flu.

Finally, the refreshing scent of eucalyptus tea can help alleviate congestion and open up airways, making it easier to breathe deeply and effectively expel mucus from the lungs.

The following procedure explaing how to make eucalyptus tea for cough.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a saucepan on medium heat.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried eucalyptus leaves (or 1 eucalyptus tea bag) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the leaves or tea bag.
  5. Sip the tea slowly while it's still warm, up to three times a day, as needed for cough relief.

9. Osha Root

Osha root tea helps with cough because it contains compounds that soothe irritated throat tissues and suppress coughing reflexes.

The plant, native to the northwestern United States and Mexico, has been used for centuries by Native American tribes for respiratory issues. Its active ingredients include alkaloids such as tannins and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce inflammation in the respiratory system. Additionally, osha root tea is rich in antioxidants that help to fight oxidative stress caused by pollution or environmental factors, further supporting respiratory health.

Overall, drinking osha root tea can provide relief for those suffering from a persistent cough due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects on the throat and respiratory system.

The following procedure explaing how to make osha root tea for cough.

  1. In a pot, boil 12 ounces of water and add 2 teaspoons of dried osha root; let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Strain the liquid to remove the osha root pieces, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  3. Add honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired, for added sweetness or to soothe a sore throat.
  4. Stir the mixture well to ensure even distribution of the added ingredients.
  5. Drink the tea up to three times a day, as needed, to help alleviate cough and other cold symptoms.

10. Coltsfoot

Coltsfoot tea helps with cough because it contains mucilage, which soothes the irritated throat tissues and coats the respiratory tract, reducing the frequency and severity of coughing fits.

Additionally, coltsfoot contains compounds such as flavonoids and polysaccharides that have anti-inflammatory properties, helping to reduce inflammation in the airways and alleviate symptoms associated with chest congestion. Coltsfoot tea also has expectorant qualities, making it effective at loosening and expelling mucus from the lungs and improving lung function. The herb's calming effects on the nervous system can further contribute to reducing coughing fits by reducing anxiety and stress.

Lastly, coltsfoot tea is a traditional remedy for respiratory issues such as bronchitis and pneumonia, and its medicinal properties have been supported by scientific research.

The following procedure explaing how to make coltsfoot tea for cough.

  1. Gather 2-3 dried coltsfoot leaves and rinse them under cold water to remove any dust or impurities.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot and add the cleaned coltsfoot leaves once the water reaches a rolling boil.
  3. Reduce heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal properties of the coltsfoot leaves.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the leaves and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Sip the coltsfoot tea slowly, 2-3 times a day, to help soothe a persistent cough and promote respiratory health.
