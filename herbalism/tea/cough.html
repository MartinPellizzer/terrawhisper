1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea helps with cough because it contains menthol, a substance with natural decongestant and expectorant properties that help to thin mucus and soothe inflamed throats, making it easier to expel phlegm and reduce the intensity and frequency of coughing fits.

The soothing warmth of the tea also helps to alleviate irritation in the throat, providing relief from irritants that can trigger coughing. Furthermore, peppermint tea has anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects that can help to reduce inflammation and pain in the respiratory tract, reducing the severity of cough and other cold and flu symptoms. Additionally, peppermint tea is caffeine-free, making it a gentle and relaxing way to hydrate and support the immune system, which is crucial for fighting off infections and promoting recovery.

Finally, peppermint tea has been used for centuries in traditional medicine as a natural remedy for cough, cold, and respiratory ailments, and its efficacy has been supported by some scientific studies, making it a popular and trusted choice for those seeking a natural way to manage cough and other symptoms of respiratory illness.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in peppermint tea that help with cough.

  1. Menthol: Acts as an expectorant, helping to thin and expel mucus, thus providing relief from cough.
  2. Mentone: Possesses anti-tussive properties, reducing the frequency and intensity of coughing.
  3. Menthyl acetate: Has calming and soothing effects, helping to alleviate irritation in the throat and suppress cough.
  4. Rosmarinic acid: Exhibits anti-inflammatory and anti-oxidant properties, aiding in reducing inflammation in the respiratory tract and improving respiratory symptoms, including cough.

The following list reveals what parts of peppermint are most commonly used to make medicinal tea for cough.

  1. Leaves: Rich in menthol and rosmarinic acid, peppermint leaves help soothe throat irritation and coughs by providing a cooling sensation and anti-inflammatory effects.
  2. Flowers: Packed with essential oils, peppermint flowers offer calming properties for a cough-ridden throat and promote expectoration of mucus.
  3. Stems: Containing similar compounds to leaves, peppermint stems contribute to a soothing tea that aids in relieving respiratory ailments, including persistent coughs.

The following procedure explains how to make peppermint tea for cough.

  1. In a small pot, boil 2 cups of water for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 heaped teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves to the boiling water.
  3. Let the peppermint leaves steep in the hot water for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a mug, discarding the spent peppermint leaves.
  5. Slowly sip the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day as needed.
