10 best herbal teas for cough

When it comes to herbal teas for cough, a few stand out for their soothing and healing properties. Thyme tea is a popular choice, as it helps to relax bronchial spasms and encourives the expulsion of mucus. Licorice root tea is another great option, as it soothes irritated throats and reduces inflammation. For a strong immune system boost, echinacea tea is an excellent choice. Lastly, slippery elm tea is known for its ability to coat and soothe irritated mucous membranes, providing relief from persistent coughing. These herbal teas can be enjoyed on their own or blended together for a powerful cough remedy.

1. Peppermint

Peppermint tea aids in cough relief due to its active ingredient, menthol, which possesses strong antitussive and expectorant properties. This organic compound works by thinning mucus, making it easier to expel, and by numbing the throat to reduce the cough reflex. Moreover, peppermint tea soothes inflammation in the respiratory tract, diminishing irritation that can trigger coughing fits. Furthermore, its warmth helps to alleviate congestion by increasing blood circulation to the chest area, promoting overall respiratory health. Overall, peppermint tea's menthol content and anti-inflammatory properties make it an effective natural remedy for cough suppression.

2. Licorice

Licorice tea provides relief from cough due to its active component, glycyrrhizin. This compound soothes irritation in the throat, reducing the urge to cough. Moreover, licorice tea liquefies mucus, making it easier to expel congestion. Additionally, it possesses anti-inflammatory properties that alleviate swelling in the throat, thereby decreasing coughing fits. Lastly, licorice tea's demulcent qualities coat the throat, creating a protective barrier against irritants, further diminishing coughing episodes.

3. Slippery Elm

Slippery elm tea provides relief from coughs due to its mucilage content, a substance that becomes a slick gel when combined with water. This gel creates a soothing barrier that covers and calms inflamed mucous membranes while easing irritation. Consequently, it reduces the urge to cough and alleviates discomfort in the throat. Slippery elm's demulcent properties promote the expulsion of stubborn mucus, facilitating expectoration and clearing airways. Furthermore, it provides a natural anti-inflammatory effect, diminishing inflammation in the respiratory tract and contributing to a decrease in coughing fits. Slippery elm tea's ability to assuage coughs is grounded in its unmatched lubricating and calming qualities, offering a time-tested solution for those seeking relief.

4. Marshmallow

Marshmallow tea, derived from the marshmallow plant's roots, contains a substance called mucilage. This mucilage turns into a slippery fluid upon contact with water, forming a soothing coating on the throat when consumed as tea. This layer helps to reduce irritation, calming coughs. Furthermore, this tea's demulcent properties ease inflammation in the throat and respiratory tract, alleviating the urge to cough. Marshmallow tea, therefore, offers an effective, natural remedy for coughs by forming a soothing barrier and decreasing throat inflammation.

5. Thyme

Thyme tea aids in cough relief due to its expectorant and bronchodilator properties, which liquefy mucus and widen airways. Its volatile oils, specifically thymol and carvacrol, contain antispasmodic and antitussive agents that soothe irritated throats and suppress coughing fits. Moreover, thyme tea provides soothing warmth that calms the respiratory system, reducing inflammation and irritation. Furthermore, it boasts antibacterial and antiviral properties, helping to combat infection and strengthen the immune system, thereby shortening the duration of a cough. Lastly, thyme tea's flavonoids offer antioxidant benefits, protecting lung tissue from oxidative stress and supporting overall respiratory health.

6. Mullein

Mullein tea helps with cough because it contains mucilaginous compounds that create a slick film over the throat, reducing irritation. This soothing film also relaxes the cough reflex, bringing immediate relief. Moreover, mullein tea has expectorant properties that promote the production of thin and loose mucus, aiding the body in expelling it. As an anti-inflammatory agent, mullein tea alleviates inflammation in the respiratory tract, further helping to calm cough. Lastly, mullein tea's demulcent properties soothe irritated mucous membranes, offering sustained relief from persistent cough.

7. Coltsfoot

Coltsfoot tea contains mucilage, a substance known for its soothing and inflammation-reducing properties. This mucilage, when in contact with water, turns into a slippery substance that coats the throat, providing immediate relief from irritation and coughing. Additionally, coltsfoot tea includes flavonoids, which possess expectorant qualities, helping to expel mucus from the respiratory tract. Furthermore, the tea's antispasmodic properties relax the muscles in the trachea, reducing the frequency and intensity of coughing fits. The demulcent and soothing effects of coltsfoot tea on the mucous membranes make it an effective remedy for a persistent cough.

8. Elecampane

Elecampane tea contains compounds called alantolactone and isoalantolactone, which have expectorant and anti-tussive properties. These substances relax tracheal muscles, soothe irritation, and help expel mucus, reducing the frequency and severity of coughing fits. Moreover, elecampane stimulates the production of fluid in the lungs, which further facilitates mucus clearance. Additionally, it demonstrates antibacterial and antifungal properties, helping combat respiratory infections that can cause coughing. Lastly, elecampane tea soothes inflammation in the mouth and throat, alleviating irritation and discomfort that accompany coughing.

9. Pleurisy

Pleurisy root tea aids in cough relief due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. This herbal tea promotes the loosening and expulsion of mucus from the respiratory tract, reducing irritation. The soothing effects of pleurisy root tea on irritated membranes alleviate coughing fits. Furthermore, this tea's antispasmodic qualities help relax the muscles in the respiratory system, minimizing coughing episodes. Lastly, pleurisy root tea contains compounds that have a calming effect on the nerves, reducing the frequency and intensity of coughing.

10. Grindelia

Grindelia tea contains compounds that relax bronchial spasms, soothe irritated throat tissues, and reduce inflammation, resulting in a cough decrease. This herbal tea's antitussive properties stem from its ability to form a protective coating on the throat lining, thereby alleviating irritation. Furthermore, Grindelia tea's expectorant effect promotes the expulsion of mucus from the respiratory tract, which assists in clearing the airways and diminishing coughing fits. The tea's flavonoids and terpenoids contribute to its calming effects on the respiratory system, further minimizing coughing. Lastly, Grindelia tea's astringent properties help tighten mucous membranes, providing additional relief from persistent coughs.
