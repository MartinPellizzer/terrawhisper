10 best herbal teas for infection

When it comes to herbal teas for infection, there are several options that are thought to be effective due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

For instance, echinacea tea is commonly used to boost the immune system and fight off infections, while goldenseal tea is known for its antimicrobial properties and ability to treat respiratory and digestive infections. Pau d'arco tea is another good option, as it has been shown to have antifungal, antibacterial, and antiviral properties. Lastly, slippery elm bark tea is often recommended for soothing inflamed tissues and helping the body expel mucus.

However, it's important to note that while these herbal teas can be helpful in supporting the body's natural defenses, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in severe cases of infection.

1. Echinacea

Echinacea tea boosts the immune system due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, promptly activating the body's natural defense mechanisms.

This herbal tea contains several active compounds, such as alkamides, glycoproteins, and polysaccharides, which are known to have immune-enhancing effects. Echinacea stimulates the production of white blood cells, empowering them to destroy harmful microorganisms and potentially shortening the duration of infections. Furthermore, echinacea inhibits the enzymes that bacteria need for propagation, preventing the spread of infection.

Finally, echinacea's anti-inflammatory properties aid in alleviating symptoms associated with infections, providing overall immune system support.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in echinacea tea that help with infection.

  • Roots: Contain high levels of antioxidants and have immune-boosting properties, making them effective for fighting infections.
  • Leaves: Rich in tannins that have astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and alleviate infection symptoms.
  • Flowers: Packed with antimicrobial and antiviral compounds, they aid in preventing and treating infections.
  • Buds: High in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents, buds help to strengthen the immune system and combat infections.
  • Seeds: Contain beneficial oils and acids that can help to reduce inflammation and fight off bacterial and viral infections.
