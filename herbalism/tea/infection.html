10 Best Herbal Teas For Infection

Herbal teas can help with infections due to their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Some herbs, like echinacea and goldenseal, contain compounds that can boost the immune system and help the body fight off infections. Certain herbal teas, such as those made from chamomile or lemon balm, have antioxidants that can help reduce inflammation and soothe irritation in the throat and mouth. Additionally, staying hydrated is crucial when fighting an infection, and sipping warm herbal tea can help maintain fluid intake and soothe symptoms like coughing and congestion.

However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can provide some relief and support the immune system, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment for severe or persistent infections.

1. Echinacea

Echinacea tea helps with infection because it contains active compounds, such as alkamides, cichoric acid, and echinacosides, which have been shown to stimulate the immune system and reduce inflammation.

These compounds can also help to relieve pain and reduce fever, making echinacea tea a popular remedy for treating colds, flu, and other respiratory infections. Additionally, echinacea has been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral properties, which can help to prevent the spread of infection and promote healing. The tea is made from the above-ground parts of the echinacea plant, including the flowers, leaves, and stems, which are rich in these beneficial compounds.

While echinacea tea is generally considered safe, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using it as a treatment for infection, especially for individuals with certain medical conditions or who are taking prescription medications.

The following procedure explains how to make echinacea tea for infection.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried echinacea flowers and/or leaves (or 2-3 teaspoons of fresh echinacea) and place them in a tea infuser or teapot with a built-in strainer.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the echinacea in the infuser or teapot.
  3. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal compounds.
  4. Remove the infuser or strain the tea to remove the plant material.
  5. Drink up to three cups of echinacea tea daily, as needed, to help fight off infection. Add honey or lemon for flavor, if desired.

2. Goldenseal

Goldenseal tea helps with infection because it contains berberine, a potent alkaloid compound with natural antibiotic, antiviral, and anti-inflammatory properties.

This compound can help to inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens, while also reducing inflammation and strengthening the immune system. Moreover, goldenseal tea is known to stimulate the production of white blood cells, which play a crucial role in fighting infections. Additionally, it can help to soothe mucous membranes, providing relief from symptoms associated with respiratory and digestive tract infections.

However, it is important to note that while goldenseal tea can be a helpful tool in managing infections, it should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment, especially in severe or chronic cases.

The following procedure explains how to make goldenseal tea for infection.

  1. In a pot, boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of dried goldenseal root, then let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let the mixture steep for an additional 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the goldenseal pieces.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for infection treatment.

3. Elderberry

Elderberry tea helps with infection because it contains antioxidants and vitamins that boost the immune system, helping the body fight off viruses and bacteria.

The elderberries used in the tea are rich in anthocyanins, which have been shown to have antiviral and antibacterial properties, further helping to combat infections. Additionally, elderberry tea can help reduce inflammation, providing relief for sore throats and easing congestion, common symptoms of respiratory infections. Furthermore, elderberry tea may help to shorten the duration of illnesses such as the common cold or flu, allowing for a quicker recovery.

Lastly, regular consumption of elderberry tea can help maintain a strong immune system, providing protection against future infections.

The following procedure explains how to make elderberry tea for infection.

  1. Rinse 2 cups of fresh elderberries, then simmer in 4 cups of water for 30 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove berries, and return liquid to the pot.
  3. Stir in 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 teaspoon of lemon juice, heating until honey is fully dissolved.
  4. Let the tea cool to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Sip the tea throughout the day for 3-5 days, or as needed, to help alleviate infection symptoms.

4. Ginger

Ginger tea helps with infection because it contains natural oils and compounds, such as gingerols and shaogals, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help the body fight off infections.

Additionally, ginger tea can help boost the immune system by increasing the production of immune cells, such as neutrophils and macrophages, which can help the body detect and destroy pathogens. Furthermore, ginger tea has been shown to inhibit the growth of various types of bacteria, including those that cause respiratory infections, and can help soothe a sore throat and alleviate coughing. The warm temperature of the tea can also help to relieve congestion and promote the elimination of mucus from the respiratory tract.

Lastly, ginger tea can help to reduce fever and alleviate pain, providing additional relief from the symptoms of infection.

The following procedure explains how to make ginger tea for infection.

  1. Peel and finely chop a 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, then steep in 4 cups of boiling water for 10 minutes; strain and optionally, add honey and lemon to taste for flavor and additional immune-boosting properties.
  2. Alternatively, grate the ginger and steep in the same proportions for a stronger tea with increased release of gingerols, the active compounds in ginger.
  3. To further enhance the tea's infection-fighting abilities, consider adding a cinnamon stick, a few cloves, or a teaspoon of raw honey during the steeping process.
  4. After straining, consume the tea while still warm for best results, and repeat as needed up to three times daily.
  5. For an on-the-go option, prepare the tea in a travel mug and sip throughout the day, reheating as necessary.

6. Clove

Clove tea helps with infection because it contains eugenol, a potent antimicrobial and antifungal compound that can help to kill or inhibit the growth of harmful microorganisms.

Additionally, clove tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce swelling and redness associated with infections. Furthermore, clove tea can help to boost the immune system, making it easier for the body to fight off infections. It also has antioxidant properties that can help to protect cells from damage caused by free radicals, which can weaken the immune system and make the body more susceptible to infections.

Finally, clove tea is a natural remedy that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat a variety of infections, including respiratory, digestive, and oral infections.

The following procedure explains how to make clove tea for infection.

  1. Crush 3-4 whole cloves and steep in boiling water for 10 minutes to extract the essential oils, making a potent clove tea.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the solid clove pieces, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable drink.
  3. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, to enhance the tea's flavor and soothe a sore throat.
  4. Sip the warm clove tea slowly, allowing it to coat your throat and mouth, promoting healing and providing relief from infection.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of clove tea daily for 3-5 days, or as needed, to help fight off the infection and support your immune system.

7. Cinnamon

Cinnamon tea helps with infection because it contains powerful antimicrobial properties, attributed mainly to its high concentration of antioxidants and essential oils.

The most potent of these oils, cinnamaldehyde, has been shown to inhibit bacterial growth and even destroy certain types of bacteria. Furthermore, cinnamon tea can help boost the immune system, making the body better equipped to fight off infections. Additionally, it's an anti-inflammatory agent, which can help reduce inflammation caused by infections.

However, it's important to note that while cinnamon tea can be a helpful adjunct, it shouldn't replace medical treatment for severe or persistent infections.

The following procedure explains how to make cinnamon tea for infection.

  1. In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil, then add 2 cinnamon sticks, reduce heat, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Remove from heat, add 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 teaspoon of lemon juice, stir until honey is dissolved.
  3. Cover and let steep for 10 minutes to allow the mixture to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Strain the tea to remove cinnamon sticks and any loose cinnamon pieces.
  5. Serve and enjoy your homemade cinnamon tea for infection. Drink 2-3 times a day.

8. Lemongrass

Lemongrass tea helps with infection because it contains a component called citral, which has antimicrobial properties that can help to inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and viruses.

Additionally, lemongrass is known for its immune-boosting capabilities, which can help the body to better fight off infections. The tea also has anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce inflammation and swelling associated with infections. Furthermore, lemongrass tea has been shown to help stimulate the lymphatic system, aiding in the removal of toxins and waste from the body, which can help to speed up the healing process.

Lastly, lemongrass tea is a natural diuretic, which can help to flush out toxins and bacteria from the body, further aiding in the prevention and treatment of infections.

The following procedure explains how to make lemongrass tea for infection.

  1. Crush 3-4 fresh lemongrass stalks (about 1/4 cup) to release their oils, then rinse and roughly chop; place in a pot and add 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer, covered, for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Turn off the heat and let the lemongrass tea steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove lemongrass pieces, then add honey or lemon to taste (optional).
  5. Drink the lemongrass tea while warm, up to three times a day, to help soothe and potentially treat the infection.

9. Oregano

Oregano tea helps with infection because it contains carvacrol and thymol, two compounds with antimicrobial properties that can help to fight off harmful pathogens.

Additionally, oregano has anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce swelling and pain associated with infections. Furthermore, oregano tea can help to boost the immune system, making it easier for the body to fight off infections. The tea can also help to soothe a sore throat and alleviate coughing, providing relief from symptoms of respiratory infections.

Lastly, oregano has been shown to have antioxidant properties, which can help to protect cells from damage caused by free radicals, and support the body's overall health and well-being.

The following procedure explains how to make oregano tea for infection.

  1. In a small pot, add 1 cup of water and bring it to a boil (3-4 minutes).
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried oregano leaves (or 2 teaspoons of fresh oregano) to the boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the oregano leaves.
  5. Sip the warm oregano tea, up to three times a day, for infection relief.

10. Thyme

Thyme tea helps with infection because it contains compounds called thymol and carvacrol, which have antimicrobial and antioxidant properties that can help to fight off infections.

Additionally, thyme tea has been shown to stimulate the production of white blood cells, which are crucial in defending the body against foreign invaders. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory effects can help to reduce swelling and pain associated with infections. Thyme tea is also a natural expectorant, which can help to clear mucus and phlegm from the respiratory tract, making it easier to breathe.

Finally, its soothing and calming properties can help to promote relaxation and rest, which are essential for the body's natural healing processes.

The following procedure explains how to make thyme tea for infection.

  1. Rinse and gently bruise 2-3 sprigs of fresh thyme (use about 1 tablespoon of crushed dried thyme leaves if fresh thyme is not available) to release its oils, then place them in a teapot.
  2. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the thyme in the teapot, then cover and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the thyme leaves or sprigs.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for flavor and additional health benefits.
  5. Drink the tea while still warm, up to three times a day, to help fight off the infection.
