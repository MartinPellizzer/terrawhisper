10 best herbal teas for female hormone balance

For females seeking to promote hormonal balance, certain herbal teas can be beneficial.

Chasteberry tea, also known as Vitex, is well-known for supporting hormonal health by potentially increasing luteinizing hormone and progesterone levels, while decreasing estrogen and follicle-stimulating hormone. Red clover tea, rich in isoflavones, can help balance estrogen levels, making it useful for menopausal women.

Another option is dandelion tea, which aids in liver function and hormone metabolism, facilitating the removal of excess hormones. Lastly, licorice root tea can help regulate hormones by inhibiting excess cortisol, which can interfere with other hormones. However, it should be used in moderation due to potential side effects like high blood pressure.

Always consult a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal remedies.

1. Chasteberry

Chasteberry tea promotes female hormone balance by reducing prolactin levels while increasing progesterone levels during the luteal phase of the menstrual cycle.

This herbal tea achieves hormonal balance by interacting with the pituitary gland, correcting any dysfunction that causes excessive prolactin production. Chasteberry contains diterpenes, such as pregnane glycosides, which exhibit dopaminergic activity, contributing to its hormone-regulating properties. Consequently, chasteberry tea alleviates menstrual irregularities, premenstrual syndrome (PMS), and postmenopausal symptoms.

Furthermore, this tea's hormone-balancing effects support fertility and breast milk production in lactating women.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chasteberry tea that help with female hormone balance.

  • Berries: The Chasteberry plant produces dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and phytonutrients, helping to support female hormone balance.
  • Flowers: The Chasteberry flowers contain iridoid glycosides, which help regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS).
  • Seeds: Chasteberry seeds are the primary source of beneficial compounds, including agnusides and flavonoids, known to promote hormonal balance in women.

2. Red Clover

Red clover tea contains isoflavones, a type of plant estrogen, which resemble human estrogen and can bind to estrogen receptors in the body.

This action helps regulate hormonal imbalances by either blocking or mimicking the effects of natural estrogen, depending on the body's needs. Furthermore, red clover tea can reduce symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and mood swings, by restoring hormonal equilibrium. Additionally, it provides relief from premenstrual syndrome (PMS) symptoms due to its ability to balance estrogen and progesterone levels.

Rich in nutrients like vitamins and minerals, red clover tea supports overall female endocrine health, enhancing its role in hormone balance.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in red clover tea that help with female hormone balance.

  • Leaves: Contain plant estrogens, supporting healthy menstrual cycle and alleviating menopause symptoms.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential fatty acids, they promote hormonal health and improve skin condition.

3. Dong Quai

Dong quai tea benefits female hormone balance due to its estrogen-like properties, which make it a natural phytoestrogen.

This natural herb contains compounds that mimic the function of estrogen in the body, helping to replace the estrogen lost during menopause. Dong quai tea also alleviates symptoms of hormonal imbalance by regulating menstrual cycles and easing menstrual cramps, thereby promoting overall reproductive health. Additionally, it contains vitamins and minerals essential for hormonal balance, such as vitamin B12, magnesium, and niacin.

Lastly, dong quai tea has a long history of use in Traditional Chinese Medicine, demonstrating its effectiveness in addressing various hormonal issues experienced by women.

  • Rhizomes: High in coumarins and ferulic acid, dong quai rhizomes possess blood-moving properties that help alleviate menstrual discomfort and promote hormone balance.
  • Leaves: Containing phytoestrogens and antioxidants, dong quai leaves support hormone equilibrium and offer protective benefits for female reproductive health.
  • Flowers: Abundant in essential oils and vitamins, dong quai flowers help balance female hormones and soothe symptoms associated with menstrual and menopausal stages.

Additionally, it facilitates liver function, which is crucial for the metabolism and excretion of hormones, further contributing to female hormone balance.

Furthermore, milk thistle tea's anti-inflammatory effects can alleviate menstrual-related symptoms, which often stem from hormonal imbalances.

8. Black Cohosh

Black cohosh tea contains active compounds, including triterpene glycosides and alkaloids, that interact with the human body to promote hormonal balance.

For women, black cohosh tea aids in maintaining estrogen levels, offering relief from symptoms associated with menstrual cycles and menopause. Research reveals that black cohosh tea inhibits the enzyme aromatase, reducing the conversion of androgens into estrogens.

Lastly, black cohosh tea's hormonal balancing properties stem from its antileutropic and antispasmodic effects, providing soothing relief for women experiencing imbalanced hormonal issues.
