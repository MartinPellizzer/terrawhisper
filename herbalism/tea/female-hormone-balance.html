10 Best Herbal Teas For Female Hormone Balance

Herbal teas can help promote female hormone balance due to the natural phytoestrogens found in certain herbs, which can mildly mimic the effect of estrogen in the body.

For instance, chasteberry tea is known to help regulate menstrual cycles and reduce symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS) by supporting the pituitary gland and balancing hormone production. Red clover tea is another option that contains isoflavones, a type of phytoestrogen, which can help alleviate hot flashes and other menopause symptoms.

Ginkgo biloba tea may also aid in hormonal balance by improving circulation and reducing symptoms of premenstrual dysphoric disorder (PMDD). Lastly, dong quai tea has been used in traditional Chinese medicine to balance hormones, regulate menstrual cycles, and alleviate menopause symptoms.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any herbal tea regimen, especially when trying to manage hormonal imbalances.

1. Chasteberry

Chasteberry tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains a compound called agnuside, which is thought to influence the pituitary gland and regulate the release of hormones such as follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH).

These hormones play a crucial role in the menstrual cycle and ovulation. Chasteberry may also increase progesterone levels and decrease estrogen levels, helping to restore hormonal balance in women with conditions such as polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) or endometriosis. Additionally, chasteberry has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can help alleviate symptoms associated with hormonal imbalances such as menstrual cramps and breast tenderness.

However, it's important to note that chasteberry may interact with certain medications and should be used under the guidance of a healthcare provider.

The following procedure explaing how to make chasteberry tea for female hormone balance.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling, then pour over 1-2 teaspoons of dried chasteberry fruit (Vitex agnus-castus) and steep, covered, for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea to remove the chasteberry pieces.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  4. Drink 1-2 cups of chasteberry tea daily, preferably between meals.
  5. Continue use for several months for best results, but discontinue use if menstrual bleeding becomes prolonged or heavy.

2. Red Clover

Red clover tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains isoflavones, a type of plant estrogen that can mimic and stimulate the effects of estrogen in the body.

These isoflavones can help regulate hormonal fluctuations in women by binding to estrogen receptors, which can help alleviate symptoms of hormonal imbalance such as hot flashes, mood swings, and irregular menstrual cycles. Additionally, red clover tea has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can further support overall hormonal health and balance. However, it's important to note that red clover tea should not be used as a sole treatment for hormonal imbalances or disorders, and individuals should consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating it into their routine.

Furthermore, red clover tea may interact with certain medications, so it's crucial to discuss any potential risks or concerns with a healthcare provider before use.

The following procedure explaing how to make red clover tea for female hormone balance.

  1. Gather 1-2 teaspoons of dried red clover flowers and steep in hot water for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove plant material.
  3. Optional: Add honey or lemon for flavor, as desired.
  4. Drink up to three cups a day, between meals, for hormone balance.
  5. Continue consumption for several months for best results, adjusting intake based on the guidance of a healthcare provider.

3. Dong Quai

Dong quai tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains phytoestrogens, plant compounds that mimic the structure and function of estrogen, the primary female sex hormone.

These phytoestrogens can help regulate menstrual cycles, reduce menopausal symptoms, and alleviate hormonal imbalances by binding to estrogen receptors and stimulating hormonal activity. Additionally, dong quai has been found to support liver function, which aids in the metabolism and elimination of excess hormones, further contributing to hormonal balance. Furthermore, this herbal tea may help reduce inflammation and improve blood flow, both of which can positively impact female reproductive health and overall hormonal well-being.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare provider before consuming dong quai tea, especially for those with specific medical conditions or taking certain medications, as it can have interactions and side effects.

The following procedure explaing how to make dong quai tea for female hormone balance.

  1. In a pot, add 1 quart of water and 4-6 thin slices of fresh Dong Quai root (also known as Chinese Angelica root), simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Turn off heat, add 1 teaspoon of dried Peppermint leaves, steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea, discard the solids.
  4. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups a day, preferably between meals.

4. Milk Thistle

Milk thistle tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains a powerful antioxidant called silymarin, which aids in the detoxification of the liver, promoting the proper metabolism of hormones.

This herbal tea can help reduce estrogen dominance by inhibiting the conversion of other hormones to estrogen, thus restoring the balance between estrogen and progesterone. Furthermore, milk thistle tea can alleviate symptoms of hormonal imbalance, such as mood swings, bloating, and irritability, by promoting a stable hormonal environment. Additionally, it may help improve insulin sensitivity, reducing the risk of hormone-related issues like polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).

Lastly, milk thistle's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties can support overall reproductive health, contributing to a balanced hormonal system in women.

The following procedure explaing how to make milk thistle tea for female hormone balance.

  1. Steep 1 teaspoon of crushed milk thistle seeds in 8 ounces of boiling water for 10-15 minutes; strain and discard seeds.
  2. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for improved flavor.
  3. Drink 2-3 cups of milk thistle tea daily, preferably between meals, for optimal hormone balancing effects.
  4. Consult a healthcare provider before using milk thistle if pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking medication.
  5. For best results, combine milk thistle tea with a balanced diet, regular exercise, and stress management techniques.

5. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains compounds that can help reduce cortisol levels and promote the healthy functioning of the endocrine system, which is responsible for hormone production.

It may also help regulate thyroid function, which can have a significant impact on hormonal balance. Additionally, ashwagandha has been shown to reduce stress and anxiety, which can contribute to hormonal imbalance. Furthermore, it may help to balance levels of progesterone and estrogen, two hormones that are crucial for reproductive and menstrual health.

However, it's important to note that more research is needed to fully understand the effects of ashwagandha on female hormone balance.

The following procedure explaing how to make ashwagandha tea for female hormone balance.

  1. In a tea pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 teaspoon of crushed ashwagandha root, and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of honey and 1/2 teaspoon of lemon juice to a cup for sweetening and vitamin C.
  3. Strain the ashwagandha tea from the tea pot into the cup.
  4. Warm up 1/2 cup of milk (cow, almond, or coconut) and add it to the tea for soothing properties.
  5. Stir well and enjoy this hormone-balancing ashwagandha tea. (Optional: add a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg for extra flavor)

6. Burdock

Burdock tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains high levels of phytoestrogens, plant-based compounds that mimic the structure and function of estrogen in the body.

These phytoestrogens can help to bind to estrogen receptors, promoting healthy hormone levels and alleviating symptoms of hormonal imbalance. Additionally, burdock tea has been shown to support liver function, which is crucial for the metabolism and elimination of hormones from the body.

By promoting liver health, burdock tea can help to prevent the buildup of excess hormones and reduce the risk of hormonal imbalances. Furthermore, burdock tea contains anti-inflammatory compounds that can help to reduce inflammation and promote overall hormonal health.

Finally, burdock tea has been used in traditional medicine to regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate menstrual symptoms, further supporting its role in female hormone balance.

The following procedure explaing how to make burdock tea for female hormone balance.

  1. Thoroughly rinse and chop 2 tablespoons of dried burdock root (Arctium lappa), then place it in a pot and add 4 cups of water; bring it to a boil with the lid on.
  2. Once boiling, reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the solids and discard them.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of warm burdock tea daily, preferably between meals, for female hormone balance.

7. Licorice

Licorice tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which can help the body to produce and regulate hormones.

This is especially beneficial for women, as the tea can help to balance levels of estrogen and progesterone. Additionally, licorice tea has been shown to have anti-androgenic effects, which can help to reduce the production of testosterone and other androgens in women. Furthermore, the tea can help to support the adrenal glands, which are responsible for producing a variety of hormones, including sex hormones.

However, it is important to note that licorice tea should be used in moderation, as excessive consumption can lead to side effects such as high blood pressure and hormonal imbalances.

The following procedure explaing how to make licorice tea for female hormone balance.

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 tablespoon of dried licorice root, 1 cup of water, and honey (to taste).
  2. Crush the licorice root to increase its surface area and potency, then add it to the water in a pot.
  3. Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root, then add honey to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for hormone balance.

8. Black Cohosh

Black cohosh tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains phytoestrogens, which are plant compounds that can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

These phytoestrogens can help to regulate menstrual cycles, reduce hot flashes, and alleviate symptoms of menopause by binding to estrogen receptors in the body. Additionally, black cohosh has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can help to reduce pain and discomfort associated with menstruation and menopause. It is important to note, however, that the exact mechanisms of how black cohosh affects hormone balance are not fully understood, and more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness and safety.

As with any supplement, it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before starting black cohosh tea or any other herbal remedy.

The following procedure explaing how to make black cohosh tea for female hormone balance.

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried black cohosh root and place it in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  2. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the black cohosh root and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the used black cohosh root.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste for flavor.
  5. Drink up to three cups of black cohosh tea daily, between meals, for female hormone balance.

9. Vitex

Vitex tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains active compounds that regulate hormone production, particularly progesterone, by supporting the pituitary gland's function in releasing hormones.

This results in a balancing effect on the entire endocrine system. Vitex tea is also known to stimulate and support the production of luteinizing hormone (LH), which in turn boosts progesterone levels during the second half of a woman's menstrual cycle. Furthermore, vitex tea can decrease estrogen levels when they are too high, while increasing them when they are too low, ultimately promoting overall hormonal balance.

Additionally, vitex tea can alleviate symptoms associated with hormonal imbalance, such as menstrual irregularities, mood swings, and breast tenderness, by promoting a healthier hormonal environment in the body.

The following procedure explaing how to make vitex tea for female hormone balance.

  1. Heat 1 cup of water to boiling point (approximately 212 degrees Fahrenheit) and pour over 1-2 teaspoons of dried Vitex (Chasteberry) flowers/berries; let steep for 10-15 minutes (adjust dosage and steeping time according to personal taste and herbalist's recommendation).
  2. Strain the tea to remove the Vitex herb, discarding the solid material.
  3. Optional: add honey or lemon to taste for flavor enhancement.
  4. Drink 1-2 cups of Vitex tea daily, preferably between meals for optimal absorption and effectiveness.
  5. Continue use for several months for best results, but discontinue use if any adverse reactions occur or if pregnancy is suspected. Consult a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal regimen.

10. Hops

Hops tea helps with female hormone balance because it contains phytoestrogens, plant compounds that can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

These phytoestrogens can help regulate hormonal fluctuations by binding to estrogen receptors, which can be particularly beneficial for women experiencing symptoms of menopause or PMS. Additionally, hops have been found to have calming and sleep-promoting properties, which can indirectly support hormonal balance by reducing stress and improving sleep quality.

Furthermore, hops may also possess mild anti-androgenic effects, which can help restore hormonal balance in women with conditions related to androgen excess. However, it is important to note that hops should not be used as a sole treatment for hormonal imbalances and that consulting with a healthcare professional is recommended for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Lastly, while hops tea is generally considered safe, it can have sedative effects and should be consumed in moderation, especially when operating heavy machinery or driving.

The following procedure explaing how to make hops tea for female hormone balance.

  1. Bring 1 cup of water to a boil, then add 1 teaspoon of dried hops flowers (Humulus lupulus), reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 15 minutes.
  2. Turn off heat and let the tea steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the hops flowers.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day for hormone balancing benefits.
