10 best herbal teas for gastroenteritis

Ginger and chamomile teas are often recommended as soothing options for individuals with gastroenteritis, also known as the stomach flu.

Ginger tea has anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce irritation in the stomach and alleviate symptoms like nausea and vomiting. Chamomile tea, on the other hand, possesses calming effects that can help relax the digestive muscles and reduce inflammation.

Both teas can help rehydrate the body, which is crucial during bouts of gastroenteritis. However, it's important to note that while herbal teas can provide relief, they should not replace medical treatment.

If symptoms persist or worsen, consult a healthcare professional.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea alleviates gastroenteritis through its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

This soothing herbal tea contains bisabolol and volatile oils, which relieve inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract and reduce muscle spasms. Additionally, its astringent qualities help minimize diarrhea. Furthermore, chamomile's drying effect on mucous membranes assists in eliminating excessive mucus in the intestines.

Therefore, chamomile tea brings relief and healing to those suffering from gastroenteritis.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with gastroenteritis.

  • Flowers: The chamomile flowers contain anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help alleviate inflammation and muscle spasms in the digestive tract, providing relief from gastroenteritis symptoms.
  • Leaves: Rich in calming compounds, chamomile leaves help soothe an upset stomach, reducing symptoms of gastroenteritis.
  • Seeds: Chamomile seeds are a source of antioxidants and essential oils that aid in digestion and help reduce inflammation, making them beneficial for gastroenteritis treatment.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for gastroenteritis.

  1. Measure - Boil 1 cup of water and let 1 chamomile tea bag steep for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Squeeze - Once steeped, gently squeeze the tea bag to release any remaining chamomile extract.
  3. Sweeten - Stir in 1 teaspoon of honey for added anti-inflammatory properties and soothe the throat (optional).
  4. Dilute - If the tea is too strong, dilute it with an equal amount of hot water.
  5. Serve - Pour the chamomile tea into a cup and enjoy while warm, up to three times a day.

3. Ginger

Ginger tea provides relief from gastroenteritis due to its carminative and antiemetic properties, which mean it helps expel intestinal gas and alleviates nausea.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, possess anti-inflammatory qualities that soothe irritated stomach lining. Furthermore, ginger stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, promoting faster recovery.

Additionally, its antimicrobial properties can combat the infection-causing bacteria or viruses in the gut, shortening the duration of gastroenteritis symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with gastroenteritis.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger's thick underground stem, or rhizome, contains powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds, making it ideal for soothing digestive discomforts caused by gastroenteritis.
  • Leaves: Rich in mineral content and volatile oils, ginger leaves provide relief from gastrointestinal ailments, as they stimulate digestion and alleviate nausea.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers contain medicinal properties that aid in treating various stomach disorders; they help reduce inflammation, cramping, and nausea associated with gastroenteritis.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds hold essential oils and bioactive compounds, offering quick relief from gastrointestinal issues due to their carminative and antispasmodic effects, lessening abdominal pain and bloating.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for gastroenteritis.

  1. Peel and thinly slice 1 inch of fresh ginger root.
  2. In a saucepan, combine ginger slices with 4 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer, covered, for 15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the ginger pieces.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste and drink while warm, up to three times a day.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tea for gastroenteritis.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of licorice root, then boil for 10 minutes.
  2. After boiling, remove the pot from heat and let it steep for 10 more minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the licorice root.
  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, up to three times a day.

  • Seeds: Slippery elm seeds contain mucilage that forms a soothing gel when mixed with water, making it helpful for treating gastrointestinal inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic slippery elm tea for gastroenteritis.

  1. In a cup, pour 8 ounces of boiling water over 1 tablespoon of dried slippery elm bark.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Stir the tea to help eliminate any thick residue.
  4. Strain the tea to remove any bark particles.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, up to 3 times a day.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for gastroenteritis.

  1. In a small pot, combine 1 cup of water and 2.5 centimeters of fresh marshmallow root, then simmer for 15 minutes to release the active compounds.
  2. Turn off the heat, add 1 teaspoon of chamomile flowers, and let the mixture steep for an additional 10 minutes to enhance the soothing properties.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup, discarding the solids.
  4. Optionally, add honey to taste, stirring until fully dissolved, for added antimicrobial benefits and a touch of sweetness.
  5. Sip the marshmallow tea slowly, allowing it to coat your digestive tract and help ease gastroenteritis symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic dandelion tea for gastroenteritis.

  1. Gather 2-3 dandelion heads and rinse them thoroughly under running water to remove dirt and bugs.
  2. Separate the bright yellow petals from the green base and place them in a tea infuser or reusable tea bag.
  3. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool for 2-3 minutes before pouring it over the dandelion petals.
  4. Steep the petals in hot water for 5-10 minutes, then discard the used petals.
  5. Drink the tea up to three times a day for gastroenteritis relief.

  4. Optional: Add honey or lemon for flavor.
  5. Drink the tea 3 times a day, between meals, to soothe gastroenteritis symptoms.
