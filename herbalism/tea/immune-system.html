Elderberry tea bolsters the immune system due to its abundance in antioxidants, which combat damaging free radicals.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic elderberry tea for immune system.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of dried elderberries, 3 cups of water, and 2 tablespoons of honey.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 30 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or heatproof jar, pressing on the solids to extract all liquid.
  4. Discard the solids and let the tea cool to drinkable temperature.
  5. Serve and enjoy up to 3 cups per day for immune support.

Lastly, reishi mushroom tea is rich in triterpenes, compounds that exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities, contributing to the tea's overall immunity-strengthening effects.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in reishi mushroom tea that help with immune system.

  • Polysaccharides: Stimulate immune system cells, enhancing their activity and ability to fight off pathogens.

  • Nucleosides: Regulate immune response, aiding in the body's defense against infection and disease.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic reishi mushroom tea for immune system.

  1. In a pot, combine 4 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried reishi mushroom pieces, and bring to a boil.

  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired, and let the tea cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Serve the reishi mushroom tea and enjoy its immune-boosting benefits.
