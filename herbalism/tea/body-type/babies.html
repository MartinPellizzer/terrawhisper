10 best herbal teas for babies

Consider chamomile, lemon balm, and fennel when selecting herbal teas for babies.

Chamomile is known for its calming properties, which can help soothe a fussy baby. Lemon balm has natural anti-viral and anti-bacterial properties, making it a great choice for when your baby is feeling under the weather. Fennel tea can aid in digestion and alleviate symptoms of gas and colic.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before introducing new substances to your baby's diet.

1. Chamomile

Chamomile tea brings a time-tested solution for babies' discomfort.

Its anti-inflammatory properties, derived from the chamomile flower, aid in soothing digestive issues like colic and teething pain. Furthermore, chamomile's natural calming and sedative effects help promote better sleep for babies. Chamomile tea's antispasmodic agents alleviate gas and stomach cramps, making it an ideal natural remedy.

Lastly, the absence of caffeine in chamomile tea ensures that babies enjoy a safe and sound slumber.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in chamomile tea that help with babies.

  • Flowers: Contain calming properties that help soothe babies and promote sleep.
  • Leaves: Known for their anti-inflammatory effects, reducing redness and irritation in babies.
  • Seeds: Rich in antioxidants, seeds support a healthy immune system in infants.
  • Stems: Provide gentle relief for baby's digestive system, aiding in issues like colic and indigestion.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in chamomile tea that help with babies.

  • Apigenin: A flavonoid that possesses anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, helping to soothe colic and reduce inflammation in infant digestive system.
  • Chamazulene: A chemical compound with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, aiding in relieving pain and discomfort caused by teething and digestive issues.
  • Bisabolol: A terpene alcohol known for its calming, anti-inflammatory, and skin-healing properties, useful for treating minor irritations, diaper rash, and promoting better sleep.
  • Matricin: A precursor to chamazulene, matricin contributes to chamomile's anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving actions, beneficial for addressing skin irritations and digestive discomforts.
  • Hippurate: A natural compound derived from amino acids, hippurate exhibits antibacterial properties, assisting in protecting babies from infections and promoting overall health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile tea for babies.

  1. Measure one cup of water and bring to a boil.
  2. Add one teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers to a tea infuser.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the chamomile flowers and steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser to prevent over-steeping.
  5. Allow the tea to cool to a safe temperature before serving it to your baby.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic fennel tea for babies.

Ginger tea offers several benefits that make it helpful for babies.

First, ginger contains active compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, known to alleviate gastrointestinal discomforts, like colic and gas. Second, its anti-inflammatory properties soothe irritation in the digestive tract, promoting smoother digestion.

Right below you will find a list of the most important active constituents in ginger tea that help with babies.

  • Rhizomes: Contain high levels of gingerols, which provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, beneficial for soothing baby's digestive system.
  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and minerals, promoting healthy growth and boosting the immune system of babies.
  • Flowers: High in antioxidants and vitamins, supporting a healthy inflammatory response, and contributing to overall well-being.

  • Gingerols: Act as powerful anti-inflammatory agents, helping to soothe inflammation in babies' delicate digestive tracts.
  • Shogaols: Exhibit antiemetic properties, reducing the likelihood of vomiting and nausea in babies.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for babies.

  1. Peel and finely grate 1 teaspoon of fresh ginger root.
  2. Combine grated ginger, 1 cup of water, and a pinch of organic honey in a small saucepan.
  3. Simmer the mixture over medium heat for 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, then let it cool to a safe temperature for your baby.
  5. Serve the tea to your baby in a feeding bottle or sippy cup.

  2. Strain the tea to remove leaves, then let it cool to body temperature.
  3. In a blender, puree 1/2 cup of fresh raspberries with 1/4 cup of the cooled tea.
  4. Pour the raspberry mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove raspberry seeds.
  5. Serve the raspberry tea to your baby immediately, at body temperature.

What is babies and how can it affect your life?

Babies are newborn or very young children who have recently entered the world.

They bring immense joy, love, and purpose to people's lives. As new family members, they strengthen emotional bonds and create a sense of responsibility among their caregivers. Additionally, they contribute to personal growth as individuals learn to care for and nurture a new life.

Undoubtedly, the arrival of a baby brings about significant changes and shapes the lives of those around them, often for the better.

What other natural remedies help with babies?

For babies, some of the most common and effective natural remedies include breastmilk, probiotics, and essential oils.

Breastmilk is a powerful natural remedy that provides numerous health benefits for babies. It contains antibodies that help protect babies from illness and infection, as well as vitamins, protein, and fat that support their growth and development. Probiotics are beneficial bacteria that promote a healthy gut and support the immune system.

They can be given to babies in powder or liquid form, and are often recommended to help prevent or treat digestive issues such as colic, diarrhea, and constipation. Essential oils, such as lavender and chamomile, can also be used to soothe and calm babies. They can be diluted and applied topically to the skin, or used in a diffuser to help babies sleep and relax.

These natural remedies are safe, gentle, and effective ways to support babies' health and well-being.
