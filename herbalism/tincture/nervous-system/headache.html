1. Feverfew tincture

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic feverfew tincture for headaches.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of feverfew plant, fresh or dried, into small pieces.
  2. Submerge the chopped feverfew in a clean jar, filling it with 8 ounces of 80-proof vodka.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for two to four weeks, shaking it daily.
  4. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the excess liquid.
  5. Store the resulting tincture in a tightly sealed glass container, in a cool, dark place, for up to one year. Take 30 drops every few hours as needed for headaches.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic willow tincture for headaches.

The primary causes of headaches treatable with herbal tincture include tension, sinus congestion, and inflammation.

These headaches often result from stress, allergies, or muscle strain. Tension headaches cause a constant ache or pressure on both sides of the head, sometimes accompanied by a tender scalp, neck, and shoulder muscles.

Sinus headaches are characterized by a deep and constant pain in the cheekbones, forehead, or bridge of the nose. Inflammatory headaches are often due to inflammation in the body, such as from an infection or autoimmune disorder.

Herbal tinctures can help alleviate these symptoms by reducing tension, clearing nasal passages, and fighting inflammation.
