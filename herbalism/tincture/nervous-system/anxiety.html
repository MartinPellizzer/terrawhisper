1. Valerian tincture

Valerian tincture helps with anxiety because it contains valuable compounds, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, that have a calming effect on the brain.

These compounds bind to receptors in the brain responsible for regulating anxiety and relaxation, promoting a sense of tranquility and reducing symptoms of anxiety. Moreover, valerian tincture increases the production of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that inhibits nerve cells from overfiring, leading to a reduction in anxiety levels.

Regular intake of valerian tincture has been shown to improve anxiety symptoms, promoting a sense of well-being and reducing feelings of stress and tension.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian that aid with anxiety.

  • Mild sedative properties: Catnip has mild sedative properties due to the presence of certain compounds such as nepetalactone and valepotriates, which can help promote relaxation and reduce anxiety.
  • Nervine effects: Catnip has nervine effects, meaning it can soothe and support the nervous system, which can be beneficial for people experiencing anxiety. This is due to the presence of essential oils such as nepetalactone and geraniol.
  • Anxiolytic effects: Catnip has anxiolytic effects, meaning it can reduce anxiety and promote feelings of calm. This is due to the presence of nepetalactone and other compounds that bind to GABA receptors in the brain, which can help reduce anxiety.
  • Muscle relaxant properties: Catnip has muscle relaxant properties due to the presence of compounds like nepetalactone and valepotriates, which can help alleviate physical tension that can accompany anxiety.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in valerian that help with anxiety.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tincture for anxiety.

  1. Measure 1 ounce of dried valerian root, chop it finely, and place it in a clean jar.
  2. Pour 5 ounces of vodka or rum (80 proof) over the valerian root, ensuring all pieces are covered.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  4. After the extraction period, strain the mixture using a cheesecloth or a fine mesh strainer, squeezing to release all liquid.
  5. Transfer the liquid (valerian tincture) to a dark glass bottle, label it, and store it in a cool, dark place. Use within 2 years.
