1. Licorice

Licorice tincture helps with sore throat because it contains glycyrrhizin, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties.

  • Expectorant: Red clover stimulates the expulsion of mucus from the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel thick mucus and reducing irritation in the throat.
  • Analgesic: Red clover contains several active compounds that have been found to have analgesic properties, which can help alleviate pain and discomfort associated with a sore throat.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in licorice that help with sore throat.

  • Roots: Contain glycyrrhizin, which soothes inflammation and relieves pain in a sore throat.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, helping to reduce irritation and fight off infections causing sore throat.
  • Leaves: Possess anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, alleviating congestion and soothing a sore throat.
  • Seeds: Contain essential oils and antioxidants that help to fight infections and reduce swelling in a sore throat.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice for sore throat.

  1. Chop 1 cup of dried licorice root into small pieces and combine with 2 cups of water in a saucepan.
  2. Simmer the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, then strain and discard the solids.
  3. Measure the remaining liquid and add an equal amount of vodka or other high-proof alcohol.
  4. Seal the mixture in a glass jar and store in a cool, dark place for 2 weeks, shaking occasionally.
  5. Strain the tincture through cheesecloth and store in a sealed glass bottle. Adults can take 1-2 dropperfuls up to 4 times daily as needed for sore throat relief.
