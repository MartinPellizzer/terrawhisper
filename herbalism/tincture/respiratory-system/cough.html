1. Elderberry

Elderberry tincture helps with cough because it contains active compounds that have been shown to reduce inflammation and mucus production in the respiratory tract.

These compounds include anthocyanins, flavonoids, and other polyphenols, which have been found to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties. By alleviating inflammation and congestion, elderberry tincture can effectively soothe a persistent cough, providing relief to individuals suffering from respiratory ailments or the common cold.

The expectorant properties of elderberry further enhance its effectiveness in treating coughs, as it helps expel mucus and phlegm from the respiratory system.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in elderberry that aid with cough.

  • Anti-tussive: Wild indigo contains alkaloids that help to suppress coughing by reducing the frequency and intensity of coughing spasms.
  • Expectorant: The plant contains compounds that help to thin and loosen mucus in the airways, making it easier to cough up and expel.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Wild indigo has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to reduce swelling and irritation in the respiratory tract, alleviating cough symptoms.
  • Antimicrobial: Wild indigo has antimicrobial properties, which can help to fight off respiratory infections that may be causing a cough.
  • Immune-stimulant: The plant can help to boost the immune system, making it easier for the body to fight off infections and viruses that can lead to a persistent cough.
  • Analgesic: Wild indigo contains compounds that can help to relieve pain, making it useful for soothing a sore throat or chest pain associated with coughing.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in elderberry that help with cough.

  • Flowers: Contain anthocyanins and flavonoids with expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, helping alleviate cough and respiratory symptoms.
  • Berries: Rich in vitamins and antioxidants, berries support immune function and have been traditionally used to soothe coughs and reduce mucus.
  • Leaves: Packed with tannins and essential oil, leaves provide astringent and anti-catarrhal properties, making them useful for treating cough and congestion.
  • Bark: Contains high levels of phenolic acids and proanthocyanidins, offering anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic benefits, which can help relieve cough and soothe sore throats.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic elderberry for cough.

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh elderberries or 1/2 cup of dried berries, and place them in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 1 cup of 80-proof vodka or rum over the elderberries, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  3. Securely fasten a lid on the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking the jar gently every few days.
  4. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth-lined sieve into a dark glass bottle, discarding the solids.
  5. Label the bottle with the preparation date and store it in a cool, dark place; take 1-2 mL up to four times daily as needed for cough relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow for cough.

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh marshmallow roots, 7 cups of water, and a large pot.
  2. Simmer the marshmallow roots in water for 30 minutes, then strain and keep the liquid.

  4. Store the marshmallow tincture in a sealed glass container for 2 weeks, shaking it daily.

The best way to treat cough with herbal tinctures is by utilizing the proven properties of nature's remedies.

Remember, individual results may vary based on dosage, consistency, and overall health.
