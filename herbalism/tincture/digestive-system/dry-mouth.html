1. Marshmallow tincture

Marshmallow tincture helps with dry mouth because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery and gel-like when mixed with water.

This mucilage coats the mouth and throat, providing immediate relief for dryness and irritation. It also helps to stimulate saliva production, further aiding in the alleviation of dry mouth symptoms.

  • Flavonoids: Possess anti-inflammatory properties, reducing inflammation in the oral tissues and providing relief from dry mouth.

  • Leaves: Rich in soothing properties, help create a film over mouth membranes to retain moisture and provide relief from dryness.
  • Flowers: Packed with anti-inflammatory agents, reduce irritation in the mouth and throat while increasing saliva secretion.
  • Seeds: Contain essential oils that help lubricate and hydrate oral tissues, providing instant and long-lasting relief from dry mouth.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tincture for dry mouth.

  1. Dissolve 1 cup of chopped marshmallows in 2 cups of boiling water, stirring constantly for 10 minutes.
  2. Reduce heat and simmer the mixture for 30 minutes, then remove from heat.
  3. Stir in 1 teaspoon of vegetable glycerin and let the mixture cool.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a jar, squeezing out any excess liquid.
  5. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for up to 2 weeks, shaking daily. Use 1 tablespoon as needed for dry mouth relief.
