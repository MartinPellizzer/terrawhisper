1. Marshmallow tincture

Marshmallow tincture helps with dry mouth because it contains mucilage, a substance that comes from the marshmallow plant's roots and leaves.

Mucilage has the ability to coat and soothe irritated tissues in the mouth, providing immediate relief from dryness. Moreover, mucilage stimulates saliva production, which further aids in maintaining moisture in the mouth.

By alleviating dry mouth symptoms and promoting saliva flow, marshmallow tincture effectively restores comfort and hydration in the mouth.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in marshmallow that aid with dry mouth.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Rosemary's anti-inflammatory properties help reduce inflammation in the mouth, providing relief from dry mouth symptoms. This is due to the presence of compounds such as carnosol and carnosic acid.
  • Astringent: The astringent properties of rosemary can help stimulate saliva production, thus alleviating dry mouth. This is attributed to the tannins found in rosemary.
  • Antimicrobial: Rosemary's antimicrobial properties can help maintain a healthy oral microbiome, preventing infections and further irritation in the mouth. This is due to the presence of compounds like eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and camphor.
  • Demulcent: Rosemary's demulcent properties can help soothe and protect irritated oral tissues, providing temporary relief from dry mouth symptoms. This is attributed to the mucilage content in rosemary.
  • Stimulant: Rosemary's stimulant properties can help increase blood flow to the mouth, promoting saliva production and providing relief from dry mouth. This is due to the presence of caffeic acid and rosemarinic acid.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in marshmallow that help with dry mouth.

  • Leaves: Contain mucilaginous compounds that help to lubricate and moisturize the mouth.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and flavonoids that aid in soothing irritations in the oral cavity.
  • Roots: Packed with anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce dry mouth-related inflammation.
  • Bark: Contains tannins that have astringent qualities to alleviate dry mouth symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tincture for dry mouth.

  1. Gather 2 cups of dried marshmallow root, 7 cups of water, and a large pot.
  2. Simmer the marshmallow root in water for at least 2 hours, covered, on low heat.
  3. Strain the mixture and press the solids to extract all liquid, then discard the solids.
  4. Measure 2 cups of the marshmallow liquid and combine it with 2 cups of vodka in a clean jar.
  5. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking it gently every day.
