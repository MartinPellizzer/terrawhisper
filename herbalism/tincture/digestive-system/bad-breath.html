Spearmint tincture helps with bad breath because it contains antibacterial and antiseptic properties that kill the bacteria causing foul odor.

  • Diptene: Exhibits anti-inflammatory effects, helping to soothe irritated oral tissues and reduce bad breath caused by inflammation.
  • Cineole: Offers antiseptic and cooling properties, which help to fight bad breath by inhibiting the growth of bacteria in the mouth.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in spearmint tincture that help with bad breath.

  • Leaves: Contain chlorophyll which helps neutralize odor-causing bacteria in the mouth.
  • Flowers: Rich in antimicrobial compounds that reduce bad breath by inhibiting the growth of bacteria.
  • Seeds: High in essential oils with anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that help freshen breath.
  • Stems: Contain trace amounts of menthol, providing a cooling effect and killing germs that cause bad breath.
  • Roots: A powerhouse of antioxidants, the roots help eliminate toxins responsible for halitosis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic spearmint tincture for bad breath.

  1. Chop 1 cup of fresh spearmint leaves and place them in a clean jar.
  2. Pour 2 cups of vodka or rum over the spearmint leaves, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-3 weeks.
  4. Shake the jar gently every other day to facilitate the extraction process.
  5. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the liquid, and store the resulting tincture in a dark glass bottle.
