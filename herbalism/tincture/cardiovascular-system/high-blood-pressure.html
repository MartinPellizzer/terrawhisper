1. Hawthorn

Hawthorn tincture helps with high-blood-pressure because it contains active compounds such as oligomeric procyanidins and flavonoids, which possess potent antioxidant and vasodilatory properties.

These compounds work together to relax and dilate blood vessels, thereby reducing the overall resistance against blood flow and decreasing the pressure on the artery walls.

Moreover, hawthorn tincture has been shown to strengthen the heart muscle, improve coronary blood flow, and increase the tolerance of the heart to oxygen deficiency, all of which contribute to its efficacy in managing high blood pressure.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in hawthorn that aid with high blood pressure.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Ginger has potent anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation in blood vessels, which is important for high blood pressure as inflammation can cause the narrowing of blood vessels, leading to increased blood pressure. The active constituents responsible for these anti-inflammatory effects are gingerols and shogaols.
  • Vasodilation: Ginger can dilate blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing pressure on the arterial walls. This is important for high blood pressure as it helps to reduce the overall pressure within the cardiovascular system. The main active constituent responsible for vasodilation is gingerol.
  • Antioxidant: Ginger contains powerful antioxidants that help protect blood vessels from oxidative damage, reducing the risk of plaque buildup and subsequent narrowing of blood vessels. This is important for high blood pressure as it helps maintain the overall health of the cardiovascular system. The primary active constituents responsible for antioxidant effects are gingerols and shogaols.
  • Anti-platelet: Ginger has been shown to have anti-platelet effects, which help prevent blood clots from forming and blocking blood vessels. This is important for high blood pressure as it reduces the risk of clot-induced blockages that can result in strokes or heart attacks. The active constituent responsible for these anti-platelet effects is gingerol.
  • Ace inhibition: Some studies suggest that ginger may have ACE (angiotensin-converting enzyme) inhibitory effects, which can help relax blood vessels and lower blood pressure. Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in hawthorn that help with high blood pressure.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hawthorn for high blood pressure.

  1. Gather 2/3 cup of fresh hawthorn berries or 1/3 cup of dried berries and place in a clean jar.
  2. Pour 2 cups of 80 proof vodka or grain alcohol into the jar, ensuring the berries are fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar and store in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking daily.
  4. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the liquid and discarding the berries.
  5. Store the finished tincture in a tightly sealed glass bottle, taking 1-2 mL up to three times a day for high-blood-pressure.

What are the most common causes of high blood pressure that are treatable with herbal tinctures?

The primary causes of high-blood-pressure that are treatable with herbal tinctures are stress, poor diet, and lack of exercise.

Stress can activate the body's fight or flight response, leading to constricted blood vessels and increased heart rate, which in turn elevates blood pressure. Poor diet, specifically a diet high in sodium, saturated fats, and processed foods, can contribute to the development of high-blood-pressure. Lastly, a sedentary lifestyle leads to weight gain, further increasing the risk of high-blood-pressure.

In these cases, herbal tinctures can be used to address the root causes of the condition.
