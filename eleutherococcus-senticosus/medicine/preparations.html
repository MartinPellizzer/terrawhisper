10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Eleutherococcus senticosus (Siberian Ginseng)

Eleutherococcus senticosus, also known as Siberian ginseng, is a plant that has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its medicinal preparations include tisanes and tinctures, which are made from the root of the plant. These preparations are believed to boost the immune system, increase energy and stamina, and improve mental clarity. The active compounds in Eleutherococcus senticosus, including eleutherosides, are thought to be responsible for these effects.

To make a tisane, the roots are typically dried and then steeped in hot water. For a tincture, the roots are soaked in alcohol, which extracts the active compounds. Both preparations are taken orally, and the appropriate dosage may vary depending on the individual and the condition being treated.

As with any medicinal preparation, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

1. Tincture

Eleutherococcus Senticosus tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking the roots or rhizomes of the Siberian ginseng plant in alcohol or glycerin to extract its active compounds.

Its main health benefits include boosting the immune system, increasing energy and endurance, reducing stress and anxiety, and improving mental clarity and cognitive function. The roots and rhizomes of the Eleutherococcus senticosus plant are primarily used to make this tincture.

To prepare the tincture, the roots or rhizomes are chopped and steeped in alcohol or glycerin for several weeks, after which the liquid is strained and stored. When using Eleutherococcus senticosus tincture, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and not to use it as a substitute for prescribed medications.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women, people with autoimmune diseases, and those taking blood pressure or diabetes medications should avoid using this tincture or consult with a healthcare provider before use.
