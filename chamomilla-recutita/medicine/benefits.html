1. Promotes Sleep

"German Chamomile promotes sleep" refers to its ability to help people fall asleep or relax, usually by consuming it as a tea or using it in aromatherapy.

The following list includes the main medicinal constituents that give Chamomilla recutita this benefit.

  • Apigenin
  • Bisabolol
  • Matricaria Recutita Flower Extract
  • Maltol
  • Nigelic Acid
  • Quercetin
  • Trans-Chalcone

Apigenin is a flavonoid present in German chamomile which promotes sleep by interacting with the GABA receptors to decrease neuronal activity and calm the nervous system. Bisabolol, another compound found in the plant, works alongside apigenin to help achieve better sleep quality by easing stress and anxiety. Matricaria recutita flower extract is a specific kind of chamomile that has been traditionally used for its sleep-promoting properties due to the presence of all these active constituents. Maltol also belongs to the chamomile family and plays a role in relaxing the mind by supporting GABA activity, thus helping in the promotion of restful sleep. Nigelic acid is another natural chemical from this herb which exhibits calming effects on the nervous system, making it easier for one to wind down at night. Quercetin is a flavonoid with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may reduce oxidative stress in the body, ultimately supporting better sleep quality. Trans-chalcone, the last constituent mentioned, has been shown to have sedative effects on the nervous system. When combined, these compounds found within German chamomile work synergistically to promote relaxation and a restful state which helps in falling asleep more easily and maintaining better overall sleep patterns.
