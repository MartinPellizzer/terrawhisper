10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Chamomilla recutita (German Chamomile)

Chamomile, with the botanical name Chamomilla recutita, is a popular medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat a variety of ailments.

Its flowers and leaves are used to make medicinal preparations such as tisanes (herbal teas) and tinctures. A tisane made from chamomile is often used to help promote sleep and relaxation, and it may also have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties.

Tinctures, which are alcohol-based extracts of the plant, can be used to treat a variety of issues including digestive problems, menstrual cramps, and skin irritations. Chamomile is generally considered safe, but it can cause allergic reactions in some people, particularly those with allergies to other members of the daisy family.

It is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedy.

1. Infusion

Chamomilla Recutita infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping dried flowers of German Chamomile in hot water, which releases the medicinal compounds found in the plant.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and promote sleep and relaxation. The flowers of the Chamomilla Recutita plant are used to make this infusion. To make the infusion, pour boiling water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried chamomile flowers and let it steep for 5-10 minutes before straining.

Some precautions to take when using this preparation include avoiding it if allergic to chamomile or other members of the daisy family, and being cautious when using it in large amounts or for extended periods, as it can cause side effects such as vomiting and skin rashes.

2. Tincture

Chamomilla Recutita tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking dried German Chamomile flowers in a mixture of water and alcohol for several weeks to extract the medicinal compounds, which includes various terpenoids and flavonoids.

The main health benefits of this Chamomilla Recutita preparation include its ability to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and alleviate anxiety and insomnia due to its calming effects on the nervous system. The main plant parts used for this Chamomilla Recutita preparation are the dried flowers of the German Chamomile plant.

To make Chamomilla Recutita tincture, dried German Chamomile flowers are soaked in a mixture of water and alcohol, typically at a ratio of 1:5 parts of herb to menstruum, and left to steep for 2-6 weeks before being pressed and filtered. When using Chamomilla Recutita tincture, it is important to follow the recommended dosage as high concentrations can have a sedative effect and may interact with other medications. Additionally, individuals who are allergic to members of the daisy family or have a history of allergic reactions to plants should exercise caution when using this tincture.

It is also recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using Chamomilla Recutita tincture, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, and individuals with medical conditions or taking other medications.

When using a Chamomilla Recutita compress, it is important to be aware that some people may experience allergic reactions, especially if they have a known allergy to ragweed or other members of the Asteraceae family.
