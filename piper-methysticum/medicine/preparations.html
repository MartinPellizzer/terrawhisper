10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Piper methysticum (Kava)

Piper methysticum, also known as kava, is a plant that has been used for medicinal purposes in many Pacific Island cultures.

Its roots are commonly used to make preparations such as tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or kava tea, is made by steeping the shredded or powdered root in hot water.

This preparation is often used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and stress-related symptoms. A tincture, on the other hand, is made by soaking the root in a solution of alcohol and water, which extracts the active compounds. Tinctures are taken orally and are used for similar purposes as tisanes, but they have a longer shelf life and are more concentrated.

Both preparations are believed to have relaxing and anxiolytic effects, although it's important to note that their use should be monitored as kava has been linked to liver toxicity in some cases.
