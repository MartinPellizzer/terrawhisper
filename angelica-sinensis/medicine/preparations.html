10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Angelica sinensis (Dong Quai)

Angelica sinensis, also known as Dong Quai, is a plant that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

Its roots are used to make medicinal preparations such as tisanes and tinctures. A tisane is an herbal tea, and an Angelica sinensis tisane is made by steeping the roots in hot water. This preparation is believed to have various health benefits, including regulating menstrual cycles and alleviating menopause symptoms.

A tincture, on the other hand, is a preparation where the roots are soaked in alcohol to extract the active compounds. The resulting solution is taken orally, often in small doses, and is believed to have similar benefits to the tisane, as well as potentially improving blood circulation and reducing inflammation. However, it's important to note that while these preparations have been used traditionally, their efficacy and safety are not always supported by scientific research.

As with any medication or supplement, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new treatment.

1. infusion

Angelica Sinensis infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the roots of the Angelica sinensis plant in hot water, which releases the plant's beneficial compounds into the water.

This infusion is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine for its many health benefits, including improving circulation, reducing menstrual symptoms, and alleviating menopausal symptoms. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the roots of the Angelica sinensis plant, which are harvested, cleaned, and dried before use. To make an Angelica Sinensis infusion, pour boiling water over the dried roots and let them steep for 10-15 minutes.

The main precautions to take when using this Angelica sinensis preparation include avoiding it during pregnancy, as it may stimulate uterine contractions, and being mindful of potential interactions with certain medications, such as blood thinners, as it may increase the risk of bleeding.

2. decoction

Angelica Sinensis decoction is a traditional medicinal preparation made by boiling the roots of the Angelica sinensis plant for several hours to extract its active compounds.

This preparation is commonly used in Chinese medicine to treat various health conditions, including menstrual disorders, fatigue, and anemia, due to its ability to regulate menstrual cycle, improve blood circulation, and alleviate oxidative stress. The root of the Angelica sinensis plant is the primary plant part used in this preparation, and it contains various bioactive compounds, such as ferulic acid, ligustilide, and butylphthalide, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

To make an Angelica Sinensis decoction, place sliced or crushed Angelica sinensis roots in a pot of water, bring it to a boil, and then simmer for 1-3 hours. After that, strain the liquid and drink it as a tea. It is important to note that Angelica Sinensis decoction may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and anticoagulants, and should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation.

Therefore, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using this preparation.
