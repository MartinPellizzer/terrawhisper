1. Lowers blood pressure

"Hawthorn lowers blood pressure" refers to its ability to reduce hypertension, resulting from consuming parts of this plant.

The following list includes the main medicinal constituents that give Crataegus oxyacantha this benefit.

  • Flavonoids
  • Proanthocyanidins
  • Oligomeric procyanidin complexes
  • Triterpenoid saponins
  • Hydroxycinnamic acids
  • Tannins
  • Phenolic compounds

Flavonoids help prevent vascular and coronary artery damage by decreasing oxidative stress and inflammation; lowering blood pressure. Proanthocyanidins are a type of flavonoid, which also contribute to this effect by protecting blood vessels from free radicals and improving circulation that supports reduced blood pressure. Oligomeric procyanidin complexes strengthen collagen in the blood vessel walls, enhancing elasticity, thereby easing strain on arteries and lowering blood pressure. Triterpenoid saponins reduce cholesterol levels and minimize risk of plaque buildup in the arteries, which can lead to a decrease in blood pressure. Hydroxycinnamic acids act as antioxidants to eliminate damaging free radicals and enhance nitric oxide production to increase vasodilation. As these artery-relaxing effects occur, there is less resistance against the flow of blood, hence decreased blood pressure. Tannins improve circulation by dilating blood vessels and increasing their elasticity, which ultimately contribute to lower blood pressure. Phenolic compounds, as antioxidants, neutralize free radicals that cause oxidative stress and inflammation within the cardiovascular system; hence lowering blood pressure.

lower blood pressure is beneficial for several health conditions, including those with raised blood pressure, as it reduces the strain on heart muscles and other parts of the circulatory system. With lesser workload, organs are less likely to be damaged or stressed during long term high pressure. Reducing blood pressure therefore minimises risk of damage associated with diseases such as hypertension, cardiovascular disease, stroke, peripheral arterial disease and atherosclerosis.
