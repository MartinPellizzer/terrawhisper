10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Cinnamomum verum (Cinnamon)

Cinnamomum verum, also known as true cinnamon, has been used in various medicinal preparations such as tisanes and tinctures for its potential health benefits.

A tisane, or herbal infusion, can be made by steeping the bark of the cinnamon tree in hot water, releasing its essential oils and volatile compounds. This soothing drink may help alleviate digestive issues, including bloating, gas, and indigestion, due to its antispasmodic and carminative properties.

Moreover, cinnamon tinctures can be prepared by soaking cinnamon bark in a mixture of alcohol and water, which extracts its active constituents. These tinctures can be used to manage blood sugar levels, as cinnamon has been shown to improve insulin sensitivity and reduce fasting blood glucose levels. Furthermore, the antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties of cinnamon may support the immune system and provide relief for respiratory ailments when used in tincture form.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating cinnamon tinctures or tisanes into a health regimen, as they can interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for everyone.

1. Infusion

Cinnamomum Verum infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the bark or leaves of the Cinnamon tree in hot water, which releases the beneficial compounds found in the plant.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to alleviate digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and regulate blood sugar levels. The bark and leaves of the Cinnamon tree are the primary plant parts used for this infusion.

To prepare the infusion, add a teaspoon of crushed Cinnamon bark or a handful of leaves to a cup of boiling water, let it steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain and drink. It is important to note that Cinnamon infusion should be consumed in moderation, as excessive consumption can lead to skin irritation, mouth sores, and liver damage.

Additionally, people with diabetes should consult their healthcare provider before consuming Cinnamon infusion, as it may interact with their medication and lower blood sugar levels.

4. Maceration

Cinnamomum Verum maceration is the process of soaking the bark or leaves of the Cinnamomum verum tree, also known as true cinnamon, in a solvent such as water, alcohol, or oil to extract its medicinal properties.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as arthritis, diabetes, and digestive issues. The bark and leaves of the Cinnamomum verum tree are the main plant parts used for this preparation. To make a cinnamon maceration, first, chop or crush the bark or leaves and place them in a clean jar.

Then, add the solvent of your choice, ensuring that the plant material is completely submerged. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking it occasionally. After the maceration period, strain the liquid and store it in a sealed container in a cool, dark place. When using this preparation, it is important to start with a small dose and gradually increase it to avoid potential skin or mucous membrane irritation.

Additionally, people with certain health conditions, such as pregnancy, liver disease, or those taking blood-thinning medication, should consult with a healthcare professional before using cinnamon maceration.
