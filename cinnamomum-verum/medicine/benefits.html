1. Reduces Blood Sugar Levels

"Cinnamon reduces blood sugar levels" refers to its ability to help manage diabetes by lowering high blood sugar.

The following list includes the main medicinal constituents that give Cinnamomum verum this benefit.

  • Coumarin
  • Cinnamaldehyde
  • Cinnamic acid
  • Procyanidins
  • Methylhydroxychalcone polymers (MHCPs)
  • Phenolic compounds
  • Lignans

Coumarin is an example of a bioflavonoid that potentially impacts on blood sugar levels by modifying glucose metabolism, which could result in a reduced blood sugar level. Cinnamaldehyde is the primary aromatic compound found in cinnamon that can reduce inflammation, promote insulin sensitivity and improve glucose absorption, thus helping to lower blood sugar levels. Cinnamic acid may decrease high blood sugar by increasing insulin secretion, boosting insulin receptor function, and improving insulin resistance. Procyanidins are a type of flavonoid found in cinnamon that can improve glucose tolerance and help manage diabetes due to their antioxidant effects which inhibit inflammation and oxidative stress, both of which may contribute to high blood sugar levels. Methylhydroxychalcone polymers (MHCPs) are also found in cinnamon and have been shown to increase glucose transport across cell membranes as well as lower blood glucose levels by inhibiting the gluconeogenesis enzyme, effectively increasing insulin sensitivity. Phenolic compounds found in cinnamon may act as antioxidants reducing free radical damage which has been shown to contribute to high blood sugar levels and help maintain normal glucose balance. Lignans are another type of phytochemical present in cinnamon, with potential anti-diabetic effects including the ability to lower blood sugar levels by improving insulin sensitivity. In total, various compounds within cinnamon have demonstrated a variety of mechanisms that can contribute to reduced blood sugar levels.

Cinnamon aids prediabetes patients, who have slightly increased risk factors, in avoiding progression to full-blown diabetes. It further offers protection to those with metabolic syndrome, type 2 diabetes and impaired glucose tolerance by helping maintain balance of insulin and glucose levels.
