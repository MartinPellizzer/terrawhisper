What is jet lag and how does it affect your body?

Jet lag, also known as desynchronosis, is a temporary sleep disorder that occurs when your body's internal circadian rhythm is disrupted by rapid travel across multiple time zones.

This disruption happens because your body is still operating on the time schedule of your original location, while your new environment has a different day-night cycle. The effects of jet lag can include fatigue, difficulty concentrating, irritability, and even digestive issues, as your body struggles to adjust to the new time zone. These symptoms typically peak within the first few days after crossing time zones and can last for several days until your internal clock realigns with the local time.

To mitigate jet lag, travelers often adjust their sleep schedules before the trip and expose themselves to natural light upon arrival to help reset their circadian rhythm.

Underlying causes and risk factors of jet lag

This disruption occurs because the body's natural rhythms are synchronized with the local environment, and sudden exposure to a new time zone throws this synchronization off. Key risk factors include the direction of travel, with eastward flights typically causing more severe jet lag than westward ones due to the body's difficulty in adjusting to a shorter day. Individuals who are more sensitive to light, have irregular sleep patterns, or suffer from pre-existing sleep disorders are also at higher risk.

Additionally, the duration of the flight and the number of time zones crossed further contribute to the severity of jet lag symptoms.

How to use herbal remedies safely for jet lag

To use herbal remedies safely for jet lag, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement, especially if you are on medication or have existing health conditions.

Common herbs like valerian root, lemon balm, and melatonin are often used to support sleep, but their effects can vary among individuals. It is advisable to start with low doses and monitor how your body responds to avoid potential side effects or interactions. Maintain a consistent sleep schedule during travel and use the herbs in conjunction with good sleep hygiene practices.

Always follow the recommended dosage and discontinue use if you experience adverse reactions.
