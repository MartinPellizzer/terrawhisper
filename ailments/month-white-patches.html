What is white patches in month and how does it affect your body?

White patches on the skin, often referred to as vitiligo, are areas where the skin loses its pigment due to the destruction of melanocytes, the cells responsible for skin color.

These patches can appear anywhere on the body, including the face, hands, and limbs, and are typically symmetrical in distribution. Vitiligo is not contagious and is not a form of skin cancer, but it can have a significant impact on a person's self-esteem and mental well-being. The exact cause of vitiligo is unknown, but it is believed to be an autoimmune disorder where the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys melanocytes.

While vitiligo itself does not pose a direct health risk, it can be a sign of other autoimmune conditions and may require medical treatment to manage its appearance and associated psychological effects.

Underlying causes and risk factors of white patches in month

White patches on the skin, often referred to as vitiligo, are primarily caused by the destruction of melanocytes, the cells responsible for skin pigmentation.
