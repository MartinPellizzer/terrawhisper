10 Best Herbal Tinctures For Hangover

Herbal tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts made from dried herbs, often used to support the body's natural healing processes.

For hangovers, tinctures containing ingredients like ginger, milk thistle, and turmeric are commonly used due to their anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits. These natural remedies are believed to help alleviate symptoms such as nausea, fatigue, and headaches by supporting liver function and reducing inflammation. Unlike pharmaceutical drugs, herbal tinctures are generally considered safer and more holistic, though they should not replace proper hydration and rest.

When choosing a tincture, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional to ensure it's appropriate for your individual health needs.

1. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, has been traditionally used to support hormonal balance and may help alleviate some symptoms associated with hangovers, such as headaches and fatigue.

As a herbal tincture, it is typically made by soaking the dried berries in alcohol to extract its active compounds, including flavonoids and essential oils. While scientific evidence specifically linking vitex to hangover relief is limited, some users report that it may help regulate mood and reduce nausea, which are common hangover symptoms. It is often used as a complementary remedy rather than a primary treatment for alcohol-related discomfort.

As with any herbal supplement, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with existing medical conditions or taking other medications.

Its active compound, silymarin, is believed to protect liver cells from damage caused by toxins, including those from excessive alcohol consumption. Silybum marianum herbal tinctures are typically made by soaking the dried seeds in alcohol, which helps extract the beneficial compounds for easier absorption by the body. These tinctures are often taken before or after drinking to potentially reduce the severity of hangover symptoms such as fatigue, nausea, and headaches.
