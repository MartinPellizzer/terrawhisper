10 Best Herbal Teas For Bad Breath

Herbal teas can be an effective natural remedy for bad breath due to their antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

Certain herbs like peppermint, ginger, and fennel are known to freshen the breath and reduce oral bacteria that contribute to odor. Drinking herbal tea regularly can help maintain a balanced oral environment and promote overall digestive health, which in turn supports fresh breath. Additionally, some herbal teas, such as chamomile and licorice root, have soothing effects that may alleviate mouth ulcers or inflammation, further improving breath quality.

For best results, it is advisable to drink herbal teas as part of a holistic oral care routine that includes proper brushing, flossing, and regular dental check-ups.

1. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to combat bad breath due to its natural minty aroma and antimicrobial properties.

The refreshing scent of peppermint helps mask unpleasant odors, making it an effective choice for freshening breath. Peppermint tea contains compounds like menthol and rosmarinic acid, which can reduce oral bacteria that contribute to bad breath. Regular consumption of peppermint tea may support overall oral health by promoting saliva production, which helps neutralize acids and wash away food particles.

However, it is best used as a complementary remedy alongside proper oral hygiene practices for optimal results.

2. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to help alleviate bad breath.

Sage contains natural antimicrobial properties that can help reduce the bacteria responsible for causing oral odor. When brewed as a tea, sage can freshen the mouth and promote a cleaner breath by neutralizing odor-causing compounds. Its essential oils, such as thujone and camphor, contribute to its breath-freshening effects.

Regular consumption of sage tea may support overall oral hygiene and complement other dental care routines.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, is often used in herbal teas to help alleviate bad breath due to its natural antimicrobial properties.

The essential oils in cinnamon, particularly cinnamaldehyde, can help reduce the bacteria that contribute to oral odor. When brewed into a tea, cinnamon can freshen the mouth and neutralize unpleasant smells. It is often combined with other herbs like ginger or mint to enhance its effectiveness.

Regular consumption of cinnamon tea may support overall oral health and contribute to long-term breath freshness.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to help alleviate bad breath.

The tea is made by steeping dried fennel seeds in hot water, releasing a mild, sweet aroma that can freshen the breath naturally. Fennel contains compounds like anethole, which have antimicrobial properties that help reduce the bacteria responsible for unpleasant odors. Regular consumption of fennel tea can support oral hygiene by promoting saliva production and neutralizing acids in the mouth.

While it is generally safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially for individuals with existing health conditions or those taking medications.

5. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is often used in herbal teas to help combat bad breath due to its natural antimicrobial properties.

The active compounds in black pepper, such as piperine, can help neutralize odor-causing bacteria in the mouth. Drinking black pepper tea can stimulate saliva production, which naturally cleanses the mouth and reduces the buildup of harmful bacteria. Additionally, its warming effects may improve digestion and reduce the likelihood of food particles lingering in the mouth.

While it can be a useful complement to oral hygiene routines, it is best used in conjunction with proper dental care for optimal results.

6. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to address various health issues, including bad breath.

The herb contains compounds such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce the bacterial load in the mouth. When used in herbal teas, licorice root can help neutralize oral odors by inhibiting the growth of odor-causing bacteria like Streptococcus mutans. Its soothing effect on the throat and mouth also makes it beneficial for individuals suffering from chronic halitosis or oral inflammation.

However, excessive consumption of licorice root tea should be avoided, as it may lead to side effects such as increased blood pressure due to its natural steroid content.

7. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to address bad breath due to its natural antimicrobial and digestive properties.

The essential oils in cumin, such as limonene and cineole, help neutralize oral bacteria that contribute to unpleasant odors. Drinking cumin tea can stimulate saliva production, which naturally cleanses the mouth and reduces the buildup of odor-causing bacteria. It is often combined with other herbs like ginger or fennel to enhance its breath-freshening effects.

Regular consumption of cumin herbal tea may support overall oral health and provide a natural alternative to conventional breath fresheners.

8. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, is a popular herbal ingredient used in teas to address bad breath due to its potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The active compound in turmeric, curcumin, helps neutralize odor-causing bacteria in the mouth and supports overall oral health. Turmeric tea can be prepared by steeping ground turmeric powder or fresh turmeric root in hot water, often combined with black pepper to enhance absorption. Regular consumption of turmeric tea may help reduce halitosis by promoting a healthier oral microbiome.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating turmeric tea into a routine, especially for those with digestive sensitivities or existing health conditions.

9. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, commonly known as eucalyptus oil, is often used in herbal teas to help combat bad breath due to its strong antibacterial and antimicrobial properties.

The essential oil derived from the leaves of this plant contains compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which can help neutralize odor-causing bacteria in the mouth. When brewed into a tea, eucalyptus globulus can freshen the breath and provide a cooling, invigorating sensation. It is typically used in combination with other herbs like peppermint or spearmint to enhance its aromatic effects.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before regular use, as it may interact with certain medications or cause sensitivities in some individuals.

10. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a versatile herbal plant often used in teas to address bad breath due to its natural antimicrobial properties.

The essential oils in rosemary, particularly cineole and rosmarinic acid, help reduce the bacteria that cause unpleasant odors in the mouth. Drinking rosemary tea can freshen breath by promoting saliva production, which naturally cleanses the oral cavity. It is also believed to support digestion, which can indirectly improve breath quality by reducing stomach-related odors.

For best results, rosemary tea can be enjoyed regularly as part of a holistic approach to maintaining fresh breath and oral health.
