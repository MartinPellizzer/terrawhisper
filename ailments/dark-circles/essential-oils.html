10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Dark Circles

Herbal essential oils are natural remedies that can help reduce the appearance of dark circles under the eyes by promoting circulation and soothing inflammation.

Oils like lavender, chamomile, and calendula are commonly used for their calming and regenerative properties. When diluted properly with a carrier oil, these essential oils can be applied topically to the delicate under-eye area to brighten the skin and reduce puffiness. They work by stimulating blood flow and reducing the visibility of blood vessels that contribute to dark circles.

However, it is important to perform a patch test before use to avoid any allergic reactions.

1. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herb used in the production of essential oils that are often touted for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Chamomile essential oil is believed to help reduce the appearance of dark circles by improving circulation and reducing inflammation around the delicate eye area. Its mild antiseptic and calming effects may also help soothe irritation and redness that can contribute to the visibility of dark circles. When diluted properly, chamomile essential oil can be safely applied topically to the under-eye region as part of a skincare routine.

However, it is important to perform a patch test before use, as some individuals may be sensitive to this oil.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a versatile herb whose essential oil is widely used in aromatherapy and natural skincare for its stimulating and antioxidant properties.

When applied topically, rosemary essential oil can help reduce the appearance of dark circles by improving circulation and reducing puffiness around the eyes. Its high concentration of antioxidants, such as rosmarinic acid, may help protect the delicate skin from oxidative stress and promote a more even skin tone. To use it safely, it should always be diluted with a carrier oil before application to avoid irritation.

Incorporating rosemary essential oil into a nightly skincare routine may contribute to a brighter, more refreshed complexion over time.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is widely used in aromatherapy and skincare for its calming and soothing properties.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. When applied topically, lavender essential oil may help reduce the appearance of dark circles by improving circulation and reducing oxidative stress around the delicate eye area. It is often diluted with a carrier oil before use to prevent skin irritation.

While it can be a beneficial natural remedy, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially for sensitive areas like the under-eye region.

4. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green and black tea are derived, is also a source of various herbal essential oils that are being explored for their potential benefits in addressing dark circles under the eyes.

These essential oils, often extracted through steam distillation, contain bioactive compounds such as polyphenols and antioxidants that may help reduce inflammation and improve circulation. When used in topical formulations, they can potentially brighten the skin and diminish the appearance of dark circles by supporting collagen production and reducing oxidative stress. Some studies suggest that the anti-inflammatory properties of Camellia sinensis essential oils may help alleviate puffiness and redness associated with under-eye darkness.

However, more research is needed to fully understand their efficacy and safety for long-term use in skincare applications.

5. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a herbal plant traditionally used for its liver-supporting properties, but it also contains essential oils that may offer benefits for dark circles under the eyes.

These essential oils are derived from the seeds and leaves of the plant and are rich in compounds like flavonoids and lignans, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. When applied topically, they may help reduce the appearance of dark circles by improving circulation and reducing oxidative stress in the delicate under-eye area. However, it is important to note that while some studies suggest potential benefits, more research is needed to fully understand their efficacy for this specific concern.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with sensitive skin or existing health conditions.

6. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a powerful plant that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and stems, contain a variety of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, antioxidants, and minerals that may help in reducing the appearance of dark circles. These oils are believed to improve circulation and reduce inflammation, which can contribute to the fading of dark under-eye circles. When used topically, urtica dioica essential oils may help brighten the skin and promote a more even skin tone.

However, it is important to dilute the essential oil properly before applying it to the delicate under-eye area to avoid irritation.

7. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, is a herbal essential oil that has gained attention for its potential benefits in reducing the appearance of dark circles under the eyes.

This oil is rich in compounds that may help improve circulation and reduce inflammation, which are common contributors to the formation of dark circles. When applied topically, it may help brighten the under-eye area and promote a more even skin tone. Its calming properties also make it a popular choice for use in skincare routines aimed at overall skin rejuvenation.

However, it is important to dilute the oil properly before application to avoid skin irritation and to consult with a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with sensitive skin or underlying health conditions.

8. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, is a natural remedy often used for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The essential oils derived from Curcuma longa are rich in compounds like curcuminoids, which have been studied for their potential benefits in skin health. When used topically, these essential oils may help reduce the appearance of dark circles by improving circulation and reducing oxidative stress around the eye area. However, due to their potency, they should be diluted with a carrier oil before application to avoid irritation.

While some anecdotal evidence suggests they may help with dark circles, scientific research on their specific efficacy for this purpose is limited.

9. Rosa damascena

Rosa damascena, also known as Damask rose, is a revered floral essential oil known for its calming and skin-rejuvenating properties.

When used for dark circles, this essential oil can help brighten the under-eye area due to its high concentration of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds. It promotes circulation and reduces the appearance of puffiness, making it an effective natural remedy for tired-looking skin. To use, it is typically diluted with a carrier oil and applied gently with a cotton pad.

Regular application of Rosa damascena essential oil can lead to a more radiant and refreshed complexion around the eyes.

10. Rosa canina

Rosa canina, also known as rosehip, is a popular herbal ingredient known for its high content of antioxidants, vitamins, and essential fatty acids.

When used in the form of essential oils, rosa canina can help improve the appearance of dark circles by promoting skin regeneration and reducing oxidative stress. The anti-inflammatory properties of rosehip essential oil may also help alleviate under-eye puffiness and redness. Additionally, its ability to stimulate collagen production can enhance skin elasticity and brightness, leading to a more youthful appearance.

For best results, rosa canina essential oil is often diluted with a carrier oil and applied gently around the eyes as part of a nightly skincare routine.
