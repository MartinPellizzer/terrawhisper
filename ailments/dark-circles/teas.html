10 Best Herbal Teas For Dark Circles

Herbal teas can be a natural and effective remedy for reducing the appearance of dark circles under the eyes.

Teas such as chamomile, green tea, and hibiscus are commonly used due to their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Applying cooled herbal tea bags to the under-eye area can help constrict blood vessels and reduce puffiness, leading to a more refreshed look. These teas also contain vitamins and minerals that may improve skin health and circulation.

For best results, it is recommended to use the tea bags consistently and in combination with a good skincare routine.

1. Camellia (Camellia sinensis)

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green and black teas are derived, contains bioactive compounds such as caffeine, antioxidants, and polyphenols that may contribute to the reduction of dark circles under the eyes.

These antioxidants help neutralize free radicals, which can cause oxidative stress and damage to the skin, potentially leading to the appearance of dark circles. While there is limited scientific evidence directly linking Camellia sinensis herbal teas to the reduction of dark circles, some studies suggest that the anti-inflammatory properties of green tea may improve circulation and reduce puffiness around the eyes. Additionally, the caffeine in these teas can constrict blood vessels, potentially reducing the visibility of darkened areas.

However, it is important to note that dark circles can have multiple causes, including genetics, fatigue, and allergies, and Camellia sinensis herbal teas should be considered as part of a holistic approach to skincare rather than a standalone solution.

2. Dog rose (Rosa canina)

Rosa canina, also known as rosehip, is a popular herbal ingredient used in teas for its high vitamin C content and antioxidant properties.

These teas are often recommended for their potential benefits in improving skin health and reducing the appearance of dark circles under the eyes. The antioxidants in rosehip tea may help brighten the skin and promote a more even complexion. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of rosa canina can help reduce puffiness and irritation around the eyes.

While it is not a substitute for medical treatments, incorporating rosa canina herbal tea into a skincare routine may support overall eye health and appearance.

3. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal tea often used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

While it is traditionally consumed for relaxation and sleep, some people use chamomile tea as a topical remedy for dark circles due to its potential soothing and antioxidant effects. The tea's active compounds, such as bisabolol and flavonoids, may help reduce inflammation and brighten the under-eye area. However, it is important to note that scientific evidence supporting its effectiveness for dark circles is limited, and results may vary among individuals.

For best results, chamomile tea can be cooled and applied as a compress, though consulting a healthcare professional is recommended before using it for skin concerns.

The tea may help improve circulation and reduce inflammation, which can contribute to the fading of dark circles over time. However, it is important to note that while some individuals report positive effects, scientific evidence supporting its efficacy for dark circles is limited.
