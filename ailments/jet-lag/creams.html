10 Best Herbal Creams For Jet Lag

Herbal creams for jet lag are natural topical treatments that aim to alleviate the symptoms of time zone disruption by incorporating soothing ingredients like lavender, chamomile, and peppermint.

These creams are often used to relieve physical discomfort, such as muscle tension and headaches, which commonly accompany jet lag. While they may not directly address the internal circadian rhythm, they can provide a calming effect and promote relaxation. Some herbal creams also contain essential oils that may enhance sleep quality when applied before bedtime.

Although they are not a cure for jet lag, they can be a complementary aid in managing its symptoms during travel.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a traditional herbal remedy often used to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

When incorporated into herbal creams, valerian may offer localized relief for muscle tension and stress-related discomfort, which can be common during long flights or travel. These creams typically contain valerian root extract, which is believed to have mild sedative properties that may help alleviate the effects of jet lag by supporting restful sleep. While not a cure for jet lag, valerian-based creams can be a complementary tool for those seeking natural ways to ease the symptoms of travel fatigue.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using such products, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a calming herb often used in herbal creams to help alleviate the symptoms of jet lag.

These creams typically contain essential oils and extracts from lemon balm leaves, which are known for their soothing and relaxing properties. When applied topically, the cream may help reduce anxiety and promote better sleep, which are common issues for travelers suffering from jet lag. The natural ingredients in melissa officinalis creams are believed to support the body’s ability to adjust to new time zones by regulating stress hormones.

While not a cure-all, these creams can be a complementary aid in managing the discomforts associated with jet lag.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is traditionally used for its immune-boosting properties, but recent research suggests it may also offer benefits for managing jet lag.

Some studies indicate that echinacea-containing creams might help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which are often exacerbated by the body's response to disrupted circadian rhythms during travel. While there is limited direct evidence linking echinacea to the treatment of jet lag, its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects could potentially support overall recovery after long-haul flights. Herbal creams infused with echinacea are sometimes used as part of a holistic approach to alleviate the physical discomfort associated with jet lag.

However, more scientific research is needed to confirm their efficacy and safety for this specific purpose.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties, and recent research suggests it may also offer benefits in alleviating symptoms of jet lag.
