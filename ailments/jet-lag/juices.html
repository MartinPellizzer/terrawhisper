10 Best Herbal Juices For Jet Lag

Herbal juices can be a natural and effective way to combat the effects of jet lag by supporting the body's circadian rhythm.

Ingredients like lemon, ginger, and turmeric are commonly included for their energizing and anti-inflammatory properties. These juices can help boost alertness and reduce fatigue during long flights or upon arrival in a new time zone. Some herbal blends also contain adaptogens such as ashwagandha or rhodiola, which may aid in stress relief and mental clarity.

Incorporating herbal juices into your routine before and after travel can complement other strategies like light exposure and proper sleep hygiene for a more balanced recovery.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.

When consumed as a herbal juice, valerian may help alleviate the symptoms of jet lag by supporting the body's natural circadian rhythms. The active compounds in valerian, such as valerenic acid, are believed to interact with the central nervous system to reduce anxiety and enhance sleep, which are often disrupted during travel across time zones. While scientific evidence on its effectiveness for jet lag is limited, many users report improved sleep patterns and reduced fatigue after using valerian juice.

As with any herbal supplement, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating valerian into a jet lag management routine.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used for its adaptogenic properties, which may help the body manage stress and fatigue.

Herbal juices made from licorice root are believed to support adrenal function and balance cortisol levels, which can be particularly beneficial for individuals experiencing jet lag. The anti-inflammatory and soothing effects of licorice juice may also aid in reducing throat irritation and promoting overall wellness during travel. While more research is needed, some studies suggest that licorice root may improve sleep quality and reduce the symptoms of fatigue associated with time zone changes.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using licorice-based herbal juices, especially for those with hypertension or other health conditions.
