7 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Jet Lag

Herbal essential oils for Jet lag are a blend of natural plant extracts used to alleviate the symptoms associated with traveling across different time zones.

These oils are rich in antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to regulate the body's internal clock, reduce fatigue, and promote relaxation.

Examples of herbal essential oils that can be used to treat jet lag include lavender oil, which promotes relaxation and reduces stress; peppermint oil, which helps to improve circulation and reduce nausea; ginger oil, which eases digestive issues and alleviates fatigue; frankincense oil, which reduces inflammation and promotes relaxation; and chamomile oil, which soothes the mind and body.

By incorporating these herbal essential oils into their daily routine, individuals can effectively manage jet lag symptoms and adjust to new time zones with ease.

Related Study

According to "Journal of psychopharmacology (Oxford, England)", essential oils such as lavender and chamomile, used in aromatherapy, may improve sleep and thus help alleviate symptoms of jet lag.

1. Cymbopogon citratus essential oils

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, also known as lemongrass oil, have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of jet lag, a common condition caused by rapid travel across different time zones.

The properties of this herbal preparation, particularly its calming and uplifting effects, help to regulate the body's internal clock and reduce fatigue.

The bioactive constituents of lemongrass oil, including citral, geraniol, and limonene, exhibit sedative and anti-inflammatory properties that aid in promoting relaxation and reducing stress associated with jet lag.

By incorporating lemongrass oil into one's routine, individuals can experience benefits such as improved sleep quality, reduced jet lag symptoms, and enhanced overall well-being.

2. Lavandula angustifolia essential oils

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of jet lag due to their calming and regulating properties.

The bioactive constituents, such as linalool and linalyl acetate, in these oils help to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and regulate the body's circadian rhythms, making it easier to adjust to a new time zone.

The benefits of using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils for jet lag include improved sleep quality, reduced fatigue, and enhanced mood.

By inhaling or applying these oils topically, individuals can help their body adjust to a new time zone more efficiently and reduce the negative effects of jet lag.

Related Study

According to "Journal of psychopharmacology (Oxford, England)", Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may improve sleep through aromatherapy, which could be beneficial for managing jet lag, although its practicality as a treatment remains to be determined.

3. Matricaria chamomilla essential oils

Matricaria chamomilla essential oils have been widely recognized for their potential to alleviate the symptoms of jet lag, a common condition experienced by travelers due to the disruption of their internal body clock.

The soothing and calming properties of this herbal preparation, particularly its ability to regulate sleep patterns and promote relaxation, make it an effective remedy for jet lag.

The bioactive constituents of Matricaria chamomilla essential oils, such as apigenin, luteolin, and chamazulene, are responsible for its therapeutic effects, including their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and sedative properties.

By using Matricaria chamomilla essential oils, individuals can benefit from improved sleep quality, reduced stress and anxiety, and a faster recovery from jet lag-related fatigue.

According to the available study on the effects of chamomile extract on sleep quality among elderly people, Matricaria chamomilla essential oils may help alleviate symptoms associated with jet lag, particularly sleep disturbances, in older adults.

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils, also known as rosemary essential oil, have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of jet lag due to its adaptogenic and antioxidant properties.

The herb's ability to regulate the body's internal clock and improve sleep quality makes it an effective treatment for jet lag.

5. Zingiber officinale essential oils

Zingiber officinale essential oils, derived from the rhizome of the ginger plant, have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms associated with jet lag.

The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of this herbal preparation help to regulate body rhythms and reduce the negative effects of time zone changes.

The benefits of using Zingiber officinale essential oils to treat jet lag include improved sleep quality, reduced fatigue, and enhanced overall well-being, making it a popular natural remedy among travelers.

Melissa officinalis essential oils have been traditionally used to treat jet lag, a common issue faced by travelers crossing multiple time zones.

7. Curcuma longa essential oils

Curcuma longa essential oils have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of jet lag due to their adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to regulate the body's internal clock and reduce fatigue.

The benefits of using Curcuma longa essential oils to treat jet lag include reduced fatigue, improved mood, and enhanced overall well-being, making it an effective natural remedy for this common travel affliction.
