10 Best Herbal Teas For Dry Lips

Herbal teas can be an effective and natural remedy for soothing and moisturizing dry lips.

Chamomile tea, for instance, is known for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce irritation and promote healing. To use it, simply brew a cup of the tea, let it cool, and then apply it to your lips with a clean cloth or cotton swab. Other beneficial herbs include calendula, lavender, and mint, which can provide additional nourishment and a refreshing sensation.

Regular use of these herbal teas can help maintain lip hydration and prevent future dryness.

1. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a powerful herb that can be used to make a soothing herbal tea for dry lips.

This plant is rich in minerals such as iron, silica, and vitamins A and C, which help nourish and hydrate the skin. To prepare the tea, simply steep a handful of dried nettle leaves in hot water for about 10 minutes. Applying the cooled tea to the lips with a clean cloth can provide relief from dryness and promote healing.

Regular use of stinging nettle tea may improve lip hydration and reduce the appearance of chapped skin.

2. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a gentle herbal remedy often used in teas to soothe and moisturize dry lips.

Its calming properties help reduce irritation and inflammation, making it an effective natural treatment for chapped or cracked lips. When brewed into a tea, lemon balm provides a subtle, pleasant aroma and can be applied directly to the lips for immediate relief. The tea's mild antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects promote healing and prevent further drying.

Regular use of lemon balm herbal tea can help maintain lip hydration and improve overall lip health.

3. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its soothing and healing properties.

When brewed into a tea, it can provide gentle relief for dry lips due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities. The tea is believed to help reduce irritation and promote the healing of cracked or chapped skin on the lips. It is often recommended as a natural remedy for those seeking a safe, plant-based solution for lip care.

However, it is advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before using yarrow tea, especially for those with sensitive skin or existing health conditions.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is often used in herbal teas to soothe and hydrate dry lips due to its calming and moisturizing properties.

The essential oils in lavender contain compounds like linalool and lavandic acid, which can help reduce inflammation and promote skin healing. When brewed into a tea, lavender can provide a gentle, aromatic remedy that not only hydrates the lips but also offers a calming effect. 6. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a fragrant herb often used in herbal teas for its refreshing and invigorating properties.

While rosemary is traditionally used for its aromatic and stimulating effects, it can also be beneficial for dry lips when used in a carefully prepared herbal infusion. The essential oils in rosemary, such as camphor and pinene, may help improve circulation and promote healing of dry, chapped skin. However, it is important to use rosemary tea in moderation, as some individuals may experience irritation or allergic reactions.

For best results, it is recommended to combine rosemary with other soothing herbs like chamomile or calendula to enhance its moisturizing and calming effects on the lips.

7. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its soothing and healing properties.

When brewed into a tea, it can provide relief for dry lips by moisturizing and reducing inflammation. The tea is believed to contain compounds that help promote skin healing and enhance the skin's natural barrier function. It is often recommended as a natural alternative to commercial lip balms, especially for those seeking herbal remedies.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before use, as it may interact with certain medications.

8. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is a versatile herb that has been traditionally used for its soothing and healing properties.

When brewed into a herbal tea, sage can provide relief for dry lips by moisturizing and reducing inflammation. Its natural antibacterial and anti-inflammatory compounds help to protect the delicate skin on the lips from irritation and infection. To prepare sage tea for dry lips, steep a few fresh or dried leaves in hot water for several minutes before applying it topically or drinking it.

Regular use of sage herbal tea can promote lip hydration and improve the overall health of the skin.

9. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, is a traditional herb that has been used for its high concentration of silica and other nutrients.

When brewed into a herbal tea, it can help soothe and nourish dry lips by promoting moisture retention and cell repair. The tea's astringent properties may also help tighten the skin around the lips, reducing flakiness and irritation. While it is not a direct treatment for dry lips, it can be a complementary remedy when used alongside moisturizing practices.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using equisetum arvense, especially for those with allergies or existing health conditions.

10. Pimpinella anisum

Pimpinella anisum, also known as anise, is a popular herb used in herbal teas for its soothing and nourishing properties.

This herb is rich in essential oils, vitamins, and minerals that can help hydrate and protect dry lips. When brewed into a warm tea, anise can provide a gentle, aromatic remedy that promotes moisture retention and reduces chapping. Its mild licorice-like flavor makes it pleasant to consume, and it can be enjoyed both hot and cold.

Regular consumption of anise herbal tea may help improve overall lip health and provide relief from dryness and irritation.
