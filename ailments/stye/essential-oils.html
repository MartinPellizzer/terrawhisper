10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Stye

Herbal essential oils, such as tea tree oil, eucalyptus oil, and lavender oil, are commonly used for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties to help treat styes.

These oils can be diluted with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil before applying them to the affected area to avoid skin irritation. Applying a few drops of the diluted essential oil to a clean cotton swab and gently pressing it against the stye may help reduce swelling and promote healing. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially for children or individuals with sensitive skin.

While essential oils can be a natural remedy, they should not replace medical treatment if the stye persists or worsens.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, commonly known as Tasmanian eucalyptus, is a popular source of essential oil used for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds like cineole and terpenes, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. When used for stye, eucalyptus globulus essential oil can help reduce inflammation and soothe the infected eyelid due to its natural antiseptic properties. However, it should always be diluted with a carrier oil before applying to the skin to avoid irritation.

While it may offer relief, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional for persistent or severe cases of stye.
