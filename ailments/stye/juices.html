10 Best Herbal Juices For Stye

Herbal juices can be a natural and soothing remedy for stye, which is an infection of the eyelid glands.

Commonly used herbs include echinacea, calendula, and chamomile, all of which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These juices can be applied topically as compresses or consumed internally to support the body's immune response. However, it's important to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal treatments, especially if the stye is severe or persistent.

While herbal juices may offer relief, they should not replace conventional medical treatment when necessary.

1. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential natural remedy for stye, a bacterial infection of the eyelid.

Herbal juices made from Hypericum perforatum may help reduce swelling and redness associated with styes due to their high concentration of flavonoids and hypericin. However, it is important to note that while some anecdotal evidence supports its use, scientific research on its efficacy for stye specifically is limited. Before using St. John's Wort juice, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional, as it can interact with certain medications.

Overall, Hypericum perforatum may offer some relief for stye symptoms, but it should not replace conventional medical treatments without professional guidance.

2. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties, making it a potential natural remedy for conditions like stye.

When prepared as a herbal juice, stinging nettle can help reduce swelling and redness around the eye by promoting drainage and reducing bacterial buildup. To make the juice, fresh nettle leaves are typically washed, blended with water, and then strained to remove the fibrous residue. It is often recommended to consume the juice internally or apply it topically with a clean cloth to soothe the affected area.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using stinging nettle, especially for individuals with allergies or existing medical conditions.

3. Dog rose (Rosa canina)

Rosa canina, commonly known as rose hip, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for its high concentration of vitamins, particularly vitamin C, and its anti-inflammatory properties.

When used in the form of herbal juices, rosa canina can support eye health and may help alleviate symptoms of a stye, which is an infection of the eyelid glands. The antioxidants and essential nutrients in rose hip juice can reduce inflammation and promote healing in the affected area. It is often recommended as a complementary treatment alongside conventional medical care for styes.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy, especially for eye-related conditions.

4. German chamomile (Chamomilla recutita)

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, is widely used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When prepared as a herbal juice, it can be applied topically to reduce swelling and redness associated with a stye. The active compounds in chamomile, such as bisabolol and flavonoids, help soothe the eye area and promote healing. To use chamomile juice for a stye, it is typically diluted with water or a carrier oil to avoid irritation.

While it may provide relief, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional for persistent or severe cases.

5. Thistle (Silybum marianum)

Silybum marianum, commonly known as milk thistle, has been traditionally used for its liver-protective properties, and its herbal juices are increasingly being explored for their potential benefits in treating stye, an inflammation of the eyelid often caused by bacterial infection or blocked oil glands.

The active compound, silymarin, possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce swelling and irritation associated with stye. While there is limited scientific evidence specifically supporting the use of milk thistle juice for stye, some anecdotal reports suggest it may aid in reducing symptoms when applied topically or consumed internally. It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using milk thistle juice, as it may interact with certain medications or have side effects in some individuals.

Overall, while it may offer some supportive benefits, milk thistle should not replace conventional treatments for stye.

6. Marigold (Calendula officinalis)

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as pot marigold, is a herbal remedy often used to treat stye, a painful red bump on the eyelid caused by a bacterial infection.

Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties make it effective in reducing swelling, redness, and discomfort associated with stye. Calendula herbal juices can be applied topically as a compress or diluted with water to create a soothing eye wash. These natural extracts help promote healing by supporting the body's immune response and preventing further infection.

Due to its gentle nature, calendula is considered a safe and alternative option for those seeking natural relief from stye symptoms.

7. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is often used in herbal juices to help alleviate symptoms of a stye, which is an infection of the eyelid.

Chamomile contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort associated with styes. To prepare the herbal juice, chamomile flowers are typically steeped in hot water and then cooled before being applied as a compress to the affected eye. This natural remedy is considered safe for most adults and can be used as a complementary treatment alongside conventional medical care.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using chamomile juice, especially if you have allergies or are using other medications.

8. Yarrow (Achillea millefolium)

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When prepared as a herbal juice, it may offer potential benefits for conditions like stye, which is an infection of the eyelid glands. The juice is believed to help reduce swelling and redness associated with styes due to its high content of flavonoids and essential oils. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using yarrow juice, as it may cause allergic reactions or interact with certain medications.

While some individuals may find relief from using yarrow-based remedies, scientific evidence supporting its efficacy for styes is limited.

9. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for stye, which is an infection of the eyelid.

Herbal juices made from fresh or dried sage leaves can be applied topically to the affected area to help reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort associated with stye. These juices contain compounds like thujone and rosmarinic acid, which may contribute to their healing effects on the eye. While sage juice may offer some relief, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially if the stye persists or worsens.

Overall, sage herbal juice can be a complementary treatment to support the body's natural healing process when used safely and appropriately.

10. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is a natural remedy that has been used for centuries to treat various skin and eye conditions, including styes.

The gel extracted from the aloe plant contains anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help reduce swelling and infection associated with styes. Applying aloe vera gel directly to the affected area can provide soothing relief and promote faster healing. It is often recommended to use pure, unscented aloe vera gel to avoid irritation.

While aloe vera can be a helpful complementary treatment, it should not replace professional medical advice, especially if the stye persists or worsens.
