10 Best Herbal Juices For Stye

Herbal juices can be a natural and soothing remedy for stye, which is an infection of the oil glands in the eyelid.

Common herbs used in these juices include calendula, chamomile, and echinacea, all known for their anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. To prepare the juice, fresh herbs are typically blended with water or a mild vegetable juice to create a nourishing and easy-to-consume beverage. Drinking these herbal juices can help support the body's immune system and reduce the duration of the stye.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

1. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential natural remedy for stye, which is an infection of the eyelid glands.

Herbal juices made from Hypericum perforatum may help reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort associated with stye by promoting healing and fighting bacterial infections. To prepare the juice, fresh leaves and flowers are typically crushed and strained to extract the active compounds. While some people use this remedy externally by applying the juice directly to the affected area, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you are on medication or have sensitive skin.

Despite its traditional use, scientific evidence supporting its effectiveness for stye is limited, so it should be used as a complementary therapy rather than a substitute for medical treatment.

2. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

When prepared as a juice, it can help reduce swelling and discomfort associated with a stye, which is a painful infection of the eyelid. The juice is typically made by blending fresh nettle leaves with water or a mild juice, and it is often consumed internally to support overall eye health. Some people also apply the juice externally as a compress to soothe the affected area.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using nettle juice, especially if you have allergies or are taking medications.

3. German chamomile (Chamomilla recutita)

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, has been traditionally used for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for natural remedies.

When prepared as a herbal juice, chamomilla recutita can help alleviate symptoms of a stye, which is an infection of the eyelid glands. The juice contains compounds like apigenin and bisabolol that may reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort associated with styes. To use it, a few drops of the diluted chamomile juice can be applied to the affected eye using a clean cotton swab.

While it can provide relief, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional if the stye persists or worsens, as more severe cases may require medical intervention.

4. Thistle (Silybum marianum)

Silybum marianum, commonly known as milk thistle, is a herbal remedy often used in the form of juice to support eye health and potentially aid in the treatment of stye.

The active compound in milk thistle, silymarin, is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce swelling and infection associated with stye. When consumed as a juice, it can support overall liver function, which in turn may contribute to better eye health. However, it is important to note that while some anecdotal evidence suggests it may be beneficial, there is limited scientific research specifically linking milk thistle juice to the treatment of stye.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.

5. Dog rose (Rosa canina)

Rosa canina, also known as dog rose, is a traditional herbal remedy often used in the preparation of herbal juices to support eye health and alleviate symptoms of stye.

The juice derived from the fruit of Rosa canina is rich in vitamin C, antioxidants, and other bioactive compounds that may help reduce inflammation and promote healing. When consumed internally, it is believed to strengthen the immune system and support the body's natural defenses against infections, including those affecting the eyelids. Some herbalists recommend using Rosa canina juice as a complementary treatment alongside conventional medical care for stye.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.

6. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a herbal remedy often used to soothe eye conditions such as stye due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Chamomile herbal juice, typically prepared by steeping the dried flowers in hot water, can be applied topically to the affected area to reduce redness and swelling. This natural treatment is considered gentle and safe for most individuals, though it should not replace professional medical advice. Some studies suggest that the essential oils in chamomile may help in reducing bacterial growth, which is a common cause of stye.

While chamomile juice can provide relief, it is important to consult a healthcare provider if the stye persists or worsens.

7. Aloe vera (Aloe barbadensis)

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is widely used in natural remedies for its soothing and healing properties.

Aloe vera juice is often recommended for treating styes, which are painful red bumps on the eyelid caused by bacterial infections or blocked oil glands. The gel and juice from the aloe plant contain anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial compounds that can help reduce swelling, redness, and infection around the affected area. Applying aloe vera juice directly to the stye can provide relief and promote faster healing due to its hydrating and regenerative effects.

However, it is important to use pure aloe vera juice and consult a healthcare professional if the stye persists or worsens.

8. Blessed thistle (Cnicus benedictus)

Cnicus benedictus, commonly known as blessed thorn or St. Benedict's thorn, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

While there is limited scientific evidence supporting its direct use for stye treatment, some herbal practitioners recommend preparing a juice from the leaves and flowers of the plant to apply topically. The juice is believed to help reduce swelling and redness associated with styes due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy, as it may interact with other medications or cause allergic reactions.

Overall, while Cnicus benedictus may offer some natural relief for styes, it should be used cautiously and in conjunction with conventional medical care.

9. Thyme (Thymus vulgaris)

Thymus vulgaris, commonly known as thyme, is often used in herbal remedies for its potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

When prepared as a herbal juice, thyme can help alleviate symptoms of a stye, which is an infection of the eyelid caused by bacteria. The essential oils in thyme, particularly thymol, work to reduce inflammation and combat the bacterial infection responsible for the stye. To use thyme juice for a stye, it is typically diluted with water or a carrier oil to avoid irritation.

While thyme juice may provide relief, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional for persistent or severe cases.

10. Echinacea (Echinacea purpurea)

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a popular herbal remedy often used to support immune health.

While it is not a direct treatment for stye, some people use echinacea-based herbal juices to help reduce inflammation and boost the body's natural defenses against infection. These juices are typically made by extracting the roots or leaves of the plant and are available in various forms, including tinctures and liquid extracts. However, it is important to note that echinacea should not replace conventional medical treatments for stye, and consulting a healthcare professional is recommended.

Always ensure that the product is of high quality and free from harmful additives when using echinacea for any health condition.
