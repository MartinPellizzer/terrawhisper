10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Frozen Shoulder

Herbal essential oils, such as eucalyptus, peppermint, and lavender, are often used in aromatherapy to help alleviate the symptoms of frozen shoulder, which includes stiffness, pain, and limited range of motion.

These oils can be diluted in a carrier oil and applied topically to the affected area to promote circulation and reduce inflammation. Some studies suggest that the anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties of these essential oils may provide natural relief for musculoskeletal discomfort. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you have sensitive skin or are pregnant.

When used properly, herbal essential oils can be a complementary therapy to support overall shoulder health and mobility.

1. Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, contains a bioactive compound called curcumin, which is often extracted into essential oils and used for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These essential oils may help reduce pain and inflammation associated with frozen shoulder by inhibiting inflammatory pathways in the body. However, it is important to note that essential oils derived from Curcuma longa are typically not the same as the powdered root used in traditional remedies, as they contain different concentrations of active compounds. While some studies suggest potential benefits, more research is needed to confirm their efficacy for frozen shoulder specifically.

As with any complementary therapy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using Curcuma longa essential oils for this condition.

2. Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus)

Eucalyptus globulus, commonly known as Australian eucalyptus, is a popular source of essential oil renowned for its therapeutic properties.

When used in the context of frozen shoulder, this essential oil may help alleviate pain and stiffness through its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. It is often diluted with a carrier oil before application to the affected area to ensure safety and effectiveness. The aromatic properties of eucalyptus globulus essential oil can also promote relaxation and reduce stress, which may indirectly support the healing process.

While it is not a substitute for medical treatment, it can be a valuable complementary therapy when used under professional guidance.

3. Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, is a herb whose essential oils have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These essential oils contain compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which may help reduce pain and inflammation associated with frozen shoulder. When applied topically, ginger essential oil can improve blood circulation and promote muscle relaxation, offering potential relief for stiffness and discomfort. However, it is important to dilute the oil with a carrier oil before application to avoid skin irritation.

While not a substitute for medical treatment, ginger essential oil may serve as a complementary therapy in managing the symptoms of frozen shoulder.

4. Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis)

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a herb whose essential oil has been widely used for its therapeutic properties, including its potential benefits for frozen shoulder.

The essential oil contains compounds like cineole and camphor, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, making them useful in reducing pain and stiffness associated with frozen shoulder. When applied topically, rosemary essential oil can help improve circulation and promote muscle relaxation, aiding in the recovery process. It is often diluted with a carrier oil before use to prevent skin irritation and ensure safe application.

Incorporating rosemary essential oil into a holistic treatment plan may offer natural relief for individuals suffering from frozen shoulder, though it should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

5. English lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is widely recognized for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice in aromatherapy.

Its essential oil contains compounds such as linalool and lavandins, which have been shown to reduce pain and muscle tension, offering relief for conditions like frozen shoulder. When used in massage or diffused in a room, lavender essential oil can help ease the stiffness and discomfort associated with frozen shoulder. It is often combined with other essential oils like eucalyptus or peppermint to enhance its therapeutic effects.

However, it is important to dilute the oil properly and consult with a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with sensitive skin or underlying health conditions.

6. Melaleuca (Melaleuca alternifolia)

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree oil, is a popular essential oil derived from the leaves of the Melaleuca alternifolia plant, native to Australia.

While it is widely used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, its application in treating frozen shoulder is less common and typically involves aromatherapy or topical use. Some proponents suggest that its anti-inflammatory effects may help reduce pain and stiffness associated with frozen shoulder, although scientific evidence supporting its efficacy for this specific condition is limited. When used, it is often diluted with a carrier oil to prevent skin irritation and is sometimes combined with other essential oils for enhanced therapeutic effects.

Despite its potential benefits, individuals with frozen shoulder should consult a healthcare professional before using melaleuca alternifolia essential oil as part of their treatment plan.

7. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These essential oils, particularly those rich in compounds like thujone and camphor, may help reduce pain and stiffness associated with frozen shoulder by promoting circulation and easing muscle tension. While scientific research on its direct effects on frozen shoulder is limited, some studies suggest that aromatic inhalation or topical application of sage oil may offer therapeutic benefits. When used in combination with other holistic treatments, such as heat therapy or gentle stretching, sage essential oils could potentially support the rehabilitation process.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using these oils, especially to ensure safe and appropriate application methods.

8. Chaste tree (Vitex agnus-castus)

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, is often used in herbal medicine for its potential hormonal balancing properties.

While it is traditionally used for menstrual and menopausal symptoms, some studies suggest it may also have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects that could benefit individuals with frozen shoulder. Essential oils derived from Vitex agnus-castus are sometimes used in aromatherapy and topical applications to reduce muscle tension and improve circulation. However, it is important to note that there is limited scientific research specifically on the effectiveness of Vitex essential oils for frozen shoulder.

As with any complementary therapy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if other medical treatments are already in place.

9. Black pepper (Piper nigrum)

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These essential oils, derived from the dried fruit of the Piper nigrum plant, may help reduce pain and improve mobility in individuals with frozen shoulder due to their ability to enhance circulation and reduce muscle stiffness. While scientific research on the specific use of black pepper essential oils for frozen shoulder is limited, some studies suggest that the active compounds in these oils, such as piperine, may have therapeutic effects when applied topically. When used as part of a holistic approach, piper nigrum essential oils can complement other treatments like physical therapy and stretching exercises.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using these oils, especially if you have sensitive skin or are taking other medications.

10. Ceylon cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum)

Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their warming and anti-inflammatory properties.

These oils, derived from the bark of the cinnamon tree, may help alleviate the stiffness and pain associated with frozen shoulder due to their potential ability to improve circulation and reduce muscle tension. The aromatic compounds in cinnamon essential oil, such as cinnamaldehyde and eugenol, are believed to have analgesic and antispasmodic effects that may support joint mobility. When used in aromatherapy or diluted and applied topically, cinnamon essential oil can offer a natural, complementary approach to managing frozen shoulder symptoms.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially for chronic conditions like frozen shoulder.
