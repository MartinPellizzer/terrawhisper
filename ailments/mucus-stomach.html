What causes mucus in stomach?

The primary causes of mucus in stomach are gastrointestinal infections, such as gastroenteritis, and inflammatory conditions, such as gastritis, which lead to inflammation and irritation in the stomach lining, causing it to produce excess mucus.

Secondary causes can include food poisoning, certain medications like antacids, and even allergies, which can further exacerbate the condition by irritating the stomach lining.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing mucus in stomach include consuming spicy or fatty foods, which can put additional pressure on the digestive system, and smoking, which can lead to chronic inflammation and irritation in the stomach.

Additionally, individuals with weakened immune systems, such as those with chronic illnesses or taking immunosuppressive medications, are also more susceptible to developing mucus in stomach.
