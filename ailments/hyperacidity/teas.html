10 Best Herbal Teas For Hyperacidity

Chamomile, licorice root, and ginger are commonly used herbs that possess anti-inflammatory and antacid properties. These teas can help reduce heartburn and indigestion by calming the stomach lining and promoting digestion. It is important to consult a healthcare provider before using herbal teas, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking medications.

1. Camellia (Camellia sinensis)

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green and black teas are derived, contains bioactive compounds that may help alleviate symptoms of hyperacidity.

These compounds, including polyphenols and catechins, possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can soothe the digestive tract. When consumed as herbal teas, Camellia sinensis can help neutralize excess stomach acid and reduce irritation in the stomach lining. However, it is important to note that some individuals may experience increased acid production due to caffeine content, so it is advisable to opt for decaffeinated varieties.

Overall, Camellia sinensis herbal teas can be a beneficial natural remedy for managing hyperacidity when used appropriately.

2. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, is a traditional herbal remedy often used in teas to help manage hyperacidity.

The plant contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which may help reduce stomach acid production and soothe the lining of the gastrointestinal tract. When brewed as a tea, licorice root is believed to have anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties that can alleviate symptoms such as heartburn and indigestion. However, long-term use of licorice root tea should be approached with caution, as excessive consumption may lead to side effects like hypertension or fluid retention.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before using licorice root tea, especially for individuals with pre-existing health conditions.

3. Cumin (Cuminum cyminum)

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is often used in herbal teas to help manage hyperacidity due to its natural antacid properties.

The essential oils in cumin, such as limonene and cumin aldehyde, help neutralize stomach acid and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Drinking cumin tea regularly can soothe heartburn and indigestion by promoting the production of protective mucus in the stomach lining. It is also believed to enhance digestion and relieve bloating, making it a popular remedy in traditional medicine.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using cumin tea, especially for individuals with existing gastrointestinal conditions.

Silybum marianum, commonly known as milk thistle, is often used in herbal teas to help manage hyperacidity due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

Herbal teas made from silybum marianum can be a natural alternative for individuals seeking relief from symptoms like heartburn and indigestion. However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using it, especially if you are on medication or have underlying health conditions.

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, is a herb often used in herbal teas to alleviate symptoms of hyperacidity.

Fennel tea is believed to help reduce excess stomach acid by promoting the production of digestive enzymes and easing the movement of food through the digestive tract. It is also thought to have a calming effect on the stomach lining, reducing irritation and discomfort.
