10 Best Herbal Teas For Hyperacidity

Herbal teas can be a natural and effective remedy for managing hyperacidity, as they often contain ingredients that help neutralize stomach acid and soothe the digestive system.

Chamomile, ginger, fennel, and licorice root are commonly used herbs known for their anti-inflammatory and antacid properties. These teas work by reducing inflammation in the stomach lining and promoting the production of protective mucus, which can alleviate symptoms like heartburn and indigestion. Drinking herbal teas regularly can also help regulate digestion and reduce the frequency of acid reflux episodes.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating these teas into a treatment plan, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

1. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green, black, and white teas are derived, contains compounds that may help alleviate symptoms of hyperacidity.

These teas are rich in antioxidants and polyphenols, which can reduce inflammation in the stomach lining and neutralize excess stomach acid. Certain varieties, such as green tea, are particularly noted for their soothing properties due to their high concentration of catechins. While not a substitute for medical treatment, moderate consumption of Camellia sinensis-based herbal teas may offer natural relief for individuals suffering from hyperacidity.

It is important to consult a healthcare provider before incorporating these teas into a regimen, especially for those with pre-existing digestive conditions.

2. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to alleviate symptoms of hyperacidity.

The essential oils in cumin, particularly cuminaldehyde and limonene, help neutralize excess stomach acid and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Regular consumption of cumin tea can soothe heartburn and indigestion by promoting the production of digestive enzymes. It is often recommended as a natural remedy due to its mild flavor and high concentration of antioxidants.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using cumin tea, especially for individuals with existing gastrointestinal conditions.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to help alleviate symptoms of hyperacidity.

The root contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which may help reduce stomach acid production and soothe the lining of the gastrointestinal tract. When brewed into a tea, licorice root can provide a calming effect on the digestive system, making it a popular remedy for heartburn and indigestion. However, excessive consumption of licorice tea may lead to side effects such as hypertension due to its potassium-depleting properties.

As a result, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using licorice root tea, especially for long-term or chronic hyperacidity issues.

Peppermint tea can also stimulate digestion and promote the production of digestive enzymes, which may help in managing acid reflux and indigestion. Regular consumption of peppermint tea in moderate amounts can provide relief from heartburn and other symptoms of hyperacidity without the side effects of conventional antacids.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating peppermint tea into a treatment regimen, especially for individuals with severe or chronic hyperacidity.

5. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is often used in herbal teas to help manage hyperacidity due to its warming and digestive properties.
