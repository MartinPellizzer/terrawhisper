10 Best Herbal Lozenges For Hyperacidity

Herbal lozenges are natural remedies designed to alleviate symptoms of hyperacidity by soothing the throat and reducing excess stomach acid reflux.

These lozenges often contain ingredients like licorice root, ginger, and fenugreek, which have anti-inflammatory and antacid properties. They work by creating a protective barrier in the throat and esophagus, minimizing irritation caused by acid reflux. Unlike conventional antacids, herbal lozenges are generally gentler on the digestive system and may offer long-term relief without harsh side effects.

However, they should be used as part of a comprehensive approach that includes dietary changes and lifestyle modifications for optimal results.

1. Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal lozenges for managing hyperacidity.

These lozenges work by neutralizing excess stomach acid and reducing the irritation caused by acid reflux, providing relief from heartburn and indigestion. The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, help in reducing gastric acid secretion and improving digestion. Herbal lozenges containing zingiber officinale are often preferred over conventional antacids due to their natural composition and minimal side effects.

They are particularly beneficial for individuals seeking alternative or complementary remedies for mild to moderate hyperacidity symptoms.

2. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, is often used in herbal lozenges to alleviate symptoms of hyperacidity due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These lozenges work by coating the mucous membranes of the throat and stomach, providing a protective barrier against excess stomach acid. The active compound, glycyrrhizin, helps reduce inflammation and may inhibit the activity of hydrogen-potassium ATPase, the enzyme responsible for acid secretion. Additionally, licorice root has mild antispasmodic effects that can ease discomfort associated with heartburn and indigestion.

While generally safe for short-term use, prolonged consumption of licorice-based lozenges may lead to side effects such as hypertension, making it important to consult a healthcare provider before use.
