1. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, is often used in herbal formulations to address a sour taste in the mouth.

The active compounds in licorice root, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, have soothing and anti-inflammatory properties that can help neutralize acidic or sour sensations. Herbal capsules containing Glycyrrhiza glabra are designed to be convenient and easy to incorporate into daily health routines. These capsules are typically used to support digestive health and may help alleviate symptoms like heartburn or sour taste caused by stomach issues.

However, long-term use should be monitored due to potential side effects related to its effects on blood pressure and electrolyte balance.

2. Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, is widely used in herbal capsules to address a sour taste in the mouth, often associated with digestive discomfort or nausea.

These capsules contain the dried rhizome of the ginger plant, which is known for its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties. The pungent and spicy nature of ginger helps neutralize acidic compounds in the mouth, thereby reducing the sour taste. Regular consumption of ginger capsules can support digestive health and alleviate symptoms related to an upset stomach.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using ginger supplements, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking medications.
