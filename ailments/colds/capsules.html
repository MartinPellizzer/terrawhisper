1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a popular herbal remedy often used to support immune health and alleviate symptoms of the common cold.

Herbal capsules containing Echinacea purpurea are typically made from the dried root or herb of the plant and are available in various formulations. These supplements are believed to help reduce the duration and severity of cold symptoms by boosting the body's immune response. Many people use them at the onset of a cold to prevent it from worsening, although scientific evidence on their effectiveness remains mixed.

Despite this, Echinacea purpurea remains a widely used and well-known herbal option for those seeking natural remedies for colds.

7. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is often used in herbal capsules to help alleviate symptoms of colds due to its soothing and decongestant properties.

These capsules typically contain concentrated extracts of fresh or dried peppermint leaves, which are rich in menthol and other essential oils that can help reduce nasal congestion and ease throat irritation. 8. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, is a widely used herbal remedy for alleviating cold symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

Licorice root capsules are often taken to soothe sore throats, reduce coughing, and ease congestion by thinning mucus and promoting its expulsion from the respiratory tract. The active compounds in licorice, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, contribute to its effectiveness in supporting the immune system and reducing viral replication. However, excessive use of licorice root can lead to side effects like hypertension and fluid retention due to its mineralocorticoid-like effects.

As a result, it is recommended to use licorice root capsules under the guidance of a healthcare professional, especially for prolonged periods or in individuals with pre-existing health conditions.
