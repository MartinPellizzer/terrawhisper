10 Best Herbal Teas For Restless Leg Syndrome

Herbal teas have gained popularity as a natural remedy for managing symptoms of restless leg syndrome (RLS).

Certain herbs, such as valerian root, chamomile, and lemon balm, are believed to promote relaxation and reduce nerve-related discomfort. These teas may help calm the nervous system and improve sleep quality, which is often affected in individuals with RLS. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating herbal teas into a treatment plan, as they can interact with medications or have side effects.

While herbal teas may offer some relief, they should be used as a complementary therapy rather than a replacement for medical treatment.

1. Valerian (Valeriana officinalis)

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to promote relaxation and improve sleep.

Herbal teas made from valerian root are often recommended for individuals suffering from restless leg syndrome (RLS), as they may help reduce the uncomfortable sensations and urge to move the legs. The active compounds in valerian, such as valerenic acid and isol valerenic acid, are believed to interact with the central nervous system to calm overactive nerves. While scientific evidence supporting its efficacy for RLS is limited, many users report improved sleep quality and reduced symptoms when consuming valerian tea regularly.

As with any herbal remedy, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you are taking other medications or have underlying health conditions.

2. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential benefits in managing symptoms of restless leg syndrome (RLS).

The herb contains compounds such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which may help reduce inflammation and support nerve function, potentially alleviating the uncomfortable sensations associated with RLS. Some studies suggest that licorice root may help regulate neurotransmitters like dopamine, which are involved in the neurological processes of RLS. While more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness, many individuals report improved sleep and reduced restlessness after incorporating licorice root tea into their routine.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with hypertension or other health conditions.

3. Maypop (Passiflora incarnata)

Passiflora incarnata, commonly known as passionflower, is a herbal remedy that has shown potential in alleviating symptoms of restless leg syndrome (RLS).

This plant contains compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids, which may help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, both of which are often associated with RLS. Passionflower herbal teas are typically made by steeping the dried leaves or flowers in hot water, creating a calming beverage that can be consumed regularly. Some studies suggest that its sedative properties may help ease the uncomfortable sensations and urge to move the legs that characterize RLS.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using passionflower, especially for individuals taking medications for sleep or anxiety, as it may interact with certain drugs.

4. Echinacea (Echinacea purpurea)

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a herbal remedy often used for its potential immune-boosting properties.

While it is traditionally associated with colds and infections, some research suggests it may also have applications for neurological conditions like restless leg syndrome (RLS). Herbal teas made from echinacea are believed to promote relaxation and may help alleviate the uncomfortable sensations associated with RLS by reducing inflammation and supporting nerve function. However, more clinical studies are needed to confirm its efficacy for this specific condition.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before incorporating echinacea into a treatment plan for RLS.

5. Lemon balm (Melissa officinalis)

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to support relaxation and reduce anxiety.

Some studies suggest that the calming properties of lemon balm may help alleviate symptoms of restless leg syndrome (RLS) by promoting a sense of tranquility and reducing nervous system overactivity. While there is no strong clinical evidence specifically linking lemon balm to the treatment of RLS, its mild sedative effects may contribute to improved sleep quality, which is often affected in individuals with RLS. Herbal teas made from Melissa officinalis are generally safe for most adults when consumed in moderation, though they should not replace prescribed treatments for severe cases.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before incorporating lemon balm into a treatment plan for restless leg syndrome.

6. Camellia (Camellia sinensis)

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green and black teas are derived, contains compounds like caffeine and L-theanine that may influence neurological activity and potentially alleviate symptoms of restless leg syndrome (RLS).

While there is limited direct research on Camellia sinensis specifically for RLS, its caffeine content can act as a mild stimulant, which might exacerbate RLS symptoms in some individuals, making it a double-edged sword. On the other hand, the amino acid L-theanine found in Camellia sinensis has been shown to promote relaxation and may help reduce anxiety, a common comorbidity in RLS. Some studies suggest that reducing caffeine intake can improve RLS symptoms, implying that herbal teas made from Camellia sinensis with lower caffeine levels might be beneficial for those with the condition.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using Camellia sinensis or any herbal remedy for RLS, as individual responses can vary significantly.

7. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to alleviate symptoms of restless leg syndrome (RLS).

The tea is believed to support nerve function and reduce inflammation, which may contribute to the discomfort associated with RLS. Rich in minerals such as iron, magnesium, and potassium, stinging nettle tea may help address nutrient deficiencies that are often linked to restless leg syndrome. Some studies suggest that the compounds in stinging nettle may promote relaxation of muscles and improve circulation, offering potential relief for those suffering from RLS.

While more research is needed, many individuals find that incorporating urtica dioica herbal tea into their routine can be a natural and soothing complement to managing restless leg syndrome.

8. Hemp (Cannabis sativa)

Cannabis sativa herbal teas have gained attention as a potential natural remedy for restless leg syndrome (RLS), due to their calming and sedative properties.

These teas typically contain cannabinoids like CBD, which may help reduce nerve irritation and promote relaxation, alleviating the uncomfortable sensations associated with RLS. While research on cannabis for RLS is still emerging, some studies suggest that it may help improve sleep quality and reduce the frequency of leg movements during the night. However, it is important to note that the use of cannabis products should be approached with caution, as they can have side effects and may interact with other medications.

Individuals considering cannabis sativa herbal teas for RLS should consult with a healthcare professional to ensure safe and effective treatment.

9. English lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, has been traditionally used for its calming properties, and recent studies suggest it may offer relief for individuals suffering from restless leg syndrome (RLS).

The essential oils in lavender contain compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have been shown to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, potentially alleviating the uncomfortable sensations associated with RLS. When brewed into a herbal tea, lavender can be consumed regularly to support overall relaxation and improve sleep quality, both of which are crucial for managing RLS symptoms. While more research is needed to confirm its efficacy, many users report a soothing effect from lavender tea, making it a natural and accessible option for symptom relief.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before incorporating lavender tea into a treatment plan for RLS.

10. Peppermint (Mentha piperita)

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to help alleviate symptoms of restless leg syndrome (RLS).
