10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Hemorrhoids

Herbal essential oils, such as lavender, chamomile, and peppermint, are often used for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties to help alleviate symptoms of hemorrhoids.

These oils can be applied topically after proper dilution with a carrier oil to reduce swelling, irritation, and discomfort in the affected area. Some studies suggest that certain essential oils may promote healing by improving circulation and reducing inflammation, though more research is needed to confirm their effectiveness. It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially during pregnancy or if you have sensitive skin.

While they can be a complementary therapy, they should not replace medical treatment for severe or chronic hemorrhoids.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is often used in the form of essential oils for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, which can be beneficial for individuals suffering from hemorrhoids.

These essential oils are derived from the gel-like substance inside the aloe leaf and are known to promote skin healing and reduce irritation. When applied topically, aloe barbadensis essential oils can help alleviate the discomfort, swelling, and itching associated with hemorrhoids. However, it is important to dilute the essential oil with a carrier oil before applying to avoid skin irritation.

While aloe vera can be a natural remedy for hemorrhoid relief, it should not replace professional medical advice, especially for severe or persistent cases.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as pot marigold, is a herb widely used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

Its essential oil, derived from the flowers through steam distillation, contains compounds such as bisabolol and flavonoids that can soothe irritated tissues and promote wound healing. When used topically, calendula essential oil may help reduce swelling and redness associated with hemorrhoids due to its antiseptic and astringent effects. However, it is important to dilute the essential oil with a carrier oil before applying it to the skin to avoid irritation.

While some studies suggest potential benefits, more clinical research is needed to fully establish its efficacy for treating hemorrhoids.

3. Rosa canina

Rosa canina, also known as dog rose, is a traditional herb that has been used for centuries in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

Rosa canina essential oil, derived from the flower of the plant, is believed to support the healing of hemorrhoids due to its ability to reduce swelling and irritation. This essential oil may help soothe the delicate tissues of the rectal area, promoting faster recovery from hemorrhoidal symptoms. It is often used in topical applications, such as in suppositories or creams, to alleviate discomfort and improve circulation.

While it is considered a natural remedy, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using Rosa canina essential oil for hemorrhoids, especially if you have any underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.

These oils have been traditionally used to support healing in conditions like hemorrhoids due to their ability to reduce swelling and strengthen blood vessel walls. When applied topically, the essential oils of Equisetum arvense may help alleviate discomfort, reduce inflammation, and promote the healing of irritated anal tissues.

8. Melaleuca alternifolia

Its effectiveness for hemorrhoids is not well-supported by clinical studies, and there is limited evidence to suggest it directly addresses the underlying causes of the condition.
