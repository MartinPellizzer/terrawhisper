10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Itchy Scalp

Herbal essential oils are natural remedies that can help alleviate an itchy scalp by soothing irritation and reducing inflammation.

Oils such as lavender, tea tree, and peppermint are commonly used due to their antimicrobial and antifungal properties, which can combat dandruff and scalp infections. These oils can be diluted with a carrier oil and applied directly to the scalp or mixed into a shampoo for regular use. They also have a calming effect that may help reduce stress-related itching.

However, it's important to perform a patch test first to avoid any allergic reactions.

1. Melaleuca (Melaleuca alternifolia)

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree oil, is a popular essential oil derived from the leaves of the Melaleuca alternifolia plant, native to Australia.

It is widely recognized for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties, making it a valuable natural remedy for various skin conditions, including an itchy scalp. When diluted properly with a carrier oil, tea tree oil can help reduce dandruff, fungal infections, and irritation that contribute to scalp itchiness. Its ability to soothe the skin and combat common scalp pathogens makes it a favored choice in both aromatherapy and dermatological treatments.

Regular use of tea tree oil in scalp treatments may provide relief from persistent itchiness and promote a healthier, more comfortable scalp environment.

2. English lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is a popular herb used in essential oils for its calming and therapeutic properties.

The essential oil derived from this plant is widely recognized for its ability to soothe skin irritations, making it a valuable remedy for an itchy scalp. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce scalp inflammation and combat fungal or bacterial infections that may contribute to itching. When diluted with a carrier oil, lavender essential oil can be safely applied to the scalp to provide relief and promote a healthier environment for hair growth.

Regular use of lavender essential oil may help alleviate chronic scalp conditions and improve overall scalp health.

3. Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis)

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a versatile herbal plant whose essential oil is widely used for its therapeutic properties, including its ability to soothe an itchy scalp.

The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation of the leaves and flowers, resulting in a fragrant, stimulating oil rich in compounds like camphor and cineole. It is known to improve blood circulation to the scalp, which can reduce dandruff and alleviate itchiness caused by dryness or irritation. When diluted properly with a carrier oil, rosemary essential oil can be safely applied to the scalp to provide a calming and refreshing effect.

Its natural antifungal and antimicrobial properties also make it effective in treating scalp conditions such as seborrheic dermatitis.

4. Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus)

Eucalyptus globulus, also known as blue gum eucalyptus, is a popular source of essential oil used for its therapeutic properties, including its ability to soothe an itchy scalp.

The essential oil contains compounds like cineole and limonene, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce scalp irritation and fungal infections. When diluted properly with a carrier oil, eucalyptus globulus oil can be applied topically to relieve itching and promote a healthier scalp environment. Its fresh, cooling aroma also has a calming effect, making it a favored choice in natural remedies for scalp care.

However, it is important to perform a patch test before use to avoid potential skin sensitivities.

5. Pogostemon (Pogostemon cablin)

Pogostemon cablin, also known as patchouli, is a popular herb known for its rich, earthy aroma and various therapeutic properties.

Its essential oil is widely used in aromatherapy and natural remedies for its potential benefits in addressing scalp issues. When applied topically, patchouli essential oil may help soothe an itchy scalp due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often diluted with a carrier oil before use to prevent skin irritation.

Many people find that incorporating patchouli oil into their scalp care routine can provide relief from itchiness and promote a healthier scalp environment.

6. Yarrow (Achillea millefolium)

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

These oils can help soothe an itchy scalp by reducing irritation and redness caused by fungal infections or dandruff. The calming effects of yarrow essential oil may also promote a sense of relaxation and comfort during scalp treatments. When diluted properly, it can be safely applied to the scalp to alleviate discomfort and support overall scalp health.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with sensitive skin or existing scalp conditions.

7. Ceylon cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum)

Cinnamomum zeylanicum, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, produces a warm, sweet, and spicy essential oil that is often used in aromatherapy for its therapeutic properties.

This essential oil is particularly beneficial for an itchy scalp due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce fungal infections and irritation. When applied topically, it can soothe the scalp and promote a healthy environment for hair growth. It is important to dilute the oil with a carrier oil before use to avoid skin irritation.

Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oil can be a valuable natural remedy for those seeking relief from an itchy scalp without the use of harsh chemical treatments.

8. Lemon grass (Cymbopogon citratus)

Cymbopogon citratus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a versatile herb that produces a refreshing essential oil with potential benefits for scalp health.

This essential oil is rich in citral, a compound known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate itchiness and irritation on the scalp. When used in diluted form, lemon grass essential oil can be applied topically to soothe an itchy scalp and reduce dandruff-related discomfort. Its natural scent also provides a calming effect, making it a pleasant addition to scalp care routines.

However, it is important to perform a patch test and consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially for those with sensitive skin or existing scalp conditions.

9. Sandalwood (Santalum album)

Santalum album, also known as Indian sandalwood, is a prized source of essential oil that is often used for its calming and soothing properties.

When applied to the scalp, this essential oil can help alleviate itchiness by reducing inflammation and balancing the skin's natural oils. Its mild antifungal and antimicrobial properties make it effective in addressing scalp conditions such as dandruff and fungal infections. To use it safely, the oil should be diluted with a carrier oil before application to prevent skin irritation.

Overall, Santalum album essential oil offers a natural and aromatic solution for those seeking relief from an itchy scalp.

10. Common teucrium (Teucrium marum)

Teucrium marum, also known as germander, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their soothing and antifungal properties, making them beneficial for alleviating an itchy scalp.

The essential oils derived from this plant may help reduce inflammation and irritation, providing relief from conditions like dandruff or fungal infections. These oils can be used in scalp massages or diluted in a carrier oil to create a calming topical treatment. Due to their potential antimicrobial effects, they may support scalp health by combating harmful microorganisms.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using these oils, especially if you have sensitive skin or underlying health conditions.
