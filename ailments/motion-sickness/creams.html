10 Best Herbal Creams For Motion Sickness

Herbal creams for motion sickness are topical treatments that aim to alleviate the symptoms of nausea and dizziness associated with motion-related discomfort.

These creams often contain natural ingredients such as ginger, peppermint, and lavender, which are known for their soothing and calming properties. While they are not a cure for motion sickness, they may help ease symptoms by stimulating blood flow and reducing stomach discomfort. Many people find them useful as a complementary remedy, especially when used in conjunction with other strategies like avoiding heavy meals before traveling.

However, it's important to consult a healthcare professional before using any herbal product, especially for those with allergies or underlying health conditions.

1. Ceylon cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum)

Cinnamomum verum, also known as true cinnamon, has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its soothing and calming properties.

When incorporated into herbal creams, it may help alleviate symptoms of motion sickness by reducing nausea and promoting a sense of well-being. These creams typically combine cinnamon with other natural ingredients like ginger or peppermint to enhance their effectiveness. The warming sensation from cinnamon can help stimulate digestion and ease stomach discomfort.

While more research is needed, some users report that applying these creams to the wrists or temples provides relief from motion sickness symptoms.

2. Black pepper (Piper nigrum)

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is often used in herbal remedies for its potential to alleviate symptoms of motion sickness.

The active compound in black pepper, piperine, is believed to enhance the absorption of other herbs and may help reduce nausea by stimulating digestive enzymes. Some herbal creams containing piper nigrum are formulated to be applied topically on the wrists or temples, where they are thought to provide a calming effect on the nervous system. While scientific evidence supporting the efficacy of these creams is limited, many users report a reduction in motion sickness symptoms when using them.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals with sensitive skin or underlying health conditions.

3. Chaste tree (Vitex agnus-castus)

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, is often used in herbal creams to help alleviate symptoms of motion sickness.

These creams typically combine the plant's extract with other calming ingredients like lavender or ginger to enhance their soothing effects. The mechanism of action is believed to involve regulating hormonal imbalances that may contribute to nausea and dizziness during travel. While some users report relief from using these creams, scientific evidence supporting their efficacy for motion sickness is limited.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking other medications.

Ginkgo biloba herbal creams are topical formulations that incorporate extracts from the ginkgo tree, known for their potential to enhance blood circulation and cognitive function.

6. Kava (Piper methysticum)

Piper methysticum, commonly known as kava, is a traditional Pacific Island plant that has been used for centuries for its calming effects.

These creams are believed to work by reducing anxiety and nausea through the calming properties of kava's active compounds, such as kavalactones. However, it is important to note that the effectiveness of kava-based creams for motion sickness has not been extensively studied in clinical trials.
