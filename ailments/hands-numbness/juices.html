10 Best Herbal Juices For Numbness In Hands

Herbal juices have gained attention for their potential to alleviate numbness in the hands, often linked to poor circulation or nerve compression.

Certain herbs, such as ginger, turmeric, and garlic, are known for their anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits, which may help improve blood flow and reduce nerve irritation. Some people use a blend of these herbs in juice form to support overall nerve health and promote sensation in the hands. While anecdotal evidence suggests these juices may offer relief, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using them as a treatment for persistent numbness.

Herbal juices should not replace medical advice or treatment, especially if the numbness is caused by a serious underlying condition.

1. Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba)

Ginkgo biloba herbal juices are often used to support circulation and may help alleviate numbness in the hands by improving blood flow and nerve function.

The active compounds in ginkgo, such as flavonoids and terpenes, are believed to enhance oxygen and nutrient delivery to tissues, which can be beneficial for individuals experiencing reduced sensation. While some studies suggest that ginkgo biloba may have neuroprotective properties, more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness for specific conditions like hand numbness. It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using ginkgo biloba, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.

As an herbal remedy, ginkgo biloba juice should be part of a holistic approach that includes lifestyle changes and medical evaluation for persistent numbness.

2. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including the relief of numbness in the hands.

The plant contains a variety of bioactive compounds such as vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that may support nerve health and improve circulation. Herbal juices made from fresh or dried stinging nettle leaves are believed to help reduce inflammation and enhance blood flow, which can alleviate tingling and numbness. While some anecdotal evidence suggests these juices may be beneficial, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using them, especially for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions.

As with any herbal remedy, the effectiveness of urtica dioica juice can vary, and it should be used as part of a holistic approach to managing numbness.

3. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential to alleviate numbness in the hands.

Some herbal juices made from fresh or dried sage leaves are believed to contain compounds that may improve circulation and reduce inflammation, which could contribute to easing hand numbness. While scientific evidence supporting these claims is limited, many holistic practitioners recommend sage-based remedies as a complementary approach to managing nerve-related conditions. These juices are often prepared by steeping the leaves in water or combining them with other herbs to enhance their effects.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using sage juice, especially for individuals with chronic conditions or those taking medications.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using vitex for numbness, as it may interact with certain medications or conditions.

As a complementary therapy, vitex agnus-castus may be part of a holistic approach to managing hand numbness, especially when combined with other lifestyle and medical interventions.

8. Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, contains curcumin, a powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compound that may help alleviate numbness in the hands by reducing inflammation and improving circulation.

Herbal juices made from turmeric can be a natural and complementary approach to managing conditions like carpal tunnel syndrome or nerve damage that cause hand numbness. These juices are often combined with other herbs such as ginger or black pepper to enhance absorption and effectiveness. Regular consumption of turmeric-based herbal juices may support nerve health and reduce symptoms associated with peripheral neuropathy.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using rosemary juice, especially for individuals with existing health conditions or those taking medications.
