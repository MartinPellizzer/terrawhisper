10 Best Herbal Juices For Numbness In Hands

Herbal juices have been explored as a natural remedy for numbness in the hands, often linked to poor circulation or nerve issues.

Certain herbs, such as ginger, turmeric, and garlic, are believed to improve blood flow and reduce inflammation, which may help alleviate tingling and numbness. Some people use a blend of these herbs in juice form to support nerve health and enhance overall circulation. However, while these juices may offer some benefits, they should not replace professional medical advice, especially if the numbness is persistent or severe.

It is important to consult a healthcare provider to determine the underlying cause and appropriate treatment for hand numbness.

1. Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba)

Ginkgo biloba, often referred to as the "maidenhair tree," is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to improve circulation and cognitive function.

Its extract is commonly found in herbal juices and supplements, which are believed to enhance blood flow and reduce inflammation. Some individuals use ginkgo biloba herbal juices to alleviate numbness in the hands, particularly in cases related to poor circulation or nerve damage. While preliminary studies suggest it may support nerve health, more research is needed to confirm its efficacy for this specific condition.

As with any herbal remedy, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if you have existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

When consumed as a herbal juice, vitex agnus-castus is believed to enhance nerve function and support the body's natural healing processes. 3. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, and some herbal juices derived from its leaves are believed to offer relief from numbness in the hands.

These juices are often prepared by infusing fresh or dried sage leaves in water or alcohol, creating a concentrated form that may support nerve health. Sage contains compounds like rosmarinic acid and thujone, which may have anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects, potentially improving circulation and reducing tingling or numbness. While some anecdotal evidence suggests its effectiveness, more scientific research is needed to confirm these benefits.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using sage juice for persistent numbness.
