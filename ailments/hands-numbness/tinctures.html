10 Best Herbal Tinctures For Numbness In Hands

Herbal tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts made from various herbs, often used to address symptoms like numbness in the hands, which can be caused by poor circulation, nerve compression, or inflammation.

Commonly used herbs in these tinctures include ginger, turmeric, and willow bark, all of which have anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits. These tinctures are typically taken orally or applied topically, depending on the formulation and the specific condition being treated. While they may offer natural relief, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before use, as they can interact with medications or have side effects.

Overall, herbal tinctures can be a complementary approach to managing hand numbness, though they should not replace conventional medical treatment.

1. Chaste tree (Vitex agnus-castus)

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chaste tree, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support hormonal balance and may offer relief for numbness in the hands, particularly when related to nerve-related conditions.

Its tinctures are believed to influence the hypothalamus and pituitary gland, potentially helping to regulate stress hormones and improve overall nervous system function. Some studies suggest that vitex may have mild neuroprotective properties, which could contribute to reducing nerve inflammation and improving circulation. When used as a tincture, vitex agnus-castus is typically taken in small doses, often diluted in water or alcohol, and is generally considered safe for most adults when used as directed.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any herbal treatment, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.

2. Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba)

Ginkgo biloba herbal tinctures are commonly used to support circulation and nerve function, which may help alleviate numbness in the hands.

This herb is known for its ability to enhance blood flow by dilating blood vessels and improving microcirculation, potentially reducing the sensation of tingling or numbness caused by poor circulation or nerve compression. While some studies suggest that ginkgo biloba may improve symptoms of peripheral neuropathy, more research is needed to confirm its efficacy specifically for hand numbness. The tincture form allows for easy absorption and can be taken orally, often with a drop or two diluted in water or under the tongue.

As with any herbal supplement, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for individuals with existing health conditions or those taking other medications.

3. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to address various health conditions, including nerve-related issues.

When prepared as a tincture, it is often used to alleviate numbness in the hands, particularly in cases of peripheral neuropathy or nerve damage. The active compounds in St. John's wort, such as hypericin and hyperforin, are believed to have anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties that may help improve nerve function and reduce sensory disturbances. However, it is important to note that St. John's wort can interact with a variety of medications, including antidepressants and birth control, so consultation with a healthcare provider is recommended before use.

Despite its potential benefits, the effectiveness of hypericum perforatum tinctures for numbness may vary, and it is often used in conjunction with other treatments for optimal results.

4. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic effects, including the management of numbness in the hands.

Some anecdotal evidence suggests that applying sage tinctures topically or using them internally may help improve circulation and reduce tingling or numbness in the hands.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using sage tinctures, as they can interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for everyone.
