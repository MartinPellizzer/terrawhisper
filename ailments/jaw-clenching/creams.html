10 Best Herbal Creams For Jaw Clenching

Herbal creams for jaw clenching are natural topical treatments designed to alleviate tension and discomfort in the jaw area caused by bruxism or stress.

These creams often contain ingredients like arnica, lavender, eucalyptus, and peppermint, which are known for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties. When applied to the jaw, they can help relax the muscles and reduce pain associated with clenching or grinding. Many users find these creams to be a gentle and effective alternative to over-the-counter pain relievers or dental interventions.

However, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal creams, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

1. Valerian (Valeriana officinalis)

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used for its calming and muscle-relaxing properties.

When formulated into creams, valerian-based products can be applied topically to the jaw area to help alleviate tension and reduce the frequency of jaw clenching. These creams work by soothing the muscles and promoting a sense of relaxation, which may help ease the symptoms of bruxism or teeth grinding. The active compounds in valerian, such as valepotriates, are believed to interact with the nervous system to reduce anxiety and muscle spasms.

While not a cure for jaw clenching, valerian officinalis creams can be a useful complementary therapy for those seeking natural relief from stress-related jaw tension.

2. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, is often used in herbal creams to address jaw clenching due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These creams typically contain extracts of licorice root, which can help reduce muscle tension and inflammation in the jaw area. The natural compounds in licorice, such as glycyrrhizin, may provide a calming effect on the muscles involved in jaw movement. When applied topically, these creams can offer relief from discomfort associated with bruxism or temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using licorice-based products, especially for prolonged periods, as they may have side effects when used excessively.
