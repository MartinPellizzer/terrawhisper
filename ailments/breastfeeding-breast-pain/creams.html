1. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as German chamomile, is a natural herb often used in the formulation of herbal creams to alleviate breast pain experienced by breastfeeding mothers.

These creams typically contain chamomile essential oils or extracts, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. When applied topically to the affected area, the cream can help reduce swelling, redness, and discomfort associated with mastitis or engorgement. Many breastfeeding women find these creams to be a safe and gentle alternative to over-the-counter pain relievers, as they are generally non-systemic and do not enter the bloodstream in significant amounts.

However, it is advisable to consult with a healthcare provider before using any herbal product, especially during lactation, to ensure safety for both mother and baby.

7. Symphytum officinale

Symphytum officinale, commonly known as comfrey, is often used in herbal creams to alleviate breast pain during breastfeeding.
