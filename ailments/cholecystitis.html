What is cholecystitis and how does it affect your body?

Cholecystitis is an inflammation of the gallbladder, typically caused by a blockage in the cystic duct, most commonly due to gallstones.

This condition can lead to severe pain in the upper right abdomen, often radiating to the back or shoulder, and may be accompanied by fever, nausea, and vomiting. The inflammation can result in the gallbladder becoming swollen and tender, and in severe cases, it may lead to complications such as gangrene or perforation. Chronic cholecystitis can cause long-term digestive issues, including difficulty in digesting fats, and may increase the risk of developing other gallbladder diseases.

If left untreated, cholecystitis can lead to serious health problems, making prompt medical attention essential for effective management.

Underlying causes and risk factors of cholecystitis

Cholecystitis, an inflammation of the gallbladder, is primarily caused by gallstones blocking the cystic duct, leading to bile stasis and bacterial infection.

The most common risk factors include a history of gallstone disease, obesity, rapid weight loss, pregnancy, and a family history of gallbladder disorders. Chronic conditions such as diabetes mellitus and metabolic syndrome also increase the risk by contributing to impaired gallbladder motility and bile composition abnormalities. Additionally, individuals with a sedentary lifestyle or a high-fat diet are more prone to developing cholecystitis due to increased gallstone formation.

Other risk factors include female gender, especially during reproductive years, and certain ethnic backgrounds where gallstone prevalence is higher.
