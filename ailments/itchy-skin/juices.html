10 Best Herbal Juices For Itchy Skin

Herbal juices have gained popularity as a natural remedy for itchy skin, offering a gentle and soothing alternative to chemical-based treatments.

Ingredients like chamomile, calendula, and licorice root are commonly used in herbal juices due to their anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. These juices can help reduce skin irritation and redness by calming the skin's inflammatory response. To prepare a soothing herbal juice, simply blend fresh herbs with water or a mild juice base and apply it topically or consume it internally for internal detoxification.

While herbal juices can provide relief, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with sensitive skin or existing medical conditions.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is widely recognized for its soothing properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal juices designed to alleviate itchy skin.

These juices often contain the gel extracted from the aloe plant, which is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and irritation. When applied topically or consumed internally, aloe-based herbal juices can help moisturize the skin and promote healing of minor abrasions or rashes. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of aloe vera make it particularly effective for conditions like eczema or psoriasis.

However, it is important to use aloe juice in moderation and consult a healthcare professional if symptoms persist or worsen.

2. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used for its soothing properties, particularly for itchy skin.

When prepared as a herbal juice, it contains compounds like histamine and formic acid, which can initially cause a tingling sensation but may later help reduce inflammation and irritation. The juice is often applied topically to the skin to alleviate conditions such as eczema, hives, and insect bites. It is also believed to support the body's natural healing processes by promoting detoxification and improving skin health.

However, it is important to dilute the juice properly and consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals with sensitive skin or allergies.

3. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, has been traditionally used for its potential soothing properties on itchy skin.

When prepared as a herbal juice, it may help alleviate skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds. The juice is often applied topically to reduce redness and discomfort associated with conditions like eczema or insect bites. However, it is important to note that internal use of St. John's Wort can interact with certain medications, so caution is advised.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before using it for skin conditions, especially if you have underlying health issues or are taking other treatments.

4. English lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is widely used in herbal juices for its soothing and antiseptic properties.

These juices are often prepared by infusing lavender flowers in water or juice bases, allowing the beneficial compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate to be extracted. The anti-inflammatory and calming effects of lavender can help reduce itching and redness associated with sensitive or irritated skin. Many people find relief from itchy skin conditions such as eczema or insect bites by applying or consuming lavender-based herbal juices.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using lavender juices, especially if you have allergies or are pregnant.

6. German chamomile (Chamomilla recutita)

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, is widely used in herbal remedies for its soothing properties, particularly for itchy skin.

The essential oils and active compounds in chamomile, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects that help reduce skin irritation and redness. Herbal juices made from chamomile flowers can be applied topically to relieve itching caused by eczema, psoriasis, or insect bites. These natural remedies are often preferred for their gentle nature, making them suitable for sensitive skin.

When used consistently, chamomile-based juices can provide long-term relief and promote skin healing without the side effects of harsh chemicals.

7. Field horsetail (Equisetum arvense)

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, has been traditionally used for its high silica content and potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Herbal juices made from this plant are believed to help soothe itchy skin by reducing irritation and promoting skin healing. When prepared properly, the juice can be applied topically to affected areas, offering a natural alternative for those seeking relief from skin conditions like eczema or insect bites. However, it is important to use the juice in moderation and consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals with sensitive skin or existing health conditions.

Overall, equisetum arvense herbal juice may provide some relief for itchy skin, though its effectiveness can vary and should be part of a broader skincare routine.

8. Marigold (Calendula officinalis)

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as pot marigold, is a popular herbal remedy known for its soothing properties, particularly for itchy skin.

Its active compounds, such as flavonoids and triterpenes, help reduce inflammation and promote skin healing. Calendula herbal juices are often used topically to alleviate conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and insect bites. These juices can be applied directly to the skin or mixed with carrier oils for enhanced absorption.

Due to its gentle nature, calendula is considered safe for most skin types, making it a versatile and effective natural treatment for various skin irritations.

9. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal remedy known for its soothing properties, particularly for itchy skin.

Chamomile herbal juices are often used topically to relieve skin irritation, redness, and inflammation due to their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic benefits. The active compounds in chamomile, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, help to calm the skin and reduce itching by calming the nervous system and reducing histamine response. When applied as a compress or diluted in a carrier oil, chamomile juice can provide natural relief for conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and insect bites.

However, it is important to perform a patch test before use to avoid allergic reactions, especially in individuals sensitive to plants in the daisy family.

10. Centella (Centella asiatica)

Centella asiatica, also known as gotu kola, is a traditional herb widely used for its skin-soothing properties.

Herbal juices made from centella asiatica are believed to help alleviate itchy skin by reducing inflammation and promoting skin healing. These juices contain compounds like asiatic acid and madecassol, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Many people use centella asiatica juice as a natural remedy for conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and insect bites.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are on medication.
