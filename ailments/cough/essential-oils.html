9 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Cough

Herbal essential oils for cough are concentrated plant extracts that have been used for centuries to provide relief from coughs and colds.

These oils have numerous benefits, including their ability to soothe a sore throat, reduce inflammation, and ease congestion, making them an effective natural remedy for treating coughs.

Some of the most commonly used herbal essential oils to treat coughs include eucalyptus, peppermint, tea tree, thyme, and rosemary, which have decongestant and expectorant properties that help to loosen and clear mucus from the airways.

Additionally, oils like ginger, frankincense, and myrrh have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling and ease discomfort in the throat and chest, providing quick and effective relief from coughs and colds.

According to the study on Traditional Persian Medicine (TPM), essential oils from 49 herbs have been recommended for treating cough, with 21 of them having a known mechanism of action for cough suppression.

1. Eucalyptus globulus essential oils

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils are widely used to treat cough ailments due to their decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The herb's active compounds, such as eucalyptol and limonene, help to relieve congestion and reduce the severity of coughs by thinning mucus and opening airways.

The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus globulus, including eucalyptol and alpha-pinene, work synergistically to provide a cooling sensation on the skin, helping to temporarily relieve coughs and congestion.

The benefits of using Eucalyptus globulus essential oils to treat cough ailments include quick relief, improved respiratory function, and a soothing, calming effect on the throat and chest.

2. Mentha x piperita essential oils

Mentha x piperita essential oils, also known as peppermint oil, have been traditionally used to treat cough ailments due to their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

The menthol content in this oil helps to thin mucus and relieve congestion, making it easier to cough up phlegm and reduce coughing spasms.

The bioactive constituents of peppermint oil, including menthone and limonene, contribute to its therapeutic effects by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and improving airway function.

By using peppermint oil topically or inhaling its vapors, individuals can experience relief from coughing and other respiratory symptoms associated with colds and flu.
