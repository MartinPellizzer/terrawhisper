10 Best Herbal Lotions For Premenstrual Syndrome

Herbal lotions have gained popularity as a natural remedy for alleviating symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS), offering a gentler alternative to conventional treatments.

These lotions often contain calming and anti-inflammatory herbs such as lavender, chamomile, and calendula, which are believed to help reduce bloating, cramps, and mood swings associated with PMS. Applying these herbal lotions topically can provide localized relief by soothing the muscles and promoting relaxation through their aromatic properties. Many women find that regular use of these lotions helps them feel more balanced and comfortable during their menstrual cycle.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using herbal products, especially if you have known allergies or are taking other medications.

1. Chaste tree (Vitex agnus-castus)

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, is a herbal remedy often used in the form of a lotion to support women experiencing premenstrual syndrome (PMS).

These herbal lotions are believed to help regulate hormonal imbalances, which are a common cause of PMS symptoms such as mood swings, bloating, and breast tenderness. When applied topically, the active compounds in vitex may be absorbed through the skin, potentially providing localized relief and promoting overall hormonal harmony. While some studies suggest that vitex can reduce PMS symptoms when taken orally, the effectiveness of topical applications is less well-researched.

As with any herbal remedy, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using vitex agnus-castus lotion, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.

2. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal remedy used in the form of lotions to alleviate symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS).

These lotions often contain essential oils extracted from the flowers of the plant, which are known for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties. When applied topically, chamomile lotions may help reduce menstrual cramps, bloating, and mood swings associated with PMS by promoting relaxation and improving circulation. Some studies suggest that the active compounds in chamomile, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, may have a soothing effect on the body.

While generally safe for most people, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before using chamomile lotions, especially if you have allergies or are pregnant.

7. Common mallow (Symphytum officinale)

Symphytum officinale, commonly known as comfrey, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to support healing and reduce inflammation.
