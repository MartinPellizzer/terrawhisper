9 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Premenstrual Syndrome

Herbal essential oils for Premenstrual syndrome are plant-based extracts used to alleviate the symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS), a condition characterized by physical and emotional discomfort experienced by women in the days leading up to their menstrual period.

These oils can provide relief from symptoms such as bloating, mood swings, and breast tenderness due to their anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antioxidant properties.

Examples of herbal essential oils that can help treat PMS include clary sage oil, which reduces stress and anxiety; geranium oil, which balances hormones and reduces inflammation; peppermint oil, which soothes digestive issues and eases cramps; and chamomile oil, which calms the mind and body.

By incorporating these oils into their self-care routine, women can experience a reduction in PMS symptoms and an overall improvement in their emotional and physical well-being.

1. Santalum album essential oils

Santalum album essential oils, extracted from the sandalwood tree, have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms associated with premenstrual syndrome (PMS).

The properties of this essential oil, including its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, help to reduce physical symptoms such as cramps, bloating, and breast tenderness.

The bioactive constituents of Santalum album essential oil, including alpha-santalol and beta-santalol, have been shown to have a relaxing effect on the body, reducing anxiety and emotional symptoms associated with PMS.

By incorporating Santalum album essential oils into aromatherapy or massage, women can benefit from its therapeutic properties to manage PMS symptoms and promote overall well-being.

2. Valeriana officinalis essential oils

Valeriana officinalis essential oils, derived from the roots of the valerian plant, have been traditionally used to treat premenstrual syndrome (PMS) due to its sedative, anxiolytic, and antispasmodic properties.

The bioactive constituents present in valerian essential oils, such as isovaleric acid, valerenic acid, and valepotriates, help to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, alleviating symptoms associated with PMS, including mood swings and cramps.

By regulating the body's hormonal balance and reducing stress levels, valerian essential oils help to alleviate the symptoms of PMS, providing relief from emotional distress and physical discomfort.

The benefits of using valerian essential oils to treat PMS include reduced anxiety and stress levels, improved sleep quality, and a decrease in the severity of PMS symptoms, ultimately promoting a better overall quality of life.

3. Cinnamomum verum essential oils

Cinnamomum verum essential oils, also known as Ceylon cinnamon, have been traditionally used to treat premenstrual syndrome (PMS) due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which help to alleviate symptoms such as cramps, mood swings, and breast tenderness.

The bioactive constituents of Cinnamomum verum essential oils, including cinnamaldehyde and linalool, have been found to have a positive effect on the hormonal balance and nervous system, thereby reducing the severity of PMS symptoms.

The benefits of using Cinnamomum verum essential oils to treat PMS include reduced stress and anxiety, improved mood, and relief from physical discomfort, making it a natural and effective remedy for women suffering from this condition.

By incorporating Cinnamomum verum essential oils into their self-care routine, women can experience a significant reduction in PMS symptoms and improve their overall well-being.

4. Melissa officinalis essential oils

Melissa officinalis essential oils have been traditionally used to treat the premenstrual syndrome (PMS) ailment due to their calming and anti-anxiety properties.

The herb's ability to reduce stress and anxiety levels helps alleviate symptoms such as mood swings, irritability, and emotional instability associated with PMS.

The bioactive constituents of Melissa officinalis essential oils, including citral and geranial, possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to regulate hormonal imbalances and reduce physical symptoms of PMS, such as breast tenderness and bloating.

The benefits of using Melissa officinalis essential oils for PMS treatment include reduced symptoms, improved mood, and enhanced overall well-being.

5. Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils

Cinnamomum zeylanicum essential oils, derived from the bark of the Ceylon cinnamon tree, have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS) due to its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

6. Mentha x piperita essential oils

Mentha x piperita essential oils, commonly known as peppermint oil, have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms associated with premenstrual syndrome (PMS).

The analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties of menthol, a major bioactive constituent in peppermint oil, help to reduce menstrual cramps and discomfort.

The essential oil's ability to relax muscles, improve mood, and reduce anxiety also contribute to its effectiveness in treating PMS symptoms.

By incorporating peppermint oil into aromatherapy or topical applications, individuals can experience relief from PMS symptoms, including bloating, headaches, and irritability.

7. Myristica fragrans essential oils

Myristica fragrans essential oils, also known as nutmeg oil, have been traditionally used to alleviate the symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS).

8. Zingiber officinale essential oils

Zingiber officinale essential oils, also known as ginger essential oil, have been traditionally used to treat the premenstrual syndrome (PMS) due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antidepressant properties.

The bioactive constituents present in ginger essential oil, including gingerol and shogaol, help to reduce pain and inflammation associated with PMS.

By reducing prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause uterine contractions and pain, ginger essential oil helps to alleviate symptoms of PMS such as cramps, bloating, and mood swings.

The benefits of using ginger essential oil to treat PMS include reduced severity of symptoms, improved mood, and enhanced overall well-being.

9. Lavandula angustifolia essential oils

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms associated with premenstrual syndrome (PMS) due to their calming and soothing properties.

The essential oil's anxiolytic and anti-inflammatory effects help to reduce anxiety and physical symptoms such as breast tenderness, headaches, and mood swings.

Regular use of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils may help to alleviate PMS symptoms, promoting a sense of well-being and balance in women experiencing this condition.
