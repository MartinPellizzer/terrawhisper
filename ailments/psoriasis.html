Psoriasis Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Psoriasis is a chronic skin condition characterized by red, itchy, and scaly patches, resulting from an overactive immune system.

Herbal remedies have gained popularity in treating psoriasis due to their potential to reduce inflammation, promote skin healing, and alleviate symptoms.

Herbs such as Aloe vera, Chamomile, and Calendula are often used to soothe and calm the skin, while Turmeric, Ginger, and Willow bark are valued for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Herbal preparations like ointments, creams, and teas made from herbs like Echinacea, Licorice root, and Silymarin can help to reduce the severity of psoriasis symptoms and promote overall skin health.

What causes psoriasis?

The primary causes of psoriasis are genetic mutations and an overactive immune system, which trigger inflammation and skin cell production.

Secondary causes of psoriasis include skin injuries, infections, and certain medications, which can trigger a flare-up by disrupting the skin's natural balance.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing psoriasis include a diet high in processed foods and sugar, as well as stress and lack of sleep, which can weaken the immune system and lead to inflammation.

Additionally, smoking and excessive sun exposure can also contribute to the development of psoriasis.

What herbs heal psoriasis?

The medicinal herbs used to treat psoriasis are rich in bioactive compounds that exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to alleviate symptoms of redness, itching, and scaling.

Aloe vera, for instance, contains aloin and aloe-emodin, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing, while chamomile and calendula possess flavonoids and triterpenoids that soothe and calm irritated skin.

Turmeric contains curcumin, a polyphenol with potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities that help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress associated with psoriasis.

Additionally, tea tree oil, rich in cineole and terpinen-4-ol, has antimicrobial properties that help control bacterial overgrowth and promote a healthy skin environment.

What preparations treat psoriasis?

The herbal preparations used to treat psoriasis are often made into teas, infusions, and topical creams.

Drinking teas infused with soothing and anti-inflammatory properties can help to reduce redness and inflammation associated with the condition, while topical creams made with emollient-rich ingredients can help to moisturize and soothe dry, itchy skin.

Infusions that promote skin health and reduce inflammation can be consumed to help alleviate symptoms of psoriasis, such as scaling and itching.

Additionally, some herbal preparations are made into ointments or salves that can be applied directly to affected areas to provide targeted relief and reduce the appearance of psoriasis lesions.
