Frozen Shoulder Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Frozen shoulder is a medical condition characterized by stiffness and pain in the shoulder joint, resulting from inflammation of the surrounding tissues and reduced mobility of the joint.

Herbal remedies have been used to treat frozen shoulder due to their anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and pain-relieving properties.

Certain herbs such as turmeric, ginger, and boswellia have been shown to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain, while others like ginseng, ashwagandha, and cayenne pepper help to improve circulation and reduce stiffness.

Herbal preparations like arnica oil, capsaicin cream, and turmeric capsules have been used to treat frozen shoulder, with arnica oil helping to reduce inflammation, capsaicin cream providing pain relief, and turmeric capsules reducing joint inflammation and promoting healing.

What causes frozen shoulder?

The primary causes of frozen shoulder are inflammation and scarring of the connective tissue that surrounds the shoulder joint, often resulting from injury, surgery, or infection.

Secondary causes of frozen shoulder can include diabetes, thyroid disorders, and certain types of arthritis, as these conditions can lead to inflammation and scarring of the connective tissue.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing frozen shoulder include working jobs that involve repetitive shoulder movements, such as construction or manufacturing, and engaging in sports that involve overhead throwing or lifting, such as baseball or weightlifting.

Additionally, sedentary lifestyles and obesity can also contribute to the development of frozen shoulder due to reduced mobility and increased inflammation.

What herbs heal frozen shoulder?

The medicinal herbs used to treat frozen shoulder are Boswellia serrata, Turmeric, and Ginger, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that help reduce pain and inflammation in the affected joint.

Boswellia serrata, in particular, contains boswellic acids that inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2, which contribute to the pain and stiffness associated with frozen shoulder.

Turmeric contains curcumin, a bioactive compound that has potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce oxidative stress and promote healing in the affected tissue.

Ginger, on the other hand, contains gingerols and shogaols that have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, which help to reduce pain and inflammation in the affected joint.
