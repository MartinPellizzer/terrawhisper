What is frozen shoulder and how does it affect your body?

Frozen shoulder, also known as adhesive capsulitis, is a condition characterized by stiffness and pain in the shoulder joint, leading to a significant reduction in range of motion.

It typically develops gradually and can affect one shoulder at a time, often without an obvious cause. The condition is believed to result from inflammation and thickening of the joint capsule surrounding the shoulder, which restricts movement. As the stiffness progresses, everyday activities such as reaching overhead or dressing become increasingly difficult.

If left untreated, frozen shoulder can lead to long-term mobility issues and may require physical therapy or medical intervention to restore function.

Underlying causes and risk factors of frozen shoulder

Frozen shoulder, also known as adhesive capsulitis, is primarily caused by inflammation and thickening of the shoulder joint capsule, leading to restricted movement.

The exact cause is often unknown, but it is frequently associated with prolonged immobility of the shoulder, such as after surgery, injury, or a stroke. Other risk factors include diabetes, which is strongly linked to the development of frozen shoulder, as well as autoimmune disorders and hormonal changes. Women are more likely to develop the condition than men, and it typically occurs in individuals between the ages of 40 and 70.

Additionally, individuals with a history of shoulder trauma or those who have had shoulder surgery are at increased risk due to the potential for joint capsule damage and subsequent scar tissue formation.

Best herbs for managing frozen shoulder

Frozen shoulder can be effectively managed with certain herbs that help reduce inflammation and improve joint mobility.

Turmeric, known for its anti-inflammatory properties, is often recommended for its active compound curcumin, which may alleviate pain and stiffness. Ginger is another beneficial herb that can help reduce inflammation and promote circulation, supporting overall joint health. Echinacea and boswellia are also used to support the immune system and reduce inflammatory responses in the body.

When using these herbs, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider to ensure they are safe and appropriate for individual health conditions.

How to use herbal remedies safely for frozen shoulder

Using herbal remedies for frozen shoulder should always begin with consulting a healthcare professional to ensure safety and appropriateness for your condition.

Choose herbs known for their anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, such as turmeric, ginger, and willow bark, and use them in forms like teas, tinctures, or topical salves. It is important to follow recommended dosages and avoid combining multiple herbs without professional guidance to prevent adverse interactions. Always check for allergies or sensitivities before using any herbal remedy, and discontinue use if irritation or worsening symptoms occur.

Herbal remedies should complement, not replace, conventional medical treatments for frozen shoulder.
