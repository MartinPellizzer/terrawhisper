10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Eye Swelling

Herbal essential oils, such as chamomile, calendula, and lavender, are commonly used to help reduce eye swelling due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These oils can be diluted with a carrier oil and applied gently around the eyes to alleviate puffiness and irritation. Chamomile is particularly effective for its calming effects, while calendula promotes healing and reduces redness. Lavender oil is known for its ability to soothe and reduce inflammation, making it a popular choice for eye care.

However, it is important to always dilute these oils properly to avoid skin irritation and ensure safe use.

1. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as German chamomile, is a popular herb used in the production of essential oils that are valued for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oil derived from chamomile is often used in aromatherapy and topical applications to alleviate various health issues, including eye swelling. Its active compounds, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, contribute to its ability to reduce inflammation and soothe irritated tissues around the eyes. When diluted properly, chamomile essential oil can be applied gently to the under-eye area to help reduce puffiness and redness.

However, it is important to perform a patch test before use, as some individuals may experience allergic reactions to this potent oil.

2. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, is a popular herbal source for essential oils known for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds such as alpha-bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to its effectiveness in reducing eye swelling. When applied topically, chamomile essential oil can help soothe irritation and reduce puffiness around the eyes due to its antiseptic and decongestant effects. It is often used in natural remedies for conditions like allergic reactions or fatigue-induced eye swelling.

However, it is important to dilute the essential oil with a carrier oil before applying it to the delicate eye area to avoid irritation.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is widely used in aromatherapy for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its essential oil contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and soothe the skin. When applied topically, lavender essential oil can help alleviate eye swelling by promoting circulation and reducing puffiness. It is often diluted with a carrier oil before use to prevent irritation.

This natural remedy is a popular choice for those seeking gentle, holistic treatments for minor eye concerns.

4. Rosa canina

Rosa canina, also known as rosehip, is a plant commonly used in herbal medicine for its rich content of essential oils and nutrients.

These essential oils derived from rosa canina are known for their anti-inflammatory and regenerative properties, which can help reduce eye swelling and irritation. The oils contain high levels of vitamins A and C, as well as essential fatty acids, making them beneficial for skin and eye health. When applied topically, rosa canina essential oils can soothe inflammation and promote the healing of minor skin conditions around the eyes.

However, it is important to dilute the essential oils properly before use to avoid skin irritation and ensure safe application.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, contains bioactive compounds that may have anti-inflammatory properties, making its essential oils potentially useful for reducing eye swelling.

When properly extracted, the essential oil of stinging nettle may help alleviate puffiness and irritation around the eyes due to its high concentration of antioxidants and minerals. However, it is important to note that the use of Urtica dioica essential oil for eye care should be approached with caution, as it can cause skin irritation if not diluted correctly. Some alternative remedies, such as cold compresses or chamomile tea, are often recommended for mild eye swelling.

Always consult a healthcare professional before using essential oils on sensitive areas like the eyes.

6. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

These essential oils may help reduce eye swelling by decreasing inflammation and constricting blood vessels, which can alleviate puffiness and redness. When applied topically, the essential oils from yarrow can soothe irritated tissues around the eyes, promoting a cooling effect that relieves discomfort. However, it is important to dilute the essential oils properly with a carrier oil to avoid irritation, as they can be potent.

While some anecdotal evidence supports its use for eye swelling, more scientific research is needed to fully understand its efficacy and safety for this application.

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a herb widely used in aromatherapy for its calming and soothing properties.

When used for eye swelling, melissa essential oil can help reduce puffiness and redness by promoting lymphatic drainage and easing muscle tension around the eyes. It is often diluted with a carrier oil before application to the delicate eye area to prevent irritation.

10. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

These essential oils, particularly those rich in compounds like cineole and camphor, may help reduce eye swelling by soothing irritated tissues and improving circulation. When diluted properly, sage essential oil can be applied around the eyes to alleviate puffiness and redness associated with fatigue or allergies. However, it is important to use caution and consult a healthcare professional before applying any essential oil near the delicate eye area.
