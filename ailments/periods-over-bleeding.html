What is over bleeding during periods and how does it affect your body?

Over bleeding during periods, also known as menorrhagia, refers to excessively heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding that can last more than seven days or involve losing more than 80 milliliters of blood.

This condition can be caused by hormonal imbalances, uterine fibroids, polyps, or other medical conditions such as thyroid disorders or pregnancy complications. The excessive loss of blood can lead to iron-deficiency anemia, causing symptoms like fatigue, dizziness, and shortness of breath. It may also result in severe cramping, weakness, and a decreased quality of life.

If left untreated, over bleeding can have long-term effects on a woman's overall health and may require medical intervention such as medication, hormonal therapy, or surgical procedures.

Underlying causes and risk factors of over bleeding during periods

Over bleeding during periods, also known as menorrhagia, can be caused by several underlying factors and risk elements.

Hormonal imbalances, particularly an excess of estrogen relative to progesterone, are a common cause, as they can lead to the thickening of the uterine lining, resulting in heavier menstrual flow. Structural abnormalities such as fibroids, polyps, or adenomyosis can also contribute by altering the uterine environment and increasing blood loss. Additionally, certain medical conditions like thyroid disorders or bleeding disorders may predispose individuals to excessive menstrual bleeding.

Lifestyle factors, including poor nutrition, obesity, and the use of certain medications, can also play a role in increasing the risk of menorrhagia.

Best herbs for managing over bleeding during periods

Managing heavy menstrual bleeding can be supported by certain herbs that help regulate hormonal balance and reduce uterine inflammation.

Chamomile is often recommended for its ability to soothe the uterus and reduce cramping, while ginger can help alleviate pain and potentially reduce the duration of bleeding. Red raspberry leaf is known to strengthen the uterine lining and may help regulate menstrual flow. Additionally, evening primrose oil contains gamma-linolenic acid, which can support hormonal health and reduce menstrual discomfort.

It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using any herbal supplements, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or are taking other medications.
