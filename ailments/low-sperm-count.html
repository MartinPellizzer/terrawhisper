Low Sperm Count Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Low sperm count is a medical condition where a man has fewer than 15 million sperm per milliliter of semen, affecting fertility and making it difficult to conceive.

Herbal remedies offer a natural and non-invasive approach to treating low sperm count, promoting overall reproductive health and fertility.

Certain herbs such as Ashwagandha, Tribulus Terrestris, and Maca root help to increase sperm count and motility by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation, while others like Ginseng and Ginkgo Biloba improve blood flow to the reproductive organs.

Herbal preparations such as capsules, teas, and tinctures like Tribulus Terrestris extract, Maca root powder, and Ginseng tea can be used to treat low sperm count, as they contain concentrated amounts of these herbs' active compounds, allowing for targeted and effective treatment.

What causes low sperm count?

The primary causes of low sperm count are hormonal imbalances, particularly low levels of follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH), which are crucial for sperm production.

Secondary causes include infections such as epididymitis and orchitis, which can damage the testes and reduce sperm count, as well as certain medical conditions like diabetes and thyroid disorders, which can disrupt hormone regulation.

Lifestyle factors that increase the risk of low sperm count include exposure to environmental toxins, such as pesticides and heavy metals, which can damage sperm DNA and impair fertility, as well as smoking and excessive heat, which can reduce sperm production and quality.

Additionally, a diet lacking essential nutrients, such as zinc and vitamin D, can also contribute to low sperm count.

What herbs heal low sperm count?

The medicinal herbs used to treat low sperm count are Ashwagandha, Tribulus Terrestris, and Maca, which have been traditionally used to enhance fertility and improve sperm quality.

These herbs exhibit therapeutic actions such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and adaptogenic properties, which help to protect sperm cells from oxidative damage and promote healthy sperm production.

The bioactive compounds found in these herbs, including withanolides in Ashwagandha, saponins in Tribulus Terrestris, and flavonoids in Maca, have been shown to improve sperm motility, count, and morphology.

By stimulating testicular function and increasing testosterone levels, these herbs can help to alleviate the symptoms of low sperm count and improve fertility.
