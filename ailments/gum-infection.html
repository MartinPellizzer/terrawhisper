Gum Infection Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Gum infection, also known as periodontitis, is a bacterial infection that affects the gums and bone supporting the teeth, leading to inflammation, pain, and potential tooth loss if left untreated.

Herbal remedies have been used for centuries to treat gum infections due to their antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce inflammation, prevent further infection, and promote healing.

Examples of herbs used to treat gum infections include Aloe vera, which soothes and calms the gums, Echinacea, which boosts the immune system to fight off infection, and Tea tree oil, which has antibacterial properties to combat the bacteria causing the infection.

Herbal preparations such as mouthwashes made with Sage, Chamomile, and Turmeric have also been effective in treating gum infections due to their antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, while herbal teas like Green tea and Ginger have been used to reduce inflammation and promote healing.

What causes gum infection?

The primary causes of gum infection are poor oral hygiene and bacterial buildup, as a result of not brushing and flossing regularly, allowing plaque to accumulate and cause inflammation.

Secondary causes include smoking and tobacco use, as well as certain medical conditions such as diabetes and HIV/AIDS, which can impair the immune system and make it harder for the body to fight off infections.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing gum infection include a diet high in sugar and acidic foods, which can lead to tooth decay and create an environment conducive to bacterial growth, as well as poor nutrition and inadequate dental care.

Additionally, people who are under a lot of stress may be more prone to gum infection due to the negative impact of stress on the immune system.
