1. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used for its potential mood-enhancing properties, but it is also being explored for its possible benefits in managing tinnitus.

While scientific evidence supporting its use for tinnitus is limited, some studies suggest that the active compounds in Hypericum perforatum may help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation, which are thought to contribute to tinnitus. Herbal capsules containing Hypericum perforatum are often marketed as a natural alternative for individuals seeking to alleviate tinnitus symptoms without pharmaceutical interventions. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using this supplement, as it can interact with other medications and may not be suitable for everyone.

Overall, while Hypericum perforatum may offer some therapeutic potential for tinnitus, more research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety in this context.

2. Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba)

Ginkgo biloba herbal capsules are commonly used as a natural remedy for tinnitus, a condition characterized by ringing or buzzing in the ears.
