What is cold sore and how does it affect your body?

A cold sore, also known as a fever blister, is a small, fluid-filled lesion that typically appears on the lips or around the mouth.

It is caused by the herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1), which becomes active when the immune system is weakened or under stress. Cold sores usually start as a red, itchy spot that develops into a blister and then ruptures, forming a yellowish ulcer before healing. The infection is contagious and can be spread through direct contact with the sore or through saliva.

While cold sores are generally not serious, they can be uncomfortable, painful, and may lead to complications such as eye infections if the virus spreads to the eye area.

Underlying causes and risk factors of cold sore

Cold sores, caused by the herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1), are primarily triggered by a weakened immune system, which allows the virus to become active.

Common risk factors include stress, illness, hormonal changes, and exposure to extreme weather, all of which can compromise the body's defenses. Individuals with compromised immunity, such as those with HIV or undergoing chemotherapy, are particularly susceptible to recurrent outbreaks. Additionally, close personal contact, like kissing, facilitates the transmission of the virus.

Genetic predisposition may also play a role, as some people are more prone to HSV-1 reactivation than others.

How to use herbal remedies safely for cold sore

Using herbal remedies for cold sores can be a natural and effective alternative to conventional treatments, but it is important to use them safely.

Always consult with a healthcare provider before starting any herbal treatment, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications. Choose high-quality, organic herbs from reputable sources to avoid contamination and ensure potency. Apply the remedy directly to the cold sore as directed, and avoid ingesting herbs unless specifically recommended by a professional.

Monitor for any adverse reactions and discontinue use if irritation or allergic responses occur.
