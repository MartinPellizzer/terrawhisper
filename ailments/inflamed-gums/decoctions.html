10 Best Herbal Decoctions For Inflamed Gums

Herbal decoctions have been traditionally used to soothe inflamed gums due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Common herbs such as chamomile, sage, and licorice root are often included in these remedies for their ability to reduce swelling and promote healing. To prepare a decoction, these herbs are boiled in water for several minutes, then strained and cooled before use. The resulting liquid can be used as a mouth rinse or applied directly to the affected areas with a clean cloth.

Regular use of such decoctions may help alleviate gum irritation and support overall oral health.

1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable herb in the preparation of herbal decoctions for inflamed gums.

When prepared as a decoction, sage leaves are simmered in water to extract their active compounds, such as flavonoids and essential oils, which help reduce gum inflammation and combat oral bacteria. This herbal remedy can be used as a mouth rinse or applied directly to the gums for localized relief, offering a natural alternative to conventional treatments. The soothing effects of sage decoctions may help alleviate discomfort and promote healing in cases of gingivitis or periodontal disease.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using sage decoctions, especially for prolonged periods or in combination with other medications.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, has been traditionally used for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal decoctions for inflamed gums.

When prepared as a decoction, aloe vera can help reduce gum swelling and irritation by its natural polysaccharides and antioxidants, which promote tissue repair and reduce bacterial growth. The gel extracted from the aloe plant is often simmered with water or other herbs to create a nourishing and calming oral rinse. This herbal remedy is particularly beneficial for individuals seeking natural alternatives to conventional treatments for gingivitis and periodontal issues.

Regular use of aloe barbadensis decoctions may contribute to improved gum health and overall oral hygiene.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, has been traditionally used for its potential anti-inflammatory and immunostimulant properties.

When prepared as a herbal decoction, it involves boiling the dried roots or flowers in water to extract its active compounds. This preparation may help reduce inflammation in the gums by inhibiting pro-inflammatory cytokines and promoting tissue healing. Some studies suggest that echinacea can decrease the severity of gingival inflammation, though results can vary among individuals.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using echinacea, as it may interact with certain medications or have side effects in some people.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When prepared as a decoction, it can be applied topically to inflamed gums to help reduce swelling and irritation. The active compounds in Hypericum perforatum, such as hypericin and flavonoids, contribute to its ability to soothe gum tissue and combat bacterial infections. To prepare the decoction, dried hypericum leaves are boiled in water and then strained for use as a mouth rinse or applied with a cotton swab.

While generally safe, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially if taking other medications, due to potential interactions.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

When prepared as a decoction, the leaves and stems of Urtica dioica can be boiled to extract their active compounds, which may help reduce inflammation in the gums. This herbal decoction is believed to contain compounds such as flavonoids and polysaccharides that support healing and reduce swelling. It is often used as a complementary therapy in natural oral care routines for individuals suffering from gingivitis or periodontal disease.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using Urtica dioica, especially if you have allergies or are taking other medications.

6. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as garden marigold, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable herb in the treatment of inflamed gums.

Herbal decoctions made from dried calendula flowers can be prepared by simmering the flowers in water for several minutes, allowing the active compounds such as flavonoids and triterpenes to be extracted. These decoctions have been shown to reduce swelling, redness, and bacterial infection in the gums, promoting healing and comfort. When used as a mouth rinse or applied topically, calendula decoctions may help soothe irritation and support oral health.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using calendula for persistent gum inflammation to ensure safety and efficacy.

7. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, making it a popular choice for treating inflamed gums.

When prepared as a herbal decoction, chamomile can be used as a mouth rinse or applied directly to the affected areas to reduce swelling and irritation. The active compounds, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, helping to combat bacterial infections that often accompany gum inflammation. Regular use of chamomile decoctions may promote faster healing and alleviate discomfort associated with gingivitis or periodontal disease.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using chamomile, especially if you have allergies or are taking other medications.

8. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

When prepared as a herbal decoction, it involves boiling the bark of the cinnamon tree in water to extract its active compounds, such as cinnamaldehyde and eugenol. These compounds possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects that can help reduce gum inflammation and soothe irritation. The decoction can be used as a mouth rinse or applied directly to the gums for localized relief.

Regular use of cinnamon decoctions may support overall gum health and complement conventional dental care practices.

9. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When prepared as a decoction, it can effectively reduce inflammation in the gums by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory compounds such as cytokines. The active compounds in licorice root, including glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, contribute to its antimicrobial and antiviral effects, which may help prevent infections that exacerbate gum inflammation. This herbal decoction can be used as a natural alternative or complementary therapy to conventional treatments for periodontal diseases.

However, long-term use should be monitored due to potential side effects related to corticosteroid-like effects from glycyrrhizin.

10. Origanum vulgare

Origanum vulgare, commonly known as oregano, has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Herbal decoctions made from the dried leaves of Origanum vulgare can be prepared by boiling the leaves in water for several minutes, then allowing the liquid to steep for additional time. These decoctions may help reduce inflammation and bacterial buildup in inflamed gums due to the presence of compounds like carvacrol and thymol. Some studies suggest that oregano extracts can inhibit the growth of oral pathogens associated with periodontal disease.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using oregano decoctions, especially for individuals with allergies or those taking medications.
