10 Best Herbal Teas For Inflamed Gums

Herbal teas can be a natural and soothing remedy for inflamed gums, offering anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce swelling and prevent infection.

Teas made from ingredients like chamomile, licorice root, and green tea are particularly beneficial due to their high concentrations of antioxidants and bioflavonoids. These teas can be used as a mouth rinse or sipped slowly to promote healing and alleviate discomfort. Regular use of herbal teas may help manage gum inflammation without the side effects associated with some conventional treatments.

However, it is advisable to consult with a dental professional to ensure proper care and rule out more serious underlying conditions.

1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable ingredient in herbal teas for inflamed gums.

The essential oils in sage, particularly thujone and camphor, help reduce gum irritation and swelling by inhibiting the growth of harmful bacteria in the mouth. When brewed as a tea, sage can soothe gum tissues and promote healing due to its high concentration of antioxidants and flavonoids. It is often recommended as a natural alternative to commercial mouthwashes for those seeking a gentler approach to gum care.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using sage tea, especially for prolonged periods or in combination with other medications.

2. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

When brewed into a tea, it can help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated gums, making it a potential natural remedy for gum issues. The tea contains compounds like flavonoids and minerals that may support oral health by reducing bacterial growth and promoting tissue healing. To prepare the tea, fresh or dried nettle leaves are steeped in hot water for several minutes.

While generally safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially for those with existing health conditions or on medication.

3. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When brewed into a tea, it can help soothe inflamed gums by reducing swelling and combating bacterial growth. The active compounds in hypericum perforatum, such as hypericin and flavonoids, contribute to its ability to calm gum irritation and promote healing. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially if you are on medication, as it can interact with certain drugs.

Regular consumption of hypericum perforatum tea, combined with good oral hygiene, may support overall gum health and reduce the risk of periodontal disease.

4. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a popular herbal remedy often used in teas to support immune health and reduce inflammation.

When consumed as a herbal tea, echinacea may help soothe inflamed gums by its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It contains compounds like alkamides and flavonoids that can inhibit the growth of bacteria, which are often responsible for gum infections. While it is generally considered safe for short-term use, it should not replace professional dental care for severe gum issues.

Incorporating echinacea tea into a daily routine may offer complementary benefits for maintaining oral health and reducing gum irritation.

5. Origanum vulgare

Orihanum vulgare, commonly known as oregano, is a versatile herb that has been traditionally used for its potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When brewed into a herbal tea, oregano can help soothe inflamed gums by reducing bacterial growth and calming irritation. The essential oils in oregano, particularly carvacrol and thymol, are known to possess strong antiseptic qualities that may support gum health. However, it is important to use oregano tea in moderation, as it can be quite strong and may cause digestive discomfort in some individuals.

While it may offer complementary benefits, it should not replace professional dental care for persistent gum inflammation.

6. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, has been widely used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When brewed into a tea, it can help reduce inflammation and irritation in the gums, making it a popular remedy for conditions like gingivitis. The active compounds in chamomile, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, contribute to its ability to calm gum tissue and promote healing. Regular consumption of chamomile tea may also help alleviate oral discomfort and reduce the risk of bacterial infection in the mouth.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using chamomile tea as a treatment for persistent or severe gum inflammation.

7. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, making it a potential remedy for inflamed gums.

When brewed into a herbal tea, yarrow can help reduce swelling and soothe irritation in the gums due to its high content of essential oils and flavonoids. The tea works by promoting blood circulation and reducing bacterial growth, which are key factors in gum inflammation. It is often recommended as a natural alternative to commercial mouthwashes, though it should be used in conjunction with good oral hygiene practices.

However, individuals with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family should exercise caution before using yarrow tea.

8. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal tea often used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

When consumed as a tea, chamomile can help reduce inflammation in the gums by soothing irritation and promoting healing. Its active compounds, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, possess potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects that may help combat gum infections. Chamomile tea can be used as a natural remedy to alleviate symptoms of gingivitis and other mild gum inflammations.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using chamomile tea as a treatment for persistent or severe gum issues.

9. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is an herbal remedy that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

When brewed into a tea, it may help reduce inflammation in the gums, making it a potential natural remedy for gum irritation and gingivitis. The active compound, silymarin, is believed to support tissue repair and reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to gum health. While it is generally considered safe, individuals should consult with a healthcare provider before using it, especially if they are on medications or have existing medical conditions.

Incorporating silybum marianum tea into a daily routine may complement other oral hygiene practices for improved gum health.

10. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is often used in herbal teas to support oral health, particularly for inflamed gums.

The gel derived from the aloe plant contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help reduce gum irritation and bacterial growth. When brewed into a tea, aloe vera can soothe inflamed tissues and promote healing due to its high concentration of vitamins and minerals. However, it is important to use pure aloe gel rather than the latex found in some products, as the latter can be harsh on the digestive system.

While aloe vera tea may offer relief for gum inflammation, it is best used as a complementary treatment alongside professional dental care.
