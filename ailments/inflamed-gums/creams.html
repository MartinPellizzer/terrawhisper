10 Best Herbal Creams For Inflamed Gums

Herbal creams for inflamed gums are natural remedies that incorporate plant-based ingredients known for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These creams often contain ingredients like aloe vera, chamomile, calendula, and tea tree oil, which help reduce swelling, redness, and irritation in the gums. They are typically preferred by individuals seeking alternatives to conventional treatments that may include harsh chemicals or side effects. Herbal creams can be used as a complementary therapy alongside professional dental care to promote gum healing and prevent further inflammation.

However, it is important to consult with a dentist or healthcare provider before using these products to ensure they are safe and effective for individual health conditions.

1. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is often used in herbal creams formulated to soothe inflamed gums due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These creams typically contain extracts of the leaves, which are rich in compounds like rosmarinic acid and flavonoids that help reduce swelling and prevent infection. The natural ingredients in salvia officinalis herbal creams can provide a gentle, non-irritating alternative to conventional gum treatments. Regular application may help alleviate gum irritation and promote healing in cases of gingivitis or mild periodontal issues.

However, it is advisable to consult a dentist before using such creams, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

2. Aloe vera (Aloe barbadensis)

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is often used in herbal creams for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for treating inflamed gums.

These creams typically contain a concentrated form of aloe gel, which can help reduce swelling, redness, and irritation associated with gum inflammation. The natural compounds in aloe vera, such as polysaccharides and enzymes, promote healing and may help prevent bacterial growth that contributes to gum disease. When applied topically, aloe-based creams can provide a cooling effect, offering relief from discomfort and promoting a sense of freshness.

However, it is important to consult with a dentist or healthcare provider before using aloe vera products, especially if you have existing oral health conditions or are taking other medications.

3. Marigold (Calendula officinalis)

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as pot marigold, is a medicinal plant widely used in herbal creams for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These creams are particularly effective for inflamed gums due to the presence of compounds like flavonoids and triterpenes, which help reduce redness and swelling. The gentle nature of calendula makes it suitable for sensitive oral tissues, offering a natural alternative to conventional treatments. When applied topically, calendula-based creams can promote healing and provide relief from gum irritation and minor infections.

Overall, calendula officinalis herbal creams are a safe and effective option for managing gum inflammation and supporting oral health.

4. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When formulated into a topical cream, it can be applied to inflamed gums to help reduce redness, swelling, and irritation. The active compounds in hypericum perforatum, such as hyperforin and hypericin, are believed to have antimicrobial and analgesic effects that may support gum health. While some studies suggest its potential benefits for oral health, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you are on medications that may interact with St. John's Wort.

Overall, hypericum perforatum herbal creams may offer a natural alternative for managing mild gum inflammation, though they should not replace professional dental care.
