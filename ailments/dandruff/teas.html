10 Best Herbal Teas For Dandruff

Herbal teas can be an effective natural remedy for dandruff due to their soothing and antifungal properties.

Ingredients like nettle, chamomile, and sage are commonly used in herbal teas to help reduce scalp irritation and excess oil production. These teas work by gently cleansing the scalp and promoting a balanced environment that discourages the growth of dandruff-causing fungi. To use, simply steep the herbs in hot water and apply the cooled liquid to the scalp, allowing it to sit for several minutes before rinsing.

Regular use of herbal tea treatments can complement other dandruff management strategies, offering a gentle and holistic approach to scalp health.

1. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as horsetail, is a herb traditionally used in herbal teas to address dandruff due to its high silica content, which strengthens hair and scalp health.

The tea is believed to promote scalp circulation and reduce flakiness by balancing oil production and soothing irritation. Rich in antioxidants and minerals like calcium and potassium, it supports overall scalp vitality and may help prevent fungal infections that contribute to dandruff. To prepare the tea, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried horsetail in hot water for 10-15 minutes, then apply it to the scalp or drink it as a daily tonic.

While generally safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with sensitive skin or existing medical conditions.

2. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential benefits for scalp health.

Herbal teas made from dried sage leaves can be brewed and applied to the scalp to help reduce dandruff due to their antimicrobial and antifungal properties. Sage contains compounds like thujone and camphor, which may help in combating the fungi that contribute to dandruff. To use sage tea for dandruff, steep a handful of dried leaves in boiling water for about 10 minutes and then strain the liquid before applying it to the scalp.

Regular use of sage herbal tea may help soothe the scalp and promote a healthier, flake-free appearance.

3. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its potential benefits in treating dandruff.

When brewed into a herbal tea, it may help reduce scalp irritation and inflammation, which are common contributors to dandruff. The tea is believed to support healthy hair growth by nourishing the scalp and balancing its pH levels. To prepare the tea, fresh or dried stinging nettle leaves are steeped in hot water for several minutes.

While some people find it effective, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have sensitive skin or underlying health conditions.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its potential benefits in treating various skin conditions, including dandruff.

When brewed into a herbal tea, it may help reduce seborrheic dermatitis, a common cause of dandruff, by soothing inflammation and regulating oil production on the scalp. The tea is believed to contain compounds like hyperforin and flavonoids, which may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using St. John's wort, as it can interact with certain medications.

While some people find relief from using hypericum perforatum tea for dandruff, results may vary, and it is often used in conjunction with other treatments for best outcomes.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a popular herb used in herbal teas for its potential benefits in treating dandruff.

The essential oils in rosemary, particularly cineole and camphor, have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce scalp infections and irritation commonly associated with dandruff. When brewed into a tea and applied to the scalp, rosemary tea may help improve blood circulation, promoting healthier hair growth and reducing flakiness. It is often used as a natural alternative to commercial dandruff shampoos, offering a gentler and more soothing option for those with sensitive scalps.

Regular use of rosemary herbal tea can contribute to a balanced scalp environment, supporting long-term dandruff management.

6. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green tea and black tea are derived, is also used in herbal teas that may help with dandruff due to its high concentration of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

These teas can help reduce scalp inflammation and excess oil production, which are common contributors to dandruff. Some formulations may include additional ingredients like neem or aloe vera to enhance their effectiveness. Regular use of Camellia sinensis-based herbal teas can promote a healthier scalp environment and reduce the frequency of dandruff.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using these teas, especially if you have sensitive skin or existing scalp conditions.

7. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, has been widely used in herbal teas for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular natural remedy for dandruff.

The gel extracted from the aloe plant contains enzymes, vitamins, and minerals that can help reduce scalp irritation and flakiness associated with dandruff. When brewed into a tea, aloe vera can be applied topically to the scalp to promote healing and balance the skin's natural oils. Regular use of aloe barbadensis herbal tea may help soothe an itchy, irritated scalp and reduce the frequency of dandruff outbreaks.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using aloe-based treatments, especially if you have sensitive skin or existing scalp conditions.

8. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree oil, is often used in herbal teas to help alleviate dandruff due to its potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help combat the fungi and bacteria that can contribute to dandruff, reducing scalp irritation and flakiness. When infused into herbal teas, melaleuca alternifolia can be applied topically or consumed internally to support overall scalp health. However, it is important to use it in properly diluted forms to avoid skin irritation.

While some studies suggest its effectiveness, more research is needed to fully understand its role in treating dandruff.

9. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is often used in herbal teas to help manage dandruff due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oils in lavender, such as linalool and lavandins, can help reduce scalp inflammation and inhibit the growth of Malassezia, a fungus commonly associated with dandruff. When brewed into a calming herbal tea, lavender can soothe the scalp and promote a healthier environment for hair growth. Regular use of lavender tea as a scalp rinse or in conjunction with other herbal remedies may help alleviate symptoms of dandruff.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using lavender tea for persistent or severe dandruff conditions.

10. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a popular herb used in the preparation of herbal teas that may help alleviate dandruff.

This herb contains essential oils such as citral and myrcene, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can combat the fungi responsible for dandruff, such as Malassezia. Regular consumption of lemon grass tea may improve scalp health by reducing sebum production and soothing irritation. It is often recommended to apply the cooled tea topically to the scalp for enhanced benefits.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment, especially for severe or persistent dandruff conditions.
