1. Piper methysticum

Piper methysticum, commonly known as kava, is a traditional herbal plant native to the South Pacific islands, and while it is more widely recognized for its calming effects when consumed as a tea, it has also been explored for its potential in herbal lotions.

Some formulations of kava-based lotions are believed to have soothing properties that may help alleviate the discomfort associated with hiccups by relaxing the diaphragm and reducing spasms. These lotions typically contain extracts of the kava root, which are diluted in a carrier oil or cream to ensure safe topical application. However, it is important to note that scientific evidence supporting the effectiveness of kava lotions for hiccups is limited, and their use should be approached with caution.

As with any herbal remedy, consulting a healthcare professional before use is recommended, especially for individuals with pre-existing health conditions or those taking other medications.
