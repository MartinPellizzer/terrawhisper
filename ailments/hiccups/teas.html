10 Best Herbal Teas For Hiccups

Herbal teas can be an effective natural remedy for alleviating hiccups due to their soothing properties and ability to relax the diaphragm.

Chamomile tea is often recommended for its calming effects, which can help ease the spasms causing hiccups. Peppermint tea may also be beneficial as it can help soothe the digestive system and reduce irritation that might trigger hiccups. Additionally, ginger tea is known for its warming properties and can help settle the stomach, potentially reducing the frequency of hiccups.

While herbal teas are generally safe, it's advisable to consult a healthcare professional if hiccups persist for an extended period.

1. Piper methysticum

Piper methysticum, commonly known as kava, is a traditional herbal plant used in various cultures for its calming and relaxing properties.

While it is often consumed as a beverage for its soothing effects, some individuals have explored its potential in alleviating hiccups, though scientific evidence supporting this use is limited. The plant contains compounds such as kavain and dihydrokavain, which may influence the nervous system and potentially reduce the spasmodic contractions that cause hiccups. However, it is important to note that kava can have side effects, including sedation and liver toxicity, and should be used with caution.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using kava for hiccups or any other health condition.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to alleviate hiccups due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties.

The essential oils in fennel, particularly anethol, help relax the diaphragm and reduce the spasms that cause hiccups. To prepare a fennel tea for hiccups, simply steep a teaspoon of dried fennel seeds in hot water for several minutes. This tea is not only effective for hiccups but also aids in digestion and can soothe the stomach.

It is generally safe for most adults, though it should be used with caution during pregnancy or by individuals with certain medical conditions.

3. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to alleviate hiccups due to its warming and carminative properties.

When brewed into a tea, cumin helps to soothe the digestive system and reduce the spasms that often trigger hiccups. The essential oils in cumin, such as limonene and cineole, may help relax the diaphragm and ease the involuntary contractions that cause hiccups. To prepare the tea, simply steep a teaspoon of whole or ground cumin seeds in hot water for several minutes.

While cumin tea is generally safe for most people, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional if hiccups persist or are accompanied by other symptoms.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to help alleviate hiccups due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
