10 Best Herbal Creams For Plantar Fasciitis

Herbal creams for plantar fasciitis are natural topical treatments that often contain ingredients like capsaicin, menthol, or arnica, which are believed to provide pain relief and reduce inflammation.

These creams are typically preferred by individuals seeking alternative or complementary therapies to conventional pharmaceutical options. Many herbal creams work by improving blood circulation and soothing the affected area, which can help alleviate the sharp, stabbing pain associated with plantar fasciitis. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using these products, especially if you have sensitive skin or are taking other medications.

While herbal creams may offer some relief, they are generally not a standalone cure and should be used in conjunction with other treatments such as stretching exercises, orthotics, or physical therapy.

1. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, is often incorporated into herbal creams for the management of plantar fasciitis due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These creams typically contain curcumin, the active compound in turmeric, which helps reduce inflammation and pain in the plantar fascia. When applied topically, the cream can provide localized relief without the systemic side effects associated with oral anti-inflammatory medications. Some studies suggest that curcumin may help in reducing oxidative stress and promoting tissue repair, which can aid in the recovery process.

However, while these creams may offer some benefit, they should be used in conjunction with other treatments like stretching exercises and proper footwear for optimal results.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it a promising ingredient in herbal creams for plantar fasciitis.

These creams often contain extracts of fresh or dried ginger root, which can help reduce inflammation and pain in the plantar fascia, the thick band of tissue that runs across the bottom of the foot. The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, are believed to work by inhibiting inflammatory pathways in the body. When applied topically, ginger-based creams may provide localized relief without the systemic side effects associated with oral medications.

While more research is needed, many users report improved comfort and reduced stiffness when using zingiber officinale herbal creams as part of their plantar fasciitis management routine.

3. Hypericum perforatum

4. Capsicum annuum

Capsicum annuum, commonly known as chili pepper, is the source of capsaicin, the active ingredient in many herbal creams used for treating plantar fasciitis.

These creams work by delivering capsaicin to the affected area, which helps to reduce pain through the desensitization of pain receptors. The application of capsaicin cream can lead to a temporary burning sensation, which is a normal part of the therapeutic process. While these creams may provide relief for some individuals, they are not a cure and should be used in conjunction with other treatments like stretching exercises and orthotics.

It is important to consult a healthcare professional before starting any new treatment for plantar fasciitis to ensure safety and effectiveness.
