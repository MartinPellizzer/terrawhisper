1. Eclipta prostrata

Eclipta prostrata, commonly known as false dandelion, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential benefits in treating baldness and promoting hair growth.

The plant contains compounds such as wedeloin and ecdysterone, which are believed to support hair follicle health and stimulate new hair growth. Herbal decoctions made from Eclipta prostrata are typically prepared by boiling the dried leaves and roots in water, and the resulting liquid is applied topically or consumed internally. Studies suggest that these decoctions may enhance scalp health and improve conditions like alopecia by reducing inflammation and improving nutrient absorption.

However, more clinical research is needed to fully confirm its efficacy and safety for long-term use in treating baldness.

Herbal decoctions made from turmeric root are often prepared by boiling the rhizome in water to extract its beneficial compounds, which can then be applied topically or consumed internally. Some studies suggest that curcumin may stimulate hair follicles and improve circulation to the scalp, potentially enhancing hair growth.
