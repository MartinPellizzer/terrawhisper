10 Best Herbal Creams For Sunburn

Herbal creams for sunburn are natural remedies that combine soothing ingredients like aloe vera, chamomile, and calendula to alleviate pain and promote skin healing.

These creams are often preferred by individuals seeking alternatives to chemical-based treatments, as they are generally gentler on the skin and less likely to cause irritation. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of herbal ingredients help reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort associated with sunburn. Many herbal creams also contain moisturizing agents such as coconut oil or shea butter, which aid in rehydrating and repairing damaged skin.

While they may not provide immediate relief like over-the-counter pain relievers, herbal creams can be an effective and calming option for managing sunburn symptoms.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is a popular herbal ingredient used in the formulation of creams designed to alleviate sunburn symptoms.

These creams are valued for their soothing and cooling properties, which can help reduce redness, inflammation, and pain associated with sunburn. Aloe vera contains anti-inflammatory compounds and antioxidants that promote skin healing and hydration, making it an effective natural remedy for damaged skin. The gel extracted from the aloe plant is often combined with other moisturizing ingredients to enhance its therapeutic benefits.

When applied topically, aloe-based creams provide a gentle, non-irritating solution for soothing sunburned skin and supporting the skin’s natural recovery process.

2. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as pot marigold, is a popular herbal ingredient used in the formulation of creams for sunburn relief.

These creams are valued for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which help reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort caused by sun exposure. The active compounds in calendula, such as flavonoids and triterpenes, promote skin healing and provide a protective barrier against further irritation. Many natural skincare products incorporate calendula officinalis to offer a gentle, plant-based alternative for sunburn treatment.

When applied topically, these creams can help calm the skin and support the recovery process without causing additional irritation.

3. Rosa canina

Rosa canina, also known as rosehip, is a traditional herbal ingredient that has been used for centuries to promote skin healing and rejuvenation.

Rosa canina herbal creams are formulated with the oil and extracts of rosehips, which are rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and essential fatty acids. These creams are particularly beneficial for soothing and repairing sunburned skin, as they help reduce inflammation and accelerate the skin's natural healing process. The anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties of rosehip oil make it an effective natural remedy for sunburn relief.

Regular use of rosa canina herbal creams can also help improve skin texture and restore a healthy glow after sun damage.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is often used in herbal creams to alleviate the symptoms of sunburn due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These creams typically contain a concentrated form of the plant's extract, which may help reduce redness, swelling, and pain associated with sunburn. The active compounds in hypericum perforatum, such as hyperforin and hypericin, are believed to support skin healing and provide a soothing effect. However, it is important to note that some individuals may experience allergic reactions or skin irritation when using these products.

As with any topical treatment, it is advisable to perform a patch test and consult a healthcare professional before applying hypericum perforatum cream for sunburn.

5. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal ingredient used in the formulation of soothing creams for sunburn relief.

These creams often contain chamomile extract, which is known for its anti-inflammatory and calming properties that can help reduce redness and irritation caused by sun exposure. The natural compounds in chamomile, such as bisabolol and flavonoids, have been shown to promote skin healing and provide a cooling effect on burned skin. When applied topically, chamomile-based creams can offer a gentle, non-irritating way to ease discomfort and support the skin’s recovery process.

As a natural alternative to commercial sunburn treatments, these herbal creams are favored by those seeking holistic and gentle skincare solutions.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is often incorporated into herbal creams for its soothing and cooling properties.

These creams are particularly beneficial for treating sunburn due to their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which help reduce redness, swelling, and pain. The essential oils in lavender are known to promote skin healing and provide a calming effect, making the creams a natural alternative to conventional sunburn treatments. Additionally, the pleasant aroma of lavender can enhance the overall experience of using the cream, offering both therapeutic and relaxing benefits.

When applied topically, these herbal creams can offer relief and support the skin's natural recovery process after sun exposure.

7. Centella asiatica

Centella asiatica, also known as gotu kola, is a traditional herb widely used in herbal creams for its soothing and healing properties.

These creams are particularly beneficial for sunburn relief as they contain compounds like asiatic acid and madecassic acid, which help reduce inflammation and promote skin repair. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects of Centella asiatica can help alleviate redness, pain, and peeling associated with sunburn. Additionally, these herbal creams are often preferred for their natural ingredients, making them a gentle option for sensitive skin.

Regular application of Centella asiatica-based creams can support faster recovery and prevent long-term skin damage from excessive sun exposure.

8. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and cooling properties, making it a valuable ingredient in herbal creams for sunburn relief.

These creams often incorporate ginger extract to soothe irritated skin and reduce redness caused by UV radiation exposure. The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, help alleviate pain and promote skin healing by improving blood circulation. When applied topically, ginger-based creams can provide a cooling sensation that offers immediate comfort to sunburned skin.

While they may not replace conventional sunburn treatments, these herbal creams can be a natural and effective alternative for mild to moderate sunburn relief.

9. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chaste tree, is often used in herbal formulations for its potential soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

While not a traditional remedy for sunburn, some herbal creams containing vitex may offer mild relief by reducing redness and irritation. These creams are typically made with a base of natural oils and extracts, which can help moisturize and cool the skin. However, it is important to note that vitex agnus-castus is not a substitute for standard sunburn treatments like aloe vera or hydrocortisone.

Always consult a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy for sunburn, especially if you have sensitive skin or existing skin conditions.

10. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green tea is derived, is often incorporated into herbal creams designed to soothe and repair sunburned skin.

These creams typically contain extracts rich in antioxidants, such as catechins, which help reduce inflammation and promote skin healing. The anti-inflammatory properties of Camellia sinensis may provide relief from the redness and discomfort associated with sunburn. While these creams are generally safe for most skin types, they should not replace sunscreen or be used as a substitute for proper sun protection.

When applied after sun exposure, Camellia sinensis-based creams can offer a natural, calming alternative to conventional sunburn treatments.
