Herbal capsules are generally considered safer than some pharmaceutical alternatives, though they should still be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

1. Thistle (Silybum marianum)

Silybum marianum, commonly known as milk thistle, is a herbal supplement often used for its potential liver-protecting properties.

While primarily studied for its effects on liver health, some research suggests that silybum marianum may also support digestive health by reducing inflammation and promoting the healing of stomach lining. Herbal capsules containing silybum marianum are sometimes used to alleviate symptoms associated with excess mucus production in the stomach, such as indigestion or discomfort. However, it is important to note that more clinical studies are needed to fully understand its efficacy for this specific use.

As with any supplement, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if you have existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

3. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, is a traditional herbal remedy often used in the form of capsules to support digestive health.
