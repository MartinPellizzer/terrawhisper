10 Best Herbal Lozenges For Excessive Sweating

Herbal lozenges are natural remedies designed to address excessive sweating, particularly in the underarm area, by incorporating ingredients such as sage, spearmint, and neem, which are known for their antiperspirant and cooling properties.

These lozenges work by reducing sweat gland activity and providing a soothing effect on the skin, making them a popular alternative to conventional antiperspirants. They are often favored for their mild, pleasant taste and the absence of harsh chemicals, making them suitable for individuals with sensitive skin. Regular use of herbal lozenges can help manage symptoms of hyperhidrosis without the side effects associated with synthetic deodorants.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional if excessive sweating persists, as it may indicate an underlying medical condition.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a traditional herbal remedy often used to support immune function and reduce inflammation.

When formulated into herbal lozenges, echinacea purpurea may help alleviate symptoms associated with excessive sweating by promoting overall health and reducing stress-related perspiration. These lozenges are typically made from dried plant parts and are designed to be dissolved slowly in the mouth, allowing the active compounds to be absorbed through the mucous membranes. While echinacea is not specifically targeted at reducing sweat production, its potential anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties may indirectly help manage excessive sweating in some individuals.

As with any herbal supplement, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with underlying health conditions or taking other medications.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its therapeutic properties, including its ability to reduce excessive sweating.

Herbal lozenges made from ginger extract are designed to provide a natural remedy for hyperhidrosis, particularly in the underarm and hand areas. These lozenges work by stimulating circulation and regulating body temperature, which can help reduce sweat production. The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, are believed to have anticholinergic effects that inhibit sweat gland activity.

While generally safe, individuals with allergies or gastrointestinal sensitivities should consult a healthcare provider before using ginger-based lozenges for excessive sweating.

3. Sutherlandia frutescens

Sutherlandia frutescens, also known as "cancer bush," is a traditional South African herbal remedy that has been used for various health conditions, including excessive sweating.

Herbal lozenges made from Sutherlandia frutescens are often marketed as natural solutions to reduce night sweats and other forms of excessive perspiration. These lozenges are typically made by drying and grinding the plant material into a fine powder, which is then formed into small, dissolvable tablets. While some studies suggest that Sutherlandia may have adaptogenic properties that help regulate body temperature and reduce stress-induced sweating, more research is needed to confirm its efficacy for this specific use.

As with any herbal supplement, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals with underlying health conditions or those taking other medications.

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herbal ingredient used in lozenges to help manage excessive sweating, particularly in the underarms.

6. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential benefits in managing excessive sweating, or hyperhidrosis.

The active compounds in licorice root, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, may help regulate sweat gland activity by influencing hormonal and nervous system pathways. Herbal lozenges made from glycyrrhiza glabra are often used as a natural remedy to reduce sweat production, particularly in cases of localized hyperhidrosis such as underarm sweating. These lozenges are typically safe for short-term use, though long-term consumption may lead to side effects like hypertension due to the mild corticosteroid-like effects of glycyrrhizin.

When used as part of a holistic approach, glycyrrhiza glabra lozenges may offer a gentle and effective option for individuals seeking to manage excessive sweating naturally.
