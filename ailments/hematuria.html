What is hematuria and how does it affect your body?

Hematuria refers to the presence of blood in the urine, which can be visible to the naked eye (gross hematuria) or only detectable under a microscope (microscopic hematuria).

It can result from various conditions such as urinary tract infections, kidney stones, bladder or kidney tumors, or injuries to the urinary system. The presence of blood in the urine may cause the urine to appear pink, red, or brown, and can be accompanied by symptoms like pain, frequent urination, or burning sensations during urination. Hematuria can indicate an underlying health issue that requires medical evaluation, as it may signal serious conditions such as kidney disease or cancer.

If left untreated, hematuria can lead to complications that affect kidney function and overall health.

Underlying causes and risk factors of hematuria

Hematuria, or the presence of blood in urine, can arise from a variety of underlying causes and risk factors, including urinary tract infections, kidney stones, bladder or kidney cancers, and glomerulonephritis.

Structural abnormalities such as congenital urinary tract malformations or an enlarged prostate in men can also contribute to hematuria. Risk factors include a family history of urinary tract disorders, chronic conditions like hypertension or diabetes, and a history of recurrent urinary tract infections. Lifestyle factors such as dehydration, excessive caffeine or alcohol intake, and certain medications may also increase the risk.

Additionally, individuals with a history of trauma to the lower abdomen or pelvis are more prone to developing hematuria due to potential damage to the urinary system.
