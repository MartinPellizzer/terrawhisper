What is stomach growling and how does it affect your body?

Stomach growling, also known as borborygmi, is the sound produced when the muscles in the stomach and intestines contract to move food and fluids through the digestive tract.

These noises are often heard when the stomach is empty and the digestive system is actively working to process food. The growling is a normal part of the digestive process and is usually not a cause for concern. However, frequent or loud stomach growling can sometimes indicate an underlying issue such as hunger, indigestion, or a sensitive digestive system.

In some cases, it may also be a sign of more serious conditions like irritable bowel syndrome or malabsorption disorders.

Underlying causes and risk factors of stomach growling

Stomach growling, or borborygmi, is primarily caused by the movement of gas and fluid through the gastrointestinal tract, which is a normal part of digestion.

The underlying causes often include an empty stomach, as the digestive system produces sounds when there is little food to absorb the digestive juices. Risk factors may include eating disorders such as anorexia or bulimia, which can lead to irregular eating patterns and frequent stomach noises. Additionally, individuals with conditions like irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) or lactose intolerance may experience more pronounced stomach growling due to increased gas production or altered digestion.

Stress and anxiety can also contribute to gastrointestinal disturbances, further increasing the likelihood of audible stomach noises.
