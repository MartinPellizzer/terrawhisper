Chickenpox Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Chickenpox is a highly contagious viral infection caused by the varicella-zoster virus, characterized by an itchy, blister-like rash and fever.

Herbal remedies have been used for centuries to treat the symptoms of chickenpox, offering a natural and effective alternative to conventional medication.

Herbs such as calendula, aloe vera, and chamomile are often used to soothe and calm the skin, while others like echinacea, elderberry, and garlic are valued for their antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties.

Herbal preparations like herbal teas, infusions, and salves, as well as topical applications such as calendula oil and aloe vera gel, are commonly used to reduce the severity and duration of the symptoms, promote healing, and prevent infection.

What causes chickenpox?

The primary causes of chickenpox are the varicella-zoster virus, which is highly contagious and airborne, and it spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Secondary causes of chickenpox may include poor hygiene, such as not washing hands regularly, and close contact with someone who has the virus, especially in crowded areas like schools or daycare centers.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing chickenpox include living in areas with poor vaccination rates, traveling to countries with low vaccination rates, and being immunocompromised, which can make it harder for the body to fight off the virus.

Furthermore, children under the age of 12 who have never been vaccinated or have weakened immune systems are also more susceptible to contracting chickenpox.

What herbs heal chickenpox?

The medicinal herbs used to treat chickenpox are Aloe vera, Calendula, and Echinacea, which have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties that help alleviate symptoms such as itching and fever.

Aloe vera, for instance, contains bioactive compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin that have antiviral and anti-inflammatory effects, while Calendula contains triterpenoids that exhibit antiseptic and antimicrobial properties.

Echinacea, on the other hand, contains phenolic acids and alkylamides that stimulate the immune system and reduce the severity of the infection.

By harnessing these therapeutic actions and bioactive compounds, these herbs can help soothe and heal the skin, reduce the risk of secondary infections, and promote recovery from chickenpox.

What preparations treat chickenpox?

The herbal preparations used to treat chickenpox are often made into teas, infusions, and poultices to soothe the skin and reduce fever.

A warm infusion may be consumed to help alleviate symptoms, while a cool compress made from a specific herbal preparation can be applied to the affected areas to ease itching and discomfort.

A calming tea may be sipped to promote restful sleep and reduce stress, which can help the body recover from the illness.

Additionally, a topical poultice made from certain herbal preparations can be applied directly to the skin to accelerate the healing process and reduce inflammation.
