Peptic Ulcers Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Peptic ulcers is a painful condition characterized by open sores in the lining of the stomach or the first part of the small intestine, often caused by the long-term use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), infection with Helicobacter pylori bacteria, or a combination of both.

Herbal remedies have gained popularity in treating peptic ulcers due to their potential to soothe and protect the stomach lining, reduce inflammation, and combat bacterial infections.

What causes peptic ulcers?

The primary causes of peptic ulcers are the overproduction of stomach acid and the prolonged use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), which irritate the stomach lining and lead to ulcer formation.

Secondary causes of peptic ulcers include bacterial infections, such as Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori), which weaken the stomach lining and create an environment conducive to ulcer development.

Certain lifestyle factors, such as smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, increase the risk of peptic ulcers by damaging the stomach lining and impairing the body's ability to heal itself.

Additionally, a diet high in spicy or fatty foods, as well as a lack of vitamin C, can also contribute to the development of peptic ulcers by irritating the stomach lining and impairing the body's ability to heal itself.
