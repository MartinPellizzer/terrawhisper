Overactive Bladder Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Overactive bladder is a medical condition characterized by a sudden and intense urge to urinate, often accompanied by involuntary loss of urine.

Herbal remedies have been found to be effective in treating this ailment due to their natural ability to relax the muscles, reduce inflammation, and regulate the nervous system.

Certain herbs such as saw palmetto, marshmallow root, and uva ursi have been traditionally used to treat overactive bladder as they have antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties that help to calm the bladder muscles and reduce the frequency of urination.

Herbal preparations such as tea, tinctures, and capsules made from herbs like bladderwrack, corn silk, and dandelion root have also been used to treat overactive bladder as they help to reduce swelling, soothe the urinary tract, and promote urinary retention.

What causes overactive bladder?

The primary causes of overactive bladder are changes in the bladder muscles and nervous system, which can be due to age-related decline or certain medical conditions such as diabetes and stroke.

Secondary causes may include urinary tract infections, which can irritate the bladder and trigger muscle contractions, as well as the use of certain medications like sedatives and antidepressants, which can affect bladder control.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing overactive bladder include consuming high amounts of caffeine and spicy foods, which can irritate the bladder, as well as obesity and lack of physical activity, which can put pressure on the bladder.

Additionally, women who have experienced menopause or pregnancy may also be more prone to overactive bladder due to hormonal changes.
