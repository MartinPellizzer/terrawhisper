What is plantar fasciitis and how does it affect your body?

Plantar fasciitis is a common condition characterized by inflammation of the plantar fascia, a thick band of tissue that runs along the bottom of the foot, connecting the heel bone to the toes.

This inflammation typically causes sharp, stabbing pain in the heel, especially with the first steps in the morning or after prolonged periods of rest. The pain may decrease as the day progresses but can return after extended standing or physical activity. The condition often results from repetitive strain on the plantar fascia, which can be caused by factors such as flat feet, high arches, obesity, or improper footwear.

If left untreated, plantar fasciitis can lead to chronic pain and affect a person's ability to walk or engage in daily activities comfortably.

Underlying causes and risk factors of plantar fasciitis

Plantar fasciitis is primarily caused by repetitive strain on the plantar fascia, a thick band of tissue that runs across the bottom of the foot.

Common risk factors include obesity, which increases pressure on the feet, and aging, as the tissue loses elasticity over time. Prolonged standing, walking on hard surfaces, or engaging in high-impact activities like running can also contribute to the condition. Individuals with foot mechanics issues, such as flat feet or high arches, are more prone to developing plantar fasciitis due to uneven distribution of weight.

Additionally, wearing improper footwear or having a sedentary lifestyle may further increase the risk by reducing foot strength and flexibility.

How to use herbal remedies safely for plantar fasciitis

Using herbal remedies for plantar fasciitis should involve careful selection and proper application to ensure safety.
