10 Best Herbal Tinctures For Anxiety

Herbal tinctures for anxiety are concentrated liquid extracts made from plants known for their calming properties, such as valerian root, passionflower, and lemon balm.

These tinctures are typically prepared by soaking herbs in alcohol or glycerin to extract their active compounds, making them easy to absorb into the bloodstream. They are often used as natural alternatives or complements to conventional anxiety treatments, offering a gentler approach with fewer side effects. Many people find relief from symptoms like restlessness and sleep disturbances through regular use of these tinctures.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any herbal treatment, especially if you are taking other medications or have underlying health conditions.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to alleviate anxiety and promote relaxation.

Valerian root tinctures are prepared by soaking the dried root in alcohol, allowing the active compounds, such as valerenic acid and isolvalerenic acid, to be extracted for medicinal use. These compounds are believed to interact with the brain's GABA receptors, enhancing calming effects and reducing feelings of stress and nervousness. Valerian tinctures are often preferred over other forms of valerian due to their longer shelf life and easier dosage control.

While generally considered safe, they should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional, especially when combined with other medications.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata, commonly known as passionflower, is a herbal remedy often used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety.

Its tincture form is popular due to its ease of use and potency, typically made by soaking the dried plant material in alcohol to extract its active compounds. The herb contains flavonoids and alkaloids that may help regulate neurotransmitters like GABA, promoting a calming effect on the nervous system. Clinical studies suggest that passionflower tinctures can reduce anxiety levels, particularly when used in conjunction with other relaxation techniques.

As with any herbal supplement, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking medications.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhiza glabra herbal tinctures are often used to support emotional well-being and alleviate symptoms of anxiety due to their adaptogenic qualities. These tinctures contain compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which may help regulate the body's stress response and promote a sense of calm. When used as part of a holistic approach, licorice root tinctures can complement other anxiety-reducing strategies, though they should be used cautiously due to potential side effects such as increased blood pressure.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures into one's wellness routine.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and mild depression.

Its tincture form, made by steeping the dried plant in alcohol, is often preferred for its convenience and potency. Studies suggest that the active compounds in hypericum perforatum, such as hypericin and hyperforin, may help regulate mood by influencing neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine. However, it is important to note that St. John's Wort can interact with various medications, including antidepressants and birth control, so consultation with a healthcare provider is essential before use.

Despite its potential benefits, it should not be used as a substitute for professional medical treatment for severe anxiety disorders.

5. Piper methysticum

Piper methysticum, commonly known as kava, has been traditionally used for its calming effects and is now available in herbal tincture form to help manage anxiety.

These tinctures are made by extracting the active compounds from the roots of the plant, which include kavalactones, known for their anxiolytic properties. When taken orally, the tinctures can promote relaxation and reduce feelings of stress without the sedative effects associated with some pharmaceutical anxiety medications. However, it is important to use them under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as they may interact with other medications or have side effects in certain individuals.

Overall, piper methysticum tinctures offer a natural alternative for those seeking relief from anxiety, though their use should be approached with caution and awareness of potential risks.

6. Avena sativa

Avena sativa, commonly known as oat straw, is a herbal tincture that has been traditionally used to support emotional balance and alleviate symptoms of anxiety.

This plant-based remedy is believed to work by promoting a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce feelings of stress and tension. The tincture is typically prepared by soaking dried oat straw in alcohol, allowing the active compounds to be extracted for use. It is often taken in small doses, either directly under the tongue or diluted in water, as a natural alternative to conventional anxiety treatments.

While more research is needed, many users report a sense of relaxation and improved mood when using Avena sativa tinctures regularly.

7. Leonurus cardiaca

Leonurus cardiaca, commonly known as heart herb or purple deadnettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming properties.

Herbal tinctures made from Leonurus cardiaca are often prepared by soaking the dried plant in alcohol to extract its active compounds, which may include flavonoids and tannins. These tinctures are believed to support emotional balance and alleviate symptoms of anxiety by promoting relaxation and reducing nervous tension. Some studies suggest that the herb may interact with the central nervous system, potentially enhancing the effects of other calming herbs.

While more research is needed, many people use Leonurus cardiaca tinctures as a natural alternative or complement to conventional anxiety treatments.

8. Withania somnifera

Withania somnifera, commonly known as ashwagandha, is an adaptogenic herb traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to manage stress and anxiety.

Herbal tinctures made from Withania somnifera are concentrated extracts that can be easily absorbed into the bloodstream, offering a convenient and effective way to harness its calming properties. Studies suggest that the active compounds in ashwagandha, such as withanolides, may help reduce cortisol levels and promote a sense of well-being by modulating the body's stress response. These tinctures are often preferred for their natural composition and potential to support long-term mental health without the side effects associated with pharmaceuticals.

When used as part of a holistic approach, Withania somnifera tinctures may offer a gentle and sustainable option for managing anxiety.

9. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a widely used herbal tincture for managing anxiety due to its calming and sedative properties.

This herb contains compounds such as rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, which are believed to interact with the central nervous system to reduce stress and promote relaxation. Lemon balm tinctures are often taken orally in small doses, typically diluted in water or taken directly under the tongue for rapid absorption. Clinical studies have shown that regular use of Melissa officinalis can significantly lower symptoms of generalized anxiety disorder.

As a natural alternative to pharmaceuticals, lemon balm tinctures are favored for their mild side effect profile and ease of integration into daily wellness routines.

10. Scutellaria lateriflora

Scutellaria lateriflora, commonly known as blue cohosh, is a medicinal plant traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming properties.

Herbal tinctures made from Scutellaria lateriflora are often utilized to support emotional balance and alleviate symptoms of anxiety. These tinctures are typically prepared by soaking the dried plant material in alcohol, allowing the active compounds to be extracted for use. The herb contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, which may contribute to its anxiolytic effects.

While some studies suggest potential benefits, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using Scutellaria lateriflora tinctures, as they may interact with certain medications or have side effects.
