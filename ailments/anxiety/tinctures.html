10 Best Herbal Tinctures For Anxiety

Herbal tinctures for anxiety are concentrated liquid extracts made by steeping plant material in alcohol or another solvent, allowing the active compounds to be absorbed for therapeutic use.

These tinctures are often used as natural alternatives or complements to conventional anxiety treatments, offering a more holistic approach to managing symptoms. Common herbs used in such tinctures include valerian root, passionflower, lemon balm, and ashwagandha, each known for its calming and stress-reducing properties. They are typically taken in small doses and can be customized to suit individual needs, making them a flexible option for those seeking natural remedies.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal tinctures, as they may interact with medications or have side effects.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to address anxiety and sleep disorders.

Its tinctures are concentrated extracts made by soaking the dried root of the plant in alcohol, which helps preserve its active compounds. These tinctures are often taken orally and are believed to work by increasing levels of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, promoting relaxation and reducing nervousness. While valerian tinctures are generally considered safe for short-term use, they may cause drowsiness or digestive upset in some individuals.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before starting valerian tinctures, especially if you are on other medications or have underlying health conditions.

2. Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata, commonly known as passionflower, is a popular herb used in tinctures to help alleviate symptoms of anxiety.

The tinctures are typically made by soaking the dried leaves and flowers in alcohol, creating a potent extract that can be taken orally. Passionflower is believed to work by increasing levels of GABA, a neurotransmitter that promotes relaxation and reduces nervousness. Its calming effects make it a natural alternative for those seeking relief from mild to moderate anxiety without the use of pharmaceuticals.

Many users report improved sleep and reduced restlessness when using passiflora incarnata tinctures regularly.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential calming effects on the nervous system.

Glycyrrhiza glabra herbal tinctures are often prepared by soaking the dried root in alcohol to extract its active compounds, including glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which may contribute to their therapeutic properties. These tinctures are believed to help alleviate symptoms of anxiety by modulating stress hormones and supporting adrenal function. However, long-term use of licorice root tinctures can lead to side effects such as hypertension due to the effects of glycyrrhizin on sodium and potassium levels.

As a result, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using glycyrrhiza glabra tinctures, especially for individuals with pre-existing health conditions or those taking other medications.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to support mental health, particularly in the management of anxiety.

Its tincture form, derived from the dried flowers and leaves, is popular for its potential calming effects and adaptogenic properties. Studies suggest that hypericum perforatum may help reduce symptoms of mild to moderate anxiety by influencing neurotransmitters such as serotonin and dopamine. However, it is important to note that St. John's wort can interact with various medications, including antidepressants and birth control, so it should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Despite its historical use and anecdotal support, more clinical research is needed to fully understand its efficacy and safety in treating anxiety.

5. Withania somnifera

Withania somnifera, commonly known as ashwagandha, is a well-regarded adaptogenic herb used in Ayurvedic medicine for its calming effects on the mind and body.

Herbal tinctures made from Withania somnifera are often used to help manage symptoms of anxiety by regulating the body's stress response and promoting emotional balance. These tinctures are typically prepared by soaking the dried root in alcohol, which extracts the active compounds such as withanolides. Studies suggest that ashwagandha may reduce cortisol levels and enhance resilience to stress, making it a natural alternative for those seeking relief from anxiety.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any herbal treatment, especially if you are taking other medications or have underlying health conditions.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is widely used in herbal tinctures for its calming properties that help alleviate symptoms of anxiety.

These tinctures are typically prepared by soaking dried lavender flowers in alcohol, allowing the plant's essential oils and phytochemicals to be extracted for maximum potency. The primary active compounds, such as linalool and lavandins, are known to interact with the central nervous system, promoting relaxation and reducing stress. Many people find relief from mild to moderate anxiety through regular use of lavender tinctures, often taking them sublingually or diluted in water.

While generally considered safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with existing health conditions or on medication.

7. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a popular herbal remedy used for its calming properties, and its tincture form is widely utilized for managing anxiety.

The tincture is typically made by steeping fresh or dried lemon balm leaves in alcohol, which extracts the plant's active compounds, such as rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, known for their anxiolytic effects. Studies suggest that Melissa officinalis may help reduce symptoms of generalized anxiety by modulating the nervous system and promoting relaxation without the side effects often associated with pharmaceutical anxiolytics. When used as part of a holistic approach, lemon balm tinctures can support emotional balance and improve sleep quality in individuals experiencing stress or anxiety.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any herbal treatment, especially for those taking other medications or with pre-existing health conditions.

8. Avena sativa

Avena sativa, commonly known as oat straw, is a herbal tincture that has been traditionally used to support mental well-being and alleviate symptoms of anxiety.

This calming herb is believed to work by promoting a sense of relaxation and reducing mental agitation through its mild sedative properties. Avena sativa tinctures are often taken orally, usually diluted in water or another liquid, to help balance the nervous system and ease stress-related symptoms. The herb contains various nutrients and compounds that may contribute to its calming effects, including saponins and silica.

Due to its gentle nature, Avena sativa is considered a safe alternative for those seeking natural remedies for mild to moderate anxiety.

9. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, has been traditionally used for its calming properties and is often incorporated into herbal tinctures for anxiety relief.

These tinctures are typically prepared by extracting the dried aerial parts of the plant with alcohol, preserving its active compounds such as azulene and essential oils. Studies suggest that yarrow may help reduce symptoms of anxiety by promoting relaxation and balancing the nervous system. Its mild sedative effects make it a popular choice for those seeking natural alternatives to conventional anxiety treatments.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals taking other medications or with existing health conditions.

10. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to support emotional and hormonal balance.

Herbal tinctures made from Vitex agnus-castus are often used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety by regulating the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, which plays a key role in the body's stress response. These tinctures are typically taken in small, consistent doses and are believed to influence neurotransmitter activity and hormonal fluctuations that contribute to anxiety. While scientific research on Vitex agnus-castus is ongoing, many users report a calming effect and improved mood when using these tinctures.

As with any herbal supplement, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting Vitex agnus-castus, especially if you are taking other medications or have underlying health conditions.
