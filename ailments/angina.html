Angina Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Angina is a medical condition characterized by chest pain or discomfort caused by reduced blood flow to the heart, often resulting from coronary artery disease or high blood pressure.

Herbal remedies have been used to treat angina due to their potential to improve cardiovascular health and reduce symptoms.

Herbs such as Hawthorn, which improves blood flow and reduces blood pressure, and Ginkgo Biloba, which increases oxygenation to the heart, have been traditionally used to alleviate angina symptoms.

Herbal preparations, including teas made from herbs like Lavender and Rosemary, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, and tinctures of herbs like Garlic and Ginger, which have vasodilatory and anti-clotting effects, have also been used to treat angina, often in combination with lifestyle modifications and conventional therapies.

What causes angina?

The primary causes of angina are coronary artery disease, where the arteries that supply blood to the heart muscle become narrowed or blocked due to atherosclerosis, and heart valve problems, which can disrupt blood flow to the heart.

Secondary causes of angina include hypertension, which can further narrow the arteries and reduce blood flow, and anaemia, where low red blood cell count reduces oxygen delivery to the heart muscle.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing angina include smoking, which damages the inner lining of the arteries, and a diet high in saturated fats and cholesterol, which contributes to atherosclerosis.

Additionally, a sedentary lifestyle and obesity can also contribute to the development of angina by increasing blood pressure and heart rate, putting additional strain on the heart.

What herbs heal angina?

The medicinal herbs used to treat angina are often rich in bioactive compounds and therapeutic actions that help alleviate symptoms and promote cardiovascular health.

For example, the herb Hawthorn (Crataegus monogyna) contains flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs) that help reduce blood pressure, improve blood flow, and prevent platelet aggregation, all of which can help alleviate angina symptoms.

Another herb, Ginkgo biloba, contains ginkgo flavonoids and terpenoids that help dilate blood vessels, improve oxygen delivery to the heart, and reduce inflammation, all of which can help reduce the frequency and severity of angina attacks.

Additionally, the herb Coleus forskohlii contains forskolin, a compound that helps relax blood vessels and increase cardiac output, both of which can help reduce angina symptoms and improve overall cardiovascular health.

What preparations treat angina?

The herbal preparations used to treat angina are diverse and include teas, infusions, and tinctures that help to improve blood circulation and reduce inflammation.

Consuming warm decoctions can help to dilate blood vessels, reducing the frequency and severity of angina attacks, while cold infusions may be used to stimulate cardiac function and improve overall cardiovascular health.

Some herbal preparations are also used to lower blood pressure, which can help to alleviate angina symptoms by reducing the strain on the heart.

Additionally, certain herbal concoctions can help to improve the body's ability to utilize oxygen, further reducing the risk of angina attacks.
