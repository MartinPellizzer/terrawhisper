2. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used for its soothing properties, particularly in herbal juices aimed at alleviating throat redness.

The active compounds in licorice, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, possess anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects that can help reduce irritation and inflammation in the throat. When brewed into a juice, elderberry can soothe irritated throats and alleviate symptoms associated with colds or sore throats. 9. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

10. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its soothing properties, and its herbal juices can be beneficial for alleviating throat redness.

When consumed as a juice, sage can provide a natural remedy that supports throat health without the need for harsh medications. To prepare the juice, fresh sage leaves are typically crushed or blended with water or a mild herbal base to enhance flavor and potency.

Regular consumption of sage herbal juice may help soothe inflammation, ease discomfort, and promote healing in cases of sore or red throats.
