10 Best Herbal Tinctures For Oily Face

Herbal tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts made from dried herbs, often used in skincare for their natural therapeutic properties.

For oily skin, tinctures containing ingredients like witch hazel, green tea, and neem can help regulate sebum production and reduce excess oil. These tinctures are typically alcohol-based, which can help in dissolving oils and providing a refreshing, astringent effect. They are easy to apply and can be used as a toner or spot treatment to control shine and prevent breakouts.

When choosing herbal tinctures for oily skin, it's important to look for products with natural, non-comedogenic ingredients to avoid clogging pores.

1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is a versatile herb often used in herbal tinctures for its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

When formulated into a tincture, sage can help regulate excess sebum production, making it particularly beneficial for oily skin. Its natural antimicrobial properties may also help reduce breakouts and prevent acne by balancing the skin's natural oils. To use sage tincture on the face, it is typically diluted with a carrier oil or alcohol to avoid irritation.

Regular application can lead to a more refined, matte complexion while promoting overall skin health.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a herbal tincture that has been traditionally used for its potential skin-soothing properties.

When applied topically, it may help reduce excess sebum production, making it a suitable option for individuals with oily skin. The tincture contains compounds like hypericin and flavonoids, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. However, it is important to note that St. John's Wort can interact with certain medications, so it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

For oily skin, it is recommended to perform a patch test first to check for any adverse reactions.

6. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, is often used in herbal tinctures for its potential benefits for oily skin.
