10 Best Herbal Decoctions For Upset Stomach

Herbal decoctions have been traditionally used to soothe upset stomachs by leveraging the natural properties of various plants.

Common herbs such as ginger, peppermint, and chamomile are frequently included in these preparations due to their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. To prepare a decoction, the herbs are typically simmered in water for an extended period to extract their active compounds. This method allows for the gentle delivery of healing properties that can help alleviate nausea, indigestion, and gastrointestinal discomfort.

While herbal decoctions are generally safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional, especially for persistent or severe stomach issues.

1. Cumin (Cuminum cyminum)

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its digestive benefits, particularly in soothing an upset stomach.

When prepared as a decoction, cumin seeds are simmered in water to extract their essential oils and active compounds, which can help alleviate symptoms such as bloating, gas, and indigestion. The aromatic compounds in cumin, including cuminaldehyde and thymol, possess carminative and antispasmodic properties that may reduce stomach discomfort and promote healthy digestion. This herbal decoction is often used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine to support gastrointestinal health.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using cumin decoctions, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking medications.

2. Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been widely used in herbal medicine for its ability to alleviate upset stomach symptoms.

Ginger contains bioactive compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce nausea and digestive discomfort. Herbal decoctions made from fresh or dried ginger root are often prepared by simmering the root in water to extract its beneficial compounds. These decoctions are traditionally used to treat conditions such as motion sickness, morning sickness, and gastrointestinal inflammation.

Due to its efficacy and mild side effect profile, ginger decoctions remain a popular natural remedy for digestive ailments.

3. Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare)

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its soothing effects on the digestive system.

A fennel herbal decoction is typically prepared by simmering the dried seeds in water for several minutes, allowing the active compounds to infuse into the liquid. This decoction is often recommended for individuals experiencing upset stomach, bloating, or indigestion due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties. The essential oil of fennel, particularly anethole, is believed to help relax the gastrointestinal tract and reduce discomfort.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using fennel decoctions, especially for prolonged periods or in conjunction with other medications.

4. Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its digestive benefits.

Its active compound, curcumin, possesses anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach. Herbal decoctions made from dried turmeric root are often prepared by boiling the root in water to extract its beneficial compounds. These decoctions may help reduce bloating, nausea, and indigestion by soothing the gastrointestinal tract.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using turmeric decoctions, especially for prolonged periods or in conjunction with other medications.

5. Dog rose (Rosa canina)

Rosa canina, commonly known as rose hip, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential benefits in soothing upset stomachs.

The decoction of rose hips is prepared by simmering the dried fruit in water, allowing the active compounds such as flavonoids and vitamin C to be extracted. This herbal remedy is believed to help alleviate digestive discomfort, reduce inflammation, and support overall gastrointestinal health. Its mild and gentle nature makes it suitable for many individuals seeking natural relief from mild stomach issues.

However, it is advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before using rose hip decoctions, especially for those with chronic digestive conditions or who are on medications.

6. Black pepper (Piper nigrum)

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential to soothe an upset stomach.

The active compound in black pepper, piperine, may enhance digestion by stimulating the production of digestive enzymes and improving gut motility. Herbal decoctions made from black pepper can help alleviate symptoms such as bloating, indigestion, and nausea by promoting healthy gastrointestinal function. However, it is important to use black pepper in moderation, as excessive consumption may irritate the stomach lining or cause discomfort.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using black pepper decoctions, especially for individuals with existing gastrointestinal conditions.

7. Peppermint (Mentha piperita)

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, has been widely used in herbal medicine for its soothing effects on the digestive system.

Peppermint herbal decoctions are often prepared by steeping fresh or dried leaves in hot water, creating a calming infusion that can help alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach. The active compounds in peppermint, such as menthol and methyl salicylate, have antispasmodic properties that can reduce intestinal cramping and bloating. These decoctions are particularly beneficial for individuals experiencing irritable bowel syndrome or mild gastrointestinal discomfort.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using peppermint herbal decoctions, especially for those with certain medical conditions or taking medications.

8. Thistle (Silybum marianum)

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a herbal remedy commonly used to support digestive health.

Its herbal decoctions are prepared by boiling the dried seeds or leaves in water, creating a potent infusion that may help soothe an upset stomach. The active compound, silymarin, is believed to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that can protect the gastrointestinal tract. Some studies suggest that silybum marianum may help reduce nausea and improve digestion in individuals with mild digestive discomfort.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using milk thistle, especially for those with existing medical conditions or taking other medications.

9. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its soothing properties, particularly for digestive issues such as upset stomach.

When prepared as a decoction, licorice root is boiled in water to extract its active compounds, including glycyrrhizin, which exhibits anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. This preparation can help alleviate symptoms like nausea, indigestion, and stomach discomfort by calming the gastrointestinal tract. However, prolonged use of licorice root decoctions may lead to side effects such as hypertension due to its effect on sodium retention.

As a result, it is often recommended to use licorice root under the guidance of a healthcare professional, especially for individuals with preexisting health conditions.

10. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a widely used herbal remedy for soothing an upset stomach due to its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.

Chamomile herbal decoctions are typically prepared by steeping dried chamomile flowers in hot water, creating a calming tea that can be consumed several times a day. This natural remedy is effective in alleviating symptoms such as bloating, gas, and nausea by reducing gastrointestinal irritation and promoting digestion. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for both adults and children, though it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with allergies or existing medical conditions.

Overall, chamomile decoctions offer a safe and accessible option for managing mild digestive discomfort.
