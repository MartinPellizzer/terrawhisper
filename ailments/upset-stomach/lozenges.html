10 Best Herbal Lozenges For Upset Stomach

Herbal lozenges can be a natural and soothing option for individuals experiencing an upset stomach, as they often contain ingredients like ginger, peppermint, and licorice root, which are known for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

These lozenges work by gently calming the stomach lining and reducing nausea, making them a gentle alternative to over-the-counter medications. Unlike some pharmaceutical remedies, herbal lozenges are typically free from harsh chemicals, making them suitable for those seeking natural remedies. They can be particularly effective for mild cases of indigestion or motion sickness, offering relief without the risk of dependency.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using herbal lozenges, especially if symptoms persist or are severe.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its digestive benefits and is often incorporated into herbal lozenges to help alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach.

These lozenges are formulated with powdered or dried ginger root, which contains bioactive compounds like gingerol and shogaol that can soothe gastrointestinal discomfort. The menthol or other natural flavorings in the lozenges may provide a cooling effect, further easing nausea and indigestion. Due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties, ginger lozenges are considered a safe and effective remedy for mild digestive issues.

However, individuals with known allergies to ginger or those on certain medications should consult a healthcare provider before use.

2. Piper methysticum

Piper methysticum, commonly known as kava, has been traditionally used for its calming and soothing properties, and some herbal lozenges containing this plant may offer relief for upset stomachs by reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation, which can indirectly ease gastrointestinal discomfort.

These lozenges are typically made from the root of the Piper methysticum plant and are often used in alternative medicine for their mild sedative effects. While they are not a direct treatment for stomach issues, they may help alleviate stress-related symptoms that contribute to nausea or indigestion. It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using kava-based products, as they may interact with other medications or have side effects.

Overall, these lozenges can be a complementary option for managing stomach discomfort when used responsibly and under medical guidance.

3. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its digestive benefits.

Cumin herbal lozenges are formulated to support digestion and alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach, such as bloating and gas. These lozenges contain essential oils and compounds like cuminaldehyde, which may help soothe the digestive tract and reduce discomfort. They are often recommended as a natural alternative to conventional remedies for mild digestive issues.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using cumin lozenges, especially for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking other medications.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, has been traditionally used for its digestive benefits, and fennel herbal lozenges are a popular remedy for upset stomach.

These lozenges contain essential oils derived from the seeds of the fennel plant, which are known to have carminative and antispasmodic properties. The compounds in fennel, such as anethol and fenchone, help soothe the gastrointestinal tract by reducing gas, bloating, and cramping. When used as lozenges, fennel can be easily incorporated into daily routines for mild digestive discomfort.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using fennel lozenges, especially for prolonged use or in individuals with existing medical conditions.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used for its soothing properties to alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach.

Herbal lozenges made from licorice root contain glycyrrhizin, a compound that helps reduce inflammation and irritation in the digestive tract. These lozenges can provide relief from heartburn, indigestion, and nausea by coating and protecting the stomach lining. However, prolonged use may lead to side effects such as water retention and increased blood pressure due to the effects of glycyrrhizin.

As a natural remedy, licorice root lozenges are often recommended as a complementary therapy for mild digestive discomfort.

6. Peperomia pellucida

Peperomia pellucida, also known as the "Mexican nettle," is a medicinal plant traditionally used for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Herbal lozenges made from peperomia pellucida are designed to provide relief for upset stomachs by calming the digestive tract and reducing irritation. These lozenges are often made by drying and crushing the plant's leaves, then combining them with natural sweeteners and binders to form a palatable form. The plant contains compounds that may help alleviate symptoms such as bloating, nausea, and indigestion.

While generally considered safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using peperomia pellucida, especially for prolonged or severe digestive issues.

7. Carum carvi

Carum carvi herbal lozenges, derived from the caraway plant, are traditionally used to soothe an upset stomach due to their carminative and digestive properties.

These lozenges help relieve symptoms such as bloating, gas, and indigestion by promoting the release of digestive enzymes and easing gastrointestinal discomfort. The essential oils in caraway, particularly limonene and apiol, have mild antispasmodic effects that can reduce cramping and improve digestion. Often used in herbal medicine, carum carvi lozenges are a natural alternative for those seeking relief without pharmaceuticals.

They are typically safe for most adults but should be used with caution during pregnancy or in individuals with known allergies.

8. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herbal remedy used in the form of lozenges to alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach.

These lozenges work by soothing the digestive tract and reducing spasms in the gastrointestinal muscles, which can help ease nausea and bloating. The cooling effect of peppermint can also provide a refreshing sensation that helps calm an irritated stomach. Peppermint lozenges are generally safe for most adults and can be a natural alternative to over-the-counter medications.

However, they may not be suitable for individuals with certain medical conditions, such as gastroesophageal reflux disease, and should be used as directed.

9. Pimpinella anisum

Pimpinella anisum, commonly known as anise, is a traditional herb often used in herbal remedies for its soothing properties.

Anise-based herbal lozenges are formulated to alleviate symptoms of an upset stomach by promoting digestion and reducing bloating. These lozenges contain natural compounds like anethole, which have mild antispasmodic and carminative effects. They are particularly beneficial for individuals experiencing mild gastrointestinal discomfort or nausea.

As a natural alternative to conventional medications, anise lozenges offer a gentle and aromatic way to support digestive wellness.

10. Satureja hortensis

Satureja hortensis, commonly known as summer savory, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used to soothe digestive discomfort.

Herbal lozenges made from satureja hortensis are formulated to provide targeted relief for upset stomach symptoms such as bloating, nausea, and indigestion. These lozenges work by leveraging the plant's natural antispasmodic and carminative properties, which help ease gastrointestinal tension and reduce gas. The essential oils in satureja hortensis, including thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its effectiveness in calming the stomach lining and promoting digestion.

As a natural alternative to conventional medications, these lozenges offer a gentle and flavorful way to support digestive health.
