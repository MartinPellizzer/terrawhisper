1. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, is a herb traditionally used in herbal medicine for its high concentration of silica, which is believed to support hair health.

Herbal syrups made from Equisetum arvense are often prepared by infusing the dried plant parts in alcohol or water to extract its beneficial compounds. These syrups are used externally as rinses or internally to strengthen hair follicles and reduce hair fall. The silica content is thought to promote the structural integrity of hair strands, making them stronger and less prone to breakage.

While some studies suggest potential benefits, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using Equisetum arvense, as it may interact with certain medications or have side effects.
