10 Best Herbal Teas For Hair Fall

Herbal teas have gained popularity as natural remedies for hair fall due to their nourishing and soothing properties.

Ingredients like nettle, rosemary, and chamomile are commonly used in herbal teas for their ability to strengthen hair follicles and stimulate scalp health. These teas can be brewed and applied directly to the scalp or used as a rinse after shampooing to enhance hair growth. Regular use of herbal teas may help reduce breakage and promote a healthier scalp environment.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating new herbal treatments into your routine, especially if you have underlying health conditions.

1. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, is a herb that has been traditionally used in herbal teas to promote hair health and reduce hair fall.

This plant is rich in silica, which is essential for strengthening hair strands and improving their overall structure. When brewed into a tea, equisetum arvense may help stimulate scalp health and encourage new hair growth by enhancing circulation to the scalp. However, it is important to use this herb in moderation, as excessive consumption may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or other side effects.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating equisetum arvense into a hair care routine.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, is widely used in herbal teas to address hair fall due to its potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The active compound curcumin in turmeric helps reduce scalp inflammation and promotes a healthy environment for hair growth. When consumed as a herbal tea, curcuma longa can improve blood circulation to the scalp, enhancing nutrient delivery to hair follicles. Regular intake of turmeric tea may also help balance sebum production and prevent dandruff, which are common contributors to hair loss.

However, it is important to use it in moderation and consult a healthcare provider, especially for those with sensitive stomachs or existing health conditions.
