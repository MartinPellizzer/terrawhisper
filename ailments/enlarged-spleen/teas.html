10 Best Herbal Teas For Enlarged Spleen

Herbal teas have been traditionally used to support overall health, including the function of the spleen, which is part of the lymphatic system and plays a role in immune function.

Certain herbs, such as dandelion root, milk thistle, and turmeric, are believed to have detoxifying and anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce spleen size and improve its function. These teas can be consumed as part of a holistic approach to managing enlarged spleen, often in conjunction with medical advice. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, as they may interact with medications or have contraindications for certain conditions.

While herbal teas may offer supportive benefits, they should not replace conventional medical treatment for an enlarged spleen.

1. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a herbal plant traditionally used for its calming and mood-enhancing properties.

While it is often associated with treating mild depression and anxiety, some herbal practitioners suggest it may also support overall immune function and reduce inflammation. Although there is limited direct scientific evidence linking St. John's Wort to the reduction of an enlarged spleen, its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties may contribute to overall spleen health. It is typically prepared as a tea by steeping dried flowers in hot water, and it is often combined with other herbs for enhanced effects.

However, individuals with an enlarged spleen should consult a healthcare professional before using St. John's Wort, as it may interact with certain medications and affect blood clotting.
