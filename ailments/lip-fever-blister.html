What is fever blister on lip and how does it affect your body?

A fever blister, also known as a cold sore, is a small, fluid-filled lesion that typically appears on or around the lips, caused by the herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1).

These blisters are usually painful and can be accompanied by symptoms such as redness, itching, and a burning sensation. The virus remains dormant in the body and can reactivate due to factors like stress, illness, or a weakened immune system. When a fever blister erupts, it can lead to discomfort, difficulty eating or speaking, and may even spread the infection to other areas of the mouth or face.

Although generally not serious, fever blisters can be contagious and may require treatment with antiviral medications to reduce the duration and severity of the outbreak.
