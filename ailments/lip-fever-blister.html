Fever Blister On Lip Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Fever blister on lip, also known as cold sore or herpes simplex, is a painful and recurring outbreak of fluid-filled blisters on the lip or mouth area, caused by the herpes simplex virus.

Herbal remedies have been used for centuries to treat this ailment, offering natural and effective alternatives to conventional medications.

Some of the herbs commonly used to treat fever blisters include aloe vera, which soothes and calms the skin, calendula, which reduces inflammation and promotes healing, and peppermint, which cools and numbs the area.

Herbal preparations such as tea tree oil-infused lip balms, echinacea tinctures, and licorice root extracts have also been found to be effective in treating fever blisters, as they contain antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce the severity and duration of the outbreak.

What causes fever blister on lip?

The primary causes of fever blister on lip are the herpes simplex virus (HSV-1), which is a highly contagious virus that can be transmitted through skin-to-skin contact, sharing food or drinks, and other forms of direct contact.

Secondary causes include stress, fatigue, and certain medications, which can trigger the reactivation of the dormant virus.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing fever blisters on the lip include smoking, excessive sun exposure, and having a weakened immune system, as these factors can further trigger the virus's reactivation.

Additionally, a diet high in sugar and low in nutrients can also contribute to the development of fever blisters on the lip.
