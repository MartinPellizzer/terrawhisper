What is stye and how does it affect your body?

A stye, also known as a hordeolum, is a red, painful lump that develops on the edge of the eyelid due to a bacterial infection, most commonly caused by Staphylococcus aureus.

It occurs when the oil glands in the eyelid become blocked and infected, leading to inflammation and the formation of a small, pus-filled bump. Styes are typically located on the outer part of the eyelid and can cause discomfort, including a burning sensation, itching, or a feeling of something in the eye. While they are usually not serious, they can be quite uncomfortable and may last for several days to a couple of weeks.

In some cases, a stye can lead to swollen eyelids, blurred vision, or even spread to the inner eyelid if left untreated, though this is rare.

Underlying causes and risk factors of stye

A stye, or hordeolum, is primarily caused by a bacterial infection, most commonly from Staphylococcus aureus, which affects the oil glands in the eyelid.

The infection typically occurs when bacteria enter the gland through a small wound or scratch on the eyelid, often due to poor hygiene or touching the eyes with unclean hands. Individuals with weakened immune systems, such as those with diabetes or undergoing immunosuppressive therapy, are at higher risk of developing a stye. Additionally, conditions like blepharitis, which involve chronic inflammation of the eyelids, can predispose someone to stye formation.

Other risk factors include wearing contact lenses improperly, using expired or contaminated eye makeup, and not cleaning the eyelids regularly.

How to use herbal remedies safely for stye

Using herbal remedies for a stye should be approached with caution and proper knowledge to ensure safety.

First, consult a healthcare professional to confirm that the stye is indeed suitable for herbal treatment and to avoid any potential interactions with existing medications. Herbal options such as tea tree oil, calendula, and chamomile can be used topically, but they should always be diluted to prevent skin irritation. It is important to maintain good hygiene by washing hands before applying any herbal remedy and avoiding touching the affected eye.

If the stye worsens or persists, seek medical attention promptly, as some cases may require antibiotic treatment.
