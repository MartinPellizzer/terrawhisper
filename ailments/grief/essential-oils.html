10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Grief

Herbal essential oils can provide a natural and soothing way to support emotional healing during times of grief.

Oils such as lavender, frankincense, and chamomile are often used for their calming and grounding properties, helping to ease emotional distress and promote relaxation. These oils can be diffused, applied topically, or used in aromatherapy practices to create a comforting atmosphere that encourages reflection and emotional release. By engaging the senses, essential oils may help individuals process their grief more gently and find a sense of peace amidst sorrow.

Incorporating these oils into a self-care routine can be a meaningful complement to other therapeutic approaches in navigating the complex journey of grief.

1. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is widely recognized for its calming and soothing properties, making it a popular choice in aromatherapy for emotional healing.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have been shown to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, offering comfort during times of grief. When used in diffusers, massage, or inhalation, lavender essential oil can help ease the emotional weight of loss by fostering a sense of peace and emotional balance. Its gentle yet powerful aroma is often used in supportive care settings to assist individuals in navigating the complex emotions associated with bereavement.

By nurturing a sense of calm and clarity, lavender essential oil can serve as a natural and accessible tool in the journey toward emotional recovery after loss.

2. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a herbal plant widely recognized for its calming properties and use in essential oils.

The essential oil derived from valerian roots is particularly valued for its ability to promote relaxation and ease emotional distress, making it a potential aid for those experiencing grief. Its soothing aroma is believed to help reduce anxiety and foster a sense of emotional balance during periods of intense sorrow. When used in aromatherapy, valerian essential oil may support the nervous system and encourage a more peaceful state of mind.

However, it is often recommended to use valerian in combination with other calming essential oils for a more holistic approach to emotional healing.

3. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a gentle herb often used in aromatherapy to support emotional well-being, including during times of grief.

Its essential oil, extracted through steam distillation, possesses calming and uplifting properties that can help ease the emotional burden of loss. The soothing aroma of lemon balm essential oil is believed to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and encourage a sense of peace, which can be particularly beneficial for those navigating the complex emotions of grief. When used in diffusers, massage, or inhalation, this oil may help individuals process their feelings more gradually and find comfort in moments of sorrow.

While it is not a substitute for professional grief counseling, Melissa officinalis essential oil can be a supportive tool in nurturing emotional healing and resilience.

4. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is not typically associated with grief, but its essential oil may offer emotional support during times of loss.

The oil contains compounds like piperine, which may help uplift mood and promote mental clarity, potentially aiding in emotional healing. While not a substitute for professional counseling, the stimulating and warming properties of black pepper essential oil can help ease feelings of sorrow and stagnation. When used in aromatherapy, it may encourage a sense of grounding and resilience.

However, it is important to use it in moderation and consult with a qualified aromatherapist, especially when dealing with profound grief.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a versatile herb whose essential oil has been traditionally used to support emotional well-being, including during times of grief.

The oil is known for its uplifting and calming properties, helping to ease the emotional burden of loss by promoting mental clarity and reducing feelings of sadness. Its aromatic compounds may stimulate the central nervous system, aiding in the release of emotional tension and encouraging a sense of peace. When used in diffusers, massage, or inhalation, rosemary essential oil can create a soothing atmosphere that supports healing and resilience.

While it is not a substitute for professional counseling, rosemary essential oil can be a complementary tool in the journey of processing grief and finding emotional balance.

6. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum, commonly known as cinnamon bark, is a traditional herb widely used in aromatherapy for its soothing and uplifting properties.

The essential oil derived from its bark contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which are known for their calming and mood-enhancing effects. When used in diffusers or through inhalation, cinnamon essential oil can help alleviate the emotional weight of grief by promoting a sense of warmth and comfort. Its aromatic profile is believed to stimulate the limbic system, which plays a key role in emotional processing and memory.

As a natural remedy, cinnamon essential oil offers a gentle yet effective way to support emotional healing during times of loss.

7. Origanum vulgare

Oregano vulgare, commonly known as origanum vulgare, is a herb widely used in aromatherapy for its potent essential oils, which are believed to support emotional well-being.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which are known for their uplifting and calming properties. When used in diffusers or through topical application, origanum vulgare essential oil may help alleviate the emotional burdens associated with grief by promoting a sense of clarity and resilience. Its strong, warming aroma is thought to stimulate the mind and encourage emotional release, making it a valuable tool in the healing process during times of loss.

While it is not a substitute for professional grief counseling, origanum vulgare essential oil can be a comforting addition to holistic self-care practices.

8. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus, commonly known as lemon grass, produces a refreshing essential oil that is often used in aromatherapy to support emotional well-being.

This herb contains compounds like citral and myrcene, which are believed to have calming and uplifting properties. When used in diffusion or topical application, lemon grass essential oil may help alleviate symptoms of grief by promoting relaxation and reducing feelings of anxiety and depression. Its subtle citrusy aroma can also help clear mental fog and encourage a sense of clarity and hope during the grieving process.

As a natural remedy, cymbopogon citratus essential oil offers a gentle yet effective way to support emotional healing and balance during times of loss.

9. Pimpinella anisum

Pimpinella anisum, commonly known as anise, is a herb whose essential oil has been traditionally used for its calming and soothing properties.

The essential oil of anise is rich in anethol, a compound known for its ability to ease emotional distress and promote a sense of peace. In the context of grief, anise essential oil can help alleviate feelings of sadness and emotional heaviness by supporting the nervous system and encouraging emotional release. It is often used in aromatherapy to create a calming atmosphere, making it a valuable tool for those navigating the complex emotions of loss.

By fostering a sense of comfort and balance, anise essential oil may aid in the healing process during times of profound emotional sorrow.

10. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming and uplifting properties, making it a potential aid for those experiencing grief.

The essential oil derived from sage contains compounds like thujone and camphor, which may help ease emotional distress and promote mental clarity during times of sorrow. While not a substitute for professional counseling, sage essential oil can be used in aromatherapy to support emotional balance and reduce feelings of overwhelm. It is often diffused in a quiet space or applied topically (diluted) to encourage a sense of peace and resilience.

Incorporating sage essential oil into a self-care routine may offer gentle support as individuals navigate the complex emotions associated with grief.
