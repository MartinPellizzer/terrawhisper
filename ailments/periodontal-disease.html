Periodontal Disease Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Periodontal disease is a chronic bacterial infection that affects the gums and bone supporting the teeth, leading to inflammation, pain, and potentially even tooth loss if left untreated.

Herbal remedies have been used for centuries to treat periodontal disease due to their antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, making them a promising alternative to conventional treatments.

Some herbs that have shown effectiveness in treating periodontal disease include Echinacea, which boosts the immune system and fights off infection, Goldenseal, which has antimicrobial properties that reduce bacterial growth, and Aloe Vera, which has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe and calm the gums.

Herbal preparations such as tea infusions, tinctures, and extracts from herbs like Clove, Turmeric, and Ginger have also been used to treat periodontal disease, with Clove oil being particularly effective in reducing pain and inflammation, while Turmeric's curcumin has potent anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce gum inflammation.

What causes periodontal disease?

The primary causes of periodontal disease are bacterial plaque and tartar accumulation, which lead to inflammation and infection of the gums and jawbone.

Secondary causes include smoking, which decreases blood flow and increases the risk of infection, as well as diabetes, which can impair the immune system and make it harder for the body to fight off infection.

Additionally, a diet high in sugar and low in essential nutrients can contribute to the development of periodontal disease, while a sedentary lifestyle and poor oral hygiene practices can also increase the risk.

Furthermore, genetics, age, and certain medical conditions can also play a role in the development of periodontal disease.

What herbs heal periodontal disease?

The medicinal herbs used to treat periodontal disease are aloe vera, chamomile, and licorice root.

Aloe vera exhibits anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties due to the presence of bioactive compounds such as aloin and acemannan, which help to reduce inflammation and kill bacteria in the mouth.

Licorice root has therapeutic actions such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which help to reduce inflammation and prevent the progression of periodontal disease, thanks to the presence of bioactive compounds like glycyrrhizin.

Chamomile, on the other hand, has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties due to the presence of apigenin and luteolin, which help to soothe and calm the gums, reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

What preparations treat periodontal disease?

The herbal preparations used to treat periodontal disease are effective in reducing inflammation and promoting gum healing, with remedies like mouthwashes made from decoctions and infusions that exhibit antibacterial properties.

These preparations, such as toothpowders infused with extracts and teas that stimulate blood flow, also help to reduce plaque and prevent further damage.

Certain herbal infusions and glycerites, when used as a mouth rinse, have been shown to inhibit the growth of pathogenic bacteria that contribute to periodontal disease.

Additionally, some herbal teas and infusions have been found to have anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm the gums, promoting a healthy environment for healing.
