Benign Prostatic Hypertrophy Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Benign prostatic hypertrophy is a non-cancerous enlargement of the prostate gland, which can cause urinary problems and discomfort in men.

Herbal remedies have been used to treat this condition due to their ability to reduce inflammation, improve urinary flow, and alleviate symptoms.

Some of the herbs used to treat benign prostatic hypertrophy include saw palmetto, which blocks the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone, a hormone that contributes to prostate growth; nettle root, which helps to shrink the prostate gland; and pygeum, which has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and discomfort.

Herbal preparations such as extracts, teas, and capsules, made from these herbs, as well as others like stinging nettle, beta-sitosterol, and lycopene, have been shown to be effective in relieving symptoms and improving urinary function.

What causes benign prostatic hypertrophy?

The primary causes of benign prostatic hypertrophy are hormonal changes, specifically the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), and genetic predisposition.

Secondary causes include obesity, which contributes to inflammation and oxidative stress in the prostate, as well as certain medical conditions, such as hypertension and diabetes, which can damage the prostate's blood vessels and disrupt hormone regulation.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing benign prostatic hypertrophy include a diet high in processed meats and sugar, which can lead to chronic inflammation, and a sedentary lifestyle, which can contribute to obesity and other underlying health issues.

Additionally, aging and family history also play a significant role in the development of benign prostatic hypertrophy.

What herbs heal benign prostatic hypertrophy?

The medicinal herbs used to treat benign prostatic hypertrophy are Saw Palmetto, Urinary Extract of Saw Palmetto, Stinging Nettle, and Pygeum.

These herbs exert therapeutic actions such as inhibiting the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone, a hormone believed to contribute to the enlargement of the prostate, and reducing inflammation and oxidative stress in the prostate.

Bioactive compounds present in these herbs, including beta-sitosterol and lupeol in Saw Palmetto, and flavonoids and phenolic acids in Pygeum, have been shown to modulate the hormonal and metabolic pathways involved in the development of BPH.

Additionally, Stinging Nettle contains sterols and flavonoids that may help to relax the smooth muscle in the prostate and improve urine flow.

What preparations treat benign prostatic hypertrophy?

The herbal preparations used to treat benign prostatic hypertrophy are teas, infusions, and tinctures that have anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties, such as decoctions that can help reduce swelling and ease urination.

Infusions made from various plants can also help to relax the muscles in the prostate and improve urine flow, while herbal poultices applied topically can provide localized relief.

Some herbal preparations have antioxidant properties that may help to slow down the growth of the prostate gland, while others can help to reduce the risk of complications such as urinary tract infections.

Additionally, certain herbal preparations can help to reduce the symptoms of benign prostatic hypertrophy by improving the overall health of the prostate and surrounding tissues.
