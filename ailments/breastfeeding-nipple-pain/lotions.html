9. Common mallow (Symphytum officinale)

Symphytum officinale, commonly known as comfrey, is a traditional herbal plant that has been used for centuries to support healing and tissue repair.

These compounds can be harmful when absorbed through the skin, especially in individuals with compromised liver function or those who are nursing. Despite its traditional use for soothing soreness and promoting tissue regeneration, the safety profile of comfrey in breastfeeding mothers remains a concern.

As a result, healthcare providers generally advise against using comfrey-based herbal lotions on the breasts during lactation and suggest safer alternatives for managing nipple pain.
