10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Jaw Pain

Herbal essential oils, such as peppermint, eucalyptus, and lavender, are often used for their potential analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties to help alleviate jaw pain.

These oils can be applied topically, diluted with a carrier oil, to the affected area to reduce discomfort and promote relaxation of the jaw muscles. Peppermint oil, in particular, is known for its cooling effect, which may help ease tension and reduce inflammation in the jaw. Eucalyptus oil is also commonly used for its ability to soothe pain and improve circulation, making it a popular choice for those suffering from temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially if you have sensitive skin or are pregnant, to ensure safe and effective use.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, is a popular herbal plant whose essential oils have been traditionally used for their therapeutic properties.

The essential oil of ginger contains bioactive compounds such as gingerol and shogaol, which possess anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. These properties make zingiber officinale essential oil a potential natural remedy for jaw pain, often associated with conditions like temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ) or dental inflammation. When applied topically, the oil can help reduce swelling and soothe discomfort in the jaw area.

However, it is important to dilute the essential oil properly with a carrier oil before use to avoid skin irritation and ensure safe application.

2. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, contains essential oils that have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which may help alleviate jaw pain.

The essential oils derived from turmeric root include compounds like curcuminoids and volatile oils, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. These oils can be used topically or in aromatherapy to reduce inflammation and muscle tension in the jaw area. Some research suggests that the bioactive components in turmeric essential oils may inhibit pain pathways and reduce swelling, offering natural relief for conditions like temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using turmeric essential oils for jaw pain, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking medications.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, commonly known as the Australian eucalyptus, is a popular source of herbal essential oils that has been traditionally used for its therapeutic properties.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds like eucalyptol and cineole, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. When applied topically to the jaw area, eucalyptus globulus oil may help reduce inflammation and alleviate pain associated with jaw issues such as temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder. It is often diluted with a carrier oil to prevent skin irritation and is used in aromatherapy or as a topical remedy.

While it can provide relief, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it for persistent or severe jaw pain.

4. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, contains essential oils that have been explored for their potential therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

These essential oils, derived from the dried fruit of the plant, contain compounds like piperine, which may help reduce inflammation and pain in the jaw area. While there is limited scientific research specifically on its use for jaw pain, some anecdotal reports suggest that topical application of diluted black pepper essential oil may offer relief. It is important to use these oils with caution, as they can be irritating to the skin and should always be diluted before application.

As with any herbal remedy, consulting a healthcare professional is advisable before using piper nigrum essential oils for jaw pain.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a versatile herb whose essential oil has been widely used for its therapeutic properties.

The essential oil of rosemary contains compounds like 1,8-cineole and camphor, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, making it a valuable natural remedy for various types of pain, including jaw pain. When applied topically, rosemary essential oil can help reduce inflammation and muscle tension in the jaw area, providing relief from conditions such as temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders. To use it safely, the oil should be diluted with a carrier oil before application to prevent skin irritation.

Its aromatic properties also contribute to a calming effect, which can further aid in reducing stress-related jaw pain.

6. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis, also known as lemon balm, is a herbal plant whose essential oil has been traditionally used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

When applied topically, melissa essential oil may help alleviate jaw pain by reducing inflammation and muscle tension in the area. It is often used in aromatherapy or diluted with a carrier oil for massage to the jaw and neck region. The oil's mild sedative effects can also help ease stress-related jaw clenching and bruxism, which are common causes of discomfort.

While it is generally safe for most people, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it for persistent or severe jaw pain.

7. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is a popular herb used in aromatherapy for its calming and analgesic properties.

Its essential oil contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and relieve pain. When applied topically to the jaw area, lavender oil can help alleviate jaw pain caused by conditions such as temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ) or muscle tension. To use it safely, the oil should be diluted with a carrier oil to avoid skin irritation.

When applied topically to the jaw area, chamomile essential oil may help alleviate jaw pain associated with conditions like temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ) or bruxism. It is often diluted with a carrier oil before application to avoid skin irritation.

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which may offer relief for jaw pain.

The essential oils derived from yarrow, particularly those containing compounds like chamazulene and bisabolol, can help reduce inflammation and soothe pain in the jaw area. When applied topically, these oils may help alleviate symptoms associated with temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders or dental-related discomfort.
