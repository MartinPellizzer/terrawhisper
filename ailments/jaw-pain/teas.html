10 Best Herbal Teas For Jaw Pain

Herbal teas can be a natural and soothing remedy for jaw pain, often caused by conditions like temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ) or bruxism.

Teas containing ingredients such as chamomile, ginger, and lavender are known for their anti-inflammatory and muscle-relaxing properties, which can help reduce discomfort and inflammation in the jaw area. Chamomile tea, in particular, is widely used for its calming effects and ability to ease muscle tension. Drinking warm herbal tea can also promote relaxation, which may alleviate stress-related jaw pain.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, especially if the jaw pain persists or is severe.

1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential to alleviate jaw pain.

Herbal teas made from dried sage leaves are often consumed to reduce inflammation and soothe the muscles in the jaw area. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of sage may help address conditions such as temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ) or dental infections that contribute to jaw discomfort. To prepare the tea, steep a teaspoon of dried sage in hot water for several minutes before drinking.

While sage tea can be a natural remedy for jaw pain, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional for persistent or severe symptoms.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a popular herbal remedy often used for its immune-boosting properties.

While it is traditionally associated with colds and infections, some people use echinacea purpurea herbal teas to alleviate jaw pain, particularly when it is related to inflammation or dental issues. The anti-inflammatory and analgesic compounds in echinacea may help reduce swelling and discomfort in the jaw area. However, it is important to note that scientific evidence supporting its effectiveness for jaw pain is limited, and it should not replace professional medical advice or treatment.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before using echinacea purpurea, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.

3. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used to support hormonal balance and may offer relief for jaw pain associated with conditions like temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ) or hormonal imbalances.

The herb is believed to influence the pituitary gland, which can help regulate hormones that affect muscle tension and inflammation in the jaw area. While scientific evidence is limited, some studies suggest that chasteberry may reduce stress and anxiety, which are common contributors to jaw clenching and pain. Herbal teas made from vitex agnus-castus are often consumed as a calming remedy, and they may be combined with other herbs like ginger or valerian root to enhance their soothing effects.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using vitex agnus-castus, especially if you are pregnant, nursing, or taking other medications.

4. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to alleviate jaw pain, particularly when caused by tension or inflammation.

The cooling and soothing properties of peppermint help reduce muscle tension in the jaw, making it effective for conditions like temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ). Peppermint tea can be consumed warm or cold, offering a gentle and natural remedy that supports relaxation of the jaw muscles. Its essential oils have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which may help ease discomfort and promote healing.

Regular consumption of peppermint herbal tea may provide long-term relief for individuals experiencing chronic jaw pain.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herb that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties.

While it is not a direct treatment for jaw pain, some individuals use stinging nettle herbal teas to help reduce inflammation and muscle tension in the jaw area. The tea is believed to support overall oral health by promoting detoxification and reducing swelling. To prepare the tea, the dried leaves are steeped in hot water, and it can be consumed several times a day.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using stinging nettle tea, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking medications.

6. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, is a popular herbal tea used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

When consumed as a tea, it may help alleviate jaw pain by reducing inflammation and soothing the muscles in the jaw area. Its natural compounds, such as apigenin and bisabolol, have been shown to possess analgesic and antispasmodic effects. Some individuals find relief from temporomandibular joint (TMJ) discomfort by drinking chamomile tea regularly.

However, while it can be a helpful complementary remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional for persistent or severe jaw pain.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal teas for alleviating jaw pain.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, help reduce inflammation and ease muscle tension, which can be beneficial for conditions like temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder. Drinking ginger tea may help soothe the pain associated with dental issues, such as toothaches or gum inflammation, by promoting circulation and reducing swelling. To prepare the tea, fresh ginger root is typically sliced and steeped in hot water for several minutes, allowing the beneficial compounds to infuse into the liquid.

While ginger tea can be a natural remedy for jaw pain, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional for persistent or severe symptoms.

8. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When brewed into a herbal tea, licorice root may help alleviate jaw pain by reducing inflammation and easing muscle tension in the temporomandibular joint (TMJ). The active compounds in licorice, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, are believed to have mild analgesic and antispasmodic effects. However, it is important to note that licorice tea should be used with caution, as excessive consumption can lead to side effects like hypertension or electrolyte imbalances.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using licorice tea for jaw pain, especially if you have underlying health conditions.

9. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a herb widely used in herbal teas for its potential therapeutic benefits, including relief from jaw pain.

The essential oils in rosemary, such as rosmarinic acid and cineole, possess anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that may help reduce pain and swelling in the jaw area. Drinking rosemary tea can promote relaxation of the jaw muscles and improve blood circulation, which may alleviate tension-related discomfort. It is often recommended as a natural alternative to over-the-counter pain medications for those seeking a gentler approach to managing jaw pain.

However, individuals with sensitive stomachs or allergies should consult a healthcare professional before incorporating rosemary tea into their routine.

10. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to alleviate jaw pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The essential oils found in cinnamon, particularly cinnamaldehyde, help reduce inflammation and numb pain in the jaw area, making it a natural remedy for conditions like temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders. When brewed into a warm herbal tea, cinnamon can soothe the muscles around the jaw and promote relaxation, easing discomfort associated with bruxism or dental issues. It is often combined with other herbs like ginger or turmeric to enhance its pain-relieving effects.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using cinnamon tea for persistent jaw pain, especially if there are underlying health conditions or allergies.
