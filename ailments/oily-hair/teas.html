10 Best Herbal Teas For Oily Hair

Herbal teas can be a natural and effective way to manage oily hair by balancing sebum production and promoting scalp health.

Chamomile, nettle, and green tea are particularly beneficial due to their anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. These teas can be used as a rinse after shampooing to cleanse the scalp without stripping it of its natural oils. Regular use may help reduce excess oiliness and prevent dandruff.

However, it's important to ensure the tea is properly cooled before applying to avoid irritation.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is often used in herbal teas to support hair health, particularly for those with oily hair.

These teas are believed to help balance scalp oil production by soothing inflammation and reducing excess sebum. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of aloe can help calm an overactive scalp, promoting a healthier environment for hair growth. When consumed regularly, aloe-based herbal teas may improve overall scalp condition and reduce dandruff.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating aloe into your routine, especially if you have sensitive skin or existing health conditions.

2. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green tea is derived, is widely used in herbal teas for its numerous benefits for oily hair.

These teas contain antioxidants, caffeine, and polyphenols that help regulate sebum production, reducing excess oiliness on the scalp. Regular use of Camellia sinensis herbal teas can help balance the scalp's natural oils, promoting healthier hair growth and reducing dandruff. The anti-inflammatory properties of these teas also soothe the scalp and prevent irritation.

Incorporating Camellia sinensis into a hair care routine can be a natural and effective way to manage oily hair and maintain a clean, refreshed scalp.

3. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is a versatile herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its therapeutic properties, including its benefits for oily hair.

Sage herbal teas are often recommended for individuals with oily hair due to their ability to balance sebum production and soothe the scalp. The tea contains natural compounds like flavonoids and essential oils that help reduce excess oiliness and promote a healthier scalp environment. When used as a rinse or incorporated into a hair care routine, sage tea can help cleanse the scalp without stripping it of its natural moisture.

Regular use of salvia officinalis herbal tea can contribute to improved hair texture, reduced dandruff, and overall healthier-looking hair.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a popular herb used in herbal teas that can benefit individuals with oily hair.

The essential oils in rosemary have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help balance scalp health and reduce excess sebum production. When brewed into a tea and applied to the scalp, it can help regulate oiliness and promote a healthier hair environment. Regular use of rosemary herbal tea can also stimulate blood circulation in the scalp, encouraging stronger and healthier hair growth.

This natural remedy is a gentle alternative to commercial treatments, offering a calming and aromatic experience while supporting overall scalp and hair wellness.

5. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a powerful herb that has been traditionally used for its cleansing and purifying properties.

When brewed into a herbal tea, it can be used as a scalp rinse to help balance oil production in oily hair, as it contains natural astringents that help regulate sebum secretion. The tea is rich in minerals like silica, iron, and potassium, which support healthy hair growth and strengthen the scalp. To use it, simply steep fresh or dried nettle leaves in hot water for several minutes, then let it cool before applying to the scalp.

Regular use of urtica dioica herbal tea can help reduce excess oil, soothe irritation, and promote a healthier, more balanced scalp environment.

6. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, is a herb that has been traditionally used in herbal teas for its potential benefits for oily hair.

This plant is rich in silica, which can help strengthen hair strands and improve scalp health. When brewed into a tea, equisetum arvense may help regulate oil production and reduce excess sebum on the scalp. Its astringent properties can also help tighten pores and provide a refreshing cleanse for oily hair.

However, it is important to use it in moderation and consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating it into your hair care routine.

7. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree oil, is often used in herbal teas for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it beneficial for oily hair.

When infused into a tea, it can help regulate sebum production, reducing excess oiliness without stripping the scalp of its natural moisture. The gentle nature of tea tree oil makes it suitable for regular use, promoting a balanced scalp environment. This herbal tea can also help prevent dandruff and scalp infections, which are common in individuals with oily hair.

Overall, melaleuca alternifolia herbal tea offers a natural and effective way to manage oily hair and maintain a healthy scalp.

8. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a popular herbal tea used for its potential benefits for oily hair.

This herb contains essential oils and antioxidants that can help balance sebum production, reducing excess oiliness on the scalp. When brewed into a tea and applied to the hair and scalp, it can help soothe irritation and promote a healthier scalp environment. Regular use of lemon grass tea may also help reduce dandruff and improve overall hair texture.

However, it is important to perform a patch test before using it to ensure no allergic reactions occur.

9. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is a popular herb used in herbal teas for its calming and soothing properties.

When used in hair care, lavender tea can help balance oil production in oily hair by regulating sebum glands without stripping the scalp of its natural oils. The essential oils in lavender have antimicrobial properties that can reduce scalp irritation and dandruff, making it beneficial for those with sensitive or oily scalps. Regular use of lavender herbal tea as a rinse can also promote a refreshed, clean feel without over-drying the hair.

Overall, lavender tea offers a natural, gentle alternative for maintaining healthy, balanced hair without harsh chemicals.

10. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a herbal remedy often used in the form of tea for its potential benefits on oily hair.

This plant contains compounds like hypericin and flavonoids, which may help regulate sebum production, reducing excess oiliness on the scalp. When used as a hair rinse, hypericum perforatum tea can soothe the scalp and promote a balanced environment for healthy hair growth. However, it is important to note that it may interact with certain medications, so consulting a healthcare professional before use is advisable.

Despite its natural origins, it should be used with caution and in moderation to avoid potential side effects.
