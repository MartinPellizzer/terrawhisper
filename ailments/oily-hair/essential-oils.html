10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Oily Hair

Herbal essential oils are natural extracts derived from plants and are often used in hair care for their therapeutic properties.

For oily hair, essential oils like tea tree, peppermint, and lavender are particularly beneficial due to their ability to regulate sebum production and soothe the scalp. These oils can help reduce excess oil without stripping the hair of its natural moisture, making them ideal for maintaining a balanced scalp environment. When properly diluted with a carrier oil, they can be safely applied to the scalp and hair to promote healthier growth and reduce dandruff.

Incorporating herbal essential oils into a regular hair care routine can offer a natural and effective solution for managing oily hair concerns.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree oil, is a popular essential oil derived from the leaves of the Melaleuca alternifolia plant, native to Australia.

It is well-known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it beneficial for various skin and hair concerns. For individuals with oily hair, tea tree oil can help regulate sebum production and reduce scalp inflammation, which often contributes to excess oiliness. When diluted properly, it can be applied to the scalp to prevent dandruff and promote a balanced, healthy scalp environment.

However, it is important to use it in moderation and ensure it is properly diluted to avoid skin irritation.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a popular herb used in the production of essential oils that are particularly beneficial for oily hair.

The essential oil derived from rosemary leaves is known for its ability to regulate sebum production, helping to reduce excess oiliness without stripping the scalp of its natural moisture. Its antimicrobial properties can also help prevent dandruff and scalp infections, promoting a healthier environment for hair growth. When used in hair treatments or scalp massages, rosemary essential oil can enhance circulation, which may lead to stronger and healthier hair.

However, it is important to dilute the oil properly before applying it to the scalp to avoid irritation, especially for those with sensitive skin or oily hair types.

3. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is a popular herb used in the production of essential oils that are particularly beneficial for oily hair.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which have soothing and balancing properties that can help regulate sebum production in the scalp. When used in hair care products, lavender essential oil can help reduce excess oil without stripping the hair of its natural moisture. Its calming aroma also promotes a sense of relaxation, making it a pleasant addition to scalp treatments.

Overall, lavender essential oil is a natural and effective choice for those seeking to manage oily hair while maintaining a healthy scalp environment.

4. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum, commonly known as cinnamon bark, produces an essential oil that is increasingly used in natural hair care, particularly for those with oily hair.

This aromatic oil contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde and eugenol, which possess antimicrobial and astringent properties that help regulate sebum production and reduce scalp inflammation. When diluted properly, cinnamon essential oil can be applied to the scalp to balance oiliness without causing dryness or irritation. Its warming effect also promotes circulation, encouraging healthier hair growth.

However, due to its potency, it should always be diluted with a carrier oil before use to ensure safety and effectiveness.

5. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, also known as Tasmanian eucalyptus, is a popular herbal essential oil widely used in natural hair care for its refreshing and purifying properties.

This essential oil is particularly beneficial for individuals with oily hair, as it helps to regulate sebum production and reduce excess oiliness without stripping the scalp of its natural moisture. Its strong, camphor-like aroma also promotes a sense of invigoration and can help soothe an itchy or inflamed scalp. When diluted properly, eucalyptus globulus essential oil can be safely incorporated into shampoos, scalp treatments, or leave-in conditioners to maintain a balanced scalp environment.

Its antibacterial and antifungal properties further support scalp health, making it a versatile and effective ingredient for managing oily hair.

6. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a popular herb used in the production of essential oils that are beneficial for oily hair.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds like citral and myrcene, which have antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help control excess sebum production. When used in hair care products, lemon grass essential oil can help balance oiliness and prevent scalp infections. It is often diluted with a carrier oil and applied to the scalp to promote a healthier, more balanced scalp environment.

Regular use of lemon grass essential oil can lead to reduced oiliness, improved scalp health, and shinier, more manageable hair.

7. Pogostemon cablin

Pogostemon cablin, also known as Java citronella, is a plant commonly used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine, and its essential oil is gaining attention for its potential benefits for oily hair.

This essential oil is known for its strong, refreshing aroma and its ability to regulate sebum production, which can help reduce excess oiliness on the scalp. When used in hair care products, Pogostemon cablin essential oil may help balance scalp health and promote a fresher, more manageable hair texture. It is often diluted with a carrier oil before application to avoid irritation, making it a safe option for those with sensitive scalps.

Overall, this essential oil offers a natural alternative for managing oily hair while providing a pleasant sensory experience.

8. Santalum album

Santalum album, also known as sandalwood, is a popular herbal essential oil that is often used in hair care for its soothing and balancing properties.

This oil is particularly beneficial for individuals with oily hair, as it helps regulate sebum production without stripping the scalp of its natural oils. Its mild, woody aroma provides a calming effect, making it a great addition to scalp massages and hair treatments. When diluted properly, Santalum album essential oil can help reduce scalp irritation and promote a healthier hair environment.

Overall, it is a versatile and natural option for those seeking to manage oily hair while maintaining a balanced and nourished scalp.

9. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a powerful herbal essential oil that is particularly beneficial for individuals with oily hair.

Its cooling and invigorating properties help to stimulate blood circulation in the scalp, promoting healthier hair growth and reducing excess sebum production. Peppermint essential oil also has natural antibacterial and antifungal qualities that can help combat scalp infections and dandruff, which are often associated with oily hair. When diluted properly, it can be applied directly to the scalp or incorporated into shampoos and conditioners for a refreshing and cleansing effect.

Overall, mentha piperita essential oil is a valuable natural ingredient for maintaining balanced oil production and keeping hair clean and fresh.

10. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris, commonly known as thyme, is a popular herb used in aromatherapy and natural remedies, including its essential oil, which is often recommended for oily hair due to its antibacterial and antifungal properties.

The essential oil of thyme contains compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which help to regulate sebum production and balance the scalp's natural oils. When used in diluted form, thyme essential oil can help reduce excess oiliness and prevent scalp infections that may contribute to oily hair. It is often combined with other essential oils such as lavender or tea tree oil to enhance its effectiveness for scalp health.

However, it is important to use thyme essential oil cautiously, as it can be quite strong and may cause irritation if not properly diluted.
