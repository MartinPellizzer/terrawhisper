10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Oily Hair

Herbal essential oils are natural extracts derived from plants and are often used in hair care for their therapeutic properties.

For individuals with oily hair, essential oils like tea tree, peppermint, and lavender can help regulate sebum production and balance scalp health. These oils have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce scalp irritation and dandruff. When diluted properly with a carrier oil, they can be safely applied to the scalp to promote a healthier, more balanced scalp environment.

Using herbal essential oils in hair care routines can provide a natural, effective alternative to chemical-based treatments for oily hair.

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree oil, is a popular essential oil derived from the leaves of the Melaleuca alternifolia plant, native to Australia.

It is widely recognized for its antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a valuable ingredient in natural remedies for oily hair. When used in hair care products, tea tree oil can help regulate sebum production, reducing excess oiliness and preventing scalp infections. Its refreshing scent also makes it a pleasant addition to shampoos and scalp treatments.

However, due to its potency, it should always be diluted with a carrier oil before applying it directly to the scalp or hair to avoid irritation.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, produces an essential oil that is highly valued for its calming and therapeutic properties.

When used for oily hair, this essential oil helps regulate sebum production, reducing excess oiliness and preventing scalp irritation. Its antifungal and antibacterial properties make it effective in combating dandruff and scalp infections. The oil also has a soothing effect on the scalp, promoting a balanced environment for healthy hair growth.

To use it safely, it should always be diluted with a carrier oil before applying to the hair and scalp.

3. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, commonly known as blue gum eucalyptus, is a popular herbal essential oil often used in natural hair care routines for oily hair.

Its refreshing, camphor-like scent and strong antibacterial properties make it effective in regulating sebum production and preventing scalp infections. When diluted properly, it can help balance oiliness without stripping the hair of its natural moisture. It is particularly beneficial for those with dandruff or scalp issues that accompany oily hair.

This essential oil can be incorporated into scalp massages, shampoos, or leave-in treatments for a revitalizing and cleansing effect.

4. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum, commonly known as cinnamon, is a popular herbal plant used in essential oils for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

When used in moderation, cinnamon essential oil can help reduce excess oiliness and promote a healthier scalp environment.
