10 Best Herbal Lozenges For Heartburn

Herbal lozenges are natural remedies that can help alleviate symptoms of heartburn by soothing the esophagus and reducing excess stomach acid.

These lozenges often contain ingredients like ginger, licorice root, and slippery elm, which have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. They work by forming a protective barrier over the lining of the esophagus and may also help neutralize stomach acid. Unlike over-the-counter antacids, herbal lozenges are generally considered safe for long-term use and can be a good alternative for those seeking natural relief.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using them, especially if heartburn persists or is accompanied by other symptoms.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been widely used for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ginger herbal lozenges are a natural remedy that can help alleviate symptoms of heartburn by soothing the esophageal lining and reducing stomach acid reflux. The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, work to relax the lower esophageal sphincter and promote digestion, thereby minimizing discomfort. These lozenges are particularly beneficial for individuals seeking non-pharmaceutical relief from occasional heartburn.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional if heartburn persists or worsens, as it may indicate a more serious underlying condition.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, is often used in herbal lozenges to alleviate symptoms of heartburn due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

These lozenges work by coating the esophagus and stomach lining, reducing irritation and providing temporary relief from acid reflux. The active compound, glycyrrhizin, helps to neutralize stomach acid and strengthen the lower esophageal sphincter, preventing acid from rising into the esophagus. However, long-term use of licorice root lozenges may lead to side effects such as hypertension and fluid retention due to its impact on the body's salt and water balance.

As a natural remedy, glycyrrhiza glabra lozenges can be a useful option for mild heartburn when used in moderation and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

3. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a herbal remedy often used to soothe digestive discomfort, including heartburn.

Chamomile lozenges are formulated to provide a calming effect on the esophagus and stomach lining, helping to reduce inflammation and irritation. These lozenges are typically made from dried chamomile flowers and are free from common irritants, making them suitable for individuals with sensitive digestive systems. The anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties of chamomile may help alleviate the burning sensation and discomfort associated with heartburn.

While they are not a substitute for medical treatment, chamomile lozenges can serve as a natural, gentle option for managing mild heartburn symptoms.

4. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, has been traditionally used for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal lozenges for heartburn relief.

These lozenges work by soothing the esophageal lining and reducing the production of excess stomach acid, which often contributes to heartburn symptoms. The essential oils in fennel, such as anethole, have natural antispasmodic effects that can help ease the discomfort caused by acid reflux. When used regularly, fennel lozenges may provide a natural and gentle alternative to conventional heartburn medications.

However, individuals with allergies to plants in the Apiaceae family should exercise caution and consult a healthcare provider before use.

5. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, has been traditionally used for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for heartburn.

When formulated into herbal lozenges, cumin may help soothe the esophageal lining and reduce the frequency of acid reflux by promoting better digestion and reducing stomach acidity. These lozenges are often preferred for their mild flavor and ease of use, offering a convenient alternative to over-the-counter medications. While they may not replace prescription treatments for severe heartburn, they can serve as a complementary therapy for mild to moderate symptoms.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using cumin lozenges, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking other medications.

6. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chaste tree, has been traditionally used to support hormonal balance and digestive health.

Herbal lozenges containing vitex agnus-castus may help alleviate heartburn by reducing excess stomach acid and promoting healthy digestion. These lozenges are often formulated with other soothing herbs like licorice root or ginger to enhance their efficacy. They are a natural alternative for individuals seeking to manage heartburn without relying on synthetic medications.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using vitex-based products, especially if you have existing medical conditions or are taking other medications.

7. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used for its potential liver-protective properties.

While it is not primarily marketed for heartburn relief, some individuals may use silybum marianum herbal lozenges to soothe gastrointestinal discomfort, possibly due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These lozenges are often made with a combination of milk thistle extract and other soothing ingredients like licorice root or ginger, which may help reduce irritation in the throat and esophagus. However, it is important to note that there is limited scientific evidence specifically supporting the use of silybum marianum for heartburn, and individuals should consult with a healthcare provider before using it as a treatment.

As with any herbal supplement, potential interactions with medications or adverse effects should be considered.

8. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential digestive benefits.

When formulated into herbal lozenges, piper nigrum may help alleviate symptoms of heartburn by promoting gastric motility and reducing the reflux of stomach acid into the esophagus. The active compound, piperine, is believed to enhance the production of saliva and digestive enzymes, which can neutralize acid and soothe irritation. These lozenges offer a natural alternative for individuals seeking relief without conventional antacids.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using them, especially for those with chronic heartburn or underlying medical conditions.

9. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is often used in herbal lozenges to alleviate symptoms of heartburn due to its soothing and antispasmodic properties.

These lozenges work by calming the digestive tract and reducing the sensation of burning in the chest and throat. The menthol in peppermint can help relax the lower esophageal sphincter, which may prevent acid reflux. While they are generally considered safe for most adults, they may not be suitable for individuals with certain medical conditions or those taking specific medications.

As a natural remedy, peppermint lozenges can offer temporary relief from heartburn without the side effects often associated with over-the-counter antacids.

10. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, has been traditionally used for its soothing properties, and cinnamon herbal lozenges are often recommended for alleviating heartburn symptoms.

These lozenges work by coating the throat and esophagus, providing a protective barrier against stomach acid reflux. The natural compounds in cinnamon, such as cinnamaldehyde, may help reduce inflammation and neutralize acid, offering temporary relief from discomfort. While they are not a substitute for medical treatment, they can be a useful complementary remedy for mild heartburn.

However, individuals with allergies to cinnamon or those on certain medications should consult a healthcare provider before use.
