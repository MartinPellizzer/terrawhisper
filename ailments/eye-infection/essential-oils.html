9 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Eye Infection

Herbal essential oils for Eye infection are highly concentrated plant extracts used to treat and soothe eye infections, which are characterized by inflammation, redness, and discharge in the eyes.

These oils offer numerous benefits, including reducing inflammation, fighting bacterial and fungal infections, and promoting healing.

Some examples of herbal essential oils used to treat eye infections include tea tree oil, known for its antimicrobial properties; lavender oil, which helps reduce inflammation and promotes relaxation; chamomile oil, used to soothe and calm the eyes; and eucalyptus oil, which helps reduce swelling and congestion.

Additionally, other oils such as frankincense oil, geranium oil, and calendula oil are also used to treat eye infections due to their antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties.

Related Study

According to "BMC complementary and alternative medicine", essential oils for eye infection, specifically thyme red oil, pine oil, and their components carvacrol, thymol, and α-pinene, have shown good activity against Candida albicans, which can cause eye infections, including fluconazole/voriconazole resistant strains.

1. Aloe barbadensis essential oils

Aloe barbadensis essential oils have been traditionally used to treat eye infections, including conjunctivitis and blepharitis, due to their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties.

The bioactive constituents of Aloe barbadensis essential oils, such as aloin, aloe-emodin, and aloe-β-barberene, help to reduce inflammation, prevent the growth of bacteria and fungi, and promote wound healing, thereby alleviating symptoms of eye infections.

The benefits of using Aloe barbadensis essential oils to treat eye infections include reduced redness, swelling, and discharge, as well as accelerated recovery and reduced risk of complications.

Regular application of Aloe barbadensis essential oils can also help to soothe and calm the eyes, promoting a healthy and balanced ocular environment.

Related Study

According to "Journal of parasitic diseases : official organ of the Indian Society for Parasitology", Aloe barbadensis essential oils, also known as Aloe vera ethanol extract, have shown a potent cysticidal activity against Acanthamoeba cysts, which can cause eye infections, with inhibitory percentages of 82.3%, 92.9%, and 97.9% respectively after 72 hours at concentrations of 200, 400, and 600 µg/ml.

Related Study
