10 Best Herbal Teas For Queasy Stomach

Herbal teas are a natural and soothing remedy for a queasy stomach, offering gentle relief without the side effects of medications.

Chamomile tea is particularly effective due to its calming properties and ability to reduce stomach irritation. Peppermint tea can help ease digestive discomfort by relaxing the muscles in the gastrointestinal tract. Ginger tea is another popular choice, as it has been traditionally used to alleviate nausea and settle the stomach.

These herbal teas are generally safe for most people, though it's advisable to consult a healthcare provider if symptoms persist or worsen.

1. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal tea used for its calming and digestive benefits.

Chamomile tea is often recommended for individuals experiencing a queasy stomach due to its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oils in chamomile, such as bisabolol and alpha-bisabolol, help soothe the gastrointestinal tract and reduce nausea. To prepare the tea, simply steep a teaspoon of dried chamomile flowers in hot water for several minutes.

Regular consumption of chamomile tea can provide natural relief from stomach discomfort and promote overall digestive wellness.

2. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, is a herb widely used in herbal teas to soothe a queasy stomach.

Its essential oils, particularly anethol, have carminative and antispasmodic properties that help relieve digestive discomfort and reduce nausea. Fennel tea is often recommended for its ability to ease bloating, gas, and indigestion, making it a popular remedy for upset stomachs. To prepare the tea, simply steep a teaspoon of dried fennel seeds in hot water for several minutes.

Regular consumption of fennel tea can also support overall digestive health and may help alleviate symptoms of morning sickness or motion sickness.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to soothe a queasy stomach due to its anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties.

The herb contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, which can help reduce irritation in the digestive tract and alleviate nausea. Licorice root tea is often prepared by steeping the dried root in hot water, resulting in a mild and slightly sweet flavored beverage. It is particularly beneficial for individuals experiencing heartburn, indigestion, or morning sickness.

However, long-term use should be cautious due to potential side effects such as increased blood pressure, and it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before regular consumption.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has long been used in herbal teas to alleviate symptoms of a queasy stomach.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, possess anti-inflammatory and antiemetic properties that help soothe nausea and reduce stomach discomfort. Drinking ginger tea can stimulate digestion and ease the feeling of an unsettled stomach, making it a popular natural remedy. To prepare the tea, fresh or dried ginger root is typically boiled in water and then strained, allowing the beneficial compounds to be absorbed.

Many people find that consuming ginger herbal tea before meals or during episodes of nausea provides relief without the need for pharmaceutical interventions.

5. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green and black teas are derived, contains compounds such as caffeine and polyphenols that may help soothe a queasy stomach.

While caffeine can sometimes exacerbate nausea, the polyphenols in Camellia sinensis may aid in digestion and reduce gastrointestinal irritation. Some studies suggest that herbal teas made from Camellia sinensis, when consumed in moderation, can help alleviate symptoms of indigestion and mild nausea. However, it is important to note that individual reactions to tea can vary, and some people may find that the bitterness or caffeine content of these teas worsens their discomfort.

For those experiencing persistent nausea, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before relying on Camellia sinensis-based teas as a remedy.

6. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is often used in herbal teas to soothe a queasy stomach due to its carminative and digestive properties.

The essential oils in cumin, such as limonene and cineole, help reduce bloating and ease gastrointestinal discomfort. When brewed into a warm tea, cumin can help calm the digestive system and relieve symptoms like nausea and indigestion. It is typically combined with other calming herbs like ginger or peppermint to enhance its soothing effects.

Regular consumption of cumin tea may support overall digestive health and provide natural relief for mild stomach upset.

7. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to soothe a queasy stomach.

The essential oils in peppermint, particularly menthol, help relax the muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, reducing spasms and easing discomfort. Peppermint tea is often recommended for its calming effect on the digestive system, making it a natural remedy for nausea and indigestion. It can be consumed as a warm infusion, typically made by steeping fresh or dried leaves in hot water.

Regular consumption of peppermint tea may provide relief from digestive issues, though it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional for persistent or severe symptoms.

8. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is often used in herbal teas to help soothe a queasy stomach due to its warming properties and potential digestive benefits.

The essential oils in black pepper, such as piperine, may help stimulate digestion and reduce nausea by improving gastrointestinal motility. When brewed into a tea, black pepper can be combined with other calming herbs like ginger or fennel to enhance its effectiveness for stomach discomfort. However, it is important to use it in moderation, as excessive consumption may irritate the stomach lining.

Overall, piper nigrum herbal tea can be a gentle and natural remedy for mild digestive upset when prepared and consumed appropriately.

9. Cymbopogon citratus

Cymbopogon citratus, commonly known as lemongrass, is a popular herbal tea used to soothe a queasy stomach due to its calming and digestive properties.

This fragrant herb contains compounds like citral and myrcene, which have been shown to reduce nausea and promote digestion. When brewed into a warm tea, lemongrass can help ease stomach discomfort and alleviate symptoms of indigestion or motion sickness. Its mild, citrusy flavor makes it an appealing option for those seeking natural relief without strong medicinal tastes.

Regular consumption of lemongrass tea may also support overall gastrointestinal health and reduce stress-related digestive issues.

10. Rosa canina

Rosa canina, also known as rosehip, is a traditional herbal remedy often used to support digestive health.

Its herbal teas are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and anti-inflammatory compounds, which can help soothe an upset stomach. The mild, slightly tart flavor of rosehip tea makes it a gentle option for those experiencing nausea or digestive discomfort. It is commonly recommended for its ability to ease symptoms of indigestion and bloating.

Regular consumption of rosa canina tea may promote overall digestive wellness and provide natural relief for a queasy stomach.
