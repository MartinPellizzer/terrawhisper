9 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Fatigue

Herbal essential oils for fatigue are plant-based extracts that have been traditionally used to alleviate and manage the symptoms of chronic fatigue syndrome, a condition characterized by persistent and profound tiredness.

These oils are highly beneficial in treating fatigue due to their ability to promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and improve sleep quality.

Some of the most effective herbal essential oils for fatigue include lavender oil, which helps regulate sleep patterns, frankincense oil, which reduces inflammation and promotes relaxation, and peppermint oil, which boosts energy and mental clarity.

Additionally, other beneficial oils for fatigue include bergamot oil, which reduces stress and anxiety, chamomile oil, which promotes relaxation and calms the mind, and rosemary oil, which improves mental focus and memory.

Related Study

According to "Complementary therapies in clinical practice", essential oils for fatigue can be effective, as aromatherapy with lavender essential oil was found to decrease the level of fatigue in patients undergoing hemodialysis compared to Benson relaxation techniques.

1. Valeriana officinalis essential oils

Valeriana officinalis essential oils, derived from the roots of the valerian plant, have been traditionally used to treat fatigue and promote relaxation.

The sedative and anxiolytic properties of this herbal preparation help to reduce stress and anxiety, which are often contributing factors to fatigue.

Valeriana officinalis essential oils contain bioactive constituents such as valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates, which are responsible for its sedative and calming effects.

The benefits of using Valeriana officinalis essential oils to treat fatigue include improved sleep quality, reduced stress levels, and enhanced overall well-being.

Related Study

According to "The Journal of Pharmacy and Pharmacology", Valeriana officinalis essential oils for fatigue may be beneficial due to their ability to inhibit the breakdown of GABA in the brain, resulting in sedation and potentially reducing fatigue.
