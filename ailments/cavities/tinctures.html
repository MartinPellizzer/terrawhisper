1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and healing properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal tinctures for addressing oral health issues such as cavities.

These tinctures typically combine sage with alcohol or other natural solvents to extract its active compounds, including essential oils and flavonoids, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria like Streptococcus mutans, a primary contributor to dental decay. When applied directly to affected areas, sage tinctures may help reduce plaque buildup and promote the remineralization of tooth enamel. However, it is important to consult with a dentist or healthcare provider before using these tinctures, as they are not a substitute for professional dental care.

Despite their natural benefits, the effectiveness of sage tinctures for cavities can vary, and consistent use alongside good oral hygiene practices is recommended for optimal results.
