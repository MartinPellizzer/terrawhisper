What is reddened corners in mouth and how does it affect your body?

Reddened corners of the mouth, also known as angular cheilitis, are a common condition characterized by inflammation, cracking, and sometimes bleeding at the corners of the lips.

This condition is often caused by a combination of factors such as saliva accumulation, lip licking, nutritional deficiencies, or fungal or bacterial infections. The affected areas may become sore, which can lead to discomfort during eating, speaking, or even smiling. While usually not a serious health issue, persistent reddened corners can indicate underlying nutritional deficiencies like iron, B vitamins, or zinc.

Proper treatment involves addressing the underlying cause, maintaining lip hygiene, and using topical treatments to promote healing and prevent recurrence.

Underlying causes and risk factors of reddened corners in mouth

Reddened corners of the mouth, also known as angular cheilitis, are often caused by a combination of factors that lead to moisture loss and irritation at the corners of the lips.

Common underlying causes include fungal or bacterial infections, such as Candida albicans or Staphylococcus aureus, which thrive in damp environments. Risk factors include lip-licking, which keeps the skin moist and creates a favorable environment for pathogens, as well as nutritional deficiencies like a lack of iron, B vitamins, or zinc. Poor oral hygiene and conditions like eczema or psoriasis can also contribute by causing dryness and inflammation.

Additionally, individuals who wear ill-fitting dentures or have a habit of chewing on the corners of their lips are at higher risk for developing this condition.

How to use herbal remedies safely for reddened corners in mouth

To use herbal remedies safely for reddened corners of the mouth, first identify the cause, such as dryness, irritation, or infection, and consult a healthcare provider if symptoms persist.

Choose gentle, antiseptic herbs like tea tree oil, calendula, or chamomile, which have soothing and anti-inflammatory properties. Dilute essential oils properly with a carrier oil or a saline solution to avoid skin irritation. Apply the remedy using a clean applicator, such as a cotton swab, and avoid direct contact with open wounds.

Maintain good oral hygiene and stay hydrated to promote healing and prevent further irritation.
