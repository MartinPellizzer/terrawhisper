11 Best Herbal Decoctions For Migraine

Herbal decoctions for Migraine are a type of traditional remedy made from various herbs that are steeped in hot water to create a soothing and potentially pain-relieving drink.

These herbal decoctions have been used for centuries to treat migraines due to their anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antioxidant properties, which help to alleviate symptoms such as pain, nausea, and sensitivity to light.

Examples of herbal decoctions used to treat migraines include Feverfew, which contains parthenolide that reduces inflammation and eases pain; Ginger, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce pain and nausea; Peppermint, which has a cooling effect that helps to relax tense muscles; Willow Bark, which contains salicin, a natural pain reliever similar to aspirin; and Lavender, which has a calming effect that helps to reduce stress and anxiety.

By incorporating these herbal decoctions into one's treatment plan, individuals may experience relief from migraine symptoms and a reduction in the frequency and severity of attacks.

According to "Phytotherapy research : PTR", decoctions for migraine using ingredients such as feverfew, butterbur, and curcumin may have potential as a treatment option, with feverfew showing mixed results and butterbur and curcumin showing positive, albeit limited evidence.
