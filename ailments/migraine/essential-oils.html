7 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Migraine

Herbal essential oils for Migraine are a natural and holistic approach to relieving the symptoms of this debilitating condition.

These oils are extracted from plants and possess potent bioactive compounds that can help alleviate migraine pain, inflammation, and nausea.

Some of the most effective herbal essential oils for Migraine include Lavender oil, which promotes relaxation and reduces stress; Peppermint oil, which cools and soothes the skin; Eucalyptus oil, which reduces inflammation and congestion; Geranium oil, which balances hormones and alleviates emotional triggers; Frankincense oil, which reduces pain and inflammation; and Rosemary oil, which improves circulation and reduces muscle tension.

By inhaling or applying these oils topically, individuals can experience relief from migraine symptoms and enjoy a more comfortable and balanced life.

Related Study

According to "Phytotherapy research : PTR", essential oils for migraine, including peppermint oil, showed positive, preliminary findings as an acute treatment, suggesting potential as an option to enhance the treatment of migraine.

1. Lavandula angustifolia essential oils

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils have been traditionally used to treat migraines due to their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

The herbal preparation helps to alleviate migraine symptoms by reducing muscle tension, calming the mind, and promoting relaxation.

The bioactive constituents of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils, including linalool and linalyl acetate, have been shown to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help to relieve migraine pain.

Regular use of Lavandula angustifolia essential oils has been reported to provide benefits in reducing the frequency and severity of migraines, promoting better sleep, and improving overall well-being.

2. Melissa officinalis essential oils

Melissa officinalis essential oils, derived from the leaves of the lemon balm plant, have been traditionally used to treat migraine ailments due to its calming and soothing properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat migraine by reducing anxiety and stress, which are common triggers for migraines, as well as by easing pain and inflammation.

The bioactive constituents of Melissa officinalis essential oils, including linalool and limonene, have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The benefits of using Melissa officinalis essential oils to treat migraine include reduced frequency and severity of attacks, improved sleep quality, and enhanced overall well-being.

Related Study

According to "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Melissa officinalis essential oils may have potential anti-nociceptive properties that could help alleviate migraine pain by modulating opioidergic and serotonergic pathways.

3. Cymbopogon citratus essential oils

Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, also known as lemongrass oil, have been traditionally used to treat migraine due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties.

The bioactive constituents, including citral, geraniol, and limonene, help to relieve migraine symptoms by reducing muscle spasms, inflammation, and pain.

The benefits of using lemongrass oil for migraine treatment include its ability to relax the mind and body, reduce stress and anxiety, and promote a good night's sleep, which are all essential for managing migraine episodes.

By incorporating lemongrass oil into a wellness routine, individuals can experience natural relief from migraine symptoms and improve their overall quality of life.

Related Study

According to "International journal of preventive medicine", Cymbopogon citratus essential oils, also known as peppermint essential oil, showed a considerable reduction in the intensity and frequency of headache and relieved the majority of patients' pain similar to lidocaine in treating migraine.

4. Zingiber officinale essential oils

Zingiber officinale essential oils, also known as ginger essential oil, have been traditionally used to treat migraine headaches due to their analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The bioactive constituents of ginger essential oil, including gingerols and shogaols, help to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and neurotransmitters, thereby reducing pain and inflammation associated with migraines.

By reducing inflammation and relieving pain, ginger essential oil helps to alleviate migraine symptoms, including nausea and sensitivity to light and sound.

The benefits of using ginger essential oil to treat migraines include its natural and non-invasive approach, making it a popular alternative to conventional pain management medications.

Related Study

According to "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Zingiber officinale essential oils for migraine are proposed to exert abortive and prophylactic effects without any side-effects.

5. Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils

Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils have been traditionally used to treat migraine due to their analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

The bioactive constituents such as camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate present in these oils help to ease the pain and reduce inflammation associated with migraines.

The essential oils from Rosmarinus officinalis may also help to improve blood circulation, which can alleviate migraine symptoms.

The benefits of using Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils for treating migraines include reduced frequency and severity of attacks, as well as improved overall well-being.

6. Ginkgo biloba essential oils

Ginkgo biloba essential oils have been traditionally used to treat migraine ailments due to their anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and vasodilatory properties, which help to relax blood vessels and reduce pain.

The bioactive constituents of Ginkgo biloba, including flavonoids and terpenoids, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators, thereby reducing migraine severity.

By improving blood circulation and reducing oxidative stress, Ginkgo biloba essential oils help to alleviate migraine symptoms, including headache, nausea, and sensitivity to light and sound.

The benefits of using Ginkgo biloba essential oils to treat migraines include natural relief from symptoms, reduced risk of medication overuse, and improved overall well-being.

7. Eucalyptus globulus essential oils

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils have been found to be effective in treating migraine due to its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and calming properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat migraine by reducing pain and inflammation, relaxing muscles, and improving mood.

The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus globulus essential oils, such as eucalyptol, limonene, and alpha-pinene, have been shown to have a positive impact on migraine symptoms by inhibiting pain pathways and reducing oxidative stress.

The benefits of using Eucalyptus globulus essential oils to treat migraine include natural pain relief, reduced frequency and severity of attacks, and improved overall well-being.
