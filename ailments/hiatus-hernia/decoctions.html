1. Cumin (Cuminum cyminum)

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its digestive benefits, including potential relief for hiatus hernia symptoms.

Cumin seed decoctions are prepared by simmering the seeds in water to extract their essential oils and active compounds, such as cuminaldehyde and thymol, which may help reduce gastric acidity and relax the lower esophageal sphincter. These properties may alleviate heartburn and discomfort associated with hiatus hernia by improving digestion and reducing reflux. However, while some studies suggest cumin may support digestive health, more clinical research is needed to confirm its efficacy for specific conditions like hiatus hernia.
