The natural compounds in the juice, including flavonoids and antioxidants, may help improve circulation and reduce swelling associated with fluid retention. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using stinging nettle juice, especially for individuals with kidney conditions or those on medication.

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a herbal remedy often used in the form of juice to support liver health and potentially reduce fluid retention.

The active compound in milk thistle, silymarin, is believed to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that may help the body eliminate excess fluids more efficiently. Some studies suggest that silymarin can improve the function of the liver, which plays a key role in regulating fluid balance in the body. While it is not a direct diuretic, milk thistle juice may aid in reducing edema by supporting overall liver detoxification processes.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using milk thistle juice, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking medications.
