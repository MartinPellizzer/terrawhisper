10 Best Herbal Syrups For Strep Throat

Herbal syrups for strep throat are natural remedies that often contain ingredients like echinacea, licorice root, and ginger, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These syrups can help soothe sore throats and reduce inflammation, providing relief from the discomfort caused by streptococcal infections. While they may not cure strep throat, they can support the body's healing process and ease symptoms while waiting for medical treatment. It is important to consult a healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment, as strep throat is often bacterial and may require antibiotics.

Herbal syrups should be used as a complementary therapy rather than a substitute for professional medical care.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

When prepared as a syrup, it can help alleviate symptoms of strep throat by reducing inflammation and irritation in the throat. The active compound, glycyrrhizin, is believed to have antimicrobial effects that may inhibit the growth of streptococcus bacteria. However, long-term use of licorice syrup can lead to side effects such as increased blood pressure due to its mineralocorticoid-like activity.

As a complementary therapy, licorice syrup may provide relief from sore throat symptoms when used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal syrups for treating symptoms of strep throat.

These syrups often combine ginger extract with honey, lemon, and other natural ingredients to soothe sore throats and reduce inflammation. While ginger may help alleviate some discomfort associated with strep throat, it is not a substitute for antibiotics, which are essential for treating bacterial infections like streptococcus. Some studies suggest that ginger can inhibit the growth of certain bacteria, potentially supporting the body's immune response.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, especially for persistent or severe symptoms.

3. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris, commonly known as thyme, is a popular herb used in the preparation of herbal syrups for treating strep throat due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oils in thyme, particularly thymol, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria, including Streptococcus pyogenes, which is responsible for strep throat. These syrups are often made by steeping thyme leaves in honey or sugar syrup, creating a soothing and effective remedy for sore throats. Many people use thyme syrup as a natural alternative to conventional treatments, especially for mild to moderate cases.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal syrups, especially for severe or persistent infections.

4. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal syrups for treating strep throat.

These syrups often combine sage with other herbs like thyme and echinacea to enhance their soothing and antibacterial effects. The active compounds in sage, such as thujone and flavonoids, may help reduce throat inflammation and inhibit the growth of streptococcus bacteria. While not a substitute for antibiotics in severe cases, sage-based syrups can provide symptomatic relief and support the body's natural healing process.

It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, especially for persistent or severe strep throat infections.

5. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea herbal syrups are commonly used as a natural remedy to support the immune system and potentially alleviate symptoms of strep throat.

These syrups are derived from the dried flowers and roots of the purple coneflower plant, which has been traditionally used in Native American medicine. While some studies suggest that echinacea may help reduce the duration and severity of colds and upper respiratory infections, its effectiveness specifically for strep throat remains inconclusive. Due to the lack of strong scientific evidence, echinacea should not replace conventional medical treatments for strep throat, such as antibiotics.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using echinacea syrups, especially for individuals with allergies or those taking other medications.
