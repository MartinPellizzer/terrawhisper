10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Over Bleeding During Periods

Herbal essential oils such as lavender, chamomile, and ginger are often used to help manage heavy bleeding during periods due to their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

These oils can be applied topically, diffused, or used in steam inhalation to promote relaxation and reduce menstrual discomfort. Lavender and chamomile are particularly effective in easing cramps and balancing hormonal fluctuations, while ginger oil is known for its ability to support blood circulation and reduce excessive menstrual flow. However, it is important to dilute these oils properly with a carrier oil to avoid skin irritation and consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with sensitive skin or medical conditions.

When used responsibly, herbal essential oils can be a natural and supportive complement to conventional treatments for managing heavy menstrual bleeding.

1. Chaste tree (Vitex agnus-castus)

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, is often used in herbal medicine to support hormonal balance, particularly in women experiencing irregular menstrual cycles or heavy bleeding.

Its essential oil, derived from the flowering plant, contains compounds that may help regulate the hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian axis, potentially reducing excessive menstrual flow. Some studies suggest that vitex essential oil may influence progesterone levels, which can help stabilize menstrual bleeding patterns. However, it is important to note that while some anecdotal evidence supports its use, more rigorous clinical research is needed to confirm its efficacy for heavy menstrual bleeding.

As with any herbal remedy, it should be used under the guidance of a qualified healthcare provider to ensure safety and appropriateness for individual health conditions.

2. Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

These essential oils may help reduce excessive bleeding during menstruation by supporting uterine health and regulating hormonal imbalances. Studies suggest that curcumin, a key compound in turmeric, can influence blood clotting factors and reduce menstrual flow. However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using turmeric essential oils, as they may interact with other medications or conditions.

While some women find relief from using turmeric-based products, more research is needed to fully understand their efficacy and safety in managing menorrhagia.
