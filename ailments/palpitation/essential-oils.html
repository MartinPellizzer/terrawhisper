10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Palpitation

Herbal essential oils have been traditionally used to support heart health and alleviate symptoms of palpitations, which are irregular or rapid heartbeats.

Oils such as lavender, chamomile, and valerian root are known for their calming properties that can help reduce anxiety and stress, common triggers for palpitations. These oils can be used through aromatherapy, topical application, or diffusion to promote relaxation and balance the autonomic nervous system. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially for individuals with existing heart conditions or those on medication.

While essential oils may offer some relief, they should not replace medical treatment for persistent or severe palpitations.

1. Valerian (Valeriana officinalis)

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a traditional herbal plant known for its calming effects and has been used for centuries to address various nervous system disorders.

Its essential oil, derived from the roots of the plant, contains bioactive compounds such as valerenic acid and sesquiterpenes, which are believed to interact with the central nervous system to promote relaxation. Valerian essential oil is often used in aromatherapy to alleviate symptoms of palpitations, which are irregular or forceful heartbeats, by reducing anxiety and stress. However, it is important to note that while some studies suggest its potential benefits, more research is needed to fully understand its efficacy and safety for this specific use.

As with any essential oil, it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional.

2. English lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is widely recognized for its calming properties and is often used in aromatherapy to help alleviate symptoms of palpitations.

The essential oil derived from this plant contains compounds such as linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their soothing and anti-anxiety effects on the nervous system. When inhaled or applied topically, lavender essential oil may help reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for palpitations. Some studies suggest that the aromatic compounds in lavender can influence heart rate and promote a sense of relaxation, making it a potential natural remedy for managing irregular heartbeats.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using lavender essential oil, especially for individuals with existing heart conditions or those taking medications.

3. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their calming and antispasmodic properties.

These essential oils, derived from the leaves of the plant, include compounds such as thujone, cineole, and camphor, which may help in reducing symptoms of palpitations by promoting relaxation and easing nervous system tension. Some studies suggest that the aromatic compounds in sage oil can help regulate heart rate and reduce anxiety, which are common triggers for palpitations. When used in aromatherapy or diluted topical applications, sage essential oil may offer a natural remedy for individuals experiencing irregular heartbeats.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using sage essential oils, especially for those with existing heart conditions or who are pregnant.

4. Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis)

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a herb widely used in aromatherapy for its calming and stimulating properties.

Its essential oil is known to support cardiovascular health and may help alleviate symptoms of palpitations by promoting relaxation and reducing stress-related heart rhythms. The oil contains compounds like cineole and camphor, which have been studied for their potential to improve circulation and regulate heart rate. When used in diffusion or topical application, rosemary essential oil can help balance the nervous system and reduce anxiety, which are common triggers for palpitations.

However, it is important to use the oil in moderation and consult a healthcare professional before incorporating it into a treatment regimen for heart-related issues.

5. Black pepper (Piper nigrum)

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its stimulating and warming properties.

While black pepper itself is not typically used as an essential oil, its essential oil can be extracted and used in aromatherapy for its pungent and invigorating aroma. Some practitioners suggest that the essential oil of black pepper may help alleviate palpitations by stimulating circulation and reducing stress-related cardiac symptoms. However, it is important to note that there is limited scientific evidence supporting the use of Piper nigrum essential oils specifically for palpitations.

As with any essential oil, it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a qualified aromatherapist or healthcare provider.

6. Ceylon cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum)

Cinnamomum zeylanicum, commonly known as cinnamon bark, produces a rich essential oil that has been traditionally used for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

The essential oil contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which may help in reducing stress and anxiety, common contributors to palpitations. When used in aromatherapy, cinnamon essential oil can promote relaxation and balance the autonomic nervous system, potentially alleviating symptoms of irregular heartbeats. However, it is important to use this oil with caution, as excessive use may cause irritation or interact with certain medications.

Always consult a healthcare professional before using cinnamon essential oil for palpitations, especially if you have underlying heart conditions or are on medication.

7. Peppermint (Mentha piperita)

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a well-regarded herb whose essential oil is widely used for its calming and soothing properties.

The essential oil of peppermint contains high concentrations of menthol, which can help alleviate symptoms of palpitations by promoting relaxation and reducing stress, a common trigger for heart palpitations. When used aromatically or topically, peppermint essential oil may help regulate heart rate and ease the sensation of rapid or irregular heartbeats. However, it is important to dilute the oil properly before applying it to the skin to avoid irritation.

While peppermint essential oil can be a supportive remedy, it should not replace professional medical advice, especially for individuals experiencing persistent or severe palpitations.

8. Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their potential cardiovascular benefits.

These oils may help regulate heart rhythms and reduce palpitations by influencing the body's stress response and hormonal balance. The active compounds in licorice, such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, are believed to have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that support heart health. However, excessive use of licorice root can lead to side effects like hypertension, so it should be used with caution.

When incorporated into a balanced herbal regimen under professional guidance, glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils may offer supportive relief for individuals experiencing palpitations.

9. Lemon balm (Melissa officinalis)

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a popular herb used in aromatherapy for its calming properties.

Its essential oil, derived from the leaves of the plant, is known for its ability to help reduce stress and anxiety, which are common contributors to palpitations. The soothing aroma of lemon balm essential oil can promote relaxation and balance the nervous system, potentially alleviating symptoms of irregular heartbeats. When used in diffusers, topical applications, or inhalation, it may support emotional well-being and reduce the frequency of palpitations.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially for individuals with heart conditions or those taking medications.

10. Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, is a popular herb used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties, including its potential to alleviate symptoms related to palpitations.

The essential oils extracted from ginger contain bioactive compounds such as gingerol and zingiberene, which are believed to have calming and anti-inflammatory effects on the cardiovascular system. These oils may help regulate heart rhythm by reducing stress and anxiety, common triggers of palpitations. When used in aromatherapy or diluted topical applications, ginger essential oil is thought to promote relaxation and support overall cardiovascular health.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using ginger essential oils, especially for individuals with pre-existing heart conditions.
