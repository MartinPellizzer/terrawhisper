10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Palpitation

Herbal essential oils have been traditionally used to support heart health and may help alleviate symptoms of palpitations by promoting relaxation and reducing stress, which are common triggers for irregular heartbeats.

Oils such as lavender, chamomile, and frankincense are known for their calming properties and may help regulate the nervous system, thereby reducing the frequency of palpitations. When used in aromatherapy or diluted for topical application, these oils can provide a natural alternative for those seeking non-pharmacological relief. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially for individuals with existing heart conditions or those taking medications.

While essential oils may offer some supportive benefits, they should not replace medical treatment for persistent or severe palpitations.

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis, commonly known as valerian, is a herb widely used for its calming properties, and its essential oil has been studied for its potential effects on palpitations.

The essential oil contains compounds like valerenic acid and sesquiterpenes, which may help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, potentially alleviating symptoms of palpitations. While research on its direct impact on heart palpitations is limited, some studies suggest that valerian may support cardiovascular health by reducing stress-related physiological responses. It is often used in aromatherapy or as a complementary treatment alongside conventional therapies for anxiety-related heart conditions.

However, individuals experiencing persistent palpitations should consult a healthcare professional before using valerian essential oil to ensure safety and appropriateness for their condition.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a herbal plant whose essential oil has been traditionally used for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

The essential oil of rosemary contains compounds such as camphor, cineole, and pinene, which may help in reducing stress and anxiety, common contributors to palpitations. Some studies suggest that the calming effects of rosemary essential oil can support heart health and potentially alleviate symptoms of irregular heartbeats. When used in aromatherapy or diluted topical applications, it may offer a natural approach to managing palpitations.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using rosemary essential oil, especially for individuals with cardiovascular conditions.

3. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, contains essential oils that have been explored for their potential effects on cardiovascular health, including palpitations.

The primary volatile compounds in black pepper essential oil, such as limonene and beta-caryophyllene, may exert mild stimulant properties that could influence heart rate and rhythm. While some anecdotal reports suggest that the essential oil might help regulate heart activity, there is limited scientific evidence supporting its use for palpitations. It is important to note that essential oils should not replace medical treatment for cardiac conditions and should be used with caution, especially in individuals with known heart issues.

Consulting a healthcare professional before using piper nigrum essential oils for palpitations is strongly recommended.

4. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their calming and soothing properties.

These essential oils, which include compounds like thujone, camphor, and cineole, may help reduce anxiety and stress, which are common triggers for palpitations. While some studies suggest that sage essential oils could have a mild sedative effect, more research is needed to confirm their efficacy in treating palpitations specifically. When used in aromatherapy or diluted topical applications, sage essential oils may offer a natural alternative for those seeking relief from heart-related symptoms.

However, individuals with cardiovascular conditions should consult a healthcare professional before using sage essential oils for palpitations.

5. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum, commonly known as cinnamon, produces a rich essential oil that has been traditionally used for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

This essential oil contains compounds such as cinnamaldehyde, which may help in reducing stress and anxiety, common contributors to palpitations. When used aromatically or topically, cinnamon essential oil can promote relaxation and balance the nervous system, potentially alleviating symptoms of heart palpitations. However, it is important to dilute the oil properly before applying it to the skin to avoid irritation.

While some studies suggest its potential benefits, it is advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before using cinnamon essential oil for palpitations, as individual responses can vary.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is widely used in aromatherapy for its calming and soothing properties.

Its essential oil contains compounds such as linalool and linalyl acetate, which have been shown to reduce stress and anxiety, common contributors to palpitations. When used in diffusion or topical application, lavender essential oil can help lower heart rate and promote a sense of relaxation. Studies suggest that the aromatic compounds in lavender may interact with the central nervous system to ease symptoms of cardiac palpitations.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using lavender oil, especially for individuals with underlying heart conditions.

7. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as lemon balm, is a herb widely used in aromatherapy for its calming and soothing properties.

Its essential oil is particularly valued for its ability to help alleviate symptoms of palpitations, which are often associated with stress, anxiety, or nervous system overactivity. The oil contains compounds like linalool and citral, which have been shown to have sedative and anxiolytic effects, promoting relaxation and reducing heart rate. When used in diffusers or applied topically with a carrier oil, Melissa officinalis essential oil can support emotional balance and ease the physiological responses that contribute to palpitations.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking medications.

8. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their potential cardiovascular benefits.

These oils may help regulate heart rhythm by influencing the body's electrolyte balance, which is crucial for maintaining normal cardiac function. Some studies suggest that the compounds in licorice essential oils, such as glycyrrhizin, may have mild effects on the sympathetic nervous system, potentially reducing the frequency of palpitations in certain individuals. However, it is important to note that excessive consumption of licorice can lead to side effects like hypertension and fluid retention, so its use should be monitored.

As with any herbal remedy, consulting a healthcare professional is recommended before using licorice essential oils for palpitations.

9. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, contains various bioactive compounds that have been explored for their potential therapeutic effects, including their use in essential oils derived from the plant.

While the essential oils of Urtica dioica are not as widely studied as other herbal oils, some preliminary research suggests they may possess anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic properties. These characteristics have led to interest in their possible application for conditions involving palpitations, which can be related to stress, inflammation, or autonomic nervous system imbalances. However, there is currently limited scientific evidence supporting the use of Urtica dioica essential oils specifically for palpitations, and more research is needed to establish their efficacy and safety.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using Urtica dioica essential oils, especially for individuals with cardiovascular conditions.

10. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herb used in the production of essential oils that may support cardiovascular health.

The essential oil derived from peppermint contains active compounds like menthol and limonene, which are known for their cooling and calming effects on the body. When used in aromatherapy or topical applications, peppermint oil may help reduce stress and anxiety, which are common triggers for palpitations. Some studies suggest that the soothing properties of peppermint oil can help regulate heart rate and ease the sensation of rapid or irregular heartbeats.

However, it is important to use peppermint essential oil under professional guidance, as it may interact with certain medications or cause adverse effects in some individuals.
