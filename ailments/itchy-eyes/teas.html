10 Best Herbal Teas For Itchy Eyes

Herbal teas can be a natural and soothing remedy for itchy eyes, often caused by allergies, dryness, or irritation.

Chamomile, green tea, and calendula are popular choices known for their anti-inflammatory and calming properties. To use, steep a few tea bags in hot water and let them cool before placing them over closed eyes for 10 to 15 minutes. These teas can help reduce redness and provide relief without the use of harsh chemicals.

However, it's important to consult a healthcare professional if symptoms persist or worsen, as persistent itching may indicate a more serious underlying condition.

1. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential soothing properties.

When prepared as a herbal tea, it may help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes by reducing inflammation and irritation. The tea is believed to contain compounds that support eye health and may help drain excess moisture from the eyes. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using it, as it may interact with certain medications or conditions.

While some people find relief from using equisetum arvense tea for itchy eyes, it should not replace professional medical treatment for underlying eye issues.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a herbal plant often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.

While it is well-known for its use in treating mild depression, some people also use hypericum perforatum herbal teas to alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes, possibly due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. However, it is important to note that there is limited scientific evidence supporting its effectiveness for eye-related conditions. The tea is typically prepared by steeping dried hypericum leaves in hot water, and it may be consumed several times a day as part of a holistic approach to eye comfort.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking other medications.

3. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal tea that has been traditionally used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

When consumed as a tea, chamomile may help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes by reducing inflammation and soothing the delicate eye tissues. The tea contains compounds like apigenin, which have been shown to have antihistamine and antioxidant effects, potentially helping to reduce allergic reactions and irritation. While chamomile tea is generally safe for most people, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it for eye-related issues, especially if you have known allergies or are taking other medications.

Overall, chamomile herbal tea can be a gentle, natural option for those seeking relief from itchy eyes.

4. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herb that has been traditionally used for its various health benefits, including its potential to alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes.

When brewed into a herbal tea, stinging nettle may help reduce inflammation and irritation, providing natural relief for those suffering from eye discomfort. The tea is believed to contain compounds that support the body's natural healing processes and may help detoxify the system, indirectly benefiting eye health. To prepare the tea, fresh or dried leaves are typically steeped in hot water for several minutes, and it can be consumed regularly as part of a holistic approach to eye care.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using stinging nettle tea, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking medications.

5. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential soothing properties.

While it is more widely recognized for its effects on the digestive and respiratory systems, some herbalists suggest that yarrow tea may help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine qualities. The tea is believed to support the body's natural healing processes and may help reduce irritation and redness associated with minor eye discomfort. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using yarrow for eye-related issues, as it may interact with certain medications or conditions.

As with any herbal remedy, individual responses can vary, and it should not replace professional medical advice or treatment.

6. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is often used in herbal teas to help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes.

The calming properties of lavender are believed to reduce inflammation and soothe irritation, making it a popular natural remedy for eye discomfort. When brewed into a tea, lavender can be applied as a compress or consumed internally to support overall eye health. Its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory compounds may help ease conditions like allergies or dryness that contribute to itchy eyes.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using lavender tea for eye issues, especially if you have existing medical conditions or are pregnant.

7. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green and black teas are derived, contains compounds like polyphenols and catechins that may have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

While it is not a direct treatment for itchy eyes, some herbal teas made from Camellia sinensis are believed to support overall eye health by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation. These teas may help alleviate symptoms associated with minor eye irritations when consumed as part of a balanced diet. However, it is important to note that they should not replace professional medical advice or treatment for persistent or severe eye conditions.

Always consult an eye care professional if itchy eyes persist or worsen.

8. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, is a popular herbal remedy often used in teas to alleviate symptoms such as itchy eyes.

The tea is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties that can help reduce redness, irritation, and allergic reactions around the eyes. To use it for itchy eyes, the tea can be cooled and used as a compress or applied directly to the affected area. While it is generally considered safe for most people, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially for those with known allergies or existing eye conditions.

Regular use of chamomile tea may provide soothing relief and support overall eye health.

9. Rosa canina

Rosa canina, also known as dog rose, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to support eye health and alleviate symptoms such as itchy eyes.

The flowers of the Rosa canina plant contain anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce irritation and redness in the eyes. When brewed into a soothing herbal tea, Rosa canina can provide a natural and calming effect for individuals experiencing mild eye discomfort. This tea is often recommended as a gentle alternative to over-the-counter eye drops, particularly for those seeking a holistic approach to eye care.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using Rosa canina tea, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or are taking other medications.

10. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its soothing properties, and its herbal teas may offer relief for itchy eyes.

The tea contains compounds such as rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects that can help reduce irritation and redness. To prepare the tea, steep fresh or dried sage leaves in hot water for several minutes, then let it cool before use. Some people apply cooled sage tea as a compress over the eyes to alleviate discomfort.

While sage tea is generally considered safe for topical use, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it for persistent eye issues.
