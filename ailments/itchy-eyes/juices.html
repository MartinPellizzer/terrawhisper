10 Best Herbal Juices For Itchy Eyes

Herbal juices can be a natural remedy for itchy eyes, often caused by allergies, dryness, or irritation.

Certain herbs like chamomile, calendula, and eyebright are traditionally used for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties. These herbs can be infused into water or juice to create a calming beverage that may help reduce redness and discomfort. However, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal juices, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking medications.

While herbal juices may offer some relief, they should not replace medical treatment for persistent or severe eye issues.

1. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, has been traditionally used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for herbal remedies.

Chamomile herbal juice, derived from the flowers of the plant, is believed to soothe irritated eyes and reduce redness when applied topically. It contains compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene, which have demonstrated anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Some people use diluted chamomile juice as a natural eye compress to alleviate symptoms of mild eye irritation or allergic reactions.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using chamomile juice on the eyes, especially for those with known allergies or underlying eye conditions.

2. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, has been traditionally used for its potential therapeutic properties, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

While it is more widely recognized for its use in treating mild depression, some studies suggest that it may also offer relief for itchy eyes by reducing inflammation and irritation. Herbal juices made from Hypericum perforatum are believed to support eye health by soothing the delicate tissues around the eyes. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using it, as it may interact with certain medications or cause sensitivity to sunlight.

Overall, while hypericum perforatum herbal juices may provide some relief for itchy eyes, they should be used as a complementary treatment rather than a replacement for medical advice.

3. German chamomile (Chamomilla recutita)

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, has been traditionally used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for soothing itchy eyes.

When prepared as a herbal juice, chamomilla recutita can provide natural relief by reducing redness and irritation caused by allergies or environmental irritants. The high concentration of antioxidants and flavonoids in the juice helps to neutralize free radicals and support overall eye health. To use it effectively, the juice should be diluted with water or a mild herbal infusion to avoid any potential irritation.

While generally safe for most individuals, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using chamomilla recutita, especially if you have known allergies or underlying health conditions.

4. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When prepared as a juice, it may offer relief for itchy eyes by reducing irritation and redness due to its high content of antioxidants and vitamins. The juice is typically made by blending fresh nettle leaves with water or a mild juice, and it is often consumed internally or applied externally. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using stinging nettle juice, as it can cause allergic reactions in some individuals.

While some people find it beneficial, scientific evidence supporting its effectiveness for itchy eyes remains limited.

5. Dog rose (Rosa canina)

Rosa canina, also known as dog rose, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to support eye health and alleviate symptoms such as itching.

The juice extracted from its berries is rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and anti-inflammatory compounds that may help reduce irritation and redness in the eyes. When consumed internally, rosa canina juice can promote overall immune support, which may indirectly benefit eye health. Some people use it as a natural alternative to commercial eye drops for mild cases of itchy eyes.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or are taking other medications.

6. Yarrow (Achillea millefolium)

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, making it a potential natural remedy for itchy eyes.

When prepared as a herbal juice, it may help soothe irritation and reduce redness by calming the delicate eye tissues. The active compounds in yarrow, such as flavonoids and essential oils, are believed to have antimicrobial and antihistaminic effects that could alleviate allergic reactions or minor infections causing eye discomfort. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using yarrow juice, as it may cause irritation in some individuals or interact with certain medications.

While some people may find relief from using yarrow-based eye drops or compresses, it should not replace professional medical advice or treatment for persistent or severe eye conditions.

7. Field horsetail (Equisetum arvense)

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential soothing properties.

When prepared as a herbal juice, it is believed to help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes due to its high concentration of silica and other minerals that may support eye health. The juice is typically made by steeping fresh or dried horsetail in water or a mild vinegar solution to extract its active compounds. Some practitioners recommend using it as a compress or diluted liquid to gently apply around the eyes.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using horsetail juice, as it may cause irritation in sensitive individuals.

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, has been traditionally used for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for itchy eyes.

Herbal juices made from lavender can help reduce irritation and redness by calming the delicate eye tissues. These juices are often prepared by infusing fresh or dried lavender flowers in water or a mild herbal base, allowing the beneficial compounds to be extracted. While not a substitute for medical treatment, lavender juice may offer relief for mild eye discomfort when used as a complementary therapy.

9. Blessed thistle (Cnicus benedictus)

Cnicus benedictus, also known as St. Benedict's thistle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential soothing properties.

When prepared as a herbal juice, it may help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes by reducing inflammation and irritation. The plant contains compounds such as flavonoids and essential oils that are believed to have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects. To use it for itchy eyes, the juice is often applied topically with a clean cloth or drops instilled into the affected eye.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedy, especially for eye conditions, to ensure safety and effectiveness.

10. Black elderberry (Sambucus nigra)

Sambucus nigra, commonly known as elderberry, is often used in herbal remedies for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

While it is more commonly associated with immune support, some traditional uses suggest it may help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes by reducing irritation and inflammation.
