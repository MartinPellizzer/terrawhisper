10 Best Herbal Juices For Itchy Eyes

Herbal juices have been traditionally used to soothe itchy eyes, often attributed to their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Ingredients like chamomile, calendula, and nettle are commonly included for their calming effects on the eyes and surrounding tissues. These juices can be prepared by steeping the herbs in water and then straining the liquid, making them a natural and gentle remedy for mild eye irritation. However, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, especially if the itching is persistent or accompanied by other symptoms.

While herbal juices may offer relief for some individuals, they should not replace medical treatment for more serious eye conditions.

1. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its potential therapeutic properties.

While it is well-known for its use in treating mild depression, some people also use it for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. When prepared as a herbal juice, hypericum perforatum may help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation. However, it is important to note that there is limited scientific evidence specifically supporting its use for eye conditions.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using hypericum perforatum, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking other medications.

2. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is often used in herbal remedies for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Chamomile herbal juices are believed to provide relief for itchy eyes by reducing irritation and redness due to their high concentration of antioxidants and flavonoids. These juices can be applied as eye compresses or diluted with water to create a gentle solution for daily use. While chamomile is generally considered safe for topical use, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially if you have allergies or existing eye conditions.

Overall, chamomile herbal juices offer a natural and calming alternative for those seeking relief from itchy eyes.

3. German chamomile (Chamomilla recutita)

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, has been traditionally used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for alleviating symptoms of itchy eyes.

The essential oils and compounds found in chamomile, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, possess soothing effects that can help reduce redness and irritation. When prepared as a herbal juice, chamomilla recutita can be applied as a compress or diluted in water to create a gentle eye wash. This natural remedy is particularly beneficial for individuals suffering from mild allergic reactions or dryness, offering a safe alternative to over-the-counter antihistamines.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using chamomile juice on the eyes, especially for those with sensitive skin or existing eye conditions.

4. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, making it a potential remedy for itchy eyes.

Herbal juices extracted from fresh or dried stinging nettle leaves can help reduce irritation and redness caused by allergies or environmental factors. The high concentration of antioxidants and vitamins, such as vitamin C and omega-3 fatty acids, may support eye health and alleviate discomfort. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using stinging nettle juice, as it may interact with certain medications or cause adverse reactions in some individuals.

While some people find relief from using nettle-based eye drops or juices, more research is needed to fully understand its efficacy and safety for eye conditions.

5. Field horsetail (Equisetum arvense)

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential benefits in treating itchy eyes.

The plant contains high levels of silica, which may help strengthen the eye tissues and reduce irritation. Herbal juices made from equisetum arvense are often prepared by infusing the dried plant material in water or a mild alcohol solution. Some practitioners believe that these juices can help alleviate symptoms caused by dryness, allergies, or minor infections.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using equisetum arvense, as it may interact with certain medications or cause adverse effects in some individuals.

6. Yarrow (Achillea millefolium)

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, making it a potential natural remedy for itchy eyes.

When prepared as a herbal juice, yarrow can be applied topically to the eyes to soothe irritation and reduce redness. However, it is important to note that direct application of any herbal juice to the eyes should be done with caution to avoid irritation or infection. Some herbalists recommend diluting the juice with water or using it as part of a compress to minimize risk.

While anecdotal evidence suggests it may offer relief, it is always advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies for eye conditions.

7. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential to alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes.

Herbal juices made from fresh or dried sage leaves may contain compounds such as rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, which possess anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities. These properties may help reduce redness, irritation, and itching associated with eye conditions like conjunctivitis or allergic reactions. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using sage juice for eye issues, as improper application could cause irritation or worsen symptoms.

While some anecdotal evidence supports its use, scientific research on the effectiveness of sage herbal juices for itchy eyes remains limited.

8. Black elderberry (Sambucus nigra)

Sambucus nigra, commonly known as European elderberry, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

While it is more commonly associated with immune support and cold remedies, some studies suggest that sambucus nigra may help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes by reducing inflammation and irritation. The juice extracted from the berries contains compounds like flavonoids and anthocyanins, which may contribute to its soothing effects on the eyes. However, it is important to note that there is limited clinical evidence specifically supporting the use of sambucus nigra juice for itchy eyes, and it should not replace professional medical advice.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before using it for eye-related issues.

9. Euphrasia (Euphrasia officinalis)

Euphrasia officinalis, commonly known as eyebright, is a traditional herbal remedy often used to soothe itchy eyes and reduce eye irritation.

Its extracts, particularly in the form of herbal juices, contain compounds like aucubin and flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These properties help to alleviate redness, swelling, and discomfort associated with conditions like conjunctivitis or dry eye syndrome. When prepared as a juice, euphrasia officinalis can be applied as a compress or diluted with water for safe topical use.

Many herbalists recommend it as a natural alternative to commercial eye drops for mild eye irritation.

10. Plantain (Plantago lanceolata)

Plantago lanceolata, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

When prepared as a herbal juice, it can help alleviate symptoms of itchy eyes by reducing irritation and inflammation in the delicate eye tissues. The juice is typically made by blending fresh leaves with water and straining the mixture to extract the beneficial compounds. Its high content of mucilage and antioxidants may support eye health and provide natural relief from discomfort.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using plantago lanceolata juice, especially if you have existing eye conditions or are taking other medications.
