10 Best Herbal Creams For Bleeding Gums

Herbal creams for bleeding gums are natural remedies that incorporate plant-based ingredients known for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

These creams often contain extracts from aloe vera, chamomile, calendula, and tea tree oil, which can help reduce irritation and promote healing. They are typically used as a complementary treatment alongside good oral hygiene practices and professional dental care. Many people find them effective in alleviating discomfort and preventing further gum inflammation.

However, it is important to consult with a dentist or healthcare provider before using herbal creams, especially if the bleeding gums are a symptom of a more serious condition like periodontitis.

1. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, making it a valuable ingredient in herbal creams designed to address bleeding gums.

These creams often incorporate sage extract to help reduce gum inflammation and promote tissue healing, offering a natural alternative to conventional treatments. The antimicrobial properties of sage may also help prevent bacterial infections that contribute to gum bleeding and irritation. When applied topically, salvia officinalis herbal creams can soothe the gums and encourage faster recovery from minor injuries or gum diseases.

However, it is important to consult with a dental professional before using such products, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

2. Marigold (Calendula officinalis)

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as pot marigold, is a flowering plant widely used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

Herbal creams containing calendula officinalis are often recommended for treating bleeding gums due to their ability to promote tissue healing and reduce irritation. These creams typically contain calendula extract, which has been shown to support gum health by strengthening blood vessels and reducing gum inflammation. The natural compounds in calendula, such as flavonoids and triterpenoids, contribute to its antibacterial and antiseptic effects, helping to prevent infection in the gums.

When applied topically, calendula-based creams can provide relief from bleeding gums while supporting overall oral hygiene.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is often used in herbal creams for its soothing and healing properties.

4. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is traditionally used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
