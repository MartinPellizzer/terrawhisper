10 Best Herbal Teas For Bleeding Gums

Herbal teas can be beneficial for soothing and healing bleeding gums due to their natural anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Certain herbs like chamomile, calendula, and licorice root are commonly used in herbal teas to reduce gum inflammation and promote healing. These teas can help alleviate irritation and prevent infection, making them a gentle alternative to conventional treatments. To use herbal teas for bleeding gums, they should be brewed weakly and applied directly to the affected area with a clean cloth or used as a mouth rinse.

However, it is important to consult with a dentist or healthcare provider before using herbal remedies, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

1. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to address various health concerns, including bleeding gums.

The tea is believed to possess anti-inflammatory and astringent properties that can help reduce gum irritation and bleeding. Rich in antioxidants and essential oils, sage tea may promote oral health by supporting tissue repair and reducing bacterial growth in the mouth. When consumed as a warm infusion, it can soothe inflamed gums and potentially ease symptoms associated with gingivitis.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using sage tea, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking medications.

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a versatile herb that has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including supporting oral health.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using stinging nettle tea, especially if you have existing medical conditions or are taking medications.
