10 Best Herbal Juices For Nail Fungus

Herbal juices have gained attention as a natural remedy for nail fungus due to their antifungal and antimicrobial properties.

Ingredients like garlic, grapefruit seed extract, and echinacea are commonly used in these juices to combat fungal infections. These juices can be applied topically to the affected nails, helping to reduce inflammation and slow the spread of the fungus. While they may not completely cure severe cases, they can be a complementary treatment alongside medical therapies.

However, it's important to consult a healthcare professional before relying solely on herbal juices for nail fungus treatment.

1. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is a succulent plant that has been widely used for its soothing and healing properties.

Its gel contains various bioactive compounds, including vitamins, minerals, enzymes, and antioxidants, which can support skin and nail health. Some studies suggest that aloe vera may help reduce fungal infections due to its antifungal and antimicrobial properties. When used as a herbal juice, aloe barbadensis can be applied topically to affected nails to potentially inhibit fungal growth and promote healing.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using aloe vera for nail fungus, as it may not be a standalone treatment for severe infections.

2. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which may make it a potential natural remedy for nail fungus.

Some studies suggest that the active compounds in Hypericum perforatum, such as hypericin and hyperforin, can inhibit the growth of fungi, including those responsible for onychomycosis. Herbal juices made from Hypericum perforatum can be applied topically to affected nails, helping to reduce fungal infection and promote healing. However, it is important to note that while some people may find relief with this approach, scientific evidence supporting its efficacy for nail fungus is limited.

As with any herbal treatment, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if other treatments have not been effective.

3. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been explored for its potential benefits in treating nail fungus due to its high concentration of antioxidants, anti-inflammatory compounds, and antimicrobial properties.

Herbal juices made from fresh or dried Urtica dioica leaves can be applied topically to affected nails to help reduce fungal growth and promote nail health. These juices may help soothe inflammation and support the body's natural healing processes, though they should not replace conventional antifungal treatments. Some studies suggest that the compounds in stinging nettle may inhibit the growth of fungi, making it a promising complementary therapy.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using Urtica dioica juice for nail fungus to ensure safety and effectiveness.

4. English lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and antifungal properties, making it a promising ingredient in herbal juices for treating nail fungus.

The essential oils derived from lavender contain compounds like linalool and lavandulol, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of fungal organisms, including those that cause onychomycosis. When incorporated into herbal juices, lavender can support the body's natural defenses against fungal infections while promoting the health of nail beds and surrounding skin. These juices are often used as a complementary therapy alongside conventional treatments, offering a more holistic approach to nail fungus management.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using lavender-based remedies, especially for persistent or severe cases of nail fungus.

5. Blessed thistle (Cnicus benedictus)

Cnicus benedictus, commonly known as blessed thistle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

While it is not a direct treatment for nail fungus, some studies suggest that its compounds may help support the body's natural defenses against fungal infections. Herbal juices made from Cnicus benedictus are often combined with other antifungal herbs like garlic or oregano to enhance their effectiveness. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using these remedies, as they may interact with medications or have side effects.

Overall, Cnicus benedictus herbal juices can be a complementary approach to managing nail fungus, but they should not replace conventional treatments.

6. Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, contains curcumin, a compound with potent antifungal properties that may help in treating nail fungus.

When consumed as a herbal juice, turmeric can support the body's natural defenses against fungal infections by reducing inflammation and boosting immunity. Some studies suggest that curcumin may inhibit the growth of fungi by disrupting their cell membranes and metabolic processes. However, while turmeric juice is generally safe, it should be used in conjunction with other treatments under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

It is important to note that turmeric alone may not be sufficient to cure severe nail fungus, and a comprehensive approach is often necessary for effective results.

7. Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for nail fungus.

Ginger contains bioactive compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which may inhibit the growth of fungi by disrupting their cellular structures. Herbal juices made from fresh ginger root can be applied topically to affected nails, helping to reduce fungal infections and promote healthier nail growth. While more scientific research is needed to confirm its efficacy, some individuals report improvement in nail fungus symptoms after regular use of ginger-based treatments.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using ginger juice for nail fungus, especially if other treatments have not been effective.

8. Polium germander (Teucrium polium)

Teucrium polium, also known as the summer sweet or catmint, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antifungal properties.

Recent studies suggest that extracts from this plant may help combat nail fungus due to their ability to inhibit fungal growth. When prepared as a herbal juice, Teucrium polium can be applied topically to affected nails to reduce infection and promote healing. The juice is typically made by steeping the fresh or dried leaves in water or alcohol, creating a potent natural remedy.

While it may offer benefits, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment for nail fungus.

9. Field horsetail (Equisetum arvense)

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as horsetail, is a plant rich in silica and other minerals, which has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits.

Some proponents suggest that horsetail herbal juices may help in treating nail fungus due to their antimicrobial and astringent properties. The high silica content is believed to strengthen nails and promote healthy nail growth, which could be beneficial for individuals suffering from fungal infections. However, scientific evidence supporting its efficacy for nail fungus is limited, and more research is needed to confirm these claims.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using horsetail juice for nail fungus treatment.

10. Thistle (Silybum marianum)

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including support for liver function.

While it is not a primary treatment for nail fungus, some studies suggest that its active compound, silymarin, may have antifungal properties that could help in managing fungal infections. Herbal juices made from silybum marianum are often marketed as natural remedies to promote nail health and combat fungal growth. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using these juices, as their effectiveness and safety for nail fungus are not fully established in clinical trials.

Combining silybum marianum with other antifungal treatments and maintaining good nail hygiene may offer a holistic approach to addressing nail fungus.
