Hiatus Hernia Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Hiatus hernia is a medical condition in which the stomach protrudes through an opening in the diaphragm, leading to symptoms such as acid reflux and chest pain.

Herbal remedies offer a natural and effective way to alleviate the symptoms of hiatus hernia, thanks to their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Certain herbs like Aloe vera, Licorice root, and Slippery elm help soothe and protect the digestive tract, while others like Ginger and Meadowsweet reduce inflammation and alleviate pain.

Herbal preparations such as teas, tinctures, and capsules made from herbs like Chamomile, Fennel, and Dandelion root can be used to calm the digestive system and prevent acid reflux, making them a popular choice for treating hiatus hernia.

What causes hiatus hernia?

The primary causes of hiatus hernia are the weakening of the diaphragm and the relaxation of the lower esophageal sphincter, which allows the stomach to push through the diaphragm into the chest cavity.

Secondary causes can include obesity, which puts extra pressure on the diaphragm and stomach, and pregnancy, which can cause the uterus to put pressure on the diaphragm and stomach.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing hiatus hernia include smoking, which can cause chronic coughing that puts pressure on the diaphragm, and a diet high in processed foods and sugar, which can lead to increased abdominal pressure.

Additionally, people who are over 50 years old, have a family history of hiatus hernia, or have conditions such as gastroparesis or scleroderma are also at higher risk.

What herbs heal hiatus hernia?

The medicinal herbs used to treat hiatus hernia are Slippery Elm (Ulmus rubra), Licorice Root (Glycyrrhiza glabra), and Marshmallow Root (Althaea officinalis), which exhibit anti-inflammatory, soothing, and protective properties to alleviate symptoms such as heartburn and acid reflux.

These herbs contain bioactive compounds like mucilages, flavonoids, and glycosides that help to coat and calm the mucous membranes of the esophagus and stomach, reducing irritation and inflammation.

Slippery Elm, in particular, contains ulmoponins, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of Helicobacter pylori, a bacteria commonly associated with stomach ulcers and acid reflux.

By modulating the gut-brain axis and promoting a healthy balance of gut bacteria, these herbs can also help to regulate digestive function and prevent future episodes of hiatus hernia.
