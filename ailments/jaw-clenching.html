What is jaw clenching and how does it affect your body?

Jaw clenching is a common habit where the muscles of the jaw tightly contract, often unconsciously, during stress, anxiety, or while concentrating.

This repetitive motion can lead to significant discomfort and tension in the jaw, face, and even the neck and shoulders. Prolonged jaw clenching may result in temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders, which can cause pain, clicking sounds, and difficulty in opening the mouth. It can also contribute to headaches, migraines, and even bruxism, which is the grinding of teeth during sleep.

Over time, chronic jaw clenching can lead to worn-down teeth, gum recession, and long-term dental issues if left untreated.

Underlying causes and risk factors of jaw clenching

Jaw clenching, also known as bruxing, is often linked to stress and anxiety, which can trigger subconscious muscle tension in the jaw.

Poor sleep habits, such as sleep apnea, may contribute by disrupting normal sleep cycles and increasing muscle activity. Individuals with misaligned teeth or bite issues are at higher risk, as their jaw muscles work harder to maintain proper alignment. Lifestyle factors like excessive caffeine or alcohol consumption can also exacerbate jaw clenching by increasing nervous system arousal.

Additionally, certain medications, particularly antidepressants, may have side effects that promote bruxism, highlighting the complex interplay between psychological and physiological factors.

Incorporating these herbs through teas, tinctures, or topical applications can be a natural and complementary approach to managing jaw clenching.
