Gerd Causes, Medicinal Herbs And Herbal Preparations

Gerd is a common digestive disorder characterized by the backflow of stomach acid into the esophagus, causing symptoms such as heartburn and chest pain.

Herbal remedies have been used to treat GERD due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which can help alleviate symptoms and promote healing.

Certain herbs such as licorice root, ginger, and slippery elm are effective in treating GERD as they have anti-inflammatory and mucilaginous properties, which can help soothe and protect the esophageal lining.

Herbal preparations like tea infusions, tinctures, and capsules made from herbs like aloe vera, chamomile, and fennel seeds can be used to treat GERD, with tea infusions being particularly beneficial as they can be easily digested and provide quick relief.

What causes gerd?

The primary causes of gerd are the relaxation of the lower esophageal sphincter (LES) and the excessive production of stomach acid, which allows stomach contents to flow back up into the esophagus.

Secondary causes of gerd include obesity, hiatal hernia, and certain medications, which can weaken the LES and increase acid production, or trigger acid reflux by relaxing the LES.

Lifestyles that increase the chances of developing gerd include consuming heavy meals, lying down after eating, and smoking, which can put pressure on the LES and delay digestion, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus.

Additionally, pregnancy and hormonal changes can also contribute to gerd due to the increased levels of progesterone, which can relax the LES and slow digestion.
