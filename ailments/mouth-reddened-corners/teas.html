10 Best Herbal Teas For Reddened Corners In Mouth

Herbal teas can be beneficial for soothing and healing reddened corners of the mouth, often caused by lip licking, nutritional deficiencies, or irritations.

Chamomile tea is particularly effective due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help reduce swelling and prevent infection. Applying cooled chamomile tea as a compress or using it as a mouth rinse can provide immediate relief and promote healing. Other herbs like calendula and licorice root also offer similar soothing effects, making them useful alternatives.

Regular use of these herbal teas can help maintain oral hygiene and prevent recurring sores in the mouth's corners.

1. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to soothe and heal reddened corners of the mouth, a condition often caused by lip licking, nutritional deficiencies, or infections.

The anti-inflammatory and astringent properties of sage help reduce irritation and inflammation, promoting faster healing of the affected area. When brewed as a gentle herbal tea, sage can be applied topically or consumed orally to support overall mouth health. Its high concentration of antioxidants and essential oils may also help combat bacterial infections that contribute to mouth sores.

While sage tea is generally safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially for prolonged periods or in individuals with certain medical conditions.

2. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal tea often used for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

When applied to the reddened corners of the mouth, chamomile tea can help reduce irritation and inflammation caused by conditions like angular cheilitis. The tea contains compounds such as bisabolol and chamazulene, which have antimicrobial and calming effects on the skin. To use it, one can steep a chamomile tea bag in warm water and apply it to the affected area with a clean cloth.

Regular application of chamomile tea can promote healing and provide relief from the discomfort associated with inflamed mouth corners.

3. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used for its potential anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

When brewed into a tea, it may help soothe and reduce inflammation in the reddened corners of the mouth, often caused by conditions like cheilitis or angular stomatitis. The tea's active compounds, such as hyperforin and hypericin, are believed to have antimicrobial and antioxidant effects that support skin healing. However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using St. John's Wort, as it can interact with certain medications.

Despite its traditional use, scientific evidence supporting its efficacy for mouth conditions is limited, and it should not replace professional medical advice.

4. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herb that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When brewed into a herbal tea, it can help alleviate reddened corners of the mouth, often caused by irritation, dryness, or fungal infections. The tea is believed to reduce inflammation and promote healing by containing compounds like flavonoids and antioxidants. To prepare the tea, dried stinging nettle leaves are steeped in hot water for several minutes.

Regular consumption of this herbal tea may support overall oral health and provide relief from persistent mouth irritation.

5. Dog rose (Rosa canina)

Rosa canina, commonly known as dog rose, is a traditional herbal remedy often used in teas to address various health concerns, including skin and mouth conditions.

The flowers of the Rosa canina plant are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and anti-inflammatory compounds, which may contribute to their soothing properties. When brewed into a tea, Rosa canina can help reduce inflammation and promote healing in the delicate tissues of the mouth. It is particularly beneficial for individuals experiencing reddened corners of the mouth, often caused by irritation, dryness, or minor infections.

Regular consumption of Rosa canina herbal tea may help alleviate discomfort and support the natural healing process of the oral mucosa.

The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of echinacea may help soothe irritated tissues and promote healing.
