10 Best Herbal Teas For Reddened Corners In Mouth

Herbal teas can be a natural and soothing remedy for reddened corners of the mouth, often caused by factors like lip licking, nutritional deficiencies, or excessive sun exposure.

Chamomile, calendula, and green tea are popular herbal choices known for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. To use these teas, simply brew a cup, let it cool, and apply it directly to the affected area with a clean cloth or cotton ball. Regular application can help reduce irritation and promote healing by providing gentle moisture and antioxidants.

Incorporating herbal teas into a daily skincare routine may offer a safe and effective way to address this common concern.

1. Rosa canina

Rosa canina, also known as rose hip, is a traditional herbal remedy often used in teas to support skin health and reduce inflammation.

The tea is rich in antioxidants, vitamin C, and essential oils, which can help soothe and heal sensitive areas like the corners of the mouth. Regular consumption of rosa canina tea may help alleviate redness and irritation caused by conditions such as angular cheilitis. Its mild and pleasant flavor makes it an appealing option for daily use.

While it is generally safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating it into a skincare or wellness routine.

2. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green and black teas are derived, contains compounds like polyphenols and catechins that may have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These properties may help reduce inflammation and irritation in the delicate tissues of the mouth, potentially alleviating reddened corners. Herbal teas made from Camellia sinensis are often recommended for their mild, calming effect, which can promote healing and comfort in the affected areas. Regular consumption of these teas may support overall oral health and reduce the likelihood of recurring mouth irritation.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal teas as a treatment for persistent or severe mouth conditions.

3. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a potent herb that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

When brewed into a herbal tea, it can help soothe and reduce inflammation in the reddened corners of the mouth, often caused by conditions like angular cheilitis. The tea is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that promote skin repair and overall oral health. To prepare the tea, fresh or dried stinging nettle leaves are steeped in hot water for several minutes.

Regular consumption of urtica dioica herbal tea may not only alleviate redness and irritation but also support the body's natural healing processes.

4. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John’s Wort, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to address various health concerns, including skin conditions and inflammation.

When brewed into a herbal tea, it may help soothe reddened corners of the mouth, a condition often associated with lip inflammation or angular cheilitis. The tea is believed to possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can reduce irritation and promote healing. To prepare the tea, steep a teaspoon of dried Hypericum perforatum in hot water for several minutes.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using this remedy, especially if you are on medication, as St. John’s Wort can interact with certain drugs.
