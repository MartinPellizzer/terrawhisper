9 Best Herbal Creams For Gum Infection

Herbal creams for gum infection are topical ointments or creams that are used to treat various gum-related ailments, such as gingivitis, periodontitis, and tooth decay, by utilizing the antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties of herbs.

The benefits of using herbal creams to treat gum infections include reducing pain and inflammation, preventing further infection, and promoting healing of the affected area.

Examples of herbal creams that can be used to treat gum infections include Neem cream, which is known for its antibacterial properties, Aloe Vera cream, which soothes and calms the gums, and Tea Tree oil cream, which has antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Additionally, other herbal creams like Echinacea cream, Clove oil cream, and Myrrh cream can also be used to treat gum infections due to their antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that help in reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

1. Aloe barbadensis creams

Aloe barbadensis creams have been widely used to treat gum infections, also known as gingivitis, due to their anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

The aloe vera extract in these creams helps to reduce inflammation, soothe irritated gums, and prevent the growth of bacteria that cause infections.

The bioactive constituents such as aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A and E in Aloe barbadensis creams contribute to their therapeutic effects, which include reducing swelling, promoting healing, and preventing further infection.

Regular use of Aloe barbadensis creams can help to alleviate symptoms of gum infections, promote healthy gum tissue, and prevent recurring infections, making it a beneficial herbal preparation for oral health.

2. Echinacea purpurea creams

Echinacea purpurea creams have gained popularity as a natural remedy to treat gum infections, also known as periodontal diseases.

The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of this herbal preparation help to reduce swelling and prevent the growth of bacteria that cause gum infections.

The bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, caffeic acid, and chicoric acid, in Echinacea purpurea creams exhibit their therapeutic effects by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and promoting the healing of gum tissues.

By using Echinacea purpurea creams, individuals can benefit from reduced pain, inflammation, and the prevention of further gum damage, ultimately leading to improved oral health and a reduced risk of systemic infections.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia creams

Melaleuca alternifolia creams, derived from the leaves of the tea tree, have been traditionally used to treat gum infections, such as gingivitis and periodontitis.

The properties of these creams, including their antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, help to reduce the severity of gum infections by killing bacteria and reducing inflammation.

The bioactive constituents, including terpinen-4-ol and cineole, in these creams help to inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, thereby treating the underlying cause of the infection.

The benefits of using Melaleuca alternifolia creams to treat gum infections include reduced inflammation, improved wound healing, and a decrease in the severity of symptoms, making them a popular natural remedy for this common oral health issue.

4. Eucalyptus globulus creams

The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus globulus, including eucalyptol, cineole, and flavonoids, help to combat the infection by reducing inflammation, inhibiting the growth of bacteria, and promoting healing of the affected area.

The benefits of using Eucalyptus globulus creams to treat gum infections include natural and non-toxic treatment, reduced risk of side effects, and promotion of overall oral health.
