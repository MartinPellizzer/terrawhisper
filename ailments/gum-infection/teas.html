10 Best Herbal Teas For Gum Infection

Herbal teas can be a natural and effective remedy for gum infections due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Teas made from ingredients like green tea, chamomile, and sage have been shown to reduce bacterial growth and soothe inflamed gums. To use these teas, they should be brewed and cooled to a warm temperature before being applied directly to the affected areas or used as a mouth rinse. Regular use of herbal teas can complement traditional dental care and help prevent the progression of gum disease.

However, it is important to consult a dentist for persistent infections, as herbal remedies should not replace professional treatment.

1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a promising ingredient in herbal teas for gum infections.

The essential oils in sage, particularly thujone and cineole, have shown effectiveness in reducing bacterial growth that contributes to gum disease. When brewed into a tea, sage can help soothe inflamed gums and reduce plaque buildup, offering natural relief for mild periodontal issues. It is often combined with other herbs like echinacea or chamomile to enhance its therapeutic effects.

However, individuals with certain medical conditions or those taking medications should consult a healthcare professional before using sage-based remedies.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

The essential oils in rosemary, such as camphor and cineole, help reduce bacteria that contribute to periodontal disease and inflammation of the gums. When brewed into a tea, rosemary can soothe irritated gums and promote healing through its natural astringent effects. Regular consumption of rosemary tea may help prevent plaque buildup and support overall oral health.

However, it is advisable to consult a dentist or healthcare provider before using rosemary tea as a treatment for severe gum infections.
