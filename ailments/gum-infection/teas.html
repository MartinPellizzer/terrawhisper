10 Best Herbal Teas For Gum Infection

Herbal teas can be a natural and effective remedy for gum infections due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Chamomile, peppermint, and green tea are commonly used herbs that help reduce inflammation and soothe gum irritation. These teas can be used as a mouth rinse by steeping a few tea bags in hot water and swishing the solution around the mouth. Regular use of herbal teas may help prevent plaque buildup and support overall oral health.

However, it is important to consult with a dentist for persistent or severe gum infections to ensure proper treatment.

1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for gum infections.

When prepared as an herbal tea, sage can help reduce bacteria in the mouth, which may alleviate symptoms such as redness, swelling, and bleeding gums. The compounds in sage, such as thujone and flavonoids, contribute to its ability to fight oral pathogens and promote healing. To use sage for gum issues, it is often recommended to steep the leaves in hot water and allow it to cool before rinsing the mouth.

While sage tea may offer supportive benefits, it should not replace professional dental care for persistent or severe gum infections.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a fragrant herb that has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential benefits for oral health.

Rosemary herbal teas may help reduce gum inflammation and combat bacterial growth due to their antioxidant and antimicrobial compounds. The essential oils in rosemary, such as camphor and rosmarinic acid, have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects, which can support the healing of gum infections. Incorporating rosemary tea into a daily routine may complement conventional treatments for periodontal disease by promoting a healthier oral environment.

However, it is advisable to consult with a dentist or healthcare professional before using rosemary tea as a treatment for gum infections.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to support oral health and combat gum infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oils found in cinnamon, such as cinnamaldehyde and eugenol, help reduce the growth of bacteria that contribute to gum disease, such as Porphyromonas gingivalis. Regular consumption of cinnamon tea may help alleviate symptoms like swelling, redness, and bleeding of the gums, promoting overall gum health. However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using cinnamon tea as a treatment, especially for severe or persistent infections.

While cinnamon can be a beneficial complementary therapy, it should not replace professional dental care or prescribed treatments for gum infections.

4. Origanum vulgare

Orihanum vulgare, commonly known as oregano, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to support oral health, particularly in the treatment of gum infections.

The essential oils in oregano, such as carvacrol and thymol, possess strong antimicrobial properties that can help combat the bacteria responsible for gingivitis and periodontal disease. When brewed into a tea, oregano can reduce inflammation and soothe irritated gums, promoting faster healing. It is often combined with other herbs like chamomile or licorice root to enhance its therapeutic effects.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using oregano tea, especially for individuals with allergies or those taking medications.

5. Thymus vulgaris

Thymus vulgaris, commonly known as thyme, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to support oral health and combat gum infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oils in thyme, particularly thymol, help reduce bacterial growth in the mouth, making it effective in preventing plaque and gum disease. Drinking thyme herbal tea can soothe inflamed gums and promote healing by reducing swelling and irritation. It is often combined with other herbs like echinacea or green tea to enhance its therapeutic effects.

While thyme tea is generally safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment for persistent or severe gum infections.

6. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, has been traditionally used for its soothing and healing properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal teas aimed at addressing gum infections.

The gel extracted from the aloe plant contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial compounds that can help reduce swelling and combat bacteria that contribute to periodontal disease. When consumed as a herbal tea, aloe vera may support oral health by promoting gum healing and reducing the risk of infection. Its mild, slightly bitter taste makes it a pleasant addition to daily tea routines, enhancing both flavor and therapeutic benefits.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using aloe vera tea, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking medications.

7. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

While it is often associated with treating mild depression, some studies suggest that its compounds may help reduce inflammation and inhibit bacterial growth, making it a potential complementary remedy for gum infections. When brewed into a tea, hypericum perforatum may support oral health by reducing swelling and promoting healing in the gums. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it, as it can interact with certain medications.

Despite its possible benefits, it should not replace conventional treatments for gum infections, such as professional dental cleanings and antibiotics if necessary.

8. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When prepared as a tea, it can help reduce swelling and irritation associated with gum infections due to its high concentration of minerals and antioxidants. The tea may also support the body’s natural healing processes by boosting the immune system and reducing bacterial growth in the oral cavity. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using stinging nettle tea, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking medications.

While it can be a complementary remedy, it should not replace conventional dental treatments for severe gum infections.

9. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, commonly known as eucalyptus oil, is often used in herbal teas to support gum health due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These teas can help reduce bacterial growth in the mouth, which is a common cause of gum infections such as gingivitis. The essential oils in eucalyptus globulus may also promote healing and soothe inflamed gums, offering a natural alternative to conventional treatments. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using eucalyptus-based products, as excessive consumption may lead to toxicity.

When used in moderation, eucalyptus globulus herbal teas can be a beneficial complementary therapy for managing gum infections.

10. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is often used in herbal teas to support oral health and combat gum infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The active compound in black pepper, piperine, helps reduce bacterial growth in the mouth, which is a common cause of gum disease. When brewed into a tea, black pepper can help soothe inflamed gums and promote healing by improving blood circulation in the oral tissues. However, it is important to use it in moderation, as excessive consumption may irritate the mouth or stomach.

While it can be a complementary remedy, it should not replace professional dental care for severe gum infections.
