10 Best Herbal Creams For Gum Swelling

Herbal creams for gum swelling are natural remedies that often contain ingredients like aloe vera, tea tree oil, and chamomile, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These creams can help reduce redness, irritation, and swelling in the gums, offering a gentler alternative to conventional treatments. They are typically applied directly to the affected areas several times a day to promote healing and comfort. Many herbal creams are free from harsh chemicals, making them suitable for individuals with sensitive mouths or those preferring natural treatments.

However, it is important to consult a dentist or healthcare professional before using these products, especially if the gum swelling is persistent or severe.

1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, making it a valuable ingredient in herbal creams designed to alleviate gum swelling.

These creams often contain essential oils and extracts from the leaves of the plant, which can help reduce inflammation and promote healing of irritated gums. The natural compounds in salvia officinalis, such as rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, contribute to its ability to soothe and protect gum tissue. When applied topically, these creams can provide relief from the discomfort associated with swollen gums, often caused by periodontal disease or gingivitis.

However, it is advisable to consult a dentist or healthcare professional before using such products, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is often used in herbal creams for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce gum swelling.

These creams typically contain a concentrated form of aloe gel, which has been shown to promote tissue repair and hydration of the gums. The natural compounds in aloe vera, such as polysaccharides and enzymes, may help decrease inflammation and prevent bacterial growth that contributes to gum irritation. When applied topically, aloe-based creams can provide a cooling effect, offering relief from the discomfort associated with swollen gums.

However, it is important to consult a dentist or healthcare professional before using such products, especially if the swelling is due to an underlying condition like periodontitis.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it a valuable ingredient in herbal creams for gum swelling.

These creams often incorporate ginger extract to help reduce inflammation and discomfort associated with conditions like gingivitis or periodontal disease. The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, are believed to inhibit the production of inflammatory cytokines, thereby promoting gum healing. When applied topically, these herbal creams can provide a soothing effect and may support overall oral health when used as part of a regular dental hygiene routine.

However, it is important to consult a dentist before using such products, especially if the gum swelling is severe or persistent.

4. Sanguinaria canadensis

Sanguinaria canadensis, commonly known as bloodroot, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When incorporated into herbal creams, it may help reduce gum swelling by soothing inflamed tissues and inhibiting bacterial growth. These creams are often formulated with other natural ingredients like calendula or chamomile to enhance their therapeutic effects. However, due to the plant's toxic alkaloids, it is crucial to use such products under the guidance of a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider.

While some users report relief from gum irritation, more scientific research is needed to fully establish its safety and efficacy for this specific application.

When applied topically, these creams can provide a soothing effect and promote healing of inflamed gums.

However, it is important to consult a dental professional before using such products, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

6. Cinnamomum zeylanicum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum, commonly known as cinnamon, has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When incorporated into herbal creams, cinnamon can help reduce gum swelling by soothing irritated tissues and preventing bacterial infections. These creams often combine cinnamon with other natural ingredients like clove or eucalyptus to enhance their therapeutic effects. The warming sensation provided by cinnamon can also promote blood circulation in the gums, aiding in faster healing.

However, it is important to consult a dentist before using such creams, especially if the gum swelling is due to a more serious condition like periodontitis.

7. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

While it is often used for skin conditions and mood disorders, some studies suggest that its extracts may help reduce gum swelling due to their ability to inhibit inflammatory responses. Herbal creams containing Hypericum perforatum can be applied topically to the gums to alleviate discomfort and promote healing. These creams are generally considered safe for most people, though they may interact with certain medications.

As with any herbal treatment, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with existing gum issues or systemic conditions.

9. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a fragrant herb often used in herbal creams for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

These creams are formulated to target gum swelling by reducing inflammation and promoting healing in the oral cavity. When applied topically, rosemary herbal creams can provide relief from gum inflammation and support overall oral health.

10. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as pot marigold, is a herbal ingredient often used in topical creams for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

These creams can help reduce gum swelling by calming irritation and promoting tissue healing. The active compounds in calendula, such as flavonoids and triterpenes, contribute to its ability to ease inflammation and support oral health. When applied directly to swollen gums, calendula-based creams may provide relief from discomfort and aid in the recovery process.
