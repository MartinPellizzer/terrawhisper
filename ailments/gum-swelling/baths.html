10 Best Herbal Baths For Gum Swelling

Herbal baths for gum swelling involve soaking the affected areas in warm water infused with natural herbs known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Commonly used herbs include chamomile, calendula, and lavender, which can help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated gums. To prepare an herbal bath, steep a few tablespoons of dried herbs in hot water for about 10 minutes, then let the solution cool slightly before using it to rinse the mouth or apply it as a compress. This remedy is a gentle, natural alternative to commercial mouthwashes and can be used regularly to promote gum healing.

Consistency in use may lead to noticeable improvement in gum health and reduced swelling over time.

1. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

When used in herbal baths, it can help alleviate gum swelling by reducing inflammation and promoting healing of the oral tissues. To prepare a St. John's Wort bath, the dried herb is steeped in hot water to create a potent infusion, which is then added to warm bath water. This method allows the active compounds in hypericum to be absorbed through the skin, offering a gentle yet effective way to support gum health.

While herbal baths can be a complementary therapy, they should not replace professional dental care, especially for persistent or severe gum issues.

2. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

When used in herbal baths, sage can help reduce gum swelling by soothing irritated tissues and decreasing bacterial buildup. To prepare a sage bath, steep a handful of dried sage leaves in hot water for several minutes, then allow the solution to cool before using it as a mouth rinse or as part of a warm compress. This natural remedy may support overall oral health by promoting healing and reducing inflammation.

However, it is advisable to consult with a dentist or healthcare professional before using sage baths, especially if there are underlying medical conditions or if symptoms persist.

3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as pot marigold, is a herbal remedy often used in herbal baths to soothe gum swelling and promote oral health.

The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of calendula help reduce inflammation and prevent infection in the gums. To prepare a calendula bath, steep dried calendula flowers in hot water for several hours, then use the infused liquid to rinse the mouth or apply it as a compress to the affected areas. This natural treatment can be a gentle alternative to conventional mouthwashes, offering a calming effect on irritated gums.

Regular use of calendula-based baths may support overall gum health and aid in the healing process of mild gum inflammation.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis
