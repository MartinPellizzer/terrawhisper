10 Best Herbal Teas For Nausea

Herbal teas are a natural and soothing remedy for nausea, often used to alleviate symptoms caused by motion sickness, pregnancy, or digestive issues.

Commonly recommended herbs include ginger, peppermint, chamomile, and fennel, each offering unique properties that help settle the stomach and reduce discomfort. Ginger tea, in particular, is well-known for its antiemetic effects and has been widely used for centuries to combat nausea. Peppermint and chamomile teas can also help relax the digestive system and ease feelings of queasiness.

When consumed in moderation, these herbal teas provide a gentle, non-invasive option for managing nausea without the side effects of over-the-counter medications.

1. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to alleviate nausea.

The essential oils in fennel, particularly anethole, have mild antispasmodic and carminative properties that can help soothe digestive discomfort. Fennel tea is often recommended for pregnant women experiencing morning sickness due to its gentle nature and potential to ease nausea without harsh side effects. To prepare the tea, simply steep fresh or dried fennel seeds in hot water for several minutes.

Regular consumption of fennel tea may provide relief from nausea associated with indigestion, motion sickness, or even chemotherapy, though it is best used as a complementary therapy under medical guidance.

2. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is often used in herbal teas to help alleviate nausea due to its carminative and digestive properties.

The essential oils in cumin, such as limonene and cineole, can soothe the gastrointestinal tract and reduce bloating and discomfort. When brewed into a warm tea, cumin can help ease symptoms of motion sickness, morning sickness, and general digestive upset. It is typically combined with other herbs like ginger or peppermint to enhance its effectiveness.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using cumin tea, especially for pregnant women or individuals with existing medical conditions.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been widely used for centuries as a natural remedy for nausea.

Ginger herbal teas are made by steeping fresh or dried ginger root in hot water, allowing the aromatic compounds to infuse into the liquid. These teas are particularly effective in alleviating symptoms of motion sickness, morning sickness during pregnancy, and nausea caused by gastrointestinal issues. The active components in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, are believed to help reduce nausea by calming the digestive system and suppressing signals that trigger vomiting.

Regular consumption of ginger tea can provide a safe and soothing alternative to over-the-counter medications for managing nausea.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to alleviate symptoms of nausea.

The herb contains compounds like glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which may help soothe the digestive system and reduce stomach discomfort. When brewed into a tea, licorice root can provide a mild, sweet flavor that is easy on the stomach, making it a gentle remedy for morning sickness or motion sickness. However, it is important to use licorice root in moderation, as excessive consumption may lead to side effects such as increased blood pressure.

Overall, licorice root tea can be a beneficial herbal remedy for nausea when used appropriately under guidance.

5. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green, black, and white teas are derived, contains compounds that may help alleviate nausea by soothing the digestive system.

Its caffeine content can sometimes irritate the stomach, but in lower concentrations, it may act as a mild stimulant that aids in digestion. Herbal teas made from Camellia sinensis, such as ginger or peppermint infusions, are often recommended for nausea due to their calming and anti-inflammatory properties. These teas can help reduce stomach spasms and ease the sensation of queasiness, making them a popular natural remedy.

However, individuals with sensitive stomachs should consume them in moderation to avoid adverse effects.

6. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to alleviate nausea.

The essential oils in peppermint leaves, particularly menthol, help soothe the digestive system and reduce stomach discomfort. Peppermint tea is often recommended for individuals experiencing motion sickness, morning sickness, or digestive upset. Its refreshing aroma and cooling effect can also help calm the nervous system, further easing nausea symptoms.

When consumed in moderation, peppermint herbal tea is generally safe and can be a natural remedy for occasional nausea.

7. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to alleviate nausea.

The tea is made by steeping dried chamomile flowers in hot water, resulting in a soothing and aromatic beverage. Chamomile contains compounds such as apigenin, which may help reduce stomach discomfort and promote relaxation. It is often recommended for individuals experiencing motion sickness, morning sickness, or general digestive upset.

While generally safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before using chamomile tea, especially during pregnancy or for those with allergies.

8. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to alleviate symptoms of nausea.

The active compound in black pepper, piperine, is believed to aid in digestion and reduce gastrointestinal discomfort. When brewed into a tea, black pepper can help soothe the stomach and ease feelings of queasiness. However, it is important to use it in moderation, as excessive consumption may irritate the stomach lining.

This herbal remedy is often combined with other calming herbs like ginger or fennel to enhance its effectiveness against nausea.

9. Pimpinella anisum

Pimpinella anisum, commonly known as anise, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to help alleviate nausea.

The seeds of this plant contain compounds such as anethole, which have mild sedative and carminative properties that can soothe the digestive system. Anise tea is often recommended for its ability to ease symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and motion sickness. It is typically prepared by steeping the dried seeds in hot water for several minutes, allowing the soothing aroma and flavor to infuse into the liquid.

Due to its gentle nature, anise tea is considered safe for most adults and can be a natural alternative to conventional remedies for occasional nausea.

10. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support hormonal balance and alleviate symptoms such as nausea.

This herb is often recommended for women experiencing nausea related to menstrual cycles, pregnancy, or hormonal imbalances. When brewed into a tea, vitex agnus-castus may help soothe digestive discomfort and reduce feelings of queasiness. It is typically consumed in mild doses, with a calming effect on the gastrointestinal system.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using chasteberry, especially during pregnancy or if taking other medications.
