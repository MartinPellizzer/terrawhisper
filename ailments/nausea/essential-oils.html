10 Best Herbal Essential Oils For Nausea

Herbal essential oils have gained attention for their potential to alleviate nausea through their calming and digestive properties.

Oils such as peppermint, ginger, and lavender are commonly used due to their soothing effects on the gastrointestinal system. Peppermint oil, in particular, is known for its ability to ease digestive discomfort and reduce feelings of nausea. When used in aromatherapy or diluted in a carrier oil for topical application, these essential oils can provide relief without the side effects of conventional medications.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using essential oils, especially for pregnant individuals or those with underlying health conditions.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, is widely used in herbal essential oils for its effectiveness in alleviating nausea.

The essential oil derived from ginger contains bioactive compounds such as gingerol and shogaol, which have been shown to possess antiemetic properties. When used aromatically, through diffusers or inhalation, ginger essential oil can help soothe the digestive system and reduce feelings of queasiness. It is particularly beneficial for individuals experiencing motion sickness, morning sickness during pregnancy, or nausea caused by gastrointestinal issues.

However, it is important to use this essential oil in proper dilution and consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating it into a treatment regimen.

2. Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a popular herb used in the production of essential oils that are widely recognized for their ability to alleviate nausea.

The essential oil of peppermint contains active compounds like menthol and limonene, which have soothing and calming effects on the digestive system. When inhaled or applied topically, peppermint essential oil can help reduce feelings of queasiness and relieve stomach discomfort. It is often used in aromatherapy to ease motion sickness, morning sickness, and nausea caused by gastrointestinal issues.

However, it is important to dilute the oil properly before use and consult with a healthcare professional, especially for pregnant women or individuals with certain medical conditions.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, produces an essential oil that has been traditionally used for its soothing properties.

The oil contains compounds like cinnamaldehyde and eugenol, which may help alleviate nausea by calming the digestive system. When used in aromatherapy, inhaling the scent of cinnamon essential oil can provide relief from motion sickness or morning sickness. It is often diluted with a carrier oil before topical application to avoid skin irritation.

While it may offer natural relief, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it for persistent or severe nausea.

4. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, is a herb widely used in aromatherapy for its calming and stimulating properties.

The essential oil derived from its leaves contains compounds such as cineole, camphor, and pinene, which are known to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of nausea. When used in aromatherapy, rosemary essential oil can be diffused, applied topically, or used in inhalation techniques to help ease feelings of queasiness and promote a sense of well-being. It is particularly beneficial for individuals experiencing motion sickness or gastrointestinal discomfort.

However, it is important to dilute the essential oil properly and consult with a healthcare professional before use, especially for pregnant women or those with sensitive skin.

5. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, is a well-known herbal plant that contains curcumin, a compound with potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

While turmeric is often consumed as a spice, its essential oil derived from the rhizomes of Curcuma longa has been explored for its therapeutic benefits, including its potential to alleviate nausea. Studies suggest that the essential oil may help reduce gastrointestinal discomfort by modulating digestive enzymes and calming the stomach lining. Its natural anti-emetic properties make it a promising alternative for those seeking natural remedies for motion sickness, morning sickness, or post-operative nausea.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using curcuma longa essential oil, especially for prolonged or severe cases of nausea.

6. Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used for their digestive and anti-nausea properties.

The essential oil of black pepper, derived from the dried fruit of the plant, contains compounds like piperine, which may help stimulate digestion and reduce feelings of nausea. These oils are often used in aromatherapy or diluted in carrier oils to provide relief from motion sickness or post-meal discomfort. Studies suggest that the pungent aroma of black pepper essential oil can help soothe the stomach and ease symptoms of nausea by promoting digestive comfort.

However, it is important to use these oils in moderation and consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals with sensitive skin or existing health conditions.

7. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice, contains essential oils that have been traditionally used to alleviate nausea due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The essential oils derived from the roots of this plant are rich in compounds such as glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, which may help regulate digestive processes and reduce stomach irritation. These oils are often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies to ease symptoms of motion sickness, morning sickness, and gastrointestinal discomfort. Studies suggest that the volatile components of licorice essential oils may interact with the central nervous system to calm the digestive tract.

However, while promising, further research is needed to fully understand the efficacy and safety of glycyrrhiza glabra essential oils for nausea treatment.

8. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is a spice and herb that has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including its potential to alleviate nausea.

The essential oil extracted from cumin seeds contains compounds like limonene and pinene, which are known for their calming and digestive benefits. Studies suggest that inhaling or applying cumin essential oil may help reduce feelings of nausea by stimulating the digestive system and promoting relaxation. It is often used in aromatherapy and can be diluted with a carrier oil for topical application or added to diffusers.

While generally safe, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using cumin essential oil, especially for those with existing medical conditions or during pregnancy.

9. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is widely recognized for its calming properties and is often used in aromatherapy.

Its essential oil, extracted through steam distillation, contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their soothing and anti-emetic effects. Studies suggest that the inhalation of lavender essential oil can help alleviate nausea by reducing stress and anxiety, which are common contributors to feelings of sickness. This oil is frequently used in diffusers, massage oils, or as a topical application to provide relief from motion sickness or post-operative nausea.

Due to its gentle nature, lavender essential oil is considered a safe and effective natural remedy for managing nausea in many cases.

10. Peperomia pellucida

Peperomia pellucida, also known as "Java nettle" or "water pepper," is a small herb that has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

While it is not commonly used for essential oils, some sources suggest that its leaves may be distilled to produce a volatile oil containing compounds like camphor and eucalyptol, which have been studied for their anti-nausea effects. These compounds may help soothe the gastrointestinal system and reduce feelings of nausea by influencing neurotransmitter activity and reducing inflammation. However, it is important to note that scientific research on the use of Peperomia pellucida essential oils for nausea is limited, and more clinical studies are needed to confirm its efficacy and safety.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before using Peperomia pellucida or its essential oils for nausea.
