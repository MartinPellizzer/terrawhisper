10 Best Herbal Lozenges For Acid Reflux

Herbal lozenges for acid reflux are natural remedies that may help alleviate symptoms by soothing the throat and reducing irritation caused by stomach acid.

These lozenges often contain ingredients like licorice root, slippery elm, or ginger, which have anti-inflammatory and antacid properties. They can provide temporary relief by forming a protective barrier over the esophagus and reducing the burning sensation associated with heartburn. While they are generally safe for most people, they should not replace medical treatment for chronic acid reflux.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal lozenges, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used for its soothing properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal lozenges for acid reflux.

These lozenges work by forming a protective barrier over the esophageal lining, helping to neutralize stomach acid and reduce irritation. The active compounds in licorice root, such as glycyrrhizin, possess anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects that can alleviate the discomfort associated with heartburn and indigestion. While licorice lozenges may offer relief for mild acid reflux, they should be used in moderation due to potential side effects from excessive consumption.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using licorice-based remedies, especially for individuals with hypertension or other underlying health conditions.

2. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal lozenges for acid reflux.

Ginger contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which may help neutralize stomach acid and reduce inflammation in the esophagus. Regular use of ginger lozenges may provide natural relief for mild acid reflux symptoms without the side effects of conventional medications.

However, individuals with severe acid reflux should consult a healthcare professional before relying solely on herbal remedies.
