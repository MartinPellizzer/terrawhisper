10 Best Herbal Teas For Acid Reflux

Herbal teas can be a soothing and natural remedy for acid reflux due to their anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Chamomile, ginger, and licorice root are commonly recommended for their ability to reduce stomach acid and ease digestion. These teas work by calming the digestive tract and reducing irritation in the esophagus, making them a gentle alternative to over-the-counter medications. However, it's important to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating new herbal remedies, especially if you have underlying health conditions.

Regular consumption of these teas may help manage symptoms, but they should be part of a broader approach to managing acid reflux.

1. Camellia sinensis

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green and black teas are derived, contains compounds that may offer some relief for individuals suffering from acid reflux.

While it is not a cure for gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), moderate consumption of herbal teas made from Camellia sinensis may help reduce stomach acid due to its mild antacid properties. However, it is important to note that caffeine, which is present in some Camellia sinensis-based teas, can exacerbate acid reflux in sensitive individuals. Therefore, opting for decaffeinated versions or herbal alternatives like ginger or chamomile may be more beneficial for those with acid reflux.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating Camellia sinensis teas into a treatment plan for acid reflux.

2. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used to support digestive health.

When brewed into a tea, licorice root may help soothe the lining of the esophagus and stomach, potentially offering relief for individuals suffering from acid reflux. The herb contains compounds such as glycyrrhizin, which may have anti-inflammatory and antacid properties that reduce irritation and neutralize stomach acid. However, long-term use of licorice root tea can lead to side effects like increased blood pressure due to its effect on the adrenal glands.

As a result, it is often recommended to use licorice root tea in moderation or under the guidance of a healthcare professional, especially for those with pre-existing health conditions.

3. Foeniculum vulgare

Foeniculum vulgare, commonly known as fennel, is a popular herb used in herbal teas to help alleviate symptoms of acid reflux.

The tea is made by steeping the seeds or dried leaves of the plant in hot water, releasing compounds that may reduce stomach acidity and soothe the digestive tract. Fennel tea is believed to contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that can ease discomfort associated with heartburn and indigestion. It is often recommended as a natural remedy for those seeking alternatives to over-the-counter medications.

However, individuals with allergies to plants in the carrot family should exercise caution, as fennel is related to that group.

4. Matricaria chamomilla

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal tea often used for its calming and soothing properties.

It contains compounds like apigenin, which may help reduce inflammation and relax the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, potentially alleviating symptoms of acid reflux. While some studies suggest that chamomile may help soothe the digestive system, its effectiveness for acid reflux is still being researched, and results can vary among individuals. It is generally considered safe for most people when consumed in moderate amounts, though it may interact with certain medications.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before using chamomile tea, especially for those with chronic digestive issues.

5. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including support for digestive health.

While it is more commonly associated with liver protection, some studies suggest that silybum marianum may help alleviate symptoms of acid reflux by reducing inflammation and supporting the digestive system. When consumed as a herbal tea, it can be a soothing option for individuals seeking natural remedies for heartburn and indigestion. However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand its effectiveness for acid reflux, and it should be used in conjunction with other lifestyle and dietary modifications.

As with any herbal supplement, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before incorporating silybum marianum into a treatment plan for acid reflux.

6. Cuminum cyminum

Cuminum cyminum, commonly known as cumin, is often used in herbal teas to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of acid reflux.

The essential oils in cumin, such as limonene and cumin aldehyde, have been shown to reduce gastric acidity and improve digestion by stimulating the production of digestive enzymes. When brewed into a tea, cumin can help soothe the stomach lining and reduce the feeling of heartburn. It is typically consumed in combination with other herbs like ginger or fennel for enhanced efficacy.

However, individuals with severe acid reflux should consult a healthcare professional before incorporating cumin into their routine, as it may interact with certain medications.

7. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to alleviate symptoms of acid reflux due to its anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, help reduce stomach acid production and soothe the lining of the esophagus. Drinking ginger tea before meals can aid in digestion and prevent the upward movement of stomach acid. However, it is important to note that excessive consumption may irritate the stomach in some individuals.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating ginger tea into a treatment plan for acid reflux.

8. Cnicus benedictus

Cnicus benedictus, commonly known as blessed thistle, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of acid reflux.

This herb is believed to stimulate the production of digestive juices, which can help improve digestion and reduce the likelihood of stomach acid reflux. While some studies suggest that blessed thistle may have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, its effectiveness for acid reflux is still under investigation. It is often used in herbal teas, typically combined with other digestive herbs like ginger or fennel, to enhance its soothing effects.

As with any herbal remedy, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for individuals with existing medical conditions or those taking medications.

9. Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as chasteberry, is a herbal remedy that has been traditionally used for various hormonal and digestive issues.

While it is often associated with menstrual regulation and fertility support, some studies suggest it may also have a calming effect on the digestive system. For individuals with acid reflux, chasteberry tea may help reduce stomach acidity and soothe irritation by promoting healthy digestion. However, it is important to note that more research is needed to fully understand its efficacy for acid reflux specifically.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before incorporating vitex into a treatment plan for acid reflux.

10. Rosa canina

Rosa canina, also known as rosehip, is a herbal tea that has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including supporting digestive health.

It contains high levels of vitamin C, antioxidants, and anti-inflammatory compounds, which may help reduce the irritation and inflammation associated with acid reflux. Some studies suggest that the anti-inflammatory properties of rosehip could help soothe the lining of the esophagus and stomach, making it a promising natural remedy for those suffering from heartburn. However, it is important to note that while rosehip tea is generally considered safe, it may interact with certain medications and should be consumed in moderation.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating it into a regular routine for acid reflux management.
