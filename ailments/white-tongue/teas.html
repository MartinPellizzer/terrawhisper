10 Best Herbal Teas For White Tongue

Herbal teas can be beneficial for addressing a white tongue, a condition often caused by oral thrush, dryness, or poor oral hygiene.

Certain herbs like licorice root, ginger, and chamomile have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that may help reduce white coating on the tongue. Drinking these teas can promote saliva production, which helps cleanse the mouth and prevent the buildup of bacteria. Additionally, herbal teas such as peppermint or green tea can soothe irritation and improve overall oral health.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional if the white tongue persists, as it may indicate an underlying health issue.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, commonly known as licorice root, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to address various oral health issues, including white tongue.

The tea is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that may help reduce the buildup of bacteria and fungi on the tongue's surface. When consumed regularly, licorice root tea can promote a more balanced oral microbiome, potentially alleviating the symptoms of white tongue. However, it is important to note that excessive consumption may lead to side effects such as increased blood pressure due to its high glycyrrhizin content.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating licorice root tea into a treatment regimen for white tongue.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a popular herbal remedy often used in teas to support immune health.

Some people use echinacea tea to address oral health issues, including white tongue, which can be caused by fungal infections or poor oral hygiene. While there is limited scientific evidence directly linking echinacea to the treatment of white tongue, its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help reduce oral inflammation and combat harmful bacteria. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using echinacea, especially for persistent or severe cases of white tongue.

Incorporating echinacea tea as part of a holistic oral care routine may offer some benefits, but it should not replace professional dental or medical advice.

3. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, is often used in herbal teas for its warming and soothing properties.

When brewed into a tea, it can help alleviate symptoms associated with white tongue, a condition characterized by a thick, white coating on the tongue's surface. The antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory compounds in cinnamon may reduce oral infections and inflammation that contribute to the condition. Regular consumption of cinnamon tea can promote oral hygiene and support a balanced oral microbiome.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using cinnamon tea as a treatment for persistent or severe cases of white tongue.

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is often used in herbal teas to address various health issues, including oral conditions like white tongue.

The cooling and soothing properties of peppermint can help reduce inflammation and discomfort associated with white tongue, a condition characterized by a white or yellowish coating on the tongue. Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herb that has been used traditionally for its various health benefits, including its potential to address oral health issues like white tongue.

6. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is a herbal plant often used in the preparation of herbal teas that may help address white tongue, a condition characterized by a white coating on the tongue.

The tea is believed to have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce bacterial buildup and soothe oral irritation. To prepare the tea, dried sage leaves are steeped in hot water for several minutes, allowing the active compounds to infuse into the liquid. Drinking sage tea regularly may promote oral hygiene and potentially improve the appearance of the tongue.
