10 Best Herbal Teas For Hay Fever

Herbal teas can be a natural and soothing remedy for alleviating hay fever symptoms.

Common herbs like nettle, chamomile, and peppermint are often used due to their anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. These teas help reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and eye irritation by supporting the body's natural defenses. Drinking herbal teas regularly can also promote relaxation and improve overall respiratory health.

However, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, especially if you have allergies or are on medication.

1. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to help alleviate symptoms of hay fever.

This plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, polyphenols, and minerals that may support the immune system and reduce allergic reactions. When brewed into a tea, stinging nettle is believed to help clear nasal passages and reduce inflammation, providing natural relief from sneezing, itching, and runny nose. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using nettle tea, especially for those with existing medical conditions or who are taking medications.

While some studies suggest potential benefits, more research is needed to fully understand its efficacy for hay fever symptoms.

2. German chamomile (Chamomilla recutita)

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, has been traditionally used for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular herbal tea for alleviating symptoms of hay fever.

This herb contains compounds like bisabolol and flavonoids, which may help reduce nasal congestion, itching, and sneezing associated with allergic reactions. While scientific evidence supporting its effectiveness for hay fever is limited, many individuals report relief from using chamomile tea as a natural remedy. It is often consumed as a warm infusion, either on its own or combined with other herbs like nettle or ginger for enhanced benefits.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using chamomile, especially if you are pregnant, nursing, or taking medications.

3. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to help alleviate symptoms of hay fever.

The plant contains compounds such as flavonoids and rosmarinic acid, which may help reduce inflammation and histamine release in the body. Drinking sage tea regularly can support respiratory health and potentially ease nasal congestion and sneezing associated with allergic reactions. Some studies suggest that sage may have antihistamine properties, though more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness for hay fever.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating sage tea into a treatment plan for allergies.

4. Field horsetail (Equisetum arvense)

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, has been traditionally used in herbal teas to help alleviate symptoms of hay fever.

This plant is rich in silica and other minerals, which may support respiratory health and reduce inflammation. While scientific evidence is limited, some studies suggest that horsetail may help thin mucus and ease congestion, common issues during allergic reactions. Herbal teas made from Equisetum arvense are often prepared by steeping dried leaves in hot water, and they are sometimes combined with other herbs like nettle or echinacea for enhanced effects.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using Equisetum arvense, especially for those with existing health conditions or taking medications.

5. Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

Matricaria chamomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a popular herbal tea often used to alleviate symptoms of hay fever.

This plant contains compounds like bisabolol and flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties that may help reduce allergic reactions. While not a cure for hay fever, chamomile tea is believed to soothe nasal congestion and ease throat irritation associated with allergies. It is typically consumed as a warm infusion, and some people find it helpful when taken regularly during pollen season.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using chamomile, especially if you are taking other medications or have known allergies to related plants.

7. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of hay fever.

Its active compounds, such as hypericin and hyperforin, are believed to possess anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties, which may help reduce allergic reactions. 8. Yarrow (Achillea millefolium)

Achillea millefolium, commonly known as yarrow, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential benefits in alleviating symptoms of hay fever.

This herb contains compounds such as flavonoids and essential oils that may help reduce inflammation and support the body's natural defenses against allergic reactions. When brewed into a tea, yarrow can be consumed several times a day to help ease sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes associated with seasonal allergies. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using yarrow tea, especially during pregnancy or if taking other medications.

While some people find relief from hay fever symptoms with yarrow tea, individual responses may vary, and it should not replace conventional medical treatments.

9. Catnip (Nepeta cataria)

Nepeta cataria, commonly known as catnip, has gained attention as a potential herbal remedy for alleviating hay fever symptoms.

This aromatic plant contains compounds like nepetalactone, which may help reduce inflammation and suppress allergic reactions. While scientific research on its effectiveness for hay fever is limited, many individuals report relief from nasal congestion, sneezing, and itching when consuming catnip tea. To prepare the tea, simply steep dried catnip leaves in hot water for several minutes.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using catnip, especially for those with allergies or taking medications, as it may interact with certain drugs.

10. Buckwheat (Plantago ovata)

Plantago ovata, commonly known as psyllium, is a herbal tea that has gained attention for its potential benefits in alleviating symptoms of hay fever.

This tea is rich in soluble fiber, which can help reduce inflammation in the airways and support digestive health. When consumed regularly, it may help thin mucus and ease congestion, common symptoms during allergic reactions. Additionally, its natural anti-inflammatory properties may contribute to reducing allergic responses in the body.

While it is not a cure for hay fever, psyllium herbal tea can be a useful complementary remedy when used alongside conventional treatments.
