10 Best Herbal Decoctions For Swollen Gums

Herbal decoctions have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of swollen gums due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Commonly used herbs include sage, echinacea, and goldenseal, which are known for their ability to reduce inflammation and combat bacterial infections. To prepare a decoction, these herbs are typically simmered in water for several minutes to extract their active compounds. The resulting liquid can be used as a mouth rinse or applied directly to the affected area with a clean cloth or swab.

Regular use of such herbal decoctions may help soothe irritation, reduce swelling, and promote healing in cases of gingivitis or minor gum infections.

1. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When prepared as a decoction, sage can help reduce swelling and inflammation associated with gum issues. The active compounds in sage, such as rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, contribute to its ability to soothe irritated gum tissues. To make a sage decoction, fresh or dried leaves are boiled in water and then strained.

Regular use of this herbal remedy may support oral health and provide relief for individuals suffering from swollen gums.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, has been traditionally used for its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for addressing swollen gums.

When prepared as a herbal decoction, aloe vera can be brewed by simmering the gel or leaves in water, allowing the beneficial compounds to infuse into the liquid. This decoction may help reduce gum inflammation, promote healing, and alleviate discomfort associated with gingivitis or periodontal issues. However, it is important to note that while aloe vera is generally safe for topical use, consuming it in large quantities or using it undiluted may cause gastrointestinal irritation.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating aloe barbadensis decoctions into a treatment regimen for swollen gums.

3. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

When prepared as a herbal decoction, it can be used to soothe swollen gums by reducing inflammation and combating bacterial infections. The decoction is typically made by simmering the dried plant material in water for several minutes, allowing the active compounds to infuse into the liquid. This preparation can be applied directly to the gums using a clean cloth or as a mouth rinse to provide relief from discomfort and promote healing.

While generally considered safe for short-term use, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using St. John's Wort, especially if taking other medications, due to potential interactions.

4. Echinacea (Echinacea purpurea)

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a herbal remedy often used for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When prepared as a decoction, echinacea can be used to soothe swollen gums by reducing inflammation and inhibiting bacterial growth in the mouth. The decoction is typically made by simmering the dried roots and leaves in water for an extended period to extract the active compounds. Some studies suggest that echinacea may support the immune system, which can aid in the body's response to gum infections.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using echinacea, especially for individuals with allergies or those taking other medications.

5. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

When prepared as a decoction, the leaves and stems of Urtica dioica can be boiled to create a potent herbal tea that may help reduce swelling and inflammation in the gums. This decoction is believed to work by soothing irritated tissues and promoting healing through its high concentration of minerals and antioxidants. To prepare the decoction, fresh or dried nettle is simmered in water for about 15-20 minutes, then strained and used as a mouth rinse or applied topically.

While it may offer some relief for swollen gums, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it as a treatment, especially if symptoms persist or worsen.

6. German chamomile (Chamomilla recutita)

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, making it a popular choice for addressing swollen gums.

Herbal decoctions made from chamomilla recutita can be prepared by steeping the dried flowers in hot water, creating a calming tea that may help reduce gum inflammation and promote healing. The active compounds in chamomile, such as flavonoids and essential oils, contribute to its ability to alleviate pain and reduce bacterial growth in the mouth. When used as a mouth rinse or applied directly to the gums, chamomile decoctions can provide localized relief and support oral hygiene.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using chamomile, especially if symptoms persist or if you have known allergies to plants in the daisy family.

7. Ceylon cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum)

Cinnamomum verum, commonly known as true cinnamon, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When prepared as a decoction, cinnamon can help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated gums, making it a beneficial remedy for swollen gums. To make the decoction, cinnamon sticks or ground cinnamon powder are simmered in water for several minutes, allowing the active compounds to infuse into the liquid. This herbal remedy can be used as a mouth rinse or applied directly to the gums for localized relief.

While generally safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using cinnamon decoctions, especially for prolonged periods or in individuals with sensitive skin or allergies.

8. Marigold (Calendula officinalis)

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as garden marigold, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular choice in herbal decoctions for treating swollen gums.

When prepared as a decoction, calendula flowers are simmered in water to extract their beneficial compounds, including flavonoids and triterpenes, which help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated tissues. This herbal remedy can be used as a mouth rinse or applied topically to the affected areas to provide relief from gum swelling and discomfort. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for individuals seeking natural alternatives to conventional treatments.

However, it is advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before using calendula decoctions, especially for those with known allergies or existing medical conditions.

9. Bloodroot (Sanguinaria canadensis)

Sanguinaria canadensis, also known as bloodroot, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When prepared as a decoction, the root of this plant can be simmered in water to extract its active compounds, which may help reduce inflammation and infection in the gums. However, it is important to note that sanguinaria canadensis contains toxic alkaloids, such as sanguinarine, which can be harmful if ingested in large amounts or used improperly. Due to these risks, it is strongly advised to consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare professional before using bloodroot for any medicinal purpose, including treating swollen gums.

Despite its historical use, modern medical research on its safety and efficacy for gum health remains limited, emphasizing the need for caution.

10. Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis)

Rosmarinus officinalis, commonly known as rosemary, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

When prepared as a decoction, rosemary can help reduce inflammation and infection in swollen gums by promoting blood circulation and soothing irritations. The essential oils in rosemary, such as cineole and camphor, contribute to its ability to combat bacterial growth and ease gum discomfort. To use rosemary decoction for swollen gums, it is typically cooled and applied as a mouth rinse or used in a compress.

However, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using it, especially for individuals with known allergies or existing oral conditions.
