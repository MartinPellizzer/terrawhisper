10 Best Herbal Syrups For Psoriasis

Herbal syrups have gained attention as a complementary treatment for psoriasis due to their potential anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties.

These syrups often contain ingredients like turmeric, ginger, and echinacea, which are known for their natural healing and soothing effects. While they are not a cure for psoriasis, some studies suggest that certain herbs may help reduce plaque formation and alleviate symptoms such as itching and redness. It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using herbal syrups, as they can interact with medications or have side effects.

Overall, herbal syrups may offer a gentle, natural option for managing psoriasis symptoms when used as part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

1. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, has been traditionally used for its potential anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties, which may offer benefits for individuals with psoriasis.

While it is more commonly associated with treating mild to moderate depression, some studies suggest that its active compounds, such as hyperforin and hypericin, may help reduce skin inflammation and slow the progression of psoriasis. Herbal syrups containing Hypericum perforatum are often used as complementary therapy, though their effectiveness can vary depending on the formulation and dosage. It is important to note that St. John's Wort can interact with various medications, so consulting a healthcare provider before use is essential.

Despite its potential, more clinical research is needed to fully understand its role in managing psoriasis symptoms.

2. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, has been traditionally used for its soothing and healing properties, and its herbal syrups are gaining attention for their potential benefits in managing psoriasis symptoms.

These syrups are often formulated with a combination of aloe vera gel, essential oils, and other natural ingredients that may help reduce inflammation and promote skin healing. Some studies suggest that aloe vera can help alleviate redness, itching, and scaling associated with psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using aloe-based syrups, as they may interact with other treatments or have side effects for certain individuals.

While not a cure, aloe barbadensis herbal syrups may serve as a complementary therapy to support overall psoriasis management.

3. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties.

Herbal syrups made from Urtica dioica are often recommended for individuals with psoriasis due to their potential to reduce skin inflammation and support overall immune health. These syrups typically contain a blend of nettle extract, honey, and other herbal ingredients that may enhance their therapeutic effects. While some studies suggest that nettle may help alleviate psoriasis symptoms, more research is needed to confirm its efficacy and long-term safety.

As with any herbal remedy, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using Urtica dioica syrups, especially for those with existing health conditions or taking other medications.

4. Blessed thistle (Cnicus benedictus)

Cnicus benedictus, also known as blessed thorn, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing properties.

Herbal syrups made from Cnicus benedictus are believed to support the treatment of psoriasis by reducing skin irritation and redness. These syrups may help modulate the immune response, which plays a key role in the development of psoriasis. While more research is needed to confirm their efficacy, some users report improved symptoms when using Cnicus benedictus syrups as part of a holistic treatment plan.

It is important to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating these herbal remedies into a psoriasis management regimen.

5. Thistle (Silybum marianum)

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a herbal remedy that has been studied for its potential benefits in managing psoriasis.

The herbal syrup derived from its seeds contains silymarin, a group of flavonoid compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Some preliminary research suggests that silymarin may help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which are key factors in psoriasis progression. However, while anecdotal evidence supports its use, more clinical trials are needed to confirm its efficacy and safety for psoriasis treatment.

It is often used as a complementary therapy alongside conventional treatments, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

6. Camellia (Camellia sinensis)

Camellia sinensis, commonly known as the plant from which green and black tea are derived, has been explored for its potential benefits in managing psoriasis due to its rich content of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

Herbal syrups made from Camellia sinensis may help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which are key factors in psoriasis development. Some studies suggest that the polyphenols in these syrups could modulate immune responses and support skin health. However, more clinical research is needed to confirm their efficacy and safety for psoriasis treatment.

As a complementary therapy, Camellia sinensis syrups may offer a natural alternative or adjunct to conventional treatments, but they should not replace medical advice from a healthcare professional.

7. Field horsetail (Equisetum arvense)

Equisetum arvense, also known as field horsetail, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for its potential anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties.

Herbal syrups made from Equisetum arvense are often prepared using the dried and powdered plant parts, which are rich in silica and other bioactive compounds. These syrups are believed to support skin health and may help alleviate symptoms of psoriasis by reducing inflammation and promoting cellular repair. Some studies suggest that the high silica content in Equisetum arvense may contribute to the strengthening of connective tissues and the improvement of skin integrity.

While more research is needed, many individuals with psoriasis use these syrups as a complementary therapy alongside conventional treatments.

8. Echinacea (Echinacea purpurea)

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, has been traditionally used for its immune-boosting properties and is sometimes incorporated into herbal syrups for its potential anti-inflammatory effects.

While there is limited clinical evidence specifically linking echinacea to the treatment of psoriasis, some studies suggest it may help reduce inflammation and support the immune system, which could be beneficial for individuals with psoriasis. Herbal syrups containing echinacea are often marketed as natural remedies to complement conventional treatments, though they should not replace prescribed medications. It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using echinacea, as it may interact with certain medications or exacerbate conditions in some individuals.

Overall, while echinacea may offer some supportive benefits, its role in managing psoriasis remains under-researched and should be approached with caution.

9. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which may offer potential benefits for individuals with psoriasis.

While there is limited scientific research specifically on sage herbal syrups for psoriasis, some studies suggest that compounds found in sage, such as rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, may help reduce skin inflammation and oxidative stress. Herbal syrups made from salvia officinalis are often used as complementary therapies to support overall skin health and immune function. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using sage syrups, as they may interact with other medications or have side effects in certain individuals.

Despite the lack of extensive clinical trials, many people with psoriasis find herbal remedies like sage syrup to be a soothing addition to their treatment regimen.

10. Ginkgo (Ginkgo biloba)

Ginkgo biloba herbal syrups are traditionally used for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may offer some relief for individuals with psoriasis.

These syrups are derived from the leaves of the ginkgo tree, known for improving blood circulation and reducing oxidative stress in the body. While scientific evidence supporting their effectiveness for psoriasis is limited, some studies suggest that ginkgo biloba may help reduce skin inflammation and slow the progression of psoriatic lesions. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using ginkgo biloba, as it can interact with certain medications and may cause side effects in some individuals.

As an alternative or complementary therapy, ginkgo biloba syrups may be considered under professional guidance to support overall skin health in psoriasis management.
