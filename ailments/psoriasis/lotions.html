10 Best Herbal Lotions For Psoriasis

Herbal lotions have gained popularity as a complementary treatment for psoriasis due to their natural ingredients, which may help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated skin.

Common herbs used in these lotions include chamomile, calendula, and lavender, all known for their anti-inflammatory and calming properties. These herbal formulations can be applied topically to affected areas to provide relief from redness, itching, and scaling associated with psoriasis. While they are generally considered safe, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have allergies or are using other medications.

Although herbal lotions may not cure psoriasis, they can be a valuable addition to a comprehensive treatment plan when used under professional guidance.

1. Stinging nettle (Urtica dioica)

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been explored as a natural remedy for psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Herbal lotions made from Urtica dioica are often used topically to soothe irritated skin and reduce the redness and scaling associated with psoriasis. These lotions may help regulate skin cell growth and reduce the overproduction of keratin, a common issue in psoriasis. However, while some individuals report relief from using Urtica dioica lotions, more clinical research is needed to confirm their efficacy and safety for psoriasis treatment.

2. St. john's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

While primarily known for its use in treating mild depression, it has also been explored as a potential remedy for skin conditions like psoriasis due to its ability to reduce skin inflammation and promote healing. Herbal lotions containing Hypericum perforatum may help alleviate symptoms such as redness, scaling, and itching associated with psoriasis by soothing the affected skin. However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using such products, as they may interact with other medications or have side effects.

Despite its traditional use, scientific evidence supporting its efficacy for psoriasis remains limited, and it should not replace conventional treatments without professional guidance.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, is a popular herbal ingredient used in lotions for psoriasis due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

These lotions often contain aloe vera gel, which helps to moisturize and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and scaling associated with psoriasis. The natural compounds in aloe vera, such as polysaccharides and antioxidants, may support skin repair and enhance the skin's natural barrier function. While aloe-based lotions are generally considered safe and gentle, they should be used as a complementary treatment alongside prescribed therapies for optimal results.

Patients with psoriasis should consult a healthcare provider before incorporating aloe barbadensis lotions into their skincare routine to ensure they are appropriate for their specific condition.

4. Field horsetail (Equisetum arvense)

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for its potential anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties.

Herbal lotions made from Equisetum arvense are often recommended for individuals with psoriasis due to their high silica content, which may help strengthen skin tissue and improve overall skin health. These lotions are typically prepared by infusing the dried plant material in a carrier oil or water, creating a soothing topical application. While some studies suggest that horsetail may support skin healing, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using it, as it can interact with certain medications.

Overall, Equisetum arvense herbal lotions may offer a natural complementary approach to managing psoriasis symptoms, though they should not replace conventional medical treatments.

5. Camellia (Camellia sinensis)

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green tea is derived, is increasingly being explored for its potential benefits in treating psoriasis through herbal lotions.

These lotions often contain extracts rich in polyphenols, particularly catechins, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce skin inflammation and redness associated with psoriasis. Some studies suggest that topical application of Camellia sinensis-based formulations can soothe irritated skin and promote healing by modulating the immune response. However, more clinical research is needed to confirm their efficacy and determine optimal formulations for psoriasis treatment.

Despite the promising preliminary findings, patients should consult with dermatologists before using these lotions as part of their psoriasis management plan.

6. Ginger (Zingiber officinale)

Zingiber officinale, commonly known as ginger, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal lotions for psoriasis.

These lotions often contain ginger extract, which may help reduce skin inflammation and slow the rapid growth of skin cells associated with psoriasis. Some studies suggest that topical application of ginger-based formulations can provide relief from psoriatic symptoms such as redness, scaling, and itching. However, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using ginger lotions, as individual skin reactions can vary.

While ginger herbal lotions may offer complementary benefits, they should not replace prescribed treatments for psoriasis.

7. Blessed thistle (Cnicus benedictus)

Cnicus benedictus, also known as blessed thistle, is a herbal remedy that has been explored for its potential benefits in managing psoriasis symptoms.

This herb contains compounds such as alkaloids and flavonoids, which may have anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. When used in the form of herbal lotions, Cnicus benedictus can provide a topical application that may help reduce redness, scaling, and itching associated with psoriasis. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using it, as it may interact with other medications or cause allergic reactions.

While some individuals may find relief with this natural treatment, it should not replace conventional medical therapies without proper guidance.

8. Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Herbal lotions containing salvia officinalis are increasingly being explored as natural remedies for psoriasis due to their potential to soothe irritated skin and reduce scaling. These lotions may help alleviate symptoms by promoting skin healing and reducing redness, although more clinical studies are needed to confirm their efficacy. The essential oils and extracts from sage are often combined with other botanical ingredients to enhance their therapeutic effects.

While some users report positive results, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating salvia-based treatments into a psoriasis management plan.

9. Dog rose (Rosa canina)

Rosa canina, also known as dog rose, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to support skin health and treat various dermatological conditions, including psoriasis.

Rosa canina herbal lotions are formulated with the extracts of the rose hip, which is rich in essential fatty acids, antioxidants, and vitamins, particularly vitamin C, that can help reduce inflammation and promote skin regeneration. These lotions are often recommended as a complementary therapy to conventional treatments for psoriasis due to their soothing and moisturizing properties. The anti-inflammatory and regenerative effects of Rosa canina may help alleviate symptoms such as dryness, redness, and scaling associated with psoriasis.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using these products, especially if you are undergoing other treatments or have known allergies.

10. Thistle (Silybum marianum)

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a herbal remedy that has been explored for its potential benefits in managing psoriasis.

Herbal lotions containing silybum marianum are often used topically to reduce inflammation and soothe the skin due to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These lotions may help alleviate symptoms such as redness, scaling, and itching associated with psoriasis by promoting skin healing and reducing oxidative stress. While some studies suggest that silybum marianum may support overall skin health, more clinical research is needed to confirm its efficacy for psoriasis specifically.

As with any herbal treatment, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating silybum marianum lotions into a psoriasis management plan.
