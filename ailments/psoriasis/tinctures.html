10 Best Herbal Tinctures For Psoriasis

Herbal tinctures have gained attention as a complementary therapy for managing psoriasis symptoms, offering a natural alternative to conventional treatments.

These tinctures are typically made by soaking herbs in alcohol or another solvent to extract their active compounds, which may have anti-inflammatory, antifungal, or immunomodulatory properties. Common herbs used in psoriasis tinctures include turmeric, echinacea, and goldenseal, each known for their potential to reduce skin inflammation and support immune function. While some individuals report relief from using these tinctures, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before incorporating them into a treatment plan, as they may interact with medications or have side effects.

Overall, herbal tinctures can be a part of a holistic approach to psoriasis management, though they should not replace professional medical advice or prescribed treatments.

1. Urtica dioica

Urtica dioica, commonly known as stinging nettle, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties.

When prepared as a tincture, Urtica dioica may support skin health by reducing inflammation and promoting the elimination of toxins from the body. Some studies suggest that the compounds in stinging nettle, such as flavonoids and lignans, may help alleviate symptoms of psoriasis by modulating immune responses. However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using Urtica dioica tinctures, as they may interact with certain medications or exacerbate existing conditions.

While not a cure, Urtica dioica tinctures may be considered a complementary therapy for individuals managing psoriasis under professional guidance.

2. Hypericum perforatum

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, has been traditionally used for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a subject of interest in the treatment of psoriasis.

Herbal tinctures made from Hypericum perforatum are often prepared using alcohol as a solvent to extract the active compounds, such as hypericin and hyperforin, which may help reduce skin inflammation and redness associated with psoriasis. While some studies suggest that St. John's Wort may offer relief for mild psoriatic symptoms, more rigorous clinical trials are needed to confirm its efficacy and safety for this condition. Due to potential interactions with other medications, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using Hypericum perforatum tinctures as a complementary therapy.

Overall, while it may provide some benefit, it should not replace conventional medical treatments for psoriasis without professional guidance.

3. Aloe barbadensis

Aloe barbadensis, commonly known as aloe vera, has been traditionally used for its soothing and healing properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal tinctures for psoriasis.

These tinctures are often prepared by extracting the gel from the aloe leaf and combining it with alcohol to create a concentrated form that can be applied topically. The anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects of aloe vera may help reduce redness, scaling, and irritation associated with psoriasis. Some studies suggest that aloe can support skin regeneration and enhance the skin's natural barrier function, which is beneficial for those with chronic skin conditions.

While aloe barbadensis tinctures are generally considered safe, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you are undergoing other treatments for psoriasis.

4. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a popular herbal remedy for psoriasis.

Curcuma longa tinctures are concentrated extracts of the rhizome, often combined with alcohol to enhance absorption and potency. These tinctures may help reduce skin inflammation and redness associated with psoriasis by inhibiting inflammatory pathways in the body. However, while some individuals report improvement in symptoms, more clinical research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety for long-term use.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating curcuma longa tinctures into a psoriasis treatment regimen.

5. Rosa canina

Rosa canina, also known as rosehip, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries to support skin health and reduce inflammation.

Rosa canina herbal tinctures are concentrated extracts made from the dried fruits of the rose plant, rich in antioxidants, omega fatty acids, and vitamin C. These tinctures are often used as a complementary therapy for psoriasis due to their anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. The bioactive compounds in rosehip may help reduce skin redness, scaling, and itching associated with psoriasis.

While not a cure, rosa canina tinctures can be a valuable addition to a holistic treatment plan under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

6. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, has been traditionally used for its immune-boosting properties, and its herbal tinctures are increasingly being explored for their potential benefits in managing psoriasis.

These tinctures contain active compounds such as alkamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides, which may help modulate the immune system and reduce inflammatory responses associated with psoriasis. Some preliminary studies suggest that echinacea may help alleviate symptoms by reducing skin inflammation and slowing the rapid growth of skin cells, though more research is needed to confirm its efficacy. While it is generally considered safe for short-term use, individuals with allergies to plants in the daisy family should exercise caution.

As with any complementary therapy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating echinacea tinctures into a psoriasis treatment plan.

7. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage, is a herbal plant that has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Salvia officinalis herbal tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts made by soaking the plant in alcohol, which helps preserve its active compounds. These tinctures may offer relief for psoriasis by reducing inflammation and soothing the skin due to their high levels of flavonoids and essential oils. Some studies suggest that sage may help regulate sebum production and reduce skin irritation, which are common issues in psoriasis.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using sage tinctures, as they may interact with certain medications or cause allergic reactions in some individuals.

8. Equisetum arvense

Equisetum arvense, also known as field horsetail, is a traditional herbal remedy that has been used for centuries due to its high concentration of silica and other bioactive compounds.

Herbal tinctures made from Equisetum arvense are often prepared using alcohol to extract its beneficial properties, making them easily absorbed by the body. These tinctures are believed to support skin health and may help reduce inflammation associated with psoriasis. Some practitioners use Equisetum arvense tinctures as part of a holistic approach to managing psoriasis symptoms, although more research is needed to confirm their efficacy.

As with any herbal treatment, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for individuals with chronic conditions like psoriasis.

9. Chamomilla recutita

Chamomilla recutita, commonly known as German chamomile, has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, making it a popular choice in herbal tinctures for managing psoriasis symptoms.

The tincture is typically prepared by soaking the dried flowers in alcohol, allowing the active compounds like flavonoids and essential oils to be extracted for therapeutic use. These compounds may help reduce skin inflammation, redness, and itching associated with psoriasis by modulating the immune response and promoting skin healing. While some studies suggest potential benefits, more clinical research is needed to fully understand its efficacy and safety for psoriasis treatment.

As with any herbal remedy, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating chamomilla recutita tinctures into a psoriasis management plan.

10. Silybum marianum

Silybum marianum, also known as milk thistle, is a herbal remedy that has been studied for its potential benefits in managing psoriasis.

The active compound in silybum marianum, silymarin, is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce skin inflammation and oxidative stress associated with psoriasis. Some research suggests that silymarin can inhibit the production of inflammatory cytokines, which play a key role in the progression of psoriasis. While preliminary studies show promise, more clinical trials are needed to confirm its efficacy and safety for psoriasis treatment.

As with any herbal supplement, it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using silybum marianum tinctures, especially if you are taking other medications or have underlying health conditions.
